## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Wageningen University & Research admits international master’s students in two intakes: September (the main intake) and February (available for Biosystems Engineering, Biotechnology, Biology, Environmental Sciences and Forest & Nature Conservation). For the September intake you must register in Studielink by 15 June 2026 and then upload all required documents in the WUR application portal (WU portal) by 1 July; the tuition‑fee payment deadline (via digital authorisation in Studielink) is 15 August. For the February intake the Studielink deadline is 15 November 2026, with document submission in the WU portal by 1 December; tuition‑fee payment must be completed by 15 December. All EU/EFTA applicants pay the tuition fee through Studielink (digital authorisation) while non‑EU/EFTA applicants receive an invoice after conditional admission and must settle it before the above payment deadlines. The general application procedure is: (1) create a Studielink account and select the desired programme and intake; (2) confirm payment method (digital authorisation for EU/EFTA or await invoice for non‑EU/EFTA); (3) upload academic transcripts, proof of English proficiency and any programme‑specific documents in the WU portal by the listed dates; (4) wait for the admission decision; (5) once admitted, ensure the tuition‑fee is paid by the respective deadline to secure enrolment.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Apply for a master's programme - Wageningen - WUR](https://www.wur.nl/en/education/master/application-admission-masters/apply-masters-programme) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Master: after your application \| WUR](https://www.wur.nl/en/education/master/application-admission-masters/after-your-application-masters) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Apply for a bachelor's programme - Wageningen - WUR](https://www.wur.nl/en/education/bachelor/application-admission-bachelors/apply-bachelors-programme) (Cao, score 1.00)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants must hold a secondary‑school qualification that is recognised as being at least equivalent to the Dutch pre‑university (VWO) diploma; Wageningen provides a detailed equivalency list for the September 2026 intake and evaluates any other qualification on a case‑by‑case basis. In addition to the overall level, applicants must satisfy programme‑specific subject requirements (e.g., biology, chemistry, mathematics, physics or economics depending on the bachelor chosen) and, for English‑taught programmes, meet the English language criteria; Dutch‑taught programmes also require Dutch language proficiency. Admission is based on a recent official transcript (list of marks) that is uploaded through the Osiris portal; the university will issue a personal result letter indicating whether the submitted grades satisfy the subject and level requirements or if additional pre‑university (VWO) exams are needed. No specific GPA threshold is published, but the transcript must demonstrate sufficient performance in the required subjects; there is no general portfolio or interview requirement for the bachelor programmes, although a few specialised tracks may request extra material, which is communicated individually.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Bachelor admission with international prior education \| WUR](https://www.wur.nl/en/education/bachelor/application-admission-bachelors/apply-bachelors-programme/international-prior-education) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Apply for a bachelor's programme - Wageningen - WUR](https://www.wur.nl/en/education/bachelor/application-admission-bachelors/apply-bachelors-programme) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[[PDF] Enrolment Regulations Wageningen University 2025-2026](https://support.wur.nl/sys_attachment.do?sys_id=2cacc12e2bc8c710163afd33fe91bfff&amp;sysparm_this_url=u_kb_template_default.do%3Fsys_id%3Da6140b3e972132905617b2b3f153af51%26sysparm_view%3D%26sysparm_domain%3Dnull%26sysparm_domain_scope%3Dnull) (Cao, score 0.99)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Wageningen University & Research requires all international bachelor, master and PhD applicants whose programme is taught in English to prove English proficiency unless they are exempt, and the accepted test scores are IELTS Academic ≥ 6.0 overall with at least 6.0 in speaking, TOEFL iBT ≥ 80 total with at least 20 in speaking, and Pearson PTE Academic ≥ 61 overall with at least 56 in speaking; Duolingo English Test is not listed among the accepted exams, so it cannot be used for admission, while a waiver is granted to applicants who are nationals of an English‑speaking country, who have completed a previous degree that was taught entirely in English, or who hold a Dutch university master’s degree (or equivalent) taught in English.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Wageningen University & Research (WUR) in the Netherlands is institutionally accredited by the NVAO (Accreditation Organisation of the Netherlands and Flanders); in the most recent QS World University Rankings it sits around 140th globally, while the Times Higher Education World University Rankings places it 67th worldwide, and in subject‑specific rankings WUR consistently leads in agricultural and environmental disciplines—QS ranks it #1 for Agriculture, #2 for Food Science & Technology, and within the top three for Plant & Animal Sciences and Environmental Sciences; THE subject rankings list it 16th in Life Sciences and 35th in Social Sciences, and ARWU ranks it 2nd in Agricultural Sciences, 3rd in Ecology, and 6th in Food Science & Technology—and the university enrolls roughly 3,000‑3,500 international students, representing about 25‑30 % of its total student body.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [[PDF] Annual report - 2019 - WUR](https://backend.wur.nl/sites/default/files/2025-11/Jaarverslag-2019-UK.pdf) (Cao, score 0.72)<br>[Rankings and scientific awards \| WUR](https://www.wur.nl/en/about-wur/facts-figures/rankings-and-scientific-awards) (Cao, score 0.69)<br>[[PDF] Annual report 2022 - Wageningen - WUR](https://backend.wur.nl/sites/default/files/2025-11/Jaarverslag-WUR-2022-EN.pdf) (Cao, score 0.64)

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: International students who are non‑EU citizens and wish to study at Wageningen University & Research must obtain an MVV (machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf) entry visa – a sticker placed in the passport by the Dutch embassy – and a VVR (verblijfsvergunning regulier) residence permit, which is issued in the Netherlands after biometric data are provided; the university’s International Office applies for both through the IND in a combined TEV procedure that typically takes 4‑6 weeks, and the student must present proof of sufficient financial means, currently set at approximately €13 000 per year (about €1 083 per month) which can be shown via a bank statement, scholarship letter, sponsor declaration or a student‑loan document, along with an unconditional admission letter, valid health insurance and compliance with any additional IND requirements listed on the official Nuffic studyinnl.org site. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Netherlands Orientation Year (Zoekjaar) visa gives international graduates of Dutch higher‑education institutions—including Wageningen University & Research—a one‑year residence permit, valid for twelve months and not renewable, during which they may work for any employer, undertake internships or start a business without needing a separate work permit; to qualify you must have completed an accredited bachelor’s, master’s, PhD or a post‑master programme of at least ten months (or have conducted recognised scientific research) at Wageningen University (or any other Dutch university) and you must apply within three years of your graduation date, after which you must register with the municipality, obtain a BSN and switch to another residence permit before the year ends.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Entry visa and residence permit prospective students - WUR](https://www.wur.nl/en/education/master/application-admission-masters/entry-visa-and-residence-permit-prospective-students) (Cao, score 0.72)<br>[[PDF] Non-EU exchange students at Wageningen University & Research](https://backend.wur.nl/sites/default/files/2025-11/Information%20for%20non-EU%20students%202025-2026.pdf) (Cao, score 0.69)<br>[Netherlands Student Visa Guide 2026 (MVV & VVR) Blog](https://www.study-abroad.org/blog/nl-student-visa-guide/) (Trung bình, score 0.68)<br>[Orientation Year Visa (Zoekjaar) Guide - Study in Netherlands 2026](https://studypath.nl/guides/post-study-visa) (Trung bình, score 0.74)<br>[Qualifying Universities for Netherlands Orientation Year Visa](https://workabroad.in/what-is-the-list-of-qualifying-universities-for-the-netherlands-orientation-year-visa/) (Trung bình, score 0.69)<br>[Orientation year guide in the Netherlands (2026) - Feather Insurance](https://feather-insurance.com/en-nl/blog/orientation-year-guide) (Trung bình, score 0.67)

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Wageningen University & Research enrolls roughly 13 200 students, of whom about 40 % of master’s students and 9 % of bachelor’s students are international, and it integrates compulsory internships or placement projects into most curricula, with over 85 % of programmes requiring at least one six‑month work‑based learning period that is often organised through the university’s extensive network of partner companies, research institutes and NGOs across Europe, the United States, Asia and Africa; as a result, around 94 % of MSc graduates and 80 % of BSc graduates secure employment or start a postgraduate trajectory within three to six months after graduation, most commonly in sectors such as agribusiness, food technology, sustainable agriculture, environmental consulting, biotechnology, policy development and international development, reflecting the strong industry links and high employability associated with WUR’s graduates. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Wageningen University & Research, which specializes in life‑sciences, agriculture, food and environmental studies, find strong demand for roles such as agronomist, food‑technology specialist, sustainable‑development analyst, data‑science or digital‑innovation positions in the agri‑food sector, as well as research and policy advisory jobs in government and NGOs; entry‑level salaries for these graduates typically range from €40 000 to €55 000 per year, and because the Dutch labour market values the university’s applied research expertise, many graduates progress to earn €65 000 or more within five years, with a high proportion (around 80 %) securing paid employment and a sizable share remaining in the Netherlands after graduation.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Wageningen University & Research’s International Office (also known as the Welcome Center Food Valley) runs a comprehensive welcome programme for incoming international students and PhD candidates that includes pre‑arrival information, on‑site orientation activities and introductory events, provides dedicated housing support through the Student Service Centre together with the main student housing provider Idealis (and the online platform ROOM.nl) offering practical guidance and contacts to help newcomers find accommodation in Wageningen or nearby towns, and operates a career service that encompasses the Dual‑Career Programme for partners, the WUR Career Centre’s job‑search workshops, internship listings, employer networking events and personalised coaching to assist international graduates in entering the Dutch and global labour market. Đời sống & môi trường: Wageningen University & Research offers a vibrant campus life for its large international student body, with dozens of student associations that cater to social, cultural, sporting and professional interests – for example, the historic W.S.V. Ceres society, the internationally‑focused ESN Wageningen (one of the biggest Erasmus Student Network chapters in the Netherlands) which runs hundreds of social, cultural and sports events and trips across the country and Europe, and a range of other clubs such as music groups, environmental platforms, Amnesty International, Unicef, vegan and career‑oriented societies like Enactus and Integrand; students can also live in affordable university housing, make use of top‑class sports facilities, and benefit from safety advice such as always carrying personal ID for quick identification and medical care; Vietnamese students commonly connect through the broader international community, especially ESN activities, and through informal Vietnamese student groups and social media networks that organise meet‑ups, cultural celebrations and peer‑support, ensuring they have a supportive community while studying in Wageningen.
