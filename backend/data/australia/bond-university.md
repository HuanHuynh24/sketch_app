### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Bond University accepts international applicants for its two main intakes each year – a March/April start and a July/August start – and you can submit your application at any time through its free, fully online portal (or via an approved agent if you prefer additional support). There is no separate application fee; the process is free, and you only need to pay the first‑semester fee deposit after you receive an unconditional offer and accept it, which then triggers the issuance of your Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) for visa purposes. To apply you must create an online account, complete the application form, upload required documents (academic transcripts, English‑language test results or evidence of prior study in English, passport details, etc.), meet the program‑specific academic and English requirements, and then submit the application; once an offer is made you accept it, pay the semester deposit, and can proceed with your student‑visa application. Recommended deadlines are to apply at least 6–8 weeks before the intended start date to allow time for assessment, offer issuance, and visa processing.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students wishing to study at Bond University must have successfully completed the equivalent of Australian Year 12 (e.g., a national high‑school diploma, IB, A‑levels, etc.) and provide an official transcript showing a minimum overall GPA of about 5.0 out of 7 (approximately a C+ average) or an ATAR‑equivalent score of 91 (IB 33) for guaranteed entry into most undergraduate programs; each degree lists specific prerequisite subjects (for example, Mathematics and Physics for Engineering, Chemistry for Health Sciences, or Business Studies for Commerce) and may require higher grades in those areas. All programs are taught in English, so applicants must meet the English language requirement—typically IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, TOEFL iBT 79, or an approved alternative test. Certain creative and professional courses (such as Architecture, Design, Fine Arts, Music, and some Media programmes) also demand a portfolio of recent work, and selective programmes like Medicine, Law or some Business streams may require an interview or additional selection criteria. The application package therefore includes the academic transcript (học bạ), proof of English proficiency, any required test scores (e.g., SAT, ACT, or specific subject exams), evidence of prerequisite subject results, and, where applicable, a portfolio and interview participation.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Bond University requires international students to demonstrate English proficiency through an approved test unless they have completed secondary education (or an equivalent senior certificate) in an English‑speaking country such as Australia, the United Kingdom, the United States, Canada, New Zealand or Ireland, or have completed post‑secondary study where English was the sole language of instruction, in which case a waiver may be granted at the university’s discretion. For most programmes the minimum is an overall IELTS Academic score of 7.0, with no individual band lower than 6.5 (some programmes require no band lower than 7.0); the equivalent TOEFL iBT requirement is an overall score of 79, with at least 21 in Writing, 16 in Speaking and 10 in each of Reading and Listening; the equivalent Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE) score is an overall 58, with a minimum of 50 in Writing and 42 in each of Speaking, Reading and Listening. Duolingo English Test is not listed among Bond’s approved tests, so it cannot be used for admission.

**4. Học phí**

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Bond University requires international applicants to have completed Australian Year 12 or an equivalent qualification (e.g., SAT ≥ 1020–1100, GCE A‑Levels ≥ 5–8 points, IB ≥ 24–28) and meet English language standards, with certain programmes such as the Bachelor of Biomedical Science also demanding successful completion of Mathematical Methods, Physics or an equivalent subject; students who do not meet direct entry criteria can enrol in the Bond University College Foundation Program, an eight‑month pathway (two semesters) worth 80 credit points (CPS) that builds core academic, English language and independent learning skills needed for undergraduate study. Undergraduate degrees are structured in credit point units, with most subjects valued at 10 CPS (equivalent to three to four US semester credits) and total bachelor programmes ranging from 240 to 360 CPS (typically 24–36 subjects), while diplomas carry 80 CPS and graduate certificates 40 CPS; the curriculum is organised into semester‑based subject blocks, each requiring approximately 84 hours of preparation, study and assessment, and learning outcomes focus on mastery of subject content, development of analytical and critical thinking abilities, and the acquisition of professional‑ready knowledge and skills.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Student visa (subclass 500) permits international students enrolled at a registered provider such as Bond University to study full‑time in Australia while maintaining required Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire duration of the visa, and it imposes work limits of a maximum of 48 hours per fortnight during teaching periods (with unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks, and no limit for research‑based Master’s or Doctoral programmes); applicants must demonstrate financial capacity of at least AUD 29,710 to cover one year of living expenses (plus AUD 10,394 for an accompanying partner and AUD 4,449 per dependent child, with additional schooling costs of about AUD 13,502 per child if school‑aged) and provide evidence such as bank statements, loan documents or scholarship letters, and the visa can be applied for through the Department of Home Affairs (immi.homeaffairs.gov.au) using the official Study in Australia portal (studyinaustralia.gov.au) where the detailed requirements and conditions are listed. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the primary post‑study work pathway for international graduates from Bond University – a CRICOS‑registered Australian institution – and it allows you to remain in Australia after your student visa expires (which normally gives you a three‑month post‑completion bridge) by providing full work rights for you and any eligible family members; if you hold a bachelor’s degree or higher you apply to the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which grants a stay of 2 years for a standard bachelor’s, 3 years for a bachelor’s with first‑class honours in a STEM field, 3 years for a master’s qualification and 4 years for a doctoral degree (with Hong Kong or British National Overseas passport holders eligible for up to 5 years), while graduates of a regional campus can later apply to the Second Post‑Higher Education Work stream for an additional 1–2 years; the visa does not require points testing or an employer sponsor, you must meet the Australian study requirement (completion of a CRICOS‑registered course of at least two academic years), and you may subsequently transition to other visas such as the Skilled‑in‑Demand (subclass 482) if you meet the relevant work‑experience criteria.
