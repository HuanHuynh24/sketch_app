## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students who wish to begin undergraduate studies at the University of Ottawa in September 2026 must submit their application through the Ontario Universities’ Application Centre (OUAC) when the application period opens on 18 September 2025, with the university’s recommended deadline to apply by 15 January 2026 and a final deadline to complete the admission file and upload all supporting documents via uoDoc by 2 May 2026; the OUAC application carries a non‑refundable fee of roughly CAD 150 (and, once an offer is received, a non‑refundable admission deposit of about CAD 250 must be paid to secure enrollment), after which the file is transferred to the university’s uoZone portal (up to 10 business days) where students upload transcripts, proof of English/French proficiency and any other required documents, accept their offer by the specified date (typically 1 April for early admits), pay the deposit, and then apply for a Canadian study permit.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [International applicants \| Study - University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/undergraduate-studies/international-applicants) (Cao, score 0.85)<br>[Application deadlines for international applicants - University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/undergraduate-studies/application-deadlines-available-programs-international) (Cao, score 0.82)<br>[Admission deadlines - international students \| Faculty of Social Sciences](https://www.uottawa.ca/faculty-social-sciences/programs/admission-deadlines-international-students) (Cao, score 0.75)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to the University of Ottawa must have completed an education that is equivalent to the Canadian high‑school diploma (or the appropriate secondary credential for graduate entry) and provide official transcripts—often sent to World Education Services for Canadian equivalency—along with proof of a minimum overall average of about 75 % (approximately a 3.0 GPA), although some programmes set the threshold at 70 % or higher and may require higher grades in specific prerequisite subjects such as mathematics, sciences, or language courses; applicants must also satisfy any programme‑specific prerequisite courses, meet the university’s English (or French, where applicable) language proficiency standards, and for certain creative or professional fields (e.g., fine arts, architecture, design, music, theatre) submit a portfolio and/or attend an interview as part of the admission assessment.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [International applicants \| Study - University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/undergraduate-studies/international-applicants) (Cao, score 0.67)<br>[Admissions from the United States of America - University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/undergraduate-studies/international-applicants/usa) (Cao, score 0.58)<br>[Application deadlines for international applicants - University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/undergraduate-studies/application-deadlines-available-programs-international) (Cao, score 0.58)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to English‑language programs at the University of Ottawa must submit an official score from a University‑approved test taken within two years of the start of their term. The minimum scores for direct admission are IELTS Academic 6.5 overall with at least 6.5 in Writing, TOEFL iBT 86 overall with a minimum 22 in the Writing section (or the same score on the Home‑edition; after 21 January 2026 the new TOEFL scale requires 4.5 overall and 4.5 in Writing), Duolingo English Test 120 overall, and Pearson Test of English Academic 58 overall (minimum 50 in each component). Faculty of Law has higher thresholds (IELTS 7.0 overall / 6.5 Writing or TOEFL 100 overall / 24 Writing). Applicants whose secondary or post‑secondary education was completed entirely in English at an institution or in a country where English is the language of instruction are exempt from providing a test result. The university also grants waivers for those who have previously completed the University’s English‑Intensive Program (EIP) or who meet the “proficiency tests administered by home institutions” criteria (e.g., institutional TOEFL, TOEFL‑ITP, TOEIC).
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Language requirements for incoming exchange students \| Study](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/international-experience/incoming-exchange-students/language-requirements-incoming-exchange) (Cao, score 0.80)<br>[Language requirements \| Study](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/undergraduate-studies/language-requirements) (Cao, score 0.75)<br>[International applicants \| Study - University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/undergraduate-studies/international-applicants) (Cao, score 0.73)

**4. Học phí**

- For international undergraduate students at the University of Ottawa the tuition is charged per term and the 2024‑2025 rates are roughly $22,000 CAD for a full‑time load of up to 33 units (about 11 courses) and decline slightly with larger loads, so a typical two‑term academic year costs about $44,000 CAD in tuition; for graduate programs the term fee is approximately $11,300 CAD, giving an annual tuition of about $22,600 CAD. The application fee for both undergraduate and graduate international applicants is $150 CAD, and additional mandatory fees include the University Health Insurance Plan (UHIP) – roughly $700 CAD per year – plus ancillary student‑services fees and union fees that together add another $400‑$500 CAD annually. All amounts are expressed in Canadian dollars.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Tuition fees - International students \| Study - University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/fees-financial-support/university-fees/tuition-international) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Tuition fees - Non-degree students and auditors \| Study](https://www.uottawa.ca/study/fees-financial-support/university-fees/tuition-non-degree-auditors) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Tuition Fees - MSc MGT - Telfer School of Management](https://telfer.uottawa.ca/en/mscmgt/fees-and-financing/tuition-fees/) (Cao, score 1.00)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at the University of Ottawa can expect to spend roughly CAD 61 000 per year for tuition, mandatory fees, on‑campus housing with a meal plan, transportation and health insurance; tuition for a full‑time undergraduate is about $22 000 per term (≈ $44 000 annually), mandatory fees per term (U‑Pass $223, health services $26, sports $60, university centre $16, dental/health insurance $83, UHIP $252, student‑union $108) total about $768, bringing the fee‑plus‑tuition cost to ≈ $45 536 for the two‑term year, while a traditional double‑occupancy suite with the mandatory 5‑day meal plan costs $15 353 for the eight‑month academic year, resulting in an overall estimated annual expense of ≈ $60 889, with cheaper shared‑apartment options lowering housing to $12 600–$17 500 and the total to roughly $58 000–$60 000.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Ottawa is ranked 189th globally in the QS World University Rankings 2025, placing it in the world’s top 200 universities and second in Canada for research citations, while in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2026 it is positioned at 187th worldwide, four places higher than the previous year; its QS subject rankings show particular strength in Law (around 27th globally), Education (≈46th), Psychology (≈81st), and it ranks within the 151‑200 band for Computer Science and Engineering & Technology, and the university is fully accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Colleges and Universities and holds professional accreditations for its programs—including AACSB for the Telfer School of Management, CAC/CAAM‑HP for health‑related faculties, and accreditation by the Law Society of Ontario for its Faculty of Law—while international students comprise roughly 20 percent of the total enrolment, amounting to about 9,000 students from over 120 countries, reflecting its bilingual (English‑French) environment and strong global appeal.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [uOttawa achieves best-ever results in QS World University Rankings 2025 \| About us](https://www.uottawa.ca/about-us/news-all/uottawa-achieves-best-ever-results-qs-world-university-rankings-2025) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Powerful research propels the University of Ottawa higher in prestigious international ranking \| University of Ottawa](https://www.uottawa.ca/en/news-all/powerful-research-propels-university-ottawa-higher-prestigious-international-ranking) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Powerful research propels the University of Ottawa higher in prestigious international ranking \| About us](https://www.uottawa.ca/about-us/news-all/powerful-research-propels-university-ottawa-higher-prestigious-international-ranking) (Cao, score 0.99)

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students admitted to the University of Ottawa must include a provincial attestation letter (PAL) with their study‑permit application unless they are applying for a master’s or PhD program submitted on or after 1 January 2026, when the PAL requirement is being phased out; the PAL is issued by the Ontario government once the admission deposit is received and confirms the student’s spot in the province. To obtain the study permit, the applicant must prove they have enough money to cover the first year’s tuition, living expenses for themselves (and any accompanying family members), and round‑trip transportation, which can be shown through recent bank statements (at least four months), a Guaranteed Investment Certificate, a Canadian‑bank account balance, a student loan letter, a bank draft, or a letter from a sponsor, and the funds must be readily available without needing to work in Canada. While studying, a holder of a valid study permit may work off‑campus for up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled breaks, provided they have applied for a Social Insurance Number and comply with any conditions on their permit. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of Ottawa may apply for a Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) as long as they have completed an eligible full‑time program of at least eight months that was listed on the university’s official program page, and they submit the application within 180 days of receiving their official completion letter while their study permit is still valid; the permit’s length mirrors the official program duration – if the program was between eight months and two years the PGWP is issued for the same period, if it was two years or longer the permit can be up to three years, and any master’s program that is at least eight months (or 900 hours for Quebec credentials) also qualifies for a three‑year PGWP regardless of its actual length, while graduate certificates and diplomas do not receive the three‑year benefit; graduates may also combine the durations of two eligible programs provided the second program is completed within two years of the first, the passport must be valid for the entire requested period, language test results must be current (generally within two years), and no job offer is required to obtain the open work permit.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: International students at the University of Ottawa can participate in paid co‑op placements, internships and other work‑integrated learning experiences through the university’s co‑op program, which alternates study terms with professional work terms and requires a separate co‑op work permit in addition to the regular study permit; placements are typically with private‑sector employers or directly with the university and may also be arranged abroad when travel is permitted, while the Faculty of Social Sciences offers additional international internship opportunities with NGOs in Africa, Asia and Latin America, and the university reports that graduates who complete these co‑op or internship experiences enjoy very strong employment outcomes, with roughly nine out of ten graduates securing a job in their field within six months of graduation and a broad range of career pathways across both the public and private sectors. Lương & thị trường: In the Ottawa labour market recent university graduates can expect median annual earnings roughly between $45 000 and $70 000, with higher salaries in professional creative and technical roles such as producers, directors and choreographers ($38 000–$60 000), library, archive and museum managers ($47 000–$84 000) and musicians and singers ($38 000–$77 000); the strongest overall demand is for occupations that consistently show the highest vacancy rates—construction trades, health‑care professions and accommodation and food‑service jobs—while among graduate‑level positions international graduates are most commonly employed as web designers and developers (about 21 % of those employed) and as mathematicians, statisticians, actuaries and data scientists (around 12 %); compared with Canadian‑trained peers, international graduates have slightly lower employment rates three years after graduation and are more likely to work in sales, service and natural‑and‑applied‑science roles rather than in education, law or government, indicating that focusing on in‑demand sectors such as skilled trades, health‑human resources, hospitality, child‑care and broader STEM fields will improve both employment prospects and earnings for University of Ottawa international graduates.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Ottawa’s International Student Services office coordinates a dedicated orientation program for newly admitted international students that includes an online “How to Prepare for University” session for families, a late‑August to early‑September in‑person orientation workshop on campus, and a virtual orientation for incoming exchange students, while also assisting with housing by offering information on on‑campus residences (including a move‑in period in late August) and a specialized off‑campus housing team that can be contacted via email for advice on neighborhoods such as Sandy Hill and Centretown; additionally, the university’s Career Centre provides international students with career‑development resources, including workshops, résumé and interview coaching, and access to co‑op and work‑study opportunities that support employment on campus and post‑graduation pathways. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Ottawa offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with more than 200 student‑run clubs and societies that cover everything from cultural groups to hobby and professional organizations; Vietnamese students can join the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA), a bilingual community that organizes cultural celebrations such as Tết, language‑exchange meet‑ups, mentorship programs and trips to explore Ottawa’s multicultural neighborhoods, providing a supportive network for newcomers. The university’s International Student Services office runs a dedicated orientation week, ongoing advising, and a “Buddy” program that pairs new arrivals with experienced students, while the Campus Life department ensures safety through 24‑hour campus security patrols, an emergency alert system, and a range of immediate‑support services—including a bilingual 24/7 crisis text line (CONNECT/ PARLER), a 24‑hour suicide helpline (988), and access to the Mindstride Student Health Program for both physical and mental health. In addition, the university’s health‑wellness centre offers free counselling, wellness workshops, and a network of international‑focused resources such as the Empower Me service for students travelling abroad, ensuring that Vietnamese and other international students have comprehensive safety, health, and community support throughout their studies.
