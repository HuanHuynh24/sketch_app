**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- SIM Global Education accepts international applicants who hold GCE “A’’‑Level qualifications, the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma, any equivalent pre‑university certification, a Singapore polytechnic diploma or the SIM Management Foundation Studies (MFS) programme; candidates whose credentials do not meet the academic threshold are usually directed to the six‑month MFS bridge course. English proficiency must be demonstrated with at least an IELTS overall band of 5.5 (no lower band requirement), or a TOEFL iBT score of 59 (or the equivalent Pearson/other test), and applicants who fall short must enrol in the Certificate in English Language (CEL) before starting the diploma. Admission documents include a certified transcript and certificate of the qualifying qualification (e.g., A‑Level results, IB scores, polytechnic results) together with a passport‑size photo and identification (passport for internationals). There is no portfolio or interview requirement for the Diploma in International Business. Once admitted, students must achieve an overall minimum GPA of 1.0 (grade D) in each module to pass and must maintain at least 90 % attendance each month (as required by the Singapore Immigration Checkpoints Authority); graduation is awarded after successfully completing all 15 modules.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- SIM Global Education requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency either through an acceptable test score or an equivalent qualification; for diploma‑level programmes an overall IELTS score of at least 5.5 (no minimum band per component) or a TOEFL iBT score of 59 (or a minimum of 4.0 on the TOEFL iBT scale) is sufficient, while for the University of Wollongong (UOW) bachelor’s pathways the bar is higher at IELTS 6.0 with no band below 6.0, TOEFL iBT 70 (minimum 18 in Reading & Writing and 17 in Speaking & Listening), or Pearson PTE Academic 54 overall (minimum 54 in Reading & Writing and 47 in Speaking & Listening); all test results must be obtained within two years of enrolment. Applicants who hold a GCE ‘O’‑Level credit pass (Grade 1‑6) in English as a first language, or any other recognized English qualification, are exempt from the test requirement, and those who do not meet the thresholds can enrol in SIM’s Certificate in English Language (CEL) programme after an English placement test; Duolingo scores are not listed as an accepted option.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students admitted to SIM Global Education must obtain a Student’s Pass by demonstrating acceptance into a full‑time course and providing proof of sufficient financial resources—typically evidence that they can cover tuition and living expenses of at least S$2,500 per month (or the equivalent annual amount) through bank statements, scholarship letters, loan documents or a sponsor’s guarantee. Once the Student’s Pass is issued, the holder may work in Singapore on a part‑time basis for up to 16 hours per week during academic terms and full‑time during school holidays, but any employment requires a Letter of Consent (LOC) from the Ministry of Manpower, which the employer applies for on the student’s behalf. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates in Singapore, including those from SIM Global Education, may remain after their Student Pass expires by applying for a Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) or LTVP+ through the ICA e‑Service before the Student Pass is cancelled; the pass is intended for graduates who need extra time to secure employment or arrange next steps and is normally granted for up to one year, after which it must be renewed or converted to a work pass. Applicants are required to upload a graduation letter, the Student Pass cancellation letter, a passport biodata page and evidence of sufficient financial support. While on an LTVP the holder is not permitted to work, employment can commence once the employer obtains a Letter of Consent (LOC) or a Pre‑approved Letter of Consent (PLOC) on the holder’s behalf, allowing the graduate to convert the LTVP into an Employment Pass, S Pass or other appropriate work pass.

Lương & thị trường: In 2025 the median starting salary for fresh university graduates in Singapore was about S$4,500 per month, with hourly earnings for graduates around S$27–S$28 (versus S$13–S$15 for non‑graduates), and more than 40 % of all job openings are entry‑level professional, managerial, executive and technical (PMET) roles that are especially sought after by international graduates such as those from SIM Global Education; roughly 58 % of graduate jobs are in these professional, managerial, executive and technician occupations, followed by about 24 % in clerical, sales and service positions and 16 % in production, transport, cleaning and labour roles, with many graduates also eligible for government‑sponsored Graduate Industry Traineeships to facilitate their entry into the labour market.
