### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students who wish to study at the Singapore Institute of Technology must apply online through the SIT Admissions Portal; the current online application window for the 2026 intake runs from 8 January to 19 March 2026, after which the portal closes and no further submissions are accepted for that year. A non‑refundable application fee (typically SGD 30) is required and must be paid before the final submission of supporting documents such as academic transcripts, passport copy, English‑language test results and any programme‑specific requirements. Once the fee is paid and the application submitted, SIT’s admissions office issues a SOLAR number that the applicant uses to apply for a Student Pass; the overall process therefore consists of (1) creating an account on the portal, (2) completing the personal, academic and programme sections, (3) uploading the required documents, (4) paying the application fee, (5) submitting the application, and (6) receiving the SOLAR number for the student‑pass application. SIT admits students in three regular intakes each year – the January (Semester 1) intake, the May (Semester 2) intake and the September (Semester 3) intake – and applicants must ensure their chosen intake’s deadline is met, with the January intake typically following the January‑March application window described above.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Singapore Institute of Technology admits international students who have completed at least 12 years of formal schooling and holds an A‑Level, International Baccalaureate (IB) or equivalent qualification; A‑Level applicants must obtain passes in a minimum of two H2‑level subjects together with General Paper (GP) or Knowledge & Inquiry (KI) in the same sitting, while IB candidates need at least a grade 5 in two Higher Level subjects and one Standard Level subject (generally a total IB score of around 30 points) and must also meet the English language requirement (e.g., IELTS 6.0 or TOEFL iBT 80). Admissions are assessed holistically, taking into account the academic transcript (GPA or grades), any additional test scores (SAT, AP, etc.), relevant work experience, personal statements and, for shortlisted candidates, a mandatory interview; programmes in design, communication, architecture and related fields additionally require a portfolio, and health‑related courses use Multiple Mini‑Interviews (MMI) as part of the selection process.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Singapore Institute of Technology requires international applicants whose secondary‑school instruction was not in English to submit an approved English‑language test result; the standard minimum scores are IELTS Academic 6.5 overall (with no component lower than 6.0) or TOEFL iBT 90 overall, and for programmes with higher language demands the threshold rises to IELTS 8.0 (minimum 7.5 in each band) or TOEFL 110. Cambridge O‑Level English at grade C6, Cambridge English C1 Advanced or C2 Proficiency are also accepted. Applicants who have completed a qualification taught entirely in English (e.g., an English‑medium high‑school diploma) are exempt from submitting a test score. While SIT’s published criteria list IELTS, TOEFL, Cambridge exams and O‑Level English, it does not currently cite PTE Academic or Duolingo English Test as accepted alternatives, so a waiver or admission cannot be based on those scores.

**4. Học phí**

- The Singapore Institute of Technology charges international students a tuition fee of S$96,879 per year (≈ S$387,516 for a typical four‑year undergraduate programme), with living expenses of about S$10,500 per year, bringing the total annual cost to roughly S$107,379; the Ministry of Education (MOE) Tuition Grant, which subsidises 40‑60 % of tuition, lowers the yearly tuition to approximately S$40,000‑S$55,000 depending on the course, resulting in a total fee after grant of around S$50,000‑S$65,000 including living costs; the application fee for SIT is S$30, and other mandatory fees include a Student Pass issuance fee of S$90 plus typical one‑off expenses such as health insurance, laptop and flight costs.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students seeking admission to the Singapore Institute of Technology must satisfy both academic and language prerequisites, which include a minimum overall IELTS score of 7.5 (with no band lower than 7.5) or an equivalent B4 score, and qualification standards such as at least two H2 passes and General Paper or Knowledge & Inquiry at GCE ‘A’ Level, a minimum of five HL grades and the IB Diploma for International Baccalaureate applicants, or the completion of the Polytechnic Foundation Programme with a grade A in English modules; once admitted, students follow a trimester‑based curriculum that typically spans three years and comprises around 120 modular credit units, beginning with foundational and core engineering or science modules in Year 1, progressing to specialised and industry‑relevant courses in Year 2, and culminating in advanced specialisation modules, a capstone project and an eight‑month Integrated Work‑Study Programme (IWSP) in Year 3; the programme’s learning outcomes are designed to equip graduates with applied technical knowledge, problem‑solving and analytical skills, professional communication abilities, and the capacity to undertake and lead industry projects, thereby preparing them for roles such as data analyst, software engineer, pharmaceutical engineer or other specialised positions within the Singaporean and global workforce.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT'S PASS, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT) is an autonomous university in Singapore whose overall position does not appear in the main QS World University Rankings top 1000 but is listed in the QS Asian University Rankings where it was placed in the 151‑200 band in 2023, and it achieved a notable subject‑specific result of 20th worldwide for Chemistry in the 2015 QS World University Rankings by Subject with its engineering programmes also ranking within the top 150 globally for engineering technology in recent subject tables; the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings does not currently include SIT in its overall list, though the university is featured in the THE Impact Rankings, scoring in the 401‑600 range for Sustainable Development Goal 4 (Quality Education) in 2023, and all SIT programmes are formally accredited by the Singapore Ministry of Education, with professional accreditations such as Engineering Accreditation Board (EAB) for engineering degrees, Infocomm Media Development Authority (IMDA) for computing, Singapore Nursing Board for nursing, and other relevant bodies for applied science programmes, while international students form a significant part of its enrolment and are eligible for tiered tuition grants.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students admitted to the Singapore Institute of Technology must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) by submitting proof that they have sufficient financial resources to support themselves for the duration of their studies; acceptable evidence includes recent bank statements showing a minimum balance of roughly S$1,000 per month, a scholarship or bursary letter, a loan sanction letter, or a guarantee from a sponsor together with the sponsor’s financial documents, and the tuition fee payment receipt or acceptance letter confirming the course fee. Once the STP is issued (now in digital form via the FileSG portal and viewable on the Singpass app), holders are allowed to work up to 16 hours per week during school terms and full‑time during school holidays, provided they obtain a Letter of Consent (LOC) from the Ministry of Manpower and their employer, and the employment must not interfere with their full‑time studies. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of the Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT) may apply for a one‑year, non‑renewable Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) issued by the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority to remain in Singapore to look for employment after completing their degree; the LTVP is valid for up to 12 months from the date of issue, does not automatically confer the right to work, and requires the holder to obtain a Letter of Consent from an employer before commencing work.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT) maintains a very high graduate employment rate, with 95 % of its 2020 graduates securing a job within six months of graduation and an overall employment figure of 91.7 % reported in earlier surveys, including 86 % of economically‑active graduates obtaining full‑time permanent positions within that period. All undergraduate programmes incorporate mandatory industry experience through the Integrated Work‑Study Programme (IWSP) and related internship or co‑op components, giving students up to 18 months of structured on‑the‑job training with partner firms in Singapore and overseas. These placements cover a wide range of sectors such as shipbuilding and rigbuilding, maritime and offshore engineering, oil and gas, defence, renewable energy, construction, building services, information and communications technology, health and social sciences, hospitality, business and design, and many others, enabling graduates to enter roles like design engineer, systems engineer, project engineer, software engineer, quality‑control specialist, analyst, consultant, and management positions across public agencies, statutory boards, multinational corporations, and specialised engineering firms. Lương & thị trường: Graduate employment data for Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT) show that in 2025, 70‑76 % of fresh SIT graduates were in the labour force, with full‑time permanent employment rates ranging from about 72 % for culinary and game‑design programmes to 90 % for engineering and health‑science degrees; median gross monthly salaries varied by discipline, with Bachelor of Engineering graduates in Chemical Engineering earning a median of S$4 259, Electronics and Data Engineering S$4 511, Mechatronics Systems S$4 424, Digital Supply Chain S$4 080 and Digital Communications and Integrated Media S$3 675, while health‑science graduates such as Nursing, Occupational Therapy, Physiotherapy and Speech‑Language Therapy earned median salaries between S$3 850 and S$4 200. The Ministry of Manpower’s latest entry‑level job list, refreshed quarterly, identifies the occupations with the strongest demand for new graduates, highlighting roles such as Sales Manager (≈30 vacancies), Retail Manager (≈20 vacancies) and Logistics/Production Planner (≈20 vacancies) as the most frequently advertised, and notes that graduate unemployment stood at 16.4 % compared with 3 % for all residents, underscoring the importance of targeting these high‑demand occupations for better salary outcomes.

From day one, international students can access the Centre for Career Readiness for extensive career support—including industry‑linked mentorship, résumé and interview workshops, and the ReadyTalent job portal that lists Integrated Work‑Study, industry attachment and full‑time opportunities—by calling +65 6592 8150 or emailing CareerReadiness@SingaporeTech.edu.sg. Đời sống & môi trường: At Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT) the campus life for international students is vibrant and well‑supported, with more than 140 student clubs and interest groups covering sports such as badminton, basketball, dragon‑boat, ultimate frisbee and football, arts and culture including piano, theatre, vocal ensembles and Chinese orchestra, as well as diversity‑and‑inclusion, peer‑support and leadership committees; a Vietnamese Student Association (or similar cultural group) provides a dedicated community where Vietnamese students can share language, food and cultural events, while SIT’s counselling services, campus security measures and clear safety guidelines create a safe and inclusive environment, and the new Punggol campus—set to be fully operational by 2024 and designed for up to 12,000 students—offers expanded spaces for co‑curricular activities that further enhance the student experience.
