## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Princeton University admits first‑year students only for the fall semester and does not offer a spring intake, with admission options limited to Single‑Choice Early Action (non‑binding) and Regular Decision; the Early Action deadline is November 1 (no other early‑action or early‑decision programs are available), while Regular Decision applications are due by January 1, and admitted students must confirm enrollment by May 1; prospective applicants—U.S. and international alike—apply through either the Common Application or the Coalition Application plus Princeton‑specific supplemental questions, submit a graded written paper, high‑school transcript, school‑report and counselor letter, two teacher evaluations, and, if required, TOEFL/IELTS or other English‑proficiency scores, with an optional interview, and the application fee is $75 (fee waivers are available for those who qualify).

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào (GPA, SAT/ACT)**

- Princeton University’s undergraduate admission for international students requires a completed application, a secondary‑school transcript that includes any internal grades, national leaving‑exam results (e.g., IB, A‑Level, national high‑school exams) or predicted scores, and either SAT or ACT scores (the university remains test‑optional but will consider them if submitted); English‑language proficiency must be demonstrated with TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo or PTE unless the student has spent at least three of the four high‑school years in an English‑medium school. The transcript is evaluated in the context of the applicant’s education system and a GPA conversion is not required if the school does not use a GPA scale. Advanced coursework such as AP, IB, or A‑Level subjects is strongly encouraged and can be reported, but there is no separate prerequisite list beyond the usual college‑ready expectations in math, science, humanities, and a foreign language. Princeton does not require a portfolio or an interview for undergraduate admission, and there are no specific subject‑grade minimums; admission decisions are based on the overall academic record, standardized‑test performance (if provided), teacher recommendations, and the applicant’s essays.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Princeton requires all undergraduate applicants whose native language is not English and who have not been educated in an English‑medium secondary school for at least three of the four high‑school years to submit an English‑proficiency test score; accepted tests are the TOEFL, IELTS Academic, Duolingo English Test (DET) and Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic). Applicants who are native English speakers or who have completed three or more years of high‑school instruction in English are exempt from the requirement. Graduate applicants face the same language‑proficiency rule, but the Graduate School lists TOEFL, IELTS Academic and PTE Academic (Duolingo is not mentioned for graduate admission) as the accepted exams, and they must demonstrate sufficient oral proficiency to succeed in coursework, research, presentations and teaching.

**2. Student Visa (F-1) và Chính sách OPT**

- Student Visa (F-1): International students admitted to Princeton University who need an F‑1 visa must receive a Form I‑20, which the university issues after confirming admission and verifying that the student can cover at least the first year’s tuition, fees, and living expenses; acceptable proof of financial support includes recent bank statements, scholarship award letters, sponsor letters, or a financial guarantee from a parent or guardian showing sufficient funds, and the I‑20 must be signed by a Designated School Official before the visa interview. To apply, students complete the online DS‑160, pay the SEVIS I‑901 fee, and schedule an interview at a U.S. embassy or consulate, bringing the signed I‑20, passport, proof of financial ability, and any additional documentation listed on the U.S. Department of State’s student‑visa webpage; after entry, they must keep the I‑20 up to date and can request travel signatures for re‑entry, while any extensions or changes in status are processed through the USCIS website using forms such as I‑539. Sau tốt nghiệp (OPT/STEM OPT): International graduates from Princeton University who are on an F‑1 visa and have earned a bachelor’s, master’s or doctoral degree in a field that appears on the Department of Homeland Security’s STEM Designated Degree Program List are eligible for a 24‑month STEM Optional Practical Training (STEM OPT) extension after completing the standard 12‑month post‑completion OPT. To obtain the extension the student must receive a formal recommendation for STEM OPT from the Designated School Official (DSO) entered into SEVIS, and then file Form I‑765 within the filing window that opens up to 90 days before the current OPT Employment Authorization Document (EAD) expires and no later than 60 days after the DSO’s recommendation is entered. The extension may be granted only once per qualifying degree, and the student may not exceed a total of 150 days of unemployment across the initial OPT period and the STEM extension. Employment must be directly related to the STEM degree, the employer must be enrolled in E‑Verify, and a completed training plan (Form I‑983) must be submitted. Princeton’s accredited STEM programs satisfy the degree‑eligibility requirement, allowing eligible graduates to apply for the full 24‑month post‑study work period.
