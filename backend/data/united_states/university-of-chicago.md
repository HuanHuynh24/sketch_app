**2. Điều kiện đầu vào (GPA, SAT/ACT)**

- International undergraduate applicants to the University of Chicago must submit an online application, a $75 fee, and an official high‑school transcript (học bạ) showing a strong academic record; while there is no fixed minimum GPA, admitted students typically have an un‑weighted GPA around 3.9–4.0 and rank in the top 10 % of their class. Standardized test scores are optional: the SAT or ACT may be submitted but the university does not set minimum scores, and many successful applicants present scores in the 1500‑plus SAT range or 34‑plus ACT range; AP and IB exam results are also optional but are considered for credit and can strengthen the application, with scores of 5 (AP) or 6–7 (IB) viewed favorably. Prospective students whose native language is not English must provide evidence of English proficiency (TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo) unless they have attended a school where English is the language of instruction. No portfolio or interview is required for most programs, though a portfolio may be requested for specific majors such as the arts or architecture and an alumni interview is optional but encouraged for additional insight.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- At the University of Chicago, international undergraduate students follow the same credit‑based curriculum as domestic students, needing to earn at least 42 semester‑units to graduate, with courses typically valued at one unit per credit hour; the core curriculum requires completion of foundational courses in humanities, social sciences, biological sciences, and quantitative reasoning, and each major imposes its own prerequisite sequence— for example, students must finish the calculus sequence MATH 13000 (or MATH 15000) before enrolling in ECON 20000/20010 and may need to have completed MATH 15250 or an equivalent proof‑based mathematics course such as MATH 15910 or MATH 20250 before advancing to higher‑level economics classes; graduate‑level courses may count toward elective credit with prior approval, and all courses include clearly defined learning outcomes that emphasize analytical rigor, evidence‑based reasoning, interdisciplinary integration, and the ability to conduct original research, while international applicants must also satisfy English‑language proficiency requirements and may receive funding opportunities specifically for international students.

**2. Student Visa (F-1) và Chính sách OPT**

- Student Visa (F-1): To study at the University of Chicago on an F‑1 visa you must first be admitted and have the school issue a Form I‑20, which lists your program, dates, and the estimated financial requirement for the first year—including tuition, fees, and living costs—typically between $70,000 and $80,000 depending on the program; you must demonstrate the ability to meet this amount through bank statements, scholarship award letters, a sponsor’s affidavit of support, or a combination of these documents, and the university’s International Student Services office will verify the proof before releasing the I‑20. After receiving the I‑20 you pay the SEVIS I‑901 fee, complete the online DS‑160 visa application, schedule a visa interview at a U.S. embassy or consortin​t, and bring to the interview your passport, DS‑160 confirmation page, visa‑application fee receipt, SEVIS fee receipt, the signed I‑20, and the original financial‑support documentation; once the F‑1 visa is issued you may travel to the U.S. up to 30 days before the program start date shown on the I‑20, and after arrival you must keep the I‑20 updated, maintain full‑time enrollment, and if you need to extend your stay beyond the program end date you file Form I‑539 with USCIS for a program extension. Sau tốt nghiệp (OPT/STEM OPT): International graduates of the University of Chicago on an F‑1 visa who have already received a standard 12‑month post‑completion Optional Practical Training (OPT) may apply for the STEM OPT extension, which adds up to 24 additional months of employment authorization for a total possible period of 36 months; eligibility requires that the degree earned be classified in a DHS‑approved Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics (STEM) CIP code, that the student have completed at least one full academic year of study, be currently in a valid post‑completion OPT period, and have a bona‑fide job offer from an employer enrolled in the E‑Verify program for at least 20 hours per week; the student must submit a completed Form I‑983 training plan signed by the employer, receive a new I‑20 recommendation from the university’s Office of International Affairs, and file the I‑765 extension application with USCIS no earlier than 90 days before and no later than the expiration date of the current OPT EAD, while adhering to reporting requirements (including a six‑month validation report and a 120‑day cumulative unemployment limit for the entire OPT period).
