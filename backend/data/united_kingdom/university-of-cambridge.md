### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply (UCAS)**

- The University of Cambridge admits undergraduate international students for three eight‑week terms—Michaelmas (autumn, early October to early December), Lent (spring, late January to late March) and Easter (summer, late April to mid‑June)—but all applicants for the standard undergraduate programme must apply through UCAS, which opens in early September for the following academic year; the UCAS deadline for both UK and overseas candidates is 15 October at 18:00 UK time (an absolute cut‑off), after which a confirmation email provides login details for the supplementary My Cambridge Application where a further £60 application fee must be paid, the required academic documents (e.g., transcript) uploaded, and additional personal information supplied before the final submission; the UCAS application itself carries a £34.50 fee for the 2026 entry cycle, and the overall process therefore consists of: create a UCAS account, select up to five courses, complete the personal statement and obtain a reference, submit by the 15 October deadline, receive the My Cambridge login, finish the college‑specific questionnaire, upload supporting evidence, pay the £60 fee, and submit the completed application.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to the University of Cambridge must apply through UCAS and meet the same academic standards as UK students, which are measured primarily by subject‑specific qualifications rather than a cumulative GPA, although a very strong overall school performance (high GPA or equivalent “học bạ”) is expected and referees are asked to comment on the context of any grades. For most courses the typical A‑Level requirement is three A‑levels with grades of A*AA (or AAA for some subjects) in subjects that match the course‑specific “môn yêu cầu” – for example Physics and Mathematics for Engineering, or Chemistry and one of Biology, Mathematics or Physics for Medicine; the International Baccalaureate is accepted with a total score of at least 40 points, including 776 at Higher Level in the relevant subjects. Candidates whose main qualification is not A‑Levels or the full IB must provide an official high‑school transcript (“học bạ”) covering the last two years, listing subjects taken and grades achieved, and may need to supplement it with SAT/ACT scores, AP results or national exams such as the Gaokao if they meet the additional achievement thresholds set by the colleges. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- Cambridge requires most international applicants whose first language is not English to meet a high‑level English proficiency condition, typically an overall IELTS Academic score of 7.5 with no element below 7.0, or a TOEFL iBT score of 110 overall with at least 25 in each component (scores must be from within the previous two years); the university also accepts other qualifications such as the Cambridge English Advanced (CAE) or Proficiency (CPE) and may consider additional tests listed on its entry‑requirements page, although PTE Academic and Duolingo English Test are not routinely cited as standard options. Applicants from a “majority English‑speaking country” as defined by UK Visas and Immigration are exempt from any English‑language test, and in those cases Cambridge issues the Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) without requiring IELTS, TOEFL, PTE or Duolingo evidence.

**4. Học phí**

- For international (overseas) students at the University of Cambridge the annual tuition fee for most undergraduate courses in the 2026‑27 academic year is £33 000, with higher rates for Medicine (approximately £65 000 per year) and some specialised programmes; postgraduate taught programmes typically charge between £30 000 and £38 000 per year depending on the subject. In addition to tuition, students must pay college fees, which for overseas undergraduates range from about £9 000 to £12 000 per year and are similar for most postgraduate taught students. The UK Student visa application fee is £558, and the Immigration Health Surcharge is £776 for each year of the visa (or £388 if the period is six months or less). The standard UCAS application fee is £27, and Cambridge does not charge a separate university‑specific application charge, though a deposit of around £3 000 is usually required once an offer is accepted. Consequently, a typical overseas undergraduate student can expect a total annual cost of roughly £42 000‑£45 000 (tuition plus college fees) plus the one‑off visa and health surcharge of £1 334 and the modest application and deposit fees.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Cambridge is ranked 6th globally in the 2026 QS World University Rankings (and held the #2 spot in the QS 2024 table) and is placed around 4th in the 2026 Times Higher Education World University Rankings, while its subject performance is exceptional – 43 Cambridge programmes are ranked in the global top 10 for the 2024 QS Subject Rankings, with Archaeology and Modern Languages taking the #1 position; the university is fully accredited under its Royal Charter and recognised by the UK Office for Students, and its degrees are awarded by the University of Cambridge itself, and it consistently draws a high proportion of overseas students, with roughly 20 percent of its total enrolment coming from abroad, making it one of the most internationally‑focused institutions in the UK.

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: To obtain a UK Student visa as an international student at the University of Cambridge you must prove you have enough money to cover both your course fees and living costs, with the financial evidence held in an accessible account for at least 28 consecutive days and the end of that period falling within the 31 days before you submit your online application; acceptable sources are your own funds, those of a parent or legal guardian, a partner (if they are in the UK or applying at the same time), a government‑backed or regulated student loan, or an official financial sponsorship from the university, a national government, an international organisation or an international company, which must be confirmed by a letter on official headed paper stating the sponsor’s name, address, the date, the amount and the period the funds will be available, and note that up to £1,529 of college accommodation fees can be recorded on your CAS and deducted from the required living‑cost amount, while funding from local authorities or state‑owned entities is not considered official sponsorship. Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): The UK Graduate visa (formerly the Post‑Study‑Work route) allows any international student who has successfully completed a degree at a UK higher‑education provider—such as the University of Cambridge—to remain in the UK to work or be self‑employed without a sponsor, provided they are still in the UK on a valid Student (or Tier 4) visa at the time of application and apply online via the government website; eligibility requires that the course lasted at least 12 months (or the full course length if shorter) and that the applicant’s most recent permission was as a Student, with the visa granted for two years for undergraduate and Master’s graduates (reduced to 18 months for those applying on or after 1 January 2027) and three years for PhD graduates, a £937 application fee, an immigration health surcharge of £2,070 for two‑year stays (or £3,105 for three‑year stays), and the ability to bring eligible dependants, while travel abroad is not allowed until a decision is made and the route does not lead directly to settlement but can count toward the ten‑year residence requirement for indefinite leave to remain.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Cambridge’s Careers Service offers a wide range of internships, summer placements and co‑op‑style work experiences to all students, including international students, drawing on the university’s links with more than 5,000 knowledge‑intensive companies in the Cambridge tech cluster and beyond, and the most recent Graduate Outcomes data (covering graduates up to 2022/23) shows that 89 % of Cambridge graduates are in employment or further study 15 months after graduation, with 91 % of those employed in high‑skilled roles across sectors such as finance, consulting, technology, research and development, and education. Lương & thị trường: In the UK labour market the median starting salary for university graduates is about £35,000, with the highest entry‑level pay in investment banking (around £60,000), law (about £56,000) and management consulting (roughly £50,000); Cambridge graduates, especially those completing the MPhil in Management, typically enter consulting, finance, technology and corporate roles where base salaries commonly range from £50,000 to £70,000, and recent Office for National Statistics data show that 49 % of recent graduates are employed in degree‑level occupations, reflecting strong demand for graduate skills and continued employer interest in international graduates for visa‑sponsored positions across consulting, financial services, technology and data analytics, engineering and industrial sectors.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Cambridge’s International Student Support office provides a comprehensive suite of services for overseas students, including a dedicated visa‑advice service that ensures immigration compliance, pre‑arrival and orientation resources that cover travelling to Cambridge, opening bank accounts, finding accommodation and other practical aspects of living in the UK, and programmes to develop academic English through the Language Centre’s ADTIS offering; in parallel, the university’s Careers Service delivers specialised support for international students with one‑to‑one appointments, workshops, employer presentations and recruitment events, access to job listings that include Tier‑2 visa‑sponsoring positions, alumni‑networking opportunities via the Cambridge Alumni Relations Office and online platforms such as Handshake and LinkedIn, and up‑to‑date guidance on UK work‑visa regulations to help students plan and launch careers in the UK or abroad. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Cambridge offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with each of its 31 colleges running a wide range of student‑run societies, sports clubs and cultural groups that welcome newcomers; the Cambridge University Students’ Union and the university’s International Student Team provide dedicated support, advice and social events, while the Vietnamese Society of Cambridge (VietCam) creates a community for Vietnamese students through language‑exchange evenings, cultural celebrations such as Tết festivals and networking sessions that help them connect with peers and navigate university life. Safety is a priority across the university: students are advised to keep passports and visas secure, avoid carrying large sums of cash and use bank cards for payments, and can call 999 in emergencies; all sports clubs must appoint trained Safety Officers who complete mandatory courses in concussion awareness, risk assessment and general safety, and clubs are required to follow the university’s Safety@Sport guidelines to ensure safe activities, including the popular “Bumps” rowing races on the River Cam each June.
