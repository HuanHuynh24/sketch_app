### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Ara Institute of Canterbury offers two main international intakes: Semester 1 (courses run 12 January – 10 April) with an application deadline of 14 December 2025 (nomination 1 October, application 31 October) and Semester 2 (courses run 13 July – 18 September) with an application deadline of 31 May 2026 (nomination 1 March, application 31 March); applications can be submitted online (preferred for faster processing) or by downloading the PDF form and emailing it to intladmissions@ara.ac.nz, and a non‑refundable NZD 150 application fee is required; the applicant must provide a completed international application form, certified English translations of all qualifications, proof of English proficiency (IELTS, PTE or equivalent), and any programme‑specific documents, after which the International Admissions Team reviews the file, issues an unconditional receipted Offer of Place (valid for a limited “please respond” period), and the student can then apply for a New Zealand student visa.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students wishing to study at Ara Institute of Canterbury must demonstrate that they have completed the New Zealand secondary school equivalents of Year 12 (NCEA Level 2) with at least 55 credits across four subjects and, for most diploma‑ and degree‑level programmes, the Year 13 (NCEA Level 3) standard of 42 credits across four subjects (University Entrance) or the overseas equivalent; overseas qualifications are assessed against these benchmarks (e.g., a Vietnamese Upper‑Secondary Graduation Certificate with an overall GPA of 7 in at least four relevant subjects, a Bangladeshi Higher Secondary Certificate with a GPA of 4.0, an Australian ATAR ≥ 74, or any recognised one‑year tertiary study). Certain creative and performance courses (e.g., Music Performance, Music Production, Visual Arts) also require a portfolio and/or interview as part of the admission assessment. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- Ara Institute of Canterbury requires international students whose first language is not English to demonstrate a minimum IELTS score that varies by programme level: 4.5 overall for entry to short‑term English language courses, 5.0 overall (minimum 4.5 in each band) for Level 3 General entry, 5.5 overall (minimum 5.0 in each band) for Level 3 Applied, 6.0 overall (minimum 5.5 in each band) for Level 4 Academic, and 6.5 overall for most postgraduate or linguistically demanding programmes; the institute also accepts equivalent scores on other recognised tests—typically TOEFL iBT 61, PTE Academic 50, or Duolingo English Test 105—but these exact equivalents are not listed in the prospectus. English‑language waivers are granted when applicants have completed New Zealand Certificate in English (NZCEL) Level 4 (Academic) or higher, hold NCEA Level 3 with University Entrance, or have completed previous tertiary study conducted entirely in English; in such cases the institute may forgo the test requirement provided the applicant can supply official proof of the qualifying qualification.

**4. Học phí**

- Ara Institute of Canterbury charges international students an annual tuition fee of roughly NZ $8,320 – $8,503 for its English‑language programmes (the most recent 2026 fee schedule lists $8,503 per year, while the 2025 prospectus cites $8,320). In addition to tuition, a standard application/administration charge of NZ $250 applies, and students must pay a compulsory Student Services Levy of NZ $370.50 per year. Other obligatory costs include exam fees such as IELTS or Pearson at about NZ $410 each, a medical‑and‑travel insurance premium of NZ $925 for a full‑year policy, a homestay placement fee of NZ $300 (over‑18s) or NZ $280 (under‑18s) plus weekly homestay rates of roughly NZ $370‑$400, and a residential accommodation fee of approximately NZ $10,800 for a 48‑week contract (≈ $225 per week). When these mandatory fees are added to tuition, the total annual cost for an international student typically ranges from NZ $10,000 to NZ $11,000, exclusive of personal living expenses.

- International students at Ara Institute of Canterbury should plan on budgeting roughly NZD 20,000 per year for all living expenses, which breaks down to about NZD 10,800 for on‑campus accommodation at Ōtautahi House (a 48‑week contract that includes power and Wi‑Fi and averages around NZD 225 per week), NZD 4,500–5,000 for food and groceries, NZD 900–1,200 for public transport and occasional travel, and NZD 500–600 for the compulsory overseas student health cover; together with an additional NZD 1,000 for textbooks, equipment and miscellaneous items, the total annual cost comes to approximately NZD 20,000, although students who share off‑campus flats can reduce accommodation costs to around NZD 6,000–8,000, which would lower the overall budget proportionally.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students enrolling at Ara Institute of Canterbury must satisfy an English language proficiency benchmark—typically IELTS 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5, or an equivalent qualification such as TOEFL iBT 79‑90—plus demonstrate a recognised prior qualification at the appropriate level, most commonly NCEA Level 3 with University Entrance or an overseas diploma that maps to New Zealand Level 4‑7 standards; the institute assigns credit points on the NZQF scale, with each 100‑hour study block worth 10 credits, so a Level 7 Bachelor’s degree normally requires 360 credits (about 120 credits per year), a Level 5 Diploma 240 credits, and a Level 4 Certificate 120 credits, while individual courses are structured as a mix of core and elective modules delivered through classroom tutorials, online discussions and, where applicable, work‑integrated learning projects or industry placements, offering both full‑time (e.g., three‑year pathways for Bachelor programmes) and part‑time options; the stated learning outcomes focus on developing critical technical and analytical abilities, problem‑solving and independent work skills, professional communication, ethical and legal understanding, and industry‑ready competencies that enable graduates to perform effectively in their chosen operational roles.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Ara Institute of Canterbury (formerly CPIT) is a New Zealand Institute of Technology and therefore does not appear in the QS World University Rankings or the Times Higher Education (THE) university tables, which list only universities; consequently it has no overall QS or THE global ranking, nor subject‑specific rankings in those systems. Nonetheless, the institute’s programmes are accredited by the relevant New Zealand professional bodies and meet the standards of the New Zealand Qualifications Framework (NZQF). For example, its engineering diplomas are accredited by Engineering New Zealand, nursing and midwifery courses are approved by the New Zealand Nursing Council, teaching qualifications are recognised by the New Zealand Teaching Council, and health‑related programmes such as medical imaging and laboratory technology hold accreditation from the appropriate health‑sector agencies. These accreditations assure that international students studying at Ara receive qualifications that are nationally recognised and meet professional standards in their respective fields.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: A fee‑paying student visa for an international student at Ara Institute of Canterbury requires an official Offer of Place from Ara, evidence that the tuition fees have been paid or that sufficient funds are available to cover them, and proof of enough money to live in New Zealand – typically a bank statement showing at least NZD 20,000 per year (about NZD 1,667 per month for shorter courses) or a sponsor’s Financial Undertaking (INZ 1014) if a guarantor is used; the visa is applied for online, costs from NZD 850, and can be granted for up to four years, allowing the holder to study full‑time and work up to 20 hours per week during scheduled teaching periods and full‑time during recognised school holidays, provided all immigration, health and character requirements are met. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Ara Institute of Canterbury become eligible for a New Zealand Post‑Study Work Visa once they have received an official “Completion of Study” letter from Ara, have completed a qualification that meets Immigration New Zealand’s criteria (generally at least 30 weeks of study), and hold a visa that allowed them to study full‑time; the visa’s length depends on the qualification level—graduates with a Master’s or Doctoral degree (level 9 or 10) can be granted up to three years, while those with qualifications at level 8 or below receive a visa duration that matches the length of their study programme.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Ara Institute of Canterbury provides a range of industry‑linked programmes in fields such as information and communication technology, early‑ childhood education, hospitality, baking, beauty therapy and creative practice, many of which include structured internship, co‑op or placement components that enable international students to gain paid work experience and often lead directly to job offers—as illustrated by a Chinese student who secured employment after an ICT internship. Graduate outcomes data show that in the most recent reporting year (2020) 68.2 percent of all graduates were employed, with 43 percent working in roles directly relevant to their study, 23 percent in unrelated fields and 31 percent still seeking work; when measured in the year after graduation, 87.2 percent of graduates were either employed or in further study, and 79 percent felt the institute had prepared them well for industry‑relevant work. Typical post‑graduation careers for international students therefore span ICT specialist positions, early‑ childhood teaching, hospitality and culinary roles, beauty therapy and salon work, creative industry jobs, and other sector‑specific occupations aligned with the institute’s vocational programmes. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Ara Institute of Canterbury typically start their careers earning between NZD $55,000 and $70,000 in information‑technology roles, $60,000‑$72,000 in engineering, $58,000‑$70,000 in healthcare, $52,000‑$65,000 in education and $50,000‑$60,000 in agriculture and environmental sectors, and labour‑market demand aligns with New Zealand’s skill‑shortage occupations, with the most sought‑after jobs for these graduates being developer programmer, systems analyst, ICT customer‑support officer, software engineer, registered nurse, early‑childhood teacher, accountant, marketing specialist and civil/structural engineer; census data for full‑time employed international degree graduates shows the top ten occupations by count are developer programmer (2.5 % of graduates), systems analyst (2.0 %), chief executive or managing director (1.8 %), management consultant (1.6 %), policy and planning manager (1.6 %), marketing specialist (1.5 %), with lower‑skill roles such as sales assistant, labourer, commercial cleaner and cafe worker together making up about 4.4 % of the cohort, indicating that while a majority secure high‑skill, well‑paid positions in technology, engineering, health and education, a smaller proportion still occupy lower‑skill jobs.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Ara Institute of Canterbury’s International Student Office serves as the central hub for all international student needs, offering a dedicated International Admissions Team (phone +64 3 940 8000, email intladmissions@ara.ac.nz) that manages enrolment and provides ongoing support, including free accommodation advice and assistance finding suitable housing; every international student is required to attend two compulsory orientations—an introductory session covering study life in New Zealand, academic expectations, support services and campus resources, followed by a programme‑specific orientation—to help them settle academically and socially, and the institute’s student services team also delivers career guidance, job‑search workshops and connections with industry partners to assist students in planning and securing employment after graduation. Đời sống & môi trường: Ara Institute of Canterbury provides a vibrant campus life for international students, including a wide range of clubs and student groups such as gaming, hiking, rainbow, Japanese, poetry and faith‑based societies, as well as the option to start new groups with guidance from student advisers; regular events and activities like surfing, rock climbing, volunteering, sports, festivals and communal dining help students build friendships and avoid homesickness, while the institute’s policies ensure that international students—including those from Vietnam, who receive dedicated Vietnamese‑language prospectuses and support through channels like the Ara WeChat account—are protected and supported from arrival through graduation, with clear information on rights, safety procedures and professional staff available to address any concerns.
