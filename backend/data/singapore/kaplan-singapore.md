### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Kaplan Singapore accepts applications from international students for its diploma, foundation, and degree programmes across three main intakes each year—January / February, May / June and September / October—with applications typically required to be submitted 4‑6 weeks before the chosen intake; the non‑refundable application fee is S$523.20 (inclusive of 9 % GST) and must be paid by cash, cheque, NETS, Visa/MasterCard/Amex, Flywire or telegraphic transfer with proof of payment provided, after which the complete application package—passport copy (personal‑detail page), notarised birth‑certificate and family‑card (if applicable), certified copies of diploma/certificates and transcripts (both original language and English translation), a passport‑sized colour photograph on a white background and the signed application form—should be either mailed to Kaplan Higher Education Academy Pte Ltd, 8 Wilkie Road, Level 2, Singapore 228095 or scanned and emailed to apply.sg@kaplan.com to the relevant Country Manager/Programme Consultant for processing.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Kaplan Singapore admits international students who are at least 15 years old and can demonstrate both the academic and English‑language standards required for the specific programme they wish to join; the school uses a tiered TOEFL iBT/IELTS scale (e.g., TOEFL 60 or IELTS 5.0 with all bands ≥ 4.5 for Level 5 entry) and, for most degree‑type courses, expects a minimum secondary‑school GPA of roughly 2.5 (CGPA ≈ C or IB 30 points) together with recognised qualifications such as A‑Levels (typically ABB or higher) or an International Baccalaureate Diploma (minimum 30 points, with at least 4 in higher‑level subjects). Applicants must submit certified copies of their passport, diploma, academic transcripts (học bạ) and proof of English proficiency, and the Admissions Office will verify these documents; for programmes with a creative or design focus a portfolio is required and candidates may be invited to an interview to assess aptitude, while even those who fall short of the formal Minimum Entry Requirements can be considered on a case‑by‑case basis if they can demonstrate suitable experience or potential through an interview or supplementary evidence.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Kaplan Singapore requires international students to demonstrate English proficiency for all proprietary and external degree programmes; accepted tests are IELTS (minimum overall 5.5 with no band below 5.0), TOEFL iBT (minimum 60), PTE Academic (minimum 50) and Duolingo English Test (minimum 105). As an alternative, students can sit the free Kaplan International Tools for English (KITE) test, which is offered at no charge for the first two attempts (a S$60 fee applies for a third attempt) and satisfies the English‑language requirement for Kaplan’s own programmes and the External Degree Programme when a recognised test score is not provided. Waivers of the English‑language condition are granted if the applicant has completed a qualification that was taught wholly in English (e.g., a secondary school diploma or university degree from an English‑medium institution), or if the partner university’s own entry criteria are met and they accept the prior qualification as proof of proficiency; in such cases the student is exempt from submitting IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, Duolingo or KITE results.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students accepted into a full‑time programme at Kaplan Singapore must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) from ICA, which is now issued only in digital form and requires a S$60 issuance fee and an appointment at the ICA Services Centre (or an off‑site enrolment arranged by the school) to submit the documents listed in the In‑Principle Approval (IPA) letter, including proof of sufficient financial resources such as recent bank statements, a scholarship or loan letter, or a financial guarantee showing the ability to cover tuition and living expenses for the duration of the course; once the STP is issued, holders are allowed to work up to 16 hours per week during term time and full‑time during recognised school holidays, provided they obtain a valid work permit (e.g., a Part‑Time Work Pass, Training Employment Pass or other ICA‑approved authorization) and comply with any additional conditions imposed by the school and immigration authorities. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed a degree, diploma or certificate at a recognised Institute of Higher Learning (IHL) in Singapore – including Kaplan Singapore when the programme is accredited as an IHL – may apply to the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority for a non‑renewable Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) that lasts for up to 12 months from the date of issue. The LTVP is intended for job‑seeking after graduation; applicants must hold a valid Student Pass, have already finished their studies, and submit the LTVP application (typically within six months of graduation) together with proof of qualification and passport details. While on the LTVP the holder may remain in Singapore to look for employment, actual work is only permitted after obtaining a Letter of Consent (LOC) or a Pre‑approved LOC (PLOC) from the employer, which the employer must apply for on the holder’s behalf. The pass is non‑renewable, so once the 12‑month period ends the graduate must transition to an Employment Pass, S Pass, Work Permit or another suitable work visa if they secure a job.
