### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Massey University accepts new international students for three main intakes: Semester One begins on 31 October 2025, Semester Two on 1 May 2026 and the Summer School on 30 October 2026, and applicants must submit their admission forms by the international‑student due dates listed for each qualification (for example, the Master of Business Administration deadline for Semester One is 15 December 2025 and for Semester Two is 27 April 2026; other programmes have similar specific cut‑offs). The standard international application fee is NZ$ 150, payable when you submit your online application. The application process consists of four steps: (1) review the international‑student checklist to confirm you meet entry requirements and have all required documents, (2) apply for admission through Massey’s online portal, (3) receive and accept the Offer of Place, and (4) pay the application fee (which generates an International Receipt of Payment for visa purposes) before enrolling in courses.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Admission application due dates - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/admission-and-enrolment/key-admission-and-course-enrolment-dates/admission-application-due-dates/) (Cao, score 0.78)<br>[International students' application checklists - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/admission-and-enrolment/application-checklists-for-international-students/) (Cao, score 0.73)<br>[Steps to apply for admission and course enrolment - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/admission-and-enrolment/steps-to-apply/) (Cao, score 0.73)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants must provide proof of English proficiency (IELTS 6.0 overall with at least 5.5 in each band for undergraduate study, 6.5 overall with at least 6.0 in each band for postgraduate study, or equivalent Cambridge English qualifications) and an academic qualification that satisfies New Zealand University Entrance, most commonly NCEA Level 3 (three approved subjects with a minimum of 14 credits each, plus 10 literacy credits at Level 2 or above and 10 numeracy credits at Level 1 or above); overseas qualifications are accepted if they are recognised as equivalent and transcripts showing grades/GPA must be submitted, with a typical minimum GPA around a C‑grade (≈2.0 on a 4‑point scale). Certain programmes—especially in design, visual arts, architecture, music, and some health‑science fields—may also require a portfolio and/or an interview as part of the entry criteria.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Entry requirements for international students - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/entry-requirements-to-study-at-massey/entry-requirements-for-international-students/) (Cao, score 0.79)<br>[Entry requirements – New Zealand and Australian students - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/entry-requirements-to-study-at-massey/entry-requirements-for-new-zealand-and-australian-students/) (Cao, score 0.75)<br>[Qualifications & courses for international students - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/international-students/qualifications-and-courses-for-international-students/) (Cao, score 0.74)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Massey University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency through IELTS, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic or the Duolingo English Test, with the specific score thresholds varying by study level and programme: for undergraduate study the minimum Duolingo overall score is 105 (with at least 95 in each band), TOEFL iBT a total of 80 points (including a minimum of 19 in Writing), and PTE Academic 50 overall with at least 42 in each communicative skill; postgraduate applicants must achieve at least 120 on Duolingo (minimum 105 per band), 90 on TOEFL iBT (minimum 20 in Writing) and 58 on PTE Academic (minimum 50 in each skill). For IELTS, the typical minimum is an overall band of 6.0 for undergraduate courses (no individual band below 5.5) and 6.5 for postgraduate study. English‑language waivers are granted when applicants have completed a recent qualification that was taught wholly in English, such as a degree from an English‑medium institution, or when they meet specific exemption criteria set by the university.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Entry requirements for international students - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/entry-requirements-to-study-at-massey/entry-requirements-for-international-students/) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[[PDF] International Prospectus 2026 - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/documents/797/International-Prospectus.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International students - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/international-students/) (Cao, score 1.00)

**4. Học phí**

- International students enrolled full‑time at Massey University for a typical 120‑credit year pay tuition of about NZ $32,343 for most bachelor‑level programmes, with higher specialist diplomas ranging from NZ $38,840 (e.g., postgraduate diplomas and PhDs) up to NZ $59,620 for the Veterinary Science diploma, all quoted inclusive of GST; the university charges a one‑off application fee of NZ $150, and students must also meet Immigration New Zealand’s living‑cost requirement of roughly NZ $20,000 per year, plus any minor non‑tuition charges such as a student services levy (approximately NZ $300) and health‑insurance costs, making the total annual outlay for a standard bachelor’s student about NZ $52,500 (tuition + application + required living expenses).
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Tuition fees for international students - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/study/fees-and-funding/tuition-fees-for-international-students/) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[[PDF] International Prospectus 2026 - Massey University](https://www.massey.ac.nz/documents/797/International-Prospectus.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Trường Massey University: Xem khóa học, học phí, học bổng và yêu cầu tuyển sinh](https://www.hotcourses.vn/study/newzealand/school-college-university/massey-university/142316/international.html) (Trung bình, score 1.00)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at Massey University the typical annual out‑of‑tuition budget in New Zealand is roughly NZ $20 000–$25 000, broken down as follows: accommodation in university‑run halls costs about NZ $13 000 – $18 000 for a 40‑week year (self‑catered rooms around NZ $13 000, catered rooms around NZ $18 000), food and groceries add another NZ $4 000–$5 000 if you are self‑catering (the catered option includes meals in the hall fee), transport expenses are usually around NZ $1 000–$1 200 per year (including the recommended NZ $500 cash on arrival and regular public‑transport costs), and compulsory health‑insurance (Studentsafe) is NZ $899 for one academic year. These figures together meet the Immigration New Zealand requirement of at least NZ $20 000 per year for living costs.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Massey University is a public New Zealand university that enrolls roughly 9,000 international students, representing about one‑third to two‑fifths of its total student body, and it holds official accreditation from the New Zealand Qualifications Authority as well as professional accreditations for specific programmes, with its Art and Design courses additionally recognised by QS accreditation and ranked second in New Zealand for that discipline; in the 2026 QS World University Subject Rankings its Veterinary Science programme was placed 14th globally (first in Oceania and Asia) and topped the employer‑reputation metric, while subjects such as Communication and Media Studies, Architecture and Built Environment fell in the 101‑150 band, Accounting and Finance in the 151‑200 band and Sports‑Related Subjects entered the 101‑150 band for the first time, and in the 2025 QS subject rankings Veterinary Science was 19th worldwide, Development Studies ranked first in Aotearoa, and English Language & Literature broke into the top‑250; the university’s overall position in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings is in the 401‑500 band globally for the most recent cycles.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: International students who enrol at Massey University must obtain a Fee‑Paying Student Visa from Immigration New Zealand, which requires proof that they can cover both tuition and living costs; the university provides a Receipt of Payment or a Statement of Account after the tuition fee is cleared and this document, together with evidence of at least NZD 15,000 per year for living expenses, must be submitted to INZ as part of the visa application. Once the visa is granted, students are allowed to work up to 20 hours per week during scheduled study periods and full‑time during official academic breaks, provided they remain enrolled in a full‑time course and continue to meet the visa’s academic progress conditions. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed an eligible qualification at Massey University can apply for a Post‑Study Work Visa (PSWV) under Immigration New Zealand rules; if the qualification is at NZQF level 7 or higher (Bachelor’s, Master’s or PhD) and was studied full‑time for at least 30 weeks, the visa is granted for up to three years with open work rights, while qualifications at levels 4‑6 that appear on INZ’s approved list allow a stay of up to one year (extendable to two years for certain long‑term or regional programmes) with work limited to jobs related to the field of study, and applicants must lodge the visa application within three months of their student visa expiring (PhD graduates have up to six months).

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Massey University delivers strong graduate outcomes for its international students, with a one‑year post‑graduation employment rate of 76.1 percent for Level 7 degree holders under 25 years—well above the New Zealand university average of 71.3 percent and placing the university among the top three in the country; this performance also contributes to Massey’s global QS ranking of 157th for graduate employment outcomes. The university embeds work‑integrated learning across many programmes, offering industry‑linked internships, placements and co‑op options especially in engineering and related technologies (where 90.4 percent of graduates secure employment within a year), health (87.1 percent), and management and commerce (82.6 percent). International students are permitted to work up to 20 hours per week during study and can remain in New Zealand for up to four years after graduation to gain professional experience, enabling them to transition into careers such as engineering, health services, business and finance, agriculture and environmental science, design, information technology and other specialised fields. Lương & thị trường: New Zealand graduate salaries are typically between NZ$55,000 and NZ$70,000 in the first year after graduation, with higher earnings in fields such as information technology, engineering and health‑sciences where starting salaries often exceed NZ$70,000, while graduates in business and the arts tend to earn toward the lower end of the range; the country’s labour market shows strong demand for occupations on the official Green List, most notably software and full‑stack developers, DevOps and cybersecurity specialists, registered nurses, midwives and medical laboratory scientists, civil, structural and electrical engineers, construction project managers and quantity surveyors, as well as physiotherapists and occupational therapists, and these roles are especially open to international graduates from Massey University, whose programmes emphasise practical, industry‑aligned learning that aligns with these high‑need sectors.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Massey University’s International Student Office runs a mandatory orientation week for new overseas students that covers campus life, study skills, visa and insurance matters and introduces the range of support services available; it also assists international students in finding on‑campus accommodation such as Massey Halls in Palmerston North, Auckland and Wellington and offers advice on private housing options, while the university’s Career Centre provides dedicated career services for international students, including résumé workshops, interview preparation, job‑search guidance and connections with employers that take visa requirements into account, and the International Office can be contacted Monday‑Friday 8:30 am‑4:30 pm via email contact@massey.ac.nz, NZ phone 0800 627 739, international phone +64 6 350 5701, text 5222 or web‑chat. Đời sống & môi trường: Massey University offers a vibrant campus life for its 4,000+ international students across its Auckland, Palmerston North and Wellington campuses, with the student association Te Tira Ahu Pae providing a wide range of academic, social, cultural and sporting clubs—including language‑specific groups such as a Vietnamese Students Association or broader Asian cultural societies—so Vietnamese students can connect with peers, celebrate their heritage and find support; safety and wellbeing are prioritised through the mandatory Studentsafe insurance, a dedicated International Student Support team that assists with visas, health and emergency issues, and student advocates who help with housing, financial emergencies and personal concerns, while clubs receive administrative assistance and access to activity grants, and students are encouraged to start new clubs if their interests are not already represented.
