## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Lincoln University admits international students for two main intakes each year – Semester One (February to June) and Semester Two (July to November). For the 2026/2027 cycle, the application deadline for Semester Two 2026 is 30 March 2026 (with a nomination deadline of 15 March 2026), and the deadline for Semester One 2027 is 30 September 2026 (nomination deadline 15 September 2026). Prospective applicants must submit an online application via the university’s portal (mylinc.lincoln.ac.nz), first reviewing the entry requirements for their chosen programme, then uploading a certified copy of their academic transcript, passport, and evidence of English‑language proficiency; an international application fee of NZD 150 is payable at submission. After the online form and supporting documents are received, the university reviews the application, issues an offer letter, and provides enrolment instructions for successful candidates.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International undergraduate applicants to Lincoln University must provide evidence of New Zealand secondary qualifications or an overseas equivalent, typically 58 NCEA credits at Level 2 or higher (including at least 30 credits across three approved subjects plus the University Entry literacy and numeracy requirements) and, for most degree programmes, a minimum of 70 Level‑3 NCEA credits with subjects such as Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, Biology, Geography or Agriculture depending on the chosen field; overseas students can meet these standards with Cambridge International Examinations achieving at least 120 UCAS‑tariff points (or comparable IB scores, A‑Level grades, etc.), and must submit official transcripts and a calculated GPA that meets the university’s 2:1 (or 55 % average) benchmark for entry; English language proficiency is required, normally IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 (or equivalent TOEFL/PEARSON scores); for programmes such as Architecture, Landscape Architecture, Creative Arts and certain design‑oriented courses a portfolio of work and/or an interview is also mandatory, while most other faculties rely solely on academic results and the documented English proficiency.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Lincoln University requires all international applicants whose first language is not English to demonstrate English proficiency, and the most common accepted tests and minimum scores are: IELTS overall 6.5 with no individual band below 6.0; TOEFL iBT total 79 with at least 13 in reading, 13 in listening, 18 in speaking and 20 in writing; PTE Academic overall 58 with no component under 50; and Duolingo English Test minimum 115. New Zealand citizens, permanent residents and applicants who have completed at least two years of full‑time study in an English‑medium institution may be exempt from these test requirements, and they can also opt to sit Lincoln’s own English Language Proficiency (ELP) test, which requires a minimum of 65 % in both writing and reading for undergraduate entry (70 % for postgraduate study).

**4. Học phí**

- Lincoln University charges international undergraduate students about NZ$32,000 per year in tuition, to which the mandatory student services levy of NZ$976, compulsory health‑insurance of NZ$899, health‑services fee of roughly NZ$150, technology fee of about NZ$120, orientation fee of NZ$100 and a graduation cap‑and‑gown fee of NZ$50 are added, bringing the typical total annual cost to approximately NZ$34,295, while the application fee for international applicants is NZ$150.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at Lincoln University the typical yearly living‑cost budget is around NZ $15,000–$20,000, broken down as follows: on‑campus or nearby accommodation usually costs between NZ $150 and $350 per week (≈ NZ $7,800–$18,200 per year); food and groceries average about NZ $70–$100 per week (≈ NZ $3,600–$5,200 per year); public transport or a modest car budget adds roughly NZ $30–$50 per week (≈ NZ $1,560–$2,600 per year); and the mandatory overseas student health insurance (OSHI) is typically NZ $300–$400 per year. Adding these items gives an overall annual cost of roughly NZ $13,260–$26,400, with most students budgeting about NZ $15,000–$20,000 to comfortably cover accommodation, food, transport and health insurance while studying at Lincoln University.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students wishing to study at Lincoln University must hold a secondary qualification equivalent to the New Zealand NCEA Level 3 or an overseas qualification that provides at least 120 credit points (or the equivalent) and meet English‑language requirements, typically IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 (or TOEFL iBT 79). Specific programmes have subject‑specific prerequisites—for example, entry to the Bachelor of Agriculture requires at least 6 credit points in each of Mathematics, Chemistry and Biology, while Business programmes usually ask for 5 credit points in Mathematics and a related commerce subject. The university operates on a two‑semester year, with each semester lasting about 15 weeks; individual courses are valued at 6, 9 or 12 credit points and a full bachelor’s degree comprises 360 credit points, including core modules, electives and a final capstone or research project. The curriculum is designed around integrated learning outcomes that develop students’ ability to apply scientific and analytical techniques to real‑world problems, practice sustainable and ethical decision‑making, communicate findings effectively, and demonstrate professional competence in their chosen field.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: International students admitted to Lincoln University on a fee‑paying student visa must first hold a confirmed Offer of Place or Confirmation of Enrolment and be able to demonstrate they can cover both tuition and living costs, which Immigration New Zealand requires at a minimum of NZD 20,000 per year for living expenses plus proof of tuition payment or sufficient funds to pay it; the financial evidence can be supplied via recent bank statements, a sponsor’s guarantee, or the Funds Transfer Scheme (FTS) offered through ANZ for Indian applicants, and a completed Financial Undertaking (INZ 1014) must be uploaded with the visa application, along with compulsory health insurance, a passport valid for at least three months beyond the intended stay, and any required medical or character certificates; once the visa is granted, fee‑paying students are permitted to work up to 25 hours per week during academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled holidays, provided the work does not interfere with their studies. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from Lincoln University who have completed a New Zealand qualification are eligible for Immigration New Zealand’s Post‑Study Work Visa (PSWV), which grants open work rights for a period linked to the level of the qualification they earned: holders of a Level 9 or Level 10 qualification (Master’s or Doctoral degree) receive a three‑year visa; holders of a Level 7 Bachelor’s degree who studied full‑time for at least 30 weeks in New Zealand receive up to three years; and holders of a Level 7 Graduate Diploma receive a visa for the same length as their study period, up to a maximum of one year (with the new short‑term graduate work visa offering an additional six‑month option for eligible graduates). Applicants must have studied full‑time for the required minimum weeks (typically 24–30 weeks), must not be on a bridging or English‑language course, can only be granted one PSWV in their lifetime, must meet health, character and financial requirements (including at least NZD 5,000 available), and may include partners and dependent children on appropriate visas.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Lincoln University in New Zealand provides dedicated support for international students to secure industry‑based internships, work‑integrated learning and placement opportunities that are embedded in most programmes, particularly in agriculture, horticulture, environmental science, food and wine, and related land‑based fields; this practical experience helps graduates move directly into roles such as agricultural/horticultural consultants or scientists, winemakers, environmental scientists, data analysts, landscape architects, urban/regional planners, production managers, food technologists, orchard managers, outdoor recreation guides, and a range of other specialist positions, and the university reports that 84 % of its graduates attain paid employment shortly after completing their studies. Lương & thị trường: New Zealand graduates enjoy high employability, with 85‑95 % securing a job within 12 months, and most entry‑level salaries for bachelor‑degree holders fall between NZD 50,000 and 70,000, while specialised computing and tech roles reported by Lincoln University can start at NZD 50,000‑70,000 and reach up to NZD 350,000 for senior positions such as IT manager or security analyst; the labour market’s Green List and immigration priorities highlight strong demand for occupations including software engineers, full‑stack and DevOps developers, cybersecurity specialists, data scientists, IT architects, civil, structural and electrical engineers, construction project managers, quantity surveyors, registered nurses, midwives, physiotherapists, and occupational therapists, and among international degree graduates the most common sectors are early‑childhood and school teaching, university lecturing, accounting, and developer/programmer roles, indicating that while many international graduates enter these in‑demand fields, they also frequently fill positions such as early‑childhood teachers, secondary teachers, accountants, and retail or hospitality managers.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Lincoln University’s International Office runs a compulsory orientation program for new overseas students that includes campus tours, social activities and information sessions to help them settle in New Zealand, and international students can contact the International Student Advisors at internationaladvisors@lincoln.ac.nz for support; the university provides on‑campus accommodation options such as halls, flats and houses—with applications handled by the accommodation team (accomm@lincoln.ac.nz, +64 3 423 0525) and limited space encouraging early applications—and the International Student Support team also offers career advice and services to assist students in planning and developing their future careers. Đời sống & môi trường: Lincoln University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with more than 20 clubs run by the Lincoln University Students’ Association (LUSA) covering cultural, sporting and special‑interest activities; the LU International and Cultural Society (LUICS) hosts regular events, trips and gatherings, and a Vietnamese Student Association (or a Vietnamese community within LUICS) meets frequently to share language, culture, food and peer support, often collaborating with other Asian and Pasifika groups. The university provides dedicated international‑student support through its International Office, offering orientation, accommodation assistance and personalised advice to help newcomers settle. Campus safety is a priority: campus security can be reached on 0800 545 388 for non‑life‑threatening incidents, and students are encouraged to use common‑sense precautions such as travelling with friends, avoiding walking alone at night, staying in well‑lit areas, keeping phones or personal alarms handy and informing friends or family of their plans. These measures, combined with a welcoming student community and a range of clubs and cultural societies, create a secure and inclusive environment for Vietnamese and other international students at Lincoln University.
