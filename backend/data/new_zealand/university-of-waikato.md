### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- The University of Waikato accepts international undergraduate and postgraduate applicants for three intakes each year – the March, July and November start dates – and candidates must submit their online application through the MyWaikato portal, paying a NZ $150 non‑refundable application fee and uploading a verified copy of their passport, student‑visa evidence (once granted), official academic transcripts, proof of English‑language proficiency and any other programme‑specific documents; for the March intake applications are normally due by mid‑February, for July by the end of May and for November by mid‑October, after which the university reviews the files, issues an Offer of Place (conditional or unconditional) via email, and the student finalises enrolment by accepting the offer in MyWaikato and paying any required deposit before the visa application deadline.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to undergraduate programmes at the University of Waikato must hold an equivalent university‑entrance qualification; for New Zealand‑based applicants this means an NCEA Level 3 certificate with at least 32 credits across three University‑Entrance‑approved subjects at Level 3 plus 10 numeracy credits at Level 1, and for those coming from overseas the university recognises the same standards through comparable qualifications (e.g., a minimum of 120 points on the CAIE/AICE tariff with no grade below D in at least three subjects, or the Australian Tertiary Admission Rank ≥ 70). All applicants must provide official transcripts and evidence of a satisfactory GPA – typically a minimum of 14–16 points per subject on the NCEA scale (equivalent to roughly a B‑grade) – and meet English‑language proficiency requirements (e.g., IELTS 6.5 overall with no band < 6.0). For Vietnamese candidates the specific requirement is successful completion of the National High School Graduation Examination together with an overall GPA of at least 8.0 / 10.0 in the four best Grade‑12 subjects as shown on the high‑school report (học bạ). **3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Waikto​e must demonstrate English proficiency unless they qualify for a waiver based on prior study in an English‑medium programme or other recognised criteria (e.g., a recent postgraduate qualification taught in English, a strong GRE/GMAT score, or a bachelor’s degree completed in English). For postgraduate programmes the minimum scores are IELTS Academic 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, IBT TOEFL 90 overall with a writing score of 21, PTE Academic 56 overall with no band under 46, and the Duolingo English Test 120 (doctoral programmes excluded). For undergraduate programmes the thresholds are slightly lower: IELTS 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5, IBT TOEFL 80 overall with a writing score of 21, PTE Academic 46 overall with no band under 36, and Duolingo 105; additional pathways such as the New Zealand Certificate in English (Level 4/5) or Language Cert B2 Communicator are also accepted. Applicants who have completed a degree or other qualification taught in English may request an English‑language waiver, which is assessed case‑by‑case and may require a supplementary language assessment at the start of study.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at the University of Waikato pay tuition in New Zealand dollars (NZD) and the standard undergraduate fee for a full‑year (two trimesters) is NZ$28,530 for 2026 (rising to NZ$29,960 for 2027), while many postgraduate and specialist programmes list higher rates—for example International Business NZ$38,252 (2026) or NZ$40,165 (2027), Law NZ$40,165 (2026) or NZ$42,173 (2027), and Materials and Processing NZ$42,231 (2026) or NZ$44,343 (2027); the university also charges a one‑time application fee of roughly NZ$150, and additional compulsory charges such as the Student Services Levy (about NZ$1,110 per year) and GST are included in the quoted tuition amounts.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- The typical annual cost for an international student at the University of Waikato is roughly NZ $25 000–30 000 per year, which includes accommodation (self‑catered student housing at about $300 per week ≈ $15 600 annually), groceries ($70 per week ≈ $3 640), transport (bus fares around $1.10 per ride, assuming two trips per day on weekdays ≈ $570, plus occasional car‑parking at $2 per day ≈ $210), a gym membership ($12.50 per week ≈ $650), compulsory health insurance ($750 per year), and miscellaneous personal expenses (≈ $6 000 for books, supplies, leisure and other incidentals). Choosing fully‑catered accommodation ($400‑$450 per week) raises the total to about NZ $28 000–33 000, while a homestay (NZ $392 per week, food included) brings it to roughly NZ $26 000–31 000.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: A Fee‑Paying Student Visa for an international student at the University of Waikato requires an official Offer of Place from the university and evidence that tuition fees have been paid or that sufficient funds are available to cover them, which can be shown by logging into MyWaikato, selecting the Finance tab and downloading the receipt for the fee payment; additionally, Immigration New Zealand expects proof of living expenses of at least NZ $20,000 per year (often demonstrated through bank statements, a loan letter or scholarship award) and mandatory health insurance for the entire stay, while the visa fee is NZ $375 online; once granted, the visa permits the student to work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full‑time during university holidays, provided the work does not interfere with study, and any internship or work placement must be approved by Immigration New Zealand. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed a New Zealand qualification at NZQF level 7 or higher (such as a bachelor’s, master’s or PhD) at the University of Waikato and studied full‑time for at least 30 weeks are eligible to apply for a Post‑Study Work Visa, which gives open work rights for up to three years—the maximum period being the length of the study programme but not exceeding three years—and the employment must be related to the field of study; the visa can be granted only once, must be applied for within three months of the expiry of the student visa, and a separate short‑term graduate work visa offering up to six months of open work rights is also available for graduates with level 5‑7 qualifications.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Waikato provides a comprehensive work‑integrated learning (WIL) framework for both domestic and international students, with many undergraduate programmes—such as the Bachelor of Engineering with Honours in Software Engineering—including a mandatory 800‑hour internship and other courses offering optional placements, while the MyCareer platform, NZUni Talent job board, regular career expos, and a dedicated International Careers Development Consultant give students structured access to internships, co‑ops and graduate‑level roles across sectors such as information technology, engineering, health, education, business and government; as a result, roughly 85 %–90 % of Waikato graduates secure employment or commence further study within six months of graduation, reflecting strong graduate outcomes for international students as well. Lương & thị trường: New Zealand university graduates, including international students from the University of Waikato, typically earn a median annual salary of about NZ $55 000 (approximately NZ $32 000‑$35 000 when working in their specific field of study) within the first year after graduation, and employment rates exceed 85 % within 12 months; the labour market most strongly demands occupations on the government’s Green List such as software, DevOps and cybersecurity engineers, ICT security analysts, civil, structural and electrical engineers, construction project managers and quantity surveyors, registered nurses, midwives, medical laboratory scientists, physiotherapists and occupational therapists, as well as accountants (CA/CPA qualified), secondary teachers in mathematics and science, aged‑care workers and hospitality managers, all of which offer higher-than‑average wages and clear pathways to residence for international graduates on a post‑study work visa.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Waikto​o’s International Student Services Office, located in the Student Centre, assists incoming students with visa and immigration matters, medical and travel insurance, pastoral care and a range of international orientations and events, and works closely with Accommodation Services to help students arrange homestay, tenancy or private rental and to resolve any housing issues during their study; students under 18 are required to live in homestay or designated caregiver accommodation and receive additional support from International Student Advisers who also keep parents informed of academic and social progress, while the university’s Careers and Employability Service offers free, comprehensive career support—including CV and interview preparation, job‑search guidance and assistance in securing employment while studying—to help international students plan and progress toward their future careers.
