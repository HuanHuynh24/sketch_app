### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students applying to the University of Alberta can choose from three main intakes—Fall (September), Spring (May) and Summer (July). For the Fall 2026 intake the online application opens on October 1, 2025 and the general deadline to submit a completed application for most programmes is March 1, 2026; a later deadline of May 1, 2026 applies for accepting an admission offer, and all outstanding academic documents must be received by August 1, 2026 (with a final transcript deadline of June 15, 2026). The Spring 2026 intake opens in October 2025 as well, with applications typically due by January 15, 2026, and the Summer intake opens in February 2026 with a deadline around April 30, 2026. The non‑refundable application fee for international undergraduate and graduate applicants is CAD $120, and once an offer is received students must pay a tuition deposit (generally CAD $100, or CAD $1,000 for the JD programme) through the university’s Launchpad portal. The application process requires creating a Launchpad account, completing the online form via applyAlberta or the university’s uab.ca/apply site, uploading required transcripts, English‑language test scores and any programme‑specific documents, paying the application fee, and after receiving an offer, confirming acceptance and paying the deposit before the specified acceptance deadline.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to undergraduate programs at the University of Alberta must meet the same academic standards as Canadian applicants – a minimum cumulative high‑school average that translates to roughly a 3.0 / 4.0 GPA (most programs list historical admission averages around 3.0‑3.2, while competitive faculties such as Engineering, Science and Business often require 3.3‑3.5 or higher) and must have completed the prerequisite secondary‑school courses required for their chosen field (e.g., English 4U, Mathematics 4U, and for science‑oriented programs additional 4U courses in Biology, Chemistry or Physics). All applicants must provide official, translated transcripts (học bạ) and satisfy English Language Proficiency through a recognized test (minimum IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, TOEFL iBT 86 total, or Duolingo 115, among other options) by the August 1 document deadline. Certain programmes add extra criteria: applicants to Fine Arts, Architecture, Design, Music, Drama, and related disciplines must submit a portfolio, audition recording or written work; health‑science programmes such as Nursing, Dentistry or Education may require an interview or written statement; and a few specialised degrees (e.g., Medicine, Radiation Therapy, Medical Laboratory Science) are not open to international students at the undergraduate level.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Alberta must demonstrate English language proficiency unless they have studied for at least three consecutive years in an English‑language institution in an ELP‑exempt country or meet other exemption criteria such as completing a recognized English‑language program; otherwise the accepted tests and minimum scores are: IELTS Academic ≥ 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 (IELTS One‑Skill Retake scores are not accepted), TOEFL iBT ≥ 90 total with no section under 21 for tests taken before 21 January 2026 (for tests on or after that date the university uses the 1‑6 scale, requiring at least 4.5 overall and in each band), PTE Academic ≥ 68 (as required by the Alberta School of Business for its MBA), and Duolingo English Test ≥ 120 overall with no integrated subscore under 100 (the MBA program specifically requires 140 with no subscore below 120).

**4. Học phí**

- International students at the University of Alberta pay tuition in Canadian dollars (CAD) and the amount depends on the level and program of study: most undergraduate programs charge roughly CAD 29,000 per year (with professional programs such as engineering or business costing up to CAD 38,000 annually), while full‑time graduate programmes are billed at a flat yearly fee of about CAD 10,018 (for the 2024‑25 academic year) plus a per‑credit charge of CAD 671.84 (standard three‑credit courses cost CAD 2,015.52). All applicants must pay a non‑refundable application fee of CAD 150 (reduced to CAD 100 for current or former U‑Alberta students). Once admitted, new students are required to submit a tuition deposit of CAD 5,000 (students already studying in Canada pay a reduced deposit of CAD 1,000); this deposit is needed to secure the admission offer, obtain the Provincial Attestation Letter for a study permit, and register for courses. In addition to tuition, students incur non‑instructional fees (e.g., student services, health and recreation, and supplies) that are calculated annually and typically add several hundred dollars to the total cost.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at the University of Alberta can expect to spend roughly $14,844 per year on housing (based on a $1,237 monthly rent for a one‑bedroom apartment), about $4,500‑$5,000 on food, $4,164 on utilities, $900‑$2,100 on internet and phone, $646 plus an additional $250 for winter clothing, $1,200‑$2,400 on recreation, $1,521 on miscellaneous expenses, and $0 on transit because the Arc transit card is included in student fees; these items together total approximately $29,775‑$32,675 annually. Adding the typical provincial health‑insurance premium for international students (around $600 per year) brings the overall annual cost of living to roughly $30,400‑$33,300 for an international student at the University of Alberta.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Alberta is consistently ranked among Canada’s top‑four research universities and sits inside the global top‑100, holding the #94 position in the QS World University Rankings and ranking #8 worldwide in the Times Higher Education Impact Rankings for Sustainable Development Goals; its subject strengths are highlighted by QS subject rankings that place its Petroleum Engineering program at #2 globally, Nursing at #9 worldwide (and #2 in Canada), and both Mineral and Mining Engineering and Sports‑Related Subjects within the global top 10, while a total of 21 disciplines are listed among the world’s top 100, and the university’s programs carry national accreditation – engineering programmes are accredited by the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board (CEAB), nursing by the Canadian Association of Schools of Nursing, and business schools by AACSB – all of which contribute to a strong reputation for international students seeking high‑quality, globally recognised education.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: To study at the University of Alberta as an international student you must submit a study‑permit application to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada that includes your official Letter of Acceptance, a valid passport, any required temporary‑resident visa, and a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) issued by the Government of Alberta – a requirement that has been in force since 22 January 2024 and is obtained by the university on your behalf after you have been accepted; proof of financial support must show that you can cover tuition, living expenses for yourself (and any dependents) for the entire duration of your program and can be provided as a recent bank statement or a letter from your bank, a scholarship or award letter, or an official letter confirming a research/teaching assistantship or other external funding, and, once your study permit is approved, you are permitted to work up to 20 hours per week off‑campus during regular academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled breaks, with on‑campus employment available without a separate work permit, provided you remain enrolled full‑time and meet IRCC’s eligibility criteria. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of the University of Alberta are eligible for a Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they completed a program that is at least eight months long at this designated learning institution, maintained full‑time study status throughout (except part‑time may be allowed in the final term), and apply within 180 days of receiving their official program‑completion letter while their study permit was still valid; the permit’s length is tied to the length of the study program – programmes of two years or more may receive a three‑year PGWP, programmes between eight months and less than two years generally receive a work permit for a period equal to the programme’s duration, and, as of the 15 February 2024 update, master’s programmes of at least eight months (or 900 hours in Quebec) are also eligible for a three‑year PGWP even if they are under two years, provided all other eligibility criteria are met and the passport remains valid for the entire period.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Alberta reports that its graduate employment rate remains strong, with 93 % of degree recipients employed two years after graduation in 2020, and a record‑high 96.4 % working in a field related to the general skills and abilities acquired in their degree; the institution treats graduate employment as a key outcome indicator and conducts the Alberta Graduate Outcomes Survey every two years, while the Canadian Graduate and Professional Student Survey is run every three years. International students can take part in work‑integrated learning, including co‑op placements, internships, practicums and mentorship programs, without needing a co‑op work permit as of 1 April 2026, provided they receive the required confirmation letter from the university. The Careers and Work Integrated Learning team posts numerous paid summer internship opportunities—most of which are located in Edmonton but also available across Canada and internationally—on CareerConnect and offers coaching throughout the search, application, interview and onboarding processes. Lương & thị trường: In Alberta the labour market shows strong demand for international graduates in technology, engineering and health‑related occupations, with the highest‑paid groups being management and natural‑applied sciences that earn average hourly wages of about $30‑$31 (roughly $70,000‑$100,000 annually depending on experience); specifically, web designers and developers account for about 21 % of hires among international grads, while mathematicians, statisticians, actuaries and data scientists make up roughly 12 % and command median salaries near $100,000, and engineering and computer‑science graduates from the University of Alberta align with these trends, benefiting from a projected 2024‑2028 outlook that lists several thousand openings each year in the Job Bank, although international graduates with bachelor’s or master’s degrees are still somewhat less likely than Canadian‑trained peers to obtain positions that fully match their level of study, especially outside health, education, law and government services where employment rates are comparable.

For housing, international students can apply to live in International House, a residence on the North Campus that provides fully furnished rooms with wireless internet, heat, water and power included, along with residence‑life and Global Education programming; rates are posted online and applications are submitted through the university’s residence services (housing@ualberta.ca).
