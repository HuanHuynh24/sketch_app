**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Western University requires international applicants to submit an official high‑school (or university‑level) transcript and proof of English proficiency, and evaluates them primarily on their overall academic average – typically a minimum of 70 % (about a B‑ or a 2.7 GPA on a 4.0 scale) for most programs, with more competitive streams such as Advanced Entry Opportunity or scholarship‑eligible applications often needing higher marks (e.g., a total IB score of 27 points or more, no individual subject below 4, and at least six courses including Theory of Knowledge and an Extended Essay). Applicants must also meet any faculty‑specific prerequisite courses (for example, Grade 12 English, Biology, Chemistry and a Functions course for the BScN, or equivalent Ontario high‑school subjects for other programs) and may be asked to provide supplemental materials such as a portfolio, personal statement, audition or interview when applying to arts, music, design or other creative programmes; these supplemental components are mandatory where listed and have their own deadlines.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Western University requires international applicants whose first language is not English to submit a recent (within two years) score from an approved test: a minimum overall IELTS 6.5 (with no band below 6.0), a minimum TOEFL iBT 86 (with at least 20 in each section), a minimum PTE Academic 58 (with 50 or higher in each component), or a Duolingo English Test score of 115 or higher for direct undergraduate admission (the English Boost pathway requires 100 overall, 100 in literacy and 90 in each of the other subscores). Applicants may also be accepted with a CAEL score that meets the university’s threshold. Waivers are granted if the student has completed full‑time English instruction that satisfies one of the following: four years of secondary school taught in English, two years of full‑time study at an Ontario college in English, or one year of full‑time university study in English; in all cases the courses must be academic, taught in English, not ESL, and (for Canadian high‑school students) include grade 12 English. Additionally, students who have studied at least two full‑time years at an English‑language university in a country where English is an official language may be exempt, although the university reserves the right to request proof for any applicant whose first language is not English (the nursing programs do not apply these waiver rules).

- Western University charges international undergraduate students an annual tuition fee of ≈ CAD 48,000 – 64,400 per year (2025‑26), with a one‑time application fee of CAD 125; mandatory UHIP health insurance adds CAD 756, a Western One Card fee is CAD 32, and ancillary campus fees (e.g., student services, transit passes) typically add a few hundred dollars more, so the total yearly cost for an international student is roughly CAD 49,500 – 65,500.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Western University requires international under‑graduates to hold a recognized secondary‑school diploma, demonstrate English proficiency (e.g., IELTS 7.0 or TOEFL 92 iBT), and meet any program‑specific subject prerequisites such as senior‑level mathematics for engineering, biology/chemistry for health sciences, or portfolio review for fine‑arts programs; transfer students must also have a minimum cumulative average of about 70 % and provide course descriptions for all previously taken courses. The university uses a credit‑point system in which a full‑term course carries 5 credits (half‑term courses are 2.5 credits), and a standard bachelor’s degree comprises 20.0 credits (120 credit points), of which at least 10.0 credits must be earned on Western’s main campus with a minimum of 5.0 credits at the second‑year level or higher. The curriculum is organized into core university requirements, faculty‑specific core courses, a major (or honours) concentration, and a range of electives, with many programs integrating experiential‑learning components such as labs, co‑ops, or community projects; students typically enroll in a full load of 5 credits per term (or an equivalent mix of full‑ and half‑courses). ## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Western University in London, Ontario is a research‑intensive institution that consistently ranks within the top 1 % of universities worldwide, appearing in the top 200–250 of the Times Higher Education World University Rankings and in the top 250–300 of the QS World University Rankings, while also being listed among the top 200 Canadian universities in QS. In the 2026 QS World University Rankings by Subject the university is placed among the world’s top 400 in 40 disciplines and is tied for second in Canada for philosophy, with a total of 34 subjects ranked in the top 10 nationally, reflecting strong performance across arts and humanities, natural sciences, social sciences and management. The university is fully accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Colleges and Universities and recognized by the Association of Universities and Colleges of Canada, with individual professional programmes holding accreditation from relevant bodies such as the CPA Canada for accounting, the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board for engineering, and other discipline‑specific agencies, ensuring that both domestic and international students receive qualifications that meet national and international standards.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students applying for a study permit to attend Western University must provide proof that they have sufficient funds to cover the full first‑year tuition plus living expenses and travel costs without relying on Canadian employment; as of 1 September 2025 the minimum amount required for living expenses is $22,895 for a single student, which is added to the tuition amount to calculate the total proof‑of‑funds, and applicants may use bank statements, scholarship letters, loan documents, or a combination of accepted evidence to demonstrate these resources. Undergraduate applicants also need to obtain a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) from the province of Ontario as part of the permit process, whereas graduate students are exempt from the PAL requirement beginning 1 January 2026, though they still must meet the financial‑support criteria. Once the study permit is issued, students are permitted to work on‑ or off‑campus for up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled breaks, provided the employment does not become the primary source of funds for meeting the financial‑support requirement. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Western University may apply for a one‑time Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they completed an eligible program at a designated learning institution that was at least eight months long, maintained full‑time status (except the final term, which may be part‑time or on approved leave), and have a valid study permit at some point within the 180‑day window after receiving their final transcript and a PGWP letter from Western; the permit’s duration depends on the length of the study program: programs under eight months are ineligible, programs lasting from eight months up to less than two years receive a PGWP for a period equal to the program’s length, and programs of two years or more can be issued a PGWP for up to three years (or up to 18 months under the temporary 2023 extension for those whose permits expired in 2023); applicants must also meet all other IRCC criteria, including having a program classified under an eligible CIP code at the time of application.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Western University in London, Ontario provides universal access to work‑integrated learning—every student can take part in co‑ops, internships or practicums, and international students are eligible for these opportunities, which typically run 8‑16 months (or 4‑12 months for summer programs), are paid and require full‑time commitment (minimum 30 hours per week). The university reports a 93.5 % employment rate for graduates within six months of completing their degree, ranking it third in Canada for employment outcomes in the QS World University Rankings 2026, and connects students with over 8,000 employer partners to support career pathways and higher earnings after graduation. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Canadian institutions, including those from Western University, typically earn lower starting salaries than domestic peers, with bachelor‑level graduates averaging about CAN $52,000 per year, master‑level graduates about CAN $70,000 and college‑level graduates around CAN $45,000, while domestic graduates in the same categories earn roughly 16‑20 % more; labour‑market data show that the strongest demand for new graduates is in occupations classified by Job Bank Canada as “high‑growth” and “high‑demand,” notably software developers and web designers (median salaries near CAN $100,000), data scientists and other natural‑ and applied‑science professionals (median around CAN $85,000‑$95,000), engineers across civil, mechanical and electrical fields (median ≈ CAN $85,000‑$90,000), and health‑care roles such as nurses and allied‑health practitioners (median ≈ CAN $70,000‑$80,000); while a large share of international bachelor graduates are employed in sales and service occupations (about 34‑30 % versus 9‑17 % for Canadians), a significant proportion of those with master’s or doctoral degrees are entering natural and applied science fields (44‑46 % compared with roughly 18‑21 % of domestic graduates), reflecting both the overall demand for skilled technical talent and the salary premium associated with those occupations.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Western University’s International Student Office (now called Western International) is housed on the second floor of the International and Graduate Affairs Building and provides a comprehensive suite of services for international students, including mandatory orientation sessions that introduce newcomers to campus life, immigration procedures, academic resources and community integration; dedicated housing support through on‑campus accommodation information and off‑campus advisor services that help students locate, lease and settle into suitable rental options; and career development assistance via the university’s Career Centre, which offers résumé and cover‑letter workshops, interview preparation, employer networking events, job‑search platforms and tailored counseling for international students seeking employment or internships in Canada. Đời sống & môi trường: Western University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, featuring more than 200 student‑run clubs and associations—including cultural groups such as the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA), which provides a community for Vietnamese students through social events, Tet celebrations, mentorship programs, and language‑exchange activities—and a broad range of academic, recreational, and intramural sports clubs accessible through the Campus Life portal; safety on campus is overseen by the Western Special Constable Service and a 24‑hour campus cruiser program, complemented by emergency phones, a campus‑wide safety app, and a dedicated Emergency Services team that respond to incidents and provide escort services, while the Office of Western International supplies orientation, settlement support, and dedicated advising for international students to help them integrate socially and academically.
