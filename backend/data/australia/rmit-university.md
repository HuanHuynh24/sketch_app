**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students must be at least 16 years old when their program begins, meet RMIT’s English language requirements (such as an approved test score or completion of an English‑language provider course), and hold a qualification recognised as equivalent to the Victorian Certificate of Education (VCE) with a minimum overall average of 65 % (or the corresponding GPA in foundation programmes – for example a GPA of 5.0 equates to 65 %, 5.5 to 70 %, 6.0 to 75 % and higher GPAs map to higher percentages). Applicants must provide official transcripts and, where required, evidence of meeting prerequisite subjects (a minimum GPA of 5.0 for Bachelor‑level prerequisite courses and 4.0 for Associate Degree courses). Some courses also require a selection task, which may include a portfolio, interview, audition or other specialised assessment (commonly for art, design and creative disciplines), and for students completing Australian Year 12 locally an ATAR may be used for entry.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- RMIT requires international students to demonstrate English proficiency through an approved test, with the standard minimum scores being an overall IELTS Academic band of 6.5 (no individual band below 6.0), an TOEFL iBT overall score of 79 (with at least 13 in Reading, 12 in Listening, 18 in Speaking and 21 in Writing), and a Pearson Test of English Academic overall score of 58 (communication band no lower than 50); for study‑abroad or exchange students the thresholds are lower (IELTS 6.0, TOEFL 60, PTE 50), and a Duolingo English Test score of 115 is accepted for most undergraduate and postgraduate programmes. Applicants who have completed at least one year of full‑time tertiary study where English was the language of instruction, or who hold a recognised English qualification such as Cambridge C1 Advanced (minimum 176 with no component below 169) or C2 Proficiency, may be granted a waiver of the test requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- RMIT University charges international students tuition in Australian dollars (AU$) and the amount varies by program, with typical full‑time undergraduate courses costing roughly AU$30,000‑AU$35,000 per year (about AU$90,000‑AU$105,000 for a three‑year degree) and most postgraduate coursework programs ranging from AU$30,000 to AU$38,000 per year; the exact annual and total fees are listed on each program’s fee page and are rounded to the nearest $10 per credit point, with a possible annual increase of up to 7.5 %; an application fee of AU$100 is required for applicants from specified countries (the fee is non‑refundable but credited toward the tuition deposit once the offer is accepted) and other international applicants may not be charged an application fee; additional mandatory charges include the Student Services and Amenities Fee (SSAF) which is calculated each semester (typically a few hundred dollars per semester), material and administrative fees for items such as books, equipment, fieldwork or laboratory practicals, and any other program‑specific expenses that are itemised separately.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- RMIT University requires international students to meet English language standards and, where applicable, subject‐specific entry criteria such as a VCE score of at least 20 in English (or 23 for EAL) and a comparable score in Mathematics for certain education and engineering pathways, while many programmes list “no prerequisite subjects” and instead rely on the foundation studies programme to guarantee entry; credit points follow the Australian standard where a typical semester comprises 24 credit points (≈12 subjects at 2 points each) and a full bachelor degree normally totals 144 points, with credit‑transfer options available for students who have completed equivalent coursework at partner institutions or another Australian university, allowing up to half of the required points to be recognised and shortening study length (e.g., a Master’s can be completed in 1.5 years with transferred credits); the curriculum is delivered year‑round across six intakes, each subject lasting seven weeks, and is structured around clearly defined learning outcomes that map industry‑relevant knowledge, skills and professional capabilities to each unit, ensuring that graduates meet the competencies expected by employers in fields such as construction management, communication, project management and other applied sciences.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The subclass 500 student visa requires applicants to demonstrate sufficient funds – typically at least AUD 29,710 for the main applicant to cover 12 months of living costs, tuition, travel and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC), with additional amounts of AUD 10,394 for a partner and AUD 4,449 per child (plus schooling costs of AUD 13,502 per child if school fees apply); valid OSHC must be maintained for the whole stay for the student and any dependents; work rights allow up to 48 hours per fortnight of paid work while classes are in session (unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks) and there is no hour limit for research master’s or doctoral students; RMIT University requires prospective international students to meet these visa conditions, hold a Confirmation of Enrolment from RMIT, maintain OSHC, and comply with the work limits, and all official guidance and eligibility criteria are available on the Department of Home Affairs website (immi.homeaffairs.gov.au) and the Study in Australia portal (studyinaustralia.gov.au). Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) lets international students who have graduated from an Australian CRICOS‑registered course such as those at RMIT University remain in Australia to work full‑time (unrestricted hours) and, if desired, include eligible family members, and its duration depends on the qualification and stream: the Post‑Higher Education Work stream (for bachelor degree or higher) grants up to 2 years for most bachelor and master graduates, up to 3 years for master‑by‑research holders and up to 4 years for doctorate graduates, while the Post‑Vocational Education Work stream (for associate degrees, diplomas or trade qualifications linked to an occupation on the MLTSSL) provides up to 2 years; from 1 July 2023, graduates with a degree on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can add a further 2 years, and those who have studied, lived and worked in regional Australia may receive an additional 1‑2 years, giving some applicants up to 6 years of post‑study work rights, provided they meet the Australian study requirement (generally two academic years), health, character and English proficiency standards and apply before their student visa expires (which normally allows a three‑month post‑completion grace period).

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: RMIT University offers a comprehensive Work‑Integrated Learning (WIL) framework that enables international students to secure internships, co‑ops and placement opportunities across a range of industries, with many programmes allowing students to find their own industry‑aligned placement (including unpaid roles that must comply with Australian Fair Work regulations) and receive academic credit through dedicated units such as the Undergraduate Business Internship; these experiences often translate into permanent employment, exemplified by graduates who have moved from RMIT‑based internships to roles at companies like Medibank, and the university’s graduate outcomes data show that in 2024 84.9 % of higher‑education graduates were employed overall (with 69.7 % in full‑time positions) shortly after completion, indicating strong post‑graduation career prospects for those who undertake the internship and placement pathways. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Australia earn considerably less than their domestic peers, with a mean full‑time pre‑tax salary of roughly AU$58 000 (about AU$54 000 for undergraduates and slightly higher for postgraduate coursework students) compared with the domestic average of around AU$70 000 for undergraduates and AU$90 000 for postgraduates, and in specific fields such as Business and Management the median international graduate salary is about AU$12 000 lower than the domestic figure; nevertheless, occupations with strong labour‑market demand—particularly health professions like dentistry, pharmacy and medicine, as well as engineering (civil, electrical, mechanical), information‑technology roles including software development, data science and artificial intelligence, and nursing and aged‑care positions—consistently show high full‑time employment rates for international graduates and appear on the Jobs and Skills Australia Occupation Shortage List, while RMIT University’s most recent enrolment data indicate that the bulk of its international cohort comes from South‑East Asia (≈22 000 students in 2023) followed by North‑East Asia (≈7 200) and Southern Asia (≈5 100), underscoring the relevance of these high‑demand occupations for RMIT’s graduate outcomes.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: RMIT’s International Student Office, operated through Student Connect, offers a one‑stop service for overseas students that includes orientation sessions to introduce campus life, academic procedures and support networks; dedicated housing assistance via a specialised portal, preferred providers and tenancy‑advice services together with specific welfare arrangements for students under 18; and a full career centre providing free one‑to‑one career consultations, online employability resources, job‑search tools and guidance on Australian work rights, post‑study visas and employment opportunities. Đời sống & môi trường: RMIT University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with a wide range of clubs and societies that can be joined simply by completing an online form and joining a Discord group, meaning there is no interview process and students can start attending events right away; many Vietnamese students recommend becoming involved with the RMIT University Student Union (RUSU) volunteer team, which runs regular activities such as the Thursday BBQ, and also suggest using RMIT’s Kirrip program and other cultural clubs like the Indonesian Association (PPIA) to make new friends and feel at home, while housing options such as Scape and UniLodge provide convenient student‑only apartments that can serve as rental references for future accommodation; safety and wellbeing are supported by a free, confidential counselling service, a Safer Community team that handles concerns about sexual assault, harassment and family violence, on‑campus health services and mandatory Overseas Student Health Cover, and the university’s international student services team offers additional guidance on accommodation, part‑time work and visa conditions, helping Vietnamese and other international students navigate study, social life and personal safety in Melbourne.
