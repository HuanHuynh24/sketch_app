**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Toronto must demonstrate English proficiency unless they qualify for an exemption; the standard tests and minimum scores are IELTS Academic 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, TOEFL iBT 100 total with at least 22 in each section (paper‑based, computer‑based and Home Edition are accepted but MyBest and ITP scores are not), PTE Academic 68 overall with a minimum of 60 in each component, Duolingo English Test 115 overall, CAEL 70 overall with no part below 60, and the University’s own ELDA/COPE assessment requiring a total of 86 (with 32 in Writing and 22 in Reading and Listening). Exemptions are granted when a student has completed secondary or post‑secondary education in English, holds a valid International Baccalaureate English A (HL or SL) score of 4 or higher (or an IB English B HL score of 7), achieved a final grade of A (I) or B (II) in Caribbean CSEC English, or provides other proof of English instruction such as a university transcript in English; in such cases no separate English test is required.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to the University of Toronto must satisfy senior‑level English proficiency and any subject‑specific prerequisite courses required by their chosen faculty, with these prerequisites counted in the admission average and needing to be completed at the Grade 12 or undergraduate level; for those who do not meet the English requirement, the International Foundation Program (IFP) offers conditional admission, requiring successful completion of a set of for‑credit foundation courses—including an intensive English component, academic‑skills modules, and a discipline‑specific course—that count toward the degree and are assessed continuously, typically structured as 0.5‑credit courses (four per term for a full‑time load) and designed to develop university‑level academic communication, critical‑thinking, and subject‑specific competencies, thereby ensuring that graduates of the IFP meet the curriculum’s learning outcomes of disciplined knowledge, analytical ability, and readiness for advanced coursework.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Toronto is Canada’s top‑ranked university in both the QS World University Rankings (25th globally and 1st in Canada) and the Times Higher Education World University Rankings (ranked 21st worldwide and 1st in Canada in the most recent edition), and it appears in the top‑20 of every broad subject area in QS—life sciences and medicine (13th), arts and humanities (14th), social sciences and management (14th), engineering and technology (17th) and natural sciences (20th)—with additional top‑10 placements in specific fields such as nursing (4th), sports‑related subjects (4th), anatomy and physiology (5th), education (10th), data science (10th), theology, divinity and religious studies (10th) and philosophy (10th); about 20 % of its student body are international students, and the university is fully accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Colleges and Universities, while its professional programs hold the appropriate national accreditations (e.g., CEAB for engineering, CACMS for medicine, and the respective bodies for law, nursing, etc.).

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students admitted to the University of Toronto for a program lasting six months or more must obtain a study permit, and the application will almost always require a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) issued by the School of Graduate Studies; the PAL can be requested through the Centre for International Experience by emailing the university’s ISA office with the student’s passport name, program details and, if applicable, a copy of an IRRC refusal letter. For study‑permit applications submitted on or after 1 September 2025, IRCC expects proof that the applicant can cover the full first‑year tuition plus at least CAD 22,895 for living expenses (higher amounts are required if a spouse, partner or children accompany the student), and this proof can be shown with bank statements, scholarship letters, or a financial undertaking from a Canadian sponsor. Once a valid study permit is issued, full‑time international students are generally permitted to work off‑campus up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions (and full‑time during scheduled breaks), although “visiting” students who are not enrolled in a Canadian degree program are not authorized for off‑campus work. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of Toronto may obtain a one‑time Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they have completed a full‑time program of at least eight months at a designated learning institution, hold a valid study permit, and apply within 180 days of receiving official confirmation that their degree, diploma or certificate requirements have been met (final marks posted on ACORN); the permit is an open work permit that allows full‑time employment for any employer from the date the application is submitted, provided the study permit remains valid, and its length is based on the study program: if the program lasted between eight months and less than two years, the PGWP is issued for up to the same duration as the program, while programs of two years or more (including master’s programmes as of 15 February 2024) qualify for a three‑year PGWP, subject to the passport’s expiry date, and the holder may not have previously received a PGWP for an earlier study period.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Toronto records exceptionally high graduate employment outcomes, with 93 % of Rotman Commerce graduates securing a job within nine months of graduation—84 % of those positions are in the Greater Toronto Area, 6 % elsewhere in Canada, 2 % in the United States and 8 % internationally—and the university’s overall two‑year employment rate exceeds 92 %, placing it first in Canada for graduate employability; extensive co‑op and internship programs embedded in faculties such as Engineering, Computer Science and Business deliver structured work‑integrated learning that translates into strong job offers, and international students—who make up roughly half of each graduating class—enjoy comparable placement success and career trajectories. Lương & thị trường: Graduates from the University of Toronto typically earn about C$96 000 annually, with alumni hired by firms such as Google, Microsoft, IBM, Scotiabank and BMO reporting salaries that often exceed C$120 000, while the most common high‑paying professions for U‑of‑T alumni include software engineering, data science, artificial‑intelligence research, finance, consulting and senior roles in health‑technology firms; international student graduates, however, earn lower median incomes than Canadian‑citizen graduates—approximately C$52 000 for those with a bachelor’s degree (about 20 % less than the C$65 200 earned by Canadian‑trained peers) and C$70 000 for master’s holders (roughly 16 % below the C$83 900 earned by Canadian graduates)—yet they still achieve strong employment outcomes, with most securing full‑time work within six months, especially in occupations flagged by Job Bank Canada as “high‑demand” such as software engineers, computer‑vision engineers, penetration testers, Ruby‑on‑Rails developers, data scientists, registered nurses and skilled‑trade specialists, where median wages range from roughly C$75 000 for nursing and trades to C$85‑90 000 for software and data‑analytics roles and can exceed C$120 000 in senior tech positions.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Toronto’s International Student Centre (ISC) — located in the Arts & Administration Building (Room AA142) and reachable at 416‑287‑7087 or isc.utsc@utoronto.ca — serves international, exchange, and newcomer students across all campuses by providing pre‑arrival guidance, comprehensive on‑campus orientation workshops (including “Before You Leave Home,” “Arriving in Canada,” “Living in Canada,” and pathways to permanent residency), and the “Get Started” academic orientation through the Academic Advising and Career Centre; it also offers immigration and travel document advice, UHIP health‑insurance support, and personalized housing assistance (covering University Family Housing, off‑campus rentals and campus residence options), while career services are integrated with the Academic Advising & Career Centre to deliver résumé reviews, interview preparation, job‑search strategies, co‑op and post‑graduation work‑permit information, and employer‑connect events and workshops. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Toronto offers a vibrant campus‑life experience for international students, including dedicated Vietnamese student groups at both the St. George and Scarborough campuses: the University of Toronto Vietnamese Students’ Association (UTVSA), founded in 1981, and the UTSC Vietnamese Students Association (UTSC VSA), which organizes cultural, social and leadership events, networking workshops, academic‑support sessions and celebrations of Vietnamese holidays to help students connect with one another and the broader community; in addition to these culturally specific clubs, the university hosts dozens of multicultural and interest‑based student organizations that provide opportunities for socializing, skill‑building and community service, while campus safety is reinforced by a 24‑hour patrol service—particularly at UTSC, where students who need to walk on campus after dark can request a patroller to escort them, and overall the campuses maintain a reputation for being safe and well‑secured with regular security presence, health‑and‑wellness resources and clear safety protocols for all students.
