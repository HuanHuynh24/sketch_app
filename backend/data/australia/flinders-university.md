### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Flinders University offers three main intakes for international students – Semester 1 beginning in early March, Semester 2 in early July and a Study‑Period 3 start in October – and expects offshore applicants to submit their StudyLink applications several weeks before each start date (typically by late November for the March intake and by late May for the July intake, with exact dates posted on the university’s key‑dates page); the application is lodged online through the StudyLink portal where you create an account, upload required documents (passport, academic transcripts, English‑language test results, and any programme‑specific prerequisites), pay the non‑refundable international application fee (generally AUD 150), and then can track progress, receive an offer and, once offered, accept the place and pay the acceptance deposit within the 2‑ to 4‑week acceptance window indicated on the offer letter.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Flinders University accepts international students into both undergraduate and postgraduate programmes provided they meet a minimum academic threshold roughly equivalent to an Australian ATAR 65 (about a GPA 2.6 – 2.8 on a 4‑point scale) and demonstrate English proficiency of at least IELTS 6.5 overall (with a minimum of 6.0 in each band for most courses, rising to 7.0 for health‑related degrees such as medicine, nursing and physiotherapy); applicants must submit certified transcripts (học bạ) showing completion of secondary education with a grade point average that converts to the required ATAR (e.g., IB 6‑7, GCE A‑Levels C‑C, US SAT 1200‑1300, Vietnamese Đại Học GPA 2.5‑3.0) and meet any subject prerequisites specified for the chosen course (typically English plus Mathematics and, where relevant, Physics, Chemistry or Biology). Certain creative and design programmes (e.g., Architecture, Visual Arts, Creative Industries) also require a portfolio and may conduct an interview to assess suitability, while graduate‑entry health programmes (Doctor of Medicine, Nursing Graduate Entry, Master of Clinical Psychology) require a weighted GPA (wGPA ≥ 2.6), the relevant entrance exam (GAMSAT for medicine, GRE/GMAT where applicable), and an interview as part of the selection process.

**4. Học phí**

- Flinders University charges international students tuition in Australian dollars (AUD) with annual fees that vary by programme: most undergraduate courses are priced between AUD 32,900 and AUD 40,200 per year, so a typical three‑year bachelor costs roughly AUD 98,700‑120,600 and a four‑year degree about AUD 120,800‑160,800; postgraduate coursework fees range from AUD 35,500 to AUD 47,300 per year depending on the field. The university’s application fee is AUD 100 for undergraduate applicants and AUD 200 for postgraduate applicants. In addition to tuition, international students must pay a Student Services and Amenities Fee (around AUD 350 per year), Overseas Student Health Cover (approximately AUD 600‑800 per year), and various compulsory course‑specific charges such as the DCSI screening fee (AUD 57.20) and equipment items (e.g., a stethoscope from AUD 160).

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at Flinders University must meet entry prerequisites that include a minimum Australian Year 11 (or equivalent) qualification, an ATAR or equivalent score (generally 50–70 for most undergraduate courses), and English language proficiency (IELTS 6.0–7.0 with no band below 5.5, depending on the program). Undergraduate courses are organized into three semesters per year (S1, S2 and S3), with each subject worth 6 credit points; a standard three‑year bachelor therefore comprises 72 credit points (24 per year), while honours pathways require higher achievement levels and award 80 credit points for a four‑year sequence. The curriculum is delivered through a mix of lectures, tutorials, labs and project work, and each unit specifies clear learning outcomes that emphasise discipline‑specific knowledge, analytical and problem‑solving abilities, effective communication, ethical and professional practice, and the capacity to apply research and technical skills in real‑world contexts.

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Flinders University is recognised internationally, ranking within the top 2 percent of global universities in the 2026 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings and placed 5th overall in Australia in THE’s 2025 Impact Rankings where it holds the world‑lead positions of 5th for Gender Equality (SDG 5) and 2nd for Reduced Inequalities (SDG 10); in the overall THE Impact Ranking it sits at 21 globally. In the QS World University Rankings the university is positioned in the #221‑240 band for 2023 and is listed in 13 subject areas, with notable placements such as Life Sciences and Medicine (both 271 globally), Arts & Humanities (top 500), English Language & Literature (251‑300), Psychology (201‑250), Education (251‑300), Pharmacy & Pharmacology (301‑350), Biological Sciences (451‑500), Agriculture & Forestry (351‑400) and Chemistry (551‑600). The institution is fully accredited by Australia’s Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) and its professional programmes hold relevant accreditation from bodies such as the Australian Nursing and Midwifery Accreditation Council, the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency and other sector‑specific agencies.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student Visa (subclass 500) requires applicants to demonstrate a minimum financial capacity of AUD 29,710 to cover 12 months of living expenses, travel to Australia, tuition for the first year and the cost of Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire period of stay, with any additional dependents needing extra funds (AUD 10,394 for a partner and AUD 4,449 per child, plus school fees of AUD 13,502 per child if applicable); OSHC must be maintained for the whole visa term and is a mandatory condition listed on the Department of Home Affairs website; visa holders are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while their course is in session, with unlimited work rights for students undertaking a master's by research, doctoral degree, or during recognised course breaks and mandatory placements, and must continue to meet enrolment, attendance and satisfactory progress requirements, as outlined by both the official Study in Australia guide and Flinders University’s international student information, which also notes that a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) is required before applying and that all visa conditions—including health insurance and work limits—must be adhered to throughout the stay. Sau tốt nghiệp: An international student who has graduated from Flinders University can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) in the Post‑Study Work stream, which gives unrestricted work rights and allows the holder’s partner and dependent children to stay and work in Australia; the visa duration depends on the qualification – typically two years for a bachelor’s or master’s by coursework, three years for a master’s by research, and four years for a doctoral degree – and, from 1 July 2023, graduates whose degree is on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List receive an additional two‑year extension, while those who have studied, lived and worked in a designated regional area (including Flinders’ regional campuses) may qualify for a further one‑ to two‑year “Second Post‑Study Work” extension, potentially extending stay to up to six years, after which they may transition to other skilled work visas such as the subclass 482 if they meet the requirements.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Flinders University reports that its international undergraduate students achieve a labour‑force participation rate of 92.8 % – the highest in South Australia – and that overall graduate employment is strong, with about 76 % of domestic undergraduates securing full‑time work within a year of graduating and roughly 88 % employed in either full‑time or part‑time roles; nearly four out of five Flinders graduates find full‑time employment quickly and nine out of ten are in paid work, while the university’s extensive work‑integrated learning (WIL) program offers industry‑aligned internships, co‑ops and placements across science, engineering, business and other fields, including partnerships such as the KPMG Accelerate Program that connect students to professional roles and a pathway into graduate graduate programs, thereby supporting career‑ready outcomes and a wide range of post‑graduation occupations. Lương & thị trường: Graduate earnings in Australia vary markedly by field and visa status, with the overall median full‑time annual salary for university graduates hovering around AUD 70,000, while a recent analysis of equity‑group graduates reported an average annual wage of roughly AUD 39,000; international graduates face considerably poorer outcomes, with only about 15 % securing full‑time work within four months of completing a bachelor’s degree and a persistent wage gap of several thousand dollars compared with domestic peers, and they are more likely to be employed in part‑time or low‑skill roles; labour‑market data from Jobs and Skills Australia highlight a strong, ongoing demand for occupations listed on the national Occupation Shortage List—particularly in health (nursing, allied health, medical practitioners), engineering (civil, structural, electrical), information‑communication technology (software development, cybersecurity, data analytics), construction management, and education—where median earnings exceed the general graduate average and long‑term skill shortages are projected to continue through the 2030s.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Flinders University’s International Student Services (ISS) is the dedicated office for on‑shore international students and offers a comprehensive support package that includes a compulsory online Flinders International Student Orientation (FISO) covering enrolment, university systems, health cover, policies and Australian regulations, as well as additional campus‑wide orientation events, course‑specific introductions and organised tours of the city and campus; ISS also assists with accommodation by providing information on on‑campus housing, off‑campus rentals and settlement services, and offers one‑to‑one confidential appointments with International Student Advisors to help with academic, social and personal adjustment challenges, while the university’s broader Career Services team—accessible to all students—provides career counselling, resume and interview workshops, internship and job search assistance, and networking events tailored to international students’ needs, ensuring they receive guidance for both study and post‑graduation employment pathways. Đời sống & môi trường: Flinders University provides a dedicated International Student Services team that welcomes new overseas students with orientation sessions, campus tours, social activities and confidential one‑to‑one advising, while the broader campus life is vibrant and inclusive, offering a wide range of clubs—from sports and spiritual groups to cultural and community societies—including a Vietnamese student community that hosts cultural nights, language‑exchange meet‑ups and peer‑support networks to help Vietnamese students settle and connect; safety on both the Bedford Park and City campuses is overseen by a university‑wide “Safety and Respect” Action Plan that requires club executives to complete mandatory training on event safety and respectful relationships, conducts regular night‑time security audits, provides 24‑hour security staff and 12 on‑site hotline phones, and offers clear reporting channels for sexual harassment, assault or other concerns, with emergency assistance available through 000 or Flinders Security on (08) 8201 2880.
