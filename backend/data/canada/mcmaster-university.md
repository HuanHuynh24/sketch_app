### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- McMaster University welcomes international applicants for three main entry terms – Fall (September), Winter (January) and Spring/Summer (May) – with the official application windows opening roughly 60 days before each term’s start; for 2026 the deadlines are 1 November 2025 for the Fall term, 1 March 2026 for the Winter term, and 2 July 2026 for the Spring/Summer term (all dates are for submitting the online application, with supporting documents due 40 days later). The university charges a non‑refundable application fee of CAD 150 for undergraduate international students and CAD 140 for most graduate programmes (the Master of Communications Management, for example, closed its international deadline on 31 January 2026). To apply, create an account on the McMaster online admissions portal (Mosaic), choose the desired admit term, search for the program, fill in personal and academic information, upload transcripts, English‑language proof and any required supplemental material, pay the fee with a credit card, and submit; you will then receive an admission‑offer letter via Mosaic, after which you must confirm acceptance and pay any required deposits. **2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International undergraduate applicants to McMaster must submit an official high‑school transcript (học bạ) showing completion of the required secondary‑school subjects for their chosen faculty and a cumulative GPA of at least 5.0 on the 12‑point scale (approximately a B‑/70 %) – limited‑enrolment programs often expect a higher average and may set a minimum of B‑ (70 %) as a baseline – in addition, proof of English proficiency is mandatory, typically an IELTS overall score of 6.5 (with no band below 6.0) or a TOEFL iBT total of 90 (minimum 20 in each component); program‑specific prerequisites apply (for example, calculus and physics for engineering, biology and chemistry for health‑science programs), and creative‑focused programs such as the BFA in Integrated Arts require a successful electronic portfolio submission and may also involve an interview, while other competitive streams (e.g., honors or limited‑enrolment majors) may request a supplemental application, higher GPA, and occasionally an interview or additional documentation; all transcripts and test scores must be official and, if not in English, accompanied by a certified translation.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- McMaster University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least a 5.0 overall on the IELTS Academic test, a 3.5 on the TOEFL iBT (as listed by the university), a minimum score of 40 on the Pearson Test of English (PTE) Academic, or a score of 75 on the Duolingo English Test; additional accepted tests include the CAEL (minimum 50) and the CAE (minimum 160). Applicants who have completed a post‑secondary degree where the language of instruction and assessment was English may be exempt from submitting an English‑language test, and those who do not meet the minimum scores can enroll in the McMaster English Language Development (MELD) Diploma program, which provides a conditional offer of admission and, upon successful completion, waives the English‑language requirement for entry into an undergraduate program.

Altogether, an international student can expect to budget roughly C$108 000–C$110 000 for a full year of study, covering tuition, housing, meals, transport and health insurance.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to McMaster University must satisfy the same secondary‑school subject prerequisites as Canadian applicants, which typically include English (U), Calculus and Vectors (U) and two additional Grade 12 U or M courses in the sciences, mathematics, or related fields; specific programs may require further prerequisites such as Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth and Space, Computer Science (M or U), or Computer Engineering (M or Technology). Once admitted, the undergraduate curriculum is organized into five academic levels, with a three‑level (30‑unit) final year and at least two additional levels (approximately 60 units total) required to be completed at McMaster, including a minimum of 18 units of program‑specific courses for the final level. Courses are assigned credit units that count toward degree requirements, and each course lists explicit learning outcomes that describe the knowledge, skills, and competencies students are expected to demonstrate upon successful completion; these outcomes are aligned with faculty‑wide program objectives and are used to guide assessment and ensure that graduates meet the standards of their chosen discipline.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students admitted to McMaster University who need a study permit must provide a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)—a document issued by the university in partnership with the Ontario government that confirms the institution’s acceptance and is required for most new undergraduate permit applications—along with proof of sufficient financial resources as outlined by IRCC, which must demonstrate available funds to cover tuition for the first year, living expenses for the student (and any accompanying family members), and transportation costs; acceptable evidence includes recent bank statements (typically covering the past four months) for Canadian or foreign accounts, a guaranteed investment certificate, a student loan letter, a bank draft convertible to Canadian dollars, or a letter of financial support from a sponsor, and, if applicable, proof of a Canadian‑banked account or scholarship funding; once the study permit is issued, the student may work up to 20 hours per week off‑campus during regular academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled breaks, or work on‑campus without a separate work permit, provided the permit includes a condition that authorizes off‑campus work. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of McMaster University are eligible for a Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they completed a program that is at least eight months long at this designated learning institution, maintained full‑time study status throughout (with part‑time allowed only in the final term), and applied for the permit within 180 days of receiving official confirmation of program completion while their study permit remained valid at some point during that 180‑day window; the PGWP’s length is determined by the length of the study program – up to the same duration as the program if it was less than two years, and up to three years if the program was two years or longer, and for master’s degrees, a three‑year PGWP can be issued even for programs under two years provided the program was at least eight months (or 900 hours for Quebec programs) and all other eligibility criteria are met, with the permit’s validity also limited by the expiry date of the holder’s passport.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: McMaster University offers international students access to paid work‑integrated learning (WIL) opportunities—including co‑ops, internships and placements—through its Careers & Experiential Education office, with eligibility beginning after completion of the Level I courses and requiring a co‑op work permit from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada; while some employers may be restricted, the university’s co‑op team assists students throughout the application process. Co‑op and internship experiences are designed as structured learning that complements coursework, builds professional skills and enhances confidence for post‑graduation careers across a range of sectors such as engineering, computer science, health sciences, business, social sciences and research. In the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science, roughly 81 percent of graduates participate in co‑op work experiences, and McMaster’s overall graduate employment rate two years after graduation consistently exceeds 90 percent, with the majority of alumni employed full‑time in roles closely or somewhat related to the skills acquired in their degree programs. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Canada experience a similar overall education‑job match to Canadian‑trained peers but are less likely to secure positions that directly require their degree level, with the most common fields for those employed being web design and development (about 21 % of international graduate workers) and mathematics‑related roles such as statisticians, actuaries and data scientists (around 12 %). For McMaster University graduates, median earnings two years after graduation are roughly CAD 55,000 for bachelor’s degree holders and about CAD 65,000 for master’s degree holders. Across the country, the Job Bank identifies strong labour‑market demand in health‑care occupations, information‑technology specialist roles, engineering positions and skilled‑trade jobs, with median wages ranging from roughly CAD 45,000 for entry‑level trades to over CAD 100,000 for senior data‑science and engineering occupations.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: McMaster University’s Office of International Affairs (OIA) oversees International Student Services (ISS), which runs comprehensive orientation programs for new international students—including welcome sessions, campus tours, immigration and settlement workshops, and peer‑mentor events—and offers ongoing personal and academic guidance through the International Students’ Advisor; housing support is coordinated through the university’s residence services with ISS helping students secure on‑campus residences, navigate off‑campus housing options, and providing resources such as roommate matching, lease information and budgeting advice; career development is delivered by the Career Development Office in partnership with ISS, offering résumé and cover‑letter workshops, interview preparation, one‑on‑one career counseling, co‑op and internship listings, employer networking events, and dedicated sessions on work‑permit and post‑graduation employment for international students, while the global office also manages exchange programs and provides information on international partnerships. Đời sống & môi trường: McMaster University provides a comprehensive support network for international students that includes 24‑hour Campus Security Services for emergencies, a Student Wellness centre for mental‑health counselling, and RO Student Services to assist with financial crises, while also offering accommodation‑related assistance and a peer‑to‑peer International Student Buddy Program that pairs newcomers with experienced upper‑year students to ease the transition to campus life; beyond academic support, students can join any of the more than 300 clubs and societies—including a Vietnamese Student Association that organizes cultural celebrations such as Tết, language‑exchange meet‑ups, and community‑building events for Vietnamese students—and benefit from a vibrant campus community that prioritises safety, inclusivity, and opportunities for social engagement.
