### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply (UCAS)**

- International students applying for undergraduate study at the University of Edinburgh submit their applications through UCAS, completing the online form, personal statement, academic history and a reference; after receiving an offer they upload supporting documents and pay any required tuition deposit via the university’s applicant portal. The main intake is the autumn (October) entry, for which the UCAS deadline is 15 October (the final deadline for most courses, including Medicine, Dentistry and Veterinary Medicine), while a spring (February) entry is available for a limited number of programmes and the UCAS deadline for this intake is typically 15 January. The UCAS application fee is £26 for a single‑choice application or £29 for multiple choices (up to five courses); the University of Edinburgh does not charge an additional application fee.

For A‑level routes the typical offer is AAA (or AAB for some courses) with specific subject requirements that may be checked against GCSE or SQA National 5 grades, and where a programme lists a required grade A the university expects a grade 7 in the corresponding IB Higher‑Level subject (a grade 6 is acceptable where the A‑level requirement is lower, e.g., a B); for IB applicants the overall diploma must normally be 36‑38 points with at least grade 5 in Standard‑Level English and the appropriate higher‑level subjects for the chosen course, and the IB results and diploma transcript are submitted through UCAS. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to the University of Edinburgh must demonstrate English proficiency regardless of nationality, but candidates from a majority English‑speaking country or those who have completed at least 50 % of a previous degree where teaching and assessment were conducted in English (including the final year) are exempt from the test requirement. For those who need to provide evidence, the university accepts IELTS (including UKVI) with a minimum overall score of 6.5 and at least 6.0 in each component, TOEFL iBT (or Paper‑based) with a minimum total of 92 (or 80 for some programmes) and minimum sub‑scores of 20‑21 in each skill, PTE Academic with a minimum overall score of 58, and the Duolingo English Test with a minimum score of 115; all test results must be no older than two years on the first day of the month the programme starts (some qualifications such as the Oxford Test of English Advanced may be accepted up to three and a half years old). If the required score is met at the time of application the entry is considered more competitive, and the university also offers pre‑sessional English courses for candidates who need to improve their proficiency.

**4. Học phí**

- The University of Edinburgh charges international (overseas) undergraduate students an annual tuition fee of £31,800 for most taught programmes in the 2025‑2026 academic year (with higher rates for subjects such as Medicine, Dentistry and Veterinary Science that can exceed £50,000 per year), the fee being quoted per year and applied each academic year of study; consequently a typical four‑year degree will cost roughly £127,200 in tuition alone. The application fee for non‑UK/EU candidates is £105 (£80 for UK/EU applicants) and must be paid when the online application is submitted. In addition to tuition, students pay a yearly student services levy of about £300 and a UK‑wide health surcharge of £470 per year, plus any programme‑specific material or field‑trip costs, all of which are denominated in British pounds (£).

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: International students applying for a UK Student visa to study at the University of Edinburgh must prove they have sufficient funds to cover both tuition fees for the first year (as stated on their Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) and the required living costs, which are £1,023 per month for up to nine months outside London (totaling £9,207) or £1,334 per month if studying in London; these funds must be held in a bank account for at least 28 consecutive days and the statement must be dated no more than six months before the visa application, or alternatively a sponsor or scholarship letter that meets Home Office standards can be used, and the applicant must also have a valid CAS from Edinburgh (which includes the correct ACL delivery code 2HE529) and meet the Immigration Health Surcharge and any TB testing requirements. Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): The Graduate visa allows international students who have completed an eligible degree at the University of Edinburgh to remain in the UK for post‑study work, granting permission for up to two years after a bachelor's or master’s qualification and up to three years after a PhD or other doctorate; applicants must be physically present in the UK, hold a valid Student visa at the time of application, have had their successful completion reported to UKVI, and meet the standard eligibility criteria such as having studied an eligible course for the required duration (generally at least two years) and being able to demonstrate the required identity checks via the UK Immigration: ID Check app.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Edinburgh’s Careers Service arranges internships, work‑placements and co‑op opportunities for all students—including international students—and provides tailored advice, CV workshops and employer fairs to help them secure graduate jobs either in the UK or abroad, with recent 2022‑2023 graduate outcomes showing that about 91 % of graduates are in graduate‑level employment; the most common sectors for these graduates are finance (42 %), education (9 %), information technology (8 %), retail and consumer goods (8 %), marketing (6 %), management consulting (4 %), media (3 %) and a range of other fields (20 %), and they are employed across 23 countries worldwide. Lương & thị trường: University of Edinburgh graduates, including international students, are most in demand in sectors that face chronic staffing shortages such as technology and computing (including data analytics, cybersecurity and AI‑related roles), engineering and industrial projects, consulting, financial services and health‑related occupations, with many of these positions offering visa‑sponsorship for overseas talent; the median starting salary for UK graduates in 2025 is about £35 000, while the highest‑paying graduate roles are in investment banking (around £60 000), law (approximately £56 000) and consulting (about £50 000), reflecting the broader trend that despite a tighter overall job market, salaries continue to rise and international graduates from Edinburgh are well‑placed to enter these high‑growth, well‑remunerated occupations.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Edinburgh provides a dedicated international student support framework that includes the Student Immigration Service and Edinburgh Global, which together run orientation programmes, visa‑and‑passport guidance, the Edinburgh Buddies scheme and a range of social and cultural events to help newcomers settle; housing support is offered through university‑run accommodation located mainly within walking distance of the central campus and through detailed advice on securing private rent for those who prefer off‑site living; and the Careers Service runs a specific international‑student hub that gives tailored advice on graduate‑level job hunting, including guidance on UK work‑visa routes, opportunities to return to students’ home countries, and practical resources to improve employability and plan post‑graduation pathways. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Edinburgh hosts a vibrant international campus community with students from over 170 countries, offering more than 300 societies and clubs—including a dedicated Vietnamese Students’ Society that organizes cultural celebrations, language practice sessions and provides a friendly network for Vietnamese newcomers—and the Edinburgh University Students’ Association promotes participation in international activities, freshers’ events and diverse societies to help all overseas students integrate socially and academically; the university also provides clear safety guidance for students living in Edinburgh, advising them to avoid walking alone on quiet or dark streets after dark, to keep valuables and bags with them at all times, to lock doors and shut windows when leaving accommodation, to report any lost or stolen items to police, and to ensure private landlords are registered with the council, while encouraging newcomers to explore residential areas such as Bruntsfield, Marchmont, Newington and Morningside before signing tenancy agreements.
