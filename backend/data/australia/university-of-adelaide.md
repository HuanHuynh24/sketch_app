**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students applying to the University of Adelaide must first choose their desired undergraduate or postgraduate coursework program, check the specific intake (Semester 1 – typically February start, or Semester 2 – typically July start) and note the program‑specific closing date, which is usually between March and April for Semester 1 courses and between October and November for Semester 2 courses (some programs have earlier deadlines such as late June or early September). Once the deadline is confirmed, applicants prepare required documents (passport, English test results, academic transcripts, proof of professional qualifications where applicable, and any syllabus for credit transfer) and then submit their application either directly through the University’s Online Application System or, if required for their country or region, through an authorised University of Adelaide education agent. A non‑refundable application fee of AUD 150 applies to every coursework application (research degrees, fully online programmes and non‑award courses are exempt), and the fee is paid at the time of submission. **2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to the University of Adelaide must satisfy both academic and English‑language criteria that vary by qualification and country, with a general minimum GPA of 4.80 / 5 (Bangladesh), 13 / 20 (Sweden), 7.80 / 10 (Vietnam), 3.2 / 4 (Thailand) or an ATAR of at least 70 for Australian secondary studies; other systems are assessed against comparable benchmarks (e.g., a US high‑school diploma plus satisfactory SAT, ACT or AP results). All applicants must provide certified transcripts (học bạ) and meet the English requirement of IELTS Academic 6.0 overall or an equivalent score, though many programmes set higher thresholds. Specific courses may also require prerequisite subjects (e.g., mathematics for engineering, biology for health sciences) and, for creative fields such as design, architecture or visual arts, a portfolio and/or interview is mandatory. Where the applicant’s prior study does not meet direct entry standards, pathway or foundation programmes are available, and successful completion of at least six months of full‑time tertiary study (AQF Level 7 or above) at a recognised institution can also satisfy entry requirements.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Adelaide must meet degree‑specific English language standards, but the most common baseline for coursework programmes is an IELTS Academic overall score of 7.0 with no band below 7, a TOEFL iBT total of 94 with at least 24 in each skill, a PTE Academic overall score of 72 with a minimum of 72 in reading, writing, listening and speaking, a Cambridge C1/C2 overall of 185, or an OET grade of B in each component; while some undergraduate courses list a lower minimum of IELTS 6.0, the higher thresholds apply to most honours, graduate and professional degrees. The university does not list Duolingo as an accepted test, so it cannot be used for admission. English‑language waivers are available when applicants have completed a qualification taught wholly in English (e.g., an Australian Diploma, an International Baccalaureate diploma delivered in English, or a prior degree from an English‑medium institution) or can provide certified evidence of professional work experience conducted in English.

**4. Học phí**

- The University of Adelaide charges international students an application fee of A$120 (including GST) and a one‑time enrolment fee of A$320, with the standard full‑time tuition fee set at A$12,490 per year plus a services fee of A$2,200, resulting in a total annual cost of A$14,690 (all amounts in Australian dollars), and additionally requires a deposit of A$14,000 when the offer is accepted (credited toward the first semester), while other mandatory expenses include overseas student health cover (OSHC) and an estimated A$500 per year for textbooks and basic study materials.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at the University of Adelaide the typical annual living budget is roughly AU $26 000–$30 000, broken down as follows: on‑campus or university‑affiliated accommodation costs range from about AU $19 000 for a shared‑room ensuite to AU $28 000 for a large studio (all‑incl utilities and a weekly evening meal), with most students spending around AU $20 000–$22 000 per year; food expenses are estimated at AU $3 000–$4 000 for groceries and occasional dining; public‑transport travel (buses, trams and trains) averages AU $1 000–$1 500 annually; and the mandatory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is between AU $600 and AU $3 400 per year, with most plans costing about AU $2 000. Adding these items gives a total living cost of approximately AU $26 500–$30 000 per year.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to the University of Adelaide must provide evidence of satisfactory completion, within the last ten years, of prerequisite courses totalling 0.125 EFTSL in Statistics and Research Methods, 0.250 EFTSL in Human Anatomy and 0.250 EFTSL in Human Physiology, and they are also required to have completed appropriate SACE Stage 2 subjects such as Mathematical Methods (or equivalent) together with one science subject (Chemistry, Physics, Biology or Earth and Environmental Science) or a 20‑credit tertiary admission subject from the Mathematics or Science learning areas; credit points are measured in EFTSL (1 EFTSL = 100 credit points) and undergraduate programmes are structured to include no more than 60 units of first‑level courses (excluding common core) and at least 48 units at the highest level, following a scaffolded sequence of courses that increases in complexity, with each programme defining clear program learning outcomes that specify the knowledge, attributes and skills graduates must demonstrate, while each individual course has its own learning outcomes and any required prerequisite courses must be successfully completed before enrolment in subsequent courses.

- The University of Adelaide is ranked 82nd in the world and 8th in Australia in the 2026 QS World University Rankings, and it placed within the global top 100 in the broad subject areas of Arts and Humanities, Engineering and Technology, Life Sciences and Medicine, Natural Sciences, Social Sciences and Management, with a total of 22 individual subjects reaching the QS subject‑ranking top 100 in 2026; while the latest Times Higher Education World University Rankings (THE) list the university around the 90‑100 band globally, it remains one of Australia’s leading research‑intensive institutions and is fully accredited by the Australian Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) together with professional accreditation for specific programmes such as Engineers Australia for engineering, the Law Council of Australia for law and relevant bodies for health and education courses, and it is also recognised as a top student destination for international students, ranking #28 in the QS Best Student Cities 2025 and #11 in the world for livability.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa is the official pathway for international students to study full‑time at a CRICOS‑registered provider such as the University of Adelaide, and applicants must hold a valid Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) before lodging the visa; to satisfy the financial capacity requirement they must demonstrate enough funds to cover their tuition fees for at least 12 months, a minimum of about AUD 21,000–24,500 per year for living expenses for a single student (with additional amounts of roughly AUD 10,400 per year for a partner and AUD 4,450 per year for each dependent child), the cost of a return air ticket and, where applicable, school fees for any school‑age children, while all visa holders are required to maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the whole period of stay, ensuring the cover starts on the day they arrive in Australia; work rights under the visa allow up to 40 hours of paid work per fortnight during scheduled course periods and unlimited hours during recognized semester breaks, with research postgraduate students generally permitted unlimited work throughout their course. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the main pathway for University of Adelaide graduates to remain in Australia after completing their studies, offering unrestricted work rights and the ability to study further while staying temporarily; most international students apply under the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which grants a stay of usually two years for a bachelor’s degree, two to three years for a master’s degree and up to four years for a PhD, with Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders eligible for five years, and additional extensions of one to two years available for those who have studied, lived and worked in regional areas, while from 1 July 2023 graduates whose qualification appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply for a further two‑year extension through ImmiAccount at no extra fee, provided the visa is lodged within six months of course completion and all health, character and English‑language requirements are met.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Adelaide offers a comprehensive suite of internship, co‑op and placement options for international students through its Work‑Integrated Learning (WIL) framework, embedding industry‑partnered projects, professional practice units and structured placement periods in many undergraduate and postgraduate programmes; these experiences are supported by dedicated career‑services staff who help students secure roles with employers ranging from engineering firms, health and biomedical organisations, finance and consulting companies, legal practices, government agencies and technology start‑ups, and the university’s 2023 Graduate Outcome Survey shows that about 93 % of graduates were employed or in further study within four months of graduation, reflecting strong graduate‑employment outcomes across a wide array of career fields. Lương & thị trường: University of Adelaide graduates enjoy strong labour‑market outcomes, with roughly 92 percent securing full‑time work within three years and a median annual salary of about $80,000, while postgraduate coursework and research graduates earn around $90,000–$95,000. The highest‑paying fields for undergraduate entrants are dentistry (≈$100,000), medicine (≈$76,000), teacher education (≈$72,000) and engineering (≈$70,000), and the median starting salary for all undergraduates is about $65,000. Jobs and Skills Australia data shows that the occupations with the greatest demand for recent graduates—including international graduates—are software and applications programmers, construction managers, and professionals in the scientific and technical services sector, with health‑related roles such as nurses, allied health practitioners and medical scientists also featuring prominently; these sectors consistently post the largest net job gains and offer salaries that exceed the overall graduate median.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Adelaide’s International Student Support office assists international students with a range of services—including visa and Confirmation of Enrolment advice, OSHC and health‑care guidance, personal‑issue referrals and one‑on‑one appointments with dedicated advisors (call +61 8 8313 5208 or toll‑free 1800 061 459); orientation for new international students is delivered through Connect Week (9‑13 Feb) and O‑Week (16‑20 Feb) featuring a mandatory International Welcome Reception, a Degree Welcome session, and social excursions such as a day trip to Victor Harbor and a beach outing at Glenelg, all run by the International Student Support team; housing support is provided through the same office, which can refer students to on‑campus residences and off‑campus accommodation services and advise on rental contracts, budgeting and tenancy rights; and career development for international students is offered by Adelaide Careers, which runs workshops, CV and interview clinics, employer networking events and specialised guidance on work‑rights, graduate employment and post‑study visa pathways. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Adelaide provides a well‑structured campus‑life experience for international students that combines robust safety services—such as a 24‑hour security escort, personal‑safety and incident‑reporting systems, locker hire at Hub Central on levels 3 and 4, SecureIT advice on digital security, and emergency contacts (000 for life‑threatening situations and a dedicated International Students line at +61 8 8313 7335) —with a vibrant network of more than 150 student clubs and societies, including a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association that organises Tet celebrations, language‑exchange meet‑ups, cultural workshops and peer‑support programmes; other multicultural, sport, arts and academic clubs are easily accessible through the university’s Campus Life portal, and all students can join the Student Association’s Buddy Programme or attend regular social events at the International Student Hub to build community, make friends and feel safe and supported while studying in Adelaide.
