### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply (UCAS)**

- Để nộp hồ sơ du học tại University of Bristol, sinh viên quốc tế phải đăng ký qua hệ thống UCAS (mã trường BRISL B78); thời gian nộp đơn cho các chương trình đại học phụ thuộc vào môn học – các ngành Y, Nha khoa, Thú y có hạn chế nộp cuối tháng 10 năm trước năm nhập học (đầu kỳ mùa thu), các ngành còn lại phải nộp trước cuối tháng 1 đối với người nộp từ Anh và trước cuối tháng 6 đối với người nộp quốc tế để được xét xét duyệt cho kỳ nhập học mùa thu; một số chương trình có thể mở tuyển sinh vào kỳ mùa xuân và thời hạn nộp sẽ được công bố trên trang mô tả khóa học. Phí đăng ký UCAS cho sinh viên quốc tế hiện tại là khoảng £29 (có thể thay đổi hàng năm), trong khi các chương trình sau đại học (master, PhD) thường yêu cầu trả phí đơn riêng được nêu trên trang đầu của mẫu đơn và có thể được miễn nếu đáp ứng tiêu chí miễn giảm; quy trình nộp đơn bao gồm: tạo tài khoản UCAS hoặc tài khoản ứng dụng sau đại học, nhập đầy đủ thông tin cá nhân, học vấn và tham chiếu, tải lên bản sao bằng chứng về trình độ tiếng Anh, trả phí (hoặc yêu cầu miễn phí), nộp đơn và sau đó theo dõi trạng thái qua cổng thông tin trực tuyến cho đến khi nhận quyết định tuyển sinh.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to the University of Bristol must meet the standard UK entry thresholds for each programme, with A‑level offers typically ranging from AAA to AAB (e.g., most science courses require AAA, many humanities accept AAB) and a minimum of a 4‑grade (4p) in each subject, except Mathematics which must be a 5‑grade (5p) to equal A‑level Maths; for the International Baccalaureate the usual baseline is an overall score of 32 points with at least 18 points at Higher Level, though competitive courses often require 36–40 points and may stipulate 7 HL points for a grade A, 6 for A‑ and 5 for B‑level subjects, while U.S. applicants need a high‑school diploma with a GPA of at least 3.2 (3.4 for highly competitive programmes) plus three additional qualifications such as AP or SAT scores. Specific subject requirements are listed on each course’s entry page, and for creative disciplines (e.g., Architecture, Fine Art, Design) a portfolio is required, whereas some professional programmes (such as Medicine, Dentistry and certain health‑science degrees) may also include an interview as part of the selection process.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to the University of Bristol must demonstrate a minimum CEFR B2 level, which is equivalent to an overall IELTS Academic score of 5.5 in each band, but most programmes use the higher “Profile C” standard of IELTS 6.5 with no element below 6.5; for TOEFL iBT the required score is 88 overall (reading 22, listening 21, speaking 23, writing 22) for tests taken up to 20 January 2026 and, from 21 January 2026, an overall 4.5 with 4.5 in each component; Pearson PTE Academic must be 67 overall with no component below 67; Cambridge C2 Proficiency (grade C) or C1 Advanced (grade C or level B2 with 176 overall) are also accepted. Duolingo English Test is not listed among the approved SELT qualifications, so it cannot be used to meet the requirement. Applicants who have completed a degree or qualification taught entirely in English in an English‑speaking country (or in an English‑medium institution listed by the university) within the past seven years are exempt from submitting an English‑language test, provided the qualification is recognized and the institution appears on the university’s approved list; otherwise, candidates who are short of the required test scores by up to 0.5 points can fulfil the requirement by completing the appropriate six‑week pre‑sessional English for Academic Purposes course before starting their programme.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at the University of Bristol pay tuition of £22,500 per academic year for undergraduate programmes (2025/26) and, for postgraduate taught courses, a tuition fee of £16,300 per year (2023/24) with research‑based postgraduate fees set at £8,150 per year, meaning the total annual cost (including tuition and typical ancillary charges) is roughly £32,200 for taught postgraduate study and £16,100 for research postgraduate study; the application fee is £70 for postgraduate applicants (and £20 for undergraduate applications via UCAS), and self‑funded international postgraduate students are also required to pay a deposit of £2,000 (with a small registration charge of about £100), all amounts being in GBP.

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: International students applying for a UK Student visa to study at the University of Bristol must demonstrate that they have sufficient funds to cover both their tuition fees for the first year (or the whole course if it is less than a year) and their living costs, which are set at £1,171 per month for up to a maximum of nine months (£10,539); the total amount required is calculated as tuition fees plus the monthly maintenance amount multiplied by the number of months of study (rounded up), minus any tuition already paid to the university, and this sum must have been held in an accessible bank or building‑society account every day for a consecutive 28‑day period, with evidence supplied as a bank statement or a bank letter dated no more than 31 days before the visa application, or, if the funding comes from an official sponsor such as a government, the British Council, an international organisation, or a UK university, a letter from that sponsor confirming full coverage of tuition and living costs can be used instead; applicants who have been living in the UK on valid immigration permission for 12 months or more at the time of application are exempt from the financial requirement. Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): The UK Graduate visa route, also known as the Graduate Route, allows international students who have successfully completed an eligible degree at a UK higher‑education institution such as the University of Bristol to remain in the UK after graduation without needing a sponsor: graduates with a bachelor's or master’s degree can stay for up to two years, while PhD graduates are granted three years, and they may work at any skill level, look for employment, start a business or be self‑employed; to be eligible the student must hold a valid Student visa (or Tier 4) at the time of application, have received a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from the university, have completed the course and received the final award, and must apply for the Graduate visa within three months of the date the results are formally recorded (or within six months for certain postgraduate programmes), with the ability to bring eligible dependents and later switch to a Skilled Worker visa if a sponsoring job is secured.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Bristol boasts one of the strongest graduate outcomes in the UK, with 95.8 percent of its graduates in full‑ or part‑time employment within twelve months of finishing their degree (2021) and 94 percent in employment or further study fifteen months after graduation (2024), placing the university joint‑7th in the UK and 49th worldwide for graduate employability; its graduates are consistently sought after by leading employers—including Google, Amazon, Pfizer, HSBC, the NHS, the BBC and the Bank of England—with the institution ranking among the top five most targeted by the Times Top 100 UK graduate employers and attracting around 350 employers each year to on‑campus recruitment events, while a robust suite of work‑experience options such as the University’s SME internship scheme, paid placement years (including a “year in industry” option) and the Bristol PLUS Award enable both domestic and international students to gain high‑quality, paid internships, develop professional networks and build sector‑specific skills that translate into careers across technology, finance, consulting, pharmaceuticals, public service and creative industries in over 190 countries worldwide. Lương & thị trường: The University of Bristol reports that recent graduates earn a median starting salary of roughly £31,000, with higher earnings in high‑demand fields such as computer science and engineering (around £34‑£35 k) and lower but still competitive salaries in humanities and social sciences (about £27‑£29 k); the labour market currently shows strong demand for international graduates in professional services, technology and data analytics, engineering and infrastructure projects, finance and consulting, as well as health‑care and life‑science roles, with employers emphasising advanced digital, analytical and problem‑solving skills and offering visa‑sponsorship positions that often command salaries at the upper end of the graduate pay range.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Bristol’s International Office, based on the 3rd floor of 31 Great George Street in the Global Engagement Division, coordinates a comprehensive support programme for international students that includes pre‑arrival guidance, a welcome orientation week with campus tours, information sessions and social events, as well as ongoing practical assistance such as help with UK visa queries, academic advice, health and wellbeing resources, and dedicated support for finding university‑managed accommodation through its student housing services; while the International Office does not run its own career service, it works closely with the university’s Careers Service to ensure that international students receive tailored employability advice, work‑experience opportunities and post‑graduation guidance, and the International Office can be contacted by phone +44 117 205 2644 or email support@international.bristol.ac.uk for any of these services. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Bristol provides a safe and supportive environment for international students, with the city recognised for its Purple Flag award for safety, university‑run security services, a 24‑hour residential life team and Student Support Centres that offer pastoral care and organise social events throughout the year; on campus students can join more than 70 sports clubs through Bristol SU, take part in free B:Active sessions, and connect with a wide range of cultural and interest‑based societies, including a dedicated Vietnamese Society that brings together Vietnamese students and anyone interested in Vietnamese language, food and traditions through language workshops, cultural festivals and regular social gatherings, while additional support is available via the Global Lounge, Multifaith Chaplaincy and NHS‑linked health services to ensure a welcoming, inclusive and secure university experience.
