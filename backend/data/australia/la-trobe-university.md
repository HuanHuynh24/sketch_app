**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- La Trobe University requires international applicants to hold an Australian‑equivalent bachelor degree (or recognised overseas qualification) with at least a C grade (≈60 % GPA) in the final year for most postgraduate programmes, and for the MBA specifically a minimum 60 % GPA plus three years of relevant managerial experience; applicants without an undergraduate degree can still be considered for professional entry if they have at least eight years of senior‑level managerial experience, and must meet the English language requirement of an overall IELTS Academic score of 6.5 with no band lower than 6.0 (higher scores may be required for certain professional or research courses). All candidates must submit a certified copy of their passport, an official academic transcript (học bạ) showing completed subjects and grades, and proof of English proficiency; additional documentation such as a portfolio or interview may be requested for courses with creative or design components, while specific subject prerequisites and any required entrance exams are listed on each course’s “Entry requirements & pathways” page and must be met before the application deadline.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- La Trobe University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency at the levels set by the Department of Home Affairs: for foundation programmes a minimum overall IELTS 5.5 (no band lower than 5.0), for diploma courses IELTS 6.0 (no band lower than 5.0), for bachelor degrees IELTS 6.0 with no individual band below 6.0, and for postgraduate coursework, research and HDR programmes IELTS 6.5 with no band below 6.0; equivalent scores are accepted for other tests—TOEFL iBT 64 overall (minimum 13 in each component), PTE Academic 50 overall (minimum 45 in each component), and Duolingo English Test 105 overall (minimum 100 in the reading‑listening‑writing‑speaking composite); applicants who have completed at least two years of full‑time study or the equivalent in an English‑medium institution, or who hold a qualification such as the Cambridge C1 Advanced (CAE) 169 with no component below 169, the Cambridge Proficiency (CPE) 176 with no component below 169, or other recognized English‑language qualifications, may be granted a waiver from the standard test requirements.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student visa (subclass 500) is the official visa for international students, including those enrolled at La Trobe University, and it requires applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity to cover tuition, living expenses (generally at least AUD 21,041 per year for a single student), travel costs and the mandatory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire duration of their stay; the visa permits work of up to 48 hours per fortnight while classes are in session and unlimited hours during recognised school holidays, with higher degree research students (master’s by research or doctoral) exempt from the work‑hour limit, and visa holders must maintain enrolment, satisfactory attendance and progress, keep their OSHC active, and comply with all conditions set out by the Department of Home Affairs. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) – specifically the Post‑Higher Education Work stream – lets international students who have finished a degree at an Australian CRICOS‑registered institution such as La Trobe University remain in Australia for typically two to three years (the exact period depends on the level of the qualification, with bachelor's and master’s graduates usually granted three years and doctoral graduates up to four) and provides unrestricted work rights, allowing you to take any full‑time employment in any sector while you look for skilled experience or a pathway to permanent residency; holders of Hong Kong or British National Overseas passports are eligible for up to five years, and you may also include eligible family members in your application.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: La Trobe University offers extensive work‑integrated learning opportunities for both domestic and international students, including industry placements, internships, co‑ops and a university‑wide Industry Placement Minor that can be taken as a single‑semester or multi‑semester experience, with many placements also available online through platforms such as Practera; in 2023 more than one‑quarter of all students completed a placement—a rise of over 20 % from pre‑COVID levels—and the Career Ready Advantage employability program engaged over 23,000 students, while graduate outcomes are among the strongest in the country, with the Graduate Outcomes Survey reporting a full‑time employment rate of 89.2 % for graduates (95.1 % overall employment) and almost 95 % of undergraduate alumni employed three years after graduation, earning a median postgraduate coursework salary of $104,400 and ranking La Trobe as the top Victorian university for employment outcomes. Lương & thị trường: In Australia, international graduates earn lower median salaries than their domestic peers—about $68,000 annually for undergraduate internationals versus $75,000 for domestics, and roughly $30,000 less at the postgraduate coursework level—and their full‑time employment rates differ markedly by field, with medicine and rehabilitation posting the highest rates (around 90 %) and creative arts the lowest (about 41.6 %); Jobs and Skills Australia highlights mining engineering, medical studies and general medicine as the occupations with the highest starting salaries and the fastest income growth (approximately 35 % increase within five years), while La Trobe University reports that its graduates enjoy strong employment outcomes, ranking top in Victoria for postgraduate employment and aligning its programmes with high‑demand sectors such as health, engineering and education, indicating that international graduates in health‑related and engineering disciplines face the strongest labour‑market demand and most competitive salaries, whereas those in creative and some environmental areas experience lower employment rates and earnings.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: La Trobe’s International Student Office delivers a comprehensive support package for overseas students that includes dedicated orientation programmes—both live and on‑demand online sessions as well as face‑to‑face events such as ASK La Trobe Welcome Zones and a Transition@La Trobe Facebook community—plus a specialised International Student Support team and specialist advisers (International, Accommodation and Accessibility) who provide one‑to‑one guidance on study plans, visa matters and campus living; housing assistance is coordinated through the Accommodation Services office located in Menzies College (open 9 am–7 pm weekdays, 9 am–5 pm weekends) with an after‑hours Night Manager service (5 pm–9 am) to help with arrivals, check‑ins and transport needs such as obtaining a Myki card; career development is offered through the university’s Careers & Opportunities hub, featuring the Career Ready programme, access to careers advisers, work‑integrated learning and placement opportunities, leadership and volunteering options and resources like LinkedIn Learning to help international students prepare for the Australian job market. Đời sống & môi trường: La Trobe University’s seven campuses – the main Melbourne bushland campus (14 km from the city centre) and regional sites in Bendigo, Albury‑Wodonga, Shepparton, Mildura, Ballarat and Sydney – provide a safe, welcoming environment with 24‑hour security, campus safety officers, well‑lit pathways, emergency call points and a dedicated international‑student support team that assists with orientation, wellbeing and any safety concerns; the Melbourne campus also boasts Australia’s only on‑campus wildlife sanctuary, extensive sports fields, cafés and modern accommodation options. International students enjoy a vibrant multicultural life through the Student Union “Agora”, which runs over 200 clubs and societies covering sports, arts, academic interests, volunteering and cultural groups, including a Vietnamese Student Association that organises cultural festivals, language‑exchange sessions, social events and a supportive network for Vietnamese students, who can also connect via university‑hosted Vietnamese‑student pages and social‑media groups on Zalo, WeChat and Facebook, ensuring they have ample opportunities to engage in campus life, make friends and feel safe while studying in Australia.
