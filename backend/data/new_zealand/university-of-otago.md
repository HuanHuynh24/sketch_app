### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- University of Otago accepts international applicants for both its main semester 1 (February‑March start) and semester 2 (July start) intakes; the standard online application window opens on 1 May each year and closes on 30 April the following year, covering the July (semester 2) intake, while the same period also serves the February (semester 1) intake for the next academic year, although some programmes (e.g., teaching, oral health, dental technology, postgraduate dentistry) have separate deadlines and PhD or thesis‑only Master’s applications can be submitted year‑round. The application fee for undergraduate international students is NZ$115 (higher fees apply for certain postgraduate courses), and the application process consists of creating an account on Otago’s online apply portal, completing personal and academic details, selecting the desired programme, uploading required documents (transcripts, English‑language test results, passport copy, etc.), paying the application fee, and submitting the form; after submission, applicants receive an acknowledgement, await a conditional offer, and if accepted must pay the tuition deposit, arrange a student visa and health insurance, and follow the university’s enrolment instructions.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Submitting your application](https://www.otago.ac.nz/study/enrolment/submitting) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Apply to enrol at the University of Otago](https://www.otago.ac.nz/apply) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[How to apply](https://www.otago.ac.nz/international/future-students/how-to-apply) (Cao, score 1.00)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students must hold a recognised university entrance qualification and meet the English language requirements; for New Zealand the standard pathway is the National Certificate of Educational Achievement (NCEA) where a minimum of 80 Level 3 credits is required, including at least 10 credits in each of English, mathematics (or a suitable alternative) and a science subject for most programmes, with higher credit totals or specific subject credits required for science‑, health‑, engineering‑ and commerce‑related degrees, while creative and design courses often ask for a portfolio and may include an interview; the University also accepts the International Baccalaureate Diploma with at least 24 points, and for some professional programmes (e.g., medicine, dentistry, dentistry, architecture, fine arts) additional assessments such as a portfolio, interview or aptitude test are required, and all applicants must provide official transcripts showing their grades (GPA or NCEA points) and, where applicable, evidence of meeting any subject‑specific prerequisites.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- All international students must demonstrate competence in written and spoken English before being admitted to any University of Otago programme, and the evidence must normally be from a test or study completed within the two‑year period before the programme starts (extendable to five years if the student can show continued immersion in an English‑speaking environment). The university accepts a range of approved tests: IELTS Academic (including IELTS Online) – minimum overall band 6.5 with no band below 6.0 for postgraduate study (undergraduate thresholds are slightly lower but not specified here); TOEFL iBT – minimum total 95 with at least 22 in writing (paper‑based TOEFL 587 with TWE 4.5 is also accepted); Pearson Test of English Academic – overall score 64 with no communicative‑skill score below 57; Duolingo English Test – accepted (specific score not listed, but it must meet the university’s minimum); Cambridge C1 Advanced (CAE) or C2 Proficiency (CPE) – overall 176 with no band below 169; APIEL – grade 4 or higher; and the New Zealand Certificate in English Language (NZCEL) or other approved pathway assessments. **4. Học phí**

- International students at the University of Otago pay tuition in New Zealand dollars that varies by programme: most undergraduate courses (e.g., Arts, Business, Humanities) are charged at about NZ $30,000 per year, science‑based degrees are around NZ $33,000 per year, while the professional Medicine programme is approximately NZ $85,000 per year. In addition to tuition, students must pay a mandatory Student Services and Amenities Fee of roughly NZ $150 each year and purchase the required Overseas Student Health Cover, which averages about NZ $650 annually; these amounts bring the typical total annual cost to about NZ $30,800 for Arts/Business, NZ $34,800 for Science, and NZ $86,800 for Medicine. The university’s online application fee is NZ $150 for all international applicants.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For a typical international student at the University of Otago, the university advises budgeting between NZ $22,000 and NZ $27,000 for a 40‑week academic year to cover living expenses. This amount includes accommodation (approximately NZ $8,000–$12,000, depending on whether you choose university‑run halls, private rentals or homestays), food and groceries (around NZ $4,000–$5,000), local transport such as bus passes and occasional trips (about NZ $1,200), and the compulsory overseas student health cover (roughly NZ $500–$700). When combined, these components bring the total annual cost of living for an international student in Dunedin to the recommended range of NZ $22,000–$27,000.

- International students wishing to enrol at the University of Otago must satisfy a set of academic and English‑language prerequisites that are measured in credit points and grades rather than a single “Môn” requirement: for example, successful completion of the International Baccalaureate with at least 24 points, Cambridge International A‑levels or Pearson Edexcel A‑levels totalling a minimum of 120 New Zealand Tariff points with a D grade or better in subjects from at least three different syllabus groups, or a University of Otago Foundation Studies Certificate with a grade of C or higher in both Academic English papers; alternatively, the NCUK International Foundation Year (minimum CCC) or a recognised pathway diploma (Arts, Commerce, Science) can be used to gain entry and, when completed, provide credit that typically allows progression directly into the second year of a relevant bachelor's degree. ## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Otago in New Zealand is ranked 197th equal in the 2026 QS World University Rankings, with its sports‑related subjects placed 22nd globally and anatomy and physiology 36th, and it boasts nine subjects in the top 200, eight between 201‑300 and six in the top 500; in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings it typically falls in the 250‑300 band, the university is fully accredited by the New Zealand Qualifications Authority and its professional programmes hold accreditations from bodies such as the Medical Council of New Zealand, the New Zealand Dental Council and the New Zealand Nursing Council, and international students comprise roughly 15 % of its total enrolment.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: A fee‑paying student visa for an international student at the University of Otago requires evidence that you have been offered a place (or a Confirmation of Enrolment for returning students), proof that you have paid your tuition fees for the first year (or can demonstrate the ability to pay them, such as a loan, scholarship or sponsor’s guarantee), and proof of sufficient living funds of at least NZ $20,000 per year (or NZ $1,667 per month for courses under 12 months) from a genuine source, which may be shown through recent bank statements, a sponsor’s financial undertaking (INZ 1014) or a guarantor’s declaration. You must also provide identity documents (passport valid for at least three months beyond your intended departure), and, if applicable, evidence that any practical work experience is part of your course and that you have permission to work; fee‑paying students are allowed to work up to 25 hours per week during term time and full‑time during scheduled holidays, with full work rights if the course includes a work‑integrated component. If you are being sponsored, the sponsor must supply recent bank statements to demonstrate they can cover living costs and, if needed, a return travel ticket, and a guarantor must complete a Financial Undertake​ment for a Student form and provide proof of their own funds. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of Otago who have completed a qualification at New Zealand Qualification Framework level 7 or higher (such as a bachelor’s, master’s or PhD) are eligible for the Post‑Study Work Visa, provided they apply within three months of their student visa expiring, meet health and character requirements and hold a qualification recognised by Immigration New Zealand; the visa grants open work rights for up to three years. Graduates who hold a level‑7 Graduate Diploma in addition to a bachelor’s degree (whether earned in New Zealand or overseas) are also eligible under the extended rules introduced in late 2026, receiving a visa for the length of their graduate‑diploma study up to a maximum of one year, after which they may transition to other work‑based visas.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Otago reports that roughly 96 % of its graduates secure full‑time employment or enrol in further study within a short period after completing their degrees, with the 2024 annual report showing a 95.6 % progression rate, and 92 % of international graduates expressing satisfaction with their overall university experience; the university’s professional programmes—including health sciences, engineering, business and agriculture—integrate structured internships, co‑op placements or industry projects that are coordinated through Otago’s Career Services, and these work‑integrated learning opportunities are a key driver of the strong graduate outcomes, helping international students transition quickly into relevant careers across New Zealand and abroad. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from the University of Otago experience very high full‑time employment rates—around 90 %—in health‑related programmes such as Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy and Rehabilitation, with median full‑time annual salaries typically ranging from NZ$70 000 to NZ$80 000 for medical graduates and NZ$55 000 to NZ$65 000 for other health professions, whereas graduates in fields like Creative Arts show markedly lower full‑time employment (about 42 %) and lower median earnings of roughly NZ$40 000; labour‑market demand is strongest for occupations that align with New Zealand’s skilled‑work‑visa priorities, notably resident medical officers, accountants, early‑childhood teachers, retail managers, café/restaurant managers, marketing specialists, ICT customer‑support officers, secondary‑school teachers, developer programmers and software engineers, and among the top ten occupations for recent graduates, nursing and midwifery professionals, finance and sales associate professionals and specialised managers hold the largest shares, with early‑career in‑field median incomes around NZ$33 000–NZ$35 000 and substantially higher salaries for those entering health‑sector roles.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Otago’s International Student Office requires all new incoming international students to register for and attend a mandatory New International Student Enrolment and Orientation seminar before completing enrolment, with seminars held during orientation week for semester‑1 and semester‑2 entrants (registration opens in November for semester 1 and April for semester 2) and PhD or thesis‑by‑research master’s students meeting an International Student Adviser on arrival; the orientation provides information on academic processes, health and safety, support services and accommodation options, and the office also offers ongoing housing support by advising on on‑campus residences, private rentals and homestays, assisting with tenancy contracts and providing emergency housing referrals, while collaborating with Otago’s Career Service to deliver tailored career workshops covering CV and interview preparation, New Zealand work‑rights for students, post‑study work visas, internship opportunities and employer networking events. Đời sống & môi trường: At the University of Otago, international students can become part of a vibrant campus life through the Otago University Students' Association (OUSA) Clubs and Societies Centre, which hosts more than 150 student‑led groups—including the Vietnamese Students Association that organizes language‑exchange evenings, Tet festivals and a peer‑support network for Vietnamese newcomers—while residential colleges, sports clubs, volunteering opportunities and academic societies provide additional ways to socialize and integrate; campus safety is overseen by a dedicated Campus Safety and Security team that operates 24‑hour patrols, key‑card access, CCTV, emergency call points and collaborates with Student Health and the International Office to respond to any incidents, and the International Office together with OUSA offers orientation programs, mentorship, counselling and other support services specifically designed for international students.
