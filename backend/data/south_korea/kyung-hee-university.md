**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International undergraduate applicants to Kyung Hee University must submit an original, translated and notarized high‑school transcript (học bạ) and any prior degree certificates together with an apostille or Korean consular authentication, and demonstrate a cumulative GPA of at least 3.0 on a 4.0 scale (or the equivalent minimum required by specific scholarships, e.g., 2.5 GPA); they must also provide proof of language proficiency such as TOPIK Level 4 or higher, TOEFL iBT 80 or IELTS 6.0, and meet any subject prerequisites (môn yêu cầu) stipulated by the chosen department (for example mathematics for engineering or biology for health‑related majors); applicants to design, art, music or other creative fields are required to submit a portfolio and may be required to attend an audition, and many departments schedule an interview (or audition) as part of the admission evaluation, with all documents needing to be original, notarized and apostilled before the deadline.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- Kyung Hee University requires international exchange students to meet language‑proficiency standards that differ by track: for the Korean‑language track, a minimum TOPIK Level 5 (or higher) is required beginning in the 2025‑26 academic year (or, for Korean‑language majors, a letter of proficiency from the department dean); for the English‑language track, applicants must provide evidence of English ability, typically TOEFL iBT 80 or higher (some notices list 82) or IELTS 6.5 or above, with IELTS 5.5 or higher accepted as a waiver for students from English‑speaking countries; the university also accepts TEPS 600 or higher, and, in recent practice, recognizes other standardized English tests such as PTE Academic and the Duolingo English Test as alternatives to TOEFL/IELTS for admission.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Kyung Hee University welcomes international students through a range of exchange and short‑term programs that require enrolment at an accredited university outside Korea, a valid English‑language Certificate of Enrollment (or comparable proof of study), and, for most undergraduate exchange tracks, a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or higher together with satisfactory language grades and, where applicable, an interview; these students may also enroll in the Global Virtual School Program where each online course carries 2 academic credits, is delivered in 10‑lecture modules of 75 minutes each, and is graded on a Pass/Non‑Pass basis provided the student completes at least 90 % of the content. Credit earned abroad is fully transferable back to Kyung Hee, ensuring continuity of academic progress, and the curriculum is designed to include major‑based, language, athletics, culture, and internship components that develop language proficiency, intercultural competence, and a global perspective; learning outcomes emphasize enhanced language abilities, broadened global outlook, and readiness for further study or professional work in an international context.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Kyung Hee University in South Korea hosts more than 5,700 full‑time international students from about 65 countries (approximately 4,300 inbound and 1,900 outbound exchange participants each year); in the 2025 QS World University Rankings it is placed 328th globally (6th in Korea) and achieved record subject performance, ranking 42nd worldwide in Hospitality & Leisure Management and placing four programmes—Hotel & Tourism, Linguistics, Petrochemicals and Dentistry—in the global top 100, while in the 2025 THE World University Rankings it stands 252nd worldwide and 5th among Korean comprehensive universities, with THE subject rankings of 15th globally in Hospitality Management (2nd in Korea) and 33rd in Telecommunication Engineering (1st in Korea); the university is fully accredited by the Korean Ministry of Education and its individual programmes hold international accreditations such as AACSB for its business school and ABET for its engineering programmes.

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): For a D‑2 student visa to study at Kyung Hee University you must first obtain a Certificate of Admission from the university and then submit a visa application at your home‑country Korean embassy or, if you are already in Korea, change status through the HiKorea portal (hikorea.go.kr). The immigration office requires proof that you can support yourself financially for the duration of your studies, typically a recent bank statement showing a balance of roughly USD 9 000–10 000 (or the equivalent in KRW) held for at least one month, a scholarship confirmation letter if you receive a KGSP or university grant, or a tuition‑payment receipt together with the bank statement; this financial evidence is also needed when you later apply for a visa extension. After receiving your Alien Registration Card (ARC) you may work part‑time on a Time‑Limited Employment Permit, which allows up to 20 hours per week during regular semesters (and up to 40 hours per week during university holidays) provided you have a valid ARC, a letter of consent from Kyung Hee University’s International Office, and the work‑permit approval issued through HiKorea. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): International graduates who have earned a bachelor's, master’s or doctoral degree from Kyung Hee University (or any Korean university) may convert their student visa (D‑2) to a Job‑Seeker visa (D‑10) within 30 days of the D‑2 expiration and are eligible for a standard one‑year stay that can be renewed as long as they remain unemployed or continue to search for work; financial‑proof requirements are waived for Korean‑university graduates and the visa is exempt from the points test, though holders must still submit their passport, ARC, degree certificate and a brief statement of job‑search activities, and may engage in paid internships that must be reported within 14 days; after securing a qualifying employment offer the D‑10 holder can have an employer sponsor a change to the Skilled Worker visa (E‑7), which requires a contract in a field matching one of the E‑1 to E‑7 categories and, if the applicant is under 29 and a graduate of a QS‑top‑200 university, the points requirement is also waived, allowing a straightforward transition to E‑7 and, after three years on E‑7, eligibility to apply for the F‑2 (resident) visa for longer‑term residency.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Kyung Hee University actively supports international students in securing internships and practical training through its Global Citizenship Education program and partnerships with United Nations agencies, NGOs and corporate partners; each year roughly a dozen students undertake three‑to‑six‑month placements such as with UNIDO, the World Food Programme, the International Organization for Migration and the UN Human Rights Council, and the university annually selects about ten undergraduate and graduate students for additional three‑to‑six‑month internships abroad, while its broader career‑service network connects students to a wide range of co‑op and placement opportunities in fields including international relations, business and finance, engineering, health sciences, arts and design, and media; as a result, around 90 % of Kyung Hee graduates—both domestic and international—secure employment or continue to graduate‑level study within six months of graduation, with many entering roles in multinational corporations, government ministries, NGOs, research institutes and startups. Lương & thị trường: In South Korea, recent graduates typically start with an annual salary of roughly 35 million won, with those entering high‑demand fields such as finance, consulting, ICT and multinational trade often earning 40 million won or more, while graduates who pursue education or public‑sector roles begin nearer 30 million won; Kyung Hee University’s official graduate outcome data indicates that both Korean and international alumni are most frequently hired into three occupational clusters that are experiencing strong labour‑market demand: (1) education and research positions such as university faculty and research institute staff, (2) roles in international trade, finance and overseas investment with private companies or public agencies, and (3) market‑oriented jobs that involve information, policy, economics and business practice linked to foreign regions—including media, advertising and communications—sectors that align with the broader trend of rising employment for foreign graduates, which reached about one‑third of the 15,000 international graduates in 2024 and is projected to continue growing as Korean firms seek multilingual, globally‑oriented talent.
