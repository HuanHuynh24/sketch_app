**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Université PSL requires proof of English proficiency for most programmes taught in English, typically an IELTS overall score of 6.5 (with no band below 6.0) or a TOEFL‑iBT total of 90 points (with at least 20 in each section); equivalent scores are accepted for PTE Academic (minimum 61 overall) and Duolingo English Test (minimum 115). Several master’s and bachelor‑level tracks list the requirement simply as “English C1”, which can be satisfied by any recognized test meeting the above thresholds. However, a language‑test certificate is not mandatory for every programme: if you have completed a previous degree in which the medium of instruction was English, you may obtain a waiver by providing an official letter from your former university confirming that the programme was taught entirely in English. For programmes that are fully taught in English, French proficiency is generally not required, though bilingual programmes may still ask for a B2/C1 French level. Waivers are evaluated case‑by‑case through the admissions portal, and applicants should upload the supporting documentation (test scores or university letter) with their application.

**4. Học phí**

- For international students at Université PSL the tuition fee is the same as for EU students and is set at €254 per academic year for programmes that award a national diploma (e.g., a Master’s degree) and €170 per year for a national Bachelor’s degree; the mandatory CVEC (Contribution Vie Étudiante et de Campus) is €92 per year, so a typical two‑year Master’s programme costs €254 × 2 = €508 in tuition plus €92 × 2 = €184 in CVEC, for a total of €692, all expressed in euros; there is no separate registration fee listed, and the CVEC is the principal additional charge for students.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To study at Université PSL you must obtain a long‑stay student visa (VLS‑TS “étudiant”) via the official France‑Visas portal after your Campus France appointment, and the visa must be validated with OFII within three months of arrival; the required dossier includes the original admission letter from PSL, a housing attestation (or hotel reservation for the first weeks or a host‑letter) confirming your accommodation in France, proof of sufficient financial resources – typically a recent bank statement showing at least €6 150 for a 10‑month academic year, a scholarship award letter, or a guarantor’s “attestation de prise en charge” together with the guarantor’s tax returns and proof of income – and proof of health insurance covering you in France, either a private policy accepted by French authorities or evidence that you will enrol in the French social security system on arrival, together with the visa fee receipt and a valid passport; for PhD students or researchers the “Passeport Talent Chercheur” VLS may be used, requiring a hosting agreement approved by both the university and the French consular authorities. Sau tốt nghiệp: The “Job seeker / new business creator” residence permit – formerly called the Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour (APS) and now issued as a temporary Carte de séjour “recherche d’emploi / création d’entreprise” – is a one‑year, non‑renewable residence authorisation that allows non‑EU graduates of a recognised French higher‑education institution (such as Université PSL) to remain in France after completing their degree to look for a job or to start a company in a field related to their studies; applicants must submit proof of a French‑issued diploma at master‑level or higher, evidence of sufficient financial resources and health‑insurance coverage to the prefecture, and the permit grants the legal right to work without a separate work‑visa and to set up a business, while citizens of EU/EEA/Switzerland are exempt, and certain bilateral agreements (e.g., with India) allow a 24‑month stay through a renewal of the same APS framework, with the process coordinated through Campus France and detailed on official Service‑Public portals.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Université PSL provides a career‑focused environment in which international students are required to complete at least one internship—either in France or abroad—during their master’s studies, and it also runs a UROP programme that offers undergraduate research and hospital‑related internships across a wide range of disciplines, with grant‑based compensation in line with French internship regulations. The university’s strong links with French and international organisations such as the Treasury, the OECD, J‑PAL and CEPREMAP facilitate networking, conference attendance and direct access to employers, and its dedicated career‑service office supports students in securing co‑ops, placements and full‑time positions. Graduate outcomes are very strong: more than 95 % of graduates who enter the labour market obtain employment within three months of graduation, many being hired before they even finish their degree, and alumni typically pursue careers as financial analysts, economic analysts, quantitative analysts, financial engineers, debt‑management consultants, auditors, accountants, consultants or managers in finance, energy, auditing, accounting and related sectors. Lương & thị trường: In France recent official statistics show that graduates from the Grandes Écoles earn on average €39 010 gross per year, with managers about €1 700 more than engineers and €2 200 more than other specialisations, a gap that widens to roughly €4 400 when bonuses are included; six months after completing a master’s programme the average gross salary is €39 705 in France and €42 439 for those employed abroad, and international graduates are considerably more likely to work outside the country (around 27 % versus 8 % of French nationals), while roughly three‑quarters of all graduate positions are concentrated in Île‑de‑France; labour‑market analyses from APEC and France Travail highlight strong demand for senior‑cadre profiles, especially in digital transformation, data science, engineering, finance and consulting, with the bulk of senior‑cadre job seekers (over 80 %) falling into category C and the highest demand concentrated in the Paris region.
