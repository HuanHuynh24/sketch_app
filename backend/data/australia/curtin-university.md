### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Curtin University accepts international students for two main intakes each year – Semester 1 (starting late February/early March) and Semester 2 (starting late July); applications generally open in May and close a few weeks before the semester begins, with the Semester 1 deadline typically in early February and the Semester 2 deadline in early July, and prospective students must pay a non‑refundable application fee (usually around AUD 100, though the exact amount can vary by program) when they submit their online application through the university’s international portal, uploading certified copies of their academic transcripts, English‑language test results and any other required documents, after which Curtin assesses the file (normally within about two weeks), issues an offer if successful, and the student then accepts the offer and proceeds to apply for an Australian student visa.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to Curtin University must provide a recognised secondary‑school qualification (such as a Vietnamese Bang Tú tài or Bang Tốt nghiệp Trung học, an Indonesian Ijazah SMA, a US high‑school diploma with AP exams, etc.) together with official transcripts showing an overall average of at least 80 % (or the Australian GPA equivalent of 5.0 on a 7‑point scale) and evidence of passing any required national examinations; course‑specific subject prerequisites also apply (for example, mathematics for engineering, chemistry/biology for health‑science degrees, English for arts, etc.), and the minimum academic entry score may be higher for competitive programmes; English proficiency must be demonstrated through IELTS ≥ 6.0 (with no band < 5.5) or TOEFL iBT ≥ 60 or PTE ≥ 50, and additional non‑academic requirements are required for certain fields – design‑related courses (e.g., Architecture, Graphic Design, Fashion, Fine Art) demand a visual portfolio of 5–10 images with brief (≤ 50‑word) descriptions and the completion of an online Qualtrics questionnaire, and some of these programmes may also conduct an interview to assess aptitude, while the UniReady Enabling Program offers a pathway for students who do not meet the direct entry GPA but can complete a preparatory study block before commencing their chosen degree.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to Curtin University must prove English proficiency through an accepted test or a qualifying exemption; the university recognises IELTS Academic (overall 6.5 with a minimum of 6.0 in each band), TOEFL iBT (overall 79 with at least 13 in Reading, 13 in Listening, 21 in Writing and 18 in Speaking), and PTE Academic (overall 58 with a minimum of 50 in each skill for tests taken from December 2024, otherwise overall 69 with 59 in each skill), as well as its own Curtin English bridging program (B‑), Cambridge C1 Advanced (score 169) and C2 Proficiency (score 190). Applicants who have successfully completed at least one year of full‑time undergraduate or postgraduate coursework at a recognised university in Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the Republic of Ireland, Singapore, South Africa, the United Kingdom or the United States are exempt from providing an English test score. The Duolingo English Test is not listed among the accepted qualifications, so a waiver can only be obtained through the degree‑completion exemption, not via Duolingo results.

- Học phí dành cho sinh viên quốc tế tại Curtin University dao động khoảng A$31.000 mỗi năm (tùy ngành học, ví dụ: kỹ thuật ≈ A$35.000, kinh doanh ≈ A$30.000, y khoa ≈ A$32.000), vì vậy tổng chi phí cho một chương trình đại học 3 năm thường rơi vào khoảng A$93.000; sinh viên phải trả phí đăng ký hồ sơ khoảng A$90 một lần và phí ghi danh (enrolment fee) một lần là A$290; ngoài ra còn có các khoản phí khác bắt buộc như Student Services and Amenities Fee (SSAF) khoảng A$300 mỗi năm và phí bảo hiểm y tế cho sinh viên quốc tế (Overseas Student Health Cover – OSHC) khoảng A$600 mỗi năm, cộng với chi phí sách vở và thiết bị học tập tùy theo chương trình học.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at Curtin University in Perth the typical weekly out‑of‑tuition budget is between A$475 (single) and A$825 (family of four), with accommodation alone ranging from A$200–$460 per week for on‑campus halls and A$196–$693 per week for private student‑housing (bills usually included). Food and groceries cost roughly A$80–$280 per week, internet and phone A$20–$55 per week, and transport A$15–$55 per week (car costs $50–$80 if you drive). Adding these items gives a minimum living cost of about A$315 per week (≈ A$18,300 per year) and a maximum of around A$1,083 per week (≈ A$56,300 per year). Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is required and typically runs about A$600–$800 for a 12‑month period, bringing the total annual cost for a single international student to roughly A$18,900 at the low end and up to A$57,100 at the high end.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Curtin University structures its undergraduate and postgraduate programmes around 25‑credit‑point units (with some variations) and requires international students to enrol in a full‑time study load—generally 100 credit points per semester—to satisfy visa conditions, while part‑time loads must be formally approved; each unit lists any prerequisite requirements (for example, advanced physics units require completion of foundational physics courses, and certain management units are limited to second‑year students), and the detailed curriculum, including the sequence of units, credit allocations and specific learning outcomes for every unit, is set out in the university’s Courses Handbook under the Course Structure heading, which also indicates the expected duration of the programme and any credit‑transfer or recognised‑learning exemptions that may apply for prior study.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Curtin University was placed 183rd in the 2024 QS World University Rankings, making it one of the highest‑ranked Australian institutions and placing it in the top ten nationally for the QS “international research network” indicator that reflects strong global collaborations and a sizeable international student body; in the 2026 QS World University Rankings by Subject the university is ranked within the global top 50 for Geology, Geophysics and Earth & Marine Sciences, fourth worldwide for Mineral and Mining Engineering, first in Western Australia and top 10 nationally for Architecture & Built Environment, and nationally 12th for Nursing, while the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings by Subject list Curtin’s Psychology programme at 10th in Australia; Curtin also holds the QS Five Stars Plus rating, the highest possible QS Stars rating, and is fully accredited by Australia’s Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) with professional accreditations including Engineers Australia for its engineering courses, the Australian Nursing & Midwifery Accreditation Council for nursing programmes, and AACSB International for its business school.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student visa (subclass 500) requires international students to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity to cover their tuition fees and living expenses for the duration of their studies, typically by providing bank statements, loan documents or a sponsor’s evidence that meets the Department of Home Affairs’ minimum threshold (approximately AU$21,041 per year for living costs plus the full tuition amount); applicants must also arrange Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire period of their visa, with Curtin University’s preferred provider being Medibank, and maintain that cover throughout their stay; while on a subclass 500 visa students may work up to 48 hours per fortnight during scheduled course periods (including exams) and unlimited hours during recognized course breaks, and those enrolled in a master’s by research or doctoral programme have no work‑hour limits, provided they continue to meet enrolment, attendance and health‑insurance requirements and do not exceed the stipulated work conditions. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Curtin University can remain in Australia after completing their studies by applying for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) under the Post‑Higher Education Work (commonly called the Post‑Study Work) stream, which gives unrestricted work rights; the visa is usually granted for two years for a bachelor’s degree, three years for a master’s by research or a doctoral qualification, and five years for Hong Kong or British National Overseas passport holders, while graduates whose degree appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List may apply for a further two‑year extension (and an additional one‑to‑two years if they studied, lived and worked in a regional area), provided the application is lodged within six months of completing the course and the applicant holds or previously held an eligible student visa.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Curtin University provides extensive work‑integrated learning for both domestic and international students, including industry placements, paid three‑month internships through the Internship Gateway Program, and overseas practicums that give students hands‑on experience with major partners such as BHP, the Fremantle Football Club and the Chamber of Commerce, and the university is recognised as Western Australia’s top public university for undergraduate employment outcomes in the Good Universities Guide 2026, with QILT data showing that around 85‑90 % of its graduates are employed or in further study within four months of graduation and that employer satisfaction scores are the highest in the state, leading graduates into careers across engineering, health sciences, business, law and the humanities. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Australia earn a median full‑time salary of about $68,000, which is roughly $7,000 lower than the $75,000 earned by domestic undergraduates, while at the postgraduate coursework level the gap widens to around $30,000, with international graduates typically earning $30,000 less than their domestic peers; the disparity is especially pronounced in business and management where international undergraduates earn about $12,000 less than domestic graduates, whereas fields such as Medicine, Rehabilitation and Mining Engineering show the strongest labour‑market demand, with full‑time employment rates for international postgraduate coursework students reaching 89.9 % in Medicine and Rehabilitation and graduates in mining, medical studies and general medicine commanding the highest starting salaries and experiencing up to a 35 % income rise within five years of graduation; additionally, vocational‑education graduates see a median income boost of roughly $14,100 after training and enjoy an 88 % post‑qualification employment rate, reflecting the broader shift toward a service‑based economy that continues to drive the majority of employment growth across finance, technology and business sectors.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**
