### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- James Cook University Singapore welcomes international applicants for three yearly intakes—March (Term 1), July (Term 2) and November (Term 3)—with application deadlines roughly eight weeks before each start (end‑January for March, end‑May for July and end‑September for November); the process requires completing the online application, selecting “International Pass”, uploading a passport copy, academic transcripts and any English‑language proof, then paying the non‑refundable SGD 150 application fee, after which an automated email supplies a login ID, the admissions team reviews the submission (usually 4–6 weeks), issues a Letter of Offer and Standard Student Contract, and the applicant must accept the offer, pay the first tuition instalment and complete ICA student‑pass formalities to enrol.

For A‑Level entrants the university typically expects three subjects with grades of at least B (or the equivalent credit) and an overall average that meets the specific programme’s GPA threshold (most bachelor degrees require a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 on a 4‑point scale, with some courses such as Games Design or Architecture asking for higher results). **3. Tiếng Anh**

- James Cook University Singapore requires all international applicants whose first language is not English to demonstrate a minimum English proficiency equivalent to an overall IELTS score of 6.0 (with no band score lower than 5.5); if the IELTS requirement is not met the university also accepts TOEFL iBT ≥ 60, PTE Academic ≥ 50 or Duolingo English Test ≥ 105, and students who achieve any of these scores within the previous two years are exempt from additional testing. Applicants who have completed at least two years of full‑time study in an English‑medium secondary school or university programme are automatically exempt from the English test requirement, as are Singapore citizens and permanent residents who can confirm English as their first language. For those who do not meet the minimum scores or exemption criteria, JCU Singapore offers an English Language Preparatory Programme (ELPP) that runs for four months at three levels, covering IELTS equivalents from 5.0 to 6.0, after which successful completion allows direct entry into the chosen foundation, diploma or degree programme.

**4. Học phí**

- James Cook University Singapore charges international under‑graduates between SGD 21,329 and 30,816 per year in tuition before any subsidy, while most postgraduate programmes range from SGD 38,520 to 41,529 per year; eligible courses can receive the Ministry of Education Tuition Grant which reduces tuition by roughly 40‑60 %, bringing typical undergraduate fees down to about SGD 12,800‑18,500 annually (≈ SGD 51,200‑74,000 for a four‑year degree) and postgraduate fees to roughly SGD 15,400‑24,900 per year, and the university requires a non‑refundable application fee of about SGD 100 together with modest ancillary charges such as a student services levy (≈ SGD 30 per year) and a registration fee (≈ SGD 50).

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- The cost of living for an international student at James Cook University’s Singapore campus typically falls between S$1,480 and S$4,200 per month, covering accommodation, meals and everyday expenses; over a year this amounts to roughly S$17,760 to S$50,400. Adding the mandatory International Student Registration Fee of S$260 and the compulsory FPS health‑insurance premium (minimum S$10.90 per year), the total annual out‑of‑pocket expense for an international student is therefore approximately S$18,030 to S$50,670, with transport costs (public bus/MRT or cycling) generally ranging from S$50 to S$150 per month and already incorporated in the monthly living‑cost estimate.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at James Cook University Singapore must satisfy entry prerequisites either by completing the Pre‑University Foundation Program—requiring ten subjects with a minimum of 50 % in each and an overall 65 % to progress to undergraduate study—or by meeting the direct entry academic standards of their chosen degree; undergraduate programmes require 72 credit points (CP) and master’s programmes require 48 CP, with most subjects assigned a standard value of 3 CP (as indicated by the suffix after the colon in the subject code, e.g., BU1002:03), and the curriculum is structured into semester‑based subject clusters that together satisfy the total CP requirement, each subject outlining specific learning outcomes that define the knowledge, skills and competencies—such as critical thinking, analytical ability and professional practice—students are expected to achieve, while international students must also maintain at least 90 % attendance and be on‑campus for a minimum of three hours per day, five days a week, in compliance with immigration regulations.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT'S PASS, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- James Cook University Singapore is ranked roughly 260th in the global QS World University Rankings and sits around 180th in the overall world league according to recent listings, with its Governance program placed near 27th worldwide; in the 2026 QS Sustainability Rankings the campus appears among the top 260 universities and is recognised as one of Australia’s top 11 institutions, while the Times Higher Education World University Rankings positions it in the 401‑500 band. Subject‑specific acclaim includes a world‑leading No. 1 ranking for Marine and Freshwater Biology (as reported by the Centre for World University Rankings) and strong performance in governance and sustainability disciplines. The university holds full accreditation from the Singapore Ministry of Education, Australian TEQSA accreditation, and its Business School is AACSB‑accredited and affiliated with the CFA Institute, confirming international standards for its MBA and related programmes, and it consistently receives QS Stars ratings placing it within the top 2 percent of universities worldwide.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students accepted into a full‑time programme at James Cook University Singapore must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) from the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority and, as part of the application, provide proof of sufficient financial support – typically a recent bank statement or scholarship letter showing at least SGD 2,000 per month (or the amount required to cover tuition and living expenses) together with the tuition fee receipt; once the STP is issued, students are allowed to take up on‑campus or off‑campus part‑time work for up to 16 hours per week during term and full‑time during vacation, but any employment must be approved through the ICA’s Part‑Time Student Employment Pass or a work‑holiday scheme and the student must not breach the work‑hour limit or engage in prohibited occupations, otherwise fines of up to SGD 20,000 or imprisonment may apply. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of James Cook University Singapore are eligible to apply to the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority’s one‑year, non‑renewable Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) for post‑study job seeking; the LTVP allows a stay of up to 12 months from the date of issue, during which the holder may look for employment (but must obtain a separate work pass or a Letter of Consent from an employer to actually work), and eligibility requires that the applicant has successfully completed a degree‑level programme at JCU Singapore, holds a valid Student Pass at the time of application, applies within six months of graduation, is a foreign national with no criminal record, and can demonstrate sufficient funds for living expenses.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: James Cook University Singapore reports that roughly 85 % of its graduates are employed within six months of completing their studies, with about two‑thirds (around 66‑67 %) in full‑time positions and a median starting salary of approximately SGD 3,500 per month, exceeding local benchmarks; the university supports international and local students in securing internships, co‑op or placement experiences through its Student Careers service, which posts opportunities on the JCU Singapore Career Portal and assists with applications (though placement is ultimately the student’s responsibility), and internships may be full‑ or part‑time, must align with the student’s program, can be undertaken in Singapore or the student’s home country, and require an employment letter and, where applicable, an approved work pass. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from James Cook University’s Singapore campus who secure full‑time permanent positions earn a median gross monthly salary of about S$3,500, which is well below the overall median for degree‑holders in Singapore of roughly S$8,650 reported by the Ministry of Manpower in its latest labour‑force survey; the labour market continues to show strong demand for graduates in professional, managerial, executive and technical roles, which together account for more than half of graduate employment, while clerical, sales, service and production‑related occupations make up the remainder of the demand profile.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: James Cook University’s Singapore campus provides a dedicated International Student Office that supports overseas students with a comprehensive orientation programme delivered the week before the start of each semester, covering enrolment procedures, campus facilities, academic expectations and local laws, as well as offering assistance with accommodation through its housing support services and arrival‑pick‑up arrangements; the university also runs a Careers Services unit that helps students secure internships and employment opportunities both locally and internationally, providing career counselling, job‑search resources and networking events to aid graduate outcomes. Đời sống & môi trường: James Cook University’s Singapore campus welcomes a vibrant community of international students and offers a well‑structured campus life that includes a wide range of co‑curricular activities (CCAs) and student clubs—such as cultural societies, sports teams, hobby groups and academic interest societies—designed to help newcomers make friends, develop leadership skills and experience life in Singapore; the university’s Student Code of Conduct and dedicated security personnel ensure a safe environment with 24‑hour campus monitoring, emergency response procedures and regular safety briefings, while its International Student Services office provides orientation, visa assistance and pastoral care, and a Vietnamese Student Association (or similar cultural club) regularly organises gatherings, language practice sessions and celebrations of Vietnamese holidays, giving Vietnamese students a familiar community and a supportive network within the broader international student body.
