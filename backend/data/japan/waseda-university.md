### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Waseda University accepts international undergraduate applicants for two yearly intakes — a spring entry in April and an autumn entry in September — and the entire process is conducted through the university’s online portal “The Admissions Office (TAO).” For the September (autumn) intake, the online application window opens on January 11 at 10:00 am and closes on February 20 at 5:00 pm (Japan time); applicants must first create a TAO account, complete the electronic application, upload all required documents, and pay the screening fee (approximately ¥30,000) before the deadline. **2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Waseda University admits international applicants who have completed or will complete a 12‑year non‑Japanese secondary education (including international schools) by the designated entry date, and there is no formal minimum GPA or EJU score required—students may submit EJU results, IB, A‑level, or other recognized transcripts as part of the academic record, but admission is judged holistically on overall academic performance, essays, and any supplemental documents; Japanese language ability is not required for English‑taught programs, while most undergraduate schools (including the School of International Liberal Studies) require a completed application form, certified copies of the high‑school transcript (học bạ) and graduation certificate, proof of any standardized test scores the applicant wishes to present, and a personal statement or essay, with an interview being scheduled for AO (Admission Office) April and September entries if the applicant’s file is selected for further review, though a portfolio is only requested for specialized fields such as art or design and is not a general requirement for most faculties.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Waseda University requires proof of English proficiency for most international applicants, accepting TOEFL iBT scores of 80 or higher (or the equivalent TOEFL PBT score of 550), IELTS overall band scores of 6.0 or higher, and TOEIC Listening & Reading scores of 620 or above; PTE Academic is not listed among the accepted tests and Duolingo English Test scores are not accepted. For graduate programmes, the university also permits applicants to demonstrate Japanese language ability in place of an English test by submitting a JLPT N1 certificate or an EJU Japanese‑subject score (recommended minimum 260 points), effectively waiving the English‑language requirement when strong Japanese proficiency is proven. Undergraduate programmes taught in English do not require any Japanese language ability at the time of application, but they still expect the same TOEFL/IELTS evidence unless the applicant can satisfy the Japanese‑language waiver option for graduate study.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Waseda University typically need to budget around 80,000 JPY per month for accommodation in central areas such as Shinjuku and an additional 80,000 JPY per month for food, transport and daily necessities, while the mandatory National Health Insurance costs roughly 2,000 JPY per month; this brings the monthly living expense to about 162,000 JPY and the total annual cost to roughly 1,944,000 JPY (about 1.9 million JPY per year), which is the amount most students use to demonstrate sufficient funds for their visa application.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Waseda University is officially accredited by Japan’s Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT) and the Japan University Accreditation Association (JUNAA), and it consistently ranks among the top Japanese institutions in global league tables: in the 2025 QS World University Rankings by Subject it placed #65 worldwide in Arts & Humanities (second in Japan) and #99 in Social Sciences & Management (third in Japan), while also breaking into the global top‑50 in History of Art (ranked within the 21‑50 band) and Modern Languages (rank 40, second in Japan); the 2024 QS Subject Rankings showed Waseda in the top‑100 for two broad subject areas, top‑50 for two individual subjects and top‑100 for eleven additional subjects, and the 2022 QS rankings highlighted seven subjects (Sports‑related Subjects, Politics & International Studies, Classics & Ancient History, Modern Languages, Geography, Linguistics and Law) within the world’s top‑100, three of which were ranked first domestically; in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings the university is positioned in the 401‑500 band globally, reflecting strong research, teaching and international outlook, and it hosts a vibrant international community of roughly 3,000 students – about 10 % of its total enrolment – drawn from over 80 countries.

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: International students admitted to Waseda University must first obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) issued by the university’s admissions office, which is then submitted together with a passport, completed visa application form and a recent photograph to the Japanese embassy or consulate in the applicant’s home country to obtain a “Student” residence status visa; the COE is valid for three months, so the visa must be obtained and entry to Japan made within that period, and applicants must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover tuition and living expenses for the full duration of their program—typically by providing a savings balance certificate, income statements or sponsor letters showing roughly ¥1 million to ¥1.5 million per year, although exact amounts are set by the university’s guidelines rather than a national minimum, and once in Japan students may work part‑time up to 28 hours per week during the academic term and up to 40 hours per week during school holidays, provided they obtain the “Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted” (commonly granted automatically for part‑time work) and do not exceed the allowed hours, allowing them to supplement their finances while complying with Ministry of Foreign Affairs regulations. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Designated Activities visa for continuing job‑hunting allows international graduates of Waseda University who have been enrolled as regular (正規生) degree‑seeking students to remain in Japan after graduation to seek employment; it is not available to non‑degree students such as Japanese‑language, exchange or research students. Applicants must submit a recommendation letter from the university’s Career Center together with evidence of their job‑hunting activities, academic record, proof of financial support and other documents to the Immigration Bureau, and the application can be made before graduation while the student still holds “College Student” status. Once issued, the visa is valid for six months and can be renewed one time for an additional six months, giving a maximum stay of one year after graduation; after the period expires the holder must obtain a work‑related status of residence if employed.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Waseda University’s Career Center reports that among international graduates (the 1,378 students who completed their degrees in 2019‑2020), roughly 30 % secure full‑time employment in Japan while about 17 % find jobs overseas, giving an overall post‑graduation employment rate of just under half of the cohort; another 40 % continue on to further studies and roughly a quarter remain actively job‑hunting after graduation. The university actively supports internships, co‑op and placement programmes through its career services, and the most common employers of international alumni include Accenture Japan, IBM Japan, ABeam Consulting, Mizuho Financial Group and Fujitsu, indicating that internship experience often translates into hiring by leading Japanese firms. Lương & thị trường: Waseda University’s most recent career report shows that roughly two‑thirds of its international graduates secure employment in Japan—967 out of 1,479 surveyed students worked domestically after graduation—while the remainder pursue overseas jobs, further study, or other paths, and the university’s placement success is reflected in its top national ranking for graduate employability; in the broader Japanese labour market, entry‑level salaries for university graduates average about ¥4 million – ¥4.5 million per year (approximately $27,000‑$30,000), with higher starting pay (up to ¥5 million or more) in high‑demand sectors such as information technology, software development, data science, finance and banking, consulting, engineering and manufacturing, as well as healthcare and pharmaceuticals, all of which consistently seek both domestic and international talent for their growth‑driven hiring cycles.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Waseda University’s International Office (International Affairs Division) conducts an orientation for incoming international students that includes distribution of MyWaseda login credentials, briefings on residence‑card procedures, city‑office registration, opening bank accounts, and on‑campus consultation services; the International Scholar Services (ISS) offers guest‑house accommodation and practical guidance for renting private housing, while the Center for International Education (CIE) provides career support such as counseling, job‑search workshops, internship and employment information, and links to recruitment events, and academic tutoring is available through the university’s Writing Center and Tutorial System, which give one‑on‑one assistance for Japanese and English academic writing, all coordinated from the International Office at Waseda Global Gate. Đời sống & môi trường: Waseda University hosts roughly 500 official student circles that span cultural, sports, academic and hobby interests, all of which receive university facilities and subsidies but must adhere to clear safety rules covering alcohol use, prohibition of illegal drugs, and established emergency contact procedures; the Center for International Education further supports overseas students with visa and residence guidance, scholarship and housing information, job‑hunting assistance, and a calendar of cross‑cultural events, while specific Vietnamese community groups such as the Vietnamese Student Association organize language‑exchange meetings, Tết festivals and peer‑mentoring for Vietnamese newcomers, and the university’s campus‑life services provide health, safety and wellness resources, an emergency notice system and virtual tours of the main and satellite campuses to help all international students navigate and feel safe in campus life.
