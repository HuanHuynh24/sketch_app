**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International candidates for the engineering programmes at Institut Polytechnique de Paris must have completed at least two years of undergraduate study (typically a Bachelor’s degree) in a science, engineering, or quantitative economics‑and‑finance field at a non‑French university, with an especially strong background in mathematics and/or physics; a minimum of three years of higher‑education study is recommended at entry. Applicants must submit official transcripts (học bạ) showing their academic record, and while a specific GPA threshold is not listed, a high academic standing comparable to a 3.0/4.0 (or its local equivalent) is expected; a French language B1 level is generally required only for programmes that teach in French, but the entrance examinations can be taken in English. Admission is based on the International Entrance Examinations administered by the institute, which assess subject knowledge in mathematics, physics, and related areas, and for some schools (e.g., École Polytechnique) an interview or physical‑fitness assessment may be required, whereas a portfolio is not part of the standard application for the engineering tracks. Additional constraints include being under 25 years old on 1 January of the admission year, not having started a Master’s or PhD program, and French citizens (including dual nationals) must apply through the French‑national track.

**4. Học phí**

- International students (non‑EU/EEA) at Institut Polytechnique de Paris pay tuition that varies by school but is generally around 4 650 € per year for most engineering schools (ENSAE, ENSTA, Télécom Paris, Télécom SudParis) and 38 000 € for the three‑year programme at École Polytechnique, while EU/EEA students pay about 1 850 € per year at the same schools and 19 000 € for the three‑year École Polytechnique programme; the currency is euros (EUR), the annual “Contribution à la Vie Étudiante et de Campus” (CVEC) is a mandatory 90 € per year for all students, and there is no separate application fee for the international admission process, so the only other recurring charge beyond tuition is the CVEC.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at the Institut Polytechnique de Paris the yearly budget breaks down roughly as follows: tuition is €1,850 per year for EU students (≈ €4,650 per year for non‑EU students) plus the mandatory €90 “CVEC” student‑life contribution; accommodation options range from free housing with a €390 annual fee to shared solidarity apartments costing €280‑€400 per month plus a €300 yearly service charge, while private rooms in the Orsay area run €400‑€450 per month and in Paris start at about €500 per month (≈ €4,800‑€6,000 annual rent on average); food typically costs €300‑€400 per month (≈ €4,200‑€4,800 per year); public transport for students (Navigo Étudiant) is about €75 per month (≈ €900 annual); basic French social security is free to register but a complementary mutuelle (supplementary health insurance) usually costs €200‑€300 per year and a compulsory civil‑liability insurance adds roughly €50 per year. Adding these items gives an estimated total annual cost of about €12,000‑€14,000 for EU students and €15,000‑€17,000 for non‑EU students, covering tuition, housing, food, transport and health‑related insurance.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to Institut Polytechnique de Paris must have completed the first year of a relevant master‑level programme (e.g., mechanics, physics, applied mathematics, or mathematics) either at IP‑Paris or an equivalent French or foreign university, and demonstrate proficiency in English (with French optional for many programmes); the second‑year curriculum is organised by semester and combines taught modules—typically 3 ECTS each for specialised courses such as fluid‑mechanics modelling, atmospheric chemistry, practical machine‑learning sessions, statistical learning theory, high‑dimensional statistics, Monte‑Carlo methods, and natural‑language processing—with a substantial internship (30 ECTS for mechanics programmes, 18 ECTS for data‑science programmes) lasting 4–6 months, all contributing to the required 60 ECTS for the master’s degree; the course structure emphasises both theoretical understanding and hands‑on research, so graduates are expected to master advanced modelling and analytical techniques, design and execute independent research projects, apply machine‑learning and statistical methods to real‑world data, and communicate scientific results effectively.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: For a non‑EU student admitted to Institut Polytechnique de Paris, you must apply for a long‑stay student visa (VLS‑TS) through the France‑Visas portal after creating a Campus France account; the application requires a valid passport, a recent photo, the admission letter, proof of financial resources of at least €6 150 for a 10‑month academic year (e.g., recent bank statements, a scholarship certificate, or a guarantor’s attestation with tax returns), proof of accommodation in France, and proof of health insurance – either a private policy covering France, an EHIC for EU students, or a commitment to register with the French social security system on arrival – and once you arrive you validate the VLS‑TS on the ANEF portal, after which the visa serves as your residence permit for up to 12 months. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Institut Polytechnique de Paris who hold a valid French student residence permit can, after obtaining a Master’s‑level diploma (including specialised master, engineering degree or equivalent), apply for the “job seeker / new business creator” residence permit (Carte de séjour temporaire « recherche d’emploi / création d’entreprise »), which has replaced the APS for most non‑EU nationals; it is issued for twelve months, non‑renewable, permits up to 60 % of full‑time work while searching for a job and also allows the holder to launch a company in a field related to their studies, provided they submit a passport, birth certificate, three recent photos, the diploma (or proof of completion within the last year), proof of health insurance, proof of sufficient financial resources, a recent proof of address and, if applicable, a business project plan, and pay the €75 tax‑stamp fee, with the application made at the local prefecture before the student permit expires (or within four years after graduation if they have left France); citizens of the EU, Algeria and of countries that have migration‑flow agreements with France (e.g., Tunisia, India, Mauritania, etc.) may still use the APS, which similarly grants a twelve‑month provisional stay for a first professional experience and requires comparable documentation.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The Institut Polytechnique de Paris reports that 95 % of its graduates secure employment within four months of finishing their studies, and international students—who make up 43 % of the student body—benefit from a dedicated Career Services office that coordinates internships, co‑op placements and apprenticeship (“alternance”) programmes with leading companies in engineering, technology, finance, consulting, research and industry across France and Europe; the school’s strong links with corporate partners and its on‑campus career fairs ensure that most master’s students complete at least one professional internship or work‑study period before graduation, and the majority of those placements translate into full‑time jobs in the same sectors after they receive their degree. Lương & thị trường: The average gross annual salary for French graduates is €39,010, with engineers seeing a 2.4 % increase and managers a 2.3 % rise year‑on‑year; graduates of Institut Polytechnique de Paris typically command higher starting earnings of roughly €45,000 – €55,000, especially in engineering, data‑science, artificial‑intelligence, finance and consulting roles, which align with the most‑in‑demand occupations identified for 2025 such as financial controllers, purchasing managers, project managers, data analysts, cybersecurity experts, accountants, retail and construction workers, installation and maintenance technicians, healthcare staff, and web developers/IT engineers; labour‑market data show that 27.2 % of international graduates take jobs abroad after completing their studies in France (11.9 % within the EU and 15.3 % outside it), while 8.4 % of French nationals work overseas, and the majority of graduate positions remain concentrated in Île‑de‑France, though 61.3 % of engineers are employed in other regions.
