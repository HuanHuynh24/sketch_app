### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International applicants to the University of Newcastle, Australia should register on the Student Portal, verify their account via email and then log in to complete the online application, uploading all required academic transcripts, English‑language test results and any additional documents, and paying the AU 150 application fee; the university recommends submitting the application at least 12 weeks before the intended start date and lists specific deadlines for 2026 – for Semester 2 the final date to apply is 22 June (with acceptance and deposit due by 8 July), while other intakes follow the standard Australian schedule of Semester 1 (February) and Trimester 2 (May); after the application is processed, a formal offer is issued, which must be accepted through the portal together with the required deposit (usually around AU 500) and proof of Overseas Student Health Cover, after which a Confirmation of Enrolment is released to enable visa processing.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to the University of Newcastle must provide official transcripts showing they have completed at least one year of full‑time university study (or the equivalent secondary qualifications) and meet the minimum academic threshold of a 2.0 / 4.0 GPA, though many undergraduate pathways and competitive programs require a higher GPA of 2.5. For Australian high‑school equivalents, a Year 11 result with a GPA of 2.0 or a High School Diploma with a GPA of 2.5 is accepted; alternatively, U.S. applicants may meet the requirement with a SAT score of 800 (or 750 for some courses) or an ACT score of 17 (or 15–19 depending on the program), and a GED score of at least 580 (with no component below 145). English language proficiency must be demonstrated through an approved test (e.g., IELTS 6.5 overall, TOEFL iBT 79). Certain creative and design courses additionally require a portfolio and may conduct an interview as part of the selection process. All documents not in English must be accompanied by certified translations, and applicants may need to attend an interview or provide further evidence of genuine temporary enrolment if requested by the university or Australian immigration authorities.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Newcastle must demonstrate English proficiency, with the standard minimum for most undergraduate courses set at an overall IELTS 6.0 (each band ≥ 5.5), which is equivalent to a TOEFL iBT total of 70, a PTE Academic score of 52, or a Duolingo English Test (DET) score of 110; higher programme requirements use the next bands (e.g., IELTS 6.5 = TOEFL 85 = PTE 59 = DET 120, IELTS 7.0 = TOEFL 94 = PTE 66 = DET 130, etc.). Applicants may be granted a waiver of these evidence requirements if they have completed the University’s English Language Bridging Program, have previously undertaken an approved English test through the University’s English Language Centre, or can prove prior tertiary study conducted in English; otherwise they may receive a conditional offer pending proof of the required scores, and they must also satisfy the Australian Department of Home Affairs English language criteria for their visa.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students seeking admission to the University of Newcastle must meet specific entry prerequisites that vary by qualification, such as achieving an International Baccalaureate aggregate of at least 24 points (with higher thresholds for more competitive programs), completing A‑Level subjects with grades ranging from B to E depending on the course, or attaining a GPA of 2.5 in a two‑ or three‑year Higher National Certificate/Diploma; English proficiency is also required, typically an IELTS Academic overall band of 5.5 (5.0 minimum in each band) for most programmes, with higher band requirements for Nursing (overall 7.0, 7.0 in speaking, listening and reading, 6.5 in writing) and Education (overall 7.5, 8.0 in speaking and listening, 7.0 in reading and writing). Once enrolled, courses are structured around the Australian credit‑point system, where a standard full‑time year comprises 200 credit points (approximately 100 points per semester), and each unit lists its credit value, prerequisites, and specific learning outcomes that describe the knowledge, skills and competencies graduates will demonstrate on completion.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Newcastle reports that 91.6 % of its undergraduate graduates and 94.5 % of postgraduate graduates secure employment within four months of finishing their studies, and its Career‑Ready Placement program, launched in 2023, now requires every undergraduate to complete a work‑integrated placement as part of their degree, with 96.8 % of undergraduates already enrolled in such programmes and a target of 100 % by 2025; international enrolments total 6,387 students from 96 countries, and the university’s strong industry links mean placements span a wide range of sectors—including health, engineering, research, pharmaceuticals, consulting and technology—evidenced by a 100 % full‑time employment rate for Bachelor of Pharmacy graduates within four months and numerous biomedical‑science students completing industry projects, ensuring that international students benefit from robust internship, co‑op and placement opportunities that translate into high graduate employment rates across diverse career pathways. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Australia typically earn slightly less than their domestic peers, with the median full‑time annual salary for international undergraduates reported at about AU$68,000 and a larger gap at the postgraduate level where the median is roughly AU$85,000, compared with domestic averages of AU$75,000 and AU$115,000 respectively. Labour‑market demand remains strongest in health‑related fields (medicine, dentistry, nursing, allied health), engineering (civil, mechanical, electrical), information‑communication technology, and education, all of which are highlighted by Jobs and Skills Australia as current skill‑shortage occupations. Graduates from the University of Newcastle in these high‑demand areas enjoy very high employment outcomes, with over 90 % securing full‑time positions within a few months of graduation, and many reporting starting salaries that align with or exceed the sector averages—often ranging from AU$70,000 for entry‑level engineers to AU$100,000+ for medical and dental professionals.

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The International Student Support office at the University of Newcastle Australia assists international students with a comprehensive suite of services, including a structured orientation programme that offers pre‑arrival information, welcome events and campus tours; dedicated housing support that helps students locate on‑ or off‑campus accommodation, understand rental contracts and budgeting, and access university‑approved residence options; and a robust career service delivered through the university’s Career Centre, providing résumé and interview workshops, employer networking events, internship and graduate job listings, and one‑to‑one counseling, while the team across the Callaghan, Central Coast, Newcastle City and Sydney campuses also offers academic, language, enrolment, financial, personal, health and broader career guidance for students from all backgrounds. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Newcastle offers a vibrant campus life for its roughly 6,400 international students, with more than 100 clubs and societies ranging from cultural and language groups to sports, academic and volunteer organisations, making it easy for newcomers to meet peers and improve English skills; among these, a Vietnamese Student Association actively brings together Vietnamese students through regular gatherings, Tet celebrations, language‑exchange sessions and mentorship programmes, while the university’s Student Wellbeing team provides round‑the‑clock safety and counselling services, a 24‑hour campus security patrol, a personal safety app, and clear emergency procedures, and the Student Experience Team assists with on‑campus accommodation at Callaghan and nearby homestays, ensuring a secure and supportive environment alongside Newcastle’s lively city centre, beaches and multicultural vibe.
