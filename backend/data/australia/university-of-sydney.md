**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 student visa requires proof of sufficient financial capacity, meaning you must show enough funds to cover the full tuition fees for up to 12 months (minus any amounts already paid), living expenses of roughly AU$21 000‑30 000 per year for yourself and any secondary applicants, travel costs for all entrants, and any school fees for dependents; you must also provide evidence that you have genuine access to these funds, and if another person is providing the money, documentation of that support is required. Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is mandatory for the entire stay, must commence on the day you arrive (or no earlier than a week before the course starts) and remain continuous with no gaps; the typical cost is about AU$500‑800 per year for a single student. Work rights for Subclass 500 holders allow up to 48 hours of paid work per fortnight during study periods (including exam weeks) and unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks, but you may not work before the course begins unless you hold a different visa that permits it; partners of postgraduate students may be eligible for full‑time work rights. The University of Sydney is an approved Australian education provider that can issue a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) for international students, and its student‑visa guidance aligns with the Department of Home Affairs requirements found on immi.homea... Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of Sydney can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) in the Post‑Higher Education (Post‑Study Work) stream, which normally grants a stay of two to three years with unrestricted work rights; holders of Hong Kong or British National Overseas passports may be eligible for up to five years, and from 1 July 2023 graduates whose degree is on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply for a further two‑year extension (with no additional fee), while those who have studied, lived and worked in a regional area may receive an extra one to two years of work rights.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Sydney provides a comprehensive suite of work‑integrated learning options for international students, including a six‑week International Industry Placement Program that offers paid placements and scholarship funding, faculty‑wide student placement schemes with 140‑hour internships in fields such as arts, media, policy and health, and credit‑bearing internships of at least 95 hours spread over a semester for most undergraduate degrees; these programmes give students practical experience, industry networks and transferable skills that Australian employers value, and the university reports that over 90 % of its graduates secure employment or commence further study within four months of graduation, with typical career pathways after graduation including finance, consulting, technology, media, health services, public policy and research, while dedicated career‑support services help international students navigate the local job market, gain casual work experience and translate their placement achievements into full‑time roles.
