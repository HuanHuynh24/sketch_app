### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Queensland University of Technology (QUT) accepts international students into three main intakes each year – Semester 1 (February), Semester 2 (July) and the special November intake – and requires applicants to submit their online application well before the start of each term (typically by early November for the February intake, early April for the July intake and early August for the November intake, with the November deadline applying to on‑shore applicants); a non‑refundable application fee must be paid at the time of submission (the exact amount varies by programme and is displayed on the application portal, and a late‑fee is charged if the deadline is missed), after which the standard six‑step process is followed: (1) identify the desired course and confirm you meet the entry requirements, (2) check tuition costs, living expenses and any available scholarships, (3) complete and submit the online application together with the required documents and the application fee, (4) await the offer letter, (5) accept the offer and pay the stipulated deposit (or provide a sponsor guarantee if applicable), and (6) enrol via QUT’s eStudent system and arrange any remaining payments before the census date.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Key dates (international students) - QUT](https://www.qut.edu.au/about/key-dates-and-academic-calendar/international-key-dates?category=Applying%20to%20study) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[[PDF] International Student Fee Policy - QUT](https://qutvirtual4.qut.edu.au/documents/4389026/4466752/International+student+fee+policy+2015.pdf/d50ae8e4-83e1-f077-625a-7d9fcbfb7ea3?t=1654693002299%20student%20fee%20policy%202015.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International applying - QUT](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/applying/international-applying) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/international) (Cao, score 0.91)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking undergraduate study at Queensland University of Technology must provide their secondary‑school results (học bạ) and an English‑language test score (e.g., IELTS ≥ 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, or TOEFL iBT ≥ 79) together with any required prerequisite subjects, and for most courses an ATAR‑equivalent result of at least 70 is required, with engineering, computer‑software, and electrical‑aerospace programmes demanding a higher threshold of 84 and typically requiring completion of Mathematical Methods or Specialist Mathematics (Units 3 & 4, C); business, finance, HR, international business, management, marketing and strategic advertising generally need no specific prerequisite beyond the ATAR, while design, architecture, creative arts and related fields may also require a portfolio and, in some cases, an interview to assess artistic and conceptual ability, and applicants are expected to submit full academic transcripts (including GPA conversion to the Australian 7‑point scale) as part of their application.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [QUT - ATAR acceptance thresholds](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/applying/international-year-12-students/atar-acceptance-thresholds) (Cao, score 0.69)<br>[International applying - QUT](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/applying/international-applying) (Cao, score 0.64)<br>[QUT College for international students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/qut-college/international) (Cao, score 0.63)<br>[International students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/international) (Cao, score 0.53)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Queensland University of Technology requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency through an approved test, typically IELTS (overall 6.0 with no band below 5.5), TOEFL iBT (total 58 with at least 13 in Reading, 13 in Listening, 18 in Speaking and 13 in Writing), Pearson PTE Academic (overall 50 and minimum 45 in each skill) or the Duolingo English Test (minimum 105); students who have completed a recent degree or diploma taught entirely in English at a recognised university, or who meet specific QUT‑determined criteria, may be granted a waiver, and for those without an approved test QUT also offers an on‑campus English assessment to satisfy the entry requirement.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [English language and pathway programs - Study - QUT](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/english-language-and-pathway-programs/english-language-programs) (Cao, score 0.99)<br>[QUT College for international students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/qut-college/international) (Cao, score 0.46)<br>[International students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/international) (Cao, score 0.38)<br>[International applying - QUT](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/applying/international-applying) (Cao, score 0.14)

**4. Học phí**

- At QUT international students pay tuition fees that are calculated at the unit level and summed for each teaching period, resulting in an annual tuition cost of roughly AUD 30,000‑45,000 for most undergraduate programmes and AUD 35,000‑45,000 for most postgraduate programmes, while specialised courses such as the Master of Information Technology can be as high as AUD 65,800 per year; a non‑refundable application fee of AUD 100 is required at the time of application, a deposit (typically 50 % of the tuition) must be paid before enrolment, and additional compulsory charges include Overseas Student Health Cover (about AUD 600 per 12 months), a student services levy of approximately AUD 300 per year, plus any textbook or material costs, all amounts quoted in Australian dollars.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [International student fee policy - QUT - QUT Portal](https://qutvirtual4.qut.edu.au/web/qut/after-leaving-qut/student-fee-refunds/international-student-fee-and-refund-policies/international-student-fee-policy) (Cao, score 0.69)<br>[Fees - QUT Online](https://online.qut.edu.au/course-fees/) (Cao, score 0.66)<br>[QUT College for international students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/qut-college/international) (Cao, score 0.62)<br>[International students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/international) (Cao, score 0.53)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at Queensland University of Technology the typical annual cost of living in Brisbane is roughly AU$18 000 to AU$25 000, broken down as follows: shared or studio accommodation costs about AU$800‑$1 600 per month (AU$9 600‑$19 200 per year), while a one‑bedroom unit runs around AU$1 600‑$2 200 per month (AU$19 200‑$26 400 per year) and larger family‑size rentals are AU$2 400‑$2 650 per month (AU$28 800‑$31 800 per year); utilities (electricity, gas, water) average AU$150‑$175 per month (≈AU$1 800‑$2 100 per year); food and groceries are typically budgeted at AU$300‑$400 per month (≈AU$3 600‑$4 800 per year); public transport (bus, train, ferry) costs about AU$150 per month (≈AU$1 800 per year) with student concessions; and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is around AU$600‑$800 per year. Adding these major items gives a total living‑cost estimate of roughly AU$18 000‑$25 000 per year, depending on the chosen accommodation type and personal spending habits.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Queensland University of Technology (QUT) is ranked 189th worldwide in the 2023 QS World University Rankings and placed 186th globally (11th in Australia) in the 2021 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, while also topping the THE Young University Rankings in 2020 as the leading Australian institution (14th globally). In QS subject rankings, QUT’s Communication and Media Studies is 1st in Australia and 16th worldwide, Nursing is 6th in Australia and 31st globally, Art and Design is 3rd in Australia and 48th worldwide, and programmes such as Architecture, Education and Training, Civil and Structural Engineering, Mineral and Mining Engineering, Performing Arts, Sports‑Related Subjects and Statistics & Operational Research all fall within the 51‑100 band internationally. The university also achieved 9th place among Australian institutions and the 101‑110 band globally in the 2020 QS Graduate Employability Ranking. QUT’s courses for international students are fully accredited under Australia’s national standards, holding a CRICOS registration (CRICOS No. 00213J) and being approved by the Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA); professional programmes such as Nursing are further accredited by the Australian Nursing & Midwifery Accreditation Council.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [QUT profile for international students](https://www.qut.edu.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0009/974790/QUT-full-profile-2021-v1.0.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[QUT - Personal best for QUT in World University Rankings](https://www.qut.edu.au/news?id=188053) (Cao, score 0.99)<br>[International students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/international) (Cao, score 0.87)<br>[QUT College for international students](https://www.qut.edu.au/study/qut-college/international) (Cao, score 0.58)

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Subclass 500 student visa requires proof of a minimum financial capacity of AUD 29,710 per year for the primary applicant (with additional amounts of AUD 10,394 for a partner and AUD 4,449 per child, plus school fees of AUD 13,502 per dependent child if applicable), and the applicant must maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the whole stay; the visa grants the holder the right to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session (with unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks) and unlimited work for those undertaking a master’s by research or doctoral degree, and it permits study at any CRICOS‑registered institution such as Queensland University of Technology. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) in the Post‑Higher Education Work (post‑study) stream lets international students who have graduated from a CRICOS‑registered course at Queensland University of Technology remain in Australia for typically two to three years, with the duration varying by qualification and extending to five years for holders of Hong Kong or British National Overseas passports; from 1 July 2023, graduates whose degree appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply for a further two‑year extension (in addition to the existing one‑ to two‑year regional extension for those who studied, lived and worked in regional areas), giving eligible QUT graduates the possibility of up to five years of stay and unrestricted work rights while they seek employment or further study.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Queensland University of Technology (QUT) embeds Work‑Integrated Learning (WIL) – including internships, co‑ops, industry placements and study tours – across all faculties, giving students, including internationals, real‑world experience that translates into strong graduate outcomes; the university’s overall graduate employment rate was 87.1 % in the most recent 2024 Graduate Outcomes Survey (up from 85.7 % in 2021), and participants in WIL programmes report employment levels approaching 100 %, with graduates entering a wide range of professional fields such as engineering, business, design, health, technology and the creative industries, while international student retention fell to 53.6 % during the same period, prompting targeted support initiatives. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries in Australia typically centre around a median of AUD 71,000, with higher‑earning fields such as dentistry (≈ 94,400), medicine (≈ 85,000), engineering (≈ 80,000‑100,000 depending on specialisation) and ICT/data analytics (≈ 75,000‑130,000) reflecting strong labour‑market demand; Jobs and Skills Australia data show that mining engineering, medical studies and general medicine lead in starting pay and experience a roughly 35 % income rise in the first five years, while 72 % of education graduates secure teaching positions and 60 % of engineering graduates enter professional engineering roles, and vacancy‑to‑unemployment ratios indicate very high demand for skilled trades (electricians, plumbers, carpenters, welders), ICT and data analytics professionals, as well as civil, mechanical, electrical and mining engineers, meaning international graduates from Queensland University of Technology who pursue these occupations can expect salaries from AUD 65,000 to 110,000+ in trades, AUD 75,000‑130,000+ in analytics and AUD 65,000‑140,000+ in engineering, with strong prospects for employment and visa sponsorship in regional and high‑skill streams.
