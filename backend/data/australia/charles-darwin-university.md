**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students wishing to study at Charles Darwin University must provide proof of a qualification recognised as equivalent to the Australian Year 12 (or a bachelor’s degree for postgraduate study) together with official transcripts and, where applicable, detailed subject‑by‑subject grades; for A‑Level entrants the university requires a minimum aggregate score of 6 (A=6, B=5, C=4, D=3, E=2, with AS‑levels counted as half points) and for most courses a minimum English proficiency of IELTS 6.5 (no band below 6.0), although selective programmes such as Nursing, Social Work and Education often require IELTS 7.0 (no band below 6.5) and may also ask for a speaking interview score; many programmes also stipulate a minimum overall grade score of about 65 % in foundation or secondary studies, and some courses (especially in the creative, health and design fields) may require additional evidence such as a portfolio, audition, CV, or interview, so applicants should check the specific course requirements for any subject prerequisites, work‑experience expectations, or supplementary assessments.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Charles Darwin University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving the minimum scores shown for their level of study: for VET courses an overall IELTS 5.5 with no band below 5.0 (or equivalent PTE 50, TOEFL iBT 60, or Duolingo 110), for undergraduate courses an overall IELTS 6.0 with no band below 6.0 (or equivalent PTE 50, TOEFL iBT 60, or Duolingo 110), and for postgraduate courses an overall IELTS 6.5 with no band below 6.0 (or equivalent PTE 58, TOEFL iBT 79, or Duolingo 120). The university also accepts IELTS One‑Skill Retake results, combined scores from up to two sittings of IELTS, PTE or TOEFL taken within six months, and the Cambridge English Advanced (CAE) test (undergraduate ≥169, postgraduate ≥176). English waivers are granted when applicants have completed a qualification from a recognised English‑speaking country where the medium of instruction was English, or when they have successfully finished a CDU enabling program of at least six months (0.5 FTE/40 credit points); in such cases the English test requirement may be exempted, though some courses may still demand higher scores.

**4. Học phí**

- Charles Darwin University charges international students an annual tuition fee that varies by program but generally ranges from AUD 22,000 to AUD 28,000 for most undergraduate courses and from AUD 28,000 to AUD 35,000 for postgraduate coursework, with the exact amount displayed on the course‑specific “Fees” tab; the total cost of study includes the Student Services and Amenities Fee (SSAF) of about AUD 46 per unit (roughly AUD 300‑400 per semester) and other incidental expenses such as textbooks (≈ AUD 200‑500 per semester), stationary, and equipment, while the non‑tuition application fee is AUD 100 and prospective students must also demonstrate the Australian Department of Home Affairs‑required living cost of AUD 29,710 per year for a single student.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Charles Darwin University can expect to spend roughly AU$28,700 to AU$65,800 per year on living expenses, which covers accommodation, food, utilities, phone‑internet, transport and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC). Weekly accommodation ranges from AU$310 to AU$750 (≈AU$16,120‑AU$39,000 annually), groceries and eating out from AU$100 to AU$300 (≈AU$5,200‑AU$15,600 annually), gas/electricity from AU$69 to AU$100 (≈AU$3,588‑AU$5,200 annually), phone/internet from AU$32 to AU$46 (≈AU$1,664‑AU$2,392 annually) and public transport roughly AU$30‑AU$55 per week (≈AU$1,560‑AU$2,860 annually). OSHC insurance typically costs about AU$600‑AU$700 per year. Adding these categories gives a total annual cost of approximately AU$28,700 at the low‑end and AU$65,800 at the high‑end, which aligns with the Department of Home Affairs’ 12‑month living cost benchmark of AU$29,710 for a single international student.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Charles Darwin University (CDU) is positioned at 584 in the 2026 QS World University Rankings, with its International Students indicator placing it 154th globally and 15th among Australian institutions; international enrolments represent roughly 17 percent of the total student body. In the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings the university falls within the 801‑1000 band (the most recent THE release). While detailed QS subject‑specific positions are not listed here, CDU is recognised in QS subject rankings for areas such as Education, Environmental Sciences and Health‑related fields, reflecting strong performance in those disciplines. The university is fully accredited by Australia’s Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) and its individual programmes hold accreditation from relevant professional bodies, ensuring compliance with national higher‑education standards.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Student visa (subclass 500) requires proof that you have at least AUD 29,710 available to cover one year of living expenses for the primary applicant, with additional minimum amounts of AUD 10,394 for an accompanying partner and AUD 4,449 for each dependent child (plus any school fees for children, currently about AUD 13,502 per child per year), and you must also demonstrate funds for travel and tuition; you must maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire duration of your stay, and while your course is in session you may work up to 48 hours per fortnight (unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks), although students enrolled in a master's by research or a doctoral programme and their family members have no work‑hour limit. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) allows international students who have graduated from Charles Darwin University to remain in Australia on a post‑study work stream for usually two to three years, with the exact length depending on the level of the qualification (e.g., a bachelor’s degree ≈ 2 years, a master’s degree ≈ 2–3 years, and a doctoral degree ≈ 3 years), while holders of Hong Kong or British National Overseas passports may be granted up to five years; during this period visa holders and eligible family members may live, study and work in Australia with unrestricted work rights, and a separate “Second Post‑Higher Education Work” stream is available for graduates of regional courses, offering a stay of between one and two years.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Charles Darwin University offers a range of work‑integrated learning (WIL) opportunities for international students, including internships, professional practice placements and industry‑linked projects across its Health, Science, Technology and Engineering faculties; these experiences are organised and negotiated by the university to meet accreditation standards and provide supervised, credit‑bearing work in real‑world settings, with options for overseas‑based placements in engineering and health‑related sectors, and dedicated career‑development support such as CV workshops and industry fairs, and the outcomes are reflected in the university’s graduate statistics which show that 80 % of graduates were in employment four months after completing their studies in 2024 (down slightly from 84 % in 2023) while graduate satisfaction remained high at 94.5 %. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Australia consistently earn lower salaries than their domestic peers, with the 2024 Graduate Outcomes Survey reporting a median full‑time undergraduate salary of $68,000 for internationals versus $75,000 for Australians and a particularly stark postgraduate coursework gap of about $30,000, while Business and Management graduates see a $12,000 shortfall; earlier IGOS data showed mean full‑time earnings of $54,000 for international undergraduates and $60,000 for postgraduates compared with domestic averages of $70,000 and $90,000 respectively, yet Charles Darwin University graduates stand out by achieving an average undergraduate earning of roughly $87,200, well above the national mean, and the labour‑market demand for international graduates is strongest in health‑related fields—especially Medicine and Rehabilitation where full‑time employment rates approach 90 %—whereas sectors such as Creative Arts and Agriculture exhibit much lower uptake, a pattern that mirrors Jobs and Skills Australia’s identified national shortages in occupations like nursing, aged‑care, information‑communication technology, engineering and construction, which continue to draw higher employment rates and better salary outcomes for graduates entering those areas.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Charles Darwin University’s International Student Support office (CDU Global/Student Central) provides a compulsory orientation program held the week before the start of classes—available on the main campuses and online—that delivers essential information on study, living in Darwin, cultural integration and required compliance, and requires new arrivals to update their local contact details within seven days; the office also assists international students with housing by offering advice on on‑campus accommodation options, guidance on off‑campus rentals, tenancy rights and referrals to external housing services, and it connects students to the university’s Career and Employment Centre which runs workshops on résumé writing, interview preparation, job‑search strategies and links to local employers and internships, all accessible through Student Central or the International Student Support Officer, with general enquiries answered at 1800 061 963 or study@cdu.edu.au. Đời sống & môi trường: Charles Darwin University offers a vibrant campus life for international students that includes a wide range of clubs and societies, from technology‑focused groups such as CDU Nexus to cultural and faith‑based organisations, and a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) that brings together Vietnamese students through cultural celebrations, food events and networking opportunities to foster a strong sense of belonging within the wider university community; the university also provides comprehensive support for international students through CDU Global, Student Central and International Student Support services, ensures safety by addressing concerns such as robbery and public‑transport risks via the Student Council’s advocacy, and offers secure on‑ and off‑campus accommodation options so students can feel safe and settled while studying in Darwin.
