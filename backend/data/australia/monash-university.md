### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Monash University nhận hồ sơ cho sinh viên quốc tế suốt cả năm, nhưng các đợt nhập học chính là học kỳ 1 (bắt đầu đầu tháng 3) và học kỳ 2 (bắt đầu giữa tháng 7); vì vậy sinh viên nên nộp hồ sơ càng sớm càng tốt, vì một số chương trình có thể đóng tuyển từ vài tháng trước ngày khai giảng và các khóa sau đại học thường đóng vào giữa tháng 6 tùy từng khoa. Phí nộp hồ sơ cho sinh viên quốc tế là 100 AUĐ, không hoàn lại, và có thể thanh toán bằng thẻ tín dụng trực tuyến qua Cổng hồ sơ của Monash; nếu có mã miễn giảm phí (waive code) thì nhập vào để được miễn phí. Quy trình nộp hồ sơ bao gồm: (1) chọn chương trình và kiểm tra yêu cầu nhập học; (2) tạo tài khoản trên Applicant Portal của Monash; (3) điền đầy đủ thông tin cá nhân, học vấn và tải lên các tài liệu chứng minh (bằng cấp, chứng chỉ tiếng Anh, hồ sơ tài chính, …); (4) thanh toán phí hồ sơ 100 AUĐ; (5) xem lại và xác nhận hồ sơ, sau đó nhấn “Submit” để nhận số hồ sơ và email xác nhận; (6) theo dõi trạng thái hồ sơ qua portal và trả lời bất kỳ yêu cầu bổ sung nào của bộ phận tuyển sinh. Đối với sinh viên đến từ các quốc gia bị hạn chế (ví dụ: Crimea, Cuba, Iran, Syria, v.v.) hoặc không có dịch vụ trực tuyến, phải nộp hồ sơ qua đại lý Monash được công nhận hoặc văn phòng IDP địa phương. Khi hồ sơ được chấp nhận, sinh viên sẽ nhận lời mời nhập học và có thể quyết định nhận, hoãn hoặc chuyển đổi chương trình/cơ sở.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to Monash University must meet both general and course‑specific entry requirements, which typically include a recognised secondary‑school qualification with an overall academic standing equivalent to an Australian GPA of about 2.7 or higher (e.g., ATAR 80+ for most undergraduate programmes, or a 3.0‑point GPA on a 4‑point scale for many overseas qualifications), a satisfactory English‑language score (IELTS ≥ 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, TOEFL iBT ≥ 79, or equivalent), and any prerequisite subjects required for the chosen field (such as Mathematics and Physics for engineering, Chemistry and Biology for health sciences, etc.); specialised courses may also demand additional evidence such as a portfolio for design or visual arts, a personal statement or interview for medicine, dentistry and some health‑related degrees, and some programmes accept national exams like China’s Gaokao for direct entry, while all applicants must provide official transcripts, proof of identity and, where applicable, evidence of prior tertiary study for credit‑transfer consideration, with application cycles opening in early March for Semester 1 and mid‑July for Semester 2.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Monash University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least an overall IELTS band score of 6.5 with no individual band below 6.0, a TOEFL iBT total score of 79 (minimum 19 in Reading, Listening and Speaking and 20 in Writing) or a TOEFL Essentials score of 8 (with a minimum 7 in Reading), a PTE Academic overall score of 58 with at least 50 in each communicative skill, or a Duolingo English Test overall score of 115; applicants who have completed at least one year of full‑time tertiary study (equivalent to 48 Monash credit points) or hold a degree that was taught and assessed entirely in English, or can provide an official certificate from their home institution confirming a CEFR C1 level, are eligible for a waiver of the test‑score requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- For international students at Monash University the annual tuition fees are quoted in Australian dollars (AUD) and vary by level and field of study: undergraduate courses in arts, business and social sciences are typically AUD 38,500 per year, engineering, information technology and health‑related programmes range from AUD 44,500 to AUD 55,000 per year, and postgraduate taught degrees are generally between AUD 42,000 and AUD 48,000 per year; the one‑time online application charge is AUD 115, and additional compulsory fees include the Student Services and Amenities Fee (approximately AUD 300 per year) and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) which costs about AUD 750 per year, bringing the total annual cost for a typical undergraduate arts student to roughly AUD 39,665 and for an engineering student to about AUD 56,415 when tuition, application, service and health‑cover fees are combined.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- A single international student at Monash University should budget roughly AUD 30,000 to 45,000 per year for all living costs (excluding tuition), which typically breaks down to about AUD 12,000‑20,000 for accommodation (e.g., share‑house rooms from ≈ $275 per week, 2‑bedroom private rentals from ≈ $565 per week, on‑campus options such as Clayton Residential Village from ≈ $331 per week or Clayton Urban Community from ≈ $399 per week), AUD 4,000‑5,000 for food, AUD 1,200‑1,800 for public‑transport travel within Melbourne, and AUD 600‑800 for Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) insurance, resulting in a total annual living expense in the $30 k‑$45 k range.

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa, which allows you to study at a CRICOS‑registered institution such as Monash University, requires you to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity to cover your tuition fees, a minimum living allowance of AUD 21,041 per year (plus a reasonable amount for travel and any dependents) and to maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the whole duration of your stay, while also meeting the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) criteria; once granted, visa holders may work up to 48 hours per fortnight while their course is in session and unlimited hours during recognised school holidays, with no work‑hour limit for students undertaking a master’s by research or a doctoral degree (and their eligible family members), and all conditions and updates are detailed on the official Department of Home Affairs site (immi.homeaffairs.gov.au) and the Study in Australia portal (studyinaustralia.gov.au). Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the official post‑study work visa for international students who have completed a degree‑level qualification at a CRICOS‑registered Australian institution such as Monash University; it is administered by the Department of Home Affairs and detailed on the Study in Australia website. Graduates apply under the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which grants unrestricted work rights and allows the visa holder’s partner and dependent children to join them, and the length of stay depends on the qualification: up to 2 years for a bachelor’s degree (including honours) and for coursework master’s degrees, up to 3 years for research master’s and PhD degrees, and from 1 July 2023 an additional 2‑year extension is available for graduates whose degree appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List, on top of any existing regional extensions of one to two years for those who have studied, lived and worked in designated regional areas.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Monash University provides a comprehensive suite of internship, co‑op and placement opportunities for both domestic and international students through its dedicated Internship and Placement Units and the Global Work‑Integrated Learning program, which includes undergraduate and graduate international internships across faculties such as Science, Medicine, Nursing and Health Sciences, allowing students to embed real‑world work experience directly into their degree pathways; the university’s latest graduate outcomes data show that 70.3 % of bachelor graduates secured full‑time employment within six months of completing their studies and 80.5 % of those who participated in work‑integrated learning programmes attained full‑time work in the same timeframe, leading to careers in sectors including medicine, nursing, biomedical science, nutrition, physiotherapy, psychology, engineering, business, information technology and other professional fields. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Australia earn a mean full‑time pre‑tax salary of about $54,000 for undergraduate degrees and roughly $78,000 for postgraduate qualifications, giving an overall average of approximately $58,000, which is lower than the $70,000–$90,000 earned by domestic graduates. Recent graduate outcome surveys show that around 70 % of international postgraduate coursework students are employed, though full‑time employment is nearer 44 % (rising to about 58 % in 2022), while research postgraduate graduates achieve full‑time rates of roughly 70 %–76 %. Labour‑market projections from Jobs and Skills Australia indicate the strongest demand for graduates in construction management, software and applications programming, health‑care and social assistance (especially aged‑care, disability care, registered nursing and child care), and education and training (notably education aides and primary school teachers), with these sectors collectively forecast to add over 250,000 new jobs by 2023.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Monash University’s International Student Services (ISS) – formerly known as the Monash International Student Service (MUISS) – runs a comprehensive support program for international students that includes a dedicated orientation week with welcome sessions, campus tours, and information on visas, health insurance and cultural adjustment, as well as ongoing orientation events throughout the year; a housing assistance service that helps students find on‑campus accommodation, connects them with the university’s residential colleges and provides guidance on off‑campus rentals and tenancy rights; a career development centre that offers specialised workshops, résumé and interview coaching, networking events with employers interested in hiring overseas talent, and access to Monash’s broader Careers & Employability platform; and a range‑up portal called Monash THRIVE International that aggregates resources on everything from academic support to wellbeing, giving students a single online hub for navigating university life in Australia. Đời sống & môi trường: Monash University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, featuring a wide range of clubs, societies and student communities—including the Vietnamese Students Association (VSA), which promotes Vietnamese‑Australian culture, organises cultural events, volunteering and charitable activities, and provides a supportive network for Vietnamese students through its Facebook page, email (vietnamese@monashclubs.org) and regular gatherings; all students can explore other cultural, academic and special‑interest clubs via the Monash Student Association portal, where the VSA is listed under the “Cultural” category alongside many other groups; to ensure a safe stay, Monash provides comprehensive safety resources such as 24‑hour emergency assistance (dial 000 in Australia), on‑campus security, safety and security briefings, and dedicated support services through Monash Connect, health, disability, legal and wellbeing teams, while encouraging common‑sense precautions and offering guidance on personal safety, accommodation security and emergency procedures for international students throughout their study period.
