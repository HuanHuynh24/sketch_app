**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Cornell University admits first‑year international undergraduates only for the fall semester, with two non‑binding application plans: Early Decision, which is binding if accepted, has an application‑and‑$85 fee (or fee waiver) deadline of November 1 and decisions are released in mid‑December, and Regular Decision, which is non‑binding, has an application‑and‑$85 fee deadline of January 2 with decisions announced in early April; applications are submitted through the Common Application (including the Cornell University essay and any college‑specific supplement), the Cornell Writing Supplement, and required financial‑aid forms such as the CSS Profile, while applicants to Architecture, Art, Landscape Architecture, Design & Environmental Analysis, Fashion Design, or Fashion Design Management must also upload portfolios, videos, or design challenges as specified; transfer applicants may apply for a spring intake with a March 15 deadline and receive decisions in May‑June, and all applicants must pay the $85 fee or obtain a waiver before the university will process their materials.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Cornell University requires all international undergraduate applicants to demonstrate a strong command of English; the requirement can be satisfied by being a U.S. citizen or permanent resident, having English as a native language, or having completed at least four years of secondary‑school instruction in English (or English as the primary language throughout secondary school). If none of these criteria apply, applicants must submit an English‑proficiency test score: for TOEFL, a minimum total of 100 for tests taken before January 2026 and a minimum iBT score of 5.0 (with a recommended 5.5) for tests taken in January 2026 or later; for IELTS, a minimum overall band of 7.5; for the Duolingo English Test, a minimum score of 130; and for Cambridge C1 Advanced or C2 Proficiency, a minimum of 191. Cornell does not list PTE or other exams as accepted alternatives.

**4. Học phí**

- Cornell University charges international (non‑resident) undergraduate students an annual tuition of $49,816 USD, to which a mandatory‑fees package of $1,024 USD (covering the student‑activity fee of $424 and the Cornell Health fee of $600) is added, resulting in a billed academic cost of $50,840 USD per year; the undergraduate application fee is $80 USD, and when the typical Cornell‑estimated living expenses for international students ($27,507 USD annually) are included, the total cost of attendance for an international undergrad is about $78,347 USD per year. For graduate students, the billed tuition is $73,946 USD, the same $1,024 USD mandatory‑fees apply, giving an academic total of $74,970 USD, and with the graduate‑level living estimate of $37,418 USD the full annual cost reaches roughly $112,388 USD; graduate application fees generally range from $80 to $85 USD. All amounts are expressed in United States dollars (USD).

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Cornell University is nationally ranked by U.S. News & World Report at #18 among U.S. national universities (its position has varied between 6th and 18th over the past decades), while globally it holds a QS World University Ranking of #20 and a Times Higher Education (THE) World University Ranking of #21 for 2024; in subject‑specific tables Cornell is placed in the top 20 for Business & Management (QS #12, THE ≈ #15), Engineering & Technology (QS #14, THE ≈ #25), Computer Science (QS #18), and the Arts & Humanities (THE ≈ #30), and it consistently appears in the top 30 for Life Sciences and Social Sciences; the university is regionally accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education, with its professional schools holding specialized accreditations such as ABET for engineering programs, AACSB for the Samuel Curtis Johnson Graduate School of Management, and ABA for the Law School, and roughly one‑fifth of its undergraduate enrollment and about one‑third of its graduate enrollment are international students.

**2. Student Visa (F-1) và Chính sách OPT**

- Student Visa (F-1): International students admitted to Cornell University must first provide the admissions office with official proof that they have sufficient funds to cover tuition, fees, living expenses and any dependents for at least the first academic year—typically bank statements, scholarship award letters, guarantor affidavits or loan documents that meet Cornell’s published cost‑of‑attendance figures; once the school verifies this financial support it issues a certified Form I‑20, which the student uses to pay the SEVIS I‑901 fee and to schedule a visa interview through the U.S. Department of State’s online portal (travel.state.gov), where the applicant must present the passport, DS‑160 confirmation, fee receipts and the original I‑20 together with the same financial documents that were used to obtain the I‑20; the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services website (uscis.gov) provides the official regulations on the required minimum financial amounts, how to maintain F‑1 status while studying, and the procedures for any future changes such as adding dependents or transferring schools. Sau tốt nghiệp (OPT/STEM OPT): The STEM OPT extension gives eligible F‑1 students who earned a U.S. bachelor’s, master’s or doctoral degree in a DHS‑approved STEM field an additional 24 months of post‑completion Optional Practical Training, allowing up to 36 months of total work authorization; to qualify a student must be in the first 12 months of regular post‑completion OPT (or in a cap‑gap extension), have a paid job of at least 20 hours per week directly related to the STEM degree, and the employer must be enrolled in E‑Verify, while the student must be physically present in the United States to file Form I‑765, must file the extension request up to 90 days before the current OPT end date and have USCIS receive it before that date, and must not have previously used a STEM extension for the same degree—though a second STEM extension is possible after completing a higher‑level qualifying degree.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm (CPT/OPT): International students at Cornell who use Curricular Practical Training (CPT) typically secure summer internships between their first and second years, with leading firms such as Amazon, Google, Meta, Intuit, Memorial Sloan Kettering and Grammarly hiring them for roles largely in data science and software development; after graduation, Cornell graduates enjoy a strong employment record—about 91 % of the Class of 2025 were employed or enrolled in graduate school within six months, and technical‑program alumni report roughly 89 % employment nine months after graduation (with the Jacobs dual‑MS tracks reaching 100 % and 82 % respectively), most entering technology, financial services, consulting, healthcare and entrepreneurship sectors and earning average starting salaries near $89 000, while CPT participation requires enrollment in a curricular‑related internship or co‑op, a detailed employer offer letter specifying dates, hours, duties and address, and issuance of a CPT‑authorized I‑20 by the university. Lương & thị trường: Cornell University reports that 91.3 % of the Class of 2025 were employed or enrolled in graduate school within six months of graduation, with full‑time U.S. employed graduates earning an average starting salary of $89,055; the most common hiring sectors are financial services, technology, consulting/professional services, human‑healthcare services and education, while the figures exclude international and military salaries. Complementing this, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics data shows that occupations in technology (software development, data analytics, cybersecurity), finance and accounting, professional consulting, engineering (mechanical, electrical, civil, computer), and healthcare (biotechnology, public health, pharmacy) are among the fastest‑growing and highest‑paying fields for recent graduates, with median entry‑level wages ranging from the mid‑$50 000s in many engineering roles to over $80 000 in technology and finance positions, underscoring strong labour‑market demand for both domestic and international graduates in those sectors.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Cornell’s Office of Global Learning (formerly the International Student and Scholar Services) serves as the central hub for all international students, offering a comprehensive orientation program that introduces newcomers to campus life, visa requirements, and academic resources; dedicated housing support through guidance on on‑campus and off‑campus options and coordination with the university’s housing office; and a suite of career services in partnership with Cornell Career Services, including a Career Development Toolkit, workshops, and one‑on‑one counseling that address the unique immigration and cultural considerations international students face; the office is located at 300 Caldwell Hall, Ithaca, NY 14853, and can be reached at +1 (607) 255‑5243 for assistance with status activation, SSN applications, dependents, and other student‑life needs. Đời sống & môi trường: Cornell University hosts a vibrant international community—roughly one‑fifth of its undergraduate population comes from abroad—and offers a wide array of student‑run clubs, including a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association that organizes cultural festivals, language‑exchange meet‑ups and networking events to connect Vietnamese students with peers and alumni; beyond this, the campus features more than a thousand organizations covering everything from academic societies to recreational groups, while Greek life remains a dominant social arena, with many students finding fraternities and sororities to be a primary gateway to campus nightlife and networking, yet concerns about safety persist, especially regarding alcohol‑related incidents, hazing and gender‑based risks, prompting Cornell to maintain a comprehensive safety infrastructure that includes a campus police department, the Good Samaritan protocol that protects students who seek emergency medical help from legal repercussions, and active hazing‑prevention programs administered by the Skorton Center for Health Initiatives, all designed to foster a secure environment while supporting the diverse social and cultural needs of its international and Vietnamese student communities.
