### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International applicants to Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin can enroll in the regular Medicine or Dentistry programmes in two intakes: the summer semester (starts in October) with an application deadline of 15 January, and the winter semester (starts in April) with a deadline of 15 July; applications are submitted through the Uni‑Assist portal, which charges a processing fee of roughly €75 plus an additional Charité application fee of about €75 (≈ €150 total), after which Uni‑Assist forwards the complete file (including certified school leaving certificates, German language proof such as TestDaF ≥ 16 or DSH ≥ 2, passport copy, CV and motivation letter) to Charité for evaluation and a decision is normally issued by late July for the summer intake and by late January for the winter intake; for the MSc International Health master’s programme the university offers an early‑admission deadline of 31 December, a regular deadline of 31 March, and a rolling‑admission window until 31 July (with the same Uni‑Assist fee and a semester contribution of about €400), and the same online upload‑and‑pay process applies.

Candidates need proof of English language proficiency (minimum IELTS 5.5, TOEFL iBT 72, or a DAAD language certificate at B2/C1 level) and, where the programme is taught in German, a German language certificate (generally Goethe‑Zertifikat C1 or TestDaF TD 4). **3. Tiếng Anh**

- Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin requires non‑native‑English applicants to prove English proficiency for degree programmes, accepting any of the following: IELTS ≥ 5.5, TOEFL iBT ≥ 72, Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE) ≥ 51, or Duolingo English Test ≥ 105. Applicants are exempt from these test scores if they are native English speakers, have completed a bachelor’s or master’s degree taught entirely in English, hold a high‑school diploma (or equivalent) in which English was the language of instruction, or have transcripts showing at least 20 ECTS (or equivalent) of English‑language coursework at a recognised university.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For international students at Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin the yearly cost of living is generally covered by the required blocked‑account balance of about €12 000, which is the amount German authorities accept as proof of sufficient funds for a year of study. In practice this amount is spent roughly as follows: accommodation (shared apartments or student housing) costs around €500 per month (€6 000 per year); food and groceries amount to roughly €250 per month (€3 000 per year); public‑transport expenses are covered by the semester ticket included in the student registration fee, about €84 per semester (≈ €168 per year); statutory health insurance for students is €110 per month (€1 320 per year); and the remaining €400–600 can be used for study materials, phone/internet and occasional leisure activities, bringing the total annual living expenses to approximately €11 000–12 000, which aligns with the €12 000 blocked‑account requirement.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin offers a Master of Science in International Health for international students that follows the European Credit Transfer System (ECTS) and requires a total of 90 ECTS, divided into a 20‑ECTS Core Course, 20 ECTS of advanced elective modules, 20 ECTS of relevant professional experience, and a 30‑ECTS research thesis with oral defence; admission prerequisites include a degree in a health‑related, natural‑science or social‑science field (e.g., medicine, nursing, psychology, pharmacy, biology, anthropology, economics, health education or programme management) and proof of English proficiency (TOEFL ≥ 550/213 or IELTS ≥ 6.0), while each advanced module additionally demands successful completion of the Core Course and basic knowledge in international/global health, with individual modules ranging from 1.5 to 3 ECTS and specifying their own learning outcomes—such as the ability to design, manage and evaluate health projects, conduct qualitative research, or write biomedical manuscripts—and clear prerequisites, for example the Psychiatric Diseases module (6 ECTS) requires basic knowledge of neuropsychological anamnesis and aims to enable students to understand, diagnose and propose treatment strategies for major psychiatric conditions.

- Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin is consistently ranked among the world’s leading medical schools: in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Subject Rankings it placed 27th out of 1,059 universities in the “Clinical and Health” category for 2024 and 29th out of 1,230 institutions in the “Medical and Health” category for 2026, retaining the top position among German universities in both editions; in the QS World University Rankings by Subject the faculty is listed in the top‑50 for Medicine, ranking around 36th in the 2024 edition, making it one of the highest‑ranked German medical schools in that system as well; the institution holds unconditional system accreditation granted by the German Accreditation Council and the relevant medical accreditation bodies, which was renewed in May 2022 and is valid through 2029, marking Charité as the first German medical school to receive such a status since 2015.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/student visa: International students admitted to Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin must prove they have sufficient financial means for the duration of their stay, typically by opening a blocked account (Sperrkonto) that contains the required yearly amount—currently €11,208 (equivalent to €1,091 per month) as stipulated by the German residency regulations and reflected in DAAD guidelines—allowing monthly withdrawals of up to €992; alternatively, a scholarship, a formal guarantee from a parent or third‑party sponsor, or a combination of these can be accepted, provided the documentation shows the total funds and the monthly availability; once the student visa is issued, the holder may work in Germany for up to 120 full days (or 240 half days) per calendar year, which can be used to supplement living costs while studying. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin may remain in Germany after completing a recognized degree by converting their student residence permit into a post‑study work permit, often called the 18‑month job‑seeker residence permit; the allowance lasts for a maximum of 18 months from the official graduation date and cannot be extended, during which time the holder may work full‑time in any job while searching for a position that corresponds to the qualification, and to be eligible they must present an official proof of graduation (diploma or transcript), a valid passport, proof of sufficient financial resources (such as a blocked account or a declaration of commitment), continuous health‑insurance coverage for the entire period, a current registration certificate (Meldebescheinigung), and biometric photos, and the application must be submitted to the local Ausländerbehörde before the student permit expires.
