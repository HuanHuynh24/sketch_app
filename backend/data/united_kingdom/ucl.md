### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply (UCAS)**

- UCL admits international students for two main entry points – the Autumn (September) term and the Spring (January) term – with the online application portal opening in February for September entry (deadline 31 March) and in August for January entry (deadline 30 September); all undergraduate applications, including those from overseas, must be made through UCAS using the UCL code U80, paying the standard UCAS fee (£26 for 2025‑26) plus a non‑refundable £75 UCL deposit when you accept an offer, after which you submit any required English‑language evidence, apply for a visa (no earlier than three months before the start date) and arrange accommodation (the accommodation deadline for September starters is 10 June), while the university gives equal consideration to all applications received by the UCAS January deadline (mid‑January) for most courses and to the specific October deadline for Medicine.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- UCL admits international undergraduates on the basis of A‑levels, the International Baccalaureate or equivalent qualifications, with most programmes requiring A‑level results between ABB and AAA (or the IB equivalent of 34‑40 points overall, including the requisite number of points in three Higher‑Level subjects) and, where specific subjects are required, the corresponding A‑level or AP/IB subject grades; applicants must submit official high‑school transcripts (học bạ) showing overall and subject‑specific results, meet the English‑language proficiency requirements, and for courses such as Architecture, Fine Art or Design also provide a portfolio and may be invited to interview, while postgraduate entry typically demands a UK upper‑second‑class (2:1) honours degree or its international equivalent (generally a GPA of around 3.4‑3.6 on a 4.0 scale) together with relevant transcripts and, where relevant, a research proposal, portfolio or interview.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- UCL requires all applicants who are not nationals of a UK Home Office “majority English‑speaking” country (e.g., UK, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland and several Caribbean nations) to provide recent proof of English proficiency, and the test result must be no older than two years before the start of the course. For undergraduate entry the university sets four levels of requirement: Level 1 and Level 2 accept an overall IELTS score of 6.0 with a minimum of 5.5 in each component; Level 3 and Level 4 require an overall IELTS of 6.5 with at least 6.0 in each component. Equivalent TOEFL iBT scores are 4.0 overall (minimum 4.0 each) for Levels 1‑2, 4.5 overall (minimum 4.0 each) for Level 2, and 5.0 overall (minimum 4.5 each) for Levels 3‑4 (the TOEFL iBT MyBestScore is not accepted). For PTE Academic the thresholds are 67 overall with 59 in each skill for Levels 1‑2 and 75 overall with 67 in each skill for Levels 3‑4. Duolingo English Test scores are not listed as an accepted qualification, so it is not a recognised option for UCL. Applicants who hold an unconditional UCL degree offer, or who are nationals of the listed majority‑English‑speaking countries, are exempt from providing any English‑language test evidence.

**4. Học phí**

- UCL charges overseas (international) students tuition in British pounds per academic year, with the 2026‑27 first‑year undergraduate fee for overseas entrants set by programme and generally ranging between £20,000 and £30,000 (typical programmes are around £28,500) and, because most undergraduate courses have a cohort guarantee, the total tuition cost for a three‑year degree is roughly three times that annual amount; postgraduate taught overseas fees are also quoted per year and usually fall between £26,000 and £33,000 depending on the subject, while successful overseas applicants must pay a tuition‑fee deposit of 10 % of the first‑year tuition (or a flat £500 for modular programmes) on acceptance, there is no separate nominal application fee, and additional charges can include an Advanced Funding Expense (AFE) for research degrees set in bands of £1,000, £2,500, £5,000 or £10,000 (or higher) and standard living costs such as accommodation, food and transport that total about £4,900 per academic year.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt & IHS**

- For an international student at UCL the typical annual living costs break down as follows: accommodation in a self‑catered hall is about £296 per week, which works out to roughly £11,544 for the 39‑week academic year; food averages £49 per week or about £1,901 per academic year; a Zone 1‑2 student travelcard (monthly Oyster photocard) costs £29.80 per week, equivalent to roughly £1,162 per academic year; and the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) for a Student Visa is £776 per year. Adding these core items gives an estimated total of around £15,383 per academic year (≈£15,400 when rounded).

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to UCL must hold the standard UK entry qualifications for undergraduate study, and if their national qualifications do not meet the required standard they can enrol in the one‑year Undergraduate Preparatory Certificate (UPC) foundation programme to gain the prerequisite knowledge and credits; the BA in International Social and Political Studies is a 180‑credit degree structured over three years, with Year 1 comprising 30–45 language credits, 45–60 humanities or social‑science specialism credits and a 30‑credit core module (ESPS0001: Introduction to History, Law, Politics and Philosophy), Year 2 offering 45 language credits, 60 specialism credits and 15 optional credits, and Year 3 providing 15–30 language credits, a 30‑credit dissertation and 60–75 specialism credits, all delivered through a mix of lectures, seminars, tutorials and independent study; the curriculum is interdisciplinary, integrating language proficiency with deep study of historical, legal, political and philosophical concepts, and the learning outcomes are to develop critical analytical skills, interdisciplinary knowledge, advanced research abilities and the capacity to produce independent scholarly work such as a dissertation, preparing graduates for international careers.

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: International students applying for a UK Student visa to study at University College London must demonstrate that they have enough funds to cover both tuition fees for the first year of their course and living costs, and the evidence must show the required amount was held in a qualifying account for at least 28 days before the visa application is submitted. The maintenance requirement for London‑based institutions is £1,334 per month for up to nine months, giving a total of £12,006, which is added to the full first‑year tuition fee (or the portion not covered by a scholarship or sponsor) to calculate the total amount that must be proven. Acceptable proof includes recent bank statements covering the 28‑day period, a letter from a financial sponsor or loan provider confirming the amount available, or a scholarship award letter that clearly states the funds allocated for tuition and living costs; the funds must be readily accessible and not tied up in assets such as property. If the applicant already has a confirmed UK scholarship that pays the tuition fee in full, they still need to meet the £12,006 maintenance requirement unless the scholarship explicitly covers living costs as well. All documents must be in English (or accompanied by a certified translation) and submitted with the visa application, and the Home Office may request additional evidence after the initial submiss... Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): The UK Graduate Route allows international students who have successfully completed an eligible undergraduate, master’s or doctoral degree at a UK higher‑education provider with a track record of compliance to remain in the UK after study to work or look for work, and the standard post‑study work period is two years for graduates of bachelor’s or master’s programmes and three years for PhD graduates (this will be reduced to 18 months for non‑doctoral graduates for applications submitted from 1 January 2027); to be eligible you must hold a valid Tier 4/Student visa at the time of application, have made the application from within the UK, have been in the UK for at least 12 months if your course lasted longer than 12 months (or for the full length of a 12‑month or shorter course), must not have previously held permission under the Graduate Route or the Doctorate Extension Scheme, and must meet the other standard immigration requirements such as not having overstayed and having a clean immigration history.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: UCL reports that 90 % of its graduates are in work or further study within 15 months of finishing their degrees, with 65.1 % in highly‑skilled employment and 12.4 % undertaking postgraduate study, and it provides a dedicated programme of career events, global‑internship resources and specialist support for international students to secure internships, co‑op placements and graduate roles both in the UK and abroad. Lương & thị trường: In the most recent UK graduate labour‑market data, international graduates are typically starting on salaries of about £35,000 median, with the highest‑paying entry‑level sectors still being investment banking (around £60,000), law (≈£56,000) and consulting (≈£50,000); UCL graduates specifically report starting salaries in the £30,000‑£40,000 range, with 93 % securing employment within six months, 54 % remaining in the UK—particularly London—and 59 % moving into consulting or finance roles; demand remains strongest for technology and data positions (engineers, data analysts, cybersecurity and AI‑focused roles), engineering and industrial projects that require visa sponsorship, and financial services, although hiring volumes have fallen in accounting and professional services (over a 30 % drop) and many middle‑skill occupations have become ineligible for Skilled Worker visas, shifting some graduates toward the care sector and raising the graduate‑level salary thresholds to £41,700 for main and £33,400 for discounted routes.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: UCL’s International Student Team runs a dedicated International Student Office that provides a comprehensive orientation programme – the International Student Orientation Course – which familiarises new students with life at UCL, UK healthcare, banking, safety and academic resources, and it also offers ongoing practical guidance throughout the year; the team helps students secure university accommodation through an online housing application form and gives advice on private‑sector options, while working closely with UCL’s Careers Service to deliver tailored career support for internationals, including CV and interview workshops, employer networking events, advice on graduate‑level work visas and post‑study work opportunities, and one‑to‑one career coaching. Đời sống & môi trường: UCL’s vibrant campus life is supported by a Students’ Union that runs more than 450 student‑led groups, ranging from sports teams to cultural societies, and among these is a dedicated Vietnamese Society that brings together Vietnamese undergraduates and postgraduates for regular social gatherings, Tet celebrations, language‑exchange evenings and networking events, helping newcomers build a supportive community while sharing their culture with the wider UCL body; the university’s International Student Support team further assists all overseas students with practical matters such as healthcare registration, banking, transport, visa guidance and personal safety, offering a 24‑hour campus security service, a safety‑alert app, well‑being counselling and orientation programmes that introduce safe travel routes, emergency procedures and local resources, ensuring that Vietnamese students and the broader international cohort can enjoy a secure, inclusive environment while taking full advantage of the numerous clubs, societies and cultural activities available across the Bloomsbury and UCL East campuses.
