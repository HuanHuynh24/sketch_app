### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- University of South Australia (UniSA) welcomes international students into two main intakes each year – Semester 1 (March) and Semester 2 (July); to secure a place you should submit your application at least two months before the start of the chosen semester, which translates into a practical deadline of early January for the March intake and early May for the July intake. The online application fee for overseas applicants is AUD 85 (payable when you submit the online form). The application process is: 1) identify the desired programme on the UniSA website; 2) create an account and complete the secure online application (or apply through an authorised education agent); 3) enter personal, academic and English‑language details, upload certified copies of transcripts, passport, English test results and any required prerequisite documentation; 4) pay the AUD 85 application fee; 5) submit the form and wait for an offer letter; 6) once an offer is received, log back into the portal to accept the offer, upload any additional documents, agree to the terms and pay the tuition deposit; 7) apply for an Australian student visa (submitting the Confirmation of Enrolment and other visa‑required paperwork). After the visa is granted you will receive enrolment instructions from UniSA to finalize your registration and attend orientation.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- The University of South Australia requires international applicants to provide official academic transcripts and evidence of English proficiency, with a typical IELTS requirement of 6.0 overall and at least 6.0 in each component; for other English tests the university uses equivalent scores. For the International Baccalaureate the minimum score is 26 points across the best six subjects, while for Australian A‑Levels a minimum of six points (calculated from the best three subjects on a 6‑5‑4‑3‑2‑1 scale) is needed, although many programmes set higher thresholds and some may require specific prerequisite subjects. UniSA calculates a Grade Point Average on a 1‑to‑7 scale, and most undergraduate courses expect a GPA that meets or exceeds the minimum points noted for the qualification used, with higher GPA expectations for competitive or specialised programmes. Where relevant, additional entry criteria such as a portfolio, interview, or audition apply only to certain creative, design or performance‑based courses; otherwise no portfolio or interview is required. Pathway options and diploma programmes are also available for students who do not meet direct entry standards.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- The University of South Australia requires most undergraduate and postgraduate programmes for international students to demonstrate a minimum overall IELTS score of 6.5 (with no band lower than 6.0) unless the applicant has completed at least three years of full‑time study in an English‑speaking country where the medium of instruction is English, in which case the IELTS requirement is waived; a lower IELTS threshold of 6.0 is applied for some programmes that accept a 6.0 overall score, also subject to the same waiver conditions. Equivalent scores are accepted for other tests: a minimum TOEFL iBT total of 79 (with at least 19 in Reading and Listening, 20 in Speaking and 21 in Writing), a minimum PTE Academic score of 58, and a minimum Duolingo English Test score of 115. Additionally, certain professional accreditation pathways (such as those approved by Exercise & Sport Science Australia) require a higher IELTS benchmark of 7.0. Waivers are also granted for candidates whose prior qualification was taught entirely in English, for example when the qualification was obtained in Australia, the UK, the USA, Canada, New Zealand, Ireland, Singapore or other recognized English‑medium institutions.

**4. Học phí**

- The University of South Australia charges international students tuition in Australian dollars (A$) and lists an indicative annual fee for each program; for example, the Bachelor of Clinical Exercise Physiology (Honours) is A$39 700 per year, the Bachelor of Exercise and Sport Science is A$31 900 per year, and master‑level courses such as the Master of Advanced Clinical Physiotherapy and the Master of Medical Sonography are A$41 400 and A$31 900 per year respectively, with fees increasing each calendar year but never exceeding the fee for new entrants; a typical three‑year undergraduate degree therefore costs roughly A$95 700 in total tuition (A$31 900 × 3), while a one‑year master’s program costs about A$41 400, and the university’s standard application fee for international applicants is A$150, with additional charges that may include a student services levy and compulsory overseas student health cover.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa permits international students to study full‑time at a CRICOS‑registered provider such as the University of South Australia, provided they hold a valid Confirmation of Enrolment and meet the genuine temporary entrant criteria, and it requires proof of financial capacity – typically evidence that the applicant (and any dependents) can cover the cost of travel to Australia, 12 months of tuition (or a pro‑rated amount for shorter courses), and 12 months of living expenses (generally estimated at AUD 21 000–30 000 per year for a single student, plus additional amounts for dependents and school‑age children) as well as any required overseas student health cover; mandatory OSHC must be purchased before arrival and remain in force for the whole stay with no coverage gaps, and the start date of the OSHC must coincide with the date of entry, not merely the course start date, while work rights allow up to 48 hours of paid work per fortnight while the course is in session, unlimited hours for research masters, PhD students, during official semester breaks and for compulsory CRICOS‑listed work placements, and all students must continue to meet enrolment, attendance and OSHC obligations throughout their visa period. Sau tốt nghiệp: International students who graduate from a degree‑level course at the University of South Australia can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) in the Post‑Study Work (Post‑Higher Education Work) stream within six months of completing their studies; the visa grants them the right to live, work and study anywhere in Australia for an initial two‑year period (four years for doctoral graduates), with unrestricted work hours and the ability to include eligible family members, and from 1 July 2023 graduates holding an eligible qualification on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List receive an additional two‑year extension of their post‑study work rights (bringing the total to up to four years for most degree holders), while those who have studied, lived and worked in a designated regional area can obtain a further one‑year (or, for some regional graduates, a second two‑year) extension under the Second Post‑Higher Education Work stream, allowing a maximum stay of up to five years in total before they must transition to another visa such as the skilled‑work (subclass 482) or permanent‑residence pathways.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of South Australia (UniSA) embeds work‑integrated learning throughout its programmes, offering international students a range of optional and required internships, 150‑hour industry placements, co‑ops, short‑term overseas work‑integrated learning and voluntary placements that are coordinated with more than 2,500 partner organisations worldwide; these experiences are built into courses in business, engineering, health, tourism, creative industries and other fields and are supported by the UniSA+ career‑readiness program and a global alumni network of over 200,000 graduates. UniSA consistently ranks at the top of South Australia for graduate employment outcomes, with the QILT Graduate Outcomes Survey showing the highest full‑time employment rate among SA public universities for 2017‑19 undergraduate graduates and a national #2 ranking for health‑related postgraduate graduates (physiotherapy, occupational therapy and related disciplines) in 2018‑20, and recent data indicate that roughly 85‑90 % of its graduates secure full‑time work within four months of completing their degree, entering sectors such as finance, engineering, health services, tourism, logistics, IT, marketing and public‑sector roles. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries in Australia remain strong, with the median full‑time starting salary for undergraduate degree holders in 2021 at about $65,000 a year, rising to roughly $70,000 for engineering graduates and reaching $100,000 for dentistry, $76,000 for medicine, $72,000 for teacher education and $70,000 for engineering, while postgraduate coursework and research graduates earn median salaries of $89,700 and $95,000 respectively; labour‑market data show that the occupations with the greatest demand for new entrants—particularly for international graduates—include technicians and trades workers (such as architectural, building and surveying technicians, carpenters, electricians, plumbers and structural steel and welding trades), community and personal service roles, construction managers and project administrators, as well as health and education professions, all of which are highlighted by Jobs and Skills Australia’s vacancy and shortage indexes and align with South Australia’s current low unemployment rate of about 4 percent and its strategic focus on expanding the defence, space and construction sectors.
