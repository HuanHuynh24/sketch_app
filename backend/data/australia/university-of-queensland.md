**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- The University of Queensland accepts international undergraduate applicants through the Queensland Tertiary Admissions Centre (QTAC) for two main intakes—Semester 1 (starting February) and Semester 2 (starting July)—with typical QTAC closing dates in early May for Semester 1 and early October for Semester 2 (exact dates vary by programme, so check the QTAC key dates), while most postgraduate coursework programmes are applied for directly to UQ (and some specialised courses via QTAC); the current non‑refundable application charge for international candidates is A$150 per application (unless a scholarship waiver is provided), and the application process generally follows these steps: (1) contact the relevant school or faculty to confirm entry requirements, (2) create a UQ Applications account (or use an authorised UQ agent if required), (3) start the online application and upload certified transcripts, English‑language test results and any other required documents (PDFs are preferred), (4) pay the A$150 fee by bank draft, Mastercard or Visa, (5) submit the completed application before the programme‑specific deadline, after which you will receive a confirmation email and, if successful, an offer that can be used to apply for a student visa.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to the University of Queensland must meet both academic and English‑language standards that vary by level and programme: for undergraduate study they need an overall senior‑school score equivalent to Queensland Year 12 plus a minimum English proficiency (e.g., IELTS 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5 or Academic English 7), and most courses also require a calculated GPA on UQ’s 7‑point scale (typically a minimum of 4.0 – 6.0 depending on the degree, with high‑competitiveness courses such as Occupational Therapy, Advanced Science or Music demanding a GPA of 6.0 and, for Music, a portfolio audition/interview); postgraduate applicants must submit official transcripts that will be converted to the 7‑point scale and meet the programme‑specific minimum GPA (often 4.0 – 5.0) together with the same English‑language benchmarks; professional programmes such as the Doctor of Medicine (provisional entry) additionally require a competitive UCAT‑ANZ aggregate score and successful performance in a multiple‑mini interview (MMI), while other specialised fields may have inherent subject prerequisites (e.g., General Mathematics or Mathematical Methods) and may request a portfolio or practical test where relevant.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Queensland must demonstrate a minimum overall IELTS score of 6.5 with at least 6.0 in each sub‑band (or an equivalent result on another accepted test); for TOEFL iBT the university accepts only the official score report on the 1‑30 scale and generally requires an overall score of around 79 with minimum sub‑scores of 13‑15 in each component; Pearson Test of English (PTE) Academic is also accepted, with a typical minimum overall score of 58 and at least 50 in each sub‑skill; the Duolingo English Test is recognised and usually requires a minimum overall score of about 115; however, a test‑based requirement can be waived if the applicant has completed at least one year (or a full degree) of full‑time study at an accredited university or higher‑education institution where English was the language of instruction and assessment, provided the study was undertaken in a designated English‑speaking country and within the five‑year period prior to enrolment (or meets similar residency‑based criteria).

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Student visa (subclass 500) allows you to study at a CRICOS‑registered institution such as the University of Queensland and requires you to provide evidence of financial capacity that covers tuition fees, a minimum living allowance (approximately AU$21,041 per year for the primary applicant, plus additional amounts for a partner and any dependent children), the cost of Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the whole duration of your stay, and a return‑air‑ticket or sufficient funds for travel; you must maintain OSHC throughout your course, and you are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while your studies are in session (with unlimited hours during scheduled breaks), although students undertaking a master’s‑by‑research or doctoral program have no work‑hour limit, and family members on the visa have the same work restrictions. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of the University of Queensland may remain in Australia on the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) under the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which grants unrestricted work rights and can include eligible family members; the permitted duration depends on the highest qualification obtained – typically two years for a bachelor’s degree (including honours), up to three years for a master’s (with research degrees often receiving the longer period), and three to four years for a doctoral qualification, while Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders may stay for up to five years and, from 1 July 2023, degrees listed on the Eligible Qualifications List can qualify for up to six years of post‑study work rights, and a second regional stream is available for those who studied and live in a designated regional area, adding an additional one to two years.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: At the University of Queensland, international on‑shore medical graduates enjoy very high internship uptake, with more than 90 % of the 2018 cohort and 94 % of the 2023 cohort securing hospital internships in Australia the year after graduation, virtually all in Queensland and a sizable share—about one‑third to one‑half—placed in regional locations; between 2018 and 2024 a total of 452 on‑shore international medical students obtained Queensland internships, split 52 % regional and 48 % metropolitan. Beyond medicine, UQ’s overall graduate employment outcomes are strong: bachelor‑degree graduates recorded a full‑time employment rate of 78.3 % in 2012 and, according to the 2021 QILT Graduate Outcomes Survey, 71.4 % were employed full‑time four months after graduation, rising to 92.3 % three years after graduation—the highest rate in Queensland. Work‑Integrated Learning (WIL) is a central pathway, with roughly 32 % of coursework students enrolled in a WIL course in 2021, and the university has invested almost $12 million in employability initiatives such as Career Development Learning and placement programs, ensuring robust internship, co‑op and placement opportunities that translate into graduate employment rates above the national average. Lương & thị trường: University of Queensland graduates under 25 entering their first full‑time job in 2012 earned median starting salaries of about AU$58,000 for men and AU$52,000 for women, well above the national median of AU$52,000, with the highest‑paid roles reported as dental practitioners (≈AU$250,000), mining‑engineer consultants (≈AU$130,000), engineering assistants (≈AU$115,000), environmental advisors (≈AU$115,000) and geologists (≈AU$110,000); more recent national data show that three years after graduation 91.5 % of Australian graduates are in full‑time work earning a median salary of roughly AU$80,000, while international graduates in full‑time Australian employment earn substantially less, with a 2018 mean full‑time pre‑tax salary of about AU$58,000 (undergraduates AU$54,000, postgraduates AU$78,000) and a 2024 median that remains below domestic peers, especially in Business and Management where international undergraduates earn about AU$12,000 less than domestic graduates; labour‑market demand remains strongest for health‑related professions such as dentistry, pharmacy and medicine, and for technical fields like mining engineering, geology and environmental advisory, reflecting the skills gaps identified by Jobs and Skills Australia.
