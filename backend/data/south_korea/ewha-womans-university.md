**Tóm tắt thông tin trường**

- Ewha Womans University (이화여자대학교) is a private women’s university located in Seoul, South Korea (Seodaemun‑gu, 52 Ewhayeodae‑gil, postcode 03760), and its official website is https://www.ewha.ac.kr/.

## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Ewha Womans University admits international students for two regular semesters – a Spring intake (starting March) and a Fall intake (starting September); for the Spring 2026 term the online application must be completed by 17:00 KST on 27 October 2025 (with hard‑copy documents mailed by 17:00 KST on 28 October 2025), while the Fall 2026 deadline is typically set for the end of July 2026 (around 31 July 17:00 KST) and follows the same schedule; the application fee is 80,000 KRW (approximately USD 70) and must be paid through the Jinhak apply portal (or directly to the Ewha bank account for applicants abroad) before the deadline; the application process requires creating an online account, completing the electronic application form, uploading all required academic and language documents, paying the fee, submitting the form, and then sending either scanned copies (for those unable to use the online system) or the original hard‑copy documents to the Office of Graduate School of International Studies by the specified submission deadline, after which applicants await the admission decision via the university’s website or personal e‑mail.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Ewha Womans University accepts international undergraduate and graduate applicants who have completed the required prior education with a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 on a 4.0 scale (equivalent to 75 points out of 100) and can provide official, apostilled transcripts (học bạ) from their most recent institution; undergraduate freshmen must submit a copy of their high‑school diploma or graduation certificate, the high‑school transcript, and evidence of language proficiency in English or Korean (such as TOEFL, IELTS, TOPIK or Ewha Language Center certification), while transfer students must also provide transcripts from all previously attended colleges; graduate applicants must submit a bachelor’s degree certificate, an official transcript showing cumulative GPA, and, for Ph.D. programs, a copy of the master’s thesis or other research publication, together with a recent English or Korean language test result, and may be asked to submit a research proposal, portfolio (for design, arts or architecture programmes) and attend an interview if the department requires it; all documents must be authenticated with an apostille or the university’s own verification stamp, and any additional prerequisite courses or subject‑specific requirements (e.g., math or science grades for engineering, or art‑related work for creative majors) are detailed on a per‑programme basis.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- Ewha Womans University requires international applicants to demonstrate proficiency in either Korean or English, with the specific thresholds varying by program level. For undergraduate freshmen, Korean proficiency must reach TOPIK Level 4 (or the equivalent Korean‑language program level 4) before graduation, while English scores are mandatory only for the Division of International Studies (TOEFL iBT 80 or higher, IELTS 6.0 or higher; home‑edition iBT accepted, MyBest scores not accepted) and optional for other majors (the same score minima apply if submitted). For undergraduate transfers and graduate applicants, any one of the following satisfies the language requirement: TOPIK Level 4 or higher, completion of a Korean‑language program (Level 4 or above), TOEFL iBT 80 or higher, IELTS 6.5 or higher (Academic only), New TEPS 266 or higher, or TOEIC 705 or higher; institutional score reporting is required for TOEFL iBT. Applicants who have completed high‑school or university education conducted entirely in Korean or English, or who receive a university‑level recognition that their language ability is equivalent to the listed standards, are exempt from submitting test scores. PTE, Duolingo, and other English tests are not listed among the accepted examinations, so they are not considered valid for admission.

**4. Học phí**

- Ewha Womans University charges international students an annual tuition of roughly 4,300,000 – 4,700,000 KRW (about US $3,850) for most undergraduate and graduate programs, with higher‑priced majors ranging from 5,000,000 to 5,300,000 KRW; the mandatory application fee is 100,000 KRW, and an optional health‑center fee is 23,200 KRW, while on‑campus housing costs about 1,396,560 – 2,172,470 KRW per semester for a single room (or 1,400,000 KRW for a twin room), bringing the typical total annual outlay—including tuition, application, health‑center and average housing—to approximately 5.5 – 7.0 million KRW.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Ewha Womans University in Seoul is fully accredited by South Korea’s Ministry of Education and the Korean Council for University Education, and its professional schools hold additional international accreditations such as AACSB for the College of Business, ABET for its engineering programmes and Korean Accreditation Board of Nursing Education for nursing; in the latest QS World University Rankings (2026) it sits in the 401‑410 global band and is ranked within the world‑wide top 200 in 21 subject areas, notably Art & Design, English Language & Literature, Modern Languages, Performing Arts, Communication & Media Studies, Education, Social Policy & Administration and Nursing, with Education, Nursing and Social Policy & Administration placed in the 50‑100 range, while the Times Higher Education (THE) subject rankings placed Ewha third among South Korean universities for Education in the 2019 table; the university maintains a highly international campus with students from over 70 countries presently enrolled and has produced 1,955 international graduates from 96 nations since its first foreign student arrived in 1951.

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): International students admitted to Ewha Womans University must apply for a D‑2 student visa through the Korean embassy or consulate in their home country using the online HiKorea portal (studyinkorea.go.kr) to download the visa‑application form and submit a Certificate of Admission and Ewha’s Business Registration Certificate; financial proof is required to demonstrate the ability to cover tuition and living expenses, typically by showing a bank balance of roughly USD 9,000–10,000 (or the equivalent in local currency) for at least one month, or a confirmed scholarship covering the same amount, and students may work up to 20 hours per week after registering for a residence‑status (ARC) and obtaining a work‑permit from the university’s international office. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): International graduates from Ewha Womans University can obtain a D‑10 Job‑Seeker visa for up to three years after graduation (the standard period for Korean university graduates), provided they hold at least a bachelor’s degree and can show sufficient financial means unless they are exempted by Korean university graduation or TOPIK 4+ proficiency, in which case the financial proof is waived for the first three years; the visa may be applied for before graduation with an expected‑graduation certificate or within 15 days after receiving the diploma, and it allows paid internships that must be reported within 14 days, after which holders can transition to an E‑7 work visa by having a Korean employer submit the E‑7 application while the D‑10 remains valid, after which the applicant receives an updated ARC showing E‑7 status and may commence full‑time employment.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Ewha Womans University’s Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS) mandates that all students—including international students—complete at least one credit‑bearing domestic or overseas internship, with the Global Career Management Center coordinating placements through more than 200 partner organizations worldwide; the school reports that over 95 % of GSIS graduates obtain employment or continue to further study within six months of graduation, entering a wide range of fields such as international organizations (e.g., United Nations, World Bank, WTO), Korean government ministries (e.g., Foreign Affairs and Trade, Strategy and Finance), research institutes and think‑tanks (e.g., Korea Institute for International Economic Policy), major corporations (e.g., SK Telecom, Arthur Anderson, IMM Asset Management), media companies (e.g., MBC, CBS), and NGOs (e.g., World Vision, Save the Children), reflecting strong internship‑to‑career pathways across diplomacy, trade, finance, consulting, communications and development sectors. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from South Korean universities are increasingly entering a tight labour market where demand is strongest in engineering, medical and pharmaceutical fields, followed by education, with employment rates of roughly 70 % or higher in those sectors, while overall hiring for foreign‑trained graduates is about one‑third of job‑seekers; starting salaries for these graduates typically range from KRW 30 million to KRW 35 million per year in entry‑level positions, climbing to KRW 40 million‑45 million for high‑demand occupations such as engineering, medical research and pharmaceuticals, and the Korean government’s E‑7 work‑visa program facilitates longer‑term employment for those who meet language and skill requirements, enabling many Ewha Women’s University international studies alumni to secure roles in multinational corporations, government agencies, international organizations and media outlets across the country.
