### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International applicants to Humboldt Universität zu Berlin submit their files online through the uni‑assist portal: they first register, log in, create a new application, choose “Humboldt‑Universität zu Berlin” under the semester offers, select the desired degree and semester, answer all questionnaire items, upload all required documents as PDFs (including certified translations for non‑German/English papers) and then proceed to checkout to pay the uni‑assist processing fee (typically €75 for bachelor programmes and €100 for master programmes). The application deadline is a strict cut‑off based on the receipt of the complete, fee‑paid dossier: for the winter semester the window runs from 1 June to 15 July, and for the summer semester from 1 December to 15 January; applications received after these dates are not considered. After the university has verified the formal admission requirements, successful candidates receive an admission letter electronically and must confirm their enrolment within the prescribed acceptance period.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Humboldt Universität zu Berlin requires proof of English proficiency for all master’s programmes that are taught in English, and the accepted certificates are IELTS (typically a minimum overall band of 6.5), TOEFL‑iBT (usually at least 80‑90 total points), and Cambridge English qualifications such as FCE, CAE or CPE; TestDaF is listed only for German‑language proof. The university does not list PTE Academic or Duolingo English Test among the accepted exams, so these are not considered valid for admission. A waiver of the English‑language requirement is possible when the applicant’s most recent bachelor’s degree was awarded by a university where the instruction language was English, provided that this is documented on the transcript or a language‑of‑instruction confirmation; otherwise, the standard IELTS/TOEFL/Cambridge evidence must be submitted. All undergraduate programmes at Humboldt are taught exclusively in German and therefore require German C1 proficiency rather than any English test.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to Humboldt‑Universität zu Berlin must hold a recognized higher‑education entrance qualification (validated through the anabin database or uni‑assist) and, for all German‑taught programmes, demonstrate German language proficiency at the C1 level (e.g., Goethe‑Zertifikat C1, telc C1, DSH‑1 or TestDaF ≥ 3 in each section); once admitted, curricula are organised in ECTS‑based modules where 1 ECTS equals 30 hours of total student work and “semester points” (SP) are used interchangeably with 1 SP ≈ 1.5 ECTS, typically ranging from 6 to 24 ECTS per module; bachelor programmes require the completion of 180 ECTS (usually six semesters) and master programmes 120 ECTS (typically four semesters), with each semester comprising a mix of mandatory and elective modules that combine lectures, tutorials and seminars; modules specify clear learning outcomes—for example, a Business‑Administration Management module aims to familiarise students with fundamental incentive and coordination problems in organisations and to develop their ability to identify, analyse and discuss such issues—while assessment is conducted through written exams, seminar papers, presentations or oral examinations, and successful completion of the required credit load and achievement of the stipulated learning outcomes leads to the award of the respective degree.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/student visa: International students admitted to Humboldt University of Berlin must demonstrate sufficient financial resources for their stay, most commonly by opening a blocked account (Sperrkonto) that contains at least €11,904 – the current requirement of €992 per month for a 12‑month period – which is transferred to a regular German bank account after arrival and released in monthly installments; this amount satisfies the visa and residence‑permit proof‑of‑funds criterion listed on the official study‑in‑germany.de portal and reiterated by the DAAD, and while studying students are permitted to work up to 120 full days (or 240 half‑days) per year, typically no more than 20 hours per week during the semester and full‑time during official holidays, without needing a separate work permit. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Germany post‑study work visa (Job‑Seeker Visa, § 20 AufenthG) allows international graduates of a German university such as Humboldt University of Berlin to stay in Germany for up to 18 months to look for a job that matches their qualification; the period begins as soon as you have official proof of graduation (e.g., a degree certificate or confirmation of results) and cannot be extended. Eligibility requires a recognised German university degree (Bachelor, Master or PhD), proof of graduation, a valid health‑insurance policy, a registered German address (Anmeldung), and proof of sufficient financial means—typically a blocked account covering at least €1,091 per month, a monthly income guarantee, or a formal commitment (Verpflichtungserklärung). While searching you may work in any full‑time or part‑time job to support yourself, but the primary purpose must be finding employment related to your field; you must apply for the visa promptly after graduation and, once you secure a qualifying position, you can convert the permit to a work visa or EU Blue Card without leaving Germany.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Humboldt‑Universität zu Berlin draws a large and growing cohort of international students, many of whom complement their studies with internships, co‑op placements and Werk‑student positions that are coordinated through the university’s Career Service and faculty‑specific networks; these practical experiences are often integrated into degree programmes, especially in fields such as engineering, natural sciences, economics, computer science and the humanities, and enable students to build professional contacts in Berlin’s vibrant research institutes, multinational corporations, startups and public‑sector organisations. Recent graduate‑outcome data show that roughly 85 % of Humboldt graduates secure employment or a relevant postgraduate position within six months of completing their studies, with the most common sectors after graduation being research and development, finance and consulting, information technology, education and the public sector, while a significant share of international alumni report that their internships or Werk‑student roles directly led to full‑time offers in these areas. Lương & thị trường: Graduates in Germany typically earn between €45 000 and €55 000 per year in their first full‑time position, with engineers and IT specialists often starting near €55 000, data scientists and AI experts around €52 000, and nurses or medical technicians nearer €48 000; these figures reflect the strong labour‑market demand for STEM and health‑care professions, as well as for renewable‑energy and fintech roles, and the same sectors actively seek international graduates from Humboldt‑Universität zu Berlin, who benefit from Germany’s skilled‑worker shortage but must secure the appropriate post‑study residence permit or EU Blue Card, a process that can take several weeks but is streamlined for graduates in high‑need occupations such as software development, mechanical/electrical engineering, data science, cybersecurity, and specialized nursing, where employers frequently sponsor visas and offer salaries at the upper end of the national graduate range.
