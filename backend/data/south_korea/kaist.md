**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- KAIST requires all international graduate applicants to demonstrate English proficiency with one of the accepted tests taken on or after 17 March 2024; the minimum scores are TOEFL iBT 83 (or 4.5 on the newer scale after 21 January 2026), IELTS Academic 6.5 overall, TOEIC 720 (listening + reading) or TEPS 326 (new TEPS) and the university does not accept Institutional Testing Program scores, PTE Academic or the Duolingo English Test. Applicants may obtain a waiver if they are citizens of native‑English‑speaking countries (United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland) or officially English‑speaking countries (e.g., India, Pakistan, Ethiopia, Kenya), if they have completed a degree in an English‑medium institution in any country, if they are graduating from a regular KAIST degree program in August 2026, or if they meet specific AfCFTA‑member‑state criteria listed by the university. Korean language proficiency (e.g., TOPIK) is not a general admission requirement because English is the official language of instruction, though TOPIK scores may be required for certain Korean‑language scholarships or programs.

**4. Học phí**

Consequently, the total annual cost for a typical international graduate student (excluding scholarships) is approximately 31 million KRW (≈19,118,000 KRW tuition + 100,000 KRW application + 1,380,000 KRW dormitory + 10,700,000 KRW other expenses).

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International undergraduate students at KAIST must complete a curriculum that blends mandatory general courses, major coursework, research activities, and language requirements, accumulating at least 33 credits for a master’s‑level program (or 66 for a doctorate). Mandatory general coursework comprises a 3‑credit Scientific Writing class (CC500) and a 1‑AU Ethics and Safety course (CC020); the Special Lecture on Leadership (CC010) is required but carries no credit and is waived for corporate‑sponsored and foreign students, while writing courses are also exempt for international undergraduates. Students must earn 6 credits of mandatory major courses, at least 15 credits of elective major courses, and a minimum of 9 research credits (including 3 seminar credits), with up to 3 of those research credits obtainable through internships of two months or longer. **2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): The D‑2 student visa for international students admitted to KAIST is obtained by submitting the visa‑application form through the HiKorea portal (hikorea.go.kr) together with a valid passport, a recent passport‑size photo, the KAIST Certificate of Admission and enrollment certificate, and proof of sufficient finances—typically a bank balance of at least USD 10,000 (or the Korean‑won equivalent) for a month, a tuition‑payment receipt, or an official scholarship award letter confirming coverage of tuition and living expenses; once in Korea, students may apply for a time‑limited part‑time work permit after six months of enrollment, allowing up to 20 hours of work per week in approved jobs while continuing their studies. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): The D‑10 Job‑Seeker Visa allows international graduates who have completed a degree at a Korean university such as KAIST to remain in South Korea after their D‑2 student visa expires in order to look for employment; it is normally issued for up to six months and can be extended to a total of one year, with a possible extension to two years for those who have earned a junior‑college‑level qualification or higher in Korea, provided they show sufficient financial resources and a concrete job‑search plan; once a qualified job offer is secured, the holder can change status to an E‑7 work visa, which grants full employment rights for an initial period of up to two years (extendable to three or four years depending on the profession) and requires the employer’s sponsorship and submission of the usual employment‑related documents.
