**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants must submit official transcripts showing a minimum average equivalent to 70 % (roughly a 3.0‑3.5 GPA) in their final secondary qualification such as Australian Year 12, the International Baccalaureate or recognised overseas school‑leaving certificates, along with proof of English proficiency (e.g., IELTS ≥ 6.5 overall with ≥ 6.0 in each component, TOEFL iBT ≥ 79, PTE ≥ 58 or Cambridge CAE), pay a non‑refundable AUD 154 application fee and meet any subject prerequisites required for the chosen program (e.g., mathematics for engineering, biology/chemistry for health sciences); for courses in design, architecture, visual arts, music, theatre and some health‑related fields a portfolio of work and/or an interview is mandatory, while other programmes rely solely on academic results, and graduate coursework applicants must hold a relevant bachelor degree with comparable GPA and satisfy the same English language standards.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- The University of Melbourne requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least an overall IELTS band 6.0 (with no individual band below 5.5 for the standard pathway, or no band below 6.0 for the fast‑track pathway), an overall TOEFL iBT score of 67 (with a minimum Writing score of 14 for the standard pathway or 19 for fast‑track), or a Pearson Test of English Academic score of 47 (with a minimum Writing score of 41 for the standard pathway or 60 for fast‑track); Duolingo English Test results are not accepted. English‑test results must be from an approved test centre in the applicant’s country of citizenship, last country of education, or current residence, and online‑at‑home versions such as TOEFL iBT Home or PTE Academic Online are not accepted; additionally, for visa purposes the Australian government only recognises TOEFL scores taken on or after 4 May 2024. Applicants who have completed a previous tertiary qualification taught entirely in English, or who meet specific exemption criteria outlined by the university, may be granted a waiver of the English‑language requirement.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to the University of Melbourne must meet the same entry prerequisites as domestic applicants, which typically include a recognised secondary qualification such as an Australian Year 12 or International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma with the required minimum scores, plus proof of English proficiency (e.g., IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 or equivalent). Once admitted, undergraduate courses are organised into subjects that each carry 12 credit points (equivalent to roughly 12 hours of contact time per semester) and a full Bachelor’s degree requires the completion of 360 credit points, usually spread over three years of study. The curriculum is delivered in a modular, semester‑based structure where students complete a combination of core (required) subjects and electives that together satisfy the program’s learning outcomes; each subject lists specific learning outcomes that articulate the knowledge, skills and competencies graduates are expected to demonstrate, and these outcomes are assessed through a mix of assignments, examinations and practical projects that contribute to the subject’s credit‑point value.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Subclass 500 student visa requires the applicant to demonstrate genuine access to a minimum of AUD 29,710 to cover 12 months of living costs, course fees and travel for a single student (with additional amounts of AUD 10,394 for a partner, AUD 4,449 per child and AUD 13,502 per dependent child’s school fees if applicable), or alternatively evidence that the applicant’s parents or partner earned at least AUD 87,856 (or AUD 102,500 if family members are included) in the 12 months before application; the visa holder and any family members must maintain continuous Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) from the date of arrival in Australia until departure, with the OSHC start date matching the arrival date rather than the course start date, and the visa grants work rights of up to 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session (with unlimited hours during scheduled school holidays) and allows the holder to commence part‑time work once the course begins; the University of Melbourne is a government‑registered CRICOS institution that enrols international students under these conditions. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the main pathway for University of Melbourne graduates to remain in Australia after completing a degree, allowing them to live, study and work temporarily; the duration of the post‑study work rights depends on the level and type of qualification – a bachelor’s (including honours) normally gives 2 years, a research master’s or a master’s with a research component gives 3 years, a coursework master’s gives 2 years, and a doctoral degree gives 3 years, while Indian nationals receive 2 years for a bachelor’s, 3 years for a bachelor’s with first‑class honours in a STEM field, 3 years for any master’s and 4 years for a PhD, and holders of eligible degrees on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply from 1 July 2023 for an extra two‑year extension (in addition to any regional extension) through ImmiAccount at no charge; applicants must lodge the 485 visa before their student visa expires, and while the 485 application is being processed they are usually on a Bridging A visa that provides full work rights, with the possibility of a Bridging B visa if travel is required.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: International students at the University of Melbourne can fully participate in the university’s extensive work‑integrated learning programme, which includes on‑campus internships through the Students@Work program that matches students with three‑ to nine‑month placements (14‑21 hours per week) and has placed over 750 students since its 2017 launch; the program is open to domestic and international students, has been listed in the Australian Association of Graduate Employers’ Top 20 Intern Programs for three consecutive years and ranked 5th for large programmes in 2025, while industry‑project and placement options require 80‑100 hours of work for undergraduates and up to 200 hours for graduate students; graduates of the university enjoy strong employment outcomes, with the Melbourne Law School reporting a 94 % full‑time employment rate for its 2020 JD cohort and the university’s overall graduate outcomes data indicating that roughly 92 % of graduates are employed or in further study within four months of completing their degree, leading many alumni into legal, business, consulting, media, government and other professional careers. Lương & thị trường: International graduates who obtain an Australian undergraduate qualification achieve employment rates close to 90 percent within four to six months of completing their degree and typically command a median full‑time starting salary of about $71 000, while VET graduates earn around $65 000; the highest‑paid and fastest‑growing occupations for recent graduates are in professional, scientific and technical services—particularly software and applications programmers, engineers and other STEM roles—as well as construction management, which together are projected to add roughly 220 000 new jobs by 2023, and persistent skills shortages are reported across technicians and trades workers, professionals and managers, indicating strong labour‑market demand for graduates in these fields.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**
