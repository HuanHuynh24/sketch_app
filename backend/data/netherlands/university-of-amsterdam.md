### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- The University of Amsterdam admits international students for two main intakes each year – a September start and a February start – and all applicants must first register in the national Dutch portal Studielink (using a DigiD), after which they receive a personal UvAnetID to log into the university’s online application system (SIS); within 14 days of the Studielink registration they must upload their diploma(s), transcripts, English‑language test results (if required) and a CV, pay the non‑refundable €100 application fee, and then wait for the admission decision; for the September intake the general enrolment‑request deadline in Studielink is 1 May for EU/EEA applicants (with the English‑proficiency certificate due by 1 May) and 1 April for non‑EU/EEA applicants (English‑test certificate due by 1 April), while for the February intake the deadline is typically 1 November (EU/EEA) and 1 October (non‑EU/EEA), and the English‑track bachelor programmes also have an early internal deadline of 15 January for the September intake, after which applicants may still switch to the Dutch track whose deadline is 1 May.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

Applicants who are nationals of, or have completed secondary education or at least two years of higher education in, Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore, South Africa, the United Kingdom or the United States are exempt from submitting a test score, as are those who hold a degree that was taught entirely in English – in that case a proof of the language of instruction (transcript note or certified letter) must be provided. **Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students wishing to study at the University of Amsterdam must first satisfy a minimum credit prerequisite: for exchange programmes such as the Politics, Psychology, Law and Economics (PPLE) college, applicants need to have completed at least 30 ECTS (or equivalent) in at least two of the four PPLE disciplines before arriving. The three‑year bachelor’s curriculum is built around a total of 180 ECTS, organised into a core curriculum of 102 ECTS (eight interdisciplinary courses, four integrative seminars and four methodological courses), a specialisation track of 66 ECTS that includes the bachelor’s thesis, and 12 ECTS of elective courses chosen according to personal interests. Most individual courses carry 6 ECTS, resulting in roughly 60 ECTS earned each academic year. The programme is designed to develop interdisciplinary analytical skills, methodological competence, and the ability to conduct independent research, culminating in a thesis that demonstrates mastery of the learning outcomes of critical thinking, problem‑solving, and effective communication within the fields of politics, psychology, law and economics. Additionally, bachelor’s students must acquire at least 50 % of the required ECTS each year to maintain eligibility for a residence permit in the Netherlands.

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: International students from non‑EU countries who have been admitted unconditionally to the University of Amsterdam must obtain both an MVV (long‑stay entry visa) and a VVR (biometric residence permit) – the university’s International Office submits the application to the IND, after which the approved MVV sticker is collected at the Dutch embassy or consulate within three months and the VVR card is issued in the Netherlands; the IND requires proof that the student can support themselves with at least €13 000 per year (about €1 083 per month) for 2026, which can be demonstrated through recent bank statements, a blocked‑account statement, a scholarship award letter or a guarantee from a recognized sponsor, and this amount is updated annually in line with the Dutch social assistance norm as referenced by Nuffic; in addition the student must provide a valid passport, an unconditional admission letter, proof of health insurance, and pay the current €210 fee for the combined MVV/VVR procedure, with processing typically taking 60‑90 days, after which the student may work up to 16 hours per week (or full‑time during June‑August) while studying. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Dutch “zoekjaar” or orientation‑year permit is a 12‑month post‑study work visa that lets non‑EU graduates of a recognised Dutch university or university of applied sciences (e.g. the University of Amsterdam) live and work in the Netherlands without a separate work permit and without needing a job offer before applying; you may be employed, self‑employed or start a business, and you can later switch to a highly‑skilled migrant (kennismigrant) or other residence permit. Eligibility requires a bachelor’s, master’s or PhD degree from an accredited programme, you must not have previously held a zoekjaar (only one is granted per lifetime), you must apply within three years of graduating (the application can be submitted before your student residence expires or up to three years after it ends), you must demonstrate sufficient financial means (roughly €1 200–€1 500 per month), and you must meet any salary thresholds if you later wish to use the 30 % tax ruling (approximately €46 107 gross per year for the standard ruling or €35 048 for employees under 30 with a Dutch master’s). **3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Amsterdam offers a dedicated Student Careers Centre that runs a job board and partners with services such as StudiJob to help all students—including international students—find internships, side‑jobs and graduate positions, with most internships arranged as short‑term work placements that provide modest financial compensation but are not classified as salaries; full‑time graduate‑level traineeships, which do carry a regular salary, are the typical pathway after degree completion, while post‑bachelor or post‑master internships are relatively rare in the Netherlands. International students are encouraged to undertake internships to gain experience in the Dutch labour market, and although Dutch language skills improve job prospects, a sizable international job market exists in Amsterdam where English is often the business language. Graduates, particularly from programmes such as Econometrics and Data Science, enjoy strong employment outcomes, with an estimated 85‑90 % securing a relevant job or further study within six months of graduation, and they are recruited by a wide range of employers including multinational corporations (e.g., ASML, Booking.com, Amazon, IKEA, Heineken), consulting firms (PwC, EY, McKinsey, Deloitte), major banks and financial institutions (ING, Rabobank, ABN AMRO, Goldman Sachs), high‑frequency trading firms (Flow Traders, Optiver), go... Lương & thị trường: International graduates from the University of Amsterdam typically start their careers earning between €36 000 and €47 000 annually, but five years after graduation a substantial share – over half – command salaries above €65 000, reflecting the strong labour‑market demand for roles such as ICT professionals, office‑associate professionals and machine‑ and plant‑operator specialists; roughly 40 % of these graduates remain employed in the Greater Amsterdam region, where the average gross yearly salary is about €56 000, making Amsterdam the highest‑paying Dutch city for university‑educated workers.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Amsterdam provides a comprehensive support network for international students that includes an International Student Housing Office, an Office of International Student Mobility (which also houses the Student Careers Centre), and a range of orientation and career services; the Housing Office assists incoming students in finding furnished accommodation for their first year by coordinating with external providers such as DUWO, De Key and Student Experience and running a randomized lottery for rooms that are typically available for one or two semesters under a one‑year limit, while also offering practical advice and resources for independent housing searches and operating a contact email (student.housing@uva.nl) and phone line (+31 (0)20 525 5372) with consultation hours on Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays 10:00‑11:00 and Tuesdays and Thursdays 16:00‑17:00, and the Office of International Student Mobility, located at Valckenierstraat 59 (and on the Roeterseiland campus at REC‑JK, ground floor), delivers orientation events, Dutch integration programmes, cultural‑exchange ambassador activities, scholarship and loan administration, and through its Student Careers Centre provides career counselling, job‑search support and employer networking for both exchange and regular international students. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Amsterdam offers a lively campus life for international students through a broad network of student associations and clubs—including Sefa, the main study association for Economics & Business, Amsterdam United which promotes diversity and supports first‑generation and migrant‑background students, UNISCA for Model United Nations, AMPA for students with disabilities or chronic illnesses, ASN the Asian Students Network, and a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) that organizes Tet celebrations, Vietnamese‑language meet‑ups, cultural workshops and mentorship programmes to help Vietnamese students integrate socially and academically; beyond these groups, UvA provides sports teams, creative workshops (animation, percussion, filmmaking, creative writing), and plenty of opportunities to explore the city’s museums, theatres and nightlife, while safety is reinforced by a low‑crime environment in Amsterdam, a campus security office and 24/7 safety app, clear guidance on registering with the local municipality, resident‑assistant support in housing, and the national emergency number 112 for any urgent situations.
