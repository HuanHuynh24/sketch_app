## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students applying to Edith Cowan University should first check the specific entry requirements for their chosen course and ensure they have a valid English‑language test result and scanned copies of their qualifications ready to upload. The university operates three main intakes: Semester 1 (February), Semester 2 (July) and a shorter Semester 3 (November) for selected programmes, and each intake has a recommended online application deadline – typically 31 October of the year before Semester 1, 31 March for Semester 2, and 31 July for Semester 3 – although applicants are advised to submit as early as possible because offers are made on a first‑come, first‑served basis. A non‑refundable application fee of AU$150 (or the equivalent in other currencies) is payable when the online application is submitted, after which the university will assess the application, send an offer letter (conditional or unconditional) and, once the offer is accepted, require payment of the first‑semester tuition and Overseas Student Health Cover as the “fees payable now”. After payment, a Confirmation of Enrolment is issued, which can be used to apply for a student visa; the whole process is managed through the ECU online portal and can be supported by authorised ECU agents in the applicant’s country.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to Edith Cowan University must provide certified academic transcripts that show completion of secondary school (for undergraduate entry) or a bachelor’s degree (for postgraduate entry) together with a minimum grade point average of roughly 5.0 out of 7 (or the equivalent of a 2:2 honours classification), and they must meet the university’s English language standards, typically an overall IELTS 6.5 (with ≥6.0 in each component) or TOEFL iBT 79 or a comparable result; for students who have an Australian ATAR, a minimum C grade in ATAR English, English Literature or English ALD is required, while VET applicants with a Certificate IV, Diploma or Advanced Diploma can be considered under the experienced‑based entry scheme provided they achieve at least an A in General English; certain creative and professional programmes (such as design, architecture, visual arts, music, drama or media) also require a portfolio of relevant work and may involve an interview or audition as part of the selection process.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Edith Cowan University requires all international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency, typically an IELTS Academic overall score of 7.0 with at least 7.0 in each of listening, reading, writing and speaking (some undergraduate courses accept 6.5 overall with a minimum 6.0 per component), a TOEFL iBT total of 79 or higher (minimum 13 listening, 18 reading, 21 writing, 20 speaking), a Pearson Test of English Academic score of 58 or above (minimum 50 in each band), or a Duolingo English Test result of 115 or higher; applicants who have completed at least two years of full‑time study in an English‑medium institution, hold a degree taught entirely in English, or meet specific pathway criteria (e.g., certain Australian qualifications) may be granted a waiver of the English‑language test requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at Edith Cowan University pay tuition in Australian dollars that varies by level and discipline, with most undergraduate programmes costing roughly AU$30,000 to AU$33,000 per year (full‑time), while postgraduate taught courses generally range from AU$32,000 to AU$38,000 per year; the university’s online MBA, for example, lists a total fee of about AU$63,000 for the full programme, and the Master of Business Administration Fast‑Track is quoted at AU$47,700. In addition to tuition, students must pay a one‑time application fee of approximately AU$100 and an annual Overseas Student Health Cover premium of about AU$600, plus a mandatory Student Services and Amenities Fee (SSAF) of roughly AU$300 per year and occasional unit‑specific fees such as laboratory or field‑trip charges; therefore, the typical all‑in annual cost for an international undergraduate is close to AU$31,000 – AU$34,000, and for a postgraduate student about AU$33,000 – AU$39,000, all expressed in Australian dollars.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- Edith Cowan University’s typical annual cost for an international student combines tuition of roughly AU$30 000–AU$35 000 (depending on the programme) with living expenses that average about AU$18 000–AU$20 000 per year: shared on‑campus or nearby accommodation costs around AU$200–AU$300 per week (≈AU$10 000–AU$15 600 annually), groceries and meals about AU$80–AU$100 per week (≈AU$4 200–AU$5 200), public‑transport travel roughly AU$100 per month (≈AU$1 200), and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) typically AU$600–AU$700. Adding these components gives a total annual outlay of roughly AU$48 000–AU$55 000 for most international undergraduates at ECU.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Edith Cowan University (ECU) is ranked in the 401‑450 band in the 2024 QS World University Rankings and falls within the 401‑500 band in the 2024 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, while the 2024 THE Young University Rankings places ECU among the world’s top 100 institutions under 50 years old (ranked 84th). In QS subject rankings ECU is positioned in the top 9 Australian universities for sport‑related disciplines and its Nursing programme is also listed among the nation’s highest‑ranked subjects. The university is fully accredited by Australia’s Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA), and its professional programmes hold additional accreditations such as ANMAC for Nursing, Engineers Australia for Engineering, the Legal Profession Admission Board for Law and other relevant bodies for health, education and business courses. International students make up roughly 12‑13 % of ECU’s enrolments, reflecting a sizeable and growing overseas cohort.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student visa (subclass 500) requires international students to demonstrate a minimum of AUD 29,710 in available funds for a single applicant, which must cover the first‑year tuition, living expenses for 12 months, return travel (approximately AUD 2,000‑3,000) and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire stay (about AUD 500 per year for a single student); additional amounts are required for dependants—AUD 10,394 for a partner and AUD 4,449 for each child, plus school fees of around AUD 13,500 per child if they attend school. Proof of financial capacity can be supplied via bank statements, loan documents, scholarship letters or sponsorship declarations. OSHC is mandatory for the whole visa period and must be maintained continuously; the cost varies with coverage type but the single‑student rate is roughly AUD 500 annually. Visa holders may work up to 48 hours per fortnight while their course is in session, with unlimited work rights for masters‑by‑research, doctoral students, during course breaks, and for compulsory work placements listed on CRICOS; they must also meet health, character and enrolment conditions, keep their address updated with the education provider, and can check visa conditions via the Department of Home Affairs VEVO system. All of these requirements are outlined on the official Department of Home Affairs website (immi.homeaffairs.g... Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the official post‑study work visa for international graduates from Australian CRICOS‑registered courses, including those who have completed a degree at Edith Cowan University; it grants unrestricted work rights for you and any eligible family members and allows you to remain in Australia for a period that depends on the level of your qualification – up to two years for a bachelor’s degree (including honours), up to two years for a master’s coursework degree, up to three years for a master’s research degree or a PhD, with an additional one‑ or two‑year regional extension if you studied and lived in a designated regional area, and longer stays (up to five years) for Hong Kong or British National Overseas passport holders.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Edith Cowan University reports that in 2024 78 percent of its graduates were employed full‑time and 89 percent were employed overall (including part‑time or casual work) within four months of graduation, and the university’s whole‑institution Employability Framework embeds work‑integrated learning, internships and co‑op placements across all schools—particularly in Business, Engineering, Health Sciences, Education and the Arts—providing dedicated industry‑linked projects and structured internship programmes for on‑shore international students; these initiatives have been identified as a major factor in the strong graduate outcomes, with many international alumni securing positions in professional services, engineering, health care, education and creative industries either in Australia or in their home countries. Lương & thị trường: In 2024 international graduates who obtain full‑time work in Australia earn a median pre‑tax salary of about $75,000 for undergraduate degrees, $100,000 for postgraduate coursework and $104,400 for postgraduate research, with the highest starting pay and fastest income growth concentrated in mining engineering, medical and health sciences, and general medicine where entry salaries frequently exceed $80,000 and can increase by roughly 35 % within five years; engineering and education also show strong labour‑market demand, with around three‑quarters of engineering graduates moving into professional engineering roles and about 72 % of education graduates becoming teachers, while other fields such as humanities and social sciences tend to yield median salaries nearer $60‑$65 000, and Edith Cowan University’s recent graduate outcomes mirror these national patterns, reporting average starting salaries for its international cohort in the $70‑$85 000 range and highlighting employer demand especially in health, engineering and mining occupations.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Edith Cowan University’s International Office supports overseas students through a comprehensive welcome programme that includes a dedicated orientation week—featuring campus tours, academic introductions, social events and free airport transfer for those staying on its Joondalup, City or Bunbury campuses—while also offering ongoing housing assistance such as on‑campus accommodation options, help finding private rentals and a liaison service for settling into Perth; the university further provides a career service tailored to international students, offering resume workshops, interview preparation, networking events with industry professionals and access to job‑search resources to help graduates transition into the Australian workforce. Đời sống & môi trường: Edith Cowan University provides a lively campus‑life experience for its international cohort, with a wide range of clubs, student societies and events that are promoted through the International Student Events portal where newcomers can meet peers, collect freebies and learn about the many interest groups on campus; among these groups is a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association that regularly organises cultural celebrations, language‑exchange meet‑ups and peer‑support sessions for Vietnamese students, helping them build a community and stay connected to their heritage while studying in Perth, and the university places a strong emphasis on safety, offering 24‑hour security patrols, well‑lit pathways, emergency call points and a campus‑security office, while the city of Perth itself is recognised as one of the world’s safest and most welcoming live‑ability destinations, ensuring that international students can enjoy both academic and social life with peace of mind.
