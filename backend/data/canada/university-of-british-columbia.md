### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students applying to the University of British Columbia must use the online eVision application system, submit all required academic transcripts and supporting documents, and pay a non‑refundable application fee with a Visa or MasterCard; for undergraduate programmes the fee for applicants on a study permit is $173.25 (CDN) and for graduate master’s and doctoral programmes the international fee is $168.25 (CDN) (with a slightly higher $166.50 fee for business‑school master’s programmes), while domestic applicants pay $78.50; the main intake for most programs is the September (Fall) term, with additional spring (May) intakes for selected graduate programmes, and the standard undergraduate deadline is January 15 for the September intake (unless a faculty specifies a later date), so applicants should check the specific programme’s deadline, ensure the fee is paid at the time of submission, upload their documents, and then await admission decisions.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of British Columbia must meet one of the university‑approved English proficiency standards: an IELTS Academic overall band of 6.5 with no component below 6.0 (general IELTS is not accepted); a TOEFL iBT overall score of 90 (minimum 22 in reading and listening and 21 in writing and speaking) or, for tests taken on or after 21 January 2026, an overall score of 4.5 with at least 5 in listening, 4.5 in reading and writing, and 4 in speaking; a Pearson Test of English Academic overall score of 65 with at least 60 in each of reading, listening, writing and speaking; a Duolingo English Test overall score of 125 with no section below 115; or a Canadian Academic English Language (CAEL) overall score of 70 (speaking minimum 60). Applicants who have completed a degree at an institution where English is the language of instruction, or who can demonstrate native‑level English ability, may request a waiver of the English language admission standard by submitting a waiver package (including any supporting evidence such as SAT or other achievement test scores) to the university by the February 15 deadline.

**4. Học phí**

- The University of British Columbia charges international graduate students a total tuition of C$68,072.25 for the 12‑month full‑time program in 2026, payable in three installments, and requires a non‑refundable deposit of C$5,000 to secure admission; the separate application fee for international applicants is C$168.25, while mandatory student fees add roughly C$606.55 per year, an extended health and dental plan of about C$633.20, a universal transit pass (U‑Pass) costing C$562.80 per year, and a one‑time iMed fee of C$237.00, bringing the overall annual cost for an international student to approximately C$69,618 including tuition, fees, and required deposits.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- The typical annual cost for an international student at the University of British Columbia is roughly $20,000 CAD per year and can be broken down as follows: on‑campus university‑administered housing averages about $4,500 CAD per term (≈ $9,000 CAD for two main terms), groceries and food cost about $1,650 CAD per term (≈ $3,300 CAD annually), personal expenses such as clothing, phone, and leisure are about $1,850 CAD per term (≈ $3,700 CAD annually), academic supplies and books run about $800 CAD per term (≈ $1,600 CAD per year), the mandatory iMED health‑insurance fee for a full‑year student is $237 CAD plus a $10.95 CAD billing fee (≈ $248 CAD) and, after the three‑month waiting period, the British Columbia Medical Services Plan costs roughly $75 CAD per month (≈ $675 CAD for the remaining nine months), and local transportation averages around $100 CAD per month (≈ $1,200 CAD per year). Adding these components gives an estimated total annual living cost of about $19,700 CAD for an international student.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students admitted to the University of British Columbia must submit a study‑permit application that includes proof of sufficient funds to cover tuition, living expenses for the student (and any accompanying family members) and transportation to and from Canada, which can be demonstrated with recent bank statements (typically the last four months), a receipt or proof of payment for the first year’s tuition, a Canadian bank account or GIC statement, a loan letter, a bank draft or a letter from a sponsor; in addition, as of 22 January 2024 every study‑permit application requires a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL), which UBC provides to support the application and is also needed when changing programs or applying from within Canada, and the permit must contain a condition allowing employment—once the permit is issued the student may work on‑campus without a separate work permit and off‑campus up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions (full‑time during scheduled breaks) but may not work until the study permit with the work condition is in hand. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of British Columbia are eligible for a Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they completed a program at a designated learning institution that lasted at least eight months, maintained full‑time study status (part‑time is allowed in the final semester), and have a valid study permit during the 180‑day period after receiving their official Letter of Completion. The length of the PGWP depends on the duration of the study program: if the program was between eight months and two years, the work permit is issued for a period equal to the length of the program; if the program was two years or longer, or if it was a master’s degree of at least eight months completed after February 15 2024, the PGWP is issued for three years. Only one PGWP may be granted in a lifetime, and the permit’s validity cannot exceed the expiration date of the holder’s passport. Applications must be submitted online within 180 days of the completion letter, and can be made from inside or outside Canada.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: University of British Columbia’s work‑integrated learning programmes—including mandatory co‑op placements, internships and practicum courses—are open to international students, who are considered full‑time for immigration purposes and must obtain a co‑op work permit to participate; these experiences are designed to give students practical, industry‑relevant skills and have been shown to boost graduate outcomes, with recent UBC data indicating that roughly 93 % of all graduates secure full‑time employment or enrol in further study within six months of graduation and that the subset who complete a co‑op or internship enjoys an even higher placement rate of about 96 % and typically finds a job more quickly; the most common career sectors for these graduates include engineering, computer science and information technology, business and finance, health sciences, and natural resources, while many international graduates also leverage their Canadian work experience to obtain post‑graduation work permits and either remain in Canada or return to their home countries with enhanced employment prospects. Lương & thị trường: In Canada, recent graduates see median earnings that rise sharply with level of education – bachelor’s‑level alumni earn about $41,000 a year on average in British Columbia, while those with master’s or doctoral degrees earn roughly $78,000, and college‑diploma holders about $35,000 – and the occupations with the strongest demand and highest wages for university‑educated graduates are in the professional, business and technology sectors, with the Job Bank and provincial labour reports listing software developers and programmers (≈$52 hourly), information‑systems specialists (≈$46 hourly), accountants and financial auditors (≈$40 hourly), and managers in computing, construction and finance (≈$51‑$63 hourly); however, international student graduates are disproportionately employed in sales and service jobs and are less represented in these high‑pay, high‑demand fields, with only about 8 % working in education, law, social or government services compared with 38 % of Canadian‑educated master’s graduates, so while the overall graduate labour market in Canada is strong, international graduates seeking the higher‑earning, high‑opportunity occupations should target fields such as software development, information systems, accounting and managerial roles where demand and wages are highest.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of British Columbia’s International Student Office provides comprehensive support for international students, beginning with a suite of orientation programs such as New‑to‑UBC sessions, Jump Start, and the UBC 101 Welcome events that introduce campus life, immigration documentation, academic resources and community services; the office also offers dedicated International Student Advising staffed by Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants and Regulated International Student Immigration Advisors who assist with study permits, work eligibility and health‑insurance questions, while a separate Off‑Campus Housing Support team supplies an off‑campus housing guide, listings through Places4Students and personalized advice for finding safe, affordable accommodation; in addition, the Global Pathways International Student Career Advising service delivers career‑focused assistance including résumé and cover‑letter reviews, interview preparation, job‑search strategies, information on work‑integrated learning and post‑graduation work permits, as well as connections to the campus job board and employer networking events, ensuring that international students receive coordinated help across orientation, housing and career development throughout their UBC experience. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of British Columbia provides a vibrant campus‑life experience for international students that includes dozens of student clubs and cultural associations—among them the Vietnamese Student Association, which regularly hosts social gatherings, cultural festivals, language‑exchange sessions and peer‑support events to help Vietnamese students build friendships and navigate life in Canada; all students can also join a wide range of other clubs through UBC Collegia, peer‑programs, orientation leadership roles and recreational groups, while the university’s Campus Safety office offers 24‑hour security patrols, emergency call stations, a Safe Walk program, and readily accessible health‑and‑wellness resources such as the Student Health Service, counselling and crisis support to ensure a safe and supportive environment for everyone on campus.
