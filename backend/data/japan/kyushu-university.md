**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Kyushu University admits international students in two yearly intakes — a spring intake that begins in April and a fall intake that begins in October — and the application procedure is essentially the same for both. For the October (fall) intake, the online application opens on 5 December and closes on 12 December 2025, the application fee is 10,000 JPY for applicants with only foreign nationality (17,000 JPY for those who also hold Japanese nationality) and must be paid by credit‑card or at a Japanese convenience store during the same period; after registration you download the application form, prepare the required documents (transcript, degree certificate, proof of English proficiency, recommendation letters, etc.) and send them by registered airmail (DHL, FedEx, EMS, etc.) so that they reach the Admission Division by 5 p.m. on 19 December 2025 (the document deadline). For the April (spring) intake, the online application period runs from early December to mid‑January, the fee and payment window are the same (10,000 JPY/17,000 JPY, payable 5 December – 12 December 2025 for the 2026 spring intake), and the completed documents must arrive by 5 p.m. on 17 January 2025. **2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Kyushu University admits international under‑graduates who have completed 16 years of formal education (high‑school graduation) outside Japan and can submit an original transcript and diploma (or IB, Abitur, French/Baccalaureate, GCE A‑Level, etc.) together with a description of the school’s grading system; a cumulative GPA of at least 3.0 on a 4.0 scale (or 2.3 on a 3.0 scale) is required, and the university also accepts EJU scores (mandatory for most programs) with subjects such as Mathematics Course 1 or 2 and “Japan and the World” (or “Japanese Society”) plus optional subjects like physics, economics or AP exams; English proficiency must be demonstrated by TOEFL iBT ≥ 80, IELTS ≥ 6.5, or proof of at least four years of secondary‑school instruction in English, and additional standardized tests (SAT, AP, etc.) may be submitted if specified by the faculty; most programmes do not require an interview or portfolio, though certain arts, design or specialized graduate schools (e.g., the Leading Graduate Schools) may request a portfolio or interview and additionally require a bachelor’s degree with a 3.0 GPA, be under 30 years of age, and meet program‑specific subject prerequisites.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Kyushu University requires proof of English proficiency for most international graduate applicants and accepts official scores from TOEFL iBT (single test‑date scores only, not MyBest), IELTS Academic (the test‑report‑form number can be entered online to waive the paper copy), and TOEIC (listening‑and‑reading component only). Applicants whose most recent degree was taught entirely in English, or who have otherwise demonstrated sufficient English ability (e.g., by completing an English‑medium program), may be exempt from submitting a test score, as English‑proficiency submission is optional for those cases. The university does not list PTE Academic or Duolingo English Test as accepted exams, so candidates should use one of the three accepted tests unless a specific program states otherwise. Japanese language ability is not a formal admission requirement, but many scholarships and some programs expect at least JLPT N2 (or an equivalent certification) to ensure candidates can read, write, and communicate in Japanese.

**4. Học phí**

- Kyushu University charges international students an annual tuition of 535,800 JPY for standard school and graduate programmes (with professional graduate programmes costing 804,000 JPY per year); the mandatory enrollment (admission) fee is 282,000 JPY for all school, graduate and professional graduate students (28,200 JPY for non‑degree/auditing students and 84,600 JPY for vocational, research or Japanese‑language students); the application fee is 17,000 JPY for undergraduate school applicants, 30,000 JPY for graduate and professional graduate applicants (9,800 JPY for non‑degree/auditing and vocational applicants); therefore, the typical total cost for an international student entering a regular programme is roughly 835,000 JPY per year (535,800 JPY tuition + 282,000 JPY enrollment + 17,000 JPY application).

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: International students admitted to Kyushu University must first obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) – the university acts as the proxy and files the application with the regional immigration bureau on the student’s behalf when a “Student” status of residence is required; the COE is issued before any visa can be applied for and serves as proof that the applicant meets the conditions for landing in Japan, including a valid reason for residence other than a temporary‑visitor status. Once the COE is issued, the student (or a designated family member) must submit the original COE together with a passport, visa application form, photos and other required documents to a Japanese embassy or consulate in their home country, because visas cannot be obtained after arrival in Japan and there is no proxy system for visa issuance within Japan. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) requires evidence that the applicant can cover all living expenses for the duration of stay, such as bank statements, sponsor’s income certificates or scholarship award letters, and the government expects the student to be financially self‑sufficient without relying solely on part‑time work; nevertheless, a student may engage in part‑time employment up to 28 hours per week (or up to 40 hours during designated vacation periods) provided a “Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted Under th... Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates (including those completing a degree or PhD) of Kyushu University who have not secured employment by the time they graduate may change their residence status to “Designated Activities (for continuous job hunting)”; this status permits a stay of six months after graduation, and a single renewal is possible, allowing a maximum total period of up to one year for job‑hunting activities, provided the applicant submits a university‑issued letter of recommendation, evidence of active job‑search efforts, and proof of sufficient living expenses; non‑degree students such as research students are ineligible for this category.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Kyushu University’s International Student Career Support Team provides dedicated Shukatsu services—including internship and co‑op placement assistance, industry‑university networking events, and one‑on‑one counseling—to help foreign students transition into the Japanese workforce, and the most recent graduate outcome data show that among the 2021 cohort of international graduates, roughly 20 % of undergraduate and master’s program alumni secured employment in Japan (13 of 83 undergraduates and 90 of 457 master’s graduates), while the doctoral segment achieved a higher placement rate of about one‑third (69 of 207). Employers that regularly recruit these students span a wide range of sectors such as automotive (Nissan Motor), electronics and semiconductors (Sony Semiconductor Solutions, Fujitsu, Micron), information technology and consulting (SoftBank Group, Rakuten, ABeam Consulting, Infosys), as well as logistics, manufacturing, and emerging tech firms (DJI Japan, Stanley Electric). The university’s career office maintains an active internship pipeline with partner companies, enabling students to gain practical experience before graduation, and the overall trend indicates that international graduates who complete internships or co‑op placements are significantly more likely to secure full‑time positions in Japan after completing their studies. Lương & thị trường: Kyushu University reports that in the 2024‑2025 academic year roughly 40 % of its international graduates secured employment in Japan, with the majority entering the private sector—particularly firms such as Toyota, Deloitte Tohmatsu Consulting, Honda Motor, and GMO Payment Gateway—and a notable share taking research or graduate‑school positions; the most common occupation categories for these graduates are IT and ICT roles, finance, and graduate‑school or clinical‑residency positions, reflecting Japan’s strong labour‑market demand for technical, analytical, and healthcare expertise, and the average starting salary for international hires in these fields ranges from about ¥3.5 million annually for entry‑level ICT and engineering jobs to ¥5 million for finance and specialized technical positions, while graduates who pursue further study account for roughly 55 % of the cohort and the remaining 5 % are either still job‑searching or in other unspecified activities.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: At Kyushu University the International Student Center (ISC) functions as the primary office for inbound international students, providing a mandatory orientation at the start of each semester that covers campus facilities, registration, health services, cultural adjustment and English‑language guidance, as well as a dedicated housing support service that assists students in securing on‑campus dormitory rooms or off‑campus apartments, advises on lease contracts and helps with applications for university‑run international residences; for career development the ISC works together with the university’s Career Center to run job‑search workshops, resume‑writing seminars, employer‑matching events and one‑on‑one counseling plus internship placement assistance specifically for international students, and it also offers academic tutoring through a tutoring desk where senior students and staff provide English‑language help, study‑skill coaching and subject‑specific support, reachable by email (intlrtan@jimu.kyushu-u.ac.jp) or phone (092‑802‑2228) during business hours. Đời sống & môi trường: Kyushu University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with dozens of student‑run circles (clubs) ranging from cultural and language exchange groups, sports teams, music and art ensembles, to academic and volunteer societies, and the International Student Center actively promotes participation by providing information sessions and a “International Student Circle” that helps newcomers connect; among the many national‑based groups, the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) regularly meets to share news, organize cultural festivals such as Tết celebrations, offer peer‑to‑peer tutoring and visa or housing advice, and serves as a friendly network for Vietnamese scholars studying in Fukuoka. Safety and well‑being are supported by the university’s Center for Health Sciences and Counseling, which operates free infirmary and first‑aid services on all campuses, offers cross‑cultural counseling, and ensures that every student is covered by the mandatory Personal Accident Insurance and Liability Insurance for overseas or outdoor activities; detailed safety guidelines are provided for field trips, and emergency contact procedures—including liaison with students’ legal guardians—are in place to protect all international students during campus events and excursions.
