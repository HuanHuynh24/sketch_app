**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to UNSW Sydney must satisfy both academic and English‑language criteria that vary by programme but follow a common framework: the university recognises the applicant’s secondary (high‑school) qualification – including the transcript (học bạ) and final exam scores (điểm thi) – or any prior tertiary study, and then applies a minimum academic entry score that is typically expressed as a Weighted Average Mark (WAM) of about 70 % (equivalent to a GPA of 5.0 on UNSW’s 7‑point scale) with a buffer of 2 points above the advertised requirement for a full offer; for postgraduate courses the applicant must have completed an undergraduate degree and usually need a WAM 5 points above the programme’s threshold. Predicted grades can be used if they meet the buffer, and standardised test results such as SAT, ACT, AP or IB are also accepted for direct entry. English proficiency must be demonstrated with recent results from an approved test (e.g., IELTS ≥ 6.5 overall with ≥6.0 in each component, TOEFL iBT ≥ 79, or equivalent). Some creative or professional programmes (e.g., Architecture, Design, Fine Arts, Music, and certain Health Sciences) additionally require a portfolio and/or an interview as part of the assessment. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- UNSW Sydney requires international applicants whose first language is not English to provide evidence of English proficiency, usually by submitting scores from an approved test taken within the two years before enrolment; the minimum scores are IELTS Academic 6.0 overall (with at least 5.5 in each component), TOEFL iBT 79 overall (minimum 21 writing, 12 reading, 11 listening and 17 speaking), TOEFL PBT 500 overall (minimum 4.5 in the TWE), or PTE Academic 50 overall (minimum 42 in each sub‑test). Duolingo English Test scores are not listed as an accepted option. Applicants may be exempt from these test requirements (an English waiver) if they have completed at least one year of full‑time study at a recognised university or other post‑secondary institution where English was the sole language of instruction, either in an English‑speaking country or at an institution listed in the World Higher Education Database, and the study was completed within two years of their intended start date (or meet other prior‑study conditions such as completing an AQF Level 5/6 qualification taught entirely in English).

**4. Học phí**

- For international students at UNSW Sydney, tuition is charged per unit of credit with most science‑, engineering‑ and health‑related disciplines costing AUD 1,245 per unit, arts‑, humanities‑ and social‑science subjects costing AUD 975 per unit, and aviation courses costing AUD 990 per unit; a typical full‑time load of 24 units therefore amounts to roughly AUD 29,880–30,000 for science programmes, AUD 23,400 for arts/humanities and AUD 23,760 for aviation, and all international students must also pay the annual Student Services & Amenities Fee (up to AUD 365) plus a non‑refundable application processing fee of AUD 150 per online application, giving a total annual cost that generally falls between AUD 24,000 and AUD 31,000 depending on the chosen discipline, with the currency being Australian dollars.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student Visa (subclass 500) required for international students at UNSW Sydney obliges applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity, typically a minimum of AUD 29,710 for a single student to cover 12 months of living expenses, tuition and travel, with higher amounts required if a partner (AUD 10,394) or children (AUD 4,449 each plus schooling costs of AUD 13,502 per child) accompany them, and evidence must show at least three months of account history and recent transactions; applicants must also obtain and maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) from the day they arrive in Australia until they depart, as entry can be refused without proof of health insurance, and while on the subclass 500 visa they are permitted to work up to 40 hours per fortnight during scheduled course periods and unlimited hours during official course breaks, with UNSW advising prospective students to apply online via the Department of Home Affairs portal and consult the official Study in Australia website for detailed eligibility, documentation and genuine student requirements. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) provides post‑study work rights for international students who have completed a qualification at an Australian university such as UNSW Sydney, and it is administered by the Department of Home Affairs; graduates who have earned a bachelor’s degree are generally granted up to two years of stay, those with a coursework master’s also receive up to two years, research master’s graduates receive up to three years, and PhD holders are entitled to up to four years, while the Graduate Work stream (for associate, diploma or trade qualifications linked to an occupation on the MLTSSL) allows up to 18 months; from 1 July 2023, graduates of eligible degrees on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply for an additional two‑year extension on the Post‑Study Work stream at no cost, and further extensions of one to two years are available to those who have studied, lived and worked in regional Australia, with the visa also permitting unrestricted work for the holder and eligible family members during the granted period.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

Lương & thị trường: Australian graduate salaries are among the highest in the region, with UNSW reporting an undergraduate median graduate salary of AU$67,500 and a postgraduate median of AU$110,000, while broader UNSW data show an average annual graduate earnings figure of around AU$120,000 and a national graduate‑program average of AU$74,944 in 2024.
