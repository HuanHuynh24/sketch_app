### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Auckland University of Technology (AUT) admits international students each year in two main intakes – the February (Semester 1) intake and the July (Semester 2) intake – and applicants should aim to submit their online application through AUT’s Arion system at least three months before the chosen start date (typically by late November for February and by late April for July); after creating an account, uploading required documents (e.g., academic transcripts, English‑language proof, passport details) and paying the non‑refundable NZ $150 enrolment fee for the first year, students receive an Offer of Place, must accept the offer and pay all tuition, learner‑services levy and any insurance fees by the payment deadline stated in the offer (otherwise the place is withdrawn), then use the payment acknowledgement to apply for a student visa, arrange travel and accommodation, and attend the university’s orientation before commencing studies.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Auckland University of Technology requires all international applicants to meet New Zealand University Entrance (UE) or an equivalent qualification, which for NCEA‑based students means completion of Year 12 plus at least 48 Level 2 credits (including a minimum of eight Level 1 credits in one approved subject such as Art, English, History, Business, etc.) and the standard UE literacy and numeracy thresholds (a D grade in mathematics and at least an E in English). Equivalent pathways include a minimum of 24 points in the International Baccalaureate, or 120 UCAS‑tariff points from Cambridge International A/AS levels with at least three D grades (including a D in math and an E in English). English language proficiency must be demonstrated with IELTS Academic 5.5 overall (no band below 5.0) – many programmes raise this to 6.0 overall (no band below 5.5). Applicants must submit official transcripts and proof of qualification; specific programmes may also require additional subject prerequisites, a portfolio (e.g., for design, visual arts, architecture) or an interview, and some professional courses require a driver’s licence. For postgraduate study the entry requirement is a completed bachelor’s degree in a relevant field plus the same English language standard.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Auckland University of Technology requires international students to demonstrate English proficiency through an approved test: for most bachelor‑level programmes the minimum is IELTS Academic 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5, TOEFL iBT 80 total (minimum 15 reading, 15 listening, 18 speaking, 20 writing) or Pearson Test of English (PTE) overall 50 with each component at least 42; for master’s programmes the threshold rises to IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, TOEFL iBT 90 total (24 writing, 20 reading, 20 listening, 20 speaking) or PTE 58 overall with each component at least 50. AUT also accepts the Duolingo English Test, typically requiring a minimum score of 115. English waivers are granted when applicants have completed a prior tertiary qualification taught wholly in English, hold an NZCEL C1 Academic Endorsement or higher, have achieved the required level on the New Zealand Accredited Pathway Assessment (Level 2 or 3 depending on study level), or possess a Trinity ISE qualification at the appropriate level with at least a Distinction.

**4. Học phí**

- Auckland University of Technology charges international students tuition in New Zealand dollars (NZ $) plus a compulsory Student Services Levy; for a full‑time year (120 points) the typical tuition is NZ $35,300 + NZ $1,221.60 levy = NZ $36,521.60, while a half‑year (60 points) programme costs about NZ $17,800 + NZ $610.80 levy = NZ $18,410.80; diploma‑level (120 points) fees are around NZ $35,400 + NZ $1,221.60 = NZ $36,621.60. The one‑time international application fee is NZ $130, and other recurring charges include the Student Services Levy (as shown), health‑insurance (≈NZ $300–$400 per year), and a student‑association fee (≈NZ $50 per year).

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: A fee‑paying student visa for an international student at Auckland University of Technology requires proof that the tuition fee for the first course or first year (whichever is shorter) has been paid or is guaranteed, which can be shown with a receipt, a letter from the university confirming payment, a scholarship award letter or an accepted loan document, and the applicant must also provide evidence of sufficient living funds – typically bank statements or a sponsor’s Financial Undertaking for a Student (INZ 1014) showing at least NZ 15,000 per year (or the amount specified by Immigration New Zealand) plus enough to cover outward travel; the visa allows the student to work up to 20 hours per week during scheduled study periods and full‑time during official holiday periods, provided the work does not interfere with the course of study. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from Auckland University of Technology (AUT) can apply to Immigration New Zealand for a Post‑Study Work Visa once they have completed a qualification that meets the official criteria: the programme must have been studied full‑time for at least 30 weeks in New Zealand (including study breaks and exam periods but excluding holiday breaks) and the qualification must be on the INZ “Qualifications Eligible for a Post‑Study Work Visa” list. If the qualification is at NZQF level 7 or above (bachelor’s degree, master’s, doctorate or higher) the visa can be granted for up to three years of open work rights; if it is at level 4‑6 (certificates or diplomas) the visa may be granted for up to one year provided the qualification appears on the eligible list and the work is related to the field of study. Applicants must submit the visa application within three months of their student visa expiring, meet health and character requirements, and can work in almost any occupation during the visa period. From late 2026 a new Short‑Term Graduate Work Visa will also be available, offering up to six months of open work rights to graduates with NZQF level 5‑7 qualifications, and eligibility for the Post‑Study Work Visa will be extended to include Graduate Diplomas at level 7 when the graduate also holds a bachelor’s degree.
