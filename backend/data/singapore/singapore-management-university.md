### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Singapore Management University (SMU) admits international undergraduate students for two intakes each year – the main August intake and a smaller January intake – and for the August semester applicants must submit their online application by mid‑March (the 2026 deadline was 19 March) with the required English‑language and academic documents, while the January intake deadline falls in mid‑November. The non‑refundable application fee is SGD 30 for most programmes (higher for certain specialised courses) and is paid through the SMU Admissions portal after the applicant creates an account, completes the personal and academic sections, uploads certified transcripts, English‑proficiency scores (IELTS/TOEFL) and any required standardized‑test results, and then confirms payment; shortlisted candidates are invited to an interview or written test and, upon successful completion, receive an offer letter with further enrolment instructions.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to Singapore Management University must have completed at least 12 years of formal education and hold a recognized national or international qualification such as Singapore‑Cambridge GCE A‑Levels, an International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma, a US High School Diploma, Australian Year 12, or any equivalent qualification listed by SMU; for A‑Level applicants a “good” result is defined as passes in at least three H2 content‑based subjects, one H1 content‑based subject, plus Project Work and either General Paper or Knowledge & Inquiry, while IB candidates need a “good” diploma (generally a score of 30 points or higher) and must submit their full transcript; all applicants are required to provide proof of English proficiency through IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, C1 Advanced, or, where applicable, SAT/ACT scores, and a minimum academic standing comparable to a GPA of about 3.0 on a 4.0 scale is expected; most undergraduate programmes do not require a portfolio or interview, though certain specialised courses (e.g., design‑related or arts programmes) may request a portfolio or conduct an interview at the discretion of the department.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Singapore Management University requires international applicants to demonstrate proficiency in English, and accepts IELTS (including IELTS Online) with a minimum overall band score of 6.5 (no individual band below 6.0), TOEFL iBT (including the Home Edition) with a minimum total score of 79, PTE Academic with a minimum score of 58, and the Duolingo English Test with a minimum score of 115; applicants who have completed at least two years of post‑secondary study or hold a diploma/degree from an institution where the medium of instruction is English, or who have attended a recognized English‑medium secondary school, may be granted a waiver from submitting these test results.

**4. Học phí**

- For Singapore Management University, an international student who receives the Singapore Government Tuition Grant (TG) pays a subsidised tuition of S$12,550 per year for most undergraduate programmes and S$13,825 for the Law degree; an annual miscellaneous fee of S$290 is added, giving a total payable amount of S$12,840 per year for non‑law programmes ( S$14,115 for Law). The fees are quoted in Singapore dollars (SGD). A one‑time application fee of roughly S$30 is also required, and the only other regular charge listed is the annual fee of S$290.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT'S PASS, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Singapore Management University (SMU) is a globally recognised institution that consistently features in the major university rankings: in the 2026 QS World University Rankings by Subject it placed 39th worldwide for Business & Management Studies, 50th for Accounting & Finance, 56th for Law, and entered the global top 60 for those subjects while its overall Social Sciences & Management ranking rose to 86th; in the QS Global Master’s Rankings 2025 SMU’s MBA was 41st worldwide and 5th in Asia, its Master of Science in Wealth Management ranked among the world’s best, and its Master of IT in Business was 24th globally and 2nd in Asia; in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings SMU is positioned in the 601‑800 band (2023/24), reflecting strong performance in teaching, research and international outlook; the university is accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB) for its School of Business and Accountancy, holds ACCA and CPA‑Australia programme recognitions, and its law programmes are approved by the Singapore Ministry of Law, ensuring that degrees meet rigorous international standards; SMU also attracts a sizable international student community, with roughly one‑fifth of its enrolment comprising students from abroad, supported by dedicated scholarships and an English‑medium, project‑based learning environment.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students admitted to Singapore Management University must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) through ICA’s SOLAR system and provide proof of sufficient financial support, typically showing that they have at least SGD 15,000–20,000 in a personal or sponsor’s bank account to cover tuition and living expenses for the first year; the exact amount may be adjusted by ICA but must demonstrate the ability to sustain oneself without recourse to public funds. While the STP is valid, SMU students are permitted to work up to 16 hours per week during term time and full‑time during school holidays, provided the employment is part‑time and does not interfere with their studies, and they must obtain a written letter of consent from the university and a valid work permit (such as a Part‑Time Student Work Pass) if the employer requires it; any work beyond the 16‑hour limit or after graduation requires conversion to an appropriate employment pass (e.g., EP, S Pass) and the STP will be cancelled within 30 days of the final examination or official graduation date, after which graduates must either leave Singapore, apply for a Long‑Term Visit Pass, or secure a work pass to remain. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Singapore Management University may apply to the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority for a one‑year, non‑renewable Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) to remain in Singapore while they look for employment; the application must be submitted before the student pass is cancelled and requires a graduation letter, the student‑pass cancellation notice, proof of sufficient financial support and a passport bio‑data page, the pass is valid for up to 12 months, does not confer an automatic right to work (any job requires the pass holder to first secure a work pass such as an Employment Pass or S Pass), and the LTVP must be converted to a valid work pass before the holder can commence employment.
