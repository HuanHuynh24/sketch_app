### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Université Paris Cité admits international students in two main intakes – a September (first‑semester) intake and a January (second‑semester) intake; for exchange students the online application and required documents must be submitted by 15 May for the September start and by 30 October for the January start, after the home‑university nominee has officially sent a nomination, while independent (non‑exchange) applicants apply through the Campus France “Études en France” portal (or directly on the university site for certain countries) with typical deadlines of mid‑March to 31 May for September entry and early October for January entry, and must pay an application fee of roughly €90 when submitting their online file; the application process consists of choosing a programme and checking language requirements, creating a Campus France (or university) account, uploading academic transcripts, language certificates, passport copy and any motivation letter, paying the fee, and then receiving a preliminary admission letter (DAP) or acceptance email followed by enrollment instructions, with exchange students additionally providing a signed Learning Agreement and, if desired, a residence request before receiving the official acceptance letter and welcome kit.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students wishing to enroll at Université Paris Cité must hold a recognised bachelor’s degree (or equivalent) with at least 180 ECTS credits in the relevant discipline (e.g., economics, English‑American studies, law, etc.) and provide official transcripts and a diploma supplement; while a specific GPA is not formally listed, candidates are expected to have a strong academic record (“good level”) and must submit proof of English proficiency—minimum scores are TOEFL iBT 92 (or 237 computer‑based, 580 paper‑based), IELTS 6.5 for most programmes, and higher thresholds (IELTS 7, TOEFL 98, Cambridge CAE/ECPE pass, PTE ≥ 76) for specialised master’s such as the Joint Master in English and American Studies; French B2 is required for programmes taught in French and Italian B2 for the joint degree with the University of Turin, where an application and interview are conducted after the second year to select candidates for the mobility stage; no portfolio is generally required, except where specific creative or professional programmes may request one, and apart from the Turin mobility interview, admissions are based on academic credentials and language test results together with a motivation letter.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to programmes at Université Paris Cité must demonstrate a high level of English, typically by submitting a recognised test score: IELTS ≥ 7.0 overall, TOEFL iBT ≥ 98, or Pearson PTE Academic ≥ 76 points; Duolingo English Test is not listed among the accepted exams. However, the English‑proficiency requirement can be waived for candidates whose most recent degree was taught entirely in English or who are admitted to a joint or specialised master’s programme that is delivered in English, in which case the university accepts a statement from the home institution confirming English instruction instead of a formal test certificate.

**4. Học phí**

- For the 2024‑2025 academic year international (non‑EU, non‑exchange) students at Université Paris Cité pay the same fees as French and European students: a Bachelor’s degree costs 175 €, a Master’s 250 €, and a PhD 391 €, all in euros, and each programme adds the mandatory Student and Campus Life Contribution (CVEC) of 103 €, resulting in total annual charges of 278 € for a Bachelor, 353 € for a Master and 494 € for a PhD; there is no separate application fee, registration is completed online and the CVEC must be paid before enrolment, and fees may be adjusted by roughly 2 % each year, with possible extra costs for specialised programmes such‑as Erasmus Mundus.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students admitted to Université Paris Cité must hold a recognised bachelor’s degree (typically 180 ECTS) or an equivalent qualification, demonstrate proficiency in English (minimum TOEFL 237‑CBT, 580‑paper, or 92‑iBT; IELTS 6.5; or comparable Cambridge/CEFR B2 scores), and submit a clear motivation statement; for the EPOG+ master’s and other joint programmes they must also complete at least 30 ECTS at a partner university and defend a thesis graded by supervisors from two different institutions, with the possibility of a double‑degree if one partner is Université Paris Cité. The university follows the European LMD structure: a three‑year Licence (L1‑L3) comprising three semesters per year, each semester worth up to 30 ECTS (total 180 ECTS for the licence); a two‑year Master (M1‑M2) of 60 ECTS per semester (120 ECTS total, 300 ECTS cumulative with the licence), and a Doctorate requiring 480 ECTS over eight years. Courses are organised by semester, and students must earn at least 30 ECTS each semester to validate it; credits are transferable across EU institutions and are recorded on a transcript that shows both grades and ECTS values. ## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Université Paris Cité is positioned in the 151‑200 band in both the 2024 QS World University Rankings and the 2024 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings; in QS subject rankings the university reaches the top‑50 globally for Life Sciences & Medicine, Chemistry, Computer Science & Information Systems, and Social Sciences & Management, while THE subject tables place it within the top‑150 for Clinical, Pre‑clinical & Health, Engineering & Technology, and Social Sciences, reflecting strong multidisciplinary performance; the institution is fully accredited by the French Ministry of Higher Education and Research, its engineering programmes carry CTI accreditation, its medical curricula are recognised by the French National Agency for the Accreditation of Medical Education, and its business schools hold EQUIS and AACSB accreditations, ensuring that degrees are internationally recognised and that a sizable international student community can study in English‑taught master’s programmes under EU‑standard quality assurance.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To obtain a long‑stay student visa (VLS‑TS) for studying at Université Paris Cité you must submit a complete France‑Visas application that includes a Campus France confirmation email, proof of accommodation for at least the first three months (or a reservation for a student residence), and evidence that you possess sufficient financial resources of at least €615 per month after deducting tuition and travel costs for the whole period of study; this can be shown by the three most recent official bank statements, a scholarship or guarantor letter, or a blocked account covering the required amount, and you must also provide mandatory health‑insurance documentation covering third‑person liability, repatriation and a complementary mutuelle (or a French social‑security registration) as required for all international students, after which the visa must be validated on arrival via the ANEF portal to obtain the residence permit. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed a master‑level (or higher) programme at a French public university such as Université Paris Cité may, after obtaining their diploma, request an Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour (APS) – the “post‑study work visa” – within four months of graduation; the APS is issued for up to 12 months, is not renewable and allows the holder to work up to 20 hours a week (or 964 € gross per month) while looking for a longer‑term job or preparing to change status. Within the validity period of the APS, or directly after the student residence permit, the graduate can also apply to the prefecture for the “job seeker / new business creator” residence permit (Carte de séjour temporaire « recherche d’emploi / création d’entreprise »), which is granted for a single 12‑month term (non‑renewable) to foreign students or researchers who hold a degree equivalent to a master’s and who wish either to seek employment in a field related to their studies or to create a company linked to their training; the permit authorises full‑time work and the establishment of a business, requires proof of the diploma, proof of previous student or researcher status, and must be filed within four years of the diploma award, after which the holder must either obtain a different long‑term residence permit, a work contract meeting the 150 % SMIC threshold, or leave French territory....

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Université Paris Cité requires all bachelor, master, engineering and DUT students – including international students – to complete at least one internship of up to six months, with many programmes embedding a mandatory 4‑8‑week placement in a company or research lab and offering the option to undertake the experience abroad; the SMARTS‑UP Graduate Schools programme further funds master‑level internships overseas (1‑6 months) with monthly stipends and travel allowances, while the university’s dedicated career service supports international students in finding co‑ops and apprenticeships, preparing CVs and interview skills, and leveraging its extensive network of European companies and research organisations, resulting in a high proportion of graduates quickly entering professional fields such as biotechnology, aeronautics, civil engineering, education, cultural and diplomatic services, publishing, tourism, public‑relations and related sectors, or continuing into PhD programmes. Lương & thị trường: In France, six months after graduation the average gross salary is about €39,700 for graduates working domestically and €42,400 for those employed abroad, rising to roughly €44,400 and €58,400 respectively after 30 months; overall graduate earnings average €39,010 with engineers earning around €55,000, IT and software specialists about €42,800, managers near €65,000, and nurses up to €71,000, reflecting strong labour‑market demand for technical, digital and healthcare occupations that are projected to remain the most sought‑after over the next decade, while international graduates are especially mobile—about 27 % take jobs outside France (11.9 % in the EU and 15.3 % elsewhere) and many later return to their home country—supported by a post‑study residence permit and the national job platform France Travail, with APEC providing executive‑level recruitment services that connect graduates to these high‑growth sectors.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**
