### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Nagoya University’s Global 30 (G30) programs admit international students for two main intakes each year – an autumn intake that begins in October and a spring intake that begins in April (the spring intake is primarily for graduate programmes, while undergraduate programmes are offered only for the October start). For the upcoming 2026‑27 academic year, undergraduate applicants must submit their first‑round application by 3 December 2025 16:00 JST (the second round opens 12 January 2026 and closes 1 February 2026), and the application fee is ¥10,000 paid online when the electronic form is submitted. Graduate applicants can apply in two rounds: the first round runs 5 – 16 January 2026 with a fee of ¥15,000, and the second round runs 7 – 24 May 2026 (same fee). The application process is completely online through the university’s International Admissions portal: create an account, fill out the personal and academic information, upload required documents (passport copy, academic transcripts, proof of English proficiency such as TOEFL/IELTS, recommendation letters, research/statement of purpose, and for some programmes a CV), pay the fee by credit‑card or bank transfer, and then submit the application; after submission the Admissions Office reviews the files, may request additional materials, and notifies successful candidates via email.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Nagoya University requires proof of English proficiency for all programmes taught in English (the G30 International Programs), accepting TOEFL iBT (minimum total score 80, with at least 20 in each section), IELTS Academic (overall band 6.0, no component below 5.5), PTE Academic (overall score 58, with at least 50 in communicative skills) and the Duolingo English Test (minimum overall score 105); applicants who have already obtained a bachelor’s degree or higher from a university where English was the medium of instruction can receive a waiver of the English‑language requirement if they provide an official letter confirming English as the language of instruction, while for programmes taught in Japanese the university requires Japanese proficiency, typically demonstrated by passing JLPT N2 (or N1 for more advanced tracks) or by passing the university’s own Japanese language assessment, and no English test is needed for those programmes.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at Nagoya University pay the same tuition as domestic students—¥267,900 per semester, which equals ¥535,800 per academic year for undergraduate and graduate programs; the university also requires a one‑time admission (enrollment) fee of ¥200,000, an application fee of ¥30,000 payable when applying, and a compulsory student health‑insurance fee of about ¥20,000 each year, making the total cost for the first year approximately ¥785,800 (tuition + admission + application + insurance) and about ¥555,800 for each subsequent year (tuition + insurance), all amounts expressed in Japanese yen (JPY).

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at Nagoya University can enroll through either the English‑taught G30 International Programs or the exchange‑focused NUPACE, and they must meet the language prerequisite for their chosen track (demonstrated proficiency in English for G30 or a minimum Japanese ability for regular‑taught courses, with intensive Japanese programmes available for those lacking it). The university operates on a semester system with two entry windows (late September and early April) and requires students to register for at least fifteen credits each semester; each course typically carries 2‑3 credits, allowing a flexible curriculum that combines mandatory Japanese language instruction, Japan area‑studies modules, and a broad selection of courses in the student’s major field—including those offered through G30. Guided research opportunities are also available for graduate participants. The curriculum is designed to ensure that students acquire measurable learning outcomes: proficiency in academic Japanese or English, a solid understanding of Japanese culture, society and regional issues, disciplinary knowledge in their chosen field, and the ability to conduct independent research or project work, all of which are reflected in course syllabi and assessment criteria that specify expected competencies and performance standards.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Nagoya University is ranked 164th globally in the QS World University Rankings 2026, placing it sixth among Japanese institutions, and it holds the 36th position in the QS World University Rankings: Asia 2026, also sixth in Japan; its QS subject rankings include Physics & Astronomy at 83rd (sixth in Japan), Chemistry at 114th (sixth), Natural Sciences at 116th (sixth), Classics & Ancient History within the 51‑150 band (fourth), Anatomy & Physiology in the 101‑170 band (fourth), and Engineering & Technology at 162nd (seventh). In the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2026 the university falls within the 201‑250 bracket worldwide (sixth in Japan), ranks 48th in the THE Asia University Rankings 2025 and 7th in the THE Japan University Rankings 2025, while its THE Impact Ranking 2025 is 301‑400 globally (eleventh in Japan). The university is officially accredited by Japan’s Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT) and holds accreditation from the Japan University Accreditation Association, confirming its compliance with national higher‑education standards.

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: To study at Nagoya University you must first obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (CoE) from the university, which requires submitting a passport copy, a recent photo and a bank statement proving that you (or your sponsor) have at least ¥1,000,000 available for tuition and living expenses (the threshold is ¥500,000 for recipients of the Global 30 scholarship); once the CoE is issued you apply for a student visa at the nearest Japanese embassy or consulate, and per the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Study in Japan) website, a student‑visa holder may work part‑time in Japan for up to 28 hours per week after obtaining permission from the Immigration Services Agency, provided the earnings are only to supplement your living costs and do not replace the required financial support. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Japanese universities such as Nagoya University may remain in Japan to seek employment by changing their status of residence from “Student” to “Designated Activities (job‑hunting)” after graduation; the change must be applied for before the student visa expires and requires a recommendation letter from the university and evidence of active job‑search activities (typically at least several company applications). The Designated Activities status is initially granted for six months and can be extended once for an additional six months, allowing a maximum stay of up to one year for job‑hunting purposes, after which the individual must either obtain a work‑related visa or depart Japan.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Nagoya University provides a comprehensive career‑development programme for its international students that includes dedicated counseling, corporate‑information sessions, on‑campus job fairs, internship and co‑op placements, and shukatsu (job‑hunting) seminars, allowing students to gain practical experience in Japanese firms and to build professional networks; as a result, the university consistently reports a graduate‑employment rate of roughly 95 % within six months of graduation, with many international graduates securing positions in engineering, manufacturing, automotive (e.g., Toyota, Mitsubishi), information‑technology, and research‑and‑development sectors. Lương & thị trường: In Japan the typical first‑month salary for new university graduates is about ¥210,000, while graduates of graduate schools start at roughly ¥230,000, with technical (science‑oriented) fields generally earning slightly more than humanities. Among international graduates, the Nagoya metropolitan area employs around 1,900 individuals, representing roughly 6‑7 % of the national total, and the region’s labour market is heavily driven by the industrial cluster centered on automotive and precision manufacturing. The strongest demand in Nagoya is for professionals in information‑communication‑technology (ICT), robotics, digital content, and creative industries that complement the local manufacturing base, especially at major firms such as Toyota Motor Corporation, Nippon Sharyo and Aichi Machine Industry. Consequently, graduates with degrees in automotive engineering, mechanical, electrical/electronic and information engineering, as well as related science and technology disciplines, find the most abundant employment opportunities and can expect salaries at or above the national graduate average.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Nagoya University’s International Student Office (the International Education Exchange Center – IEEC) runs a comprehensive support program for all incoming overseas students that includes a university‑wide orientation for new international students, separate orientation sessions for each graduate school or department and a dedicated orientation at the university dormitories, during which students are introduced to staff, receive information on registration, health checks, meeting their academic advisors and the school’s international student advisor, and are given details on housing options and how to apply for on‑campus accommodation; the office also operates an English‑speaking “International Zone” that provides ongoing counseling, tutoring and teaching‑assistant opportunities, and it coordinates career‑service activities such as job‑hunting seminars, company information sessions, resume‑writing workshops and one‑on‑one career counseling specifically aimed at helping international students secure employment in Japan. Đời sống & môi trường: Nagoya University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with more than 100 active clubs and circles spanning cultural, academic and athletic interests—including the Federation of Liberal Art Circles, which organizes concerts, performances and the large annual MEIDAI‑SAI festival that attracts about 50,000 visitors each June—and a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association that meets regularly to celebrate Vietnamese culture, run language‑exchange sessions and provide peer‑to‑peer support for Vietnamese scholars, all of which are coordinated through the university’s broader International Student Association; the institution also prioritises safety by providing a detailed safety guide in the student handbook that covers disaster preparedness, loss of personal items, harassment prevention, counseling services and student insurance, while offering comprehensive student‑support services such as orientation programs, health and counseling resources, career development offices and a campus‑wide network of advisors to help international students adjust smoothly to life in Nagoya.
