### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- PSB Academy welcomes international students to apply for its full‑time, part‑time and short‑course programmes, with three main intakes each year (January, May and July/September) and a specific deadline that is usually set 4–6 weeks before each intake; applicants must complete the online International Student Application Form, upload the required documents (passport copy, passport‑photo, birth certificate, notarised academic transcripts, English‑language proof such as IELTS/TOEFL or an institutional letter, and a bank‑balance statement of at least S$30,000 for visa‑required countries), and then pay the non‑refundable application fee of S$490 for non‑E‑Learning courses or S$160 for E‑Learning courses (waived for progressing international students). After payment, the academy processes full‑time and part‑time applications within 10 working days and short‑course applications within 3 working days; once the offer is issued, students can request PSB to apply for a Student’s Pass on their behalf (which takes about four weeks).

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to PSB Academy must submit certified copies of their academic transcripts (học bạ) and meet the minimum secondary‑school qualification criteria, which can be any of the following: a pass grade in at least two GCE A‑Level subjects, an IB Diploma with a total score of 28 or higher, a GCE O‑Level (or equivalent) with English at grade 1‑6, a NITEC/Higher NITEC, or other recognized qualifications assessed case‑by‑case (including mature applicants with ≥30 years of age and eight years of work experience); English language proficiency must be proven with IELTS 5.5, TOEFL, PTE, or an equivalent local qualification; for Vietnamese applicants the academic benchmark is a Grade 12 GPA of 7.5 (out of 9) or the same A‑Level/IB thresholds, and similarly for other ASEAN countries (e.g., Malaysian SM3 average 75 %, Indonesian SMA 85 %). No general portfolio or interview is required for most programmes, but certain specialised courses (such as design or media) may ask for a portfolio and/or interview at the faculty’s discretion.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- PSB Academy requires all international students whose first language is not English, or whose previous studies were not taught entirely in English, to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least an overall IELTS score of 6.0 with a minimum of 5.5 in each band (or an equivalent qualification); the same threshold applies to other recognised tests, with a PTE Academic score of 50 or higher and a Duolingo English Test score of 105 or higher generally accepted, provided the test results are dated within two years of enrolment. Applicants may be exempted from submitting test scores if they can provide a letter from their most recent school or university confirming that English was the medium of instruction throughout their studies, or if they have completed their entire pre‑university education in an English‑speaking country; in such cases the academy may instead require an in‑house English assessment and an oral interview to confirm proficiency. If the required scores are not met, students must enrol in PSB Academy’s Certificate in English Proficiency (CEP) programme before commencing their degree.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at PSB Academy pay all fees in Singapore dollars (S$); the standard non‑e‑learning application fee is S$490 (waived for progressing students) and S$160 for e‑learning programmes, and mandatory student‑pass administration (ICA) fees add S$150, while a deferment request costs S$75, with other course‑specific miscellaneous charges applied as listed in the 2024 fee schedule; full‑time undergraduate tuition is roughly S$30,000 per year (about S$60,000 for a typical two‑year degree), postgraduate certificates and diplomas range from S$15,000 to S$20,000 for the entire programme, and the MOE Tuition Grant is only available to Singapore citizens and permanent residents, so it does not apply to international students, making the approximate total annual cost for an international undergrad about S$30,150 (tuition + application + ICA fee) plus any optional fees.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT'S PASS, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- PSB Academy itself is not listed as an independent institution in the QS World University Rankings or the Times Higher Education (THE) Rankings, so it does not have an overall QS or THE rank or subject‑specific QS/THE rankings; instead, its reputation is derived from the rankings of its overseas university partners, such as the University of Newcastle, Australia, which was placed 179th in the QS World University Rankings 2024, and other partners that appear in both QS and THE league tables. The academy’s programmes are delivered in partnership with universities that hold recognised business‑school accreditations – notably AACSB, EQUIS and AMBA – and the institution itself is approved by Singapore’s Committee for Private Education, holds the EduTrust certification and meets the Ministry of Education’s standards for private education providers, ensuring that its qualifications are recognised and meet quality‑assurance requirements for international students.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students admitted to PSB Academy must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) and provide proof of sufficient financial resources, typically recent bank statements or a sponsorship letter showing enough funds to cover tuition fees and living expenses for the duration of the course; the exact amount is not fixed but must demonstrate the ability to support oneself without needing employment. Under Singapore’s Ministry of Manpower (MOM) regulations, holders of a Student’s Pass studying at private education institutions such as PSB Academy are not permitted to engage in any paid or unpaid work, nor to run a business, while the pass is valid; work is only allowed after the pass is converted to an appropriate work pass or upon graduation under any post‑study work scheme approved by ICA. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from PSB Academy who hold a valid Student’s Pass can apply for a Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) to remain in Singapore and look for employment after completing their studies; the ICA issues the LTVP for a period of one to two years, which may be renewed provided the holder continues to meet the eligibility criteria, and while the LTVP does not automatically confer a right to work, the holder may obtain a Letter of Consent from the Ministry of Manpower to take up employment during the pass’s validity.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: PSB Academy in Singapore offers a robust set of career‑support services for its international students, including a dedicated Career Services office that runs a Next Step Virtual Internship Programme and a Kinobi‑powered Career Portal that links students and graduates to a wide range of internship, co‑op and full‑time job opportunities across sectors such as accounting and finance, analytics and data science, allied health, business management, construction, cyber security, engineering, hospitality and tourism, human resources, IT and computer science, life sciences, logistics and supply chain, marketing, media and communications, nursing and sports science. Through these platforms, students receive resume and cover‑letter building tools, interview training and direct access to employer listings, enabling many to secure industry‑relevant placements during their studies and achieve employment shortly after graduation; the academy reports that a substantial majority of its graduates—typically around 80 %—obtain relevant employment or further study within six months of completing their programmes, with alumni commonly entering roles such as financial analyst, data scientist, health care practitioner, project manager, cybersecurity specialist, software engineer, hospitality manager, supply‑chain analyst and marketing executive. Lương & thị trường: PSB Academy’s most recent graduate employment survey shows that 73.5 % of its graduates were in the labour force, with 45.4 % in full‑time permanent jobs and a median gross monthly salary of S$3,350, while the broader private‑education sector reports a median of S$3,500; in the overall Singapore labour market, the Ministry of Manpower’s salary data for 570 occupations reveal that the highest‑paid roles—often the most in‑demand for both local and international graduates—include international market agents/representatives (S$9,530), harbour pilots (S$8,833), safety‑and‑security consultants (S$8,318), software developers (S$8,887), ICT business‑process consultants/business analysts (S$8,500), automation/robotics engineers (S$8,588), data analysts/statisticians (≈S$8,500) and senior engineering or medical specialists such as veterinarians, radiologists and anaesthesiologists (S$8,500‑S$6,700), indicating that occupations in information and digital technologies, engineering, finance and health care command the highest salaries and thus represent the strongest demand for graduates, especially those with specialised technical or professional qualifications.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: PSB Academy’s International Student Office, operated through the Student Success Office, provides a comprehensive support suite for overseas students that includes a formal orientation program covering campus life, academic services and Singapore settlement basics, dedicated housing assistance that arranges transport from Changi Airport, helps secure accommodation with partner providers such as Evo House Services, 31 Homestay, Dwell Student Living, Coliwoo Youth, Milchel Hostel and Westwood Hostel, and offers practical aid for medical check‑ups and student‑pass collection, while its Career Services team delivers ongoing career counseling, internship placement, job‑search workshops and employer networking to help international graduates transition smoothly into the professional world. Đời sống & môi trường: PSB Academy welcomes more than 17,000 students each year from over 50 nationalities, and its City Campus at Marina Square offers a vibrant campus‑life ecosystem that includes a central Townhall communal space, The Hive support centre, fully equipped lecture theatres, computer labs and specialised studios, as well as a suite of co‑curricular activities such as Student Clubs, an Interest Hub, Leadership Development programmes and a virtual notice board that keep students connected; safety and wellbeing are supported through dedicated Student Care services, comprehensive student insurance, a Student Success Office and orientation programmes that introduce newcomers to campus security measures and emergency procedures, while the international student community—including a growing number of Vietnamese learners—benefits from cultural and language‑specific groups, informal Vietnamese student networks and university‑wide events that celebrate Vietnamese traditions, providing both social support and opportunities to share their heritage with peers.
