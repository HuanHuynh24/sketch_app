### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Yonsei University admits international students for two regular intakes each year: a spring semester (starting in September) and a fall semester (starting in March). After the online submission, admitted students must post the original documents to the Office of Admissions by 22 June 2026. For the 2026 fall intake the online application period runs from 10 March to 26 March 2026 (17:00 KST); the same registration‑fill‑upload‑pay procedure applies (the standard fee for most majors is 150,000 won, with 200,000 won for pre‑medicine, pre‑dentistry or pharmacy programs). After online completion, applicants must print the application and mail it together with the original documents to the Office of Admissions by 10 April 2026 (registered or international express mail). **2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International freshmen must hold a high‑school diploma (or be expected to graduate by August) and submit an official transcript that includes semester‑by‑semester grades and the calculated GPA; the transcript must be authenticated with an Apostille or consular verification (and, if not in Korean or English, accompanied by a notarized translation). Yonsei evaluates applicants holistically, reviewing the transcript, any standardized test scores (SAT, ACT, IB, A‑Levels, AP, etc.), language‑proficiency results (TOPIK, TOEFL, IELTS or equivalent), awards, certificates and a school profile; there is no fixed minimum GPA but a competitive academic record is expected. For most programs a portfolio or interview is not required, but applicants to design, art, music, architecture or other creative fields must submit a portfolio and may be called for an interview as part of the admission track. Required documents also include a passport copy, completed application form, and, for Chinese applicants, the HUIKAO verification and original graduation certificate; all materials must bear the Yonsei application number and be submitted by the document‑submission deadline.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- Yonsei University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by submitting an official score from one of the accepted tests—TOEFL iBT (minimum 80), IELTS Academic (minimum 6.0), PTE Academic (minimum 55) or the Duolingo English Test (minimum 105)—and the score must be reported directly to the Office of Admissions through the designated reporting system; applicants who have completed a secondary‑school or university program taught entirely in English (or hold an equivalent English‑medium credential) may be granted a waiver of the English‑language requirement, while Korean language proficiency is mandatory for all international students, with a TOPIK Level 3 (or a Yonsei Korean Language Institute certificate) required before entering their major and TOPIK Level 4 required for graduation.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Yonsei University’s international student programs operate on a credit‑based curriculum where students must enroll in a minimum of 12 and up to 18 credits per semester, with each credit representing 15 hours of classroom instruction and a 2‑to‑1 conversion to European Credit Transfer System (ECTS) units; courses are offered in both Korean and English, and while exchange students can select from more than 1,000 English‑taught classes, enrollment is subject to prerequisite fulfillment, quota limits, and scheduling constraints, and each course listing specifies the required prior knowledge or completed courses needed for registration, with learning outcomes detailed in the syllabus to guide mastery of subject‑specific competencies, and assessment follows Yonsei’s grading scale—including pass/fail options for certain courses—ensuring that successful completion of the stipulated credits and achievement of the defined outcomes satisfy both university and home‑institution transfer requirements.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Yonsei University is ranked 50th globally in the QS World University Rankings 2026 (having been 73rd in the 2023 edition) and leads Korean institutions in several subject areas, notably religious studies where it sits 17th worldwide and 1st nationally; in the Times Higher Education Impact Ranking 2024 it placed 11th globally and is featured among the top‑200 universities in the overall THE World University Rankings, while the campus hosts approximately 7,248 international students and 377 international faculty and its programmes carry national accreditation from the Korean Ministry of Education and the Korean Council for University Education together with international accreditations such as AACSB for its business school and ABET for engineering.

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): International students admitted to Yonsei University must apply for a D‑2 (Student) visa using the online HiKorea portal (studyinkorea.go.kr), where the downloadable visa application form, passport copy, recent passport‑size photo, Certificate of Admission and Certificate of Enrollment (issued by Yonsei’s International Office) are submitted together with an application fee and proof of residency; to satisfy the financial‑proof requirement applicants must show sufficient funds to cover tuition and living costs—typically a bank statement or scholarship award demonstrating at least USD 20,000 (or the equivalent in Korean won) for the duration of study, plus any required tuition‑payment receipts—while the visa does not automatically grant work rights, students may apply for a part‑time employment permit (시간제취업허가) after six months of residence once they have an Alien Registration Card, submitting a written employment contract, the permit form from HiKorea, a current enrollment certificate, and an academic transcript, and they may work up to the legally prescribed hours at the minimum wage of KRW 9,860 per hour, with employer‑provided industrial accident insurance and enrollment in the National Health Insurance system (premium roughly KRW 70,000–90,000 per month). Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): The D‑10 Job‑Seeker Visa allows recent graduates of Korean universities—including international graduates of Yonsei University—to stay in South Korea to look for employment for an initial period of up to six months, which can be extended to a maximum of two years depending on individual circumstances and proof of active job‑search activities; eligibility requires at least an associate‑degree level qualification earned within the past three years (or a bachelor’s degree or higher for most applicants), and graduates may be exempt from the points‑based evaluation if they hold a TOPIK Level 4 or higher, have passed the KIIP Level 4/5 assessments, or otherwise meet the criteria listed on the Hi‑Korea portal; while on D‑10 holders are not permitted to engage in full‑time work, limited internships or part‑time work are allowed under strict conditions, and once a suitable job offer is secured the visa holder must obtain an employer‑sponsored E‑7 Specialized Employment Visa to commence full‑time employment.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Yonsei University offers extensive internship and co‑op support for its international students through the Global One‑Stop Service Center, which provides job and internship listings, individual advising, résumé coaching, mock interviews and regular career‑focused events across all schools—including the Underwood International College, the Graduate School of International Studies and the School of Business—and most graduates secure employment quickly, with overall placement rates exceeding 90 % within six months of graduation; alumni typically enter fields such as finance, consulting, multinational corporations, technology, government, NGOs and academia, and the Global MBA program, for example, ranks among the top in South Korea for three‑month post‑graduation job offers and demonstrates strong salary growth and networking outcomes. Lương & thị trường: In South Korea the hiring rate for international graduates rose to 13.8 % in 2024, up from 11 % the year before, while 33.3 % of those graduates earned a monthly salary exceeding 3 million won (about $2,100), marking the highest proportion since the survey began in 2007; by contrast, the overall hiring rate for Korean graduates stood at 69.5 %, and roughly one‑third of all graduates secured positions in high‑demand fields such as business development, engineering, information technology, finance, consulting, marketing, sales and project management, with many Yonsei University alumni being placed at leading firms including Samsung, SK and LG Electronics and reporting an average starting salary comparable to the 3 million‑won benchmark.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Yonsei University’s Office of International Affairs (OIA) runs a comprehensive International Student Office that welcomes new international students with an orientation program, assists them in finding both on‑ and off‑campus housing, offers career‑development services through its Global One‑Stop Service Center (GOSC) and provides Korean language instruction through the Korean Language Institute (KLI); the OIA can be contacted at study@yonsei.ac.kr or +82‑2‑2123‑3535, the KLI at yskli@yonsei.ac.kr or +82‑2‑2123‑3464‑5, and GOSC’s student services at iservice@yonsei.ac.kr or +82‑2‑2123‑7378, and together they ensure international students receive orientation, housing support, career counseling, and language training throughout their stay in Seoul. Đời sống & môi trường: Yonsei University’s campus life is vibrant for international students, offering a wide range of clubs and associations—including the Mentors Club and Yonsei Global, both run by the Office of International Affairs, as well as numerous foreign‑student groups such as the Vietnamese Student Association, which meets regularly to celebrate Vietnamese holidays like Tet, provides Korean‑Vietnamese language exchange, and offers a buddy‑system and counseling support for new Vietnamese arrivals; the university hosts more than 5,000 international students across its main and Mirae campuses and provides extensive safety infrastructure, including a dedicated campus police unit, 24‑hour patrols, emergency call stations and a safety‑alert app, as well as mandatory orientation sessions that cover personal security, fire‑drill procedures, and resources such as on‑site medical services and counseling centers to ensure a safe and inclusive environment for all students.
