## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): To obtain a D‑2 student visa for Korea University you must submit the HiKorea visa‑application form, a valid passport with a recent 3.5 cm × 4.5 cm photo, the university‑issued Certificate of Admission, a Certificate of Enrollment (or “Certificate of Registration & Payment” showing tuition receipt), proof of residence (dormitory confirmation or rental contract), and a financial statement that demonstrates sufficient funds – typically a minimum balance of US$10,000 for one semester or US$20,000 for two to four semesters, which the university translates to roughly 12 million KRW for six months or 24 million KRW for one year; scholarships or sponsor letters are accepted as long as they clearly state the amount and duration. The visa fee is 130,000 KRW and the application is made at the Korean embassy or consulate in your home country. Once in Korea, you must obtain an Alien Registration Card within 90 days and can work up to 20 hours per week on‑ or off‑camp after obtaining written permission from the university and immigration (on‑campus work usually requires only university approval, while off‑campus employment needs a separate work‑permit application). For extensions you must present an updated enrollment certificate and renewed financial proof, and all detailed guidelines are available on the official immigration portal studyinkorea.go.kr. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): The D‑10 (Job‑Seeker) visa lets international graduates of Korean universities—typically those who have earned at least a bachelor’s degree from a Korean‑accredited four‑year university within the past three years—remain in South Korea to look for employment, and it is normally issued for six months but can be extended up to two years depending on individual circumstances and compliance with reporting requirements; applicants who hold a TOPIK 4 or higher, have passed the KIIP Level 4 assessment, or meet other point‑based criteria are exempt from the standard point system, and while on D‑10 full‑time work is not permitted (only limited internships or part‑time work under strict conditions), once a qualifying job offer is secured the holder must switch to an E‑7 (Specialized Occupation) work visa, which is sponsored by the employer and allows full employment in the designated field.

Fields that command the strongest demand and highest starting salaries include medical and pharmaceutical programs, which lead with an employment rate of about 79 percent, followed by education (71 percent) and engineering (70 percent); across all sectors, entry‑level salaries for new graduates typically range from roughly 2.5 million won per month in manufacturing and construction roles up to 6.5 million won for university lecturers, with the most lucrative positions in IT and software development averaging around 5 million won, healthcare and medical specialties around 5.8 million won, and senior professional roles such as surgeons, CEOs or CFOs often exceeding 8–9 million won and sometimes surpassing 20 million won.
