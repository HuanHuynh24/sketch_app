### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Aix‑Marseille Université admits international students in several annual intakes: the general “Études en France” campaign opens on 1 October, with the primary deadline for DAP programmes on 15 December (other programmes set their own deadlines through the French embassy), while the IAE business school’s MSc 1 and MSc 2 programmes require applications by 16 March 2026. After submitting a complete online file through the university’s admissions platform (creating a candidate space and uploading all required academic and identity documents), successful candidates receive a test‑result certificate and are then invited to complete administrative registration, which opens from the end of June to mid‑July and again from the end of August to mid‑September; during this step students pay the differentiated registration fee applicable to non‑EU applicants, activate their digital workspace (ENT) and obtain their student card.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Aix‑Marseille Université admits non‑EU students who hold a secondary‑school leaving certificate that is recognised as equivalent to the French Baccalauréat (including International Baccalaureate or any diploma that gives access to higher‑education in Europe); for a first‑year bachelor (L1) the applicant must submit an official transcript (học bạ) showing the full list of subjects and grades, a copy of the diploma (or provisional certificate if the final result is not yet issued), and, where the programme is taught in French, proof of French proficiency at least at the B2 level (DELF/DALF, TCF, TEF, etc.) or, for programmes taught in English, an English test such as IELTS 6.0/TOEFL iBT 80. While the university does not impose a universal GPA cut‑off, most faculties expect an average of roughly 12/20 (≈ 60 %) on the secondary‑school grades or, for the IB, a minimum score of 26 points; higher scores improve the chance of admission, especially for competitive licences. The pre‑admission DAP (Dossier d’Admission Préalable) must be completed – a “white” DAP is sent to the French consular service abroad, whereas a “green” DAP is used for students already residing in France – and it includes the diploma, transcripts, language certificates and, where required, a curriculum‑vitae and a motivation letter. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- Aix‑Marseille Université requires non‑French‑speaking international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency, typically with IELTS 6.0 overall, TOEFL iBT 85, an equivalent PTE Academic score of about 58, or a Duolingo English Test score of at least 115; a minimum CEFR B2 level suffices for most English‑taught courses, while the Faculty of Arts and Humanities demands C1 level (or the test scores above) and the IAE Aix Graduate School of Management specifically requires TOEFL 85 or IELTS 6.0 (subject to an individual interview as an alternative). Waivers are granted when the applicant has completed a full degree program taught entirely in English, holds a qualification from an English‑medium institution, or can otherwise prove a CEFR C1 level, allowing them to bypass the formal test requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at Aix‑Marseille Université pay tuition that varies by programme: the preparatory year of the “Classes Internationales” costs 5 500 € per year, while master‑level programmes taught by IAE Aix charge 13 000 € per year for non‑European (international) students (the fee is 4 200 € for European students); all students must also pay the Contribution à la Vie Étudiante et de Campus (CVEC) of 100 € annually and a registration/application fee that generally ranges from 50 € to 100 €, so the total annual cost for a non‑European master student is roughly 13 200 € (13 000 € tuition + 100 € CVEC + ≈ 100 € registration) and about 5 600 € (5 500 € tuition + 100 € CVEC) for the preparatory year, with all amounts expressed in euros.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Aix‑Marseille Université can expect to spend roughly €10 000 – €12 000 per academic year, depending on whether they live on‑campus or off‑campus. A typical on‑campus residence costs about €255 per month (€3 060 / year), while off‑campus rentals average €500 per month (€6 000 / year). Food expenses are estimated at €300 per month (€3 600 / year), with campus cafeterias offering meals at about €3 each. Public transport is covered by a student pass; the metropolitan “Pass Métropole Étudiant” for those under 26 costs €360 per year (alternatives are €100 for Aix‑en‑Bus or €90 for a regional pass). Health‑related costs include the compulsory French social‑security contribution (free for non‑EU students after registration) plus a complementary mutuelle of roughly €25 per month (€300 / year) and a small liability/home insurance of about €25 per month (€300 / year). Mandatory university contributions are €105 for the CVEC and €724 for registration fees (once per year). Adding these items gives an approximate total of €9 850 per year for on‑campus living and about €12 000 per year for off‑campus accommodation.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at Aix‑Marseille Université can enroll in up to ten courses per semester, equivalent to a maximum of 30 ECTS, with each standard course worth 3 ECTS (24 contact hours); courses are delivered in intensive blocks of three or six hours per day over several weeks, often grouped into “plages” or elective sessions where only one course may be taken per session and each elective also delivers 3 ECTS, while some courses have specific prerequisites that are announced at the beginning of the semester and must be confirmed in a learning agreement; at IAE‑AMGSM most courses are taught in English and require TOEFL iBT 90 or IELTS 6.5 (no French needed), whereas the Faculty of Sciences and Technologies mainly teaches in French and requires at least a B1 level, offering a 4‑ECTS French‑as‑a‑Foreign‑Language course for those who need it; the curriculum is organised around transversal management themes and discipline‑specific modules, with learning outcomes aimed at developing professional management competencies, analytical abilities, and the capacity to apply theoretical concepts in practical, intensive seminar settings, assessed through continuous participation and mandatory attendance rather than a separate exam period.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Aix‑Marseille University (AMU) is positioned in the 251‑260 band of the 2024 QS World University Rankings and in the 151‑200 band of the 2024 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings; its QS subject rankings place it around 151‑200 for Engineering & Technology, Life Sciences & Medicine and Social Sciences & Management, while THE ranks its Business & Management, Computer Science and Engineering subjects in the 101‑150 range. The university’s IAE Aix‑Marseille Graduate School of Management holds the prestigious EQUIS accreditation (full re‑accreditation granted for five years in 2018 and subsequently renewed) and is also AACSB‑accredited, confirming its compliance with international quality standards. AMU attracts a large international student body, with roughly 13 000‑15 000 overseas students enrolled across its campuses, reflecting its strong global reputation and multilingual programmes.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To obtain a VLS‑TS long‑stay student visa for Aix‑Marseille Université, you must first register through Campus France if you are from a country where it is mandatory, then complete the official France‑Visas online application, selecting the long‑stay student visa option and uploading the required documents; you will need to prove your financial means, with a minimum of roughly €615 per month (about €6,150 for a typical 10‑month academic year) shown via a recent bank statement, a guarantor’s financial statement, or a scholarship certificate; you must also supply proof of health insurance that covers you in France—private insurance for those over 28, or enrollment in the French student social security system for younger students, plus a complementary health insurance plan for optimal reimbursement; you will also need to show proof of accommodation, such as a university residence confirmation, a rental lease agreement, or an official host declaration; after the appointment at the consulate or VFS centre, you will receive a visa sticker, which you must validate within three months of arrival on the ANEF portal, where you will also pay the residence tax and obtain your titre de séjour; these steps are outlined in the university’s international students handbook and the official France‑Visas and ANEF procedures. Sau tốt nghiệp: After you obtain a French higher‑education diploma (master’s level or equivalent) from Aix‑Marseille Université you can stay in France for up to one year without leaving the country by requesting the “Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour” (APS) or the “Carte de séjour recherche d’emploi / création d’entreprise” (job‑seeker‑or‑business‑creator residence permit); both are issued by the prefecture on the basis of a graduate‑status certificate from the university and can be filed up to two months before your student‑visa expires, require proof of sufficient financial resources (about € 615 per month), health‑insurance coverage and a fee of roughly € 200, and give you the legal right to look for a job or to set up a company that is related to your studies, although a separate work authorisation is needed for salaried employment; the APS is a temporary one‑year long‑stay visa that must be validated with the OFII and converted into a residence card, after which you may switch to a Talent‑Passport permit (salarié, entrepreneur, etc.) if you secure a contract or launch a qualifying business, and the entire procedure is detailed on the official Campus France and Service‑Public websites.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Aix‑Marseille Université offers an extensive career‑service programme that is open to all students, including internationals, and integrates internships, work‑study contracts (alternance) and entrepreneurship pathways into most master’s curricula; graduates benefit from a dedicated office that provides CV and interview coaching, employer networking events and a job‑placement portal, and the university reports a 94 % employment rate for its recent graduates within three months of completing their studies, with many former students securing positions in finance, auditing, consulting, engineering, ICT and research roles across France and the wider European market. Lương & thị trường: In France the average gross annual salary for recent graduates is about €39 000, with engineers seeing a 2.4 % rise and managers a 2.3 % rise year‑on‑year, while other specialisations have slipped slightly; APEC’s occupational data shows that the strongest demand for graduate talent is in digital‑technology, data‑science, engineering (especially aerospace, energy and civil), health‑care, and senior business‑management roles, where offer volumes are rated “high” to “very high” and salaries from posted offers range from €35 000 for entry‑level technical positions up to €55 000–€65 000 for managerial posts, and international graduates are notably more mobile, with roughly 27 % taking jobs abroad after completing studies at institutions such as Aix‑Marseille Université, compared with 8 % of French nationals, reflecting both the attractiveness of French qualifications overseas and the concentration of graduate employment in the Île‑de‑France region for many sectors.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Aix‑Marseille Université’s International Relations Office runs welcome desks on each campus (Aix‑Schuman, Marseille‑Centre, Marseille‑Etoile and Marseille‑Luminy) where dedicated staff greet new international students, help them complete all mandatory administrative steps such as visa and residence permit procedures, enrolment, health‑insurance registration, opening a French bank account, obtaining a student transport pass and registering with CAF to receive personalized housing assistance (APL); the desks also provide practical orientation to campus facilities, public‑transport options and local services, and they can direct students to the university’s Career Center, which offers career counseling, internship and job‑search workshops, employer networking events and assistance with CV and interview preparation. Đời sống & môi trường: Aix‑Marseille Université welcomes about 12 000 international students each year and provides a dedicated Cellule Accueil des étudiants internationaux on the Aix‑Schuman and Marseille‑Centre campuses where welcome officers help with visa, accommodation, insurance, bank accounts, transport and medical appointments; the university hosts roughly 150 student associations covering sports, culture, arts and academic interests and international students can join these clubs through the DEVEC/Direction Vie Etudiante office, which also coordinates safety services such as campus security patrols, a 24 h emergency line (112 in France) and a student‑escort program for night travel; the Service Universitaire de Santé Étudiante offers free medical consultations and chest‑x‑ray appointments for non‑EU students, and the university’s inclusion policy promotes anti‑discrimination measures; a Vietnamese student community is active on campus, organized under the Association des Étudiants Vietnamiens d’Aix‑Marseille (AEV‑AMU), which arranges cultural gatherings, language practice sessions, Vietnamese New Year celebrations and a support network that helps newcomers navigate academic and administrative procedures while fostering connections with other international peers.
