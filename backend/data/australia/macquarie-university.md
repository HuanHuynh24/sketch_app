**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Macquarie University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least IELTS Academic 6.0 overall (no band lower than 5.5) for most undergraduate and postgraduate courses, a TOEFL iBT score of 64 overall (minimum 4 in each skill), or a PTE Academic overall score of 50 with at least 36 in each component; Cambridge C1 Advanced (minimum 180) and C2 Proficiency (minimum 200) are also accepted, while Duolingo English Test is not listed among the recognised tests. An English‑language waiver is possible if the applicant has completed at least 50 % of the university’s English Language and Australian Culture Studies (ELACS) with a passing grade, holds a prior degree that was taught in English, or has successfully completed a Macquarie University College English Language Program (Functional, Vocational, Intensive or Standard Foundation) that meets the required pass levels.

**4. Học phí**

- Macquarie University charges international students tuition on a per‑year basis that varies by programme, with most undergraduate courses listed at AU$39,600 to AU$44,500 per year (e.g., Bachelor of Arts AU$39,600 and Bachelor of Information Technology AU$44,500) and postgraduate coursework courses ranging from AU$44,500 to AU$46,200 per year (e.g., Master of Commerce AU$45,100 and Master of Business Analytics AU$46,200); the typical three‑year undergraduate total tuition therefore falls between roughly AU$119,000 and AU$133,500, while a two‑year postgraduate total is about AU$89,000 to AU$92,400. In addition to the tuition, international applicants pay an application fee of about AU$120, and once an offer is accepted a “commencement fee” of AU$16,000 to AU$23,500 is required (this amount is credited against the first semester’s tuition). Other mandatory charges for international students include the Student Services and Amenities Fee (approximately AU$300 per year), compulsory Overseas Student Health Cover (around AU$600 per year), and typical textbook costs, all quoted in Australian dollars.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- Accommodation for international students at Macquarie University ranges from on‑campus student housing at AU$283–AU$592 per week (approximately AU$14,700–AU$30,800 per year) to off‑campus one‑bedroom apartments at AU$550–AU$850 per week (about AU$28,600–AU$44,200 per year); food and groceries typically cost AU$200–AU$300 per week (AU$10,400–AU$15,600 annually); public transport (train, bus, metro) is AU$40–AU$60 per week (AU$2,080–AU$3,120 per year); and compulsory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is AU$600–AU$800 per year. **2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Student Visa (subclass 500) for an official international student at Macquarie University requires a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from the university, proof of English proficiency, and evidence that you can financially support yourself (and any dependents) for at least the first 12 months of study, which the Department of Home Affairs specifies as a minimum of AUD 29,710 for a single applicant (additional amounts of AUD 10,394 for a partner and AUD 4,449 per child, plus schooling costs of AUD 13,502 per child if school‑aged). You must also maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire duration of your stay. While your course is in session you may work up to 48 hours per fortnight, with unlimited work permitted during scheduled course breaks; however, students enrolled in a master’s by research or a doctoral program have no work‑hour limit. All conditions, including health insurance, enrolment compliance, and attendance, must be met throughout the visa period. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) – Post‑Higher Education Work stream – lets international students who have finished a CRICOS‑registered degree at Macquarie University remain in Australia after graduation to live, work and study temporarily, with unrestricted work rights in any sector and the ability to include a partner and dependent children; the permitted stay varies by qualification level, with most bachelor graduates receiving a 2‑year visa (extended to 3‑4 years for certain specialised bachelor programmes), master’s graduates a 2‑3‑year visa (extended to 3‑5 years for selected master’s courses), doctoral graduates up to 4 years, and Hong Kong or British National Overseas passport holders who complete a bachelor, master or doctoral degree eligible for a 5‑year stay.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Macquarie University’s distinctive PACE (Professional and Community Engagement) programme gives international students a fully integrated internship pathway that starts with a virtual internship in the first year, adds a dedicated employability unit in the second year, and culminates in a real‑world work‑integrated placement in the third year where students apply fifteen key employability skills, and can even complete dual majors to boost their competitiveness; the university also provides specialised international‑student career counsellors and supports part‑time work while studying, and its most recent graduate destination survey reports that roughly 93 % of graduates are employed or in further study within four months of completing their degree, with only about 12 % still seeking full‑time or part‑time work, leading graduates into a wide range of roles such as investment manager, market analyst, marketing consultant, stockbroker, superannuation adviser, taxation specialist, venture capitalist and other professional positions. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries in Australia have risen steadily, with the 2024 Graduate Outcomes Survey reporting a median full‑time starting salary of about $75,000 for undergraduate graduates, $100,000 for postgraduate coursework graduates and $104,400 for postgraduate research graduates; the highest‑paid fields remain health‑related, where dentistry graduates earn roughly $94,000–$100,000 and medicine graduates about $85,000, followed by social work at $77,000 and teacher education at $75,000, while engineering graduates start around $70,000. Labour‑market analyses from Jobs and Skills Australia show persistent shortages in a range of skilled occupations—particularly health professionals (doctors, nurses, allied health), information‑technology and cybersecurity specialists, engineers, construction trades, and qualified teachers—indicating strong demand for graduates in these areas. International graduates, whose specific outcomes have been tracked annually since 2021, generally achieve salaries only slightly below their domestic peers (typically 5‑10 % lower) but still benefit from the same sectoral demand, with the majority securing employment within four months of graduation and experiencing comparable employment growth over three years.
