### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International degree‑program admissions at LMU Munich run on a bi‑annual schedule: applications for the summer semester must be submitted by 15 January and those for the winter semester by 15 July each year; exchange‑student nominations are due on 15 May for the winter semester and 15 November for the summer semester. Prospective students first create an account on the LMU online application portal (or via Uni‑Assist for many non‑EU applicants), upload the required documents — recognised university‑entrance qualification, certified transcripts, proof of German or English language proficiency, passport copy, CV and, where required, a motivation letter or program‑specific tests — and pay the processing fee (generally €75 for non‑EU applicants, while EU applicants are usually exempt). After submission the International Office reviews the file and, if complete, issues an admission letter and a registration appointment; the student then completes the formal enrolment (Rückmeldung) at the university registrar, pays semester fees and, if needed, obtains a student visa.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to LMU Munich must hold a university‑entrance qualification equivalent to the German Abitur (e.g., a national high‑school diploma, an International Baccalaureate, or a recognised bachelor’s degree for postgraduate study) and submit the original certificates together with officially certified translations (English, French, Italian, Catalan, Latin, Portuguese, Romanian and Spanish are exempt). For most countries the application is processed through uni‑assist, which also issues a VPD (Vorprüfungsdokumentation) that confirms the document’s validity and converts grades to the German scale (usually a 2.5 – 3.0 GPA equivalent is the informal minimum, though exact cut‑offs vary by programme). Applicants from China, India and Vietnam must additionally provide an APS (Akademische Prüfstelle) certificate. Proof of language proficiency is required: German‑medium programmes need at least B2 level (TestDaF 4 – 5, DSH‑2 or higher) and English‑medium programmes accept TOEFL iBT ≥ 90, IELTS ≥ 6.5, or comparable tests. Certain faculties (e.g., Fine Arts, Architecture, Music, Design, and some humanities) also demand a portfolio, audition or interview as part of the aptitude assessment, which is organised by the respective department and must be submitted together with the application. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- LMU Munich requires proof of English proficiency only for programmes that are taught in English, and the university’s standard thresholds are a TOEFL‑iBT score of 100 (or the equivalent computer‑based score of 250 or paper‑based score of 600) and an IELTS overall band of 7 points; Cambridge C‑level certificates (CPE or CAE) are also accepted, while the school does not list PTE Academic or Duolingo English Test as admissible alternatives and therefore they are not recognised for admission, although applicants who have completed a previous degree that was taught entirely in English can usually obtain a language‑waiver and are exempt from submitting an English‑language certificate.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/student visa: To study at LMU Munich as a non‑EU citizen you must obtain a German student visa and, when applying, prove that you have at least €11,208 per year (≈ €934 per month) available, which is most commonly done by opening a blocked‑account in Germany and receiving a confirmation that the full amount is deposited and that you may withdraw the monthly allowance; alternative proofs such as a scholarship, a bank guarantee or a parent’s income statement are also accepted, but the blocked‑account is the standard route. After arriving you will need to register your residence and convert the visa into a residence permit, presenting the same proof of funds again at the foreigners’ office. While studying you may work up to 20 hours per week during the semester (or up to 120 days/240 half‑days per year) without paying social‑security contributions, provided you obtain a social‑insurance card through your employer, and you may work full‑time during semester breaks; any work beyond these limits requires special permission from the immigration office. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from Ludwig‑Maximilians‑Universität München (LMU) who have successfully completed a recognised bachelor’s, master’s or doctoral degree are entitled to a residence permit for job‑seeking that allows them to stay in Germany for up to 18 months, starting on the official date of graduation; the permit cannot be extended. To be eligible they must apply promptly after graduation (ideally before the student residence permit expires), provide proof of their degree (e.g., a certified transcript or diploma), have health‑insurance coverage, and demonstrate sufficient financial means (approximately €947 per month) to support themselves while searching for a job; they may work full‑time in any occupation during the 18‑month period, but must eventually secure a qualified position that matches their qualifications in order to convert the permit to a work visa or EU Blue Card.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: LMU Munich offers a dedicated Career Services programme for international students that includes career events, seminars, professional‑language courses, a mentoring network of roughly 300 volunteers, and a job board listing internships, Werk‑student (working‑student) positions, part‑time jobs and permanent roles in Germany and abroad; international students may work up to 140 days (or 280 half‑days) per year without a separate permit, while university‑assistant (HiWi) jobs have no time limit as long as studies are not impaired, and internships required by a degree are exempt from the 140‑day cap. The university recommends internships of three to six months to ensure meaningful integration and advises careful assessment of longer placements to avoid exploitation. LMU’s graduate outcomes are strong: recent internal statistics show that roughly 94 % of graduates secure employment or begin a postgraduate programme within six months of graduation, with the most common sectors being engineering, natural sciences, economics and business, medicine, and information technology, reflecting the university’s broad academic profile and strong links to industry through its extensive employer network. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Germany typically earn between €40,000 and €50,000 per year after a bachelor’s degree, with engineering and IT graduates starting closer to €45,000‑€52,000 and business graduates to €40,000‑€48,000; master’s graduates command substantially higher salaries, ranging from €50,000 to €65,000 overall, with technical master’s programmes in engineering, computer science and data science reaching €55,000‑€70,000, MBA programmes around €60,000‑€75,000, and finance or consulting roles often offering €65,000‑€80,000. The German labour market shows strong demand for these graduates, especially in STEM fields—engineering, IT, computer science and data science—as well as in finance, consulting, law, economics and social sciences, sectors where skill shortages are pronounced; LMU Munich’s career services actively support international students in entering these high‑need occupations, and graduates can stay in Germany for up to 18 months after completing their studies to seek employment.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: LMU’s International Office greets new students with a structured orientation programme that includes welcome parties, information evenings, the TutoRIA tutoring service and individual orientation interviews with guidance counsellors, while also coordinating housing assistance through the Studentenwerk München – which offers a limited number of single rooms and apartments in its student residences (applications are submitted via the International Office’s online portal) and points students to the Apartment Bureau in the Student Village and private‑market notice boards; beyond these services, LMU’s Career Service (Career Center) supports international students by providing one‑to‑one career counselling, workshops on CV writing and interview techniques, a job‑search portal with internships and graduate positions, and regular networking events and employer fairs to help students transition from study to the professional world. Đời sống & môi trường: LMU Munich offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with more than a hundred student‑run groups covering everything from tutoring (the TutoRIA program) and study‑abroad support (the Munich Erasmus Students Association, MESA) to sports, cultural events, religious communities and language clubs, and these organisations regularly host welcome parties, karaoke nights, pub crawls, castle tours and virtual activities that help newcomers make friends and explore Munich; the university’s International Office and the Student Services Organization (Studentenwerk München) also provide dedicated counselling, health‑insurance guidance, 24‑hour emergency assistance and a campus security office, making the campus one of the safest in Germany, and Vietnamese students can join the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) at LMU, which arranges language‑exchange evenings, Vietnamese‑culture celebrations such as Tết, informational meet‑ups about visas, housing and internships, and collaborates with other Asian and international groups to create a supportive community within the broader LMU international network.
