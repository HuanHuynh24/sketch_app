### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD) admits international students through two yearly intakes for its graduate programmes – one in January (the “January intake”) and another in September (the “September intake”) – with the most recent schedule showing the January 2027 intake opening on 16 April 2026 and closing on 30 September 2026; applicants must pay a non‑refundable application fee of S$21.80 (GST‑inclusive) at the time of submission, and the application is completed entirely online through the SUTD portal where candidates upload English‑language (or certified English translations) of all required documents, pay the fee, and may be invited to an interview before a formal offer is made; for undergraduate admissions SUTD typically runs a single main intake in August (with a secondary intake in January for certain programmes), and prospective international undergraduates follow a similar online‑application process, adhering to the published application window (usually a few months before the intake) and paying the same S$21.80 fee.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- SUTD admits international students with Singapore‑Cambridge GCE A‑Levels, International Baccalaureate, or equivalent qualifications, requiring good passes in at least three advanced subjects—typically H2 Mathematics and a H2 Science (Physics or Chemistry) for A‑Levels, and three H2‑equivalent subjects plus General Paper and Project Work for IB, together with a pass in Mother‑Tongue Language (MTL) at B grade (or S grade for H1 MTL) for A‑Levels or a pass in Mandarin or another MTL at Standard or Higher Level for IB; there is no fixed minimum CGPA, as the university evaluates overall GPA alongside performance in relevant subjects and advanced modules, and applicants must upload full transcripts of all semesters with final results when available, provide English‑language proof (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, SAT or C1) if their prior study was not in English, and for design‑oriented programmes such as Architecture a portfolio is required and shortlisted candidates may be invited to an interview or to sit the SUTD University Entrance Examination.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Singapore University of Technology and Design requires all international applicants whose previous study was not conducted in English to submit an English‑language proficiency test; accepted tests are IELTS (Academic), TOEFL iBT, Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic) or Cambridge C1 Advanced (and, for some programmes, the SAT English component), and there is no fixed minimum score because each application is assessed holistically on academic and non‑academic achievements. A waiver of the English test is granted automatically when the applicant can demonstrate that the medium of instruction for their most recent qualification was English (for example, through an official letter from the institution, a certificate stating English as the language of instruction, or by holding qualifications that are recognized as English‑medium such as Singapore‑Cambridge GCE O‑Levels/A‑Levels, International Baccalaureate with English A, or a degree from an English‑language university). SUTD does not list Duolingo English Test as an accepted qualification, so candidates needing to meet the requirement must use one of the accepted tests unless they obtain a waiver based on English‑medium prior study.

**4. Học phí**

- For international students at the Singapore University of Technology and Design the standard (non‑subsidised) tuition fee is S$33,120 per academic year, while the higher‑rate fee listed for AY 2024 for students on a Graduate Assistantship Programme is S$52,994 per year and the Master of Science in Sustainable Design (MSSD) programme charges S$26,054 per year; the Ministry of Education Tuition Grant is not available to international students, so they pay the full non‑subsidised amount. In addition to tuition, a one‑time enrolment fee of S$10 is charged and a compulsory miscellaneous fee (CMF) of S$232 is applied per term (approximately S$464 per year), bringing the typical total annual cost for a regular undergraduate international student to roughly S$33,594 (S$33,120 + S$10 + S$464). The non‑refundable application fee for overseas applicants is S$30. All amounts are quoted in Singapore dollars (SGD).

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students accepted to the Singapore University of Technology and Design must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) through ICA’s SOLAR system, and the application requires a valid passport, an acceptance letter, and proof of sufficient financial resources—typically a recent bank statement, a scholarship or sponsorship letter, or a guarantee that at least S$10,000 per year (or roughly S$2,000 per month) is available to cover tuition and living expenses; the ICA may also ask for the sponsor’s employment proof and tax documents. Once the STP is issued, students are automatically permitted to work up to 16 hours per week during term time and unlimited hours during school holidays without needing a separate work permit, provided that employment does not become their primary activity; any business activity must be low‑key and secondary to studies, and students must ensure they do not exceed the weekly hour limit to avoid pass cancellation or other penalties. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD) are eligible to apply for a Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) specifically for job‑seeking after completing their studies; the pass is issued by the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) for a maximum period of 12 months, is non‑renewable, and does not require a prior job offer, provided the applicant holds a valid Student Pass, has successfully graduated from the Institute of Higher Learning, and meets the standard health and character criteria set by ICA.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: SUT D’s graduates enjoy strong employment outcomes, with roughly 88 % securing a job within six months of graduation and about 73 % in full‑time permanent positions; in the 2022 graduate employment survey 87.6 % were in full‑time permanent work and 91.5 % were employed overall, while the 2025 follow‑up shows a median gross monthly salary of about S$4,900 and a mean of S$5,000, and 30 % of those in permanent roles received return offers from the companies where they previously interned, indicating that internships are a key pathway to employment—SUT D requires all undergraduates to complete at least one industry internship, and many students complete two or more, with the majority of graduates finding roles in Information & Communication, Financial & Insurance, and Public Administration & Defence sectors, and design‑AI graduates achieving an 81.8 % placement rate. Lương & thị trường: In 2022, fresh graduates from the Singapore University of Technology and Design earned an average gross monthly salary of about S$5,100, with a median of S$4,800, and the highest employment rates were in the Information & Communication, Financial & Insurance, and Scientific Research & Development sectors where more than half of graduates secured full‑time permanent roles; the Ministry of Manpower’s most recent entry‑level vacancy data also shows that the top posted occupations for new graduates are sales‑manager positions in wholesale trade, retail‑manager roles, and logistics or production‑planner jobs, each attracting roughly 20‑30 openings in the latest quarter, indicating strong demand for these functions among both local and international graduates.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: SUTD’s Office of International Relations (global@sutd.edu.sg) coordinates support for all international students, including a compulsory two‑day orientation programme on arrival that introduces exchange‑student buddies, campus resources and the surrounding neighbourhood; the university also assists with Student Pass applications and any visa‑related queries. Housing assistance for international students is managed through the Student Life Office (studentlife@sutd.edu.sg) and the Student Administration Office, which provide information on on‑campus halls of residence, off‑campus rental options and related support services. Career development is delivered by the Career Development Centre (careers@sutd.edu.sg), offering career counseling, skills workshops, employer networking events and internship opportunities for both local and exchange students. Đời sống & môi trường: Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD) provides a lively campus experience for international students through a wide range of co‑curricular activities (CCAs) such as sports teams, design and robotics clubs, music and performing arts groups, entrepreneurship societies and cultural clubs, including a Vietnamese Student Association that organises Tet celebrations, language‑exchange meet‑ups and mentorship for new Vietnamese students; the university’s Student Life Office oversees well‑being services, offering free counselling, mental‑health resources and a 24‑hour security system with campus police, CCTV monitoring and an emergency‑alert app, while on‑campus housing (Freshmore halls for undergraduates and dedicated graduate residences) is staffed by resident advisers to ensure a safe, supportive living environment, making SUTD a secure and engaging place for students from Vietnam and around the world.
