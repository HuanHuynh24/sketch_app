### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Unitec Institute of Technology welcomes international students twice a year, with semester 1 starting in late February and semester 2 beginning in late July, and the typical application windows close about three months before each start date – mid‑October for the February intake and early March for the July intake – with final submission of the online application form required by early November for semester 1 and early April for semester 2; applicants must create an account on the Unitec portal, complete the online application, upload certified copies of academic transcripts, proof of English proficiency (e.g., IELTS, TOEFL or a confirming letter from the previous institution), a copy of their passport, evidence of at least NZ$4,200 in available funds for living expenses, and then pay the non‑refundable enrolment fee (approximately NZ$150) before the deadline, after which Unitec reviews the material, issues an offer of place, and the student can proceed to apply for a student visa.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students wishing to study at Unitec must be at least 16 years old and meet both academic and English‑language entry standards: for diploma‑level programmes the minimum qualification is successful completion of Year 12 (equivalent to NCEA Level 2) or an overseas secondary qualification recognised as comparable, while for undergraduate degree programmes the requirement is successful completion of Year 13 (NCEA Level 3) with roughly 48‑57 credits across three subjects (often expressed as at least 57 Level 3 credits) and a minimum overall GPA of about a B‑ (approximately 2.7 on a 4‑point scale), with equivalent standards such as an International Baccalaureate score of 24 + or Cambridge International Examination total of 120 UCAS points accepted; applicants must provide certified copies of all previous tertiary transcripts and certificates (the “học bạ”), a certified copy of their passport, and evidence of English proficiency meeting Unitec’s criteria (e.g., IELTS 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5, TOEFL iBT 60, or comparable test scores) if English is not their first language; depending on the programme, additional documentation may be required such as a statement of purpose, CV/resume with detailed work experience, work‑related references, and for Architecture, Design, Visual Arts and related fields a portfolio (and occasionally an interview) is mandatory, while...

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students wishing to enrol at Unitec Institute of Technology must meet the NZQA‑mandated Rule 2025 English language entry standards, which are usually expressed as a minimum IELTS overall score (with at least 5.0 in each band) that varies by programme – for example, courses that require IELTS 5.0, 5.5, 6.0 or 6.5 correspond respectively to NZ Certificate in English Level 3 (General/Applied), Level 4 (Academic), Level 5 (Academic) and higher; equivalent thresholds accepted by NZQA are TOEFL iBT 45 – 79 (aligned with the same IELTS bands), PTE Academic 30 – 50 and Duolingo English Test 95 – 115. Candidates who have successfully completed an approved Unitec English language programme (NZ Certificate Level 3, 4 or 5) or who hold a prior qualification that was taught entirely in English, or who are nationals of an English‑speaking country, may be granted a waiver of the English test requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- For the 2026 academic year Unitec charges international tuition in New Zealand dollars (NZ $) per programme as follows: the New Zealand Certificate in English Language costs NZ $7,158; the New Zealand Certificate in Study and Career Preparation (e.g., Architecture, Art & Design, Business, Engineering, Health, Applied Science, Screen Arts, Acting and Technical Production, Social Work) costs NZ $13,208; most New Zealand Diplomas, Bachelor degrees (including Business, Applied Technology, Architecture, Landscape Architecture, Design & Contemporary Art, Performing & Screen Arts, etc.) are NZ $26,415 per academic year; and postgraduate diplomas and master programmes (such as Creative Practice, Architecture, Landscape Architecture) are NZ $30,415 per year. An application fee of NZ $150 is required for international applicants, and when paying by international bank transfer an additional NZ $20 bank‑fee is added to the total amount.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Unitec Institute of Technology can expect to pay tuition that ranges from about NZD 13,200 for a one‑year certificate to roughly NZD 26,400 per year for diploma or bachelor‑level programmes, while the cost of living in Auckland typically amounts to around NZD 15,000 annually. This living budget breaks down into roughly NZD 9,000–12,000 for accommodation (shared apartments or student housing), NZD 4,000–5,000 for food, NZD 1,200–1,500 for public transport and occasional travel, and NZD 600–800 for the mandatory medical and travel insurance required for the duration of study. Adding these components together, an international student’s total yearly expense in New Zealand generally falls between NZD 28,000 (for lower‑fee certificates) and NZD 41,000 (for diploma or bachelor programmes).

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Unitec Institute of Technology in Auckland is a New Zealand polytechnic rather than a university and therefore does not appear in the QS World University Rankings or the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, nor does it have subject‑specific QS or THE rankings; its programmes are instead recognised through the national quality framework, being fully accredited by the New Zealand Qualifications Authority (NZQA) and, where applicable, by professional bodies such as Engineering New Zealand for engineering programmes, the Nursing Council of New Zealand for nursing, and the New Zealand Teaching Council for teacher‑education, ensuring that its diplomas, bachelor’s and postgraduate certificates meet the standards required for both domestic and international students.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: International students applying for a fee‑paying student visa to study at Unitec must submit a completed Financial Undertaking (INZ 1014) together with six months of bank statements that show they have enough money to cover both tuition and living costs; for a programme of 36 weeks or longer this means proving at least NZ$15,000 per year for living expenses (or NZ$20,000 for a full year/ NZ$10,000 for a single semester as a minimum threshold) plus evidence that the first year’s tuition fees are paid and that sufficient funds are available for the balance of the study pathway, and they must also demonstrate the means to purchase an outward travel ticket. While studying, a student visa holder is permitted to work up to 20 hours per week during scheduled course periods and full‑time during recognized holiday breaks, and after completing at least 30 weeks of full‑time study they may be eligible to apply for a Post‑Study Work Visa within three months of their student visa’s expiry. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Unitec Institute of Technology can apply for a Post‑Study Work Visa (PSWV) if they have successfully completed a qualification that meets New Zealand’s study‑to‑work requirements, have undertaken at least 30 weeks of full‑time study in that programme, have held a student visa for the same qualification and lodge the PSWV application within three months of their student visa expiring; the visa length depends on the level and length of the qualification – a level 7 or higher qualification (such as a bachelor’s degree or higher) studied for at least one academic year grants up to three years of open work rights, a level 4‑6 qualification that required two academic years (or two sequential level 4‑6 qualifications each requiring one academic year) grants up to two years, and a graduate diploma (level 5‑7) is now limited to the period of study in New Zealand, up to a maximum of one year.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Unitec Institute of Technology in Auckland embeds mandatory industry‑based internships, work‑integrated projects and co‑op placements across its certificates, diplomas and degree programmes; for international students these placements are a key recruitment pathway, with the majority converting to full‑time jobs either with the host organisation or elsewhere in the sector, and the institute regularly reports graduate employment rates of roughly 80‑85 % within six months of graduation, leading graduates into a range of occupations such as business and accounting, marketing, construction and the built environment, health science, creative design, information technology and hospitality, with strong links to employers like Foodstuffs, Gilmours, IBM and other New Zealand firms that actively hire from Unitec’s talent pool. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Unitec Institute of Technology benefit from strong labour‑market demand in New Zealand’s construction, engineering and information‑technology sectors, which are listed on the country’s Green List and consequently attract higher graduate salaries; typical earnings for entry‑level IT professionals range from NZD 80,000 to 130,000 for software engineers and NZD 120,000 to 160,000 for IT managers, while engineering graduates can expect salaries of roughly NZD 70,000 to 120,000 depending on specialization, and construction‑related roles such as civil engineers and site managers command around NZD 65,000 to 110,000, reflecting the high demand for skilled workers in these fields and the favourable post‑study work pathways offered to Unitec’s Level 6‑7 diploma and degree programmes.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Unitec’s International Office requires all overseas students to attend the International Welcome Day as part of their programme orientation, which also introduces campus maps, class timetables and a range of support services; students who cannot make the scheduled session are advised to contact the Student Central team (email study@unitec.ac.nz, phone 0800 10 95 10 or 09 815 4321) for assistance and will be welcomed on their first day of class. The university provides dedicated International Student Advisors and a volunteer‑run Student Connectors programme to help newcomers settle into life in Auckland, and it offers specific insurance advice and support for medical and travel coverage. For housing, the International Office supplies an accommodation guide and one‑to‑one advice on finding on‑campus residences, university‑approved private rentals and shared housing options, including information on costs, tenancy agreements and nearby transport links. Career development is delivered through Unitec’s Careers service, which offers personalised career counselling, employability workshops, CV and interview preparation, job‑search resources and connections with industry partners to support international students in securing internships and post‑study employment. Đời sống & môi trường: At Unitec, international students can take part in a vibrant campus life with dozens of student clubs ranging from technology and cybersecurity groups to architecture, sports, cultural and community‑service societies, and they can also join informal cultural networks such as a Vietnamese student community that organises meet‑ups and shares information through platforms like WeChat and Facebook; the university’s Student Connectors programme pairs volunteer international students with newcomers to help them navigate support services, find appropriate clubs, and stay informed about safety resources—including campus security, insurance, wellbeing advisors and clear complaint procedures—while dedicated Student Support Advisors are reachable by phone (+64 9 815 4321) or email (internationalsupport@unitec.ac.nz) to address any safety or wellbeing concerns.
