**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Victoria University (VU) runs four international intake periods each year—Semester 1 Block 1 (applications close 9 January, classes start 16 February), Semester 1 Block 3 (applications close 9 March, classes start 27 April), Semester 2 Block 1 (applications close 8 June, classes start 27 July) and Semester 2 Block 3 (applications close 3 August, classes start 28 September); prospective overseas students must first create an online account on the VU application portal, carefully gather required documents (including proof of identity, academic transcripts, English‑language evidence and any prerequisite certificates), complete the six‑step application form, upload the documents and then pay the AUS $75 course‑application fee (which is waived for current VU students, alumni and certain scholarship applicants); after submission the international admissions team assesses the file, issues a Letter of Offer and Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) for visa purposes, and the student must accept the offer, pay the first tuition instalment and meet any English‑language or credit‑recognition conditions before the start date.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at Victoria University the Australian Government requires proof of at‑least A$21 041 per 12‑month period to cover living expenses, and the university’s own Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) costs about A$435 annually, bringing the minimum annual budget to roughly A$21 500. In practice students typically spend between A$7 800 and A$18 000 on accommodation (shared rental ≈ A$95–215 per week, on‑campus ≈ A$150–378 per week, private ≈ A$185–440 per week), about A$7 200–10 400 on groceries and eating out (≈ A$140–280 per week), around A$1 560 on public transport (≈ A$30 per week, with a 50 % discount available via an International Student Travel Pass), and another A$2 000–3 000 on utilities, phone/internet, personal care, and entertainment. Adding these typical costs gives an overall annual outlay of roughly A$28 000–30 000, including OSHC, while the absolute minimum (based on the 2012 estimate) was about A$18 720 plus OSHC.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at Victoria University must meet both academic and English‑language entry criteria, typically requiring a recognised bachelor's degree (or equivalent Australian qualification) and an overall IELTS score of 6.5 with no individual band below 6.0 for postgraduate programmes, while diploma pathways accept an IELTS of 5.5 and completion of Australian Year 12; the university’s credit system treats most subjects (units) as 12 credit points, so 48 points equate to one semester (four units) and 96 points to a full year of study, with prior‑learning credit allowing reduction of study length when earlier learning outcomes align with VU unit outcomes; course structures combine core units—such as Business Ethics and Sustainability, Applied Business Project (24 credit points), and professional core units like Predictive Analytics, Business Analytics and Visualisation, Experience Management Analytics, Project Management, and specialised electives (e.g., Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning for Business, Internship, Study Tour)—organized across semesters to ensure students acquire analytical, technical, and managerial competencies, and each unit specifies learning outcomes that map to the broader programme objectives, enabling clear progression and the possibility of credit recognition for relevant past study.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Victoria University (VU) in Melbourne is a TEQSA‑accredited Australian university that also holds programme‑level accreditation from a range of professional bodies, and it routinely welcomes thousands of international learners—including roughly 169 on‑shore enrolments and about 7 000 offshore students in 2024 from a wide array of countries. In global league tables VU sits in the 401‑500 band of the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings for 2024‑2025, placing it in the top 2 % of institutions worldwide, and it is ranked 70th in the THE Young University Rankings (universities under 50 years old) and between 201‑300 in the 2024 THE Impact Rankings. In the QS World University Rankings by subject, VU achieved a subject‑specific position of #43 for Sports‑Related Subjects in the 2024 QS rankings, while its broader QS standing does not place it in the overall top 1 000. Domestically, VU is recognised for strong outcomes in skills development and professional readiness, ranking #1 in Victoria for skills development (QILT) and #6 in Australia for sport science (Shanghai Ranking).

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa requires applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity by providing evidence that they can cover tuition fees, living expenses of at least AUD 29,710 per year for the primary student (or show a combined parental or partner annual income of AUD 87,856, rising to AUD 102,500 if family members accompany them), plus travel costs and any additional dependents’ expenses, and they must also supply proof of genuine access to those funds such as recent bank statements, loan documents, or official tax assessments; a mandatory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) from an approved provider must be maintained for the entire duration of the stay; work rights allow up to 48 hours of paid work per fortnight during study periods (including exam times) and unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks, with any breach potentially leading to visa cancellation; prospective international students intending to study at Victoria University must first obtain a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from the university, then submit a visa application through ImmiAccount that includes the offer letter, CoE, proof of financial capacity, OSHC evidence, and meet all health and character requirements as set out by the Department of Home Affairs. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the primary post‑study pathway for international graduates of Victoria University, allowing you to remain in Australia with unrestricted work rights after your student visa expires; under the Post‑Higher Education Work stream the stay is usually between two and three years, extending to up to five years for Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders, while Indian nationals can stay for up to two years with a bachelor’s degree, up to three years with a bachelor’s (first‑class honours in STEM) or a master’s qualification, and up to four years with a doctoral degree, and you may include eligible family members in the application.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Victoria University (VU) provides a comprehensive suite of work‑integrated learning (WIL) opportunities—including internships, cooperative education, industry‑linked placements and field experiences—across its business, health, engineering, IT, education and creative arts faculties, and it actively supports international students through dedicated career services, industry partnerships and the nationally recognised CareerTrackers internship program, which delivers 12‑week professional projects that enhance employability and have been shown to raise six‑year completion rates to 91 %; as a result, VU graduates—including those from overseas—enjoy strong labour‑market outcomes, with more than eight‑in‑ten securing paid employment, further study or professional registration within six months of graduation, and many enter fields such as finance, consulting, health services, engineering, information technology, education, community development and creative industries, reflecting the university’s emphasis on practical experience, networking and industry‑aligned skill development. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries in Australia remain strong, with full‑time undergraduates earning a median $75,000 annually, postgraduate coursework graduates $100,000 and postgraduate research graduates $104,400, while labour‑market projections from Victoria University (through the Jobs and Skills Australia Employment Forecasting Model) show the highest demand for occupations such as construction managers, aged‑and‑disabled carers, registered nurses, child carers, education aides and primary school teachers, as well as software and applications programmers, reflecting robust growth in health‑care, construction, education and technology sectors; these demand trends apply to both domestic and on‑shore international graduates, who benefit from the same shortage‑driven opportunities highlighted in the national Occupation Shortage List and related Jobs and Skills Australia data.
