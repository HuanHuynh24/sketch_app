### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply (UCAS)**

- International students who wish to study at the University of Oxford must submit their undergraduate applications through UCAS, with the deadline for the main autumn intake (the only undergraduate intake) set for 15 October at 6 pm UK time; there is no separate spring undergraduate entry, although many graduate programmes admit students in January or October and each of those programmes sets its own deadline and usually requires an application fee of roughly £75‑£120. The UCAS fee (around £27‑£28 for 2026) covers the undergraduate application, and no additional Oxford‑specific fee is charged for undergraduate applicants, while graduate applicants pay the individual programme fee directly to the university. The application process involves registering on UCAS, completing the personal statement and entering predicted grades, selecting any required admissions tests (such as the TSA, BMAT or LNAT) and arranging to sit them, and providing a referee’s report; after UCAS closes, Oxford may request further documentation, invite shortlisted candidates to interview, and, if successful, issue an offer that must be accepted through UCAS. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- Oxford requires all international applicants whose first language is not English to demonstrate proficiency by achieving the minimum scores set for one of the accepted tests: typically an overall IELTS Academic score of 7.5 with no component below 7.0, an TOEFL iBT total of 110 with minimum scores in each section (reading, listening, speaking and writing), a PTE Academic overall score of 68 with at least 62 in each communicative skill, or a Duolingo English Test overall score of 130; applicants who have been educated in an English‑speaking country for at least two years, who grew up in an English‑speaking environment, or who have completed a degree taught entirely in English may apply for a waiver, but the waiver is not automatic and must be requested early, with the university retaining the right to require a test if it deems the evidence insufficient.

**4. Học phí**

- For the 2026 entry year Oxford classifies overseas (international) undergraduate tuition at £27,840 per year for most subjects (with higher rates for courses such as Medicine at roughly £48,000 per year); the typical three‑year degree therefore costs about £83,520 in tuition alone, while a four‑year course (e.g., many science or engineering degrees) totals about £111,360. The university’s application fee for international applicants is £75, payable in GBP, and all other fees are quoted in the same currency. In addition to tuition, students should budget for college fees of roughly £3,000‑£4,000 per year, a health surcharge of about £470 per year, and any optional costs such as accommodation, meals, and travel.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt & IHS**

- For an international student at the University of Oxford in the 2026‑27 academic year, the estimated monthly living costs (covering accommodation, food, local transport and other daily expenses) range from £1,405 to £2,105, which translates to an annual cost of roughly £16,860 to £25,260. In addition, the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) must be paid upfront for the whole visa period and is currently set at £624 per year. Adding the IHS to the living‑cost estimate gives a total yearly outlay of approximately £17,484 at the low end and £25,884 at the high end for an international student at Oxford.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students applying to Oxford must satisfy subject‑specific academic prerequisites rather than a credit‑based system; most undergraduate courses require A‑level or equivalent qualifications such as 7‑8 IB points with higher‑level subjects matching the course (for example, Mathematics and Further Mathematics for Mathematics, or Physics and Chemistry for Engineering), and non‑native English speakers must meet the English language entry criteria or complete a six‑week Pre‑sessional English language course that develops academic literacy and communication skills. The curriculum for each course is organised around the three Oxford terms (Michaelmas, Hilary and Trinity), with a combination of weekly tutorials, lectures, practicals or seminars, and a core syllabus supplemented by optional papers where offered; assessment is primarily through end‑of‑year examinations and, for some subjects, written essays or coursework. Learning outcomes focus on attaining deep subject knowledge, the ability to analyse and construct rigorous arguments, proficiency in independent research and critical enquiry, and the development of high‑level written and oral communication suitable for scholarly and professional contexts.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: International students applying for a UK Student visa to study at the University of Oxford must prove they have enough money to cover both tuition fees for the first academic year (the exact amount will be shown on the Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) and must be paid or guaranteed) and living costs for nine months, which is £1,265 per month if the course is in London or £1,015 per month elsewhere, meaning a total of £11,385 or £9,135 respectively; the required funds must be held in a regulated bank account in the applicant’s (or a parent’s/partner’s, if they provide a signed letter of consent) name for at least 28 consecutive days, with the most recent evidence provided no more than 31 days before the visa application, and acceptable proof includes bank statements, building‑society passbooks, certificates of deposit or official letters on bank‑letterhead, while overdrafts, cryptocurrency, stocks, shares, pensions and non‑electronic records are not permitted, and a government‑backed student loan or official sponsorship from a national government or university is also accepted as evidence. Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): The UK Graduate Route is a post‑study work visa that allows international students who have successfully completed an eligible degree at the University of Oxford to stay in the UK after graduation; holders of a bachelor’s or master’s qualification can remain for up to two years (with this period set to be reduced to 18 months for applications made on or after 1 January 2027), while those who have earned a PhD or other doctoral qualification are granted three years, provided they apply from within the UK before their current student permission expires, have studied for at least 12 months in the UK on a valid student visa, meet the standard immigration requirements (including English language proficiency and no breaches of immigration rules), and satisfy any additional eligibility criteria such as having a valid Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from Oxford.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Oxford University provides a comprehensive internship framework for its international students through the Careers Service’s Summer Internship Programme, which places students in private firms, NGOs, research institutions and international organisations for 2‑12 weeks of full‑time work during the summer vacation and includes a defined project, a supervisor or mentor, a stipend paid at or above the national living wage (and sometimes travel or accommodation assistance); this scheme is open to undergraduates and DPhil researchers across all subjects and is designed to deliver real‑world value for the host and valuable learning for the student. In parallel, Oxford’s Graduate Outcomes Survey—conducted 15 months after graduation—shows that roughly 96 % of Oxford graduates are in either employment or further study, with the majority securing work in sectors such as finance, consulting, law, technology, academia and the public sector, and a median starting salary of about £45 000, indicating strong graduate‑employment prospects for both domestic and international alumni. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries for international graduates entering the UK labour market are now centred around a median starting pay of roughly £35,000 in 2025, with the top‑paying occupations still being investment banking (about £60,000), law (around £56,000) and consulting (circa £50,000), while demand remains strong for technology and data roles such as engineers, data analysts, cybersecurity and AI specialists, as well as for engineering, industrial and financial‑services positions that continue to sponsor visas; about 60 % of those switching from a study visa to the Skilled Worker route earned at least £30,000 in the year to June 2023 and the salary thresholds for graduate‑level Skilled Worker jobs have risen to £41,700 for the main rate and £33,400 for the discounted rate, reflecting tighter immigration rules, and overall 70 % of international graduates are employed (74 % of postgraduate‑taught graduates), though they tend to earn less than their UK‑domestic peers.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Oxford provides a dedicated International Student Office that runs an Oxford International Orientation Programme each term, offering welcome sessions, campus tours, academic briefings and practical advice on finding accommodation, while the university’s housing teams and the Oxford Student Services office help international students secure university‑owned rooms or locate private rentals and understand tenancy regulations; additionally, the Oxford Careers Service offers tailored support for international students through a “Staying in the UK to Work” pathway, specialised events, one‑to‑one adviser appointments and resources on CV writing, interview techniques and UK visa considerations. Đời sống & môi trường: Oxford’s campus life is centred on a vibrant network of more than 200 student societies and clubs, ranging from sports teams and performing arts groups to cultural and academic organisations, and among these is the active Oxford Vietnamese Society which regularly hosts language‑exchange evenings, Vietnamese‑New‑Year celebrations, cooking workshops and networking events that help Vietnamese and other international students build a supportive community; the university’s inclusive student life framework provides dedicated wellbeing and safety services such as a 24‑hour community‑and‑safety team, the Oxford University Police, a safety‑alert app, confidential counselling, and secure college accommodation with key‑card access, while the International Students page encourages newcomers to join societies to meet peers from their own country or region and offers practical guidance on adjusting to life in the UK, ensuring that Vietnamese students and other internationals can enjoy a rich social environment with reliable safety and support resources.
