## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Sciences Po’s international admission cycle for the 2026 intake opens at the end of September and the main deadline for most bachelor and two‑year master programmes (including the one‑year master tracks) is 22 February 2026 at 23:59 Paris time, while the Journalism school’s master programmes close earlier on 4 January 2026; applicants must create an account on the Sciences Po admissions portal, select their desired programme and campus, fill in all mandatory fields, upload the required documents (including transcripts, language certificates and references), pay the non‑refundable €150 application fee (with a waiver only for recognized refugee status) and finally click “Submit my application” to ensure the file is officially received—applications submitted after the deadline or without the fee are not considered.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [International undergraduate admissions - Sciences Po](https://www.sciencespo.fr/admissions/en/undergraduate/foreign-secondary-schools/) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Admission to a one-year master's programme \| Sciences Po Admissions](https://www.sciencespo.fr/admissions/en/one-year-master/) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Deadlines - International graduate admissions \| Sciences Po Admissions](https://www.sciencespo.fr/admissions/en/graduate/international-admissions/deadlines/) (Cao, score 1.00)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Sciences Po admits international students to its undergraduate programmes after a two‑stage assessment that combines a written dossier (including official secondary‑school transcripts – with a French or English translation if the original is in another language – and, optionally, SAT/ACT scores) and a mandatory remote interview that is scored out of 50 and contributes to a final mark out of 100, with applicants needing to meet the annually set minimum admission mark; there are no fixed subject prerequisites – for example, IB candidates are not disadvantaged if none of their six subjects are sciences – and language‑proficiency proof is not required at application time provided the candidate can follow courses in the chosen language. For its two‑year master’s programmes, Sciences Po requires a minimum undergraduate grade‑point average (the exact threshold is set each year) and automatically rejects applicants whose GPA falls below that level; the selection then proceeds with a holistic review of the academic record (official transcript showing the Bachelor’s average), CV, personal statement and relevant professional or extracurricular experience, but does not ask for a portfolio, while an interview is not part of the standard graduate admissions process.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [International undergraduate admissions - Sciences Po](https://www.sciencespo.fr/admissions/en/undergraduate/foreign-secondary-schools/) (Cao, score 0.61)<br>[Selection process - International Graduate Admissions \| Sciences Po Admissions](https://www.sciencespo.fr/admissions/en/graduate/international-admissions/selection-process/) (Cao, score 0.60)<br>[Undergraduate Admissions: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) \| Sciences Po Admissions](https://www.sciencespo.fr/admissions/en/undergraduate/faq/) (Cao, score 0.60)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Sciences Po requires international exchange students to demonstrate English proficiency at a C1 level, which can be proven with an IELTS score of 6.5, a TOEFL iBT (including Home Edition) score of 100, a Duolingo English Test score of 130, or a Cambridge English C1‑level certificate such as CAE or CPE; students whose mother tongue is English are exempt from providing a test result. For its undergraduate admissions, the school expects applicants to have a C1‑level command of English (assessed during the interview) but does not mandate a specific test, while French is not a prerequisite. PTE is not listed among the accepted English tests.

**4. Học phí**

- International students whose tax residence is outside the European Economic Area pay a flat tuition fee of €14 900 per year for undergraduate programmes and €20 640 per year for master’s programmes at Sciences Po, with the compulsory student life‑campus contribution (CVEC) set at €105 per academic year; the non‑EU application fee is €100, and no separate registration fee is required beyond these amounts.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at Sciences Po the typical yearly budget consists of accommodation (≈ €600‑€900 per month in Paris, €380‑€450 in Dijon, €340‑€440 in Poitiers, €400‑€600 in Reims, etc.), food (≈ €250‑€300 per month in Paris, €200‑€250 in most regional campuses), and transport (annual student passes range from about €315 in Dijon to €392 for the Paris ImagineR card, with monthly Navigo options up to €1 090 per year). French student‑social‑security coverage provides health insurance free of charge, so no additional cost is required. Adding these items gives a total annual cost of roughly €10 500‑€15 000, with the lower end based on the cheapest regional campus and the higher end reflecting Paris‑level rents and transport.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Sciences Po consistently ranks among the world’s elite in the QS World University Rankings by Subject for Politics & International Studies, placing fourth worldwide in the 2025 QS subject table and rising to third in the 2026 edition, while remaining the top university in France and the European Union for this discipline for more than a decade; in the overall QS World University Rankings the school is usually positioned within the top 100‑150 institutions, reflecting strong performance across many fields, and in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings it sits in the 151‑200 global band with especially high placement in social‑science subject rankings; the university is fully accredited by the French Ministry of Higher Education and Research, has received institutional quality validation from the Haut Conseil de l’Évaluation de la Recherche et de l’Enseignement Supérieur (HCERES), and its programmes meet European higher‑education standards, which together with its robust international reputation attract a sizable international student community—approximately 30‑35 % of its enrolment comes from abroad.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To obtain a VLS‑TS student visa for studying at Sciences Po you must first create a Campus France profile, book a France‑Visas appointment and bring your passport, a completed long‑stay visa form, the official admission letter from Sciences Po and a recent photo, then provide proof of financial resources of at least €6 150 for a 12‑month academic year (or €125 per month) which can be shown through recent bank statements, a scholarship award letter or an “attestation de prise en charge” from a French guarantor together with the guarantor’s tax notice, plus evidence of accommodation in France (housing attestation, hotel reservation or host letter). You also need health‑insurance proof: either a private policy covering you in France until you can enrol in the French social‑security system, or, for EU citizens, a valid European Health Insurance Card; once in France you must register online with the CPAM to obtain a social‑security number and later a Carte Vitale. After entering France you validate the VLS‑TS on the ANEF portal, upload the required documents (visa page, passport scan, proof of address) and pay the €75 residence‑permit tax, after which the visa itself serves as your residence permit for the duration of your studies. Sau tốt nghiệp: The “Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour” (APS), officially titled the temporary residence permit “Recherche d’emploi ou création d’entreprise” (often shortened to “job‑seeker / new‑business‑creator” permit), allows non‑EU graduates who have obtained a French higher‑education diploma—such as an international Master’s from Sciences Po—to remain in France for up to 12 months after graduation to look for a job or to prepare and launch a company in a field that corresponds to their studies; the permit is non‑renewable, grants the right to work full‑time without a weekly‑hour cap, and can be converted to a standard work‑permit once a qualifying employment contract is secured; to apply you must file the request at the prefecture (or via the online “France‑Visas” portal where available) within two months of receiving your diploma, presenting your passport, current residence card, proof of graduation, proof of sufficient financial resources (approximately €615 per month), proof of health‑insurance coverage, and a recent passport‑size photo, and the APS is also offered to nationals of countries that have migration‑flow agreements with France (e.g., India, Tunisia, Gabon, etc.) and is described on the official Campus France website under the “Job seeker/ new business creator residence permit” section.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Sciences Po provides extensive career support for international students, offering free individual and collective guidance—including internship assistance, job‑fair events, career‑resource workshops and a two‑year post‑graduation follow‑up—from the first day of study through two years after graduation, with dedicated help for placements both in France and abroad; internships and apprenticeships are the principal entry route into the labour market, accounting for 47 % of graduates’ first jobs, and the school’s graduate‑outcome figures show that 84 % of alumni secure employment within six months, 90.7 % within a year, with 69 % working in the private sector, 22 % in the public sector, 9 % in international or European organisations and 34 % employed outside France, while average gross annual salaries reach €54,846 in France and €59,883 abroad (overall average €37,164), reflecting strong placement and employment outcomes for international graduates. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries in France are roughly €39 000‑€40 000 gross per year on average, with managers and engineers earning slightly more (around €41 000‑€42 000) and other specialisations a bit less; Sciences Po graduates command an average gross salary of €39 700 in France six months after graduation and €42 400 internationally, rising to about €44 400 after thirty months on the French market. The labour market shows strong demand for engineers, managers, and IT‑related roles, especially within the French Tech ecosystem, as well as for green‑jobs, retail trade, and catering positions; permanent contracts remain high (over 86 % for engineers and managers) and executive‑level hires exceed 86 % for these groups, while APEC data highlights that positions requiring a university degree or master’s level qualification typically offer around €40 000 gross annually for contracts longer than three months.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Sciences Po’s International Student Office, located at 27 rue Saint‑Guillaume in Paris (phone +33 1 45 49 50 50), runs a dedicated welcome desk and “Meet the Services” days that introduce new international students to administrative registration, social security, health insurance, residence‑permit renewal, and especially accommodation assistance, while the broader Student Services Centre offers a Welcome Programme and orientation sessions for exchange students, a “Bienvenue en France” label‑recognised integration framework, and a career‑services department that supports internships and employment, reflecting the university’s commitment to its 49 % international student body. Đời sống & môi trường: Sciences Po’s seven undergraduate campuses host more than 1,000 exchange students each year from partner universities worldwide and actively encourage them to join the full range of campus life, including a vibrant network of student clubs and associations that organise cultural, academic and leisure activities in both French and English; among these, a Vietnamese Student Association exists on several campuses, providing a community for Vietnamese students through regular gatherings, language‑exchange events, celebrations of Vietnamese holidays and a support hub for newcomers navigating visas, housing and everyday life in France. The university’s “Bienvenue en France” label recognises the quality of its welcome services, offering orientation programmes, assistance with visa and residence permits, a disability support service and practical guidance on banking, phone and internet set‑up. Security on campus is generally high, with violent crime rare in well‑populated areas, though petty crimes such as bag‑snatching and phone theft can occur in transport hubs, stations and crowded streets; students are advised to stay alert, keep valuables secure and follow the university’s safety recommendations, which include using campus‑controlled access points, respecting evacuation and confinement procedures, and adhering to the rules governing the use of premises and information systems.
