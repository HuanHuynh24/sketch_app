**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to the University of Manchester must provide a full academic transcript and meet the university’s general entry thresholds, which for most undergraduate programmes correspond to UK A‑level grades of AAA‑AAB (or the international equivalent) and an International Baccalaureate total of around 38 points with at least 6‑6‑6 at Higher Level subjects; a minimum GPA of roughly 3.0 (or a 2:1 classification) is normally expected for postgraduate study, although exact grade conversions vary by programme. In addition to these qualifications, candidates must demonstrate the required level of English proficiency—typically IELTS 6.0 – 7.5 (or equivalent TOEFL/CAE scores) and, where applicable, submit subject‑specific evidence such as a portfolio for art, design, architecture or related creative courses, or attend an interview for highly competitive programmes (e.g., Medicine, some engineering tracks). Certain courses also have prerequisite subjects (for example, Chemistry for many science degrees or Mathematics for engineering) and may ask for additional written work or relevant work experience, so applicants should check the specific course requirements on the university’s site.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- The University of Manchester requires international applicants to demonstrate a minimum CEFR B2 level (equivalent to IELTS 5.5) for a UK‑Student visa, but most undergraduate courses ask for IELTS 6.0 – 7.0 (or 5.5 for foundation programmes) and most taught postgraduate programmes require IELTS 6.5 – 7.0, with TOEFL iBT equivalents of 80 – 100 (minimum 72 for the lowest band) and a minimum component score of 22 – 23; higher‑level courses may ask for IELTS 7.0 (TOEFL 100 – 109) and some schools set stricter component thresholds (e.g., IELTS 5.5 in each skill, TOEFL 22 in each section, and a writing minimum of IELTS 6.0). The university also accepts other qualifications such as LanguageCert Academic (scores 60 – 81+), Cambridge English (CAE/CPE grades 162‑230), Trinity ISE (scores 80‑114+), and International Baccalaureate English at higher‑level, and it will waive the English test if you have already completed a degree taught in English or hold a qualifying qualification (e.g., IB English HL, A‑level English, GCSE/IGCSE grade A/8). Manchester does not list PTE Academic or Duolingo English Test among its accepted tests, so applicants must use one of the accepted examinations unless they qualify for a waiver through prior English‑medium study.

**4. Học phí**

- For international students enrolling at the University of Manchester for the 2026/27 academic year, the typical annual tuition fee is £20,500 for most undergraduate programmes, rising to around £24,000 for engineering and technology courses and £45,000 for medicine; taught postgraduate programmes charge between £12,300 per semester (£24,600 per year) and up to £25,000 for specialised degrees, with the total cost for a standard three‑year undergraduate degree therefore ranging from roughly £61,500 to £75,000 and a one‑year taught master’s costing about £24,600; the university requires an application fee of £75 for undergraduate and £80 for postgraduate applicants, paid at the time of submission, and other mandatory charges include a submission‑pending fee of £225 for postgraduate students who exceed the thesis deadline, as well as placement fees of £1,430‑£1,965 for year‑abroad or industry placements, all quoted in British pounds (GBP).

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt & IHS**

- For an international student at the University of Manchester the typical annual cost of living is roughly £13,300 to £19,600. Accommodation is the largest expense: self‑catered university halls run about £487 per month (£5,844 per year) while catered halls can be up to £982 per month (£11,784 per year); private‑hall rooms are around £860‑£1,010 per month (£10,320‑£12,120 per year) and privately rented house‑shares cost about £645‑£710 per month (£7,740‑£8,520 per year). The university’s estimated non‑accommodation living costs for undergraduates total about £6,740 per year (covering groceries, take‑aways, transport, socialising, phone, clothes, health and wellbeing, course materials, holidays, gifts, etc.). In addition, the Immigration Health Surcharge for a student visa is £776 per year. Adding accommodation, living expenses and the IHS gives an overall yearly budget of roughly £13,300 at the low end to £19,600 at the high end.

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: International students applying for a UK Student visa to study at the University of Manchester must demonstrate that they have enough funds to cover both their tuition fees and living costs, as set out on the official gov.uk guidance for the Student route. For courses outside London, the living‑expense threshold is £1,265 per month for up to 9 months (a total of £10,270), which must be shown as cash available for a continuous 28‑day period that ends no later than 31 days before the visa application is submitted; this amount is added to the tuition fee amount shown on the CAS. Acceptable financial evidence includes original or certified bank or building‑society statements (or a letter from the bank on official headed paper) showing the required balance, fixed‑deposit or savings account statements, or a loan or scholarship letter from a recognised government, the British Council, a UK university, or another official sponsor – the loan or scholarship must be confirmed on the CAS. If the funds are held in a parent’s or partner’s account, a signed consent letter and a copy of the applicant’s birth or marriage certificate are also required. Applicants who have already been studying in the UK for 12 months or more at the time of application are considered to have met the financial requirement and need not provide additional evidence. Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): International graduates who have successfully completed an eligible undergraduate or master’s degree at the University of Manchester can apply for the UK Graduate Route visa, which allows them to remain in the UK to work or look for work without a sponsor; applications made on or before 31 December 2026 are granted a stay of 2 years (up to 3 years for PhD graduates), while applications submitted on or after 1 January 2027 will be granted 18 months for bachelor’s and master’s graduates (PhD graduates continue to receive 3 years). Eligibility requires a valid Student visa at the time of application, completion of the degree at a UK higher‑education provider that holds a Home Office sponsor licence, no maintenance fund requirement, and no need to demonstrate English language proficiency because the degree was taught in English; applicants may bring any dependants who held government or scholarship sponsorship in the previous 12 months, provided they meet the relevant consent requirements, and there is no minimum salary threshold for employment under this route.
