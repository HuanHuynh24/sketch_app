**Tóm tắt thông tin trường**

- Đại học Keio (Keio University) là một trường đại học tư thục ở Nhật Bản, tọa lạc chủ yếu tại thành phố Tokyo (cơ sở Mita) và có cả khuôn viên Shonan Fujisawa ở Fujisawa, Kanagawa; trang web chính thức của trường bằng tiếng Anh là https://www.keio.ac.jp/en/.

## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Keio University admits international undergraduate students in two yearly intakes – a spring entry (April) and an autumn entry (September). For the spring (April) intake, the online application opens in early December and must be completed by early February (the deadline is 3:00 p.m. JST on the second Thursday of February); applicants first obtain an Applicant ID on the online system, fill out the application and pay the application fee of JPY 35,000 by credit‑card (a reduced fee of JPY 5,000 is available for those who will graduate from a high school outside Japan), and then submit all required documents by postal mail within the stipulated window (typically mid‑January to mid‑February). For the autumn (September) entry, Keio offers three application periods: Period I (January 26 – February 13, 2026), Period II (February 26 – March 19, 2026) and Period III (May 25 – June 19, 2026); each period follows the same three‑step process – payment of the JPY 35,000 application fee, completion of online enrollment registration, and submission of supporting documents – with the final document deadline extending to July 31. In all cases, the fee is paid by credit card, the online system provides the Applicant ID needed for later steps, and applicants must ensure an active email address for correspondence.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to Keio University must submit official academic transcripts (in English or Japanese) and a certificate of graduation or expected graduation from their previous institution, with translations certified if the original is in another language; they are also required to provide proof of language proficiency (TOEFL iBT, IELTS, or a lecturer‑issued certificate) for courses taught in English or the relevant language, and while the Examination for Japanese University Admission (EJU) scores are strongly recommended and can be included as part of the application, they are not an absolute requirement; applicants should present their cumulative GPA or grade evaluation as part of the transcript package, and many faculties additionally request a statement of purpose and may require an interview or portfolio for programs such as the School of Media and Governance, the Faculty of Business and Commerce, and the Graduate School of System Design and Management, where the interview evaluates academic ability, motivation and fit, and a portfolio may be required for design‑oriented courses.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Keio University requires international graduate applicants to demonstrate English proficiency with either a TOEFL iBT score of at least 70 or an IELTS overall band of 5.5; these are the only English‐language tests officially accepted (TOEIC is not accepted, and the university does not list PTE or Duolingo as options). Applicants who have earned a degree from a program taught entirely in English are exempt from submitting TOEFL or IELTS scores. For students enrolling in the April term, sufficient Japanese ability is also required; however, if the applicant has completed a university program taught entirely in Japanese, a JLPT (or other Japanese‑language test) is not needed, as the university will assess Japanese proficiency during the essay and interview stages.

**4. Học phí**

- Keio University’s graduate programs charge an annual tuition of ¥1,920,000 for a full‑payment Master’s (or Doctoral) student admitted in April (¥960,000 if paid in two installments), plus a one‑time Basic Affiliation (registration) fee of ¥70,000 (¥35,000 for installment payment), a Student Health Insurance Union fee of ¥2,600 (¥1,350 for installment), and a mandatory orientation fee of about ¥30,000; the total cost for the first academic year therefore amounts to roughly ¥1,992,600 (or ¥996,350 with installment rates), all denominated in Japanese yen, and an application fee of ¥35,000 is required when the application is submitted.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Keio University can expect to spend roughly ¥700,000 – ¥900,000 per year on accommodation (average dormitory rent of about ¥60,000 per month plus annual management fees of ¥100,000 – ¥150,000), about ¥420,000 per year on food (≈¥35,000 per month), roughly ¥120,000 per year on local transport (≈¥10,000 per month), and around ¥22,000 per year for National Health Insurance, bringing the typical total annual living cost to approximately ¥1.3 – ¥1.5 million (≈¥1.4 million).

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Keio University’s international student programs require students to satisfy specific prerequisite courses and credit thresholds before advancing in the curriculum: for example, Fundamentals of Information Technology 1 must be completed before enrolling in any subsequent Fundamentals of IT courses, Physical Education 1 is required for Physical Education 2, and Graduation Project 1 must be finished before taking Graduation Project 2, which awards a pass/fail credit after the student submits a research outcome such as a thesis or practical work; most undergraduate courses offered in English to short‑term and exchange students carry two credits (with a few four‑credit options), and students may also take up to two Japanese language courses per semester, all of which are designed to develop disciplinary knowledge, research competence, and language proficiency, with the credit system and prerequisite structure ensuring that learners acquire the necessary foundational skills before progressing to more advanced or capstone modules.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Keio University hosts roughly 2,000 international students from more than 85 countries (2022) and is ranked 197th worldwide, 9th in Japan and the highest‑placed private university in the QS World University Rankings 2023; in the QS subject rankings it sits at 100th globally in Social Sciences & Management, 46th in Classics & Ancient History, 83rd in Modern Languages, 99th in Economics & Econometrics, 138th in Business & Management Studies, and falls within the 101‑200 band for Data Science & AI, Computer Science, Engineering‑Mechanical, Aeronautical & Manufacture, History and Medicine, while also ranking in the top‑200 for 13 narrow subjects; the Times Higher Education World University Rankings places Keio in the 301‑350 band worldwide (2023‑2024); the university is fully accredited by Japan’s Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT) and its business programmes hold AACSB accreditation.

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: International students admitted to Keio University must first obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) – which the university issues on the student’s behalf as the proxy – by submitting proof of their ability to cover all living and tuition costs in Japan, such as personal bank‑balance certificates, income statements, scholarship letters or a guarantor’s financial documents; once the COE is issued, the student applies for a “Student” visa at a Japanese embassy or consulate, providing passport, visa‑application form, photo and the COE copy. After entering Japan, the student may work part‑time up to 28 hours per week during the academic term and full‑time during school holidays, but only after obtaining written permission to engage in activity other than that permitted under the “Student” status (commonly called a part‑time work permit) from the Immigration Bureau, which requires a letter of acceptance from Keio confirming the employment does not interfere with studies. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed a regular degree program at Keio University may change their status from Student to a Designated Activities visa for “continuing job hunting,” which allows them to remain in Japan for up to one year: an initial six‑month period that can be renewed once for another six months. Eligibility requires that the student was enrolled as a regular (non‑exchange, non‑short‑term, non‑Japanese‑language‑program) Keio student, has a recommendation letter from the university confirming active job‑hunting activity (generally evidence of applications to at least six companies before graduation), and can demonstrate sufficient financial means for living expenses; no overseas university degree is required for Keio graduates. If a job is secured during this period, the visa must be changed to the appropriate work‑status before commencing employment.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Keio University provides a comprehensive career‑support system for international students that mirrors the “shūkatsu” process used by Japanese undergraduates, including access to information‑session briefings, interview preparation, job‑hunting diaries, alumni‑visit networks and a Japanese‑only job‑vacancy navigation platform, with services generally available to students in their penultimate or final year; the university also runs internship, co‑op and placement opportunities through its corporate‑partner network, enabling students to gain practical experience in sectors such as finance, consulting, technology, manufacturing and law before graduation, and these experiences are reflected in the university’s strong graduate‑outcome statistics—approximately 70 % of undergraduate graduates from the Faculty of Science and Technology continue on to Keio’s graduate schools while the remaining 30 % secure employment, and overall Keio’s employment rate for new graduates (including international students) consistently exceeds 95 % within six months of graduation, with many alumni taking positions at major Japanese corporations, multinational firms and start‑ups or pursuing further studies abroad. Lương & thị trường: In Japan the 2026 graduating cohort secured job offers at a rate of roughly 92 percent, reflecting intense demand across sectors such as manufacturing, information technology, healthcare and engineering, and the average first‑month salary for new university graduates rose to about ¥228,500, with technical fields typically earning higher starting pay than humanities; for international students the job‑offer rate was about 52.5 percent in 2023—substantially lower than the 86 percent rate for domestic graduates—but they are increasingly recruited for roles that require bilingual skills, especially in global‑focused businesses, and Keio University’s own employment surveys show its graduates command some of the highest entry salaries in the country, averaging around ¥300,000 per month, with the strongest demand in consulting, finance, technology and multinational corporations that value the university’s rigorous academic training and extensive alumni network.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Keio University’s International Center and Office of Student Services provide a comprehensive support system for international students, including an annual orientation that for the 2026 spring term takes place on Monday 30 March 2026 from 10:00 am to 11:15 am in Seminar Room 1 (2nd floor, 14th Building, Sosokan) on the Yagami Campus, followed by media‑center guidance and a brief counselling‑room briefing; housing assistance is coordinated by the Student Life Services Group within the International Exchange Services Group, which handles accommodation applications and related queries at each campus (e.g., Mita Campus, 8:45 am–4:45 pm, South School Bldg. B1F; Hiyoshi, Shinanomachi, and Shiba‑Kyoritsu locations); career support is offered through the Placement and Careers section of the Office of Student Services, where a Job‑Hunting Guidebook is available and students can receive individual consultations on internships and employment; academic tutoring for graduate international students is organized under the Tutorial Services program, which assigns individual tutors to full‑time international graduate students (including MEXT scholars) and operates during standard office hours across the various campuses; the International Center’s website and bulletin boards also disseminate all relevant information, and the main contact number for the International Center is 03‑5427‑1629. Đời sống & môi trường: Keio University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with more than 400 student‑run “circles” that welcome non‑Japanese members; many of these, such as the KOSMIC International Students Club, conduct activities in English and meet on weekdays at the Hiyoshi campus and online, while discipline‑specific societies like the International Politics and Economics Research Society provide academic‑focused gatherings, and the university classifies clubs into officially authorized, registered and unofficial groups, allowing only the authorized clubs to use the Keio name in publicity. For Vietnamese students, Keio has a Vietnamese Student Association that organizes cultural festivals, language‑exchange sessions and a support network for newcomers, and the International Center connects Vietnamese and other international students with mentors, housing assistance and safety briefings; campus safety is reinforced by 24‑hour security patrols, a health‑center with counseling services, clear emergency procedures, and clubs promote safety by holding meetings in well‑located campus facilities and providing contact information such as the International Center’s email (kess.cc2021@gmail.com).
