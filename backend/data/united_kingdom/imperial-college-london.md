**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Imperial College London requires international applicants whose first language is not English to demonstrate proficiency at either the standard or higher level: IELTS 6.5 overall (minimum 6.0 in each component) for standard entry and 7.0 overall (minimum 6.5 in each component) for higher‑level programmes; TOEFL iBT 92 total (minimum 20 in each section) for standard and 100 total (minimum 22 in each section) for higher; PTE Academic scores are accepted (valid for two years) with the typical thresholds of around 58 overall (minimum 50 each) for standard and 64 overall (minimum 55 each) for higher‑level courses; Duolingo English Test scores of 4.5 overall (minimum 4.5 each) satisfy the standard requirement and 5.0 overall (minimum 5.0 each) satisfy the higher requirement. **4. Học phí**

- Imperial College London charges international (non‑Home) students tuition in British pounds (GBP), with annual undergraduate fees ranging from about £35,100 for programmes such as Mathematics to £52,000 for clinical years of Medicine; typical fees are £37,900‑£40,300 for Engineering, £38,000‑£41,200 for Computing, £35,500‑£38,900 for Life Sciences and £47,000‑£52,000 for the clinical phase of Medicine, while the first two years of Medicine are £37,500 per year. The non‑refundable application fee for international applicants is £115. Assuming a standard three‑year undergraduate programme, total tuition typically falls between £105,300 and £120,900, a four‑year Medicine degree totals roughly £190,000‑£210,000, and a two‑year MSc costs between £56,000 and £92,000 over the full course.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Imperial College London is ranked 2nd in the world and 1st in Europe in the QS World University Rankings 2026, and it also features among the top ten institutions in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings for 2026, typically placed within the top 6 globally; its subject‑specific performance is strongest in Engineering & Technology, Natural Sciences/Life Sciences and Medicine, where it consistently holds top‑tier positions in both QS and THE subject rankings, often ranking 1st or within the top 5 worldwide in these fields, and the university’s programmes are fully accredited by the relevant UK professional bodies—its engineering courses are accredited by the Engineering Council through institutions such as the IET and ICE, its computer science degrees are accredited by the British Computer Society, and its medical school is approved by the General Medical Council—ensuring that all qualifications meet national and international standards.

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: International students applying for a UK Student visa to study at Imperial College London must first obtain a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from the university, which will list the tuition fee amount and, if any, payments already received; if the tuition has not been paid in full, a receipt or an official sponsor letter from an approved financial sponsor (such as Imperial, a UK or overseas government, or a recognised scholarship body) must be submitted to UKVI to meet the tuition‑fee portion of the maintenance requirement. In addition, applicants must demonstrate they have enough personal funds to cover living costs—currently the UK immigration rules require the cost of the course fee plus £1,265 per month for up to nine months (or the equivalent amount for longer courses) – and these funds must be held in an account for at least 28 consecutive days, with the final day of that period falling within 31 days of the visa application. Acceptable evidence includes recent bank statements (or a bank‑verification form with a contact at the bank), a student‑loan letter, or a formal sponsorship letter that meets UKVI standards; funds held in the personal accounts of friends or unrelated relatives are not accepted. All financial documents are uploaded in the “Documents” section of the online visa form accessed via the green “Start Now” button on the official GOV.UK Stude... Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): The UK Graduate Route allows international graduates of Imperial College London – and any other UK higher‑education institution with a sponsor licence – to remain in the UK after successfully completing their degree without needing a job offer, provided they hold a valid student visa at the time of application, apply within three months of receiving official confirmation that their degree has been awarded, meet the English‑language requirement and have no serious criminal record; the visa is granted for up to two years for those who have earned a bachelor’s or taught‑master’s degree and for up to three years for PhD (research) graduates, and while a 2025 government white‑paper has proposed reducing the two‑year period to 18 months, that change has not yet been enacted, so the current duration remains two years for under‑graduates and taught‑masters and three years for doctoral graduates.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: International students at Imperial College London can undertake industry‑linked placements that are formally assessed and count toward their degree classification, often termed a “year in industry” or “commercial placement,” and these can be either compulsory or optional depending on the programme; they are permitted to work up to 20 hours per week during term time and unlimited hours during holidays, and if the placement is a required part of the course no separate work permit is needed, although some employers may prefer longer‑term residency for security‑sensitive roles. Imperial’s career‑services team provides dedicated support for internships, co‑ops and graduate‑level employment, advising students to begin networking early because many large multinational graduate schemes recruit more than a year in advance. The college consistently reports one of the highest graduate‑outcome rates in the UK, with around 96 % of its graduates in 2023 either in paid employment or in further study within six months of graduation and a median starting salary of roughly £45,000, and alumni commonly enter sectors such as finance, consulting, engineering, technology, pharmaceuticals and academic research, reflecting the broad range of career paths available to both domestic and international graduates. Lương & thị trường: Imperial College London graduates enjoy strong labour‑market outcomes, with roughly 81 % of undergraduates and 90 % of postgraduate students securing employment within six months and typical starting salaries ranging from £32,000‑£44,000 for bachelor‑level degrees and £60,000‑£75,000 for master’s programmes, while about one‑third of Imperial alumni enter finance and the most common entry‑level roles include associate, business analyst, consultant, product manager, and strategy‑or‑operations positions; the broader UK graduate market similarly rewards STEM and business disciplines, with London offering the highest average starting salary of around £32,600, and sectors such as technology, engineering, data science, finance, consulting and renewable energy showing the greatest demand for talent, meaning that international graduates who secure sponsorship‑eligible offers in these high‑growth occupations can expect competitive remuneration and strong career progression prospects.
