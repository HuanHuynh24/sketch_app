**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- California Institute of Technology admits first‑year students in a single fall intake; the only early‑action option is a non‑binding Restrictive Early Action (REA) with an application deadline of November 1 (supplemental materials must be submitted by November 6 and standardized testing completed by November 30), and decisions are released in mid‑December, while the regular‑decision deadline is January 5 with decisions announced in mid‑March; the application fee is a one‑time, non‑refundable $75 (waived for applicants who demonstrate financial‑aid hardship), and all applicants—including international students—apply online through the Common Application (or QuestBridge), following the standard Caltech application process that includes academic transcripts, teacher and counselor recommendations, test scores, and essays.

Academic prerequisites include at least one year of calculus (or higher), one year of chemistry, one year of physics, and a year of biology (or comparable courses) or, if such courses are unavailable, documented exam scores or certification proving mastery; the university expects a very high GPA—typically 3.9 / 4.0 unweighted or the equivalent of a 3.6 / 4.0 minimum for competitive consideration. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- California Institute of Technology requires all international applicants to submit an English‑language proficiency test score unless the applicant’s native language is English or English was the primary language of instruction both in secondary school (and, for transfer students, also at the university level); in those cases the exam is waived. Accepted tests are the TOEFL iBT (minimum total score 100 with at least 25 on each section), IELTS (overall band 7 with a minimum of 7 in each component), the Duolingo English Test (minimum score 130, which must also be the score in each area), and for graduate applicants the Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE) is also accepted though a specific minimum is not listed. Scores must be reported before the application deadline.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Caltech is a private research university in Pasadena, California that is regionally accredited by the WASC Senior College and University Commission (WSCUC) and its engineering programs hold ABET accreditation; it consistently ranks among the world’s elite institutions, holding the #2 spot in the QS World University Rankings 2024 (and remaining in the top‑3 in subsequent editions), the #3 position in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2024, and a #6 placement among U.S. national universities in the U.S. News & World Report 2024 rankings; its graduate programs are especially distinguished, with Earth‑science subdivisions (geochemistry, geology, geophysics/seismology) ranked #1, physics sub‑fields such as elementary particle/field/string theory, cosmology/relativity/gravity and quantum physics also at #1, atomic/molecular/optical physics at #3 and condensed matter physics at #9, chemistry sub‑disciplines (organic and physical chemistry) at #2 and inorganic chemistry at #4, and engineering specialties (aerospace, chemical, mechanical and electrical/electronic/communications engineering) placed between #2 and #4, while mathematics ranks #8; internationally, roughly one‑third of Caltech’s graduate student body and about 30 % of its undergraduate enrollment are students from outside the United States, reflecting its strong international outlook.

Sau tốt nghiệp (OPT/STEM OPT): International graduates from the California Institute of Technology on an F‑1 visa are eligible for the 24‑month STEM Optional Practical Training (STEM OPT) extension after completing the standard 12‑month post‑completion OPT, provided their degree is classified under a CIP code on the Department of Education’s STEM Designated Degree Program List and they have a job offer from an employer enrolled in E‑Verify. The student must receive a formal recommendation for STEM OPT from their Designated School Official (DSO) entered into SEVIS and may file Form I‑765 up to 90 days before the current OPT EAD expires but no later than 60 days after the DSO’s recommendation. Only one STEM OPT extension may be granted per qualifying degree, and the total period of unemployment across the initial OPT and the extension cannot exceed 150 days. The STEM extension automatically begins the day after the initial OPT period ends, regardless of the adjudication date, and runs for a continuous 24 months.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm (CPT/OPT): Caltech graduates enjoy strong post‑graduation outcomes, with roughly 43 % of the 2023 undergraduate class entering graduate or professional school and about 44 % securing full‑time industry positions; the median base salary for those entering the workforce is in the $110‑119 K range and the 90th‑percentile earners make $150‑159 K. In addition, 7 % of the class reported taking a fellowship, internship or a similar opportunity immediately after graduation, while 5 % opted to take time off to explore other paths. For international graduate students who wish to undertake internships, Caltech requires Curricular Practical Training (CPT) authorization through the International Student Programs office; part‑time internships are limited to a maximum of eight hours per week while the student remains fully enrolled, and full‑time internships are typically pursued under a personal leave of absence, with the stipend cap tied to the institute’s graduate stipend guidelines. These policies ensure that international students can gain practical experience in industry, research labs, or government agencies such as JPL while remaining compliant with immigration regulations, and the strong placement record reflects Caltech’s reputation for placing graduates in high‑paying technical, engineering, and scientific careers worldwide. Lương & thị trường: Among international graduates of the California Institute of Technology, the occupations with the strongest labour‑market demand and highest median salaries reported by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics are software developers (about $120,000‑$130,000), information security analysts (around $103,000), data scientists and statisticians (roughly $95,000‑$110,000), aerospace engineers (approximately $124,000) and physicists (near $138,000); nursing also remains a fast‑growing field with a median salary of about $75,000, while mathematicians earn roughly $111,000 and occupational therapists about $86,000, reflecting the high growth of these professions nationwide and particularly in California’s technology and aerospace hubs.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Caltech’s International Student Programs (ISP) serves as the primary office for international students, providing immigration advising, mandatory pre‑term International Student Orientation, and a range of support services to help newcomers adjust to life in the United States; while ISP does not directly manage housing, it offers guidance on on‑campus and off‑campus housing options and coordinates with the university’s housing office to assist students in securing accommodation, and the university’s Center for Career Development extends career‑service resources—including resume workshops, employer networking events, and internship and job authorization counseling—to international students, ensuring they receive the same career‑planning support as domestic peers while also addressing the specific visa and work‑authorization considerations they face. Đời sống & môi trường: Caltech’s International Student Programs office provides mandatory orientation, immigration advising, and a range of social and cultural events that help international students adjust to life in the United States and build a supportive community on campus; the university supports a vibrant student life with more than 100 student‑led clubs and organizations—including cultural affinity groups such as a Vietnamese Student Association or broader Asian‑Pacific American clubs that host cultural celebrations, language‑exchange sessions, and mentorship opportunities—while the Center for Inclusion & Diversity offers additional safe spaces and workshops for identity‑based communities; unlike many larger universities, Caltech does not have Greek life, as fraternities and sororities are not part of the campus culture; safety is overseen by the Caltech Police Department, which provides 24‑hour patrols, an emergency notification system, and close coordination with the Residential Experience team for crisis intervention, residential programming, and day‑to‑day support, ensuring a secure environment for all students.
