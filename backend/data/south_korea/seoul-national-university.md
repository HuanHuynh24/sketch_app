## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Seoul National University admits international students for two main intakes each year – a Spring intake (usually beginning in March) and a Fall intake (starting in August); for the 2026 Fall cycle the online application window opened on 3 March 2026 and closed on 5 March 2026 (with recommendation letters accepted until 6 March 2026), and applicants must submit all required hard‑copy documents by the deadline indicated on the admissions guide (typically late March), while the Spring cycle follows a similar timeline shifted earlier in the year (applications generally open in early September of the preceding year and close in early November, with final document submission due shortly thereafter). The application fee for undergraduate international applicants is about USD 50 (≈ 55,000 KRW) and for graduate applicants ranges from USD 70 to USD 90 (≈ 80,000–100,000 KRW), payable online after the electronic form is completed. The required process (quy trình apply) is: visit the SNU admissions website, create a new account and log in, fill in personal and academic information, upload scanned copies of transcripts, passport, and other required files, write the personal statement and study plan, pay the application fee, print the generated summary, and finally submit the original documents by post or in person to the Office of Admissions before the stated deadline (deadline nộp hồ sơ).

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Seoul National University admits international under‑graduate applicants through two tracks: International Admission Ⅰ (both the applicant and the parents are non‑Korean) and International Admission Ⅱ (the applicant has completed all primary‑through‑high‑school education abroad). In either case you must submit a certified high‑school graduation certificate and an official transcript (học bạ) showing your cumulative GPA—SNU typically expects a GPA equivalent to at least 3.0 on a 4.0 scale (or the Korean 3.5/4.5 scale) and strong grades in the subjects required for your intended major (môn yêu cầu). Standardized test scores (TOEFL/IELTS for English‑language programmes, and Korean proficiency tests for Korean‑medium programmes) are also required, and the university may ask for additional subject‑specific exams (e.g., SAT, ACT, or Korean CSAT scores) depending on the department. All documents issued abroad must be legalized with an apostille or authenticated by the Korean embassy/consulate (or the “Certificate of Overseas Education Institutions”) and submitted with a notarized translation. **3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- Seoul National University requires international applicants to demonstrate proficiency in either Korean or English: for Korean, a TOPIK Level 3 (or TOPIK IBT equivalent) or completion of Level 4 at a Korean university language centre is sufficient; for English, the minimum accepted scores are TOEFL iBT 80, IELTS Academic 6.0, or TEPS 269 (with TOEFL MyBest scores, iBT Home Edition and IELTS online scores accepted, but TOEFL ITP and IELTS Indicator not recognized). Applicants who have completed their entire bachelor’s or master’s program in Korean or in English may submit official documentation confirming the language of instruction to obtain a waiver of the language‑proficiency test requirement; other English tests such as PTE or Duolingo are not listed among the accepted qualifications.

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): A D‑2 student visa for international students admitted to Seoul National University requires a Certificate of Admission (issued by SNU), a valid passport, a completed visa‑application form and passport‑size photos, plus proof of sufficient funds — typically a recent bank statement showing roughly USD 9,000‑10,000 (or the KRW equivalent) held for at least one month, or an official scholarship or sponsor letter confirming the same amount; these financial documents demonstrate that you can cover tuition, living expenses and health insurance while in Korea. After arriving, you must book an appointment on the HiKorea portal (studyinkorea.go.kr) to register at the Sejongno Immigration Office, where you will obtain your Alien Registration Card (ARC) and confirm your enrollment certificate; only after the ARC is issued may you apply for a part‑time work permit, which allows up to 20 hours per week of employment (subject to university approval and immigration permission) without endangering your D‑2 status. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): The D‑10 Job‑Seeker Visa permits international graduates of Korean universities—including Seoul National University—to stay in South Korea to search for employment for up to one year per issuance (a minimum of six months is typical and points‑exempt new‑graduates can accumulate a total of three years), provided they have graduated within the last year, score at least 60 points in the HiKorea point system (which includes core criteria such as TOPIK 4 or higher Korean language ability, no prior E‑visa history, and proof of sufficient funds of roughly USD 3,000‑5,000), and submit required documents like academic certificates, a resume and a job‑search plan; once a job offer is secured, the D‑10 holder can switch to an E‑7 work visa through employer sponsorship.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Seoul National University (SNU) enjoys a very strong graduate‑outcome record that is recognised internationally – it earned the QS Recognition of Employability and scored 97.7 points in the pilot Employment Outcomes evaluation, with an employer‑reputation rating of 97.8 points, reflecting a high employment rate for its graduates and strong alumni performance. For international students, SNU runs a dedicated Career Camp and an Outstanding Global Internship program that provide English‑resume coaching, mock‑interviews, and financial support for internships in global companies and organisations such as the Asian Development Bank, Inter‑American Development Bank, OECD and other MOU‑affiliated bodies; a Global Internship Connection platform also lists opportunities with multinational firms. The university’s Career Services Center offers an online career‑solutions platform, English‑resume clinic and access to job‑search databases (NACE, Vault, Interbridge) to help students secure internships, co‑ops and full‑time positions in fields ranging from business, engineering and technology to public policy and research after graduation. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries in South Korea vary by degree level, with four‑year university alumni earning an average of about 3.15 million won per month, master’s holders about 4.96 million won, and doctoral graduates roughly 6.53 million won. Employment rates differ by discipline: medical and pharmaceutical fields lead with nearly 80 % of graduates employed, followed by education (≈71 %) and engineering (≈70 %). International graduates face a lower job‑placement rate of roughly one‑third, with about 33 % securing positions after completing their studies, although this represents a notable rise from the previous year. Labour‑market demand is strongest for engineers, IT and software developers, biotechnology, finance, and advanced manufacturing, sectors where Korean firms—especially large corporations and SMEs—are actively recruiting foreign talent to address skill shortages. Seoul National University’s official graduate outcomes data confirms these trends, showing that its graduates most commonly enter the tech, finance, and biotech industries, with an average starting annual salary of about 45 million won (≈3.75 million won per month).

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Seoul National University’s Office of Global Affairs serves as the central hub for international students, providing a pre‑arrival briefing and an on‑campus orientation week that introduce academic procedures, campus life, and support services; it assists exchange students with applications for on‑campus dormitories and even offers a modest housing‑support allowance, while visiting students must secure their own accommodation. Korean language education is delivered by the Korean Language Education Center, which runs intensive courses for all international students and gives exchange students a partial tuition subsidy for these classes, whereas visiting students receive no financial aid for language study. Career development is coordinated through the International Career Center, which offers tailored workshops, one‑on‑one counseling, internship and job‑search resources, and employer networking events specifically for foreign students. For inquiries, the Office of Global Affairs can be reached at +82‑2‑880‑8634 (exchange/visiting students) or yunju96@snu.ac.kr, and the Korean Language Education Center at +82‑2‑880‑8570 or klp@snu.ac.kr. Đời sống & môi trường: Seoul National University (SNU) offers a comprehensive support system for its international student body, including a dedicated International Students Association (SISA) that serves as the official student organization representing overseas students, arranging events such as the International Students Sports Festival, Korean Culture Trip, International Food Festival and regular SISA parties while also protecting student rights, providing practical information on academics and daily life, and coordinating volunteer activities; the campus hosts a wide array of student clubs covering sports, arts, culture, volunteerism and academic interests, all accessible through the university’s online portal and the student‑life website, and safety is reinforced by a 24‑hour campus security service, emergency call stations, a medical centre, counseling services and clear procedures for reporting concerns; Vietnamese students are well‑represented through a Vietnamese Student Association that collaborates with SISA and other cultural groups to celebrate Vietnamese holidays such as Tet, offer language‑exchange meet‑ups, share housing and scholarship tips, and create a close‑knit community that helps newcomers navigate both university and life in Korea.
