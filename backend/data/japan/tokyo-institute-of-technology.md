### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Tokyo Institute of Technology nhận sinh viên quốc tế cho hai kỳ nhập học chính: kỳ xuân (bắt đầu vào tháng 4) và kỳ thu (bắt đầu vào tháng 9). Đối với kỳ thu, thời gian nộp hồ sơ trực tuyến cho các chương trình sau đại học (IGP) thường mở từ tháng 1 và kết thúc vào ngày 17 tháng 4 (23:59 JST), và sau khi nộp xong các tài liệu cần thiết (đơn đăng ký, giấy tờ học vấn, thư giới thiệu) sinh viên phải trả phí nộp hồ sơ khoảng 30 000 JPY (đối với ứng viên nước ngoài) hoặc 15 000 JPY (đối với ứng viên nội địa). Đối với kỳ xuân, thời gian nộp hồ sơ thường bắt đầu vào khoảng tháng 9 năm trước và kết thúc vào giữa tháng 11 (vào khoảng ngày 15‑30 tháng 11), với mức phí nộp hồ sơ tương tự. Quy trình nộp đơn gồm: (1) đăng ký tài khoản trên hệ thống tuyển sinh của Tokyo Tech, (2) hoàn thành mẫu đơn trực tuyến và tải lên các tài liệu hỗ trợ, (3) yêu cầu người tham chiếu và trường đại học hiện tại gửi thư và bảng điểm qua hệ thống, (4) thanh toán phí nộp hồ sơ, (5) chờ thông báo kết quả (thường vào tháng 6 cho kỳ thu và tháng 2‑3 cho kỳ xuân), (6) nếu được nhận, thanh toán phí nhập học và học phí, (7) nhận giấy chứng nhận đủ điều kiện (COE) từ trường, (8) xin visa du học tại lãnh sự quán Nhật Bản và chuẩn bị cho ngày nhập học.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Tokyo Institute of Technology requires international applicants to submit official academic transcripts (in English, with grading‐scale explanations) covering at least the last two years of study, a copy of their passport, and proof of payment of the application fee; for undergraduate admission, candidates must take the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) – typically needing scores of around 115 points in each subject (Japanese, Mathematics, and Science) and a total of roughly 400 points – or pass the Common Test for University Admissions together with Tokyo Tech’s own General Entrance Examination, while privately funded students may also apply through the Special Screening Examination; graduate applicants must provide official transcripts, a copy of their degree certificate, a GPA transcript (generally a minimum of 2.0/4.0 or equivalent), a research plan or portfolio where required, and may be asked to attend an interview or submit additional materials such as recommendation letters, English proficiency test scores (TOEFL/IELTS/TOEIC) if they are not native English speakers, and, for design‑related programs, a portfolio; overall, meeting the minimum EJU or Common Test scores, having a satisfactory GPA, and supplying authenticated transcripts are the core prerequisites for admission.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [[PDF] Application Guide for International Graduate Program (B)](https://www.titech.ac.jp/english/admissions/pdf/application-guide-igp-b-riken-202209re.pdf) (Cao, score 0.61)<br>[International Visiting Students \| Non-degree Programs \| Prospective Students \| Science Tokyo formerly Tokyo Tech](https://www.titech.ac.jp/english/international-student-exchange/prospective-students/non-degree/visiting) (Cao, score 0.54)<br>[Explore Bachelor's Degree Programs - 東京工業大学](https://www.titech.ac.jp/english/admissions/prospective-students/explore-undergraduate) (Cao, score 0.51)

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Tokyo Institute of Technology requires proof of English proficiency for all international applicants unless they have earned a degree in which the language of instruction was English or have been granted a formal exemption by a department chair; acceptable tests and minimum scores are TOEFL iBT ≥ 80 (including Home Edition), TOEFL ITP (as an internal Tokyo Tech test), IELTS Academic ≥ 6.0 (overall band), PTE Academic ≥ 50, and the Duolingo English Test ≥ 105, while TOEIC L & R scores are accepted only for internal exchange‑program tuition waivers; applicants who hold a Japanese‑language qualification such as JLPT N1 may also be considered for a waiver, and Japanese‑national students are exempt from any English‑language test requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- The Tokyo Institute of Technology charges international undergraduate students an entrance‑examination (application) fee of JPY 17,000, an enrollment fee of JPY 282,000 and tuition of JPY 635,400 per year, for a combined first‑year cost of JPY 934,400; international graduate (master’s or doctoral) students pay an entrance‑examination fee of JPY 30,000, the same enrollment fee of JPY 282,000 and tuition of JPY 635,400 per year, totaling JPY 947,400 for the first year, while privately‑funded international research students have a lower fee structure of JPY 9,800 (application), JPY 84,600 (enrollment) and tuition of JPY 356,400 per year.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Tokyo Institute of Technology can expect to spend roughly ¥1.4 million per year on living expenses, broken down as follows: accommodation in a studio‑type apartment near the Ookayama campus averages about ¥70,000 per month (≈¥840,000 per year); food costs are typically around ¥35,000 per month for groceries and occasional meals out (≈¥420,000 per year); public transportation and commuting amount to about ¥10,000 per month (≈¥120,000 per year); and enrollment in the National Health Insurance scheme adds roughly ¥20,000 per year, bringing the total annual cost to approximately ¥1,400,000.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Tokyo Institute of Technology requires international exchange students to enroll in graduate‑level courses (400‑level or higher) that are listed on the institute’s official course list, with each course typically carrying 2–3 credit units and being offered in one of the four quarterly terms (1Q–4Q); prerequisites are specified by the instructor and may include foundational knowledge such as basic quantum‑mechanics and electronic properties of solids for semiconductor‑physics courses, a solid understanding of signal‑and‑system fundamentals for wireless‑communication courses, and completion of the undergraduate‑level power‑electronics curriculum for power‑electronics courses, while eligibility also demands that students be final‑year undergraduates or at an equivalent level and be enrolled in an approved exchange program; the curriculum is organized under six schools (Science, Engineering, Materials and Chemical Technology, Computing, Life Science and Technology, Environment and Society), each offering a two‑year master’s, a three‑year doctoral, and a professional master’s program, and the learning outcomes for each course are explicitly stated in the syllabus, outlining the expected mastery of subject‑specific theory, analytical techniques, and practical applications relevant to the discipline.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Tokyo Institute of Technology (Tokyo Tech) enrolls roughly 1,700 international students from about 78 countries, making it one of Japan’s most globally diverse research universities; in the QS World University Rankings it is placed 58th worldwide (3rd in Japan) and in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings it ranks 32nd globally (2nd in Japan), while its individual subject standings are consistently strong—engineering programmes appear in the top 30 worldwide (QS Engineering ≈ 22nd, THE Engineering ≈ 25th), computer science and information technology rank within the top 40 (QS Computer Science ≈ 36th, THE Computer Science ≈ 38th), and natural sciences such as physics and chemistry sit in the top 50; the university’s overall quality is officially recognised by Japan’s Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT) and it holds institutional accreditation from the Japan University Accreditation Association (JUA) together with program‑level accreditation for its engineering curricula from the Japan Accreditation Board for Engineering Education (JABEE) and, for several internationally oriented programmes, ABET accreditation.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [[PDF] national university corporation tokyo institute of technology](https://www.titech.ac.jp/english/public-relations/pdf/110-profile-0809e.pdf) (Cao, score 0.64)<br>[[PDF] Leading the world in Science and Technology - 東京工業大学](https://www.titech.ac.jp/english/public-relations/pdf/110-profile-0910e.pdf) (Cao, score 0.58)<br>[[PDF] STUDY AT TOKYO TECH](https://www.titech.ac.jp/english/admissions/pdf/study-at-tokyotech.pdf) (Cao, score 0.57)

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: To study at the Tokyo Institute of Technology you must first obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) issued by the school’s international office, which serves as the school’s proxy in applying to the regional immigration bureau; the COE confirms that you have been admitted, that you meet the academic and financial criteria, and it is required before you can apply for a student visa at a Japanese embassy or consulate. When the COE is issued, the visa application must include proof that you can cover tuition and living expenses for the entire period of study—typically demonstrated by a bank balance or guarantor’s income statement showing roughly ¥1 million‑¥1.5 million per year (the exact figure is set by the university and reflected in the COE sponsor letter). Once you enter Japan with the “Student” residence status, you may engage in part‑time work up to 28 hours per week (or 40 hours during university holidays) provided you obtain a “Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted under the Status of Residence Previously Granted” from the immigration office; this permission is free and must be applied for after arrival, and any earnings may be used to supplement your living costs but cannot replace the required proof of funds shown for the COE. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of the Tokyo Institute of Technology (or any Japanese university, junior college, college of technology, or approved vocational school) who hold a student visa may change their status to “Designated Activities (Continuing Job Hunting)” to remain in Japan after graduation; eligibility requires a recommendation letter from the school, proof of active job‑search activities (commonly demonstrated by having applied to at least six companies while still enrolled), a graduation certificate, and sufficient financial resources to support living expenses, and the applicant must apply before the student visa expires; the visa is normally issued for an initial six‑month period and can be renewed once for an additional six months, allowing a maximum stay of one year for job hunting, as outlined on the Immigration Services Agency’s official site (studyinjapan.go.jp).

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: International students at Tokyo Institute of Technology enjoy strong graduate outcomes, with about 80 % of master’s graduates securing employment (most in private firms, followed by universities, research institutes and public service), roughly 15 % continuing to doctoral programs, and around 90 % of undergraduates advancing to graduate school; the university supports internships, co‑op placements and job hunting through regular career‑support events—including an annual seminar for international students, internship guidance sessions and a global career fair—that link students with Japanese and foreign‑affiliated companies across engineering, IT, life sciences, materials and consulting sectors, resulting in a high overall employment rate for international graduates both in Japan and abroad. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Tokyo Institute of Technology typically enter the private‑sector, with roughly 154 of the most recent cohort joining private firms (about 118 in Japanese companies and 36 in foreign‑affiliated firms) and a smaller share moving into universities, research institutes, or government, while the overall demand in Japan’s labour market remains strongest for engineering and IT roles—positions that command the highest salaries, with new university graduates earning on average ¥281,000 per month (about ¥3.4 million annually) and IT engineers in Tokyo receiving roughly ¥6.2 million a year, reflecting a broader trend that foreign‑trained talent in technology fields commands a premium of around 15 % over domestic peers and is actively recruited by major Japanese and multinational firms such as Micron, IBM, Accenture, Bosch, Huawei, Amazon, Google, and many others, concentrated largely in the Tokyo metropolitan area.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Tokyo Institute of Technology’s International Student Office offers a comprehensive support system for overseas students that includes an International Student Orientation program covering visa procedures, campus navigation and essential living information, as well as dedicated tutors who assist with academic and daily‑life adjustments; the office also provides housing support by helping students apply for university dormitories, offering guidance on off‑campus rentals and information on living costs in Japan, and it operates a Career Support Service that delivers job‑search counseling, internship and recruitment fair coordination, résumé workshops and employer networking events to aid students in transitioning from study to professional life. Đời sống & môi trường: Tokyo Institute of Technology offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with more than 200 circles and clubs ranging from sports such as tennis, aikido and sepak takraw to cultural groups like the Orchestra, Guitar Society, Photography Club and International Development Circle, and dedicated time on Wednesday afternoons for club activities; the Tokyo Tech International Students Association (TISA) runs orientation sessions, an International Welcome Party each quarter, and a peer‑support system that helps newcomers navigate language barriers, housing, health services and safety procedures, while academic advisors and staff provide ongoing assistance, English‑language classes, and improved residential facilities; Vietnamese students typically connect through TISA, informal Vietnamese‑language meet‑ups and social media groups, and they can also join any club of interest, benefiting from the university’s campus security presence, emergency call stations, 24‑hour dormitory staff and clear safety guidelines that make the campus widely regarded as safe for all residents.
