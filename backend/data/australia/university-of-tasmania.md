### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- The University of Tasmania does not charge an application fee for international applicants and, for most programmes, there is no fixed closing date, although overseas students are advised to submit their applications at least five months before the start of the semester and domestic‑or‑on‑shore applicants no later than one month prior; the university runs two main intakes each year – Semester 1 beginning in February and Semester 2 beginning in July – and the application process for undergraduate and postgraduate coursework follows six steps: (1) choose a course, (2) check your eligibility, (3) prepare the required documents, (4) verify your finances, (5) lodge the application through the online portal, and (6) apply for any scholarships you wish to be considered for.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students wishing to study at the University of Tasmania must satisfy both English‑language and academic entry requirements: a minimum overall IELTS score of 6.5 (with at least 6.0 in each band) or an equivalent test result, and academic qualifications equivalent to Australian Year 12, which are assessed using the university’s ATAR/International Equivalency tables; for most undergraduate courses the minimum ATAR is around 65‑70 % (or its overseas equivalent), while postgraduate coursework programmes generally require a completed bachelor’s degree with a minimum GPA of 2.0 (C) – higher GPAs (often 2.5–3.0) are needed for competitive or research‑intensive programmes; subject‑specific prerequisites apply (e.g., mathematics for engineering, biology or chemistry for health sciences, and relevant prior study for business, law or education), and applicants must provide certified transcripts of their high‑school (học bạ) and, where applicable, tertiary results; certain creative and design courses additionally require a portfolio of work and may involve an interview as part of the selection process, whereas most other programmes do not require a portfolio or interview, and students who do not meet the direct entry criteria can consider enrolling in the International Pathway College to meet the required standards.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Admission requirements \| University of Tasmania](https://www.utas.edu.au/study/apply/admission-requirements) (Cao, score 0.79)<br>[Entry requirements by country](https://www.utas.edu.au/study/apply/admission-requirements/entry-requirements-by-country) (Cao, score 0.67)<br>[Apply for uni as an international student](https://www.utas.edu.au/study/apply/international-students) (Cao, score 0.58)<br>[International students](https://www.utas.edu.au/study/international) (Cao, score 0.52)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Tasmania must meet a course‑specific English language requirement, which is most commonly satisfied by achieving an overall IELTS score of 6.5 (with no individual band below 6.0), a TOEFL iBT total of at least 79, a PTE Academic score of 58, or a Duolingo English Test result of 115; higher minimums may apply for certain programmes such as health or law. Applicants who have completed a recent (usually within the past two years) qualification that was taught entirely in English—such as a bachelor’s degree from an institution where English is the language of instruction, or a secondary school qualification meeting the university’s English-medium criteria—can be granted a waiver of the English test requirement, and those who enrol in the university’s English language programme can also satisfy the entry standard.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at the University of Tasmania pay an annual tuition fee that varies by programme – most undergraduate courses are priced between AUD 30,000 and AUD 38,000 per year, while many postgraduate courses range from AUD 31,000 up to about AUD 42,000 annually; the university does not charge an application fee, and the primary additional compulsory costs are the Student Services and Amenities Fee (approximately AUD 300 per semester, or about AUD 600 per year) and Overseas Student Health Cover (typically AUD 600‑ AUD 700 per year), bringing the total yearly expense to roughly AUD 30,600‑ AUD 38,600 for most undergraduates and up to AUD 42,600‑ AUD 43,300 for certain postgraduate programmes.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students wishing to study at the University of Tasmania must meet the same entry requirements as domestic undergraduates, which typically involve completing qualifications equivalent to Australian Year 12 and satisfying any subject‑specific prerequisites listed for their chosen degree; those who do not meet these standards can enrol through the International Pathway College to achieve the necessary foundation. Courses are structured around a credit‑point system where a full‑time semester normally carries 100 credit points (approximately 600 points per year), and programmes are composed of core units, elective options and, where applicable, a final capstone or research project, with each unit delivering clearly defined learning outcomes that map to the university’s graduate attributes such as critical thinking, communication, ethical reasoning and professional practice; credit for prior learning or advanced standing may be awarded after assessment, allowing eligible students to receive recognition for relevant previous study and reduce the number of units they need to complete.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Tasmania (UTAS) is a publicly funded Australian university that in the QS World University Rankings 2024 is placed in the 351‑400 band worldwide, while the Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2024 positions it in the 401‑500 range; in QS subject rankings the university appears at 151‑200 for Environmental Sciences, 251‑300 for Education, 301‑350 for Business & Management and 351‑400 for Medicine, and in THE subject rankings it is within the top 200 for Earth and Marine Sciences and around 250‑300 for Social Sciences, and it is fully accredited by the national Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) with additional professional accreditations such as ANMAC for nursing, Engineers Australia for engineering and the Law Council of Australia for law, while roughly 4,000 international students are enrolled across its undergraduate, postgraduate and research programmes offered on‑campus and online.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa requires that each applicant demonstrate genuine access to sufficient funds to cover at least 12 months of living costs (currently AUD 29,710 for the primary student, AUD 10,394 for an accompanying partner and AUD 4,449 for each child, plus a minimum school‑fee amount of AUD 13,502 per child if applicable) together with tuition fees, travel to Australia and any required schooling costs, and these funds must be evidenced by recent bank statements, loan documents or scholarship letters showing a three‑month transaction history; all visa holders and their dependents must maintain continuous Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) from an approved provider starting on the day they arrive in Australia and remaining in force for the entire stay, with no gaps between any previous health cover and the OSHC, and the visa permits the student to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while classes are in session and unlimited hours during recognised school holidays, provided the enrolment remains full‑time at a registered institution such as the University of Tasmania, which is an approved education provider listed on the Department of Home Affairs and Study in Australia websites. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of Tasmania can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) in the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which normally provides a stay of two to three years with unrestricted work rights, and allows eligible family members to be included; because Tasmania is classified as a regional area, graduates who have studied, lived and worked there receive an additional year of post‑study work rights, and if their qualification appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List they are entitled to a further two‑year extension, meaning many can remain in Australia for up to four years after graduation while working full‑time in any sector.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Tasmania provides extensive work‑integrated learning options—including internships, professional placements and industry‑focused projects—that are built into many courses to help students become job‑ready, and its international student community benefits from these opportunities as well as from up to four years of post‑study work rights on a Temporary Graduate visa; the university consistently ranks #1 in Australia for postgraduate international student satisfaction and its graduate outcomes data show that employment rates for UTAS alumni are above the national average, with graduates entering a wide range of professional fields and reporting strong career prospects after completing their studies. Lương & thị trường: In Australia the occupations showing the strongest growth and highest demand for graduates—including international graduates from the University of Tasmania—are construction management roles, software and applications programmers, education and training managers (such as primary school teachers), and a broad range of professional, scientific and technical positions, with employers reporting persistent skill shortages across both professional‑managerial and technician‑trade categories; full‑time median earnings for a bachelor‑level graduate are about $75,000 per year, rising to roughly $96,600–$100,000 for postgraduate coursework graduates and $100,000–$104,400 for postgraduate research graduates, with the highest discipline‑specific medians in dentistry ($94,400), medicine ($85,000), social work ($77,300) and teacher education ($75,000), while Tasmania’s Skilled Graduate Pathways award points for graduates earning at least $57,000, $68,863, $76,515 or $102,956 annually, reflecting local market salary thresholds used by Jobs and Skills Australia.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Tasmania’s International Student Advisers (available via ISA@utas.edu.au) run the International Student Office, which provides a comprehensive support package for overseas students that includes a detailed International Student Guide, personalised assistance with adjusting to life in Australia, help navigating university systems, and direct support for orientation activities such as the “Orientation and settling in” program; the university also offers dedicated accommodation services across its Hobart, Launceston and Cradle Coast campuses to assist international students in finding on‑campus or nearby housing, and its Support and Wellbeing division includes career guidance and counselling to help students develop job‑search skills, access career workshops and connect with the university’s broader career services. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Tasmania offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with a wide range of clubs and societies—including cultural, sporting, and hobby‑based groups—accessible through its “Clubs and societies” hub, and it runs dedicated programmes such as Leadership and Volunteering and a robust Counselling and Wellbeing service to support student safety and mental health; for Vietnamese students there is a specific “supportive Vietnamese community” that provides networking events, peer mentorship, and cultural activities, helping newcomers feel welcomed and connected while the university’s campus security, safety and wellbeing teams maintain a safe environment across Hobart, Launceston, Cradle Coast and the Sydney satellite campus.
