### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Queen’s University admits international students through several intakes, the primary undergraduate winter (January) entry opening for first‑year full‑time study on campus, with applications available online from June 1 and a CAD 50 application fee per program; the winter‑entry window closes on September 30, after which supporting documents must be uploaded by October 15 and the residence‑deposit deadline is November 27 (4 PM ET), with classes beginning January 6, 2027, while graduate programs such as the Master of Earth and Energy Resources Leadership (MEERL) start each August and require online submission of an application, fee payment (typically CAD 50), official transcripts, and two referee contacts, with two deadlines for the 2026 cohort—an early February 28 deadline (recommended for international applicants needing study permits) and a final May 31 deadline; all applicants must create a separate application for each degree, pay the fee, upload transcripts, provide references, and, for certain programmes like Health Sciences, complete a supplementary application as part of the admission process.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Queen’s University admits international under‑graduates who have completed Grade 12 (or equivalent) with at least a B‑ (≈ 2.7 GPA) in four senior‑level courses, and requires an official high‑school transcript (học bạ) plus any national graduation exam results – for example Chinese applicants must submit verified Gao Kao or Hui Kao/Xuekao scores, British‑system students need three A‑Level subjects (minimum grade B) and two GCSE/IGCSE subjects (minimum C/4), and IB candidates must present the full diploma with a comparable overall score; standardized tests such as the SAT or ACT are optional but may be submitted, and proof of English proficiency (TOEFL, IELTS, or an exemption if the applicant has completed a full year of post‑secondary study in English) is mandatory; most programmes do not require a portfolio or interview, although select faculties such as Fine Arts, Architecture, Music, and some design‑related majors may ask for a portfolio and/or an interview as part of the assessment.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Queen’s University requires all international applicants whose first language is not English to demonstrate proficiency by achieving at least a 6.5 overall on the Academic IELTS (with no band below 6.0), an overall iBT TOEFL score of 88 (with a minimum of 20 in each of Writing, Speaking, Reading and Listening), a PTE Academic overall score of 60, or a Duolingo English Test overall score of 120; applicants are exempt from submitting any test if they have completed the full International Baccalaureate diploma (SL English B alone is not sufficient), have lived in a country where English is the recognized primary language for the three years immediately before beginning their university studies, have attended a full‑time institution where English was the medium of instruction for the three years immediately prior, or, for graduate‑level candidates, have completed at least one full year of post‑secondary study in an English‑language institution within the 12 months before applying.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students admitted to Queen’s University must have completed a secondary‑school diploma with specific prerequisite subjects that vary by program—for example, engineering programs require English 4U, Calculus and Vectors 4U, Chemistry 4U, Physics 4U and Advanced Functions 4U, while health‑science and life‑science programs typically require English, Mathematics and a combination of Biology, Chemistry and/or Physics at the senior‑level; French‑language programs may accept two French courses in place of English. All courses are measured in credit units, most being 3.0 units for a single‑term offering and 6.0 units for a full‑year (Fall‑Winter) course, with an honours degree demanding a total of 120.0 units; a full‑time student normally carries five courses per term (30.0 units per academic year). The curriculum is structured around first‑year registration guides that list required and elective courses (e.g., MATH 121 Differential and Integral Calculus) and progresses into upper‑level courses that build on these foundations. Across faculties the stated learning outcomes include applying disciplinary knowledge to solve problems, performing quantitative and qualitative analysis, communicating ideas effectively in written and oral forms, demonstrating ethical reasoning and collaborative teamwork, and cultivating lifelong learning and critical‑thinking skills.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Queen’s University in Kingston, Ontario is currently ranked 191st in the 2026 QS World University Rankings (up two places from the previous year) and 9th among Canadian institutions, with an international‑student ratio of 16 percent contributing a QS score of 26.6 in that metric; in the Times Higher Education Impact Rankings it placed 6th globally and 1st in Canada in 2025, while its overall position in the THE World University Rankings 2024–25 falls within the 151‑200 band; subject‑specific QS rankings list Queen’s among the world’s top‑50 for Accounting & Finance (≈ 40th), Business & Management (≈ 46th), and Engineering (≈ 70th) and the university’s programs are formally accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Colleges and Universities, the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board for its engineering degrees, the AACSB for the Smith School of Business, and the Law Society of Ontario for its Faculty of Law, with all degrees also recognised by Universities Canada and the Council of Ontario Universities.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students admitted to Queen’s University must include a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) with their study‑permit application; the PAL is issued after the offer is accepted and is available in the SOLUS Student Centre, and it is required for virtually all new undergraduate students unless they qualify for an exemption, because it confirms the student is counted against the province’s allocation within the national cap. To obtain a study permit, applicants must provide proof of financial support that covers tuition, transportation and living expenses for themselves and any accompanying family members, and for the 2024‑2025 period the government‑mandated minimum living‑expense amounts (excluding tuition and transport) are $22,895 for a single applicant, $28,502 for two persons, $35,040 for three, $42,543 for four, $48,252 for five, $54,420 for six, $60,589 for seven, with each additional family member requiring $6,170. In addition, students may work off‑campus up to 20 hours per week while classes are in session and full‑time during scheduled breaks, provided their study program is at least six months long; on‑campus employment is permitted without a work permit, and those in co‑op or internship programs must apply for a separate co‑op or intern work permit. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Queen’s University are eligible for a Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they completed a program at a designated learning institution that was at least eight months long (or 900 hours for Quebec credentials), maintained full‑time study status in Canada throughout (with part‑time allowed only in the final semester), and apply within 180 days of receiving official confirmation of program completion while their study permit was still valid. The length of the PGWP depends on the duration of the study program: if the program was between eight months and less than two years, the work permit may be issued for up to the same number of months; if the program was two years or longer, the PGWP is generally issued for three years. Additionally, as of 15 February 2024, master’s degree graduates can receive a three‑year PGWP even when the degree program is less than two years, provided it meets the eight‑month minimum and all other eligibility criteria, and the permit cannot exceed the expiry date of the holder’s passport.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

Lương & thị trường: International‑trained graduates entering the Canadian labour market are most likely to find work in high‑growth, high‑pay sectors such as information technology and data analytics, with 21 % of recent international student graduates employed as web designers or developers and about 12 % in roles like mathematicians, statisticians, actuaries and data scientists, occupations that the Job Bank lists among the top‑demand fields with median early‑career salaries ranging from CAD 70 000 to CAD 90 000. Health‑related occupations and natural‑and‑applied‑science jobs also show strong demand, while international graduates are less represented in education, law and government services and are over‑represented in sales and service roles, which tend to offer lower earnings (often under CAD 45 000). Queen’s University reports very high placement rates for its graduates—over 95 % of law graduates secure positions within months of graduation—and its alumni in business, engineering and science programmes typically earn starting salaries comparable to national averages (roughly CAD 55 000–CAD 75 000), with those entering the tech and data sectors achieving the higher end of that range. **4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**
