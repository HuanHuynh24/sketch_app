## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students can apply to the University of Calgary for the fall intake each year; the application window opens on August 15 for those with non‑Canadian credentials and closes on March 1 of the following year (e.g., August 15 2025 to March 1 2026). The required application fee for applicants with international education is $145 (Canadian‑credential applicants pay $125) and the university does not normally offer fee waivers except in rare, fully‑funded cases. To apply, prospective students first create a UCalgary eID, then start and complete the online application using the university’s application guide; after the application is submitted they must pay the fee (credit card is the preferred method) and later upload any required documents as instructed in their Student Centre, after which the admissions team will review the file.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [October 2023 FAQ Blog](https://discover.ucalgary.ca/portal/oct23_faq) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[How to Apply 2026](https://discover.ucalgary.ca/portal/howtoapply_2026) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[How to apply \| Future Students - University of Calgary](https://www.ucalgary.ca/future-students/undergraduate/admissions/how-to-apply) (Cao, score 1.00)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to the University of Calgary must submit official high‑school (or equivalent) transcripts with notarised English translations, a completed high‑school diploma (or the equivalent credential recognized for the country of origin), and meet the university’s minimum grade average – typically a 70 % overall mark (approximately a 2.5 / 4.0 GPA) for most undergraduate programmes, with more competitive faculties such as Engineering, Business and Health Sciences requiring around 80 % (≈3.0 / 4.0 GPA) and a minimum of five graded subjects; transfer applicants to the 2+2/3+2 articulation routes must have a cumulative GPA of 3.0 / 4.0 (or 80 %) from their most recent 30 credit hours and may need additional prerequisite courses; all applicants must provide proof of English proficiency (minimum TOEFL iBT 86 or IELTS 6.5, unless a degree was completed in English) and may be asked to sit additional examinations if the admissions office doubts equivalency; certain professional and creative programmes (e.g., Architecture, Fine Arts, Design) also require a portfolio and/or an interview as part of the assessment, while most other programmes rely solely on academic records and language scores.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to the University of Calgary must demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least a 6.5 overall on the Academic IELTS, a minimum total score of 80 on the internet‑based TOEFL iBT (or 213 on the CBT or 550 on the PBT), a score of 60 on the Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE), or a Duolingo English Test score of 110 – 120 (the university lists 120 as the current threshold). Higher scores are required for Nursing and Education programs. Applicants are exempt from testing if they have completed three years of secondary education in an English‑language‑exempt country or at an accredited high school where English is the primary language of instruction, or two years of post‑secondary study in Canada or another exempt country; completion of the University’s Academic Communication Certificate or certain English courses with a minimum grade (e.g., B+ in designated ESL or ACC courses) also satisfies the requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- University of Calgary charges international undergraduate students roughly CAD $22,500 in tuition per academic year (≈ $45,000 for a full‑year of two terms), which brings the total cost of a typical four‑year bachelor’s programme to about CAD $90,000 in tuition alone; on top of tuition, students pay an annual application‑type fee of CAD $120 (the non‑refundable fee required when the admission letter is issued) plus mandatory campus‑wide charges such as the student‑services fee (about CAD $950 per year) and health‑insurance/UBC‑UCalgary health plan (approximately CAD $200 per year), while Continuing‑Education international programmes list tuition at CAD $7,950 per term (four terms = CAD $27,950) with a CAD $200 application fee and an additional CAD $3,900 Work‑Integrated‑Learning fee.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Tuition and Fees \| International Student Services \| University of Calgary](https://ucalgary.ca/student-services/iss/settling-calgary/finances/tuition-and-fees) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Tuition and Fees \| Office of the Registrar \| University of Calgary](https://www.ucalgary.ca/registrar/finances/tuition-and-fees) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Fees and Payment Deadlines \| International Students \| University of Calgary Continuing Education](https://conted.ucalgary.ca/international/info/fees.jsp) (Cao, score 1.00)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at the University of Calgary can expect to spend roughly CAD 17,200 per academic year for on‑campus housing plus a full‑board (7‑day) meal plan in a single‑room suite, with a slightly lower option of CAD 16,250 for a 5‑day plan; off‑campus or shared‑room options range from about CAD 4,300 for a double room without meals up to CAD 14,100 for a single suite with a full‑board plan. A UPass for unlimited public‑transit service is bundled into the fee structure for most international programs and typically costs around CAD 300 annually, while mandatory health coverage (GuardMe emergency medical insurance) is roughly CAD 350 per year. Adding these elements together, the total yearly cost of living, food, transport and health insurance for an international undergraduate student falls between CAD 20 000 and CAD 22 000, depending on the chosen accommodation and meal‑plan level.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at the University of Calgary enroll in courses that are each worth 3 credit units, with exchange and other undergraduate international programs requiring a minimum of three courses (9 units) and allowing up to five courses (15 units) per semester; admission prerequisites vary by faculty but commonly include English language proficiency, a foundation in mathematics (pre‑calculus or college algebra), and one or two approved science or humanities courses such as Chemistry, Biology, Physics, Economics, History, or a language other than English, and each program’s curriculum is organized into a sequence of required and elective courses that together articulate specific learning outcomes—ranging from critical thinking, quantitative reasoning, and scientific literacy to discipline‑specific competencies and communication skills—ensuring that students achieve the knowledge, skills, and abilities outlined in the university’s program‐level outcome statements.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Calgary is ranked 211th in the world in the QS World University Rankings 2025‑2026 and 200th globally (9th in Canada) in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2026, while it was placed #1 in Canada and 25th worldwide in the THE Young University Rankings 2019; its Haskayne School of Business’s Executive MBA program is listed among the top‑20 joint EMBA programmes worldwide by QS and the school holds AACSB accreditation, and in QS subject rankings the university typically appears in the 151‑200 band for Engineering & Technology and the 201‑250 band for Natural Sciences, reflecting solid performance across key disciplines, and the campus hosts a sizable international community of roughly 7,000‑8,000 students out of a total enrolment of over 38,000.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students admitted to the University of Calgary must include a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) with their study‑permit application to prove that their admission has been allocated under the provincial or territorial cap, and IRCC will return any application that lacks a PAL unless the applicant is exempt; the PAL is not required when extending a permit for the same program, but a new PAL is needed if the level of study changes, such as moving from an undergraduate to a graduate program. To obtain the study permit, applicants must also demonstrate proof of financial support showing they have sufficient funds—without relying on Canadian employment—to cover tuition, living expenses for themselves (and any accompanying family members), and transportation to and from Canada, as required by IRCC. Once the permit is issued, students are allowed to work off‑campus up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled breaks, provided the work condition is included on their permit. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of the University of Calgary may obtain an Open Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they completed a full‑time program that lasted at least eight months, have a valid study permit and passport, and provide official proof of completion such as a Confirmation of Credential Letter and final transcripts; they must submit the application online within 180 days of receiving their final grades (or within 90 days if they are already in Canada and before their study permit expires) and may only receive a PGWP once in their lifetime. The permit’s validity matches the official length of the study program, up to a maximum of three years: if the program was less than two years but longer than eight months, the work permit is issued for a period equal to the program’s duration, whereas programs of two years or more (including master’s programmes of at least eight months approved after 15 Feb 2024) qualify for a three‑year permit. Applications cost CAD $255, must be filed through an IRCC secure account, and after 21 June 2024 cannot be submitted at a border entry point. Graduates of certificate or diploma programs submitted after 1 Nov 2024 must also be in an eligible field of study to qualify.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Calgary offers extensive work‑integrated learning for international students, including paid co‑op, internship and practicum placements across undergraduate and graduate programs, and aims for every student to complete at least one to two experiential learning experiences by 2025; these opportunities are coordinated through the Centre for Career & Personal Development and faculty‑specific offices, and international students are eligible to work in Canada during their studies, providing employers with culturally diverse talent, while the university reports a strong graduate outcome with a 94.1 % employment rate for recent graduates and two‑thirds of its 191,000 alumni living or working in the Calgary region, reflecting robust career prospects for those who complete co‑op or internship experiences. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Canada generally achieve employment outcomes comparable to Canadian‑trained peers, though they are less represented in health‑related, education, law, social and government service occupations and are more concentrated in fields such as information technology and quantitative analysis; the most in‑demand roles for recent graduates in the Calgary labour market include web designers and developers (about one‑fifth of hires), followed by mathematicians, statisticians, actuaries and data scientists (roughly one‑tenth), with median annual salaries reported by Job Bank ranging from around CAD 70 000 for entry‑level web development positions to approximately CAD 95 000 for data‑science and analytics roles, while health‑sector graduates typically command higher wages of CAD 80 000‑CAD 100 000 depending on specialization.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Calgary’s International Student Services (ISS) serves as the central office for international students, offering a comprehensive orientation program each fall and winter that includes information sessions, drop‑in events and a short‑run Welcome Centre transition program to help newcomers adjust to campus and city life; ISS also provides extensive housing support, advising students on on‑ and off‑campus options, roommate matching and navigating Calgary’s rental market, while the university’s Career Services department—accessible to all students including internationals—offers tailored career counseling, résumé and interview workshops, co‑op and internship placement assistance, and guidance on work‑permit and post‑graduation employment requirements. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Calgary welcomes students from more than 140 countries and supports a vibrant campus life through its International Student Services (ISS), which offers pre‑arrival guidance, visa assistance, mentorship programs, workshops, and a wide range of social and cultural events that help newcomers settle into Calgary; the Students’ Union runs dozens of clubs and societies—including a Vietnamese Student Association that organizes gatherings, cultural celebrations, language‑exchange evenings, and networking opportunities for Vietnamese students and anyone interested in Vietnamese culture—while campus safety is ensured by the UCalgary Safety & Security team that provides 24‑hour patrols, emergency call stations, a SafeRide service for night‑time travel, regular safety workshops, and close coordination with the Calgary Police Service, all of which together create a welcoming, secure environment for international students to study, socialize, and thrive.
