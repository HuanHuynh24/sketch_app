**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- University of Technology Sydney (UTS) accepts international undergraduate and postgraduate applications for three main intakes each year: the Autumn session (February/March start) with a deadline of 30 November for applicants based outside Australia, the Spring session (July start) with a deadline of 30 April, and the Summer session (November start) with a deadline of 31 August; the application fee is A$100 (waivable under certain conditions) and the typical process involves creating an account on the UTS international application portal, completing the online application form, uploading certified copies of all required documents, paying the fee (by credit‑card, bank draft or cheque), submitting the application either online, by mail or in person at the UTS International Student Centre, awaiting a decision (usually within one to two weeks for complete coursework applications), and finally accepting the offer and completing enrolment steps such as paying tuition and obtaining a Confirmation of Enrolment for a student visa.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- U Technology Sydney requires all international applicants to meet an English‑language benchmark, with the standard minimums being IELTS 7.0 overall (with at least 6.5 in writing), TOEFL iBT 94 overall (with minimum component scores of 23‑24 in speaking, listening, reading and writing), or PTE Academic 66 overall (with at least 66 in speaking, listening and reading and 56 in writing); higher thresholds apply for some programmes (e.g., IELTS 7.5 overall, TOEFL 102‑109, PTE 73‑78). Applicants may be exempt from testing if they have completed a recent degree or substantial study in English at an Australian university or another institution whose qualification is recognised as meeting the UTS English‑language standard; otherwise a valid IELTS, TOEFL iBT or PTE Academic result is required, as Duolingo English Test is not listed among the accepted assessments.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at the University of Technology Sydney the typical annual budget for living expenses is between A$29,710 and A$37,037. Accommodation in shared student housing near campus runs about A$370‑A$550 per week, which translates to roughly A$19,200‑A$28,600 per year (plus a one‑off start‑up cost of around A$5,200 for furniture and utilities). Food and groceries average about A$200‑A$300 per month (≈ A$2,400‑A$3,600 per year), with a typical restaurant meal costing A$35+, a takeaway A$16+, and a coffee A$5+. Public transport (bus, train and ferry) is usually budgeted at around A$150 per month, or about A$1,800 annually, especially if a Opal card concession is used. Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for a full year costs roughly A$600‑A$750. Adding these components gives a total living cost of approximately A$24,000‑A$35,000 per year, which fits comfortably within the university’s recommended range of A$29,710‑A$37,037 for a modest but realistic student lifestyle.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa is the official pathway for international students to study full‑time at a CRICOS‑registered institution such as the University of Technology Sydney, and all requirements are published on the Department of Home Affairs site (immi.homeaffairs.gov.au) and the Study in Australia portal (studyinaustralia.gov.au); applicants must provide evidence of sufficient funds to cover their travel to Australia, 12 months of tuition fees (or a pro‑rata amount for shorter courses), and the minimum living cost of AUD 21,041 per year for a single student (higher amounts are required for families, plus school fees for any dependent children), and they must also purchase Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) costing roughly AUD 500‑800 per year for a single person; while the visa grants the right to work up to 48 hours per fortnight during scheduled study periods and unlimited hours during recognised semester breaks, all visa holders must maintain enrolment, meet attendance requirements, keep OSHC active, and any family members joining later must separately demonstrate financial capacity for their stay. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) – Post‑Higher Education Work stream allows international graduates of an Australian university such as the University of Technology Sydney to remain in Australia for typically two to three years after completing a bachelor, master or doctoral degree (the exact period depends on the qualification level, with higher degrees generally granting the longer three‑year term) and provides unrestricted full‑time work rights in any sector, while also permitting study and the inclusion of eligible family members; holders of Hong Kong or British National Overseas passports are eligible for a five‑year stay under this stream.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Technology Sydney (UTS) provides a dedicated International Student Centre that serves as the main point of contact for all international students, offering daily drop‑in advising, email and phone support, and regular e‑newsletters on security, wellbeing and visa or policy changes; new arrivals are required to attend the Essential International Advice session during Orientation, which also includes peer‑network volunteers, library introductions and information on academic and support services, and UTS runs a comprehensive pre‑departure program that lets students activate their university email, enrol, register for orientation and apply for short‑term or long‑term accommodation through the UTS Housing Service, which manages on‑campus and off‑campus housing options; additionally, the UTS Careers Service operates an International Student Career Hub that provides specialised one‑on‑one guidance, workshops, resources and a Careers Guide to help international students navigate the Australian job market, understand workplace culture and rights, and access internships, graduate and part‑time opportunities. Đời sống & môi trường: UTS offers a comprehensive campus‑life experience for international students, with more than 180 clubs and societies run through ActivateUTS that cover cultural groups (e.g., Chinese, French, Indonesian and Vietnamese student societies), sports (cricket, Tae Kwon Do, ski), gaming, arts and leadership programmes such as BUiLD (Beyond UTS International Leadership and Development) and the Exchange and Study‑Abroad Club, plus social hubs like the Network Cafe, Research Cafe and the on‑campus bars (the Loft and the Underground).
