**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International applicants to Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam can enroll in the two main intakes, which start in September (fall) and February (spring). To be considered for the September intake you must submit your request for enrolment in Studielink and complete the full VU‑online application (including all required documents and the €100 application fee for candidates with a non‑Dutch diploma) by the application deadline, which is typically 1 May; for the February intake the corresponding deadline is usually 1 December. After submitting in Studielink you receive a VU‑NetID and password, log in to your personal dashboard on VU.nl, answer the programme‑specific questions, upload transcripts, proof of English proficiency and any other requested items, and pay the fee; the completed application must be 100 % uploaded no later than one week after the deadline. Once the application is complete the International Office Admissions team checks it within about a week, after which you will receive a decision; if admitted you have until 31 August (or 15 July for numerus fixus bachelor programmes) to enrol by paying tuition, and for the spring intake the residence‑permit paperwork must be finalized by 1 December (fall intake 1 June) to allow the IND to process the permit in the required 6‑8 weeks.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam requires all non‑native‑English speakers applying for a master’s programme to submit an officially recognised English test result – typically IELTS (minimum overall 6.5 with at least 6.0 in each component), TOEFL iBT (minimum 90 with at least 20 per component), PTE Academic (minimum 58 with at least 50 per component) or Duolingo English Test (minimum 115, with at least 100 overall). Applicants can be exempted from providing a new test score if they have already completed a bachelor’s or master’s programme (or a substantial exchange period) that was entirely taught in English in the EU/EEA, Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, the United Kingdom, the United States, Singapore or South Africa, or if they hold a Dutch VWO secondary‑school diploma with a grade of 7 in English language and literature. To obtain an exemption, students must submit a formal request after receiving conditional admission, attaching the original English‑test result (if any), a detailed motivation and all supporting documentation as a single PDF to the International Student Advisor of their faculty or via the VU Dashboard; the request is evaluated individually per programme and approval is not guaranteed.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam must be enrolled at a recognised home university and meet the programme‑specific language and academic standing requirements—typically a minimum of 90 ECTS completed for semester‑in‑Amsterdam programmes and proof of sufficient English (or Dutch for certain tracks); exchange students are required to register for at least 24 ECTS per semester (48 ECTS per academic year) with a maximum of 36 ECTS, and cannot exceed three 6‑ECTS courses (18 ECTS) in periods 1, 2, 4 or 5 nor more than two courses (12 ECTS) in periods 3 or 6, while a standard workload is around 30 ECTS per semester; the curriculum is built around the European Credit Transfer System where one ECTS equals 28 study hours (including lectures, tutorials and independent work) and most individual courses carry 6 ECTS (≈168 hours) and are organised into six teaching periods, with the Basic Dutch on Campus language course offering 6 ECTS and counting toward the required total; each course is defined by explicit learning outcomes that correspond to the workload and are assessed through a mix of graded components (e.g., exams, assignments and participation) aligned with the VU grading scale, and detailed syllabi—including objectives, weekly topics, required readings and assessment criteria—are provided to students at the start of each course.

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: International students admitted to Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam must obtain two separate authorisations – an MVV (Machtiging tot Voorlopig Verblijf) entry visa and a VVR (Verblijfsvergunning Regulier) residence permit – which are processed by the Dutch Immigration and Naturalisation Service (IND) on behalf of the university acting as a recognised sponsor; the application begins after accepting an unconditional admission offer and registering in Studielink, after which the university submits the IND a checklist that includes a valid passport, recent passport‑size photos, proof of health insurance, a criminal record extract and, crucially, evidence of sufficient financial means, typically a blocked bank account or a formal guarantee showing at least €13 000 (the 2026 study allowance set by Nuffic is €1 130,77 per month, amounting to the same yearly total) to cover living costs for one year, and once IND approves the dossier (decision period usually 60–90 days) the student collects the MVV sticker from the Dutch embassy or consulate within three months, enters the Netherlands within the MVV’s 90‑day validity, and then picks up the biometric VVR card at the IND desk during the university’s orientation week, after which the permit is valid for the full duration of the study programme and allows part‑time work up to 16 hours per week (or full‑time during the summer months). Sau tốt nghiệp: The Dutch orientation‑year (zoekjaar) residence permit gives non‑EU graduates a non‑renewable 12‑month stay in the Netherlands to look for a job, start a business or work as a freelancer without needing a separate work permit, and it must be applied for within three years of completing an accredited bachelor’s, master’s or PhD programme at a recognised Dutch institution such as Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (or, for foreign graduates, a degree from a top‑200 globally‑ranked university that meets IND criteria); during the year you may work freely, but after it expires you must switch to another residence permit (e.g., Highly Skilled Migrant or startup visa) if you wish to remain in the country.

Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam typically start their first Dutch jobs with gross annual salaries of roughly €30 000–€36 000, but earnings increase rapidly and a significant proportion earn more than €65 000 five years after graduation; the labour market shows especially strong demand for VU alumni in data‑analysis and business‑intelligence roles, software development and IT consulting, finance and accounting, management consulting, engineering (particularly sustainable‑energy and civil‑engineering projects), and health‑care management, with the highest placement rates in the Greater Amsterdam region where about 40 % of these graduates remain employed five years after completing their studies.
