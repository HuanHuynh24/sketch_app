**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to the National University of Singapore must demonstrate English proficiency unless they have completed a previous degree taught entirely in English in an English‑speaking country (e.g., Australia, Canada, Hong Kong, India, New Zealand, Pakistan, Philippines, Singapore, the United Kingdom or the United States), in which case the test requirement can be waived. For those who need to provide test scores, the minimum acceptable results are: IELTS overall 6.5 (with at least 6.5 in reading and writing), TOEFL iBT overall 92‑93 (paper‑based 580), TOEFL Essentials 10, PTE Academic overall 62, Cambridge C1 Advanced (CAE) 180, and the Qualifying English Test (QET) if applicable. Duolingo English Test scores are not accepted for admission.

**4. Học phí**

- For international under‑graduates at NUS the full tuition is typically SGD 38,000‑45,000 per year, but the Ministry of Education (MOE) Tuition Grant reduces it by 40‑60 % so that most programmes now cost about SGD 17,500‑24,000 annually – for example Arts/Social Sciences SGD 17,500, Business/Accounting SGD 21,000‑24,000, Engineering SGD 19,000‑21,000 and Computer Science SGD 21,000 – giving a four‑year total of roughly SGD 70,000‑96,000; the fees are quoted in Singapore dollars (SGD); an application fee of SGD 30 is charged with the online application, and students also pay a one‑time registration fee of roughly SGD 30‑50 together with an annual GST‑inclusive student‑service/administrative surcharge of about SGD 1,000‑1,200.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For international students at the National University of Singapore, the typical annual out‑of‑pocket cost breaks down as follows: tuition fees range from about S$21,000 to S$33,000 per year depending on the faculty (with most programmes around S$25,000 annually); on‑campus or nearby private accommodation averages S$12,000 to S$15,000 per year; food and groceries cost roughly S$3,500 to S$4,500 per year; public transport and occasional taxi rides are about S 600 to S 800 annually; and the compulsory health‑insurance (NUS Student Insurance Scheme) is around S 300 to S 350 each year. Adding these components together, the total yearly expense for an international student typically falls between S$38,000 and S$44,000 , with a common average figure of about S$41,000 per academic year.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students pursuing a degree at the National University of Singapore must complete a total of 160 modular credits (MCs) – equivalently 160 units of workload (UT) – to graduate, with the credit distribution varying by programme but typically comprising roughly 20 MCs (≈5 courses) of major‐specific requirements, 36–44 MCs (≈9–11 courses) of NUS College core modules, 20 MCs (≈5 courses) of the Common Curriculum (e.g., CDE, CHS), and the remaining 20–28 MCs (≈5–7 courses) as unrestricted electives; each MC corresponds to 2.5 hours of weekly study and preparation, and every course lists explicit prerequisite modules that must be satisfied before enrolment, ensuring that students possess the foundational knowledge needed for advanced study, while the curriculum is deliberately interdisciplinary, embedding learning outcomes such as proficiency in 21st‑century transferable skills, design thinking, scientific inquiry, digital and data literacy, artificial‑intelligence concepts, effective writing, and the ability to integrate knowledge across humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students admitted to the National University of Singapore must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) from the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) and, as part of the STP application, provide proof of sufficient financial resources—typically a recent bank statement or fixed‑deposit certificate showing at least SGD 15,000–20,000 (or the amount required to cover tuition plus living costs for one year) together with official tuition‑fee receipts or a sponsor’s letter if the funds are not in the applicant’s own name; once the STP is issued, holders are not permitted to take any paid employment in Singapore unless they also hold a valid work pass (such as a Part‑Time Student Employment Pass, Training Employment Pass, or Work Holiday Pass), although ICA’s work‑pass exemption allows full‑time students to work without a separate pass during school vacations provided they are enrolled in an approved institution, have not exceeded the stipulated weekly hour limit during term (generally 16 hours), and meet any additional criteria set by the Ministry of Manpower, while all other employment during term requires the appropriate work permit. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the National University of Singapore may apply for a Singapore Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) to remain in the country while seeking employment; the pass is issued for up to two years, allowing the holder to stay and look for a job after completing their studies. Eligibility requires that the applicant be a recent graduate of an accredited Institute of Higher Learning (such as NUS), hold a valid Student’s Pass at the time of application, and provide supporting documents including a copy of the digital Student’s Pass, passport, recent colour photograph, graduation proof and any required marriage or birth certificates for dependants; the application is submitted through ICA’s online e‑Service, with a non‑refundable SGD 30 fee at submission and an additional SGD 60 payable within 30 days of approval.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: National University of Singapore (NUS) delivers a robust internship and placement ecosystem that links students—including international students—to more than 40 leading employers such as ByteDance, KPMG, Mediacorp, HP Singapore, Huawei, Infineon, JD.com, Philips, PSA International, Shopee, Singapore General Hospital, ST Engineering, Tencent and the Coca‑Cola Group, and this exposure translates into strong graduate outcomes: across the university, 89.8 % of fresh graduates secured employment within six months of graduation and 75.2 % were in full‑time permanent roles, while the Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy reported a 89.3 % employment rate for its 2021 cohort with 31 % obtaining new jobs in Singapore—38 % of those hires being international students—and a further 30 % finding positions abroad; graduates typically enter sectors such as finance, consulting, technology, government, healthcare, and not‑for‑profit organisations, reflecting the breadth of internships and co‑op experiences built into programmes like the Graduate Diploma in Systems Analysis and other NUS‑ISS offerings. Lương & thị trường: Recent graduate data show that NUS graduates entering the Singapore labour market earn median gross monthly salaries ranging from roughly S$4,300 for Bachelor of Social Sciences and S$4,500‑S$4,700 for arts‑related programmes to about S$5,000 for engineering majors such as Industrial and Systems Engineering and Materials Science, with the overall median for full‑time permanent positions hovering around S$4,900. The Ministry of Manpower’s occupational demand reports identify technology (software development, data analytics, cybersecurity), healthcare (nursing, allied health), finance and fintech, engineering (particularly civil, environmental and advanced manufacturing), and life sciences as the fastest‑growing sectors hiring both local and international graduates, often offering starting gross salaries of S$4,800 to S$5,500 depending on the role and discipline.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The National University of Singapore’s International Student Services Office, together with the Office of Student Affairs, runs a comprehensive first‑year orientation programme that includes a welcome week of campus tours, faculty‑specific sessions, and social‑integration activities designed to help new international students settle into university life; it also provides dedicated housing support by assisting students with on‑campus hostel applications, offering a curated list of approved off‑campus rentals and guidance on lease agreements, and coordinating with the university’s accommodation office to resolve any residency issues, while the university’s Career Services Centre delivers tailored career support for international students through résumé‑building workshops, interview preparation, internship and job‑search platforms, regular career fairs featuring multinational employers, and one‑on‑one counseling that takes into account visa and work‑permit requirements. Đời sống & môi trường: Singapore is widely regarded as one of the safest cities in the world, allowing international students at the National University of Singapore (NUS) to run or study late at night without concern, and its welcoming multicultural atmosphere makes it easy for foreigners to feel at home. Campus life at NUS is vibrant and diverse, offering a broad spectrum of Co‑Curricular Activities (CCAs) that span sports, arts, culture, technology and community service; examples include the NUS Fingerstyle guitar group under the Cultural Activities Club, numerous student‑run interest groups in the School of Computing, and the extensive directory of clubs managed through NUSync. In addition to these, there is an active Vietnamese Student Association that brings together Vietnamese nationals and those of Vietnamese heritage, organising language meet‑ups, cultural festivals, mentorship programmes and social events, thereby providing a supportive community for Vietnamese students alongside the wider international student network.
