### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Waterloo admits first‑year undergraduate students only for the September start (Fall term), with the 2026 intake opening in early March 2026; the application deadline for this September‑December term is June 19 2026 and the deadline to submit supporting documents (transcripts, English‑language test scores, etc.) is July 17 2026, while international applicants follow the same schedule, and for the winter 2026 term (January–April) the application deadline is October 31 2025 with a documents deadline of November 28 2025, and for the spring 2026 term (May–August) the application deadline is February 27 2026 with documents due March 27 2026. Prospective students apply online through the Waterloo Admissions portal, create an account, select their desired program, and pay the CAD $140 application fee required of international undergraduate applicants (graduate applicants pay a similar fee unless applying as an exchange student, which is fee‑free); after payment they upload all required documents, then monitor their Quest account for admission decisions, which for graduate programs can take up to eight weeks after receipt of a complete file.

- International applicants to the University of Waterloo must submit an official transcript of all post‑secondary study (with an English translation if the original is not in English) and meet the university’s minimum academic standing, which is expressed as a country‑specific GPA or percentage equivalency; for most countries the benchmark is roughly a 70 % (or 3.0/4.0) overall average, with examples such as 8 / 10 in Albania, 4 / 5 in Hungary, 2 : 1 (Upper Second Class) in the United Kingdom, 73 / 100 in Afghanistan, 14 / 20 in several francophone nations, and 80 / 100 in the United Arab Emirates. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Waterloo must demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least an overall IELTS Academic score of 6.5 with no band below 6.0, an internet‑based TOEFL iBT overall score of 85 (minimum 20 in each band) for tests taken before January 2026 (later tests are assessed on a 4.5 overall scale), a PTE Academic overall score of 62 with a minimum of 60 in each band, or a Duolingo English Test overall score of 120 with Literacy and Production sub‑scores of 120 for tests completed after 1 July 2024 (the requirement was 105/105 for earlier tests). A waiver of these requirements is granted if the applicant has completed at least one full academic year (or the equivalent) of full‑time study in an English‑language post‑secondary institution in Canada, has graduated from a Canadian secondary school where English was the language of instruction, or otherwise meets the university’s “first‑language‑English” exemption criteria.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at the University of Waterloo pay tuition that is considerably higher than for domestic students, with annual tuition fees ranging from approximately CAD $51,000 to CAD $74,000 depending on the program and faculty; in addition to tuition, students must budget for the standard incidental fees of about CAD $1,500 per term (≈ CAD $3,000 per year) and the mandatory co‑op fee of CAD $836 for those in co‑op programs, bringing the typical total annual cost to roughly CAD $54,000–$78,000. The application fee for an international applicant through the Ontario Universities’ Application Centre (OUAC) is CAD $156 for the first three program choices plus a CAD $10 international service surcharge, for a total of CAD $166, with an additional CAD $50 for each extra program choice beyond the initial three.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- At the University of Waterloo all undergraduate courses are worth 0.5 unit, so a typical four‑year Honours degree (including for international students) requires 40 courses or 20 units, with specific composition rules such as a minimum of 25 courses (12.5 units) at the 200‑level or above, at least six courses (3 units) at the 300‑level or higher, a 0.5‑unit Undergraduate Communications Requirement, ten breadth courses (5 units), and a residency requirement of 20 Waterloo courses; in addition, the curriculum mandates depth requirements that can be met by completing a prerequisite chain of three courses in the same prefix (e.g., PHYS 111 → PHYS 112 → PHYS 224) where at least one course is at the 300‑level, and electives may be satisfied by Math or non‑Math units as defined by the faculty, while each course and program includes explicit learning outcomes describing the knowledge, skills and competencies students must achieve, and prerequisites, corequisites and antirequisites are formally listed to ensure students (including internationals) have completed the required prior coursework, program registration or academic standing before enrollment.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Waterloo is ranked 112 th in the 2024 QS World University Rankings overall and 172 nd for its share of international students, while it is placed around 119 th in the 2024 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings; in subject‑specific QS rankings the university is in the global top 100 for 15 disciplines, notably 27 th in Computer Science, 29 th in Mathematics, 30 th in Electrical & Electronics Engineering, 38 th in the broad Engineering & Technology category, 40 th in Data Science & Artificial Intelligence, 51‑100 th in Civil & Structural Engineering and Architecture, and 65 th in Mechanical, Aeronautical & Manufacturing Engineering, and it ranks second in Canada in areas such as Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Architecture and Hospitality Management; the institution is publicly accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Colleges and Universities and its engineering programs hold accreditation from the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board (CEAB), with other professional programmes similarly recognized by the relevant accrediting bodies.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: International students admitted to the University of Waterloo must submit a study‑permit application to IR IRCC that includes a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) for most undergraduate programmes (the PAL is a letter from the province of Ontario confirming the student’s place in a designated learning institution; master’s and PhD students are exempt from the PAL requirement as of 1 January 2026) and must also provide proof of sufficient financial resources – typically evidence that they can cover tuition for the first year plus living expenses of at least CAD 10,000 per year (or the higher amount shown on the IRCC “proof of funds” table for the specific province), which can be shown through bank statements, scholarship or bursary letters, a letter of financial support from a sponsor, or a student loan confirmation; once the study permit is issued, the student may work off‑campus up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled breaks, and may also work on‑campus without a separate work permit, provided they remain in good standing with their study permit conditions. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of Waterloo may be eligible for a Post‑Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) if they have completed a program that is at least eight months long, have maintained full‑time study status in Canada for the majority of the program, and have not previously been issued a PGWP. The length of the permit is determined by the duration of the study program: if the program was between eight months and less than two years, the PGWP can be issued for up to the same length as the program; if the program was two years or longer, the PGPermit is generally valid for three years. Effective 15 February 2024, graduates of master’s programmes that are shorter than two years can also receive a three‑year PGWP provided the program was at least eight months, the student met all other eligibility criteria (including having studied at least two years—six terms—in Canada and completing less than 50 % of courses by distance learning), held a valid study permit at the time of application, and applied within 180 days of receiving the official transcript and completion letter, with a passport that remains valid for the entire permit period. Graduates must not have previously held a PGWP and must satisfy any additional IRCC requirements such as language proficiency and proof of financial support.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Waterloo’s co‑op and internship programs are core components of its work‑integrated learning (WIL) system, offering full‑time paid work terms (co‑op) and discipline‑specific supervised internships that are open to both domestic and international students, with the option to complete work terms abroad supported by dedicated international co‑op advisors; the university calculates its co‑op employment rate by dividing the number of students who have secured approved employment by the total number of students participating in co‑op recruitment (excluding those who change sequences or withdraw), and historically reports that more than 95 % of co‑op participants obtain a paid position by the end of the recruitment cycle, while graduate employment surveys show over 90 % of Waterloo graduates secure relevant employment within six months—typically in fields such as engineering, computer science, business, and emerging technologies—demonstrating strong career outcomes for all students. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from the University of Waterloo command some of the highest earnings among new entrants to Canada’s labour market, with recent cohorts earning roughly 37 % more than their Canadian‑born peers—translating to average annual T4 earnings in the mid‑$90 000 range compared with about $70 000 for domestic graduates—and they are concentrated in the occupations that the Job Bank identifies as experiencing strong demand, notably web designers and developers (about 21 % of Waterloo international graduates work in this field, with median salaries near $75 000) and mathematicians, statisticians, actuaries and data scientists (about 12 % of graduates, with median earnings around $95 000). These sectors, together with broader technology, engineering and health‑care roles, are listed by the Job Bank as having high vacancy rates and above‑average wage growth, reflecting a labour‑market environment in which Waterloo’s internationally‑trained alumni are both well‑paid and highly sought after.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Waterloo’s International Experience Centre (IEC) serves as the hub for international students, offering a dedicated International Orientation program that brings new students together before the main campus orientation and provides a comprehensive International Student Guide along with one‑on‑one advising and free immigration‑consultant appointments; the IEC also assists with housing by supplying information on on‑campus residences, off‑campus rental resources, and referrals to trusted housing services to help students secure safe and affordable accommodation; for career development, the Centre for Career Development provides specialized support for international students, including virtual and in‑person career advising, résumé and cover‑letter reviews, job‑search strategies, workshops on working in Canada, and access to CareerHub and other online tools to prepare for internships, co‑ops, and full‑time employment in the Canadian market. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Waterloo offers a safe environment for international students, with a 2023 survey showing that 88 % of them find the Waterloo region welcoming and 76 % feel safe, supported by 24/7 campus security, emergency response systems and round‑the‑clock mental‑health services; campus life is vibrant, featuring more than 200 student clubs ranging from sports and recreation to cultural and academic groups, and dedicated programs such as the International Peer Community (IPC) that pairs newcomers with upper‑year volunteers for bi‑weekly gatherings and larger monthly events, as well as the Connection Collective which creates equity‑focused spaces for in‑person and virtual networking; among the cultural communities, a Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) at Waterloo regularly hosts Vietnamese New Year celebrations, language‑exchange meet‑ups, cooking workshops and mentorship activities to help Vietnamese‑origin students connect, share traditions and navigate university life, while the broader international student body benefits from a welcoming, inclusive atmosphere that encourages participation in a wide array of clubs and support networks.
