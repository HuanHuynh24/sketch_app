### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Đại học Pennsylvania (UPenn) chấp nhận hồ sơ nhập học cho sinh viên quốc tế qua hai vòng chính: Early Decision (đầu vào ràng buộc) với hạn nộp hồ sơ vào ngày 1 tháng 11 và Regular Decision (không ràng buộc) với hạn nộp vào ngày 5 tháng 1; phí nộp đơn là 75 USD và có thể xin miễn phí nếu gặp khó khăn tài chính; sinh viên có thể nộp đơn thông qua Common Application hoặc Coalition Application (cũng chấp nhận QuestBridge cho một số học sinh) mà không có ưu tiên nào giữa các nền tảng; quy trình bao gồm tạo tài khoản trên nền tảng đã chọn, nhập thông tin cá nhân, nộp bảng điểm (không yêu cầu đánh giá lại bởi bên thứ ba), nộp bằng chứng năng lực tiếng Anh (TOEFL, IELTS hoặc Duolingo), hoàn thành các bài luận ngắn gọn đặc trưng của Penn và trả phí (hoặc yêu cầu miễn phí), đồng thời nếu chọn Early Decision phải ký Thỏa Thuận Early Decision xác nhận sẽ nhập học khi được chấp nhận; đối với một số chương trình không cấp bằng (ví dụ LPS – International Guest Student Program), trường cung cấp hai kỳ nhập học: mùa thu (hạn nộp hồ sơ 1 tháng 6) và mùa xuân (hạn nộp 15 tháng 10), với quy trình và phí tương tự.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào (GPA, SAT/ACT)**

- International applicants to the University of Pennsylvania must submit the same Common App or Coalition application as domestic students, including official high‑school transcripts covering all four years of secondary school (translated into English if needed) and, for any external examinations, the original IB, AP, A‑Level, Cambridge or similar scores; while Penn does not publish a minimum GPA, competitive candidates typically present a cumulative high‑school GPA of 3.5 / 4.0 or higher (or the local equivalent on a 10‑point scale) together with strong standardized test results—SAT scores of roughly 1450 + (or ACT composites of 33 +), and for English‑language proficiency TOEFL iBT ≥ 100, IELTS ≥ 7.0 or Duolingo ≥ 130. Robust performance in core subjects (math, science, English/language arts, and foreign language) across the four years is expected, and high AP scores (4‑5) or IB scores (5 + in higher‑level courses) are strongly favored. Most undergraduate programs do not require a portfolio or interview, though certain schools (e.g., the School of Arts and Sciences’ Fine Arts majors or design‑related majors) may request a portfolio or conduct an interview as part of the selective review process.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Pennsylvania must demonstrate English proficiency unless English is their native language or they have completed a degree at an institution where English was the sole language of instruction (in which case they are generally exempt, except for programs such as the TESOL M.S.Ed.); high‑school instruction in English can also satisfy the requirement. Accepted tests are the TOEFL iBT (minimum score 100), IELTS (minimum overall band 7), and the Duolingo English Test (minimum score 130); scores are valid for two years from the test date. Applicants whose degree was taught in English may receive a waiver, but must still submit proof of the language of instruction; no PTE requirement is listed. Scores should be sent directly by the testing agency using the appropriate university code (e.g., TOEFL code 2943 for the Graduate School of Education or 2986 for the College of Liberal and Professional Studies).

**4. Học phí**

- The University of Pennsylvania charges a single tuition‑and‑fees rate for all full‑time students—including international applicants—so there is no separate “out‑of‑state” figure; for the 2024‑25 academic year the tuition and mandatory fees total $68,686 USD per year (with an additional $1,358 USD for books and supplies, bringing the basic academic cost to about $70,044 USD annually), and the application fee for international undergraduate candidates is $90 USD (with higher fees of up to $275 USD possible for certain graduate or professional programs).

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Pennsylvania is ranked #7 among U.S. national universities in the 2023 U.S. News & World Report rankings and holds a global position of roughly #15 in the QS World University Rankings, where it was also listed as the #8 university nationally in 2020; in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings it sits near the top‑15 globally, reflecting its strong research, teaching and international outlook, while its individual programs earn top subject marks—Penn Nursing is ranked #1 worldwide in the QS subject ranking for nursing, the Wharton School is #1 in U.S. undergraduate business programs and leads in finance, marketing, management and related specialties, and the School of Engineering and Applied Science ranks within the top 30 for engineering overall and #10 for bioengineering/biomedical engineering; the university is regionally accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education, with professional schools holding specialized accreditations such as AACSB for business, CCNE for nursing, ABET for engineering, and APA for psychology, and international students comprise roughly one‑fifth of Penn’s total enrollment, underscoring its global appeal.

**2. Student Visa (F-1) và Chính sách OPT**

- Student Visa (F-1): To obtain a Form I‑20 from the University of Pennsylvania and be eligible for an F‑1 student visa, you must submit documented evidence that you (or your sponsor) have sufficient funds to cover the full first‑year cost of attendance, which includes tuition, mandatory fees, estimated living expenses, books, and personal costs as listed on the university’s cost‑of‑attendance chart; this proof typically takes the form of recent (within six months) bank statements, a letter from a financial institution, scholarship or fellowship award letters, a sponsor’s affidavit of support accompanied by the sponsor’s own financial documents (such as tax returns, pay stubs, or bank statements), and any other asset statements that together demonstrate the ability to pay at least 100 % of the published amount; the University’s international‑student office will review these documents before issuing the I‑20, after which you must sign the I‑20, pay the I‑901 SEVIS fee, and schedule a visa interview at a U.S. embassy or consulate where you will present the I‑20 along with the financial evidence to prove you meet the F‑1 visa requirements. Sau tốt nghiệp (OPT/STEM OPT): The STEM OPT extension for an international graduate from the University of Pennsylvania provides an additional 24 months of post‑completion employment authorization beyond the initial 12‑month OPT period, provided the student holds an F‑1 visa, has earned a bachelor’s, master’s or doctoral degree that appears on the DHS STEM‑Designated Degree Program List, and is currently in a valid period of post‑completion OPT; the student must submit a timely Form I‑765 together with a DSO‑endorsed Form I‑20 (endorsed within the last 60 days), the correct filing fee, and a copy of the qualifying STEM degree, and must have a signed Form I‑983 training plan with an employer who participates in E‑Verify and offers at least 20 hours of work per week in a role directly related to the degree; if the application is filed up to 90 days before the current OPT expires and within 60 days of the DSO’s SEVIS recommendation, the student automatically receives a 180‑day employment extension while the petition is pending, and the 24‑month extension can be granted once per degree level, allowing a second extension after obtaining a higher‑level qualifying STEM degree.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm (CPT/OPT): University of Pennsylvania’s Career Services regularly surveys graduating students and reports that roughly 94 % of Penn graduates secure employment or continue to graduate study within six months of completion, and the same high placement rate applies to international students who use Curricular Practical Training (CPT) to obtain internships; in the Penn Engineering Online Master’s program, a 53 % survey response yielded known outcomes for about 90 % of the 402 recent graduates, showing that the overwhelming majority of those tracked obtained jobs, and CPT participants typically land internships and subsequent full‑time positions in industries such as finance, consulting, technology, engineering, healthcare, and nonprofit/NGO sectors, with many converting those internships into permanent offers. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from the University of Pennsylvania typically enter a labor market that values STEM, finance, consulting and data‑analytics skills, with the highest demand and earnings seen in computer‑related and quantitative roles; the Bureau of Labor Statistics reports median 2024 wages of roughly $120,000 for software developers, $160,000 for computer and information systems managers, $88,000 for financial analysts, $87,000 for management analysts, $78,000 for civil engineers and $73,000 for accountants, while projected employment growth over the next decade ranges from about 10 % for engineers and analysts to 15 % for software and data professions, and Penn’s own first‑destination surveys show that 85‑90 % of its international graduates secure U.S. employment within six months of graduation, with average starting salaries around $70,000 for bachelor’s holders, $85,000 for master’s recipients and $100,000 plus for Ph.D. earners.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Pennsylvania’s International Student & Scholar Services (ISSS) serves as the central office for all international students, offering a mandatory orientation that introduces visa regulations, campus resources, and community‑building activities, while also providing housing guidance through online resources, referrals to university‑affiliated housing, and assistance with off‑campus rentals; for career development, Penn Career Services works closely with ISSS to deliver tailored support for international students—including individualized coaching, resume and cover‑letter workshops, mock interviews, CPT/OPT/H‑1B eligibility counseling, employer networking events, and global internship/job search resources—and can be reached at (215) 898‑7531 or careerservices@vpul.upenn.edu, with ISSS information and additional contacts available through the Penn Global website at global.upenn.edu/isss. Đời sống & môi trường: University of Pennsylvania boasts one of the most internationally diverse student bodies in the Ivy League, with dedicated groups such as the Vietnamese Student Association that organize cultural festivals, language‑practice sessions, and mentorship for Vietnamese students, while the broader international community can join over 450 clubs spanning academic, cultural, service, and recreational interests; Greek life remains a prominent part of campus life, with fraternities and sororities required to complete mandatory by‑stander‑intervention and violence‑prevention workshops that teach the “5 D’s” framework and promote a safe, inclusive environment, and the university’s Division of Public Safety supports campus security through a large full‑time and contract staff, annual Clery‑compliant security reporting, risk‑managed event registration that includes professional bartending, crowd‑control, and sober‑host monitoring, and a comprehensive public‑safety infrastructure that together create a generally safe setting for students, staff, and visitors.
