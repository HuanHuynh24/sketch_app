### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- POSTECH admits international graduate students in two regular intakes — the Spring semester beginning in March and the Fall semester beginning in September — with application deadlines of 30 November for Spring admission and 31 July for Fall admission; an application fee of roughly USD 100 (approximately KRW 120,000) must be paid when the online application is submitted, and the process requires creating an account on the POSTECH International Admissions portal, completing the online form, uploading certified copies of academic transcripts and degree certificates, proof of English proficiency (e.g., TOEFL ≥ 80 iBT or IELTS ≥ 6.0), a research proposal, two recommendation letters, and a passport copy, then paying the fee and submitting the package for review, after which successful candidates receive an admission letter that is used to apply for a D‑2 student visa.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- POSTECH requires international exchange students to have a cumulative GPA of at least 3.0 on a 4.3 scale (equivalent to a B letter grade) and demonstrate English proficiency with a TOEFL iBT score of 79 or higher, or an IELTS Academic score of 6.0 or above (a letter from the home institution may substitute for the test). Applicants must submit a Certificate of Enrollment (apostilled if the student is from a non‑OECD country), an official academic transcript in English, a copy of the passport, a recommendation letter, a CV, a cover letter, and a Letter of Nomination from the home‑institution coordinator; non‑OECD students also need to provide financial proof (e.g., US $700 per month). No portfolio or interview is listed as a requirement for the standard exchange program.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- POSTECH requires international graduate applicants to demonstrate either Korean or English proficiency: a TOPIK level 4 or higher, or an English test score of at least TOEFL 530 (CBT 197 or iBT 71), IELTS 5.5, CEFR B2, or TEPS 601 (NEW TEPS 327). Applicants from countries where English is the mother‑tongue or an official language are exempt from providing any English‑language certificate, so they receive a waiver for IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, Duolingo, etc. Only the listed tests (TOEFL, IELTS, CEFR B2, TEPS) are accepted; other English tests such as PTE or Duolingo are not mentioned as acceptable alternatives.

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): To obtain a D‑2 student visa for POSTECH you must first secure an admission letter from the university and then demonstrate that you have enough funds to cover tuition and living expenses for the entire period of study; this is done by submitting a recent bank statement (or a scholarship/sponsor letter) that shows a balance of at least the amount required by immigration—generally the equivalent of KRW 2.1 million per month or roughly KRW 30 million for a year—along with the POSTECH‑specific “Proof of Finance” document listed among the required D‑2 paperwork. Once you are in Korea and have your Alien Registration Card, you may apply for a Time‑Limited Employment Permit through the HiKorea portal (hikorea.go.kr) to work up to 20 hours per week during the semester (and up to 40 hours during vacation periods); the application requires the employer’s contract, a copy of your ARC, and a completed permit form, and the permit is usually issued within two weeks, allowing you to legally earn money while completing your studies. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): International graduates from POSTECH can switch from a student visa (D‑2) to a Job‑Seeker visa (D‑10) immediately after graduation; because they hold a degree from a Korean university they are exempt from the points‑based D‑10‑1 requirement and the usual financial‑support proof, and they may stay for up to one year (the standard D‑10 period) which can be extended to a maximum of two years depending on individual circumstances. While on D‑10 full‑time employment is not permitted, paid internships are allowed and must be reported within 14 days, and there is no legally defined minimum internship length before applying for an E‑7 work visa, though companies typically prefer three to six months of performance evaluation. Once a concrete job offer that matches the E‑7 categories (e.g., professional, technical, or specific activities) is secured and a contract signed, the employer can file the E‑7 sponsorship while the graduate remains on D‑10, after which the status can be changed in‑country to E‑7 and the graduate may begin full‑time work.
