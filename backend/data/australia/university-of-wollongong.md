**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to the University of Wollongong must submit official transcripts and meet both academic and English‑language thresholds that vary by program: for most undergraduate courses a minimum overall secondary‑school average of roughly 70 % (or the equivalent ATAR/IB score) is required, with specific prerequisite subjects such as Mathematics and Physics for engineering or Chemistry and Biology for health‑sciences, while creative programmes (e.g., Design, Architecture, Fine Arts) often demand a portfolio and may also require an interview; for postgraduate entry the university calculates GPA on a three‑year full‑time equivalent basis using the GEMSAS GPA calculator, with a weighted GPA of at least 5.5 on a 7‑point scale required for the Doctor of Medicine program (and generally a minimum 5.0–5.5 on the same scale for other coursework master’s), and a minimum 2.0 / 4.0 (or 50 % overall) for most bachelor‑level courses; English proficiency must be demonstrated through an accepted test (e.g., IELTS 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5, TOEFL iBT 60, Pearson 550, Cambridge C1, or OET B), and where the GPA hurdle is met the university may invite candidates to an interview for competitive courses, after which offers are made without further GPA ranking.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to the University of Wollongong must prove English proficiency unless their most recent degree was taught in English, in which case a waiver may be granted; acceptable tests and minimum scores are IELTS (Academic, UKVI or OSR) overall 7.0 with at least 6.5 in writing, PTE Academic overall 66 (minimum 56 in writing), TOEFL iBT overall 94 (minimum 24 in reading, 24 in writing, 23 in speaking and 24 in listening), and Cambridge C1 Advanced or C2 Proficiency with an overall score of 185. The English test must have been taken within two years of the course start date, and the university does not list Duolingo as an accepted test.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa, which is the official pathway for international students to study at registered Australian providers such as the University of Wollongong, requires applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity by providing evidence (e.g., bank statements, loan documents, sponsor letters) that they can cover the full tuition fees for the first 12 months of the course, the minimum living costs set by the Department of Home Affairs (currently AU$21,041 per year for a single student, plus additional amounts for a partner and any dependent children), and travel expenses for themselves and any family members, and they must also obtain and maintain valid Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire period of stay, with the OSHC start date matching their arrival in Australia; while on this visa, students are permitted to work up to 40 hours per fortnight during scheduled course periods and unlimited hours during recognized school breaks, and any family members who join later must also provide evidence of financial capacity and maintain OSHC for the duration of their stay. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of the University of Wollongong who meet the Australian study requirement can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) under the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which normally grants a stay of between two and three years (generally two years for a bachelor’s degree, up to three years for most master’s qualifications and up to four years for a doctorate) and allows unrestricted work, study and the inclusion of eligible family members; holders of Hong Kong or British National Overseas passports may be granted up to five years, and a separate Second Post‑Higher Education Work stream is available for those who studied and live in a regional area, providing an additional one‑to‑two‑year period, while the temporary two‑year extension introduced on 1 July 2023 for certain skill‑shortage qualifications has been discontinued from mid‑2024, so only the standard stream durations now apply.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: University of Wollongong (UOW) places a strong emphasis on work‑integrated learning for international students, offering dedicated subjects such as GWP 900 International Workplace Practice, CRLP 100 Career Ready Learning and CRLP 200 Career Ready Learning & Practice that embed internships, industry‑partner projects and placement opportunities within the curriculum; strategic collaborations with organisations including Deloitte, TAFE NSW, Swinburne University and NSW government departments underpin programmes like the Cyber Academy, which fast‑tracks up to 1,200 graduates into cyber‑security roles while they complete a combined diploma and bachelor’s degree. This focus translates into robust graduate outcomes: 87.1 % of UOW graduates secure full‑time employment within four months of completing their course—exceeding the national average of 86.3 %—and employer satisfaction with UOW graduates stands at 91.7 %. Recent graduate‑outcome data also show a full‑time employment rate of roughly 80 % for recent graduates and a median full‑time salary of $67,800, with many alumni entering fields such as information technology, cyber security, engineering, business, health and sport based on the industry partnerships and placement experiences gained during their studies. Lương & thị trường: Recent University of Wollongong graduates are entering a labour market where demand is strongest for high‑skill roles in health care and social assistance, professional, scientific and technical services, construction, education and training, and accommodation and food services, and the median full‑time salary for UOW graduates rose to $67,800 in 2022; occupation‑level data show that roles such as accountants earn a median weekly wage of about $2,003 (approximately $104,000 annually) while accounting clerks earn around $1,442 weekly (about $75,000 annually), reflecting the broader earnings potential across the sectors with the highest demand for both domestic and international graduates.
