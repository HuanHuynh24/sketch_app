### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Columbia University admits first‑year international students only for the fall semester, with two annual intake deadlines: a binding Early Decision deadline on November 1 (for the 2025‑2026 cycle) and a Regular Decision deadline on January 1 2026; the school does not offer an Early Action option. Applicants must apply online through either the Common Application or the Coalition Application (available via Scoir), create an account, complete the personal‑information sections, submit the required essays, provide official transcripts (with certified English translations if not originally in English), submit English‑proficiency test scores or videos if needed, and upload any additional documents such as recommendation letters; the application fee is $85 (waivers are available for eligible students). International students are permitted to apply Early Decision, though highly competitive candidates who request need‑aware financial aid may be deferred to Regular Decision for full pool review. In Vietnamese, the “kỳ nhập học” is the fall term, the “deadline nộp hồ sơ” is November 1 for Early Decision or January 1 for Regular Decision, the “application fee” is 85 USD, and the “quy trình apply” follows the steps outlined above.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Columbia University requires international undergraduate applicants to demonstrate proficiency in English by meeting one of the following minimum scores: TOEFL iBT ≥ 105 (for tests taken on or before 20 January 2026; scores after 21 January 2026 may be satisfied with an IELTS band ≥ 5.5), IELTS ≥ 7.5, Duolingo English Test ≥ 135, or Cambridge C1 Advanced/C2 Proficiency ≥ 191; PTE and other exams are not accepted. Applicants can be exempt from submitting test scores if they have completed at least one full year of full‑time study at an institution where English is the primary language of instruction, or if they hold a degree from an English‑medium university, in which case a waiver is granted and no TOEFL/IELTS/DET score is required. Online versions of TOEFL iBT (Special Home Edition) and IELTS Indicator are accepted, and all scores must be reported directly to Columbia using report code 2116.

**2. Student Visa (F-1) và Chính sách OPT**

- Student Visa (F-1): International students admitted to Columbia University who need an F‑1 visa must obtain a Form I‑20, which will list the total estimated cost of attendance for the first academic year—including tuition, mandatory fees, health insurance, and living expenses (typically around $80 000 for undergraduate students and $70 000–$85 000 for graduate students, depending on the program). To have the I‑20 issued, Columbia requires documented proof that the student (or a sponsor) has sufficient funds to cover this amount, such as recent, unexpired bank statements (or a certified bank letter) showing liquid assets, official scholarship or fellowship award letters, or a notarized affidavit of support accompanied by the sponsor’s financial documents. The documentation must demonstrate that the funds are readily available and not contingent on future earnings. Once the I‑20 is received, the student must pay the I‑901 SEVIS fee and retain the receipt, then schedule a visa interview at a U.S. embassy or consulate, where the I‑20, proof of financial support, passport, DS‑160 confirmation, and the SEVIS fee receipt are presented as part of the F‑1 visa application process outlined by the Department of State’s student visa page and USCIS guidance on maintaining student status. Sau tốt nghiệp (OPT/STEM OPT): International graduate students at Columbia University who are on an F‑1 visa may first receive up to 12 months of post‑completion Optional Practical Training (OPT) and, if they earned a degree in a U.S. Department of Homeland Security‑designated STEM field, they can apply for a STEM OPT extension that adds a further 24 months of work authorization for a total of up to 36 months; eligibility requires that the student be in a valid period of post‑completion OPT, have a paid employment offer of at least 20 hours per week from an employer enrolled in E‑Verify, obtain a new Form I‑20 with a STEM OPT recommendation from Columbia’s International Students and Scholars Office, and submit the extension application to USCIS within 60 days of the start of the initial OPT period (the application must be filed while the original OPT is still active).
