### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Australian National University admits international students for two main intakes each year – Semester 1 (starting late February) and Semester 2 (starting late July) – with the usual application windows closing around 31 October of the preceding year for Semester 1 and around 30 April for Semester 2 (these dates apply to both undergraduate and postgraduate programmes, although some specialised courses may have slightly different cut‑off dates); the online application is submitted through the ANU Direct Application Portal or the Universities Admissions Centre (UAC) after creating an account, selecting your desired programme, uploading required documents (such as academic transcripts, English‑language test results, a personal statement and any required references), paying the non‑refundable application fee (approximately AU$100 for undergraduate courses and AU$115 for postgraduate courses), and then waiting for the assessment outcome, after which successful candidates must accept the offer, arrange a student visa and, for first‑year undergraduates, secure on‑campus accommodation through the ANU Accommodation Portal.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Information for International Students and How to Apply](https://rsaa.anu.edu.au/study/phd/information-international-students) (Cao, score 0.69)<br>[International undergraduate & postgraduate](https://study.anu.edu.au/apply/international-applications) (Cao, score 0.59)<br>[Applying for exchange at ANU \| The Australian National University](https://study.anu.edu.au/apply/applying-exchange-anu) (Cao, score 0.56)<br>[Information for - Prospective international students](https://study.anu.edu.au/related-information/information-prospective-international-students) (Cao, score 0.52)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Australian National University requires international applicants to have completed the Australian Year 12 senior secondary certificate or an equivalent qualification (such as IB, A‑levels, STPM, Hong Kong DSE, etc.) and to submit certified academic transcripts that are converted to ANU’s 7‑point GPA scale, with most undergraduate courses expecting a minimum GPA of around 5.0 (out of 7) and postgraduate coursework or research degrees generally requiring at least a credit average (≈ 5.5 / 7) plus a recognised bachelor’s degree; specific programmes list prerequisite subjects (e.g., mathematics for science and engineering, biology for health sciences) and minimum subject scores (e.g., IB total 32 or A‑level grades AAA), while design‑related degrees such as Bachelor of Design or Visual Arts additionally require a portfolio and an interview; English language proficiency must also be demonstrated (typically IELTS 6.5 overall with ≥ 6.0 in each component or equivalent), and successful applicants must obtain a Confirmation of Enrolment, organise Overseas Student Health Cover and a subclass 500 student visa, meeting the university’s academic progress conditions throughout their study.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [[PDF] 2024 MINIMUM ENTRY REQUIREMENTS](https://www.anu.edu.au/files/guidance/documents/2024%2520Admissions%2520table2024July.pdf) (Cao, score 0.68)<br>[Assessment \| The Australian National University](https://study.anu.edu.au/apply/international-applications/assessment) (Cao, score 0.62)<br>[International student visa requirements \| The Australian National University](https://study.anu.edu.au/apply/international-student-visa-requirements) (Cao, score 0.62)<br>[[PDF] Postgraduate International Student Guide 2024/25](https://study.anu.edu.au/files/2023-08/Postgraduate%20International%20Student%20Guide.pdf) (Cao, score 0.58)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Australian National University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency through recognised tests: IELTS Academic — overall 6.0 with no band below 5.5 (or 5.5 overall with no band below 5.0 for the 20‑week pathway); TOEFL iBT — overall 67 with minimum scores of Writing 14, Speaking 17, Reading 12, Listening 12 (or 51 overall with Writing 9, Speaking 14, Reading 8, Listening 8 for the pathway); PTE Academic — overall 50 with no communicative skill below 42 (or 42 overall with Reading 36, Listening 33, Speaking 24, Writing 29 for the pathway); Cambridge C1 Advanced — overall 169 (162 for the pathway); and LanguageCert Academic — overall 61 (54 for the pathway). The Duolingo English Test is not listed as an accepted test. English‑language waivers are possible if the applicant has completed prior study in English (e.g., a degree taught in English), holds citizenship of an English‑speaking country, or provides other qualifying evidence such as certain high‑school qualifications completed in English.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [English language program - The Australian National University](https://study.anu.edu.au/apply/international-applications/english-language-program) (Cao, score 0.76)<br>[Exchange Entry Requirement \| The Australian National University](https://study.anu.edu.au/apply/applying-exchange-anu/exchange-entry-requirement) (Cao, score 0.73)<br>[International students \| ANU College of Systems and Society](https://systems.anu.edu.au/study/more-information/international-students) (Cao, score 0.73)<br>[[PDF] Undergraduate International Student Guide 2024](https://study.anu.edu.au/files/2023-08/Undergraduate%20International%20Student%20Guide.pdf) (Cao, score 0.70)

**4. Học phí**

- International students at the Australian National University pay an indicative annual tuition fee of about AUD 45,000 for most undergraduate programmes (rising to roughly AUD 55,000 for high‑cost professional courses such as Medicine), while taught postgraduate courses are generally priced between AUD 42,000 and AUD 48,000 per year; the non‑refundable application fee is AUD 140, and all international students must also cover the Services and Amenities fee of approximately AUD 300 per year together with any programme‑specific compulsory costs (e.g., field trips, equipment), so the typical total annual cost ranges from roughly AUD 45,300–55,300 for undergraduates and AUD 42,300–48,300 for postgraduates, with amounts expressed in Australian dollars and subject to yearly indexation.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [International tuition fees - The Australian National University](https://www.anu.edu.au/students/program-administration/fees-payments/international-tuition-fees) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Tuition Fees Summary \| Australian National University](https://www.anu.edu.au/students/program-administration/fees-payments/tuition-fees-summary) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Fees Schedules \| Australian National University](https://www.anu.edu.au/students/program-administration/fees-payments/fees-schedules) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[[PDF] Offer Acceptance Declaration and Payment - Cloudfront.net](https://study.anu.edu.au/files/guidance/documents/Offer_Acceptance_Declaration_and_Payment.pdf) (Cao, score 0.97)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at the Australian National University the typical annual budget breaks down as follows: shared on‑campus or off‑campus housing costs about AU$312 per week (≈ AU$16,200 per year), while private solo accommodation is roughly AU$673 per week (≈ AU$35,000 per year); groceries average AU$150 per week (≈ AU$7,800 per year); public‑transport fares average AU$15 per week (≈ AU$780 per year) with a daily cap of about AU$4.80; utilities, internet and phone together run roughly AU$40 per week (≈ AU$2,100 per year); and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is normally between AU$600 and AU$700 annually. Adding these items gives a basic cost of roughly AU$27,000 – AU$29,000 per year for a student sharing accommodation, and about AU$38,000 – AU$42,000 per year for a student living alone, covering accommodation, food, transport, utilities and health insurance.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Australian National University requires international students to meet specific prerequisite subjects and credit‑point thresholds before enrolment, with many programmes stipulating subjects such as Advanced Mathematics I as a minimum entry requirement; courses are measured in units (typically six units each) and degrees are built from curriculum blocks of 24 units, meaning a standard bachelor’s degree usually comprises 96 units (up to 16 courses) or 72 units (12 courses) if a 24‑unit block is not used, while a flexible three‑year professional degree may combine discipline‑specific units, a professional block, and at least 24 elective units; credit for prior study or relevant work experience can be applied to reduce the number of units a student must complete, and every course includes clearly written learning outcomes that are aligned with the Australian Qualifications Framework, the Higher Education Standards Framework and ANU’s graduate attributes, including mandatory outcomes related to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander knowledges and critical thinking, ensuring that graduates demonstrate the knowledge, skills and competencies expected of ANU’s interdisciplinary and research‑oriented curriculum.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The Australian National University (ANU) is ranked 32nd in the world in the QS World University Rankings 2026 (fourth in Australia) and is similarly placed among the global top‑50 in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2026, while its subject performance is outstanding: in the 2026 QS by‑subject tables ANU holds nine positions in the global Top 20 and leads Australia in eleven narrow subjects, including Philosophy (4th), Anthropology (9th), Archaeology (10th), Development Studies (14th), Politics & International Studies (14th), History (17th), Sociology (19th), Earth & Marine Sciences (20th) and Geophysics (20th); in THE’s 2026 subject rankings ANU is 35th globally for Social Sciences, 39th for Law, 53rd for Arts & Humanities, 60th for Physical Sciences, 70th for Business & Economics, 83rd for Computer Science, 87th for Life Sciences, 91st for Medical & Health, 96th for Psychology and placed in the 101‑125 band for Engineering, and the university is fully accredited by Australia’s Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) with its professional programmes holding the relevant external accreditations (e.g., law by the Law Society, engineering by Engineers Australia); roughly 30 % of ANU’s student body are international students, placing it among the Australian universities with the highest overseas enrolment rates.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The subclass 500 student visa for international students at the Australian National University requires proof of sufficient funds, with the post‑10 May 2024 thresholds set at AUD 29,710 for the primary applicant, AUD 10,394 for a spouse or de‑facto partner, AUD 4,449 per dependent child and an additional AUD 13,502 for annual school costs, while personal annual income must be at least AUD 87,856 for a single applicant or AUD 102,500 if a family unit is included; applicants must also maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire duration of their stay, remain enrolled and attend classes with satisfactory progress, and they are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while their course is in session (with unlimited hours during scheduled breaks), although students undertaking a master's by research or a doctoral degree have no work‑hour limits. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) Post‑Higher Education Work stream lets ANU graduates stay in Australia with full work rights for typically two years if you hold a bachelor’s degree or a master's by coursework, three years for a master’s by research and four years for a PhD (Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders can stay up to five years); you must apply within six months of receiving your final qualification, can include your partner and children, and are allowed unrestricted work hours in any sector.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Australian National University provides a robust work‑integrated learning (WIL) framework that includes paid and unpaid internship, co‑op and placement opportunities across most faculties – notably a three‑stream Computing Internship Program for undergraduate and postgraduate students, and a 120‑ to 240‑hour CBE Internship Programme offered twice yearly that counts for credit and is advertised through ANU Career Central – and these experiences are linked to strong graduate outcomes: the university reports that more than 90 % of its graduates are employed or engaged in further study within four months of completing their degree, with international students benefiting equally and often securing roles in sectors such as technology (IBM, Google, Microsoft, Intel), consulting (PwC, Accenture, Deloitte), finance (National Australia Bank, Citi, Bloomberg), government and academia; engineering alumni frequently move into accredited engineering positions, while computing graduates enter software development, data science, artificial intelligence and related fields, reflecting the high employability and diverse career pathways that ANU’s internship and placement programmes support. Lương & thị trường: Graduates in Australia who enter high‑paying, high‑demand fields such as mining engineering, medical studies and general medicine enjoy the strongest salary outcomes, with starting salaries well above the national average and earnings typically rising about 35 % within five years of graduation; engineering graduates also see strong labour‑market linkage, with roughly 60 % securing professional engineering roles, while education graduates achieve a 72 % transition rate into teaching positions. By contrast, international graduates earn noticeably less than their domestic peers—most clearly in business and management where the median full‑time salary is around $12,000 lower—and are more likely to be employed in jobs that do not fully utilise their qualifications, a pattern driven by visa‑related work‑hour limits and employer preferences for domestic candidates in professional and managerial occupations.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The Australian National University’s International Office serves as the central hub for international student support, coordinating pre‑arrival advice, a dedicated orientation programme that includes tailored welcome sessions, online resources and a series of welcome emails, as well as a free airport pickup service during Orientation Week; all first‑year undergraduate and postgraduate international students are guaranteed a place in on‑campus accommodation where residence advisors provide 24‑hour wellbeing support, easy access to campus facilities and public transport, and the university also offers comprehensive career services through its student support network, including career‑focused learning, career information and counselling, academic skills workshops and connections to industry‑linked employment opportunities. Đời sống & môi trường: International students at the Australian National University enjoy a vibrant campus life in Canberra, with more than 50 clubs and societies—including ANUSA‑run student groups, the SET4ANU mentoring program that pairs newcomers with experienced peers, and a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association that organises cultural evenings, language‑exchange meet‑ups and trips to local Vietnamese community events; many embassies also host gatherings that help Vietnamese students connect with compatriots. Safety is a priority on campus: the ANU Security Services operate 24 hours a day (phone 6125 2249), offer a security‑escort service for anyone travelling alone after hours, and provide clear guidelines such as staying in well‑lit areas, moving in pairs at night and keeping emergency contacts programmed into your phone, while the university’s “How to stay safe” resources and regular safety briefings ensure students know how to report incidents promptly and access support whenever needed.
