**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Tohoku University accepts international applicants who are at least 18 years old and whose academic abilities are judged equivalent to those of a Japanese high‑school graduate; you must submit proof of graduation (or a certificate of expected graduation) and an official academic transcript that includes your GPA and an explanation of the grading scale. Admission to most undergraduate courses (including the Future Global Leadership program) is based on meeting the required subject standards through one of the recognized examinations – International Baccalaureate (with higher‑level Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry), SAT, GCE A‑Level (with Mathematics and any two of Physics, Chemistry, Biology), Cambridge Pre‑U, ACT, AP, or the Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU). For the EJU, you must obtain a score equal to or higher than the program’s threshold in Mathematics (Course 2) and in any two of the science subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Biology); the Japanese language test is not required. In addition, English proficiency must be demonstrated with TOEFL‑iBT, TOEFL‑PBT or IELTS scores that meet the program’s minimum. No general interview or portfolio is required for the standard science‑oriented programs, although certain specialized majors (e.g., art, design, architecture) may request an interview or a portfolio as part of the selection process.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Tohoku University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency with a minimum IELTS overall band of 6.0 (online version accepted), TOEFL iBT 79 (or TOEFL PBT 550), TOEIC 730, or an equivalent CEFR B2 level (e.g., Cambridge CAE C, Cambridge FCE B2, etc.); the Duolingo English Test is not accepted for the exchange program. The English requirement is waived for students who are currently enrolled in, or have completed high school or university studies in Australia, Canada, New Zealand, South Africa, Singapore, the United Kingdom, or the United States. Japanese‑language proficiency (e.g., JLPT) is only required for programs taught in Japanese and does not affect the English‑language waiver.

**4. Học phí**

- For international undergraduate students at Tohoku University the required fees are ¥17 000 for the application, ¥282 000 as the enrollment (admission) fee, and a tuition charge of ¥535 800 per academic year (the standard rate for most faculties; law school tuition is ¥804 000 per year and the school of accounting charges ¥589 300 per year). The total amount payable in the first year therefore amounts to roughly ¥834 800 (¥17 000 + ¥282 000 + ¥535 800). For international graduate students the application fee is ¥30 000, the enrollment fee remains ¥282 000 and tuition is also ¥535 800 per year, giving a first‑year total of about ¥847 800 (¥30 000 + ¥282 000 + ¥535 800). Additional fees such as accident and liability insurance are modest (around ¥4 660 for new students in non‑medical faculties) and accommodation costs range from ¥10 000‑¥22 800 per month for on‑campus single rooms to ¥40 000‑¥70 000 per month for private off‑campus housing.

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: International students admitted to Tohoku University must obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) from the Immigration Services Agency before applying for a student visa; the university’s International Support Center issues the COE electronically, it is valid for three months from issuance and the screening process typically takes 1‑3 months, so applicants should start as soon as their travel plans are confirmed, and the visa application—including the COE, passport, visa‑application form and photos—must be submitted to the nearest Japanese embassy or consulate using the official forms and document lists posted on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs’s studyinjapan.go.jp site; applicants must demonstrate sufficient financial support such as scholarship awards, personal savings, a guarantor’s statement or a combination thereof, and while holding a “Student” residence status they are allowed to work up to 28 hours per week (or 40 hours during university holidays) provided they obtain written permission from Tohoku University and register the employment with the immigration office, otherwise any unauthorized work is prohibited. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Tohoku University who wish to remain in Japan to look for a job must change their residence status from “Student” to “Designated Activities (job‑hunting)”; the visa is granted for an initial six‑month period and, if the applicant can demonstrate ongoing job‑search activities, a single renewal is allowed, allowing a maximum stay of up to one year. Eligibility requires having earned a degree from a Japanese university or graduate school (graduation from a language school alone is not sufficient), obtaining a recommendation letter from the university’s International Affairs office, and providing evidence of job‑hunting efforts and sufficient financial means to support oneself during the stay.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Tohoku University provides extensive career‑support services for international students, including a dedicated Google Classroom, regular job‑hunting workshops (offered in both Japanese and English), and a calendar of company information sessions and internship opportunities that align with the Japanese “shukatsu” hiring timeline; these programs enable students to secure internships and co‑op placements with a wide range of Japanese firms, particularly in engineering, information technology, pharmaceuticals, and research‑related fields, and the university reports that roughly 95 % of its graduates—international and domestic combined—obtain employment or graduate‑school admission within six months of graduation, with many international alumni entering multinational corporations, Japanese tech companies, and academic research positions. Lương & thị trường: Japan’s graduate labour market is currently driven by strong demand for engineers, IT and AI specialists, data‑science analysts, and health‑care professionals, with typical starting salaries ranging from ¥3.5 million to ¥4.5 million a year and engineering or AI positions often reaching ¥5 million; international graduates from Tohoku University benefit from a high employment success rate of roughly 92 % within three months of graduation, most securing positions in engineering, technology firms, and consulting roles linked to social‑science expertise, and while overall retention of international alumni is about 31 %, engineering graduates see a slightly higher retention of around 32 %, reflecting the university’s expanded AI and global‑leadership programs and strong industry partnerships.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Tohoku University’s International Support Center (often called “TU Support”) provides a comprehensive suite of services for international students, including an orientation program held twice a year in April and October that introduces newcomers to campus life, academic procedures and local resources; a dedicated Help Desk staffed by multilingual student assistants who can assist in English, Japanese and several native languages with everything from daily‑life questions to translation and phone‑call support, operating in person at the Education and Student Support Center on Kawauchi North Campus, the International OASIS on Kawauchi South Campus, the Aobayama Commons entrance, and the School of Engineering Center Hall on Aobayama East Campus (with online access via Google Classroom); housing support through the International Support Center’s guidance on finding accommodation and related paperwork, coordinated with the university’s residential services; a Career Support Center that offers seminars, workshops and one‑on‑one counseling to help students plan post‑graduation employment or further study; and a tutoring‑type academic assistance program through the Learning Support Center that provides Japanese conversation practice, academic writing help and other study‑skill support specifically for international students. Đời sống & môi trường: Tohoku University offers a vibrant campus‑life environment for international students, with free campus‑bus service, a wide range of officially registered clubs and circles (organized under the Students’ Friendship Association – Gakuyu‑kai) covering sports, culture, research and hobby interests, and an International Support Center that assists with housing, health care, language support and everyday life in Sendai; the university also provides comprehensive safety information, requiring new students to enroll in mandatory personal‑liability and accident insurance, urging caution when joining any group and recommending verification of a club’s registration status before participation, while unregistered groups are discouraged due to potential security risks; among the many student‑run organizations, there is an active Vietnamese Student Association that regularly holds gatherings, cultural events, language‑exchange sessions and peer‑mentoring for Vietnamese and other international students, helping newcomers integrate, share information about campus resources, and navigate safety guidelines, and all clubs and circles are listed on the Campus Life webpage where students can browse and join activities that match their interests.
