### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International candidates can join École Polytechnique either through the FUI (International University Track) entrance exam for the engineering cycle or via the Master of Science & Technology programmes; the engineering intake begins in September 2027 with applications opened online from mid‑May 2026 and closing in mid‑September 2026 (candidates must have completed at least two years of university science/engineering study and meet French defence‑minister fitness requirements, may submit a maximum of two applications and must upload all required documents by the September deadline), while the master’s intake starts in September 2026 with the application portal opening on 17 September 2025 and four selection rounds – Round 1 deadline 13 October 2025, Round 2 deadline 12 January 2026, Round 3 deadline 9 March 2026, Round 4 deadline 22 April 2026 – after which admissibility juries, interviews and admission decisions follow; the master’s application fee is €90 (paid by credit‑card or electronic bank transfer, non‑refundable), and the step‑by‑step procedure is to create an account on the Polytechnique application site, complete the online form, upload all required documents, pay the fee, then await the admissibility jury result, interview invitation and final admission notice.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to École Polytechnique’s Bachelor program must be completing or have completed a recognized high‑school leaving certificate (such as the French Baccalaureate, International Baccalaureate, British A‑levels, Swiss Maturité or equivalent European diploma) with a strong overall academic average, advanced mathematics courses and at least one other advanced science subject; while there is no formal minimum GPA, candidates are expected to have a very high academic standing. For the exchange‑student route, a cumulative GPA of at least 3.0 on a 4.0 scale is required, along with proof of excellent mathematics ability and sufficient language skills. All applicants must demonstrate English proficiency at CEFR level C1, typically via TOEFL iBT ≥ 90, IELTS ≥ 6.5 (with a minimum of 6 in each component), or an equivalent Cambridge C1/C2 qualification, and provide official transcripts (translated into English or French if necessary), a copy of their passport, a CV, and a personal statement; no portfolio or interview is normally required, though some specialised tracks may ask for additional documentation or recommendations.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- École Polytechnique requires all non‑native‑English international applicants to prove full English proficiency with an internationally recognised test; the only accepted exams are the IELTS (Academic), the TOEFL iBT and the Cambridge English Advanced (CAE) or Proficiency (CPE) exams. For the Bachelor programme the minimum scores are IELTS 6.5 overall (with at least 6.0 in each skill) and TOEFL iBT 90, while the Master of Science & Technology programme requires IELTS 7.0 overall and TOEFL iBT 95; the Cambridge tests must reach a C1 level (CAE B‑176, CPE C‑200). PTE and Duolingo are not accepted. An English‑test waiver is granted only if the applicant’s native language is English (as evidenced by passport or national ID) or if the applicant’s most recent degree was obtained from an institution where all instruction was delivered in English, with official confirmation of the language of instruction on the degree certificate or transcript.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at École Polytechnique pay tuition in euros; for the three‑year Bachelor of Science the fee is €19 200 per year (EU students €15 900), while most specialised master programmes charge €15 400 annually for external students (the AI‑for‑Markets and Quantitative Investment master is €19 000 per year); the Ingénieur Polytechnicien engineering programme costs €7 000 for the first year and €15 500 for each of the second and third years, totalling €38 000 for the three‑year track (EU students pay €3 500, €7 750 and €7 750 respectively, i.e., €19 000 in total). In addition to tuition, every student must pay the French CVEC (Contribution Vie Étudiante et de Campus) of €100 per academic year, which functions as a registration/administrative fee; no other mandatory fees are listed, though scholarships or industrial agreements may reduce tuition in specific cases.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students admitted to École Polytechnique’s Bachelor of Science program must hold a French‑recognised secondary school diploma (or equivalent) with strong proficiency in mathematics and a sufficient level of French (or be prepared to follow the dedicated preparatory year that combines intensive French language, cultural immersion and foundational scientific courses); the three‑year curriculum is built around 180 ECTS (≈ 60 ECTS per year) and follows a double‑major structure where students choose two complementary tracks among Mathematics, Physics, Economics and Computer Science, beginning with a first‑year “Human Training” phase that includes French language lessons, introductory scientific modules and cultural activities, followed by a second‑year period that blends core technical courses with a mandatory in‑company internship, and a third‑year research‑oriented phase offering advanced electives (e.g., Machine Learning, Quantum Physics, Econometrics, Molecular Genetics, etc.), a possible exchange semester abroad, and a final scientific option project; the program’s learning outcomes are to provide a rigorous foundation in quantitative and analytical skills, interdisciplinary problem‑solving abilities, fluency in French and English, hands‑on research experience, and the credentials required for entry into French or international master’s programmes.

- École Polytechnique welcomes a highly international community, with roughly 41 % of its students coming from over 100 nationalities, and it has been awarded the three‑star “Bienvenue en France” label and the three‑star “Qualité Français Langue Étrangère” certification for its campus services; in the QS World University Rankings 2026 the Institut Polytechnique de Paris – of which École Polytechnique is a founding member – is placed 41st globally and 2nd in France (behind PSL), while in the QS 2023 rankings it was 48th worldwide and 2nd in France and ranked 11th worldwide for graduate employability, and its MSc&T in Data Science for Business was ranked 3rd worldwide among comparable programs; in the Times Higher Education (THE) rankings the institute was ranked 34th worldwide and 1st in France in 2022 and 48th worldwide (2nd in France) in 2023, with a 26th‑place position in THE’s employability‑focused list of 250 universities; subject‑specific QS rankings also list IP Paris among the top 30 global institutions in four disciplines and top 50 in four others, and the school’s engineering degree is fully accredited by the French Commission des Titres d’Ingénieur (CTI) and carries the EUR‑ACE label for European engineering standards.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: École Polytechnique actively supports its international students in securing internships, apprenticeships, and post‑graduation employment through a combination of dedicated career services, an extensive alumni network, and structured mentorship programs; each year the International Affairs Office coordinates an International Internship Program that connects students with research topics across the school’s 23 joint laboratories in fields such as mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, mechanics and economics, while the alumni‑run “l’AX” network offers mentorship, one‑time financial aid and professional introductions that help students integrate into French culture and the global job market, and the school’s career service, in partnership with the alumni, promotes graduate outcomes that consistently rank among the highest in France, with roughly 95 % of graduates—both French and international—finding employment or continuing to PhD studies within six months of completing the Ingénieur Polytechnicien program, many entering leading multinational companies, research institutions, finance, consulting or launching startups. Lương & thị trường: In France, recent official surveys show that graduates from the country’s top Grandes Écoles earn an average gross annual salary of roughly €39 000, while École Polytechnique graduates report about €53 000, with managers earning around €1 700 more than engineers; the labour market demand highlighted by France Travail and APEC concentrates on ICT occupations such as software developers, data scientists, cybersecurity and cloud‑computing engineers, followed by health‑care professionals, construction trades, industrial engineers and technicians, commerce and sales roles, and logistics/transport positions; about 11 % of graduates work abroad, and Polytechnique’s latest cohort indicates strong international mobility, especially to the United Kingdom, the United States and France, with 36 % pursuing data‑science, mathematics or physics and 27 % entering management, finance or economics, while APEC focuses on senior‑level vacancies and France Travail offers public training programmes to address these skill shortages.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: École Polytechnique provides a dedicated international student office called BasiX, which operates a Welcome Desk that assists incoming students and researchers with all administrative formalities such as residence permits, medical insurance and campus housing, and organizes orientation activities including a Welcome Week with airport reception, administrative procedures, cultural outings and team‑building events; the school also offers free on‑campus accommodation (or a host‑family placement for most students), extensive French‑language instruction (up to 375 hours over 17 weeks) and integration programmes, while its career service, closely linked with the alumni network and the X‑Job Board, supports students in job searching, internships and professional development throughout their studies. Đời sống & môi trường: École Polytechnique hosts a highly international campus, with roughly 40‑45 % of its 3,900 students coming from more than 100 countries, and offers a vibrant student life that includes dozens of clubs covering sports, culture, technology, arts and social causes; these associations are open to all students and are coordinated through the school’s student life office, while the dedicated Welcome Desk (BasiX) provides practical assistance on housing, residence permits, medical insurance and integration activities such as cultural outings and team‑building events. Safety is ensured by an on‑site security service, 24‑hour campus police, well‑lit residential areas, emergency call points and a health centre that offers first‑aid and mental‑health support, and the school’s “Bienvenue en France” label attests to the high quality of its reception and safety standards. Vietnamese students are represented by a Vietnamese student association that organises gatherings, cultural celebrations (such as Tet), language practice sessions and networking events, linking newcomers with senior Vietnamese alumni and helping them navigate academic, social and administrative matters; the association works closely with the international office and other multicultural clubs to foster inclusion and a sense of community for Vietnamese and other international students alike.
