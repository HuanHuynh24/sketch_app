**Tóm tắt thông tin trường**

- Hanyang University (한양대학교) is a private research university in South Korea, with its main Seoul campus located in Seongdong‑gu, Seoul and a secondary ERICA campus in Sangnok‑gu, Ansan, Gyeonggi‑do; its official website is https://www.hanyang.ac.kr (English portal at https://www.hanyang.ac.kr/web/eng).

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Hanyang University admits international students for two main intakes each year: the Spring semester (which starts in March) and the Fall semester (which starts in September). Applications for the Spring intake are accepted online from 1 October to 30 November, and those for the Fall intake are accepted from 1 April to 31 May; the respective deadlines for submitting all required documents are the last day of each application window. The online application is made through the university’s HY‑in portal, where applicants create an account, fill out the admission form, and upload required files such as a certified transcript, proof of language proficiency, a study‑plan or personal statement, a recommendation letter (if required), and a copy of the passport; after the portal submission, a printed copy of the application form and any original documents must be sent to the department office for final verification. Once the documents are approved, the university announces acceptance within two weeks, after which admitted students pay the application fee (approximately KRW 45,000 ≈ US $35 for undergraduate programmes and KRW 80,000 ≈ US $62 for graduate programmes) and the tuition deposit, obtain a D‑2‑8 student visa, and complete course registration before the semester begins. **2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Hanyang University accepts international undergraduate and graduate students through its Visiting, Exchange, and ERICA/Seoul international programs, and the basic entry criteria are that applicants must be currently enrolled in a higher‑education institution (or hold a completed bachelor’s degree for graduate study), cannot be in their final semester or have graduation status pending, and must submit official academic transcripts (học bạ) that are typically required to be notarized or apostilled for visa processing; for most long‑term exchange placements a cumulative GPA of at least 2.5 on Hanyang’s 4.5‑point scale (including any F grades) is required (graduate applicants must also show a combined undergraduate‑graduate GPA of 2.5 or higher, and transfer students need a combined GPA of 2.5 from both institutions), while short‑term exchanges accept a minimum GPA of 2.0, the Visiting Student Program even waives GPA and language prerequisites; language proficiency scores (e.g., TOEFL, IELTS, or TOPIK) are required for programs taught in the respective language, and certain departments such as Architecture, Fine Arts, Design, or Media may request a portfolio and/or an interview as part of the assessment, with the specific subject‑related prerequisites (môn yêu cầu) varying by major and detailed on the program’s application portal.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- Hanyang University requires international students to demonstrate English proficiency unless they are nationals of an English‑speaking country (U.S., U.K., Canada, Australia, Ireland, etc.), which exempts them from testing; for all other applicants the minimum scores are TOEFL iBT 80 (or 89 for the International Studies track) and IELTS Academic 6.0 (or 6.5 for International Studies), while TOEFL iBT 75 (or IELTS 6.0) is accepted for exchange programmes and TOEFL ITP 550 may be used for certain partner universities in Europe, South America or other Asian countries; English scores are optional for Korean‑track applicants. Korean language proficiency is not mandatory for English‑track students but can be submitted as an optional document, with TOPIK, TOPIK Speaking or TOPIK‑IBT certificates accepted (they must be valid after the application start date); Korean‑track applicants are expected to provide Korean language proof. Hanyang does not list PTE, Duolingo or other English tests as accepted alternatives, so waivers are only granted based on citizenship from an English‑speaking country or when a partner university’s specific requirements allow omission of the English test.

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): The D‑2 student visa is the standard entry permit for international degree‑seeking students at Hanyang University and must be applied for through the Korean embassy or consulate before arrival using the official application form downloadable from the HiKorea portal (studyinkorea.go.kr); required documents include a Certificate of Admission and Business Certificate issued by Hanyang, a valid passport, recent color photo, completed visa application form, health‑insurance certificate, and a Financial Statement that demonstrates sufficient funds for living expenses and tuition—typically a bank balance of at least USD 20,000 (or the equivalent in Korean won) together with proof of tuition payment or a scholarship award; after entering Korea the student must obtain an Alien Registration Card (ARC) within 90 days, which then authorizes a legal stay and permits part‑time employment of up to 20 hours per week (or up to 40 hours during university holidays) provided the student obtains written permission from the university’s International Affairs office and the immigration office, while on‑campus work and internships related to the field of study are also allowed under the same ARC‑based work‑permit rules. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): The D‑10 “Job Seeker” visa is the official bridge for international graduates who have completed a degree at a Korean university such as Hanyang University and wish to remain in South Korea to look for employment, and it can be applied for within six months of graduation (or within three years for TOPIK‑4‑qualified Korean‑university alumni); Korean‑university graduates are exempt from the usual financial‑capacity requirement, and the standard D‑10 stay is up to one year (with a six‑month stay for applicants scoring 60–79 points under the points‑based system), during which paid internships are allowed and must be reported within 14 days; once a qualified job offer is secured, the holder can change status to an E‑7 work visa without leaving the country, provided the employing company submits the required documentation and the occupation matches the E‑7 eligibility list, allowing the graduate to work legally in Korea after completing their studies.
