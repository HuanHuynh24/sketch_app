### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students who wish to study at the University of Auckland should begin their application as early as possible to allow time for visa processing, and must submit their online Application for Admission through the university’s portal (www.auckland.ac.nz/apply_now) together with the required supporting documents and an application fee; the fee is NZ $85 if the applicant’s highest qualification is from an overseas secondary school (including the International Baccalaureate) or NZ $100 if it is from an overseas tertiary institution, while applications made through a registered agent, an articulation agreement, or the NZ Aid Scholarship incur no fee. For 2026, undergraduate and postgraduate programmes follow the university’s published closing dates: applications for Semester One must be received by 8 December 2025 (any later submissions are only considered if places remain) and for Semester Two by 4 July 2026 (with the same condition), and international applicants are advised to meet these deadlines to ensure full processing of their admission and visa requirements.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [2026 Closing Dates for Admission – University of Auckland](https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/about-us/about-the-university/the-university/official-publications/university-calendar/current-calendar/key-university-dates/closing-dates-for-admission.html) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Application Fee information – University of Auckland](https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/study/applications-and-admissions/how-to-apply/application-fees.html) (Cao, score 0.99)<br>[Undergraduate application closing dates – University of Auckland](https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/study/applications-and-admissions/how-to-apply/undergraduate-application-closing-dates.html) (Cao, score 0.99)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to the University of Auckland must hold an acceptable university‑entrance qualification such as the New Zealand NCEA, with typical entry thresholds ranging from 90 to 120 NCEA points (often requiring a minimum of 14‑16 points in each of three approved Level 3 subjects, including English Literature or English Language and the subject(s) relevant to the programme), and must submit official transcripts (học bạ) showing the full record of results; most undergraduate programmes also require a minimum English proficiency of IELTS 6.0 overall (no band below 5.5) or an approved equivalent test, while postgraduate courses list specific GPA requirements (e.g., a GPA of 4.0–5.0 on the 7‑point scale for many master’s programmes) and may demand a recognised Bachelor’s degree in a relevant field; additional entry criteria can include subject‑specific prerequisites (such as mathematics for engineering or biology for health sciences), and for creative, design or architecture programmes a portfolio is often required and some professional programmes may also conduct an interview to assess suitability.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Entry requirements - University of Auckland](https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/study/international-students/entry-requirements-international-students.html) (Cao, score 0.74)<br>[International Students Entry Requirements & Fees - UoA Online](https://www.online.auckland.ac.nz/international/) (Cao, score 0.70)<br>[International Students - University of Auckland](https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/about-us/about-the-university/the-university/official-publications/university-calendar/current-calendar/academic-statutes-and-regulations/international-students.html) (Cao, score 0.69)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to the University of Auckland must demonstrate English proficiency, with the standard minimum scores set at an overall IELTS 6.0 (no band below 5.5) for undergraduate study and an overall IELTS 6.5 (no band below 6.0) for postgraduate study, although individual faculties may require higher scores; equivalent TOEFL iBT scores are 79 – 80 (undergraduate) and 88 – 90 (postgraduate) and PTE Academic scores are 58 (undergraduate) and 64 (postgraduate), and applicants whose results fall just short may be granted a waiver – for example, an IELTS overall score up to 0.5‑1.0 points below the requirement is acceptable if the reading and writing bands meet the minimum, a TOEFL overall score up to 2 points lower is allowed if the writing component meets the minimum, and a PTE overall score up to 8 points lower is permitted if the communicative score meets the threshold – while Duolingo English Test is not listed as an approved test, alternative pathways such as successful completion of the Foundation Certificate in English for Academic Purposes, the English Pathway for Undergraduate Studies or the English Pathway for Postgraduate Studies are also accepted for meeting the English language requirement.

**4. Học phí**

- For international undergraduate students at the University of Auckland in 2026, tuition fees range from roughly NZ $40,496 per year for the Bachelor of Sport, Health and Physical Education up to NZ $86,561 per year for the Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (Year 2 onward), with most programmes falling between NZ $42,000 and NZ $55,000 annually; in addition to tuition, students pay a compulsory Student Services Fee of NZ $1,108.80 per year, an application fee of NZ $150, a health and travel insurance fee of NZ $449.50 per semester for study‑abroad students, and visa application fees that vary by nationality, all amounts being in New Zealand dollars.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at the University of Auckland should budget roughly NZ $22,000 – $24,000 per year, which covers all major living expenses: accommodation in university residences runs about NZ $350‑$430 per week (approximately NZ $18,200‑$22,400 annually) or NZ $7,000‑$10,000 per semester; food and groceries average NZ $69 per week (≈ NZ $3,600 per year); public transport costs about NZ $21.50 per week (≈ NZ $1,120 per year); the required health and travel insurance is NZ $449.50 per semester (≈ NZ $900 per year); and a modest allowance of around NZ $1,500 for textbooks, entertainment and other study‑related costs brings the total to the cited range.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Auckland is the highest‑ranked New Zealand university, holding the 65th position in the 2026 QS World University Rankings and 152nd in the 2025 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, and it scores strongly in the THE indicator for international students; in the 2026 QS subject tables the university places within the global top 30 for Archaeology (rank 28), Psychology (rank 28), and Education (rank 29), and within the top 50 for Sports‑related subjects (33), Civil & Structural Engineering (48), Anatomy & Physiology (49), Linguistics (49), and English Language & Literature (50), while also entering the top 100 for Data Science and Artificial Intelligence for the first time, and all its programmes are fully accredited by New Zealand’s Ministry of Education and relevant professional bodies such as Engineering New Zealand, the New Zealand Dental Council, and the Medical Council of New Zealand, ensuring that degrees meet national quality standards and professional accreditation requirements.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The University of Auckland’s Fee‑Paying Student Visa requires you to submit an official Offer of Place together with a tuition receipt or funding letter and clear evidence that you have sufficient funds to cover the full cost of your course plus living expenses for the duration of your stay (typically a minimum of NZ $15 000 per year in addition to tuition, shown through recent bank statements, a scholarship letter, or a sponsor’s guarantee); you must also provide proof of health insurance, a valid passport, a recent photograph, and any required health or character documents, and you will need a RealMe login to complete the application on the Immigration New Zealand (INZ) website. Once the visa is granted you are permitted to work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full‑time during scheduled holidays, although the visa is not contingent on you taking a job. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed an acceptable New Zealand qualification at the University of Auckland can apply for a Post‑Study Work Visa, provided the qualification is at least NZQCF level 7 (bachelor degree or higher) or an eligible lower‑level qualification on the immigration list, was studied full‑time for a minimum of 30 weeks (including study breaks but excluding holiday breaks), and the graduate holds a valid student visa that was held at the time of study; the visa allows open work rights for up to three years, with the exact duration (one, two or three years) depending on the level of the qualification, whether the graduate is working toward occupational registration and the timing of their student visa, and it costs NZD 1,670, can be processed within about five weeks, and may provide a pathway to resident status.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: University of Auckland graduates enjoy very strong labour‑market outcomes, with the overall employment rate for survey respondents remaining above 94 % in recent years (94 % in 2024, 96 % in 2023 and 97 % in 2021) and a median first‑year salary consistently in the NZD 60 000–69 999 bracket. Employment rates are especially high in health‑related disciplines, where Medical and Health Sciences graduates report a 99 % employment rate and Engineering graduates a 96‑99 % rate depending on level, while Arts, Business and Economics, Law and Creative Arts also exceed 90 % employment. International (overseas) undergraduate alumni showed a 92.7 % employment rate in 2021, indicating comparable success to domestic peers. The university embeds work‑integrated learning across most faculties, offering structured internships, co‑op placements and industry projects that are coordinated through dedicated career services and faculty partners; a large share of graduates secure such placements during their studies, which contributes to the high full‑time/part‑time employment outcomes and facilitates entry into a wide range of careers ranging from professional practice and research to corporate, government and entrepreneurial roles. Lương & thị trường: Graduates of the University of Auckland enjoy a very high employment rate, with 97 % of the 2019‑2021 cohort in full‑time or part‑time work, and the median annual salary for the 2021 respondents falling in the NZD 60,000–69,999 band, up from the NZD 50,000–60,000 range reported in earlier surveys; the labour market shows strong demand for international graduates in high‑growth occupations such as information technology, engineering, health and medical sciences, as well as finance and business services, where employers like Xero, Fonterra and major banks actively recruit, meaning that graduates from the University of Auckland can generally expect to secure well‑paid positions in these sectors shortly after completing their studies.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Auckland’s International Office runs a dedicated International Orientation programme each semester to help new overseas students settle in, offering pre‑departure and visa webinars, health‑and‑travel‑insurance guidance and practical advice on finding accommodation and navigating campus life, while also linking students to the university’s Career Development and Employability Services (CDES) – including career tools, the MyCDES+ job board and one‑to‑one employability support – and to housing support resources that assist with short‑term and long‑term rentals; the office can be reached by phone at +64 9 923 1969, via email at int‑questions@auckland.ac.nz, or in person at Alfred Nathan House, 24 Princes Street, Auckland. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Auckland provides a vibrant campus life for international students that includes a broad array of clubs and societies—among them the Vietnamese Student Association, which organises cultural festivals, language‑exchange evenings and social gatherings to connect Vietnamese students and offer a supportive community—as well as numerous sports, arts, academic and interest‑based groups accessible through the Student Hub; safety is a priority with a dedicated campus security team, 24‑hour emergency contact numbers, comprehensive policies on harassment, bullying and sexual assault, plus free health and counselling services, while international‑student support offers orientation programmes, language assistance, mentorship, part‑time‑job advice and on‑campus accommodation options, all designed to help Vietnamese and other overseas students integrate, make friends and enjoy a secure, engaging university experience.
