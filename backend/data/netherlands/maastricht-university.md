- International applicants to Maastricht University first register their study choice in the national Studielink system (open each year on 1 October) and then complete the university’s MyApplication portal where they upload documents, pay the application fee (typically €100 for EU/EEA applicants and €150 for non‑EU/EEA applicants) and receive a student number; for a September start the full application (including payment and all required documents) must be completed by 1 May 2026 for non‑EU/EEA students and by 1 June 2026 for EU/EEA/Swiss students (for bachelor programmes the corresponding deadlines are 1 February 2026 and 1 April 2026), after which Maastricht sends the file to the Dutch Immigration and Naturalisation Service (IND) before 1 July and the visa/legal‑residence file must be finished with the UM Visa Office by 30 June 2026; for programmes that start in February the deadlines are shifted later, with the application to be completed by 1 December 2026 for non‑EU/EEA and by 1 August 2026 for EU/EEA/Swiss applicants, the IND submission required before 1 December and the visa file due by 30 November 2026, and a strongly recommended tuition‑fee payment deadline of 1 August 2026 for both intakes.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Maastricht University requires all non‑native‑English‑speaking applicants to demonstrate proficiency in English, typically by submitting an IELTS, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic or Duolingo English Test score; the usual minimums are an overall IELTS score of 6.5 (with no band lower than 6.0), a TOEFL iBT score of at least 90, a PTE Academic score of 58, or a Duolingo English Test score of 115. Applicants whose most recent diploma was obtained from an institution where English is the language of instruction, or who have completed a prior degree taught entirely in English, are generally exempt from providing an additional test result, and similar waivers may be granted for certain nationalities or programmes that accept other proof of English competence.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at Maastricht University must progress at least 50 % of the yearly credit load, meaning they must earn a minimum of 30 ECTS each academic year to retain their residence permit, and in the first year they are required to obtain at least 45 out of 60 ECTS to avoid a negative Binding Study Advice; the university’s programmes are organised into semester‑based modules, each assigned a specific credit value (typically 5 ECTS per course) and a clear set of intended learning outcomes that describe the knowledge, skills and competencies students should acquire, with most courses listing any prerequisite requirements (often none, though some advanced modules may require completion of earlier courses or a waiver based on prior experience); credit transfer is allowed for up to 60 ECTS from other recognised higher‑education institutions, but transferred credits do not automatically shorten the study duration and may affect course selection, while the curriculum includes detailed syllabi that outline the course description, learning resources, teaching activities, assessment methods and the explicit learning outcomes for each module.

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: Non‑EU students who intend to study at Maastricht University for more than 90 days must obtain a Dutch long‑stay entry visa (MVV) and a regular residence permit (VVR); both are arranged by the university’s Visa Office at no charge to the student aside from the IND legal fee of about €254 (which covers the VVR and, if required, the MVV). The applicant must present an unconditional admission letter, valid health insurance, and proof of sufficient financial resources – the IND sets the minimum at roughly €13 000 per year (≈ €1 083 per month) and accepts a blocked‑account statement, a scholarship or guarantee letter from a recognised sponsor such as a Nuffic‑approved institution; the amount is indexed annually. After the university forwards the application, the IND typically processes the MVV/VVR within 60–90 days, after which the MVV sticker is collected at the Dutch embassy or consulate and the VVR is issued as a biometric card valid for the duration of the study programme. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Orientation Year (Zoekjaar) residence permit allows recent graduates—those who have completed an accredited bachelor’s, master’s or PhD programme at a Dutch higher‑education institution such as Maastricht University, or who have graduated from a designated foreign university that ranks within the top 200 according to recognised lists and is validated by Nuffic—to stay in the Netherlands for up to one year to look for a job, start a business or work without a separate work permit; the permit is not extendable, must be applied for within three years of graduation, can be obtained only once per person, requires proof of sufficient financial means (roughly €1,200‑€1,500 per month), and the IND aims to decide the application within 90 days, after which the holder can transition to a highly‑skilled migrant (kennismigrant) permit or other residence status.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Maastricht University integrates internships, co‑op and placement modules into most bachelor’s and master’s programmes, offering international students optional part‑time thesis internships that run for three to six months (starting in September or February) and can be undertaken in the Netherlands or abroad; the university’s career services support students in securing these positions and in translating the experience into graduate employment, and recent graduate outcome data show that roughly 94 % of Maastricht graduates secure paid work within six months to a year after graduation, with many entering sectors such as health care, finance, consulting, technology, research and non‑governmental organisations, reflecting strong employability across a wide range of “ngành nghề sau tốt nghiệp.” Lương & thị trường: International graduates in the Netherlands who enter fields such as education, healthcare and engineering tend to earn the highest salaries, with more than half receiving gross annual incomes above €40,000 five years after graduation; overall, Maastricht University alumni report a median gross monthly income of €3,430 (about €41,160 per year) 1½ years after completing their degree, rising to €4,500 (approximately €54,000 per year) after five years, with science‑ and engineering graduates earning roughly €3,680 per month early on and reaching higher levels later. The Dutch labour market is projected to generate over 2.3 million job openings by 2030, driven largely by replacement needs, and persistent shortages are expected in healthcare, education and technology sectors, making these occupations especially attractive for international graduates seeking strong employment prospects and competitive pay.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

Đời sống & môi trường: Maastricht University offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with a wide range of student organisations and associations that cater to diverse interests—from academic societies and cultural clubs to civic‑engagement and volunteering groups—making it easy to meet peers and join activities such as the INKOM introduction days; the International Student Helpdesk provides practical assistance on Dutch bureaucracy, visa and residence‑permit issues, and even emergency support for students whose families are in danger, while mandatory health insurance and readily available campus safety services, local police patrols, and a 24‑hour emergency number (112) help ensure a secure environment; for Vietnamese students, the campus hosts a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association that organises cultural events, language‑exchange sessions, and social gatherings, offering a supportive community where newcomers can find information about housing, academic advice, and everyday life in Maastricht.
