### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students wishing to start a regular master’s programme at the University of Groningen can enrol in either the September or February intake; the September start requires a completed application by 1 May of the preceding year, while the February start must be submitted by 15 October of the preceding year, and these deadlines apply to both EU/EEA and non‑EU/EEA applicants. Prospective candidates first create a Studielink account, select their desired programme on the Studielink portal and submit the admission request; after submission they receive an email (check spam) with a payment link for the non‑refundable €100 application fee, which must be paid before the deadline, after which the university sends a confirmation and the applicant finalises the remaining details in the university’s Progress Portal. The fee is waived only in very specific cases, and paying it once covers multiple applications in the same academic year.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying for a bachelor’s programme at the University of Groningen must hold a qualification that is equivalent to the Dutch VWO pre‑university diploma (such as the International Baccalaureate, A‑levels or other recognised non‑EU/EEA secondary school qualifications) and achieve a minimum GPA of 7 / 10 on the Dutch grading scale (roughly a 3.0‑3.5 on the U.S. scale); the average of all pre‑university marks is used to calculate this GPA. Required documents include a scanned academic transcript (or a school‑issued list of pending courses), a passport, a document stating the original name of the qualification, proof of English proficiency that meets the programme’s threshold (e.g., IELTS ≥ 6.5, TOEFL ≥ 90 or equivalent), and for Dutch‑taught programmes an additional proof of Dutch proficiency. For non‑EU/EEA applicants a valid GMAT or GRE score (if the programme requests it) may be required, and a CV, motivation letter and, for selective research master tracks, two academic recommendation letters are also mandatory. **3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to English‑taught programmes at the University of Groningen must demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least IELTS 6.5 (with a minimum of 6.5 in each of Reading, Listening, Speaking and Writing), TOEFL iBT 90 (minimum 21 in Reading, Listening and Speaking and 24 in Writing; the older TOEFL scale of 4.5 overall with 4–4.5 in the sub‑scores is also accepted), Pearson PTE Academic 66 (minimum 64 in Reading, 58 in Listening and Speaking, and 70 in Writing), LanguageCERT Academic 70 (minimum 70 in each component), or Cambridge CAE/CPE with a score of 180 or higher; scores must be from the academic version of the test, be no older than two years, and institutional TOEFL or MyBest scores are not accepted. Applicants are exempt from these requirements if they hold a Dutch VWO diploma, are native speakers from the USA, UK, Ireland, New Zealand or Australia, have completed a full‑time bachelor’s degree in one of those countries, or possess an International Baccalaureate or European Baccalaureate where English was the medium of instruction. Duolingo is not listed among the accepted tests, so it cannot be used for admission.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at the University of Groningen follow a curriculum built from ECTS‑based course units (syllabus units) whose workload is defined as 28 hours per credit, with a typical full‑time load of 30 ECTS per semester (10–15 ECTS per block when taking 2–3 courses); the first 60 ECTS of any bachelor’s programme constitute the propaedeutic phase and must be completed in the initial year, which includes a minimum of 480 contact hours, and each degree programme requires that at least half of the total credits be earned at Groningen (for double‑degree programmes a minimum of 45 ECTS, or one quarter, must be completed there); every course unit has explicitly stated learning outcomes that must be achieved irrespective of the teaching mode, and enrollment in a specific course is subject to meeting any listed prerequisite units and having the necessary credit capacity within the programme’s structure.

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: International students who are non‑EU/EEA nationals must obtain both an MVV (provisional entry visa) and a VVR (residence permit) to study at the University of Groningen; the university, as a recognised sponsor, files a single “Entry and Residence” (TEV) application on the student’s behalf and pays the IND fee of €210 for the combined MVV + VVR procedure, with a further €210 required for any VVR renewal. The IND requires proof that the student can support themselves for the full duration of the programme, which for full‑time degree study at Groningen is quantified as €1 180 per month (approximately €14 160 per year), and the university accepts any of the standard forms of evidence such as a blocked Dutch bank account, a Nuffic‑approved scholarship, a guarantee letter from a sponsor, or a student loan, provided the funds are readily available for the entire period; the proof must be submitted with the passport scan and other required documents, after which the MVV can be collected from the Dutch embassy within three months and the VVR is issued within two weeks of arrival in the Netherlands. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Dutch Orientation Year (Zoekjaar) is a 12‑month residence permit that allows recent graduates – including those who have earned an accredited bachelor’s, master’s or PhD from a Dutch higher‑education institution such as the University of Groningen – to stay in the Netherlands after graduation to look for a job, start a business or work as a freelancer without needing a separate work permit; applicants must submit their request within three years of receiving their diploma (or, for the “top‑university” route, within three years of completing a degree at a recognised top‑200 foreign university), must register with the municipality and obtain a BSN, cannot extend the permit beyond the one‑year period, and must switch to another residence permit (for example a highly‑skilled migrant visa, which for eligible graduates in 2026 has a reduced gross salary threshold of €3,122 per month) before the Zoekjaar expires.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: International students at the University of Groningen benefit from a well‑structured internship and placement system coordinated by the university’s Career Services and individual faculties such as the Faculty of Economics and Business, where recent graduate surveys show that roughly 80 % of graduates (job rates 7.9–8.1) secure employment within a few months after finishing their studies, with about 42 % entering permanent contracts and the remainder on temporary terms; the average gross starting salary for FEB graduates is €4 182 per month, with lower figures for the Analytics & Data Science track (€2 965) and the Marketing Management track (€3 628), and graduates commonly report working 38–43 hours per week in multicultural teams (≈65 % of respondents) in roles such as international account manager, data engineer/analyst, policy analyst, sales coordinator, financial trainee, IT business consultant, corporate banker or supply‑chain specialist, for employers ranging from ABN AMRO, FrieslandCampina and IKEA to Deloitte, Huawei and the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Lương & thị trường: Graduates from the University of Groningen enter the Dutch labour market with strong earnings and high demand, especially in sectors such as information technology, sales and finance where the need for highly‑educated starters grew 19 % last year and trainee positions jumped 46 %; the typical graduate secures a job within about three months, works roughly 43 hours per week and earns an average gross starter salary of €4 182 per month (≈ €50 000 annually), with discipline‑specific averages of €2 592 per month for MSc Marketing, €2 947 for MSc Finance and €2 632 for MSc Business Administration, while many also attain salaries comparable to international benchmarks (around US $108 k average for alumni in the United States); common first‑job titles include consultant, data analyst, (junior) marketing manager, finance trainee, business analyst, international account manager, data‑quality expert, policy analyst and sales coordinator, and about two‑thirds of graduates work in multicultural teams, with 42 % on permanent contracts and the remainder in temporary roles.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Groningen supports international students through a dedicated International Office that coordinates orientation activities, including welcome sessions and airport pick‑up by student ambassadors, and offers a range of practical assistance such as visa and immigration advice via the Immigration Service Desk (isd@rug.nl) and scholarship information through the Mobility and Scholarship Desk (msd@rug.nl); housing support is provided by the independent non‑profit SSH (SSH XL) which manages affordable, all‑inclusive student residences located 10‑15 minutes by bike from campus and can be contacted at groningen@sshxl.nl for registration, while career development is handled by the university’s Career Services (careerservices@rug.nl) that offers counseling, employer networking events, job‑search workshops and a dedicated office for international students seeking internships or jobs abroad, and additional student wellbeing and academic support is available through the Student Service Centre (ssc-info@rug.nl). Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Groningen hosts more than 4,000 international students from over 120 countries, and campus life is built around a wide network of student‑run associations that cater to academic, cultural, sports and social interests; newcomers can join the large ACLO sports federation (with 49 clubs and about 100 disciplines), the USVA for film and performing‑arts activities, or participate in the KEI and ESN introductory weeks that help them meet peers and explore the city, while many international student associations—including a Vietnamese student group that meets regularly through the university’s International Student Associations platform—offer a familiar community for Vietnamese students to share experiences, organise cultural events and provide mutual support; the university also prioritises safety through the Social Safety Students project, which provides a code of conduct, active‑bystander training, confidential contact persons in both study programmes and student associations, and visible support structures to ensure a socially safe and inclusive environment for all students.
