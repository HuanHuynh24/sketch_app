### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Stanford admits only a fall‑quarter intake and does not offer spring or winter entry, so all first‑year applicants—U.S. or international—must plan to begin classes in the fall; prospective students can apply either via the Common Application or the Coalition Application, paying a $90 application fee (with fee‑waiver options available), and must submit their secondary‑school diploma (or equivalent) by the time they enroll, though it is not required at the time of filing; the university’s single early‑action option is a Restrictive Early Action deadline of November 1 with decisions released by mid‑December, while the regular‑decision deadline is January 2 with final decisions by early April, and the application process includes completing personal‑information sections, academic history, essays (personal statement and short‑answer prompts), optional standardized‑test scores, transcripts, school‑report and two teacher recommendations, and, for international applicants, any required English‑proficiency documentation.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Regular Decision and Restrictive Early Action : Stanford University](https://admission.stanford.edu/apply/first-year/decision_process.html) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Application and Essays : Stanford University](https://admission.stanford.edu/apply/first-year/apply.html) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International Applicants - Stanford Admissions](https://admission.stanford.edu/apply/international/index.html) (Cao, score 1.00)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào (GPA, SAT/ACT)**

- International students applying for undergraduate admission to Stanford must submit a Common Application (or Coalition Application) with a $100 non‑refundable fee (or a fee waiver), an official secondary‑school diploma (or the equivalent in their home country), official transcripts (including a mid‑year transcript by February 15) and a School Report completed by a counselor or other school official, plus two teacher recommendations; while SAT or ACT scores are optional for the Class of 2028, students may self‑report them (and must send official scores if admitted) and the middle‑50 % range for admitted SAT scores is 1510‑1570 and for ACT 34‑35; AP, IB, or A‑Level coursework is strongly encouraged, and applicants can self‑report AP or IB exam scores (with the expectation that exams are taken when possible), though no specific minimum scores are required; there is no formal GPA cutoff, but successful candidates typically rank in the top 10 percent of their class with a GPA around 3.9 – 4.0 on a 4.0 scale; an arts portfolio is required only for applicants to the School of Art and Art History, while a personal interview is not mandatory but alumni volunteers are available for optional interviews.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [First-Year Applicants - Stanford Admissions](https://admission.stanford.edu/apply/first-year/) (Cao, score 0.97)<br>[International Applicants - Stanford Admissions](https://admission.stanford.edu/apply/international/index.html) (Cao, score 0.97)<br>[Standardized Testing : Stanford University](https://admission.stanford.edu/apply/first-year/testing.html) (Cao, score 0.96)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Stanford requires all international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency, but the university does not mandate a single preferred test—TOEFL iBT, IELTS Academic, Duolingo English Test, and PTE Academic are all accepted, with typical minimums of 100 on the TOEFL iBT (and at least 22 on each section), 7.0 overall on the IELTS (minimum 6.5 per component), 125 on the Duolingo English Test, and 68 on the PTE Academic; however, applicants who are native English speakers, U.S. citizens, or who have completed at least three years of full‑time secondary or post‑secondary education where English was the language of instruction may receive a waiver of the English‑language requirement, though graduate programs may set higher score thresholds for admission.

**2. Student Visa (F-1) và Chính sách OPT**

- Student Visa (F-1): To obtain an F‑1 visa for Stanford University, an admitted international student must receive a certified I‑20 form from Stanford’s Bechtel International Center, which is issued only after the university verifies that the student (and any dependents) has sufficient funds to cover tuition, fees, and living expenses for at least the first academic year—often requiring proof of support for two years or the full program length. Acceptable financial evidence includes recent, official bank statements or certified financial institution letters showing liquid assets equal to or exceeding Stanford’s published cost of attendance, scholarship or fellowship award letters that specify the amount and duration, a sponsor’s affidavit of support accompanied by the sponsor’s proof of income (such as tax returns, pay stubs, or employment letters), and, if applicable, a loan commitment letter from a U.S. financial institution. All documents must be in English (or accompanied by a certified translation), signed where required, and submitted through the Stanford portal for review; once approved, Stanford mails the I‑20, which the student then uses to pay the SEVIS I‑901 fee, schedule a visa interview, and present to the U.S. consular officer, who may also request the same financial documentation as part of the visa adjudication process outlined on travel.state.gov and USCIS.gov. Sau tốt nghiệp (OPT/STEM OPT): International graduates on an F‑1 visa at Stanford University may work in the United States after completing their degree through Optional Practical Training (OPT), which grants up to 12 months of post‑completion employment, and if the degree is classified in a USCIS‑designated STEM field they can apply for a STEM OPT extension that adds a further 24 months for a total of 36 months of work authorization; to be eligible for the STEM extension the student must have earned a qualifying STEM degree, be employed (or have a job offer) with an employer enrolled in the E‑Verify system, submit a timely Form I‑765 with a new Form I‑20 endorsed for STEM OPT, and maintain all F‑1 reporting requirements, while any pre‑completion OPT time previously used reduces the available post‑completion OPT period accordingly.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm (CPT/OPT): Stanford’s graduate programs report very high post‑graduation placement rates, with employment status known for roughly 95 % of the 2024 cohort and almost all of those graduates employed or in further study shortly after graduation; about 46 % of PhD and master’s students completed at least one internship during their program, and the MBA Class of 2025 saw 90 % of job‑seekers receive offers within three months and 81 % accept them, while 16 % launched their own ventures, and median first‑year salaries for master’s graduates in fields such as Education Data Science are around $129 k (U.S. only) or $110 k globally, with graduates entering sectors like technology (including AI product and go‑to‑market roles), finance, consulting, health care, and education, and international students commonly use CPT‑eligible internships that place them with both U.S. and overseas employers. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Stanford’s graduate programs report strong earnings that align with the high‑demand occupations highlighted by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics; for example, graduates of the Education Data Science master’s program who are employed in the United States earn a median salary of roughly $129,000, while the broader cohort of Stanford graduate‑school alumni earning in the U.S. see a median salary of about $89,300, and those reporting globally earn a median of $97,800. These figures are consistent with BLS data that show the most sought‑after U.S. occupations for advanced‑degree holders—including data scientists, software developers, information security analysts, and health‑care managers—command median annual wages well above $100,000 and are projected to grow at rates ranging from 10 % to 30 % over the next decade, reflecting both robust labor‑market demand and the premium placed on specialized technical and analytical skills that international graduates typically possess.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Stanford’s International Student Services Office (ISSO), housed within the Bechtel International Center, provides a comprehensive suite of support for international students that includes a mandatory International Student Orientation (ISO) held the weekend before the university‑wide New Student Orientation, with sessions on immigration regulations, campus resources and community‑building activities, as well as practical details such as the move‑in date of September 13 (students arriving earlier must arrange their own housing and can access hotel discount information); the office also offers ongoing assistance with housing through advice on on‑campus residence options and off‑campus arrangements, and partners with the Stanford Career Center and Bechtel’s own career‑development team to deliver specialized career services for international students, including workshops on résumé writing, interview preparation, networking, and guidance on work authorization programs such as Optional Practical Training (OPT) and Curricular Practical Training (CPT). Đời sống & môi trường: Stanford University welcomes a large and active international student community, with the Bechtel International Center and eight campus community centers providing orientation, visa assistance, mentorship, language support, and a wide range of academic and social resources; campus life is vibrant, featuring more than 600 student organizations—including cultural groups such as the Vietnamese Student Association and other Asian‑Pacific Islander clubs—plus over 30 fraternities and sororities that offer social, leadership, and service opportunities, while safety is a top priority through the Stanford Police Department’s 24‑hour patrols, emergency phones, a safety mobile app, and publicly reported Clery Act crime statistics, and students can also take advantage of extensive wellness services, free tutoring, and numerous recreational, intramural, and club‑sport options that together create a supportive and engaging environment for Vietnamese and other international students.
