### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students who wish to enroll at Heidelberg University must apply online through the UniAssist portal, upload certified copies of their academic transcripts, proof of language proficiency and any required test scores, and pay the mandatory application fees (a €75 processing charge to UniAssist plus a €75 university‑administrative fee, totalling €150); for the winter semester (starting 1 October) the final deadline for non‑EU/EEA applicants is 15 June each year, while for the summer semester (starting 1 April) the deadline is 15 January, after which successful candidates receive an admission letter that specifies the enrolment deadline and the semester contribution to be paid before registration.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to Heidelberg University must hold a recognized higher‑education entrance qualification (e.g., a national secondary‑school leaving certificate or a foreign equivalent assessed by the Central Office for Foreign Education) and, for most programmes, demonstrable German language proficiency—typically DSH‑2 (or higher) or TestDaF minimum scores of 16‑16‑16; some programmes offered in English may accept TOEFL or IELTS instead. All non‑EU/EEA candidates must submit their documents through uni‑assist, which issues a Vorprüfungsdokumentation (VPD) confirming the equivalence of grades, transcripts and GPA (generally an above‑average grade, roughly a German 2.5 – 2.0 or a 2:1 UK degree, though exact thresholds are set by each faculty); certified copies can be replaced by regular copies once the VPD is received. For master’s programmes a first university degree in a related field is required, often with a minimum number of credit points and a specified minimum grade point average; certain fields such as architecture, design, fine arts, or music additionally demand a portfolio or an interview as part of the selection process. Applicants must also provide proof of a valid German residence permit (or visa) after receiving an admission letter, while EU/EEA citizens are exempt from visa requirements.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Heidelberg University requires German language proficiency for virtually all undergraduate and most master’s programmes, but German certificates are not needed for master’s courses that are taught entirely in English; in those cases the university generally expects proof of English proficiency, most commonly a TOEFL iBT score of at least 90 – 100 or an IELTS band of 6.5 – 7.0, although the exact minimum can vary by faculty and is stated in the specific programme’s admission rules. Applicants who have completed a previous degree in which the language of instruction was English can usually obtain a waiver for the English‑language test, provided they submit an official letter from the awarding institution confirming the language of instruction. While TOEFL and IELTS are explicitly mentioned as accepted tests, Heidelberg does not list PTE Academic or Duolingo English Test among its standard English‑language certificates, so those tests are not accepted for admission or for obtaining a waiver.

**4. Học phí**

- Heidelberg University charges non‑EU/EEA undergraduate and consecutive master students a tuition fee of €1,500 per semester, which totals €3,000 per academic year (two semesters), and all students must also pay the semester contribution of €161.10 per semester (comprising an €80 administration fee, €66 social fee, €10 student‑body contribution, €2.60 nextbike fee and €2.50 theatre‑flat‑rate), amounting to €322.20 per year; therefore the overall yearly cost for an international non‑EU/EEA student is €3,322.20, while EU/EEA students and those with a German university entrance qualification are exempt from the €1,500 tuition but still pay the €322.20 contribution; the standard application fee for international applicants is €75, and a second‑degree (Zweitstudiengebühren) incurs an additional €650 per semester.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Heidelberg University requires international applicants to hold a recognised higher‑education entrance qualification equivalent to the German Abitur for bachelor programmes and a completed first university degree for master’s programmes, with many master’s courses further restricting admission by a minimum number of ECTS credits earned in a related subject (often at least 60‑90 ECTS) and by above‑average grades; prospective students must demonstrate very good German language skills for German‑taught programmes (e.g., passing the DSH or TestDaF after at least 1,000‑1,200 hours of prior instruction) or very good English for English‑taught master’s programmes, and the curriculum follows the European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System, where bachelor degrees normally comprise 180‑240 ECTS earned through a combination of compulsory modules, electives and a bachelor thesis—each module typically worth 5‑10 ECTS and linked to clearly defined learning outcomes that specify the knowledge, skills and competencies to be achieved—while master’s programmes generally require 90‑120 ECTS, including advanced seminars, research projects and a master’s thesis, with learning outcomes articulated in the programme’s study and examination regulations.

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Heidelberg University is positioned as one of Germany’s leading institutions, ranking 80th worldwide in the 2025/2026 QS World University Rankings and third among German universities, while in the 2025/2026 Times Higher Education World University Rankings it holds the 49th spot globally, also third in Germany and 13th in Europe; its strong proportion of international students is a key factor in both rankings, and subject‑specific QS and THE tables consistently place Heidelberg within the top 100 globally and often within the top 30 for disciplines such as Medicine, Life Sciences, Chemistry, Physics and the Humanities, reflecting its research impact and reputation, and the university is fully accredited by the German Ministry of Science and the German Council of Science and Humanities (Wissenschaftsrat) with individual programmes receiving accreditation from recognised agencies such as ACQUIN and ASIIN.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/student visa: To obtain a German student visa for Heidelberg University you must provide a Finanzierungsnachweis that shows you have sufficient funds to cover living expenses, which since 1 January 2023 is set at €11 208 per year (approximately €992 per month) and is most commonly demonstrated by a blocked account (Sperrkonto) that releases the amount in monthly instalments; alternative proof such as a scholarship, a parental income guarantee, or a bank guarantee is also accepted, but the blocked account is the standard method endorsed by the DAAD and study‑in‑germany.de, and while studying you are permitted to work part‑time for up to 120 full days (or 240 half‑days) per year, generally limited to 20 hours per week during the semester, which can supplement but not replace the required financial proof. Sau tốt nghiệp: The post‑study work visa (Job‑Seeker Residence Permit, § 20 AufenthG) allows an international graduate of a German university such as Heidelberg to remain in Germany for up to 18 months to look for a job that matches the qualification; the clock starts on the official graduation date, not on the date the permit is issued, and the permit cannot be extended beyond 18 months. To be eligible you must hold a recognized German degree (Bachelor, Master or PhD) and provide proof of graduation (certificate or official confirmation), proof of sufficient financial resources (e.g., a blocked account covering €1 091 per month or a formal declaration of commitment), valid health insurance, a registered German address (Anmeldung) and evidence of your job‑search efforts; once you secure suitable employment you can convert the permit to a work visa or EU Blue Card without leaving the country.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Heidelberg University offers international students a well‑structured support system for gaining practical experience while studying, including a dedicated job‑ and internship exchange platform operated by the university’s Career Service and additional listings through the Studierendenwerk and local newspapers, and it permits up to 120 working days per year for non‑EU students (or 140 days if the internship is classified as part‑time work) provided the residence permit allows it; most positions, especially those beyond the mandatory “voluntary” internships, require solid German language skills, while English‑speaking roles are mainly available in research labs, multinational companies, and IT‑related fields, and the university’s strong research orientation and extensive industry contacts mean that a large majority of graduates secure employment quickly—official university reports and recent graduate surveys place the post‑graduation employment rate for Heidelberg alumni at roughly 90 % within six months, with many entering careers in medicine, life sciences, engineering, finance, and academia as Werkstudenten, research assistants, or full‑time professionals. Lương & thị trường: Graduates in Germany typically start with salaries ranging from about €38 000–€42 000 in humanities and social sciences up to €50 000–€55 000 in engineering and computer‑science fields, while medical graduates and those in high‑tech specialties such as data science, artificial‑intelligence and renewable‑energy engineering often exceed €65 000, with top earners at prestigious institutions like Heidelberg University reaching over €70 000; the labour market shows the strongest demand for engineers (mechanical, electrical, automotive, and civil), IT and software‑development professionals, data‑science and AI experts, healthcare workers (especially physicians and nurses), and specialists in renewable‑energy and logistics, and international graduates—who make up roughly 13 % of all students and are heavily represented in engineering, law, economics and the STEM disciplines—benefit from these demand trends but must navigate visa and residence‑permit procedures before entering the German labour market.
