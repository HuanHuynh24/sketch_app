### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- The University of Canberra admits international students in two main intakes each year—Semester 1 (starting in February) and Semester 2 (starting in July); the current deadline for applications to begin in Semester 2 2024 is 26 May 2024 (applications for Semester 1 usually close around early October for the February start), the non‑refundable international application fee is AU$100 (the same for undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programmes), and the application process consists of creating an account on UC’s online portal (or applying through an authorised agent), completing the electronic form, uploading all required supporting documents (certified academic transcripts, English‑language proof, passport copy and, if applicable, a statement of financial capacity), paying the AU$100 fee, and then awaiting the initial outcome (typically within five business days); if offered a place, you may need to complete a Genuine Student (GS) assessment, accept the offer, pay the tuition deposit, receive your Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) and subsequently apply for a student visa.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students wishing to study at the University of Canberra must meet the university’s core academic and English‑language thresholds, which typically require a secondary‑school qualification equivalent to the Australian Year 12 certificate (or a recognised tertiary diploma, advanced diploma, bachelor’s or postgraduate degree) and a minimum grade‑point average or ATAR roughly equivalent to a 70 % (around a 3.0 GPA on a 4.0 scale), with exact GPA requirements varying by program; all applicants must provide certified transcripts (học bạ) and evidence of exam results (điểm thi) for the qualifications they are claiming, and must also satisfy the Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) condition for a student visa as well as any course‑specific inherent requirements. In addition to meeting the standard academic and English‑language criteria (e.g., IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 or equivalent), candidates applying to creative or design‑focused degrees such as Film Production, Architecture, Built Environment, Communication and Media, etc., are required to submit a portfolio of up to five pieces of work that demonstrates their design process, critical thinking and relevant skills, accompanied by a written statement; some programs may also request an interview to further assess suitability, while other courses rely solely on the academic record and English test scores.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students must satisfy the University of Canberra’s English language standards, which are set by test and minimum score: most undergraduate and postgraduate courses require IELTS Academic 7.0 overall with no band below 6.5 (higher‑requirement programmes need IELTS 7.5 overall with no band below 7.0); the equivalent TOEFL iBT scores are 94 overall (no sub‑score below 20) for the 7.0 band and 102 overall (R 24, L 24, S 23, W 27) for the 7.5 band; Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE) equivalents are 65 overall (no sub‑score below 55) for the 7.0 band and 73 overall (minimum 350 in each component) for the 7.5 band; Duolingo English Test is not listed as an accepted test. English waivers are possible when applicants have completed a prior qualification taught in English or otherwise meet the inherent English requirement, allowing them to forgo a test. Pathway programmes have lower thresholds: International Foundation Studies and the Standard Diploma accept IELTS 5.5 overall (no band below 5.0) and TOEFL iBT 51, while entry to a standalone ELICOS course needs at least IELTS 3.0 overall and a packaged ELICOS course requires IELTS 5.0 overall.

**4. Học phí**

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at the University of Canberra the typical annual cost of living is roughly AUD $18 000 – $20 000, broken down as follows: on‑campus or university‑guaranteed accommodation starts at about $162 per week (≈ $8 400 per year), while off‑campus shared housing can run $160 – $500 per week (≈ $8 300 – $26 000 annually) so most students budget around $8 400 – $9 500 for housing; food and groceries generally amount to about $150 per week (≈ $7 800 per year); public‑transport concessions for students cost roughly $1.61 per trip capped at $4.80 per day, which works out to roughly $1 000 – $1 200 per year for regular bus or light‑rail travel; and compulsory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is about $550 per year. Adding these core items (accommodation ≈ $8 400 + food ≈ $7 800 + transport ≈ $1 100 + OSHC ≈ $550) gives a total annual living cost of approximately $18 000 – $19 000 AUD (higher if choosing private rentals or a larger house).

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- At the University of Canberra each degree is built from individual “units” that are the equivalent of subjects, and every unit carries a specific number of credit points that together form the total points required for graduation (typically 144 credit points for a standard three‑year bachelor). Units are classified as core (mandatory) or elective (chosen from a range of options) and are usually delivered over one teaching period (semester). Before enrolment, many units have prerequisite conditions such as the completion of a certain number of credit points in the programme or the successful completion of a specific preceding unit, and these prerequisites apply equally to domestic and international students. International students can gain entry through direct admission on the basis of an Australian Year‑12 equivalent or a recognised tertiary qualification, or via pathway programmes such as UC Connect, the Graduate Certificate in Academic Foundations (GCAF) or a Diploma of Science, which provide credit that can be counted toward the bachelor’s curriculum. The curriculum for each course outlines the sequence of units, required credit totals, and any prerequisite units, while each unit’s description includes clearly defined learning outcomes that specify the knowledge, skills and competencies students are expected to achieve on successful completion.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 student visa, administered through the Department of Home Affairs (immi.homeaffairs.gov.au) and detailed on Study Australia (studyinaustralia.gov.au), requires applicants to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity – the current government guideline (2024‑25) sets a minimum of AUD 29,710 per year for the principal student, AUD 10,394 per year for a partner and AUD 4,449 per year for each dependent child, although the actual cost of living may be higher depending on the location – and mandates continuous coverage by Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire stay; holders of the visa are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while their course is in session (with unlimited hours during scheduled breaks) and those enrolled in a master's by research or doctoral program have no work‑hour limit, while family members may also work but are generally restricted to the same 48‑hour fortnightly cap unless the student is in a research postgraduate course; the University of Canberra is a registered education provider approved for the Subclass 500, meaning its international students can meet the enrolment and course‑progression requirements while complying with the visa’s financial, health‑insurance and work‑rights conditions. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the University of Canberra who have completed a bachelor (including honours), master’s (coursework or extended) or doctoral degree can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) Post‑Higher Education (Post‑Study) Work stream, which grants unrestricted work rights and allows the visa holder’s partner and children to accompany them; the standard period of stay is 2 years for a bachelor (or honours) qualification, 2 years for a master’s coursework or extended qualification, 3 years for a master’s research qualification and 3 years for a doctorate, with Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders eligible for up to 5 years, Indian nationals under the Australia‑India Economic Cooperation and Trade Agreement receiving 2 years for a bachelor (or 3 years for a bachelor with first‑class honours in STEM) and 3 years for master’s and 4 years for a doctorate, and, as of 1 July 2023, graduates whose degree appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply for a further 2‑year extension (subject to the Department’s announced cessation of this extension from mid‑2024), while those who have studied, lived and worked in regional Australia may also be eligible for an additional 1‑2 years.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**
