### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Utrecht University offers two main intakes for international students – the regular September start and a smaller February start – and all programmes have their own specific deadline that must be met at 23:59 CET; for most bachelor programmes the September deadline is 1 May (non‑EU) or 1 July (EU/Netherlands) and for master programmes it is 1 June (non‑EU) or 1 July (EU/Netherlands), while the February intake deadlines are usually 1 November (non‑EU) and 1 December (EU/Netherlands); applicants first create a Studielink account (using DigiD if they already live in the Netherlands or “Create Studielink‑account without DigiD” if they live abroad), enter all secondary and higher‑education details, then submit the enrolment application in Studielink, after which they receive OSIRIS login details to upload required PDFs (max 2 MB each) and pay the application fee – typically €85 for bachelor programmes and €100 for master programmes – before the deadline; once the fee is paid and the file is complete, the university processes the application, after which successful candidates complete enrolment and arrange tuition‑fee authorisation in Studielink (authorisation can be set up from 1 June to 31 August of the enrolment year).

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students who wish to enroll in a bachelor’s programme at Utrecht University must hold a Dutch VWO diploma (any profile) or an internationally recognised qualification that is deemed equivalent – such as the International Baccalaureate, European Baccalaureate, International A‑Levels, Caribbean Advanced Proficiency Examination, or a US high‑school diploma from an international school – and they must meet the university’s grade threshold, which is a minimum average of 70 % for a three‑year university programme or at least 80 % (or a 3.5 GPA on a 4‑point scale) for a four‑year or longer programme; if the grade requirement is not met, eligibility can be gained by completing one year of university study in an academic subject with the same grade standards. In addition to the diploma and grades, applicants must provide proof of English proficiency (e.g., IELTS ≥ 6.5, TOEFL iBT ≥ 90, or comparable scores on PTE Academic, Cambridge exams, etc.). Certain bachelor programmes impose extra subject‑specific prerequisites – for example, a minimum score in mathematics, biology or a foreign language – and a few specialised tracks (such as art‑related or design‑oriented programmes) may also request a portfolio or conduct an interview as part of the selection process.

**4. Học phí**

- For non‑EU/EEA (international) students at Utrecht University the yearly institutional tuition fee for the 2024‑2025 academic year is €15 219 (full‑time), while at University College Utrecht the tuition component is €17 073 per year; campus fees add €8 231 for a single‑room or €4 939 for a double‑room option and a mandatory UCSA membership of €100, resulting in a total annual cost of €25 690 for a single‑room or €22 398 for a double‑room, all amounts being quoted in euros; the standard application fee is €100.

- Utrecht University structures its programmes around the European Credit Transfer System (ECTS), where one credit corresponds to roughly 28 hours of study and a full semester normally carries 30 ECTS; students—both Dutch and international—are expected to enrol in a full course load, and exchange students may select Bachelor‑level courses (levels 1‑3) and, if they are Master’s candidates, also Master‑level courses, with level 3 courses requiring a stronger background in the relevant discipline. ## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Utrecht University in the Netherlands remains a highly regarded institution for international students, still appearing in the 2024‑2025 QS World University Rankings at approximately 60th worldwide and in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2023 at around 38th, despite having withdrawn from actively supplying data to these league tables; its QS subject rankings place it within the global top‑10 for Life Sciences & Medicine, Earth & Marine Sciences and Psychology, while THE subject rankings rank it among the top‑20 in Clinical, Pre‑Clinical & Health as well as in Social Sciences, and the university’s programmes are officially accredited by the Dutch‑Flemish accreditation body NVAO, ensuring compliance with national quality standards.

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: Non‑EU students who want to study at Utrecht University must obtain an MVV entry visa (machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf) and a VVR residence permit (verblijfsvergunning regulier) through the Dutch Immigration and Naturalisation Service (IND); the university forwards the application and the IND fee of €286 must be paid via the OSIRIS portal. To satisfy the IND’s financial‑means requirement you must demonstrate that you have at least €1 225 per month available for living expenses, which totals €14 700 for the first 12 months of study and cannot be used for tuition fees; the full amount must be shown as available in a bank account before you apply, and it can be proven through a scholarship, personal funds, or a sponsor who does not reside in the Netherlands, with the bank statement meeting the IND’s detailed formatting rules. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Dutch Orientation Year (Zoekjaar) residence permit lets an international graduate – for example a recent bachelor’s, master’s, PhD or post‑master researcher from Utrecht University – stay in the Netherlands for up to twelve months, working for any employer, freelancing or starting a business without needing a separate work permit; the permit is non‑renewable and must be applied for within three years of completing the qualification (or, for graduates of a top‑200 globally ranked foreign university, within the same three‑year window), after which the holder must register with the municipality, obtain a BSN and, if wishing to remain longer, switch to another residence permit such as a Highly Skilled Migrant or Startup visa, as set out by the IND (the official Immigration and Naturalisation Service).

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Utrecht University attracts a large share of international applicants—around 40 % of total applications—and most of those accepted go on to start their programmes, while the university strongly encourages practical work experience by allowing students to arrange independent internships or placements during summer or winter breaks and offering dedicated support through the Futures Centre; as a result, graduate outcomes are robust, with roughly 85 % of all graduates securing a paid job or commencing a postgraduate programme within six months of graduation, and international graduates achieving comparable rates, many entering fields such as life sciences, engineering, business, health care and public policy, despite the broader Dutch graduate unemployment rate of about 23 % indicating that Utrecht’s career services and employer connections help its graduates attain better‑than‑average employment prospects. Lương & thị trường: In the Utrecht region there are roughly 12 150 vacancies for highly educated workers, with software and application developers enjoying the strongest labour‑market prospects, while healthcare, financial services, construction, technology and engineering, education and government also show solid demand; graduates entering these sectors—especially in technical study groups such as chemical engineering, electronics, industrial automation, construction, civil engineering, transport, logistics and software development—see employment rates above 50 % within a year of graduation, and the typical starting gross salary for Dutch and international master’s graduates ranges from €33 000 to €38 000 annually, rising sharply for high‑skill technical roles, with many international alumni earning over €65 000 per year five years after graduation, particularly in the Greater Amsterdam and Eindhoven clusters where about 40 % and nearly 50 % of international graduates, respectively, remain employed in the Netherlands.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Utrecht University’s International Service Desk (the International Office) is the central hub for international students, offering semester‑start orientation sessions, detailed housing support—including advice on student accommodation, municipal registration and finding private housing—and close coordination with the Utrecht International Center for practical life matters; it is located at Heidelberglaan 8, room 0.38 on the Science Park campus, with walk‑in hours on Monday, Wednesday and Thursday 9:00‑12:30 and reachable by email internationalservicedesk@uu.nl or phone +31 30 253 35 50. For wider student affairs such as enrolment, Studielink and tuition issues, Student Services (email studentservices@uu.nl, phone 030 253 7000) provides assistance, while the Utrecht University Career Center delivers dedicated career services—counselling, workshops, internship and job‑search support—tailored to the needs of international students and working in partnership with the International Office. Đời sống & môi trường: Utrecht University hosts a lively international student scene in which more than half of the student body comes from over 50 different countries and a broad network of student organisations—ranging from social clubs, sports teams, religious groups and course‑based study associations—offers regular activities, lectures, excursions and cultural events that help newcomers integrate and make friends; safety on campus is overseen by a dedicated security service that can be reached for non‑urgent matters at 030 253 1300 and for emergencies 24/7 at 030 253 4444 (or by dialing 4444 from any campus phone), ensuring a secure environment for all residents, and Vietnamese students are supported by a Vietnamese Student Association that organises language‑exchange meet‑ups, cultural celebrations such as Tết, informational sessions on visas and housing, and a buddy‑system that connects new Vietnamese arrivals with senior members of the community.
