**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Victoria University of Wellington welcomes international students to apply at any time of the year and does not charge an application fee; prospective students should create an account on the university’s Pūaha portal (or use an authorised agent) to complete the online application, upload certified copies of required documents and submit the form, and to ensure sufficient time for visa and accommodation arrangements they should aim to meet the trimester‑specific intake deadlines – for the February (Trimester 1) start apply by 8 December, for the July (Trimester 2) start apply by 1 May, and for the November (Trimester 3) start apply by 1 September.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Victoria University of Wellington requires international students to demonstrate English proficiency by meeting specific test scores that differ for undergraduate and postgraduate programmes: for undergraduate study the minimum IELTS Academic score is an overall 6.0 with no band below 5.5, TOEFL iBT 80 total, PTE Academic 50 with a Communication sub‑score of at least 42, and the Duolingo English Test 105 with no individual band under 95; for postgraduate study the thresholds are higher, with IELTS 6.5 overall and no band under 6.0, TOEFL iBT 90 total (minimum 20 in Writing, or 21 for PhD candidates), PTE 58 overall and a Communication score of at least 50, and Duolingo 120 with no band below 105; applicants who have studied in countries listed in the university’s exemption lists may be waived from these requirements, and those who do not meet the test criteria but can provide documented proof of strong English ability—such as academic writing samples, lecturer reference letters, or other relevant evidence—may also apply for an individual waiver which is assessed case‑by‑case.

- International students at Victoria University of Wellington can expect to spend about NZ$578 per week for catered hall accommodation (including electricity and internet), plus NZ$15 for a mobile phone plan, NZ$15 for toiletries, NZ$10 for transport and roughly NZ$70 for personal spending, bringing the weekly total to around NZ$688; over a full 52‑week year this works out to roughly NZ$35,800, and when occasional annual items such as holiday travel, furnishings, textbooks and other misc‑expenses (about NZ$8,200) and the mandatory health‑insurance premium (approximately NZ$899) are added, the overall annual living cost for a single international student is close to NZ$44,000 – excluding tuition fees.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Victoria University of Wellington is ranked 240th in the 2026 QS World University Rankings, placing it in the top 2 percent of universities worldwide, and it holds a QS Five‑Stars‑Plus rating for overall institutional excellence; the university also appears in the top 2 percent of the 2026 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings and is ranked within the global top 1 percent for 16 QS subjects (including Library and Information Management and Politics & International Studies, which are ranked first among New Zealand universities) and for two subjects in the THE rankings, with six subjects in the QS top 100 and a total of 29 subjects in the QS top 200; its Wellington School of Business and Government has achieved the prestigious “Triple Crown” accreditation—AACSB, EQUIS and AMBA—making it one of the very few business schools worldwide with this distinction, and the university’s international student body comprises roughly 30 percent of its total enrolment, reflecting its strong global reputation and appeal.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: A fee‑paying student visa for an international student at Victoria University of Wellington requires an official Offer of Place from the university, proof that the tuition fees have been paid (or a visa‑support letter from the university’s financial administrator if you are using an approved international student loan), and evidence of sufficient living funds – typically NZD 20,000 per year (or NZD 1,667 per month for shorter courses) shown through recent personal bank statements, a sponsor’s financial undertaking (INZ 1014) with their bank evidence, or an approved education‑loan sanction letter. Immigration New Zealand also expects you to have health insurance and meet standard visa criteria such as a valid passport, police certificate and medical clearance. While studying you are permitted to work up to 25 hours per week during academic sessions and full‑time during scheduled holidays, provided the work does not breach the conditions of your student visa. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Victoria University of Wellington who have obtained a New Zealand qualification are eligible for the Post‑Study Work Visa if they studied full‑time in New Zealand for at least 30 weeks (for level 7 or higher qualifications) and hold a valid visa while in the country; they must lodge the application within three months of the expiry (or end date) of the student visa that allowed them to study (PhD graduates have up to six months). The visa length depends on the level of the qualification: a bachelor’s degree (level 7) or a postgraduate diploma (level 8) generally grants a visa for the same period as the study programme (up to a maximum of three years for a bachelor’s if the course was longer), while a master’s degree (level 9) or a PhD (level 10) entitles the graduate to a three‑year work visa, and a short‑term graduate work visa of up to six months is available for diploma‑level (level 5‑7) courses of at least six months. No employer sponsorship or job offer is required.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: International students at Victoria University of Wellington benefit from a wide range of work‑integrated learning options—including paid or unpaid internships, law clerkships, summer placements and research scholarships—that are embedded in many undergraduate and postgraduate programmes and are commonly used as pipelines into graduate schemes, with the university reporting that roughly 88–90 % of its graduates are in paid employment or further study within six months of completing their degree—a rate that is similarly high for international graduates—and typical early‑career pathways for these students include roles such as law clerk or legal associate, accounting or finance graduate analyst, management or strategy consulting analyst, policy analyst or research assistant in the public sector, marketing and communications coordinator, information‑technology developer or analyst, and various positions in engineering, health and science organisations, all supported by the university’s Careers and Employment service which helps students secure placements and tracks graduate outcomes annually. Lương & thị trường: Victoria University of Wellington’s 2023‑24 graduate destinations survey shows that 87 % of its recent graduates were employed within six months of completing their studies, with a median first‑full‑time salary for bachelor‑level graduates of roughly NZD 65 000 and a notable 13 % of those without prior full‑time work experience earning NZD 80 000 or more; engineering and business graduates typically command salaries in the NZD 70‑80 000 range, while health‑related graduates such as nurses, physiotherapists and occupational therapists see earnings of NZD 65‑75 000, and the labour market demand for international graduates aligns closely with New Zealand’s official Green List, highlighting strong hiring prospects for occupations including software, full‑stack and DevOps engineers, cybersecurity analysts, data scientists, civil, structural and electrical engineers, construction project managers, registered nurses, midwives, physiotherapists, occupational therapists, secondary school teachers in mathematics and science, and fully qualified accountants, all of which tend to offer salaries at the upper end of the graduate scale and often exceed NZD 80 000 after a year of experience.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Victoria University of Wellington’s International Office offers a comprehensive support package for overseas students that begins with a compulsory International Orientation introducing campus life, academic expectations and New Zealand culture, and includes a free Arrival Meeting service where a staff member greets newcomers and a International Buddy Programme that pairs new arrivals with current students for social and practical help; the office also provides dedicated accommodation assistance, offering advice on finding safe, affordable housing, a searchable on‑campus and off‑campus accommodation portal, and guidance on tenancy rights and the university’s duty‑of‑care policies; in addition, the university’s Careers Service works closely with the International team to deliver tailored career support for international students, including one‑to‑one counseling, CV and cover‑letter reviews, interview preparation, employer networking events, workshops on job‑search strategies and information on work‑rights and post‑study work visas. Đời sống & môi trường: Victoria University of Wellington offers a vibrant campus life for international students, with a wide range of student‑run clubs and societies—including cultural groups such as a Vietnamese Student Association that organizes language practice, cultural celebrations, and peer‑support events—alongside academic, sports, and hobby clubs that meet throughout the year; the university’s International Student Support team helps newcomers connect to these activities and provides guidance on accommodation, visa matters, and wellbeing, while on‑campus security is available 24 hours, personal safety tips are promoted, and in emergencies students can dial 111 and then contact the International Student Experience Team at +64 27 600 6864, and most students are covered by the university‑provided Studentsafe insurance for medical needs, all within Wellington—a city consistently ranked among the world’s safest, offering a friendly environment for students to study, socialize, and feel secure.
