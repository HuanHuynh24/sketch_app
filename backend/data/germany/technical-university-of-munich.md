## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Kỳ nhập học của Đại học Kỹ thuật Munich (TUM) có hai đợt: học kỳ mùa đông (bắt đầu vào tháng 10) với thời gian nộp hồ sơ từ ngày 1 / 2 đến 31 / 5, và học kỳ mùa hè (bắt đầu vào tháng 4) với thời gian nộp hồ sơ từ ngày 1 / 10 đến 30 / 11; sinh viên quốc tế phải đăng ký qua cổng TUMonline, tạo tài khoản, điền đầy đủ thông tin, tải lên các giấy tờ bắt buộc (bằng cấp tốt nghiệp, chứng chỉ ngôn ngữ, bảng điểm, CV, thư motivation…) và thanh toán phí nộp hồ sơ, thường là 75 € cho các ứng viên ngoài EU (các sinh viên EU không phải trả phí). Sau khi nộp hồ sơ và phí, hồ sơ sẽ được xem xét; nếu được chấp nhận, sinh viên sẽ nhận giấy báo nhập học qua TUMonline, sau đó thực hiện các bước cuối cùng để đăng ký nhập học như nộp bản gốc bằng cấp, chứng minh tài chính và thanh toán học phí học kỳ.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Data Engineering and Analytics - Master of Science (M.Sc.) - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/degree-programs/detail/data-engineering-and-analytics-master-of-science-msc) (Cao, score 0.76)<br>[Admission Procedures - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/application/application-info-portal/admission-procedures) (Cao, score 0.62)<br>[FAQ - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/support-and-advice/faq) (Cao, score 0.60)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to the Technical University of Munich must first obtain a Preliminary Documentation (VPD) from uni‑assist, which verifies that their secondary‑school leaving certificate (e.g., A‑Levels, high‑school diploma, or equivalent) or, for bachelor‑level entry, any required Studienkolleg assessment is comparable to the German Abitur and determines whether admission is granted generally or subject‑specifically; the VPD must be issued no earlier than the dates specified for each country (e.g., after 01.12.2020 for Jordan) and is required in addition to the direct TUM application. Along with the VPD, applicants must submit certified copies of their transcripts showing GPA or grade point average (học bạ), the original certificates of any qualifying examinations (điểm thi), and, where applicable, proof of language proficiency (German TestDaF/DSH or English TOEFL/IELTS). Certain master’s programmes additionally require standardized test scores such as the GRE (or the engineering‑specific GATE) and, for the TUM School of Management, a GMAT of at least 640 points; programs with a creative or design focus (e.g., Architecture, Product Design) may request a portfolio and, in some cases, an interview to assess suitability. Finally, all documents must be notarised and, for many countries, accompanied by an apostille before submission.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Special Conditions for Certain Countries - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/application/application-info-portal/special-conditions-for-certain-countries) (Cao, score 0.65)<br>[International Higher Education Entrance Qualification - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/application/application-info-portal/higher-education-entrance-qualification/international-higher-education-entrance-qualification) (Cao, score 0.64)<br>[Preparing for your stay at TUM - International exchange students](https://www.international.tum.de/en/global/exchangestudents/general-information-for-international-students/preparing-your-stay/) (Cao, score 0.61)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Technical University of Munich (TUM) requires proof of English proficiency for international applicants, but a formal language‑certificate is not mandatory for bachelor programmes if the applicant can demonstrate that at least half of the instruction in their previous secondary school or university studies was in English; in that case a transcript or degree certificate confirming the language of instruction is sufficient. When a certificate is needed, TUM recognises TOEFL iBT (scores shown on the new 1‑to‑6 scale, with TOEFL ITP not accepted), IELTS (including Cambridge CPE/CAE grades A‑C), and Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic) with an overall score of at least 65 points (the same minimum applies to the online version). Duolingo English Test is not listed among the accepted exams, so it cannot be used for admission. For master’s programmes the English verification is usually carried out during the aptitude assessment, and a certificate may be required only if the applicant’s prior education does not already satisfy the 50 % English‑instruction criterion.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Language Certificates - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/application/application-info-portal/admission-requirements/language-certificates) (Cao, score 0.92)<br>[Aerospace - Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/degree-programs/detail/aerospace-bachelor-of-science-bsc) (Cao, score 0.86)<br>[FAQ - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/support-and-advice/faq) (Cao, score 0.48)

**4. Học phí**

- International students at the Technical University of Munich pay tuition fees that vary by program, ranging from 4,000 EUR per semester for Architecture and Management to 6,000 EUR per semester for fields such as Automotive Engineering, Communications and Electronics Engineering, Computational Mechanics and Informatics, with the semester contribution (student‑union fee) set at 97 EUR each semester; consequently the annual cost (two semesters) is between 8,194 EUR (4,000 EUR × 2 + 97 EUR × 2) and 12,194 EUR (6,000 EUR × 2 + 97 EUR × 2), and applicants must also pay a one‑time application fee of 75 EUR, all amounts being expressed in euros.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Tuition Fees for Students from Non-EU Countries - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/fees/tuition) (Cao, score 0.76)<br>[Campus Munich, Garching, WZW and TUM Asia - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/fees-and-financial-aid/campus-munich-garching-wzw-and-tum-asia) (Cao, score 0.70)<br>[Informatics - Master of Science (M.Sc.) - TUM](https://www.tum.de/en/studies/degree-programs/detail/informatics-master-of-science-msc) (Cao, score 0.69)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For international students at the Technical University of Munich the typical yearly budget is built on a monthly cost of roughly €1,300 to €2,000, which covers rent for a shared flat or studio (about €800 – €1,200), food (around €300), local public transport (€29 – €49), tele‑communication (€50 – €60) and health insurance (€90 – €150) if they are not employed or on a scholarship; multiplying the monthly range by twelve gives an annual living expense of approximately €15,600 – €24,000. In addition, German student‑visa regulations require a blocked‑account deposit of at least €10,236 per year to prove financial support, so the total amount an incoming student should be prepared to secure is roughly €25,800 – €34,200.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at the Technical University of Munich must first meet language and enrolment prerequisites—typically a German proficiency of C1 (minimum DSH 2 or TestDaF 4) for German‑taught programmes and proof of health insurance, a valid visa or residence permit, and registration through TUMonline—before they can begin their curriculum, which follows the European Credit Transfer System (ECTS) where a bachelor’s degree normally requires 180 ECTS and a master’s 120 ECTS, with each course clearly specifying its credit value, prerequisite knowledge and learning outcomes; for example, foreign students may earn up to 3 ECTS as a support elective by completing the German‑for‑Engineers course (SZ0324 or SZ0328) and can have additional credits from abroad recognised up to 18 ECTS as “electives in Management & Technology” or “electives in Engineering & Natural Sciences” provided the courses are related to their TUM study programme, do not duplicate required modules, and are documented with an English course description that includes learning objectives, the graduate‑level designation and the exact number of ECTS.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The Technical University of Munich (TUM) is ranked 28th in the latest QS World University Rankings and 26th in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, making it the top‑ranking German university in both tables; in subject‑specific rankings TUM holds a QS position of 19th for Engineering, 23rd for Natural Sciences and is the leading German institution for Business & Management, while THE places it 14th globally in Computer Science, 19th in Physical Sciences, 22nd in Engineering, 31st in Business and Economics, 34th in Life Sciences, 42nd in Education and 53rd in Medicine and Health, and in the Shanghai (ARWU) subject rankings it is among the world’s top‑10 in Remote Sensing, Agricultural Sciences and Biotechnology; the university’s School of Management is AMBA‑accredited for its three Executive MBA programmes, and TUM overall is recognised for its high proportion of international students, which consistently accounts for roughly one‑fifth of the total student body (about 10 000‑11 000 students) and contributes to its strong global reputation.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/student visa: To obtain a German student visa for the Technical University of Munich you must demonstrate that you have at least €11,208 per year (the current requirement from 1 January 2023) available to cover living costs, which is most commonly done by opening a blocked‑account (Sperrkonto) that releases up to €992 each month; acceptable alternatives include a scholarship, a parent’s income and asset documents, or a formal guarantee from a German resident, and once in Germany you may supplement your budget by working up to 120 full days (or 240 half‑days) per year, generally limited to 20 hours per week during the lecture period, with no restriction on hours during semester breaks, all information being outlined on the official DAAD and study‑in‑germany.de portals. Sau tốt nghiệp: The post‑study “Job Seeker” residence permit in Germany allows an international graduate – for example a recent graduate of the Technical University of Munich – to remain in the country for up to 18 months to look for a job that corresponds to the qualification earned; the period starts as soon as the final exam results or a degree certificate confirming completion are received. To be eligible, the applicant must hold a recognized German university degree (Bachelor’s, Master’s or PhD), provide proof of graduation (certificate, diploma or official confirmation), demonstrate sufficient financial resources (e.g., a blocked account with at least the monthly amount required for living costs, currently €1,091 per month, or a formal declaration of commitment), have valid health insurance for the full period, be registered at a German address (Anmeldung), and submit standard documents such as a passport, biometric photos, proof of accommodation and, where applicable, evidence of language proficiency and a CV. While any job may be taken during the search, conversion to a regular work permit or EU Blue Card later requires securing a position that matches the degree and meets the salary threshold for the chosen permit.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Technical University of Munich (TUM) attracts a large proportion of international students—about two‑thirds of those enrolled on the Munich campus are from abroad—and offers extensive industry‑linked opportunities such as internships, Werk‑student (working‑student) positions and co‑op placements across a broad range of sectors, with partner companies that include BMW, Siemens, Bosch, Audi, Google, Infineon, SAP and many others; the university’s dedicated Career Service and its internal job portal actively match students to theses, trainee programmes and permanent roles, and TUM’s graduates are among the most employable worldwide, ranking 12th globally in the latest Global University Employability Ranking and achieving a graduate employment rate exceeding 90 % within three to six months of graduation, most often securing positions in engineering, computer science, management, natural sciences and related technology fields. Lương & thị trường: Graduates of the Technical University of Munich enjoy strong labour‑market demand in Germany and internationally, with the university ranked 13th worldwide and fourth in Europe for graduate employability; entry‑level salaries for TUM alumni typically start around €60,000 per year and rise sharply with experience, especially in sectors such as consulting, finance, technology, manufacturing and consumer goods, while high‑demand occupations for new graduates include IT‑related roles that command average base salaries of €57,500 for IT technicians, €92,000 for web developers, €87,000 for computer programmers and €84,000 for system analysts, and the overall employment outlook remains robust in major German cities like Munich, Frankfurt and Berlin for both domestic and international graduates.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The Technical University of Munich’s International Office (often referred to as the TUM Global & Alumni Office) is the central contact point for all international students, offering visa and residence‑permit advice, pre‑arrival information and a range of orientation activities such as first‑year welcome events, campus tours, preliminary courses, tutoring and buddy programmes that help newcomers get acquainted with university life; housing support is provided through the Studierendenwerk München, which assists students in applying for university dormitories, gives guidance on finding private accommodation and can answer questions about tenancy and related finances; for career development the TUM Career Service offers one‑to‑one counselling, workshops, an extensive job and internship portal, networking events, alumni contacts and entrepreneurship support, all reachable by phone at +49 89 289 22245 or by e‑mail at studium@tum.de. Đời sống & môi trường: The Technical University of Munich (TUM) offers a vibrant campus life for international students with more than 250 student‑run clubs covering everything from sports, music and arts to cultural and professional societies; among these is an active Vietnamese Student Association (VSA TUM) that organizes language‑exchange meet‑ups, cultural celebrations such as Tet, information sessions on visas and scholarships, and a supportive network for Vietnamese newcomers, while the broader Asian‑European Society and other intercultural groups also welcome Vietnamese participants. Campus safety is a priority: the university grounds are monitored by a 24‑hour campus security service and the Munich Police, emergency call points are installed throughout the campuses, well‑lit pathways and bike‑lane safety training are provided by clubs like the TUM Cycling Club, and a dedicated International Office offers guidance on health insurance, personal safety, and crisis support for all international students.
