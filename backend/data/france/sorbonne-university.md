**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Sorbonne University admits international students through the national Campus France “Études en France” application process, requiring a valid passport, a copy of the secondary‑school leaving certificate (baccalaureate‑equivalent) or university diploma with official transcripts, and proof of French language proficiency—typically a B2 level for most programmes, rising to C1 for highly specialised or research‑intensive courses—demonstrated by TCF, TEF or DELF scores (or an English test such as IELTS/TOEFL where programmes are taught in English). While the university does not publish a universal GPA cut‑off, applicants are expected to have achieved at least a “good” standing in their previous studies (roughly 12‑14/20 or a comparable GPA of 2.5‑3.0 on a 4.0 scale) and to meet any faculty‑specific subject prerequisites (e.g., mathematics and physics for engineering, biology for health sciences). Candidates for arts, design or architecture programmes must submit a portfolio of recent work, and selective master’s or doctoral tracks may require a personal statement, letters of recommendation and, in some cases, an interview either in person or via video conference. After the faculty reviews the dossier and issues an admission letter, the student proceeds to obtain a French student visa (or a talent‑passport “chercheur” visa for doctoral candidates).

**3. Tiếng Anh**

**4. Học phí**

- Sorbonne University does not charge tuition fees to any students – French public universities are tuition‑free – and international students pay the same modest registration fees as French students; for the 2025‑2026 academic year the annual registration (or “tuition”) fees are €178 for a bachelor's programme, €254 for a master’s programme, €397 for a PhD year and €628 for an engineering year, all payable in euros, and the mandatory Student and Campus Life Contribution (CVEC) is €103 per year for 2024‑2025 (the most recent amount announced), so the total annual cost for an international student is roughly €281 for a bachelor’s cycle (€178 + €103), €357 for a master’s cycle (€254 + €103), €500 for a PhD (€397 + €103) and €731 for an engineering programme (€628 + €103); there are no additional tuition charges, only the registration fee and the CVEC contribution.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students who wish to enroll at Sorbonne University must first satisfy academic and language prerequisites: a recognized secondary‑school diploma (or bachelor’s degree for master programmes) that grants access to the French higher‑education system, and documented French proficiency ranging from B2 to C1 (e.g., TCF, TEF, DELF) for programmes taught in French, while English‑taught programmes may require IELTS/TOEFL scores. The university follows the European Bologna structure, offering a three‑year Bachelor (180 ECTS ≈ 60 ECTS per year) and a two‑year Master (120 ECTS ≈ 30 ECTS per semester), with each module (“môn”) assigned 5–10 ECTS based on workload, learning outcomes and assessment criteria. Courses are organised through a major‑minor system that allows students to combine a primary discipline with one or more thematic minors or a double‑cursus, and each module specifies prerequisite knowledge (e.g., completion of introductory courses before advanced seminars) and explicit learning outcomes covering knowledge, analytical skills, methodological competence and, where applicable, professional or research capabilities aligned with the European Qualifications Framework. Successful completion of the required ECTS credits and attainment of the defined learning outcomes leads to the award of the corresponding degree.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Sorbonne University in Paris enrolls more than 53,000 students, including roughly 11,500 international students, and offers a wide range of programmes across arts, humanities, sciences, engineering, medicine and health‑related fields; in the 2024 QS World University Rankings the university is positioned in the low‑70s globally (around 73rd) and in the 2024 Times Higher Education World University Rankings it sits in the low‑70s as well (approximately 71st), although the institution announced in September 2025 that it will cease submitting data for THE rankings from 2026 onward; subject‑specific rankings highlight its strong performance, with QS placing Sorbonne among the top 20 worldwide for Arts & Humanities, top 50 for Engineering & Technology and top 30 for Life Sciences & Medicine, while THE ranks it within the top 30 for Arts & Humanities, top 40 for Engineering and top 25 for Clinical, Health & Life Sciences; the university’s degrees are officially accredited by the French Ministry of Higher Education and Research and regularly evaluated by the High Council for Evaluation of Research and Higher Education (HCERES), ensuring full recognition within the European Higher Education Area.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To obtain a long‑stay student visa (VLS‑TS) for Sorbonne University you must first register on Campus France (or complete the “Études en France” procedure if it is available in your country), then fill out the France‑Visas online application, pay the €99 visa fee and book a consular or VFS appointment; the required documents include a valid passport (with at least three months validity beyond your stay and two blank pages), two recent passport‑size photos, the official “attestation d’inscription” from Sorbonne confirming your enrollment, the Campus France receipt, proof of accommodation for at least the first three months, and proof of financial resources showing a minimum of €615 per month (approximately €6 150 for a ten‑month stay) through recent bank statements, a scholarship letter or a sponsor’s guarantee; you must also provide evidence of health insurance covering you for the entire period (either enrolment in the French social security system or a private policy meeting the minimum coverage requirements) and, after arrival, validate the visa online via the ANEF portal and pay the €75 timbre fiscal as well as the €105 CVEC student contribution. Sau tốt nghiệp: After completing a French higher‑education diploma (generally a Master’s or an equivalent professional bachelor) at Sorbonne University you may stay in France for up to 12 months to look for a job or to create a company. The current scheme is the “job seeker / new business creator” residence permit (Carte de séjour temporaire « recherche d’emploi / création d’entreprise »), which replaced the former APS for most nationals; it is issued for a single 12‑month period, is not renewable, and authorises any type of paid activity as well as the establishment of a business that matches your field of study. Graduates who hold a student residence permit must submit the application to the prefecture (or sub‑prefecture) in France before that permit expires, or to the French consulate abroad within four years of receiving the diploma. Citizens of the EU/EEA, Switzerland or Algeria follow separate rules and do not need this permit, while graduates from countries that have concluded migration‑flow agreements with France—such as India, Tunisia, Morocco, Senegal, Gabon, Benin, Burkina Faso, Cape Verde, Macedonia, Montenegro, Serbia, Lebanon, Mauritius and Cameroon—can still request the provisional residence authorisation (APS) under the same conditions. **3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Sorbonne University offers a comprehensive career service for international students that includes dedicated counseling, workshops and an online platform where students can find internships, work‑study contracts (alternance) and apprenticeship placements; the university’s Student Jobs scheme allows up to 15 hours per week of paid employment compatible with study schedules and provides access to a wide range of opportunities in research labs, companies, public institutions and NGOs across sectors such as finance, consulting, technology, health, education and public administration, and the institution reports that roughly 90 % of its graduates secure a job or continue into further study within six months of completing their degree. Lương & thị trường: In France, master’s graduates earn roughly €39‑€40 k gross per year within the first six months after finishing their degree, rising to about €44‑€45 k after thirty months, while those who take international positions see slightly higher earnings of €42‑€58 k depending on experience; managers command a premium of roughly €1.7 k over engineers and about €2.2 k over other fields, with bonuses widening the gap further, and engineers and managers together represent the most sought‑after occupations in the labour market, especially in sectors such as digital technologies, data science, artificial intelligence, green‑energy transitions and health services, which are highlighted by APEC and France Travail as areas of strong demand; international graduates are considerably more mobile, with around 27 % taking jobs abroad—often returning to their home country—while domestic placement is heavily concentrated in Île‑de‑France, where about three‑quarters of positions are located, and a high proportion of graduates (around 70‑73 %) secure permanent or civil‑service contracts, indicating robust employability for Sorbonne University alumni.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Sorbonne University’s International Office, managed by the Department of International and European Affairs, runs an International Welcome Desk that serves as the central hub for all incoming international students, PhD candidates and academic staff, offering a multilingual (French and English) online platform that guides newcomers through every stage of their mobility—from pre‑arrival preparation to departure—by providing practical information sheets, personalized guidance, and key timelines; the office also organises on‑campus orientation sessions that introduce students to university life, academic resources and French cultural basics, assists with housing by coordinating access to university residences, private student‑housing partners and support for finding accommodation in Paris, and complements these services with a dedicated career service that delivers workshops on CV writing, interview techniques, internship and job search strategies, hosts regular career fairs with French and international employers, and offers one‑to‑one counseling to help international students integrate into the French job market. Đời sống & môi trường: Sorbonne University offers a lively campus life for international students, including a dedicated “Parismus” association and a Buddy System that pairs newcomers with local students to help with housing, language, cultural activities and integration into Parisian life, while the Maison de la Vie Étudiante on the Pierre et Marie Curie campus provides a social‑action team that assists with residence permits, health‑insurance registration (Ameli) and access to complementary mutuelle coverage, and offers a computer lab for administrative support; a variety of student clubs and societies—ranging from sports and arts to academic interest groups—are open to all, and Vietnamese students can connect through informal community networks and the broader Vietnamese student association that meets regularly for cultural events and mutual support, all within a safe environment overseen by campus security, 24/7 emergency services, and French health‑care provisions that ensure prompt medical assistance and reimbursement for any incidents.
