### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- RWTH Aachen University admits international students for two main intakes each year — the winter semester (starting in October) with a document‑submission deadline of 1 August and the summer semester (starting in April) with a deadline of 1 February; applications are submitted exclusively through the RWTHonline portal and are generally free of charge (except for RWTH Business School programmes, which require a €50 fee that is later deducted from the first‑semester tuition), and the typical procedure for non‑EU/EEA applicants is to create an online account, upload all required documents (recognised bachelor degree, transcripts, language certificates, etc.), have the International Office verify formal eligibility before the file is forwarded to the relevant examination board, receive the admission offer, sign the contract and pay the first tuition installment (or the €50 fee for Business School) to complete enrolment.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to RWTH Aachen must submit their complete application through uni‑assist, including a certified translation of their secondary‑school leaving certificate (or bachelor’s degree for master programmes) together with the Vorprüfungsdokumentation (VPD) that confirms the German‑equivalent grade point average; the university requires that this GPA be at least 2.5 on the German scale (or the equivalent of a “good” mark) and, if the converted average is lower, the shortfall can be compensated by a satisfactory TestAS result, which is mandatory for medicine and dentistry applicants; in addition, proof of German (TestDaF, DSH) or English (IELTS, TOEFL) language proficiency is required, and applicants must provide official transcripts of all previously completed courses (học bạ) and, where applicable, evidence of passing required entrance examinations for the chosen field; certain study programmes such as architecture, design or fine arts also request a portfolio and may schedule an interview to assess creative suitability, while most technical and engineering programmes do not require a portfolio or interview beyond the academic and language criteria.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- RWTH Aachen requires proof of English proficiency for all programmes taught in English, accepting TOEFL iBT ≥ 90 points, IELTS ≥ 6.0, Pearson Test of English Academic ≥ 60 points, Cambridge B2 First (grade B) or higher, or any school‑issued certificate that legally demonstrates B2 level English (or a placement test at the RWTH Language Centre of at least B2.2.1); applicants who have completed their secondary or university education in an English‑medium institution or whose previous degree was taught entirely in English are generally exempt from submitting an additional test, and the university also accepts the Duolingo English Test with a minimum score of 115 for those who meet the same exemption criteria.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at RWTH Aachen should budget roughly €1 100–€1 300 per month for living expenses, which translates to about €13 200–€15 600 per year. Monthly costs break down as follows: accommodation (including utilities) €400‑€600; food €200‑€300; health insurance €100‑€150; study materials (books, printing etc.) €20‑€40; internet and mobile phone €20‑€50; additional personal expenses such as clothing and hygiene €50‑€200. A transportation/mobility fee is included in the semester fee, which is part of the overall monthly budget. To obtain a residence permit, non‑EU students must prove a minimum monthly income of €992, corresponding to a blocked‑account requirement of €11 904 per year (the current legal minimum). Consequently, a realistic annual cost estimate for an international student living in Aachen ranges from approximately €13 200 to €15 600, covering accommodation, food, transport, health insurance, and other typical expenses.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International applicants to RWTH Aachen must hold a recognized first degree (typically a Bachelor’s) and, if they are from outside the EU/EEA, must achieve an overall grade of 2.5 or better (or provide a compensating TestAS score where required); EU/EEA candidates are judged primarily on their academic records. The university’s study programmes are organised in a modular structure where each module groups related courses and is completed by passing an exam or assignment, with mandatory and core modules defined in the programme‑specific Examination Regulations. Academic workload is measured in ECTS credits, with a standard semester comprising 30 ECTS points and each credit representing roughly 30 hours of combined classroom and self‑study work; students are expected to have earned at least two‑thirds of the total required credits by the end of the 2nd, 4th or 6th semester, otherwise they are invited to a counselling meeting. Learning outcomes for every module are explicitly stated in the module handbooks and must be demonstrated through the corresponding assessments, which contribute to the final grade point average and are prerequisites for progression to subsequent examinations and for the final thesis.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- RWTH Aachen University is a highly international German technical university that attracts students from 137 countries, and its engineering programmes are accredited by the German Accreditation Council (AQAS) and the engineering-specific agency ASIIN, ensuring compliance with national quality standards; in the 2025 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings the university placed 92nd overall worldwide and 36th in engineering, 60th in computer science and 71st in the natural sciences, while in the latest QS World University Rankings by Subject RWTH Aachen leads German institutions in Mechanical Engineering, Aeronautical Engineering, Manufacturing Engineering and Materials Science and ranks among the top four in Architecture, Civil Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Computer Science and Electrical Engineering.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/student visa: To study at RWTH Aachen as an international student you must prove you have at least €11,208 per year (≈ €1,091 per month) available, most commonly by opening a blocked‑account (Sperrkonto) in Germany before you apply for your visa and presenting the official confirmation that shows the total amount deposited and the monthly allowance; alternatives such as a scholarship award letter, a legally binding third‑party guarantee, or proof of parental income are also accepted, but the blocked account is the standard method recommended by the DAAD and the study‑in‑germany portal. Once you are in Germany you are allowed to work up to 120 full days or 240 half‑days per year (roughly 20 hours per week) in a part‑time job, and you may also take a student‑assistant (Hiwi) position at RWTH Aachen with no strict hour limit, provided the work does not interfere with your studies and you continue to meet the financial‑resource requirement each year. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have successfully completed a degree (Bachelor’s, Master’s or PhD) at RWTH Aachen University are eligible for Germany’s post‑study job‑seeker residence permit (Section 20 AufenthG), which allows them to stay in the country for up to 18 months to look for a job that matches their qualifications; during this period they may work in any employment, full‑time or part‑time, to support themselves, provided they show proof of sufficient financial means (e.g., a blocked account or a monthly income of at least €1 091), have valid health insurance, a registered German address and the official proof of graduation, and must apply for the permit as soon as they receive their final exam results, typically within three months of completing their studies.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: RWTH Aachen University provides extensive support for international students seeking internships, Werk‑student (working‑student) positions and post‑graduation employment through its dedicated Career Services for Internationals, which runs a three‑semester “Career Success in Germany” certificate and the Fit for Success@RWTH Aachen initiative that connects students with more than 200 partner companies in the Aachen‑Düsseldorf‑Cologne region; most study programmes include a mandatory internship or research stay and the university reports that roughly 94 % of its graduates secure a job or begin a PhD within six months of completing their degree, with the most common career fields after graduation being mechanical and automotive engineering, electrical and computer engineering, materials science, information technology, energy and consulting, while language courses, job‑search workshops and personalised counseling further help international students transition into these roles. Lương & thị trường: RWTH Aachen’s official data show that doctoral positions in high‑demand fields such as engineering, computer‑science and applied mathematics are paid under the TV‑L E13 tariff, which currently yields a gross monthly salary of roughly €4 000 for a single employee and rises to about €4 300 after the first year, with a 39.83‑hour work week, 30 days of paid vacation and full social‑security contributions; less‑in‑demand occupations are often compensated at reduced percentages (e.g., 50 % or 75 % of the full TV‑L rate). The university’s graduate profile indicates that Germany graduates about 50 000 international students each year, with roughly 40 % earning degrees in engineering and over half in STEM overall; employment rates for these graduates climb from around 35 % at program start to 55 % by graduation and reach approximately 70 % within three months after completing their studies, while about two‑thirds intend to remain in Germany, supported by an 18‑month post‑graduation residence permit that facilitates job seeking.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: RWTH Aachen’s International Office (Department 2.0) is the central contact point for incoming and outgoing international students, offering comprehensive advice on applications, study planning, and housing and coordinating orientation programmes such as the Welcome Week, FAQ portals, and mentoring schemes like BeBuddy and StaffMentor; within the university’s Division 2.2 International Support Services, a dedicated Housing Advice Service (phone +49 241 80‑90681, email housing@rwth-aachen.de) assists students in finding accommodation, while the Humboldt‑Haus provides further housing information, and the Studierendenwerk Aachen operates university dormitories, dining facilities and social counseling for all students; for career development, the Career Service for International Students runs targeted seminars, a certificate programme and one‑on‑one advising to help students enter the German labour market, with a specialised International Career Service team that can be contacted via e‑mail for questions or employer partnerships. Đời sống & môi trường: More than 30 % of RWTH Aachen’s roughly 45 000 students come from abroad, and the university supports a lively campus life through dozens of student‑run clubs and societies that cover sports, music, culture, engineering projects, volunteering and hobby interests; the International Meeting Point at Humboldt Haus serves as a central hub where newcomers can meet peers, join language tandems and discover existing groups such as the Vietnamese Student Association, which organises cultural festivals, Tet celebrations and networking events for Vietnamese students and anyone interested in Vietnamese culture. In addition to these clubs, the International Office and International Student Center provide practical assistance on visa, housing and academic matters, while campus safety is overseen by a dedicated Campus Security service that operates a 24 hour emergency line, patrols key locations, runs regular information sessions on personal safety and scams, and offers a mobile app for quick reporting of incidents, ensuring that international students—including the Vietnamese community—can study and socialize in a secure environment.
