### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Sungkyunkwan University admits international students in two main semesters — Spring (March‑May) and Fall (September‑December). For the 2026 Fall intake, undergraduate applicants submit their online application and the non‑refundable fee of KRW 100,000 (≈ USD 100) between March 4 10:00 AM and April 3 10:00 PM KST; graduate applicants follow the same dates, after which written tests or interviews are held (e.g., April 18) and admission decisions are announced (e.g., May 6). The Spring intake follows a similar timeline shifted earlier in the year, with applications typically opening in early December and closing by late January, the same KRW 100,000/US 100 fee, and subsequent interviews in February and results in late March. After receiving an admission notice, candidates must pay tuition (invoice issued mid‑July for Fall, mid‑March for Spring), complete enrollment registration within the prescribed window, obtain a visa, and submit the required documents (passport copy, academic transcripts, language test scores, and any department‑specific forms) to the Office of International Student Services. The whole process is conducted online via the SKKU Global Admissions portal, where applicants upload files, pay the fee electronically, receive a confirmation number, and track subsequent steps such as interview scheduling and final enrollment.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Sungkyunkwan University admits undergraduate international students who hold a recognized high‑school diploma (or an official graduation‑equivalency certificate) and can submit an official transcript of all high‑school grades that is no older than one year and, if issued abroad, must be authenticated by an apostille (or the equivalent confirmation for non‑apostille countries); applicants must also provide a personal statement in Korean or English, a valid passport copy, a recent photo, and meet the academic minimum of roughly a 2.5 / 4.0 GPA (or the equivalent 3.0 / 4.5 or 3.3 / 5.0 scale) together with proof of language proficiency—any one of TOEFL iBT ≥ 80, TOEFL ITP ≥ 550, IELTS ≥ 6.0, CEFR B2, CET‑4, TOPIK ≥ 3, or Duolingo ≥ 110—and may be required to sit for an interview if the admissions board deems it necessary; a portfolio is not a general requirement unless the chosen major specifically asks for one (e.g., design‑related programs).

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Hàn (TOPIK)**

- Sungkyunkwan University requires international undergraduate applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least TOEFL iBT 80 or IELTS 5.5 for most programmes, while certain competitive tracks such as Global Economics, Global Business Administration, and Software raise the bar to TOEFL iBT 90 or IELTS 6.5; scholarship applicants for English‑language excellence must meet higher thresholds (e.g., IELTS 7.0/TOEFL iBT 95 for a 50 % tuition waiver and IELTS 8.0/TOEFL iBT 110 for a 100 % waiver). Korean‑language requirements stipulate a minimum TOPIK level 3 for admission to Korean‑English selective tracks, with TOPIK 5 or 6 required for Korean‑language scholarship eligibility (TOPIK 5 for a 50 % waiver, TOPIK 6 for a 100 % waiver). English test scores may be waived for students whose nationality uses English as a mother‑tongue or who can provide official proof of completing secondary or higher education in an English‑medium institution; otherwise only TOEFL iBT and IELTS are accepted, as PTE Academic and Duolingo English Test are not recognized by SKKU.

**4. Học phí**

- International applicants to Sungkyunkwan University pay a non‑refundable application fee of KRW 100,000 and a one‑time entrance fee of KRW 991,000 (charged only for the first semester); tuition is billed each semester and the annual cost depends on the chosen program, ranging from KRW 3,651,000 × 2 = KRW 7,302,000 per year for Humanities, Social Sciences and Business, KRW 4,080,000 × 2 = KRW 8,160,000 for Global Economics, KRW 5,680,000 × 2 = KRW 11,360,000 for Global Business Administration, KRW 4,784,000 × 2 = KRW 9,568,000 for Film, Television & Multimedia, Theatre, Fashion Design, Software and Biomedical Engineering, KRW 4,236,000 × 2 = KRW 8,472,000 for Natural Sciences and Sport Science, and KRW 4,784,000 × 2 = KRW 9,568,000 for Engineering, Architecture and Electronic & Electrical Engineering; therefore the total first‑year expense (tuition plus entrance fee) falls roughly between KRW 8.3 million and KRW 12.4 million, with additional costs such as dormitory fees, health insurance, and student activity fees applying separately.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Sungkyunkwan University can expect to spend roughly 11 – 12 million KRW per year on living expenses. Dormitory fees are the largest item: on‑campus housing (e.g., G‑House) costs about 1,500 USD for four months, which works out to roughly 4,500 USD (≈ 6.0 million KRW) for a full year; off‑campus twin rooms (C‑House/I‑House) are about 1,700 USD per four months (≈ 5,100 USD or 6.8 million KRW annually). Food costs for a typical student diet are around 300,000 KRW per month (≈ 3.6 million KRW per year). Public transport (subway, bus, campus shuttle) averages about 60,000 KRW per month (≈ 720,000 KRW annually). National Health Insurance for D‑2 visa holders is billed at roughly 60 USD per month, or about 720 USD per year (≈ 965,000 KRW). Adding these components gives an estimated total yearly cost of about 11.3 million KRW for on‑campus dorms and up to 12.1 million KRW for off‑campus twin rooms.

**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): To obtain a D‑2 (overseas study) visa for Sungkyunkwan University you must submit the HiKorea‑downloaded visa application form, a copy of your passport, a recent passport‑size color photo, the university’s Certificate of Admission, proof of tuition payment, a residence document and a financial statement that demonstrates sufficient funds for living expenses (typically a bank‑statement showing at least 1,150,000 KRW per month of intended stay or an overall amount of about USD 20,000, including tuition and any scholarship); after entering Korea you are required to register your D‑2 status within 90 days at the local immigration office, obtain an Alien Registration Card and, once you have the card, you may work part‑time up to 20 hours per week (subject to university approval and a work‑permit application through the immigration office). Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): The D‑10 “Job‑Seeker” visa allows a foreign graduate of a Korean university such as Sungkyunkwan University to remain in South Korea to look for employment for an initial period of six months, which can be extended once for an additional six months if sufficient evidence of active job‑search activities (e.g., interview invitations, application records, participation in career programs) is provided through the Hi‑Korea portal (hikorea.go.kr); eligibility requires the holder of a D‑2 student visa to have completed a bachelor’s degree or higher at a Korean institution (or an associate degree or higher from abroad) and to submit proof of financial capacity, a resume, the degree certificate, and a job‑seeking plan within 30 days of the D‑2 expiration, after which, once a qualified job offer is secured, the D‑10 holder can transition to an E‑7 “Specialized Employment” visa with the employer’s sponsorship, which generally requires a relevant degree, at least two years of professional experience in the field, a contract specifying salary and job title, and the employer’s foreign‑worker hiring plan.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU) in South Korea runs a structured internship program for international students, offering a four‑week, two‑credit research internship each summer (late July to late August) on either the Seoul campus (humanities and social sciences) or the Suwon campus (natural sciences and engineering) in fields such as psychology, business/economics, computer science, engineering, biology, physics, chemistry and related disciplines; participation is limited to students enrolled in at least three International Summer Semester courses, including the Research Internship (ISS3320). In addition to this summer placement, SKKU’s Office of International Affairs and Career Development Center provide co‑op and year‑long internship opportunities with partner companies and research institutes, and support graduates through job‑search workshops, networking events and a dedicated startup incubator. The university consistently reports a graduate employment rate of roughly 94 % within six months of graduation, with alumni entering a wide range of professions—including finance, consulting, technology, manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, education and entrepreneurship—both in South Korea’s leading multinational firms and abroad, reflecting strong outcomes for international graduates. Lương & thị trường: South Korea’s 2024 graduate labour market shows that Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU) leads comprehensive universities with a 71.3 % overall employment rate and an 89.2 % maintenance‑employment rate, indicating that most alumni remain in stable jobs after graduation; the typical starting monthly salary for SKKU graduates is around 3.2 million won (≈ $2,250), while among international graduates in the country 33.3 % earn more than 3 million won a month and the overall hiring rate for foreign graduates rose to 13.8 % in 2024, reflecting growing employer interest in globally trained talent, especially in high‑demand sectors such as information technology, artificial‑intelligence and software development, semiconductor and electronics engineering, biotechnology and life‑science research, finance and consulting, where salaries tend to start at or above the 3 million‑won threshold and career prospects are strongest.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Sungkyunkwan University’s Office of International Student Services (ISS) is the central hub for all support offered to international degree‑seeking students, handling everything from pre‑arrival orientation and on‑campus housing to career development and Korean language instruction. New students receive an orientation program that introduces campus life, visa procedures, health services, transportation, and academic resources, after which the ISS assists with securing dormitory accommodation through its dedicated dormitory office. The office also runs the RISE (Career Development) program, providing career counseling, internship placement, job‑search workshops, and alumni networking to help students transition into the Korean job market. For language preparation, the university’s Language Institute offers structured Korean language courses (including beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels) and TOPIK preparation, coordinated through the ISS’s Korean‑proficiency support unit. All of these services are overseen by a team of specialist staff, reachable via the main ISS email (dokyong@skku.edu) and phone (+82‑2‑760‑0020), and are designed to ensure that international students can study, live, and work in Korea with comprehensive guidance and resources. Đời sống & môi trường: Sungkyunkwan University offers a comprehensive support system for international students that includes an International Student ID Card, dedicated counseling services, a campus healthcare center, student insurance, and a 24‑hour campus security patrol, plus an easily accessible lost‑and‑found office for personal safety; the university’s Campus Life portal lists a wide range of student clubs and organizations such as campus media, cheerleading, wind orchestra, cultural societies and numerous interest‑based groups, and within this framework there is an active Vietnamese Student Association (often called the SKKU Vietnam Club) that organizes regular social gatherings, language‑exchange events, cultural festivals and provides a peer‑network for Vietnamese students to share information on housing, visas, academics and daily life, while the broader International Student Support Center coordinates orientation programs, Korean‑language courses, mentorship pairing and emergency assistance, ensuring that international students—including those from Vietnam—have clear channels for safety concerns, health services, and community engagement throughout both the Humanities and Social Sciences campus in Seoul and the Natural Sciences campus in Suwon.
