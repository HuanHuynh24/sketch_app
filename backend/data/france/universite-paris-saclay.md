### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Université Paris‑Saclay admits international students for a September (Fall) intake and, for many master’s programmes, a January (Spring) intake; applications for the September intake are submitted online via the eCandidat portal and must be completed by early May (typically around 5 May) for the 2026 academic year, while January‑intake applications close in early November, and the online application fee is €75 for bachelor’s programmes and €100 for master’s programmes; the process consists of creating an eCandidat account, filling out the personal and academic sections, uploading required documents (identification, transcripts, proof of language proficiency and, where applicable, a motivation letter), paying the fee, and then waiting for the university’s admission decision, after which admitted students create an account on the Études en France portal to confirm acceptance and complete enrolment formalities.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students who wish to enroll at Université Paris‑Saclay must first submit an application through the national Parcoursup platform if they are from the EU, EEA, Switzerland, Monaco or Andorra; students from other countries must complete a Preliminary Request for Admission (DAP) for a first‑year Licence, and the dossier must include a certified copy of the secondary‑school leaving certificate (or its foreign equivalent to the French Baccalauréat), an official transcript of all grades, and proof of language proficiency (typically a DELF/DALF for French‑taught programmes or TOE‑FL/IELTS for English‑taught programmes). Although French universities do not use a GPA scale, the academic record is evaluated for overall performance and relevance to the chosen field; for selective programmes such as engineering, architecture or design, a portfolio of previous work and/or a subject‑specific entrance exam may be required, and some master’s programmes may request an interview or additional written tests to assess motivation and suitability.

- International applicants to Université Paris‑Saclay must demonstrate at least a B2 (upper‑intermediate) level of English, which can be proven by any of the following standardised tests: IELTS 6.5, TOEFL iBT 90 (or TOEFL PBT 567), Cambridge FCE, or an equivalent score on other recognised exams such as PTE Academic 58 – 60 or Duolingo English Test 115 – 120; however, students whose bachelor’s degree was taught entirely in English are exempt from submitting a separate language test and may instead provide an official certificate from their university confirming the English‑language instruction.

**4. Học phí**

- International students at Université Paris‑Saclay pay the same statutory tuition rates as French‑national students for the 2025‑2026 academic year: €178 per year for Bachelor programmes, €254 per year for Master programmes and €397 per year for Doctoral programmes, all amounts expressed in euros (EUR). In addition, every student must contribute the Student and Campus Life Contribution (CVEC) of €105 per year, payable to CROUS before completing administrative registration and obtaining a CVEC certificate. The total annual cost therefore amounts to €283 for a Bachelor degree (tuition + CVEC), €359 for a Master degree and €502 for a Doctorate. A separate administrative registration fee is required to finalise enrolment, but its exact amount is not detailed in the available data.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Université Paris‑Saclay is a French public university whose degrees are accredited by the French Ministry of Higher Education and Research and recognised throughout the European Higher Education Area; it is also a member of several international university networks such as CESAER, EUA, LERU, U7 + and U21. In the 2024 Times Higher Education World University Rankings the institution is placed 58th worldwide, while in the 2024 QS World University Rankings by Subject it ranks 13th globally (2nd in France) for Mathematics, 14th (1st in France) for Physics & Astronomy, 53rd (1st in France) for Materials Science, 60th (2nd in France) for Biological Sciences, 67th (2nd in France) for Pharmacy & Pharmacology, 75th (3rd in France) for Chemistry, 93rd (2nd in France) for Engineering – Chemistry, 102nd in the broad Life Sciences & Medicine area (including Medicine), 147th for Economics and between 151‑200th for Law. The university also appears in the QS overall rankings around the top 100‑150 range, reflecting its strong international reputation and the large proportion of overseas students it attracts.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To obtain a VLS‑TS (Visa Long Séjour valant Titre de Séjour) as an international student at Université Paris‑Saclay you must submit a completed Cerfa 14571‑05 form, a valid passport with at least two blank pages, two recent passport‑size photos, your university enrollment certificate (attestation d’inscription), a Campus France receipt (attestation de pré‑inscription or Études en France validation), and pay the visa fee (currently €99 at the consulate) plus the €75 fiscal stamp for the residence‑permit tax; you must also provide proof of financial resources showing at least €615 per month (roughly €6 150 for a 10‑month academic year) via recent bank statements, a scholarship award or a guarantor’s attestation, and evidence of health insurance that is either a private policy covering you in France or a declaration that you will register with the French social‑security system upon arrival, all of which are uploaded and validated through the official France‑Visas portal (administration‑etrangers‑en‑france.interieur.gouv.fr). Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Université Paris‑Saclay can remain in France after completing their studies by requesting either the temporary “job seeker / new business creator” residence permit (Carte de séjour temporaire « Recherche d’emploi / création d’entreprise », often called the RECE) or, if they are citizens of a country that has signed a migration‑flow agreement with France (e.g., Gabon, Benin, Tunisia, Mauritius, Cape Verde, Burkina Faso, North Macedonia, Montenegro, Serbia, India), an Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour (APS). The RECE is a 12‑month, non‑renewable permit that authorises the holder to look for a job and to create a company in a field related to their training; it requires submission at the prefecture of a valid passport, birth certificate, three recent photos, the current student residence permit, a diploma or proof of graduation within the last year, proof of health insurance, proof of address (≤ 3 months old), and a document demonstrating the intent to start a business, together with a €75 tax‑stamp. The APS also lasts 12 months (with a possible extension) and similarly allows job‑search activities for graduates from French higher‑education institutions; it is accessed via the same prefectural procedures and is listed on the official Service‑public portal (F17319). **3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Université Paris‑Saclay regularly hosts international students for internships across its 220 research laboratories, with any placement lasting more than two months qualifying for a statutory stipend and requiring a formal internship agreement; students can also apply for Erasmus+ International Credit Mobility grants that provide up to €900 per month to help cover living costs. The university’s graduate school of engineering and systems sciences reports that roughly 80 % of its graduates secure employment before completing their studies, and the employment rate rises to about 90 % within six months after graduation, with 17 % of graduates choosing to pursue a PhD; the majority enter the private sector at executive‑level positions, primarily on permanent contracts (≈ 87 %). Typical post‑graduation roles include systems engineer, development engineer, cybersecurity consultant, smart‑grid project engineer, design engineer, nuclear engineer, digital manager, statistician, IT expert and research engineer in high‑performance computing, reflecting the strong industry focus of the university’s career services and placement programmes. Lương & thị trường: Six months after completing a master’s degree in France, graduates earn on average about €39,700 gross per year, rising to roughly €44,400 after thirty months, while those who work abroad earn about €42,400 at six months and €58,400 after thirty months; Paris‑Saclay graduates, particularly from the engineering‑focused Polytech programmes, receive an average gross annual salary of approximately €45 000 (including bonuses) twenty‑four months after graduation, with managers earning roughly €1 700 more than engineers and about €2 200 above other specialisations, and the labour market shows the strongest demand for engineers, digital‑technology, data‑science and AI roles, followed by management and health‑science positions as highlighted by APEC’s France Travail benchmarks; international students are highly mobile, with about 27 % of foreign‑origin graduates finding employment abroad (≈12 % in the EU and 15 % outside it) and 58 % of those returning to their home country after their French studies, while overall graduate employment remains concentrated in Île‑de‑France—nearly three‑quarters of all positions—although roughly 61 % of engineers secure jobs in other French regions.
