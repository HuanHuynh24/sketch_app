**Tóm tắt thông tin trường**

- James Cook University (JCU) is a public university in Australia, headquartered in Townsville, Queensland (with additional campuses in Cairns and Brisbane), and its official website is https://www.jcu.edu.au/.

## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- James Cook University (JCU) accepts international applications through an online portal where you first choose a course, confirm you meet the entry requirements, and then follow the five‑step “Choose – Check – Explore – Check dates – Meet the Genuine Temporary Entrant criteria” process; the application fee is generally AUD 150 for undergraduate programmes and AUD 200 for postgraduate programmes, payable at the time you submit your online form. For the 2026 intake the key deadlines are 27 November 2025 for Trimester 1 (the main intake), 22 December 2025 for Study Period 1, and 14 March 2026 for Trimester 2 (with a corresponding Study Period 2 deadline later in March); these dates are the latest you can submit all required documents, pay the fee and receive an offer before the start of each semester.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- James Cook University requires international applicants to provide official transcripts (học bạ) showing completion of a recognized secondary‑school qualification or, for postgraduate study, an undergraduate degree, together with English‑language evidence (e.g., IELTS 6.5 overall with ≥6.0 in each component or equivalent) and a minimum academic standing of a credit average – 5.0 on a 7‑point GPA scale (≈65 %). For undergraduate entry the university lists subject‑specific prerequisites (e.g., a minimum grade of credit in a research methods or analysis subject for some health and science courses) and may require a portfolio or interview for design‑related programmes; certain professional degrees (such as Veterinary Science, Medicine, Nursing, Speech Pathology, Occupational Therapy and Psychology) also mandate an interview (in person or via video) and may ask for additional evidence of suitability. All applicants must submit their official grade‑point transcripts, any relevant exam results (điểm thi), and meet any course‑specific subject requirements (môn yêu cầu) noted on the JCU programme pages.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Entry requirements for international students - JCU Australia](https://www.jcu.edu.au/applying-to-jcu/international/coursework/academic-and-english-language-entry-requirements) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[2025-International-Guide.pdf](https://www.jcu.edu.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0018/2205414/2025-International-Guide.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[jcuprd1_065093.pdf](https://www.jcu.edu.au/international-students/media/pdf/jcuprd1_065093.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International students \| Study at James Cook University](https://www.jcu.edu.au/international-students) (Cao, score 0.99)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- James Cook University (JCU) requires international applicants to demonstrate a minimum level of English proficiency that is typically met by an overall IELTS score of 6.5 with no band less than 6.0, a TOEFL iBT score of 79 with no component below 13, a PTE Academic score of 58 with no sub‑score under 50, or a Duolingo English Test result of 115 or higher; however, for entry into the university’s English Language Preparatory Program the thresholds are lower (IELTS 4.5 with no band under 4.0, TOEFL iBT 32 with no component under 7, and PTE Academic 30 with no component under 22). Applicants can obtain a waiver of these English‑language requirements if they have completed at least two consecutive years of full‑time study in an English‑medium institution, hold a degree that was taught entirely in English, or have successfully finished an approved English‑language pathway programme, and similar conditions apply for specific scholarships or faculty‑specific exemptions.

**4. Học phí**

- For international students at James Cook University the fees are quoted in Australian dollars as annual tuition amounts (24 credit points per year); undergraduate programmes for 2023 range from about AUD 32,960 per year (e.g., Bachelor of Laws) to AUD 43,960 per year (e.g., Bachelor of Marine Science) with many courses such as the Bachelor of Information Technology at AUD 37,960, while postgraduate coursework fees are typically AUD 16,480 for a one‑semester Graduate Certificate and similar per‑year rates for master’s degrees, meaning a typical three‑year bachelor's degree costs roughly AUD 120‑130 k in total and a two‑year master’s around AUD 30‑35 k; the standard application fee for international applicants is AUD 150, and students should also expect an annual student services levy of about AUD 300 plus mandatory Overseas Student Health Cover, which varies with age and duration of study.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- James Cook University (JCU) is ranked #440 in the world in the QS World University Rankings 2026 and is placed in the top 100 globally for Earth and Marine Sciences in the QS subject tables for the same year; it also holds a 5‑Star rating for teaching and employability in the QS Stars system. In the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, JCU falls within the 801‑1000 band in the most recent edition (2024). The university is fully accredited by Australia’s Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) and its professional programmes carry additional accreditation from the relevant Australian bodies, such as the Australian Nursing and Midwifery Accreditation Council for nursing, the Australian Medical Council for medicine, and the Australian Institute of Architects for architecture.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Student visa (subclass 500) for international students requires proof of sufficient financial capacity, typically a minimum of AUD 29,710 to cover 12 months of living costs, travel expenses of roughly AUD 2,000‑3,000, the first year of tuition fees and the cost of Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the whole stay, with additional amounts required for any accompanying partner (about AUD 10,394) or children (about AUD 4,449 per child plus school fees if applicable); OSHC must be maintained for the entire visa period, and visa holders may work up to 48 hours per fortnight while classes are in session (unlimited during scheduled course breaks), although students undertaking a master's by research or a doctoral degree have no work‑hour limits, and James Cook University is an officially recognised Australian education provider that issues the required Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) for the subclass 500 visa as outlined by the Department of Home Affairs and Study in Australia guidelines. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) – specifically the Post‑Higher Education Work (post‑study work) stream – lets international students who have graduated from a CRICOS‑registered Australian institution such as James Cook University live, study and work in Australia temporarily, with unrestricted work rights for the duration of the visa; most graduates receive a stay of two years for a bachelor’s or master’s degree (except a master’s by research, which also qualifies for two years) and three years for a doctoral qualification, while eligible regional graduates can add an additional one‑to‑two years, and from 1 July 2023 graduates whose qualification appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List may apply for a further two‑year extension, giving up to five years in total, and Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders can stay for five years; the visa must be lodged within six months of the official course completion date, requires health insurance, and allows the inclusion of a partner and dependent children who meet health and character criteria.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: James Cook University’s Careers and Employability service provides international students with a structured pathway to gain work‑integrated learning through professional experience placements, industry‑based internships and co‑operative education projects that are embedded in many undergraduate and postgraduate programmes, and the university reports that these experiences significantly boost graduate outcomes, with JCU ranking No. 1 in Australia for median graduate salary and No. 2 for full‑time employment among Australian universities, and an overall graduate employment rate of around 90 % of graduates securing full‑time work or further study within four months of completing their degree. Lương & thị trường: James Cook University graduates earn a median full‑time salary of roughly AU $72,000 six months after completing a bachelor’s degree, with higher earnings in health sciences, engineering, information technology and environmental studies, while entry‑level academic staff start at about AU $80,000 and senior lecturers reach AU $150,000 plus; international graduates earn noticeably less, typically AU $12,000‑$15,000 below domestic peers in fields such as business and management, and they face higher rates of under‑employment because visa restrictions limit work hours, yet the Queensland State Migration Plan and the international postgraduate alumni sponsorship scheme actively target skilled occupations where demand is strongest—particularly marine and tropical science, nursing, physiotherapy, pharmacy, accounting, professional engineering and IT—aligning with Jobs and Skills Australia data that highlights health, engineering, information technology and construction as the top labour‑market demand sectors for both domestic and international graduates.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: James Cook University’s International Student Office runs a dedicated International Student Orientation program alongside the general JCU Orientation week, offering sessions that introduce campus life, academic expectations, cultural adjustment and practical information for new overseas enrolments; it also provides housing support by maintaining a curated list of approved accommodation providers in Cairns and Townsville and assisting international students in finding suitable on‑campus or off‑campus residence; in addition, the university’s Careers and Employability team delivers tailored career‑planning services for international students, including employability workshops, resume and interview preparation, job‑ready programmes and one‑to‑one guidance to help them transition into the Australian workforce. Đời sống & môi trường: James Cook University provides a vibrant campus life for international students, offering dozens of clubs and sporting teams through the JCU Student Association—including cultural groups such as a Vietnamese Student Society that organises language‑exchange evenings, Vietnamese festivals, and a peer‑support network for Vietnamese‑origin students—while safety is a top priority with a dedicated Safety and Wellbeing unit, 24‑hour personal‑safety service (Sonder), campus security patrols, comprehensive orientation briefings and a student‑mentor programme, plus assistance with accommodation, health cover and wellbeing resources to help all international students feel safe and supported throughout their studies.
