### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students wishing to enroll at Université Grenoble Alpes must first check whether their country participates in the “Portal for studies in France” (PEF) agreement; if it does, applications open in October 2025 through the PEF portal and each embassy sets its own deadline, while non‑PEF students apply via the French‑language MonMaster platform (for first‑year master programmes) with an opening date of 17 February 2026 and a fixed deadline of 16 March 2026 (the same dates apply for French students), and for second‑year master tracks non‑PEF applicants use the eCandidat system opening in March 2026 (no specific deadline is listed). The application process generally requires creating an online account, completing the preliminary admission request (DAP Blanche for non‑PEF cases), uploading academic transcripts, proof of language proficiency and a copy of the passport, and paying the non‑EU application fee, which is typically around €75 (with fee‑exemption possibilities for eligible candidates). After submission, applicants await an admission decision before proceeding to administrative registration and tuition payment, noting that differentiated tuition fees apply to non‑EU students.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to Université Grenoble Alpes must hold a foreign diploma that gives access to higher‑education at the baccalaureate level (or its equivalent) and provide official transcripts (học bạ) showing their grades; while the university does not publish a fixed GPA cut‑off, the transcript is assessed and a minimum average around 2.5 / 4.0 (or the local equivalent) is typically expected for competitive programmes. Admission also requires proof of language proficiency: for French‑taught programmes a French B2 level (DELF/DALF) is required, and for English‑taught programmes an IELTS ≥ 6.0 or TOEFL iBT ≥ 80 is normally required. Applicants must submit the relevant exam scores (e.g., national high‑school exams, SAT/ACT if applicable) and meet any subject prerequisites specific to the chosen field (e.g., mathematics for engineering, biology for life sciences). Applications are made through the Campus France portal for countries that have a PEF agreement, otherwise via a paper “Demande d’Admission Préalable” (DAP) sent to the university; some creative or design programmes additionally ask for a portfolio and may conduct an interview to assess artistic competence.

**4. Học phí**

- At Université Grenoble Alpes international students pay the same statutory tuition rates as French‑resident students, which for the 2025‑2026 academic year are €178 per year for a Licence (Bachelor’s) program, €254 per year for a Master’s degree, €628 per year for the preparatory engineering cycles (3rd‑5th year), and €397 per year for a doctoral programme; these amounts apply to the main degree and are reduced for an additional qualification (e.g., €118, €166, €419 and €264 respectively). In addition, every student must pay the national “Contribution Vie Étudiante et Campus” (CVEC) of €100 per year before enrollment, which is the only mandatory campus‑life contribution. There is no separate university‑specific application fee; the registration fee you pay at enrolment combines the tuition amount for your programme level plus the €100 CVEC, so the total annual cost for an international student is the sum of the relevant tuition figure and €100.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For international students at Université Grenoble Alpes the typical annual budget is roughly €9 300 – €10 200, depending on housing choices: rent for a student studio or shared flat runs about €400‑€600 per month (≈€4 800‑€7 200 a year) plus a security deposit equal to one month’s rent, agency fees up to €10 / m² and an inventory fee of €3 / m² (≈€200 one‑off for a 15 m² unit), a €50 connection fee for electricity and around €50‑€60 per month for utilities, and home‑insurance of €40‑€100 per year; food costs are €200‑€300 per month (≈€2 400‑€3 600 yearly); a monthly public‑transport pass costs about €60 (≈€720 per year); mobile phone/internet ≈€30 per month (≈€360 yearly); French health insurance is free for international students, though a complementary private plan may add €100‑€200 per year, so the first‑year total (including the one‑off deposit and agency fees) is close to €10 000 and subsequent years (without those start‑up costs) around €9 300. ## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Université Grenoble Alpes (UGA) is a French public research university that consistently ranks among the top French institutions in global league tables: in the most recent QS World University Rankings it sits in the 200‑250 band worldwide, while the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings place it in the 201‑250 range; in the 2026 QS subject rankings UGA is highlighted as one of the leading French universities and appears within the global top 100 for Natural Sciences, and it is recognised across dozens of disciplines with positions in the top 50, top 150 and top 200 depending on the field. The university is fully accredited by the French Ministry of Higher Education, Research and Innovation and its engineering programmes are accredited by the Commission des Titres d’Ingénieur (CTI), while its business and management curricula hold AACSB and AMBA accreditations; all degrees conform to the European Higher Education Area (Bologna Process) standards. With about 57 000 students, roughly 30 % are international, giving UGA a vibrant multinational community that benefits from its strong research output, extensive patent portfolio and high innovation rankings.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To obtain a VLS‑TS (long‑stay student visa) for studying at Université Grenoble Alpes you must first register on the Campus France (or EEF) portal for your country, obtain an official admission letter from the university and then submit a complete visa dossier that includes a valid passport with blank pages, recent passport‑size photos that meet France‑Visas specifications, proof of accommodation in France for the first weeks (e.g., student‑residence reservation, rental contract or host attestation), proof of sufficient financial resources – normally at least €6 150 for a 10‑month academic year shown by recent bank statements, a scholarship certificate or a guarantor’s attestation with tax returns – and valid health‑insurance coverage, either a European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) or an S1 form, or private travel/medical insurance that covers you until you can register on the French state health‑insurance site (etudiant‑etranger.ameli.fr) after arrival; the visa fee (currently €99) is paid at the appointment, and once the VLS‑TS is issued you must validate it online within the prescribed period (using the ANEF portal) to convert it into a residence permit for its validity of up to 12 months. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed a master’s degree or higher at Université Grenoble Alpes can remain in France after their studies by obtaining the “Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour” (APS), officially titled the “job‑seeker / new‑business‑creator” residence permit, which is administered through the local préfecture with guidance from Campus France and the service‑public website; the APS is normally granted for twelve months (extendable to a second twelve‑month period for citizens of countries that have bilateral migration agreements with France, such as India) and must be applied for before the current student residence permit expires, requiring proof of the French diploma, a valid passport, proof of residence, sufficient financial resources, health insurance and a confirmation of graduation from Campus France, and it permits the holder to work full‑time or part‑time, undertake internships, and create a company in a field related to their studies, after which they may switch to a longer‑term work visa (e.g., the talent‑passport or a CTT) if they secure employment.
