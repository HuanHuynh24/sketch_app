### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International applicants to Radboud University must first register in the national Dutch enrollment system Studielink, where they choose the desired programme and indicate the intended intake; for the September intake the Studielink window normally opens on 1 October and closes on 1 November, after which a personalised OSIRIS‑Application login is sent by email and the applicant has up to 56 days (or until the programme‑specific deadline) to upload all required documents, pay the non‑refundable application fee (typically €100 for bachelor programmes and €150 for master programmes for non‑EU/EEA students, while EU/EEA applicants are exempt) and submit the complete application, with the final deadline being 1 May for those who wish to participate in the housing lottery and 1 July for all other September‑intake candidates; for the February intake the Studielink opening date is similar (early October) but the exact deadline is later in the spring (usually early March), and the same OSIRIS upload‑and‑fee procedure applies, after which the Admissions Committee reviews the file and informs the candidate of the outcome.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Admission and application \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/bachelors/international-business-administration/admission-and-application) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Admission and application \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/bachelors/computing-science/admission-and-application) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Admission & Application \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/masters/metaphysics-and-philosophical-anthropology/admission-application) (Cao, score 1.00)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to Radboud University must hold a secondary‑school qualification that is recognised as equivalent to the Dutch VWO diploma (i.e., a Level 6 qualification in the European Qualifications Framework) and provide certified copies of the diploma and academic transcript (học bạ) for verification; the overall GPA must be at least 4 out of 6 (roughly 6 / 10) and for most programmes the required subjects include mathematics plus a science such as physics, chemistry, biology or informatics, with at least a 4 / 6 grade in those subjects, while programs taught in English also require proof of English proficiency (e.g., IELTS ≥ 6.5 or TOEFL ≥ 90); certain specialised programmes—particularly in the arts, design, music or architecture—additionally request a portfolio and/or an interview as part of the admission process, but the majority of bachelor’s and master’s programmes admit students solely on the basis of the diploma, GPA, subject prerequisites and language scores before the enrolment deadline, after which the university verifies all documents online or via certified paper copies.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Admission and application \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/masters/international-business/admission-and-application) (Cao, score 0.98)<br>[[PDF] Diploma specific admission requirements](https://www.ru.nl/sites/default/files/2023-10/diploma-specific-admission-requirements-bsc-computing-science-2024-2025.pdf) (Cao, score 0.97)<br>[Enrolment procedure for international students \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/application-and-admission/enrolment/enrolment-procedure-for-international-students) (Cao, score 0.97)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Radboud University requires all international students applying for English‑taught bachelor’s programmes to prove English proficiency with an accepted test result that is still valid on the enrolment date (IELTS, TOEFL iBT, Pearson PTE Academic, Cambridge C1 Advanced or C2 Proficiency, and LanguageCert Academic are accepted; online versions and one‑skill retakes are not). For bachelor’s admission the minimum scores are IELTS Academic ≥ 6.5 overall (each component ≥ 6.0), TOEFL iBT ≥ 90 total (each component ≥ 20), Cambridge C1 Advanced ≥ 176 overall (≥ 169 component) or C2 Proficiency ≥ 180 overall (≥ 169 component), and PTE Academic ≥ 61. For exchange students the thresholds are lower: IELTS Academic ≥ 6.0 overall, TOEFL iBT ≥ 80 total (or new TOEFL ≥ 4.0), PTE Academic ≥ 61, LanguageCert Academic ≥ 65, Cambridge B2 First ≥ 173, Cambridge C1 ≥ 169, C2 ≥ 180, and TOEIC ≥ 865 (listening/reading) + 335 (speaking/writing). Duolingo is not listed among the accepted tests, so it cannot be used. English‑language waivers are granted when applicants hold a recognised diploma that is considered equivalent to the Dutch VWO (or a university‑level certificate of an English course at B2 level for EU/EEA students), allowing them to meet the requirement without a test score.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Language requirements for English-taught programmes \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/application-and-admission/language-requirements/english-language-requirements) (Cao, score 0.79)<br>[Admission and application \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/bachelors/philosophy-politics-and-society/admission-and-application) (Cao, score 0.79)<br>[English language test results for exchange students \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/application-and-admission/language-requirements/english-language-requirements/minimum-test-scores-exchange-students) (Cao, score 0.78)

**4. Học phí**

- International (non‑EU/EEA) students at Radboud University pay an institutional tuition fee that for the 2026‑2027 academic year ranges from €1,379 to €2,985 per month (approximately €16,500 – €35,800 per year) and must also pay a non‑refundable application fee of €100, so the total annual cost is roughly €16,600 – €35,900, all in euros.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Student budget and expenses \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/education/studying-at-radboud-university/finances/costs-and-expenses) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Tuition fees \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/students/studying/tuition-fees) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[Tuition fee amounts \| Radboud University](https://www.ru.nl/en/students/studying/tuition-fees/tuition-fee-amounts) (Cao, score 1.00)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For EU/EEA students at Radboud University the typical monthly living budget is about €561‑€600 for accommodation, €244 for groceries, €72 for public transport and €55 for health‑and‑liability insurance, which together amount to roughly €1,642‑€1,680 per month; multiplied over a full year this gives an annual cost of approximately €19 700‑€20 200 covering housing, food, transport and health insurance (plus the usual incidental expenses such as books, leisure, clothing and contingencies). Non‑EU/EEA students face higher monthly totals of €2 751‑€4 396, equating to €33 000‑€52 800 per year for the same categories, and must also budget a one‑time visa‑and‑residence‑permit fee of about €254 and, if required, a tuberculosis test of €58.5.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Radboud University in the Netherlands is accredited by the Dutch Ministry of Education and the Accreditation Organisation of the Netherlands and Flanders (NVAO), ensuring that all its bachelor’s and master’s programmes meet national quality standards; it welcomes a diverse international student body with roughly 13.7 % of first‑year students and about 10.6 % of the total enrolment coming from abroad, and in the most recent global league tables it is positioned at 279 in the QS World University Rankings (2026) after previously ranking 222 in 2024, while the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings place it at 154 in 2026, up from 140 in 2024; in QS’s subject‑specific rankings the university is typically placed within the 51‑100 band for Psychology, 151‑200 for Life Sciences & Medicine, and similarly strong positions in fields such as Computer Science and Environmental Sciences, reflecting its recognised research strength across multiple disciplines.

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: International students from outside the EU/EEA who wish to study at Radboud University must obtain an MVV (provisional entry visa) and a VVR residence permit, usually through the “entry‑and‑residence” (TEV) procedure that the university submits on their behalf; the MVV is collected from the Dutch embassy or consulate within three months and must be used to travel to the Netherlands within 90 days, after which the VVR is issued within two weeks of arrival. The IND requires proof of sufficient financial means covering the first‑year tuition fee (if not already paid) plus living expenses of roughly €13 000 per year (about €1 083 per month), so a student whose tuition is €12 000 would need to demonstrate approximately €25 000 in total. Financial proof can be a recent (no older than three months) bank statement or bank declaration in the applicant’s own name or a sponsor’s name showing the full amount available for unrestricted withdrawal, and must include the date, holder’s full name, account number and current balance; if a sponsor is used, a signed statement of financial support and a copy of the sponsor’s ID are also required. The documents may be in Dutch, English, French or German, otherwise a translation is needed. Alternatively, funds may be transferred directly to the Dutch account specified in the OSIRIS application. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Dutch “orientation year” (zoekjaar) is a one‑year residence permit that allows non‑EEA graduates and researchers who have completed their studies or research in the Netherlands within the last three years to stay in the country and look for a job or start a business without needing a separate work permit; eligibility includes having earned an accredited Bachelor’s or Master’s degree, a Dutch post‑master programme of at least ten months, a PhD, or having conducted scientific research in the Netherlands, and graduates from top‑ranked foreign universities (top‑200 according to THE, QS or ShanghaiRanking and approved by Nuffic) may also apply, provided the application is submitted within three years of graduation; during the zoekjaar the holder can work as an employee, intern, or self‑employed entrepreneur, must register with the municipality to obtain a BSN, cannot extend the permit beyond the twelve months and must switch to another residence status before it expires, and for Highly Skilled Migrant applications the IND applies a reduced gross monthly salary requirement of about €3 122 (2026 figure).

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Radboud University supports international students in securing internships, co‑ops and other work placements through its Radboud Career Platform and the university‑run student‑temp agency Campus Detachering, offering a range of side‑jobs, graduation projects and temporary positions while complying with the 16‑hour‑per‑week work‑hour limit (or full‑time work during the summer months) and required work‑permit rules for non‑EU/EEA nationals; graduates enjoy very strong employment outcomes, with roughly 90 % of all degree holders finding a job within six months of graduation, about 40 % of humanities master’s alumni continuing to PhD programmes and the remainder moving into sectors such as government, journalism, NGOs, consultancy and policy‑making, while data‑science and AI master’s graduates benefit from an “excellent” job market, most entering industry as consultants, data analysts or entrepreneurs at companies including ING Bank, Philips, ASML, Capgemini, Booking.com and even Google, and a smaller share also securing PhD positions. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Dutch universities, including Radboud, enjoy strong labour‑market prospects: about 80 % hold a paid job five years after graduating and 57 % remain in the Netherlands one year after completing their 2023 studies, with a notable share earning high incomes—international alumni are more likely than Dutch peers to earn over €65 000 annually. Salary levels reflect Dutch university scales and sector demand: PhD candidates receive roughly €2 770–€3 530 per month, junior academic staff €3 700–€5 500, while specialists in IT, data analytics, software engineering and digital infrastructure command €3 000–€5 500 monthly, and senior technical or research engineers earn up to €6 800. The most in‑demand occupations for recent graduates span technology and engineering (which attracts 16 % of international students versus 9 % of Dutch peers), law and business services, journalism and social sciences, as well as health‑care, laboratory and research support, administrative and HR roles, and international partnership coordination—all offering salaries ranging from €2 600 to €8 800 depending on seniority and function.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Radboud University’s International Office, operated by the Student Life and International Mobility (SLIM) department, coordinates the orientation programme for incoming international students through events such as Radboud Intro and the Go Abroad Week, while also providing dedicated housing assistance for exchange and master’s students—including a specialised application process, deadline‑driven offers of furnished rooms and guidance on the SSH& lottery for bachelor and pre‑master’s students; in addition, the university’s Career Centre offers a full suite of career services to all students, including international students, with workshops on CV writing, interview techniques, networking events, an online job portal and one‑to‑one coaching to support their transition to the Dutch labour market. Đời sống & môi trường: Radboud University in Nijmegen offers a vibrant international campus life with over 150 student associations and organisations, ranging from sport clubs at the Radboud Sports Centre and creative theatre groups to study‑related societies such as KNUS and Perplex, a study café especially welcoming to overseas students; the university’s dedicated Radboud International Students (RIS) team runs the Radboud Intro week, mentorship programmes, free Dutch courses through Radboud in’to Languages, and the ESN Nijmegen network that organises social events, city tours and intercultural activities, all of which provide easy entry points for Vietnamese students who can also join the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) – a community that meets regularly for cultural celebrations such as Tet, language practice and networking – while campus safety is supported by well‑lit pathways, 24‑hour security patrols, an emergency phone system, a university chaplaincy that offers inclusive support, and dedicated counselling services through the Student Life and International Mobility (SLIM) department to address any personal or academic concerns international students may face.
