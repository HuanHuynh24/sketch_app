### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students wishing to start an undergraduate program at McGill University must submit their online application through the MyMcGill portal, following a five‑step process: choose a program, confirm eligibility, create an account and complete the application form, upload all required academic transcripts, test scores (including any program‑specific exams such as the CASPer for health‑related fields) and supporting documents, and finally pay the non‑refundable application fee of $136.08 CAD before the deadline; for applicants from outside Canada the primary deadline for Fall 2026 admission is January 15 (with document submission allowed until April 30), and once the first application and fee are submitted, a second program choice can be added at no extra charge while any additional applications thereafter each incur the same $136.08 CAD fee. For graduate studies, the non‑refundable fee is $139.63 CAD (Summer and Fall 2026) and deadlines vary by program and citizenship status, so prospective students should verify the specific deadline on the program’s page, then create a graduate‑application account, select up to two program choices, upload transcripts, references, and other required materials, and pay the fee before the posted deadline.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to McGill must submit a self‑reported grade summary and an unofficial transcript, and they are required to provide proof of English (or French) proficiency unless they have attended school for at least four years in a country where the language of instruction is English (or French). Admission is competitive and evaluated primarily on cumulative GPA (CGPA) from the last three years of secondary or pre‑university study, with recent minimums ranging from about 3.6 to 3.95 on a 4.0 scale depending on the faculty (e.g., Architecture ≈ 3.6‑3.8, Arts ≈ 3.8‑3.95, Science groups ≈ 3.75‑3.9, Education ≈ 2.7‑3.4). Prospective students must also meet program‑specific high‑school prerequisites, typically Grade 12 English or French, Grade 12 mathematics (pre‑calculus or calculus), and two of the three science subjects (biology, chemistry, physics); Architecture additionally requires one semester of differential calculus, one semester of linear algebra, and one semester of physics (mechanics). Most programs do not require a portfolio or interview, but the Faculty of Architecture may request a portfolio and conducts interviews as part of its selection process.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students at McGill must enroll in a full‑time credit program, which is defined as taking a minimum of 12 credits each term, and they must obtain a Study Permit after securing a Québec Acceptance Certificate (CAQ); once admitted, the university’s undergraduate curricula are organized by credit units that total 90 or 120 credits depending on the program length, with core requirements (typically 42 credits), major or honours components (ranging from 30 to 45 credits), foundation or language components (up to 12 credits), experiential learning (0‑3 credits), and electives (up to 9 or 21 credits); each course (“môn”) lists any prerequisite courses or knowledge, assigns a specific credit value (usually 3 credits per semester‑long course), and includes clearly defined learning outcomes that describe the knowledge, skills, and competencies students are expected to acquire, ensuring that progression through the curriculum builds on prior learning and meets the program’s overall educational objectives.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDY PERMIT, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- McGill University is ranked 27th worldwide in the 2026 QS World University Rankings, making it the top‑ranked Canadian institution in that list, and it holds the 41st spot globally in the 2026 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings; within QS subject rankings, McGill boasts 29 programmes in the top‑50 and a total of 43 programmes in the overall top‑50 across disciplines, with Life Sciences & Medicine tied for 28th, Social Sciences & Management at 32nd, Arts & Humanities at 35th, Natural Sciences at 39th and Engineering & Technology at 41st, while its Engineering – Mineral and Mining programme achieved a historic high of second‑best globally in the latest QS subject ranking (the most recent release places it third, continuing a decade‑long top‑10 presence); the university is fully accredited by the Quebec Ministry of Higher Education and its professional schools carry international accreditations, notably the Desautels Faculty of Management’s AACSB and EQUIS accreditations, and McGill is recognized as a research‑intensive, medical‑doctoral university with a large, diverse international student body reflected in its strong international faculty‑student ratios used in both QS and THE methodologies.

**2. Study permit và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/study permit: To obtain a Canadian study permit for McGill University, an international student must submit a valid Letter of Acceptance from McGill, proof of financial support showing sufficient funds to cover tuition, fees and living costs (typically the current tuition amount plus at least CAD 10 000 for a year of living expenses, demonstrated with recent bank statements, a Guaranteed Investment Certificate, scholarship letters or a sponsor’s letter), a passport‑size photo, the application fee receipt and any required biometrics; a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) is not required for Quebec schools such as McGill, as PALs are only issued for institutions in Ontario, and once the study permit is issued the student may work off‑campus up to 20 hours per week during regular study periods and full‑time during scheduled breaks, as well as on‑campus without a separate work permit, provided the study permit includes the “Can work off‑campus” condition and the student remains enrolled full‑time and maintains satisfactory academic standing. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from McGill University who have completed a degree‑level program that is at least eight months (or 900 hours for Quebec credentials) are eligible for a post‑graduation work permit (PGWP) that can be issued for up to three years; this three‑year duration applies to bachelor’s, master’s and PhD programs regardless of whether the master’s program is shorter than two years, and it also applies to any program that is two years or longer, while programs shorter than two years receive a permit for a period equal to the length of the study program; graduates may combine the length of multiple eligible programs completed at an eligible DLI within two years to reach the maximum three‑year term, must apply within 180 days of receiving official proof of completion, and can receive the PGWP only once.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: McGill University provides a robust work‑integrated learning framework for international students that includes mandatory co‑op placements and a wide range of internship opportunities coordinated through the Career Planning Service, with programs such as International Co‑op, Young Professional and Working Holiday pathways that allow students to gain professional experience in Canada and abroad; recent graduate outcomes show that roughly 62 % of all graduates secure employment shortly after completing their studies (a figure that is slightly higher for international students), about 85‑91 % say a McGill degree positively influences their job prospects, and the most common sectors for alumni are health‑care and hospitals, artificial‑intelligence and information‑technology, and financial services, while approximately half of recent international‑student graduates remain in Canada after finishing their programs. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Canada, including those from McGill University, are most commonly employed in sales and service occupations but also show a notable concentration in technology‑related fields such as web design and development (about 21 % of their employment) and data‑science roles (around 12 %); the average base salary reported by McGill graduates seeking employment is roughly $75,600, with the highest‑paying positions—software developers, cybersecurity analysts and systems engineers—earning between $90,000 and $91,300, while Job Bank Canada identifies computer and information systems professionals, engineers (civil, mechanical, electrical), health‑care workers and business‑analytics specialists as the occupations with the strongest labour‑market demand, mirroring McGill’s functional‑area distribution where roughly 44 % of employed graduates work in data analysis/business insights and sizable shares are placed in consulting, finance and technology roles.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: McGill’s International Student Services (ISS) is the central office for all international student needs, located in the Brown Building (Suite 4100) and open Monday‑Friday, 10 am‑4 pm, offering orientation sessions that introduce new students to immigration regulations, health insurance, work permits, campus resources and community life; the office also provides housing support through dedicated advisors who help students navigate university residence applications, off‑campus rentals and short‑term accommodation, and it collaborates with the Career Planning Service (CaPS) – based at the Brown Student Services Building (Suite 2200) on the downtown campus and at the Centennial Centre on the Macdonald Campus – to deliver career counseling, resume and interview workshops, job‑search strategies, information on on‑ and off‑campus employment, co‑op and internship opportunities, and guidance on obtaining Social Insurance Numbers and post‑graduation work permits for international graduates. Đời sống & môi trường: McGill University hosts a highly international campus where students from over 150 countries, including a vibrant Vietnamese community, can enjoy a lively campus life supported by numerous clubs and safety resources; the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA) organizes cultural events such as Tet celebrations, language‑exchange meet‑ups and social gatherings that connect Vietnamese undergraduates and graduate students, while the broader International Students’ Association, International Student Spouses Club, and the Buddy Program pair new arrivals with experienced peers, and a wide array of over 200 student‑run clubs—ranging from cultural, academic, religious, and recreational groups—provide opportunities to explore interests and build networks; McGill’s campus safety is reinforced by a dedicated campus security service that patrols the downtown and Macdonald campuses 24 hours a day, offers a safe‑walk program, emergency phones, and a well‑publicized incident‑reporting system, and the university’s orientation week and free airport welcome service further help international students settle securely into Montreal’s friendly, multilingual environment.
