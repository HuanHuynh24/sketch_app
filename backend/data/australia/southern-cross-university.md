### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Southern Cross University offers six intakes for international students each year – Summer Term (starts 12 January), Term 1 (9 March), Term 2 (4 May), Term 3 (6 July), Term 4 (31 August) and Term 5 (26 October) – and the corresponding online application closing dates are 7 January, 4 March, 29 April, 1 July, 26 August and 21 October respectively, so you should submit your application at least three months before the intended start date; there is no separate application fee when you apply directly to SCU (though a non‑refundable enrolment fee of AU$260 and a “fee payable on acceptance” are charged after you receive an offer), and the application process consists of (1) selecting a course that suits your academic and English‑language profile, (2) completing the online international application at scu.edu.au/international and uploading certified transcripts, English test results (e.g., IELTS/TOEFL), and any required references or advanced‑standing documents, (3) optionally using an approved education agent if your country requires one, (4) receiving an acknowledgement within about 10 days and, if successful, an International Offer Package by email, (5) reviewing and signing the Offer Acceptance Agreement before the offer lapse date, (6) paying the acceptance fee and meeting the Genuine Student Requirements (visa, health insurance, etc.) to secure enrolment.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants must provide a certified copy of their academic transcripts (showing grades or GPA and, for Australian equivalence, an ATAR score or its overseas assessment), meet English language proficiency (minimum IELTS 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5 for most undergraduate courses and IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 for most postgraduate courses; alternatives include TOEFL iBT 60 (no band <17/11), PTE 50, CAE 169‑175, etc.), and supply any required references; undergraduate entry is based on the Australian Year 12 ATAR score listed for each course (or its overseas equivalent), while postgraduate entry requires a qualification equivalent to an Australian bachelor degree (generally a minimum GPA of about 2.0/5.0 or 50 % local grading) plus any specific subject prerequisites; certain creative, design, architecture and media programs also demand a portfolio and may require an interview, and health‑related programs have inherent subject requirements, with additional English pathway options available through SCU College if language scores fall short.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Southern Cross University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency through an accepted test or an approved university pathway, with the benchmark being Academic IELTS; most undergraduate programmes need an overall IELTS score of 6.0 with no individual band below 5.5, most postgraduate programmes require 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0 for the highest‑level courses such as nursing and midwifery), and diploma courses accept 5.5 overall with no band below 5.0. Equivalent scores are accepted from TOEFL iBT – 60 overall with at least 17 in writing and speaking and at least 11 in reading and listening for the 6.0 level, 79 overall with a minimum 21 in writing for the 6.5 level, and 94 overall with at least 24 in writing for the 7.0 level – and from Pearson PTE – 50 overall with a communicative score of 42 for the 6.0 level, 58 overall with a communicative score of 50 for the 6.5 level, and 65 overall with a communicative score of 65 for the 7.0 level. The university also recognises Cambridge CPE/CAE, ISPLR, and CEFR B2 equivalents. English language waivers are available for students who successfully complete an English for Academic Purposes (EAP) or diploma pathway with SCU College or an approved partner institution, but Duolingo English Test results are not listed as an accepted alternative.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Southern Cross University admits international students who hold a qualification equivalent to Australian Year 12 and meet English language standards (typically an overall Academic IELTS 6.0 with no band below 5.5), though higher IELTS scores (e.g., 6.5) are required for certain specialisations; entry into many programmes also requires completion of a relevant diploma or prerequisite subjects, and successful completion of a diploma (such as the Diploma of Information Technology, Business, Education, Engineering, Science, Health, Hotel Management, Legal Studies or IT) grants up to eight university units—equivalent to 48 credit points—towards a bachelor’s degree. The university’s curriculum follows the “Southern Cross Model,” dividing the academic year into four six‑week terms, with students undertaking two units (subjects) per term (usually 6 credit points per unit), allowing a typical bachelor’s degree to be completed in three years (24 units, 144 credit points). Courses are designed around clear learning outcomes that emphasise practical fieldwork, critical thinking, professional and intercultural competence, and the ability to apply theoretical knowledge to real‑world contexts, with embedded support and active‑learning formats to ensure international students achieve both academic and industry‑ready skills.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student visa (subclass 500) is the official visa for international students such as those enrolled at Southern Cross University, a CRICOS‑registered institution, and requires a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from the university as part of the application; applicants must demonstrate financial capacity by providing evidence that they can cover up to 12 months of tuition fees (or the remaining balance if a fee has already been paid), the minimum living expense of AU$21,041 per year for a single student (higher amounts apply for families and include travel costs for all applicants), and must obtain and maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire visa period, with the OSHC start date matching or preceding the arrival date; while studying, visa holders are allowed to work a maximum of 48 hours per fortnight during scheduled course sessions and unlimited hours during official course breaks, with higher degree‑by‑research students and eligible postgraduate research or master’s by coursework students permitted unlimited work hours, and eligible partners of such students also eligible for unrestricted work, all of which must be complied with to keep the visa valid. Sau tốt nghiệp: International students who graduate from Southern Cross University with a bachelor’s, master’s or doctoral degree can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) under the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which normally grants a stay of two to three years (five years for Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders); the visa must be lodged within six months of course completion while the applicant holds a current or recent student visa, and, if the degree appears on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List, the holder can apply for a further two‑year extension through ImmiAccount at no extra charge, effectively extending the total post‑study work period to up to four to five years (or up to seven years for eligible Hong Kong/BN(O) passport holders).

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Southern Cross University offers a comprehensive work‑integrated learning pathway for international students that includes a structured internship programme (GradWISE) linking students to supported workplace environments, shadow‑day experiences with industry partners, personalised employability audits, mentor‑matched coaching, masterclasses on graduate applications and interview techniques, and ongoing neuro‑zone optimisation coaching; these services are designed to build the confidence, skillset and professional networks needed for successful employment, and the university’s Graduate Outcomes Survey 2023 shows that 93 % of postgraduate students secure full‑time work after completing their degree while 90 % of all graduates obtain a job, placing SCU among the top Australian institutions for graduate income and employment outcomes and confirming that its internship, placement and co‑op options translate into strong career prospects across a wide range of industries. Lương & thị trường: Southern Cross University graduates enjoy strong earnings, with undergraduate alumni reporting a median full‑time salary of about $72,500 and postgraduate coursework graduates earning a median starting salary near $110,000, both above national university averages; however, international graduates in Australia still earn less than their domestic peers, with median full‑time salaries of roughly $68,000 for undergraduates versus $75,000 for domestic students and a $30,000 gap at the postgraduate coursework level, and their employment rates vary markedly by field—international students in Rehabilitation and Medicine achieve full‑time employment rates close to 90 percent, while those in Creative Arts fall to around 42 percent and face a 36‑point disadvantage in Agriculture and Environmental Studies; labour‑market demand for graduates is strongest in occupations projected to grow fastest, notably construction management, software and applications programming, and professional, scientific and technical services, alongside high demand for education aides and primary school teachers, reflecting broader Australian trends that favour skilled roles in construction, tech, and health‑related sectors.
