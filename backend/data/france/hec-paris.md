## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- HEC Paris welcomes international applicants to its Master in Management (MiM) and Master in International Finance programmes, requiring a bachelor’s degree (or higher) from any country; the application fee is €250 per candidate and candidates must choose either to apply directly through HEC or via the Join a School in France platform (which allows a single application to five French business schools) – once a method is selected the first validated application is processed for the September 2026 intake. For the 2025‑2026 cycle the school runs four rolling rounds: Round 1 closes on 8 October 2025 at 18:00 Paris time, Round 2 on 6 January 2026 at 18:00, Round 3 on 26 February 2026 at 18:00, and Round 4 on 16 April 2026 at 18:00; all supporting documents must be uploaded before the respective deadline. Successful MiM candidates begin on the campus on 17 August 2026, while the International Finance cohort starts on 1 September 2026. The online application requires personal data, academic transcripts, a CV, two recommendation letters, a motivation essay and, where relevant, proof of English proficiency; after submission, candidates receive an admissibility decision, followed by an admission decision, after which they must confirm enrollment by the date indicated in the admission letter.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International candidates applying to HEC Paris must submit an official transcript of their most recent studies and demonstrate a minimum academic average of 12 / 20 (roughly a 3.0 / 4.0 GPA or a UK 2:1 honour) — the same threshold is applied for both master‑level and MBA programmes, with the possibility of waiving GMAT/GRE scores for top‑ranked MiM graduates or exceptional profiles; in addition, proof of English proficiency (TOEFL, IELTS or equivalent) is required, a current passport‑size photo and a résumé are uploaded during the online registration, and most programmes require a 20‑30‑minute interview conducted in English to assess motivation and fit (creative‑oriented tracks may also ask for a portfolio, though this is not a general requirement across all HEC degrees).

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- HEC Paris requires all international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency at a C1 level, and accepted tests include TOEFL (minimum 100 iBT for PhD programs; for Master’s and EMBA programs the usual minimum is 90 iBT), IELTS (overall band 7.0), Cambridge English (C1 Advanced or Proficiency), TOEIC (minimum 850), and the Duolingo English Test (minimum 120) – the Duolingo test is accepted for the Executive MBA. The PTE Academic is also accepted, typically with a minimum overall score of 58. Applicants are exempt from providing a test result if they are native English speakers or if they have completed at least two years of university study (bachelor’s or master’s) fully taught in English; in those cases they can request a waiver by submitting proof of their English‑language education.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- HEC Paris requires that international students enrolling in its master‑level programmes hold a bachelor’s degree equivalent to 180 ECTS credits and will complete an additional 120 ECTS credits at HEC, bringing the total to the 300 ECTS required for the French “bac + 5” master grade; the curriculum is built around a core of six mandatory courses supplemented by at least twelve electives that can be chosen to match career goals, and every student must complete a four‑month professional experience (internship, full‑time job or equivalent abroad if the student has no prior degree or work experience outside France). Course structures vary by subject but typically combine in‑person lectures (e.g., 20‑hour modules) with defined learning outcomes such as mastering fundamental statistical concepts for econometrics, understanding corporate law and governance, and applying mathematical tools used in finance and economics; most courses list “not applicable” as a prerequisite, although a few preparatory modules (e.g., a summer prep in French mathematics for finance) require specific prior tracks such as a literary‑focused M1 background, and each module is assigned a specific number of ECTS credits (often zero for short preparatory sessions) that contribute to the overall 120‑credit master requirement.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- HEC Paris is consistently recognised as a top‑tier business school, holding the Triple Crown accreditation (AACSB, EQUIS and AMBA) and full approval from the French Ministry of Higher Education; in the 2026 QS World University Rankings for business masters it leads globally in Marketing, Strategic Management and Finance (all ranked #1 worldwide), places #2 in Business Analytics, and its full‑time MBA is ranked #5 worldwide, while its Master in International Finance also tops the QS Business Masters Finance list; in the Times Higher Education (THE) rankings HEC Paris is featured among the world’s leading business schools, typically ranking within the top 10 in THE’s Business School Rankings and appearing in the overall global university tables near the top 100, and the school’s campus is highly international, with roughly 40 % of its students coming from outside France.

**2. Visa sinh viên và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa sinh viên: To obtain a VLS‑TS student visa for HEC Paris you must first register on the Campus France portal for your country, receive the “attestation de pré‑inscription” (or Études en France validation) and then submit your application through the official France‑Visas website, where you will upload a completed Cerfa 14571‑05 form, a passport valid for at least three months beyond your stay, two recent photos, the HEC Paris enrolment letter (showing study mode, weekly hours and tuition invoice), proof of accommodation and a health‑insurance policy that covers medical emergencies and repatriation for the whole duration of your stay (typically a private policy meeting the €30 000 minimum until you are affiliated with the French social‑security system, CPAM). You must also demonstrate sufficient financial resources of at least €615 per month (approximately €6 150 for a ten‑month programme) by providing recent bank statements, a scholarship award letter or a guarantor’s attestation, and pay the visa fee (€99), the Campus France fee (≈ €190), the OFII validation stamp (timbre fiscal €75) and the student‑life contribution (CVEC €105); after arriving in France you must validate the VLS‑TS online on the ANEF/OFII platform within three months. Sau tốt nghiệp: The APS (Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour) is the French post‑study work authorisation issued for 12 months to non‑EU graduates who have completed a master’s degree or higher – such as a Master’s or MSc from HEC Paris – while holding a student residence permit; it permits full‑time employment for any employer without a separate work permit and can be converted to a Salarié residence permit or a Passeport Talent visa once a qualifying job offer (meeting the salary threshold) is secured, and the application is filed at the prefecture of the city where the studies were completed together with the official degree certificate, transcripts, passport, current residence permit, proof of address, photos and the completed CERFA form, after which a récépissé is issued to maintain legal status while the APS is processed (typically 4–8 weeks). In parallel, the “Job seeker / new business creator” residence permit – also detailed on the Campus France and service‑public.fr portals – is available to the same category of graduates (Licence Professionnelle, Master, Specialised Master, or Grande‑Ecole MSc) who have resided in France on a student permit and wish to stay up to 12 months to look for a job or launch a company; EU, EEA, Swiss citizens do not need it, and nationals of certain partner countries (including India) may instead continue with the APS. **3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: HEC Paris’s MBA programme is overwhelmingly international (95 % of the Class of 2025 came from 49 different countries) and offers both 16‑month and accelerated 12‑month formats that include a summer internship (July‑August) and an end‑of‑studies internship (January‑April) before full‑time placement beginning in January of the graduation year; 75 % of the 2025 graduates secured full‑time offers within three months of completing the programme, 64 % obtained positions outside their home country, 57 % switched sectors, 54 % changed function and 57 % relocated, with 52 % of jobs located in Europe, 25 % in Asia‑Oceania, 11 % in North America and the remainder across Africa‑Middle East and Latin America, while the most common post‑graduation sectors were consulting, technology and financial services, accounting for roughly two‑thirds of placements, and the school’s career services support these outcomes through dedicated corporate partnerships, networking events and personalised coaching for international students seeking internships, co‑ops or full‑time employment. Lương & thị trường: In 2024 HEC Paris MBA graduates who took positions in France earned an average annual salary of roughly €86,700 (about $86,735) with a median of €82,300 (about $82,270), while the preceding 2023 cohort posted a higher mean of €109,400 (≈ $109,424) and a median of €97,200 (≈ $97,241); APEC and France Travail identify consulting, financial services, technology and digital‑transformation, engineering and data‑analytics roles as the occupations with the strongest labour‑market demand in France, with hiring growth in these sectors consistently above 5 % year‑on‑year, and the HEC Paris MBA class remains overwhelmingly international—about 94 % of students are non‑French nationals—and roughly 60 % of graduates secure employment outside their home country, while a comparable share obtain positions in France, reflecting both the school’s global network and the high demand for these key sectors among French and multinational employers.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: HEC Paris’s International Office coordinates all exchange partnerships and provides a dedicated welcome desk and compulsory “International Office Welcome Meeting” for exchange students, during which participants receive administrative information, visa and immigration guidance, French‑language support, library access, sports‑activity details and presentations from the Career and Corporate Centre, as well as an overview of on‑ and off‑campus events organized by the HEC IS Society; while the school no longer offers on‑campus housing, students can be placed in the nearby ECLA Massy‑Palaiseau residence or find accommodation through the Studapart platform, and the Orientation/Carrières department delivers comprehensive career services—including internships in France and abroad, long‑term corporate missions, job listings, and a series of sector‑specific and general career fairs (legal, fiscal, consulting, finance, humanitarian and “Carrefours HEC”) together with personalised career counseling and employer presentations. Đời sống & môi trường: HEC Paris hosts a highly international campus where roughly half of the student body comes from abroad, representing 135 different nationalities, and offers a green 340‑acre setting with more than 2,000 students living on‑site in about 1,800 rooms across 18 residences; the university’s Student Life department supports over 130 student clubs and associations—including professional, humanitarian, cultural, and sports groups—through a dedicated Clubs Life Office that provides resources, guidance and event space, and the campus is equipped with 24‑hour security personnel, CCTV monitoring, a campus police service and well‑lit common areas to ensure a safe environment for all residents, while the diverse community naturally includes a Vietnamese student network that organizes cultural events, language meet‑ups and mentorship activities for Vietnamese and other Asian students, helping them integrate socially and academically within the broader international student body.
