### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International applicants to Nanyang Technological University (NTU) Singapore apply through the online NTU admissions portal, where they first create an account, select their desired programmes and upload the required academic transcripts, English‑language test results and other supporting documents. After submitting the checklist, they must pay a non‑refundable application fee of SGD 25 (the fee is non‑waivable and must be paid by Visa/MasterCard or PayNow before the deadline). NTU runs two main intake cycles for undergraduates: the Semester 1 intake (programme start in August) with an application deadline typically in early January, and the Semester 2 intake (programme start in January) with a deadline around early July; exact dates are posted each year on the admissions site. Once the fee is paid, applicants can track their application status, update programme choices, and apply for scholarships online. Successful candidates receive an admission offer, which must be accepted within the stipulated acceptance window (usually a few weeks), after which they confirm their enrolment, pay the tuition deposit, apply for on‑campus housing (if desired) and complete the post‑acceptance procedures such as the student‑pass application, medical report submission and payment of any issuance fees.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [How to apply \| NTU Singapore](https://www.ntu.edu.sg/applicationtrimester3) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International Qualifications \| NTU Singapore](https://www.ntu.edu.sg/admissions/undergraduate/admission-guide/international-qualifications) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[[PDF] ENROLMENT PROCEDURES FOR INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS](https://www3.ntu.edu.sg/oad2/website_files/international/After_Acceptance-Foreign.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International applicants to NTU must submit an official academic transcript showing a GPA of at least 3.0 on a 4.0 scale (or the local equivalent) together with final year results (e.g., up to Grade 12 Semester 1 for high‑school students) and a complete final transcript within three days of the results being released; for A‑Level entrants the usual requirement is three passes with grades of ABB or better, including required subjects such as Mathematics (H2) and English, while IB Diploma candidates need a minimum of 30 points with at least 4 in each Higher Level subject and must meet the subject prerequisites of the chosen programme (e.g., Mathematics Analysis and Approaches or Applications and Interpretations for engineering, plus a pass in Mother‑Tongue Language or an MOE‑approved exemption). All applicants must also provide proof of English proficiency (TOEFL iBT ≥ 80, IELTS ≥ 6.0 or PTE ≥ 58) and upload official result slips, university transcripts (if applicable), and any required MOE approval letters in colour PDF/JPG format. Certain programmes—such as Architecture, Design, Fine Arts and other creative disciplines—require a portfolio (and may be followed by an interview), whereas most other faculties admit solely on academic merit without a portfolio or interview.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to Nanyang Technological University must demonstrate English proficiency unless their previous education was taught in English for at least three consecutive years, in which case a waiver can be obtained by providing an official medium‑of‑instruction letter from the institution. For undergraduate admission, acceptable evidence includes IELTS with a minimum overall score of 6.0 (with at least 6.0 in both Writing and Speaking), TOEFL iBT with a minimum overall score of 90 (and a minimum Verbal score of 25) for tests taken before 2026 – for tests from January 2026 onward the requirement is an overall score of 5.0 with at least 5.0 in Speaking – or Pearson Test of English Academic with a minimum overall and Speaking score of 55. Duolingo English Test is not listed as an accepted qualification. For graduate programmes, the baseline requirements are higher (IELTS 7.0 or TOEFL iBT 100, with some programmes requiring TOEFL 105), and waivers are granted when the applicant’s most recent degree was completed in English, provided a certification letter confirming the English medium of instruction is submitted.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT'S PASS, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in Singapore was ranked 12th worldwide in the 2026 QS World University Rankings and placed 5th globally in the 2025 Times Higher Education (THE) rankings for interdisciplinary excellence; in the 2026 QS World University Rankings by Subject, NTU achieved six positions inside the global top 5—Communication & Media Studies (2nd), Materials Science (3rd), Data Science & Artificial Intelligence (4th), Electrical & Electronic Engineering (4th), Library & Information Management (5th) and Mechanical, Aeronautical & Manufacturing Engineering (5th)—and a total of 38 subjects within the global top 50, including strong showings in Chemical Engineering, Civil & Structural Engineering, Chemistry, Education, Computer Science & Information Systems and Mathematics; the university is fully accredited by Singapore’s Ministry of Education and its professional programmes hold relevant national and international accreditations (such as ABET for engineering), while international students comprise roughly 30 % of the student body, reflecting NTU’s global appeal and commitment to a diverse, high‑quality education.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students admitted to Nanyang Technological University must obtain a Student’s Pass (STP) from Singapore’s Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) and, as part of the application, provide proof that they have sufficient financial resources—typically a bank statement showing at least SGD 15,000‑20,000 in personal or sponsor funds to cover tuition and living expenses for the duration of the course. Once the STP is issued, students are automatically permitted to work part‑time for up to 16 hours per week during academic terms (Monday to Sunday) and unlimited hours during official vacation periods, without needing a separate work permit; however, any business activity must remain a secondary, low‑intensity pursuit and not replace the primary purpose of studying, as ICA expects the student’s main activity to remain academic. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from Nanyang Technological University (and other Singapore autonomous universities) may apply to the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority for a one‑year, non‑renewable Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) specifically designed for job‑seeking after study; the pass allows the holder to remain in Singapore for up to 12 months to look for employment, but does not confer an automatic right to work – any employer must obtain a Letter of Consent for the holder to be employed, and the LTVP cannot be renewed beyond the 12‑month period.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Nanyang Technological University (NTU) reports that about nine out of ten graduates secure employment within six months of graduation, with many programmes achieving even higher rates—for example, Accountancy (96.7 %), Biomedical Sciences (96.4 %), Accountancy and Business (96.0 %), Civil Engineering (95.9 %) and Chinese (93.9 %). The university’s Career & Attachment Office (CAO) provides extensive internship, co‑op and placement support, coordinating industry partnerships, career workshops, resume reviews and one‑to‑one coaching that help students—including international students—gain relevant work experience before graduating; a notable case is an Electrical and Electronic Engineering graduate who, after early engagement with the CAO, secured an Assistant Systems Engineer Trainee internship at Tata Consultancy Services. By the 12‑month mark, 95 % of NTU graduates have recorded wages, reflecting strong graduate outcomes across a wide range of career pathways. Lương & thị trường: In 2025 NTU graduates who entered full‑time permanent employment earned a mean gross monthly salary of about S$4,812 and a median of S$4,550, with salaries differing markedly by discipline – Accountancy graduates earned roughly S$5,233 on average, Civil Engineering about S$4,324, Electrical & Electronic Engineering around S$4,593, Information Engineering & Media near S$5,071, Mechanical Engineering roughly S$4,538, Biomedical Sciences about S$3,769, Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering approximately S$4,161 and Environmental Engineering close to S$4,205 – and more than 96 % of graduates in fields such as Accountancy, Biomedical Sciences, Civil Engineering and Chinese secured employment within six months. MOM’s latest Graduate Salary Survey corroborates these figures and highlights that the occupations most in demand for NTU international graduates are software engineering, data science and artificial‑intelligence roles, fintech and financial analysis, biomedical research and development, and sustainable or green engineering, with typical entry‑level salaries ranging from roughly S$4,500 to S$6,000, underscoring strong labour‑market demand for STEM and finance‑related skill sets.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: NTU’s International Office (Office of International Engagement) oversees all matters for international students, running a dedicated induction programme on arrival that guides new students through visa, enrolment and campus‑life basics and offers social‑integration events through the CI Club; housing assistance is coordinated by the Office of Campus Housing, which provides a chatbot and an “Ask Campus Housing” service for students with SSO logins, as well as a public email (och‑housing@ntu.edu.sg) and a helpline (+65 6790 5155) to help secure on‑campus accommodation and resolve housing queries; career development is delivered by the Student Career Services & Attachment (Career and Attachment Office), which offers career counselling, internship and attachment placement, employer networking, entrepreneurship support and workshops to equip international students with the skills and connections needed for the Singapore and global job markets. Đời sống & môi trường: NTU provides a vibrant campus life for international students through a comprehensive Inclusion & Integration programme that offers orientation briefings, mentorship and community‑building activities, and a wide‑range of clubs and societies—including cultural groups such as the Tamil Literary Society, Muslim Society and Catholic Students’ Apostolate, interest‑based clubs like the Aerospace Society, Red Cross Youth NTU Chapter, AIESEC NTU Local Committee, Engineers Without Borders, and numerous sports, arts and volunteer organisations—so students can easily find a community that matches their hobbies and cultural background; safety is a priority with 24‑hour campus security patrols, emergency call points, regular scam‑awareness alerts and Singapore’s generally low crime environment, while the Vietnamese Student Association at NTU actively supports Vietnamese scholars by organising cultural festivals, language‑exchange sessions and social gatherings that link them to the broader international student network.
