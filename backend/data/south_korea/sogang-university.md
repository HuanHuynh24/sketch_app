**2. Student Visa (D-2) và Job Seeker Visa (D-10)**

- Visa (D-2): The D‑2 (Student) visa for international students admitted to Sogang University requires the submission of a completed HiKorea application form, a valid passport, a recent passport‑size photo, the university’s Certificate of Admission (and, after enrollment, a Certificate of Registration), and proof of sufficient financial resources—normally a bank statement, bank book, or scholarship letter showing funds equivalent to about USD 10,000 (roughly KRW 12 million) to cover one year of tuition and living expenses, with the statement issued within the last 30 days; the visa fee is US$50 for single entry or US$80 for multiple entry. After arriving in Korea and receiving an Alien Registration Card, D‑2 visa holders may legally work part‑time (up to 20 hours per week during the semester and up to full‑time during vacations) provided they obtain a work‑permit from the immigration office, but they cannot engage in remunerative activity before the permit is granted, and Sogang University advises that applicants who intend to rely on part‑time earnings for tuition may face restrictions during the visa issuance process. Sau tốt nghiệp (D-10): The D‑10 Job Seeker Visa lets recent graduates of Korean four‑year universities such as Sogang University stay in South Korea for up to six months to look for employment, and it can be extended—often to a total of two years—if the holder can demonstrate ongoing job‑search activity; eligibility requires a completed bachelor’s degree or higher obtained at Sogang, a valid D‑2 student visa at the time of graduation, proof of financial capacity (typically around USD 3,000‑5,000), a passport, a notarised sponsor letter and any required fees, and because Sogang graduates satisfy the “Korean 4‑year university graduate” exception the usual two‑year work‑experience requirement for an E‑7 visa is waived, allowing the holder to convert to an E‑7 work visa as soon as a job offer and employer sponsorship are secured, with the official guidelines and application procedures posted on the HiKorea portal (studyinkorea.go.kr).

Lương & thị trường: Sogang University’s graduates enjoy one of the strongest outcomes among Seoul’s four‑year comprehensive universities, with an employment rate of 73.1 % in early 2026 and a job‑retention rate of 91 % after the fourth reporting round, indicating both high placement and durable employment; the Korean labour market continues to prioritize science and engineering occupations, which command the highest demand and starting salaries—typically exceeding 3 million won (about US $2,100) per month for Korean graduates, while international graduates see a rising hiring share of 13.8 % in 2024 and, for the first time, one‑third (33.3 %) of them earn a monthly salary above 3 million won, reflecting growing but still lower overall employment compared with domestic graduates.
