### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students can enroll at TU Delft for the main September intake or the secondary February intake; for the September intake the enrolment deadline in Studielink is 15 January for numerus‑clausus programmes and 1 April for all other programmes with a non‑Dutch (international) diploma, while the February intake follows a similar schedule a few months earlier (typically 1 October for numerus‑clausus and 1 January for other programmes). The application process begins by checking the specific admission requirements, then registering on Studielink with a DigiD, after which an email with instructions to continue the registration in the university’s OSIAAN system is sent; applicants must then upload their academic documents, pay the non‑refundable €100 application fee (or the equivalent amount for non‑EU applicants), and await the admission decision. Once admitted, students activate their NetID, receive a TU Delft email address, and complete any remaining tuition‑fee payment before receiving the official certificate of enrolment.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Đại học Công nghệ Delft (TU Delft) chấp nhận học sinh quốc tế có bằng trung học tương đương với bằng VWO Hà Lan; bằng này phải bao gồm kỳ thi cuối cấp môn Toán bắt buộc và, tùy theo chương trình BSc mà sinh viên muốn theo học, các môn Toán, Vật lý, Hóa học và/hoặc Sinh học (ví dụ: Kỹ thuật Cơ khí yêu cầu Toán và Vật lý, Kỹ thuật Hóa học yêu cầu Toán và Hóa học). Nếu sinh viên đã hoàn thành ít nhất một năm (60 ECTS) tại một trường đại học công nhận ở nước ngoài, điểm trung bình (GPA) của năm đầu phải đạt tối thiểu 75 % (khoảng 3,0/4,0). Đối với sinh viên chưa có bằng đại học, cần nộp bản sao có chứng thực của bằng trung học và bảng điểm chi tiết (học bạ), kèm bản dịch công chứng nếu không phải tiếng Hà Lan hoặc tiếng Anh. Ngoài ra, yêu cầu chứng minh năng lực tiếng Anh (TOEFL, IELTS hoặc Cambridge), CV bằng tiếng Anh, và giấy tờ tùy thân (hộ chiếu). Đối với một số chương trình thiết kế hoặc kiến trúc, TU Delft có thể yêu cầu nộp portfolio và/hoặc tham gia phỏng vấn, nhưng hầu hết các chương trình BSc không yêu cầu portfolio hay phỏng vấn.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Country specific requirements - TU Delft](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/education/admission-and-application/bsc-international-diploma/admission-requirements/country-specific-requirements) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[BSc International - TU Delft](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/education/admission-and-application/bsc-international/1-admission-requirements/) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[BSc International](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/education/admission-and-application/bsc-international-diploma/1-admission-requirements/) (Cao, score 1.00)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- TU Delft requires all non‑English‑speaking international applicants to demonstrate a C1‑level proficiency in English, which can be shown by an IELTS Academic score of at least 6.5, a TOEFL iBT score of 90 or higher, a PTE Academic score of at least 58, a Duolingo English Test score of 115 or higher, or a Cambridge Certificate of Proficiency in English (CPE) or Certificate in Advanced English (CAE); for Erasmus+ partner universities a written statement confirming a C1 level in the nomination letter is sufficient, and students from native‑English‑speaking countries (USA, UK, Ireland, Australia, New Zealand, Canada) are automatically exempt from providing any English‑language test result.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at TU Delft can expect to spend roughly €34 000 – €40 000 per year, depending on the study level. For a non‑EU bachelor the total comprises the tuition fee of about €19 900, the university‑charged housing fee of €285, the residence‑permit/visa fee of €254 and an average living‑cost package of €12 240 (covering groceries, transport, clothing, phone and leisure). Adding a typical Dutch health‑insurance premium of roughly €1 600 per year brings the annual budget to around €34 300. For a non‑EU master the tuition rises to about €25 600, so with the same housing, visa, living‑cost and insurance components the yearly total is approximately €40 000.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Delft University of Technology (TU Delft) is the top‑ranked university in the Netherlands in both the QS World University Rankings (2024 ≈ 57th globally, 14th in Europe) and the Times Higher Education (THE) World Reputation Rankings (2025 ≈ 40th worldwide, 20th in Europe, 1st nationally). In THE’s 2025 “most international universities” list it is placed 14th worldwide, 7th in Europe and 1st in the Netherlands, underscoring its strong appeal to international students. Subject‑specific rankings consistently place TU Delft among the world’s leaders, especially in engineering and technology: QS ranks it within the global top 15 for Engineering & Technology overall and in the top 15 for Architecture/Built Environment, Civil Engineering and Mechanical Engineering, while the 2024 QS subject ranking lists it 3rd worldwide for Mechanical, Aeronautical & Manufacturing Engineering, ahead of Cambridge, Harvard and ETH Zurich. THE also highlights top‑50 positions in many engineering disciplines. The university’s programmes are fully accredited by the Dutch‑Flemish Accreditation Organisation (NVAO), which guarantees that all bachelor’s, master’s and doctoral programmes meet the national quality standards required for higher education in the Netherlands.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [TU Delft in International Rankings](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/about-tu-delft/organisation/facts-and-figures/tu-delft-in-international-rankings) (Cao, score 0.86)<br>[Rankings \| TU Delft Learning for Life](https://learningforlife.tudelft.nl/rankings/) (Cao, score 0.80)<br>[TU Delft in third place in QS subject ranking 2024](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/2024/lr/tu-delft-in-third-place-in-qs-subject-ranking-2024) (Cao, score 0.74)

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: Non‑EU students accepted to TU Delft must obtain an MVV (entry visa) and a VVR residence permit for study; the university files both applications with the IND on the student’s behalf and the applicant cannot apply directly. After receiving the university’s financial letter, the student must show proof of at least €13 000 of available funds for one year (approximately €1 083 per month), which can be a blocked bank account, a scholarship or sponsor statement that is Nuffic‑certified, or a guarantee from a recognised institution; the IND checks this documentation together with a valid health‑insurance policy and an unconditional admission letter. The MVV, collected from the Dutch embassy or consulate after biometric enrolment, is a 90‑day entry sticker; once in the Netherlands the student picks up the biometric VVR card from an IND desk (usually within two weeks of a positive decision) and must register at the local municipality (BRP). The combined MVV + VVR fee is €210 (2026) and processing takes about 60–90 days; a TB test may be required for some nationalities, and the student may work up to 16 hours per week (or full‑time in the summer months) while studying. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Netherlands Orientation Year (Zoekjaar) is a one‑year, non‑renewable residence permit that lets recent graduates and researchers stay in the country to look for a job, start a business or work as a freelancer without needing a separate work permit, and it can be applied for up to three years after completing a Bachelor’s, Master’s or PhD at an institution accredited by the NVAO—including TU Delft—or, for the foreign‑top‑university route, from a university ranked within the global top 200 by QS, THE or Shanghai Rankings; applicants must not have previously held a Zoekjaar, must have sufficient financial means (roughly €1,200–€1,500 per month) or a sponsor declaration, and must submit the IND application (e.g., via studyinnl.org) before the three‑year deadline, after which they can work freely, start a company, or transition to a Highly Skilled Migrant or other long‑term permit.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: TU Delft integrates practical experience into virtually every programme, with mandatory internships for Applied Sciences, Aerospace Engineering and Electrical Engineering, Mathematics & Computer Science students and optional or minor‑based placements for all other faculties, coordinated through dedicated internship offices that connect students with companies in engineering, ICT, design, sustainability, aerospace, construction and health‑care sectors; the university’s Career Centre further supports graduates—particularly international students—through personal coaching, employer networks and alumni links, resulting in a graduate employment rate that consistently exceeds national averages (well above the 80.7 % Dutch employment rate reported for Q2 2021) and typically sees more than 90 % of TU Delft alumni securing relevant jobs within six months of graduation, often in high‑tech engineering, innovative ICT, creative industries and emerging sustainable energy fields. Lương & thị trường: International graduates from Delft University of Technology typically enter a Dutch labour market that strongly values technical and engineering expertise, with the most in‑demand occupations including software developers, data scientists, AI specialists, mechanical, civil and aerospace engineers, as well as specialists in renewable energy, high‑tech manufacturing and logistics; these roles are concentrated in the Brainport region around Eindhoven, in Amsterdam’s tech and finance sector, and in the Rotterdam‑The Hague corridor for maritime and infrastructure engineering. Starting salaries for TU Delft graduates reflect this demand: bachelor‑level engineers usually earn between €45,000 and €55,000 per year, while master’s graduates command €55,000 to €70,000, and those entering highly specialised fields such as AI, cybersecurity or advanced robotics can start nearer €80,000. Five years after graduation, a growing share of international alumni earn more than €65,000 annually, and roughly 25 % remain in the Netherlands, with the majority employed in the sectors mentioned above.
