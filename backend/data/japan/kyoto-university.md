### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Kyyo University admits international students for two entry periods – a spring intake that starts in April and an autumn intake that starts in October – and both follow a similar multi‑step procedure: applicants must complete the online application form, upload all required academic and personal documents, and then pay the non‑refundable application fee of JPY 9,800 (a processing surcharge of JPY 650 is also charged); for the autumn intake the supporting documents must be submitted through the online inquiry system between 1 July and 18 October of the preceding year and the fee must be paid via the Examination Settlement Service between 1 November and 2 December, while the spring intake generally requires submission of documents by mid‑November of the year before the April start and fee payment by early December; after the initial eligibility screening, successful candidates are invited to take the qualifying exam (usually held in January–February), confirm their intent to enroll by early May, and then complete the final enrollment steps, which include arriving in Japan by the end of September for the autumn cohort or by late March for the spring cohort.

**3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Kyoto University requires international applicants to demonstrate sufficient Japanese proficiency (generally JLPT N‑1 or N‑2 level, or equivalent university‑level Japanese study) for most undergraduate and graduate programs, since classes are taught in Japanese; however, English‑taught degree programs across ten graduate schools and the Kyoto University International Education Program (KUINEP) accept English as the medium of instruction, and for these applicants a minimum TOEFL iBT score of 72 (or equivalent) or an IELTS overall band score of 5.5 is required, while PTE Academic and Duolingo English Test are not listed as accepted alternatives; applicants who have completed a bachelor’s degree entirely in English or who hold other recognized English‑medium qualifications may be granted a waiver of the TOEFL/IELTS requirement, but Japanese language proficiency remains mandatory unless the specific program is fully English‑taught.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Kyoto University can expect to spend roughly JPY 28,000 per month on accommodation (about JPY 336,000 per year), JPY 38,000 per month on groceries and meals (≈ JPY 456,000 per year), and JPY 4,000 per month on local transportation (≈ JPY 48,000 per year); National Health Insurance for students typically adds around JPY 20,000 annually, while other regular expenses such as utilities, personal items and leisure amount to about JPY 23,000 per month (≈ JPY 276,000 per year). Altogether, the average annual cost of living for an international student in Kyoto—including housing, food, transport and health insurance—is therefore about JPY 1.14 million.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Kyoto University’s international undergraduate pathway begins with a six‑month preparatory course that combines intensive Japanese language classes and foundation subjects, after which students enter a four‑year degree program in which they register for courses prescribed by their faculty alongside Japanese and other international peers; the curriculum is structured around Liberal Arts and Sciences courses, faculty‑specific specialized courses, and Japanese language courses, each typically worth two academic credits, and students must accumulate at least seven credited courses each semester (including up to two Japanese language classes) to satisfy full‑time status, while the preparatory stage serves as a prerequisite for entry into regular faculty programs that generally require sufficient Japanese proficiency, and the program’s learning outcomes include achieving defined Japanese language proficiency levels and acquiring the core knowledge and skills outlined in the faculty’s designated curricula.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Kyoto University is ranked joint 57th in the QS World University Rankings 2026 and 61st in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2026, placing it within the top 5 percent of universities worldwide; domestically it was ranked 4th in the THE Japan University Rankings 2025 and consistently scores highest in outcomes, while its subject‑specific performance is strong across many fields, with QS subject rankings typically placing its engineering, natural sciences and life‑sciences programmes among the top 10 globally, and its humanities and social‑science programmes within the top 30‑50; the university is accredited by the Japan University Accreditation Association (JAA), meets all national quality‑assurance standards, and maintains a notable international community, with roughly 8 percent of its student body comprising overseas students, reflecting its high international outlook score in both QS and THE metrics.

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: International students accepted by Kyoto University must first obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (CoE) from the Japanese Immigration Bureau—Kyoto University acts as a proxy and the issuance typically takes about one month—before applying for a student visa at a Japanese embassy or consulate, and the CoE is required for the visa application; applicants must demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover tuition and living expenses for the full duration of their programme, which universities usually require proof of at least roughly ¥1.5 million per year in bank statements, scholarship awards, or a guarantor’s guarantee, and once in Japan they may engage in part‑time work of up to 28 hours per week during the academic term and up to 40 hours per week during university holidays provided they obtain a “Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted under the Status of Residence” (a work permit) from the Immigration Bureau—this permit is free and can be applied for after arrival—while short‑term stay visas (up to 90 days) do not need a CoE, and the official visa categories, financial requirements, and application procedures are detailed on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs’ study‑in‑Japan website (studyinjapan.go.jp). Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates of Kyoto University (or any Japanese university or graduate school) who have not secured employment by the time they graduate may remain in Japan to continue job hunting by changing their status of residence to “Designated Activities (Continuing Job Hunting)”; eligibility requires a graduation certificate, a letter of recommendation from the university confirming the graduate’s intent and suitability for continued job‑search activities, evidence of ongoing job‑hunting efforts, and proof of sufficient living expenses, and the application must be made through the Immigration Services Agency with a newly issued Certificate of Eligibility; the initial period of stay under this status is six months, and one renewal is permitted, allowing a maximum total stay of up to one year for job hunting after graduation.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Kyoto University’s International Undergraduate Program (iUP) provides a 4.5‑year curriculum that combines a six‑month preparatory course, intensive Japanese language training, and English‑taught majors, and it actively supports international students in securing internships and co‑op placements with Japanese corporations—typically 2‑3‑month research or industry assignments where each lab can host up to two students and the participation fee is waived for exchange students—while offering extensive shukatsu (job‑hunting) workshops, career counseling, and a network of more than 200 recruiting companies; as a result, roughly 95 % of iUP graduates obtain employment or continue to master/doctoral studies within six months of graduation, entering sectors such as engineering, life sciences, information technology, finance, and academic research. Lương & thị trường: In Japan the typical first‑month salary for a university graduate is about ¥210,000, rising to roughly ¥230,000 for those with a graduate‑school degree, with technical (science‑ and engineering‑focused) fields usually commanding a premium of 5‑10 % over business‑oriented tracks; Kyoto University’s own graduate outcomes mirror this pattern, reporting average starting salaries near ¥240,000 for engineering and information‑technology graduates and around ¥220,000 for humanities and social‑science graduates, while the labour market shows strong demand for specialists in information technology, artificial‑intelligence and data‑analysis, advanced manufacturing, biotechnology, healthcare, and financial services, making these occupations the most attractive for both domestic and international graduates seeking employment in the country.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Kyoto University supports its roughly 4,000 international students each year through a comprehensive set of services: the International Support Center (International Student Division) organizes orientation programs to introduce newcomers to campus life and Japan, provides housing referrals, manages university dormitories and assists with accommodation issues, and runs a tutoring system in which graduate (and occasional undergraduate) students—both Japanese and experienced international students—offer daily‑life guidance, Japanese‑language study help, and academic‑field assistance; the iUP (Kyoto iUP) Career Support Center delivers dedicated career services for international students, including career counseling, job‑hunting workshops, employer information sessions and individualized support to navigate the Japanese employment market, while the International Education and Student Mobility Division offers general consultation for any personal or academic concerns international students may encounter. Đời sống & môi trường: Kyoto University offers a vibrant campus‑life environment for international students through an extensive network of more than 400 officially recognised clubs and circles that cover sports, culture, arts, academic interests and language exchange, and students can join any organization directly by contacting the Extracurricular Activities Office; among these groups is a Vietnamese Students Association that brings together Vietnamese undergraduates and graduate students for social events, information sharing, peer‑to‑peer support and assistance with everyday matters such as housing, budgeting and navigating Japanese bureaucracy, while the International Student Mobility Division, the International Support Center and the Education Center for Japanese Language and Culture provide dedicated orientation programmes, counseling, language courses and practical guidance on safety, including 24‑hour campus security, emergency medical services, police contacts and a detailed pocket‑guide handbook that lists procedures at city offices, immigration, counseling services and nearby hospitals, ensuring that Vietnamese and other international students can enjoy campus life with confidence and a strong community network.
