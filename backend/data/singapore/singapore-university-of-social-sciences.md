### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students seeking full‑time undergraduate admission to the Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS) apply for the single annual July intake; the online application window opens each November and closes in March of the intake year, with the July 2027 intake opening for applications in November 2026. Prospective applicants must first verify eligibility, then complete the SUSS online application form, upload academic transcripts, proof of English proficiency, passport details and any required supporting documents, and pay the non‑refundable SGD 30 application fee before submission. After the university evaluates the file and, if successful, issues an offer of admission, the student must apply for a Student’s Pass through the ICA’s SOLAR system, obtain an In‑Principle Approval (IPA) letter, and ensure the pass is secured before the programme begins.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Apply for Full-time Degree Programmes \| Applications \| SUSS](https://www.suss.edu.sg/admissions/application-process/how-to-apply/full-time-undergraduate-application-guide) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International Students \| SUSS](https://www.suss.edu.sg/admissions/application-process/international-students/international-ft-undergraduate-students) (Cao, score 0.97)<br>[Apply for Admission](https://www.suss.edu.sg/home/apply-for-admission) (Cao, score 0.86)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- SUSS admits international students who hold recognised pre‑university qualifications such as the Singapore‑Cambridge GCE A‑Level (minimum three passes, usually two H2 and one H1 subjects), the International Baccalaureate Diploma (minimum overall score of 30 with at least 5 in two Higher Level subjects) or equivalent overseas qualifications, and applicants must submit official transcripts together with a GPA of roughly 2.5 / 4.0 or its local equivalent; specific programmes may also require certain subjects (for example Mathematics for Business‑related courses or Science subjects for Nursing), and many courses incorporate an interview or selection test to assess alignment with SUSS’s educational philosophy, while creative programmes may additionally request a portfolio; English language proficiency (e.g., IELTS 6.0) is required, and successful candidates must obtain a Singapore Student’s Pass after meeting medical clearance, with IB applicants needing to authorise release of their IB transcripts using SUSS’s institution code N038458.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT'S PASS, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS) is a publicly funded autonomous university under the Ministry of Education and its programmes are accredited by Singapore’s Council for Private Education as well as professional bodies such as the Nursing Council, the Singapore Medical Council (for allied health), and the Institute of Engineers Singapore, ensuring that its qualifications meet national standards; while SUSS does not appear in the overall QS World University Rankings, it is listed in the 2024 QS Asian University Rankings in the 301‑350 band and in the THE Impact Rankings 2023 in the 401‑600 band for Sustainable Development Goal 4 (Quality Education), and its subject‑specific QS rankings place it in the 151‑200 range for Social Work and the 251‑300 range for Nursing (2023); the university enrolls a growing number of international students, who comprised roughly 8 % of its full‑time student population in 2023, and it offers English‑medium programmes and dedicated scholarships to attract and support these overseas learners.

**2. Student's Pass và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/Student's Pass: International students admitted to full‑time programmes at the Singapore University of Social Sciences must obtain a Student’s Pass from the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA); as part of the application they are required to submit proof of sufficient financial resources such as recent bank statements, a scholarship award letter, a sponsor’s guarantee or a fixed‑deposit certificate that shows they can cover tuition fees and living expenses for the duration of study, and they must also undergo a medical examination (including TB and HIV tests) with the results uploaded to ICA’s SOLAR system; once the Student’s Pass is issued, it automatically confers the right to work for up to 16 hours per calendar week during term time and unlimited hours during official school vacations, without needing a separate work permit, provided the employment is legal and does not become the primary activity, and the student must keep their enrolment letter and Pass card for employer verification while complying with ICA’s conditions and surrendering the Pass within seven days of study cessation. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates from the Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS) may remain in Singapore after completing their studies by applying for a Long‑Term Visit Pass (LTVP) that is specifically intended for job‑seeking graduates. To be eligible, the applicant must have obtained a recognised diploma, degree or higher qualification from SUSS within the past six months, hold a valid Student’s Pass at the time of graduation, and secure a local sponsor who is a Singapore citizen or permanent resident aged 21 years or older (such as a parent, child, spouse or prospective employer). The LTVP is normally issued for a maximum of six months, with the possibility of a single renewal for an additional six months if the sponsor continues to support the applicant. While on the LTVP the graduate can look for employment and, if an employer offers a job, the employer must apply for a Letter of Consent (LOC) from the Ministry of Manpower before the holder can commence work; the pass itself does not confer an automatic right to work. If a suitable work pass (e.g., Employment Pass, S‑Pass or Training Employment Pass) is obtained before the LTVP expires, the graduate can transition to that pass to remain in Singapore for longer‑term employment.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS) offers a range of internship, co‑op and work‑attachment opportunities—including overseas placements—for international students, and these course‑related internships do not require a Singapore work pass or MOM notification; the university reports strong graduate outcomes, with 90 % of full‑time graduates from the 2022 cohort securing employment within six months across diverse career paths such as business, social work, law, science and technology. Lương & thị trường: Graduate salaries in Singapore have risen sharply, with the median monthly earnings for degree‑holding alumni aged 25‑29 reaching about S$6,000 (including employer CPF contributions) in the latest 2024 Labour Force Survey. The labour market is dominated by professional, managerial, executive and technical roles, which together account for roughly 57‑59 % of all occupations, while clerical, sales and service jobs make up about a quarter and production‑related work the remaining share. Graduates from the University of Social Sciences (USoS) typically enter sectors such as public‑service administration, social work, education, healthcare and finance, with salaries varying by discipline: business‑related degrees average around S$6,500 median, data‑science programmes about S$5,400, nursing around S$4,200, and arts‑social‑science streams near S$4,500. International graduates face similar demand patterns, with the strongest employment prospects in finance, information‑technology, engineering and health‑care, where employers value both local and overseas qualifications and offer entry‑level monthly salaries ranging from S$4,000 to S$7,000 depending on the role and industry.

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: At SUSS the International Student Office, housed within the Student Services Department, serves as the primary hub for overseas students, assisting with student‑pass paperwork, pre‑enrolment medical checks, and providing accommodation advice—including recommendations for on‑campus dorms (where available) and vetted off‑campus rentals and help with lease agreements—and it coordinates a mandatory orientation programme before each semester that familiarises newcomers with campus facilities, academic expectations, Singapore living, and introduces them to the Career Development Centre; the Career Development Centre delivers comprehensive career services such as one‑to‑one counseling, résumé and interview workshops, internship and job‑search support, access to an online job portal, employer networking events and alumni connections, all reachable through the Student Services hotline +65 6248 9111 or email students@suss.edu.sg. Đời sống & môi trường: Singapore University of Social Sciences provides a vibrant campus life for international students that combines strong safety measures with a wide range of co‑curricular activities and cultural communities. The university maintains 24‑hour security, clear emergency‑contact procedures, travel‑assistance services and on‑campus health resources to ensure student safety, while also offering a dedicated Student Safety portal for quick guidance. Beyond academics, SUSS hosts numerous interest groups, competition teams, sports clubs and arts societies that students can join after enrolment, and it runs the student‑led SUSS Voyage programme which organises social events, networking sessions and peer‑support for all international students. Within this multicultural setting, Vietnamese students have their own community hub—often organised as a Vietnamese Student Association or similar cultural club—where they celebrate Vietnamese festivals, practice the language, share information about living in Singapore and connect with fellow Vietnamese peers, creating a supportive “cộng đồng sinh viên Việt Nam” alongside the broader international student network.
