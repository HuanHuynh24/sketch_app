### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply (UCAS)**

- International undergraduate applicants to LSE must apply through UCAS – direct applications are not accepted – and the only entry point is the September (autumn) intake; LSE does not offer a spring start for most programmes. While UCAS accepts applications from 1 September to 30 June, LSE advises that all applicants, including those from outside the EU, submit their UCAS form by 14 January 2026 (18:00 GMT) for full and equal consideration for the September 2026 entry (the next cycle’s deadline will be 13 January 2027 18:00 GMT). The cost is the standard UCAS application fee (currently £26) and, if LSE requires you to sit its Undergraduate Admissions Assessment (or the LNAT where applicable), an additional LSE assessment fee of about £30 may be charged. The application process therefore consists of: creating a UCAS account, completing personal details, education history and a personal statement, selecting the desired LSE courses, paying the UCAS fee (and any LSE assessment fee if requested), submitting by the January deadline, and then awaiting any further testing (e.g., LNAT/UGAA) and the university’s decision.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- LSE’s undergraduate entry requirements for international students are expressed in A‑Level and International Baccalaureate (IB) terms, with equivalents accepted from other grading systems via a transcript‑based GPA conversion; applicants must provide full academic transcripts and, where applicable, a converted UK‑style GPA (generally a minimum of a 2:1 equivalent, i.e., about 55 % average or higher). For A‑Level routes the usual standard offer is AAA, while IB candidates need an overall score of 37 points (typically 6‑6‑6 at Higher Level) for most programmes and up to 39 points (7‑6‑6) for more competitive courses, with individual subject requirements listed on each programme page. LSE does not normally require portfolios or interviews for its social‑science programmes, though certain courses may ask candidates to sit the Undergraduate Admissions Assessment (UGAA) or submit additional evidence if specified; Advanced Placement qualifications are generally not considered for admission.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- LSE requires all international applicants whose first language is not English to demonstrate proficiency in reading, writing, speaking and listening by achieving the minimum scores set for the test they choose, and the result must be taken within two years of the programme start date (by 1 September for undergraduate entry and by 1 October for postgraduate entry). For most undergraduate programmes the required IELTS Academic score is 7.0 overall (with at least 6.5 in each component); the equivalent TOEFL iBT target is 100 overall (minimum 25 Reading, 22 Listening, 27 Writing and 22 Speaking) and the Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic) score must be 70 overall with at least 62 in each component. For postgraduate taught programmes the standard requirement is IELTS 7.0 overall (6.5 in each skill), TOEFL iBT 100 overall (same component minima as undergraduates) and PTE Academic 70 overall (62 minimum per component), with some programmes such as Law requiring a higher IELTS overall of 7.5 (7.0 in each skill) and TOEFL 100 with component minima of 27 Reading, 22 Listening, 27 Writing and 22 Speaking. **5. Chi phí sinh hoạt & IHS**

- International students at LSE should budget roughly £11,450 for accommodation (single‑room basis for the 39‑week academic year), about £4,095 for food, and around £3,485 for other living expenses such as transport, laundry and personal items, bringing the estimated total to £19,030 for the 39‑week period; a broader guideline of £1,550 per month for all living costs (including accommodation, travel, food and miscellaneous items) works out to roughly £18,600 over a full 12‑month year, and the UK Immigration Health Surcharge adds £624 per annum, so the overall annual cost for an international student at LSE is approximately £19,600 – £19,700.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- LSE is ranked among the world’s elite institutions, sitting in the top ten of both the QS World University Rankings overall and the Times Higher Education World University Rankings (most recently 9th in THE 2024/25), and it is placed 5th globally for Social Sciences and Management in the QS Subject Rankings 2026, achieving top‑five positions in Geography (2nd), Development Studies (4th), Social Policy and Administration (4th) and Politics & International Studies (5th) while also appearing in the top ten for Communication & Media Studies, Economics & Econometrics, Sociology, Accounting & Finance, Anthropology, Philosophy, Law, History and Marketing; about 70 % of its student body are international students, and its programmes are professionally accredited – for example, its Accounting and Finance courses hold ACCA, ICAEW and CFA Institute accreditation and its Law programmes are recognised by the Law Society, with the School of Management holding AACSB accreditation.

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: International students applying for a UK Student visa to study at the London School of Economics must demonstrate that they have enough funds to cover the full tuition fee shown on their Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) plus the required living costs of £1,529 per month for up to nine months, which equals a maximum of £13,761. The financial evidence can be a recent (held for at least 28 days) bank statement, a letter from an official financial sponsor, a government‑issued loan letter, or a scholarship award letter. An official financial sponsor is defined as Her Majesty’s Government, the applicant’s home government, the British Council, an international organisation, a company, a university, or a UK independent school; support from relatives or non‑official bodies does not count as official sponsorship. If the funds are held in a parent’s or partner’s account, the applicant must also provide a birth or marriage certificate and a signed consent letter confirming the relationship and permission to use those funds, and the partner must be applying at the same time or already have UK immigration permission. Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): The UK Graduate Route allows international graduates – including those who have completed a degree at the London School of Economics – to remain in the UK after their studies without needing a job offer, providing up to two years of stay for those with a bachelor's or master’s degree and up to three years for PhD holders; to be eligible you must have successfully finished an eligible course at a UK higher‑education institution that meets Home Office compliance standards, hold a valid student visa at the time of application, apply online from within the UK before your student visa expires, have your university confirm your qualification, possess a valid passport and Biometric Residence Permit, meet the English‑language requirement, and be prepared to pay the application fee and Immigration Health Surcharge for the length of the Graduate Route visa.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: LSE supports international students in securing internships, placements and graduate jobs through a dedicated year‑long “Careers in the UK” programme that helps them navigate recruitment, visa sponsorship and networking, and many students use the Student Circus platform to find paid, structured internships ranging from a few weeks to a year that often lead to permanent roles, especially in finance, consulting, professional services, technology, media and the public sector; graduate outcomes data collected by HESA for cohorts graduating between 2018/19 and 2022/23 show that roughly 94 % of LSE graduates are in employment or further study about 15 months after graduation, with a large proportion working in financial services, management consulting, government and international organisations, and the university’s career resources also guide students toward co‑op and placement opportunities that enhance employability and align with the most common post‑graduate career pathways. Lương & thị trường: In 2024, 87.6 % of working‑age graduates in England were employed, with 67.9 % holding high‑skill jobs (SOC codes 1‑3) and the median nominal graduate salary reaching £42 000 (about £2 000 above the 2023 level); demand is strongest for occupations that appear on the UK Shortage Occupation List and for sectors that traditionally recruit large numbers of international graduates, notably nursing, other health‑care roles, and engineering (civil, mechanical, electrical and software), as well as finance, consulting and technology positions, all of which typically offer starting salaries of £30 000‑£45 000 and higher for experienced professionals, making them the most viable pathways for international graduates seeking skilled‑worker visas.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The London School of Economics and Political Science’s International Office supports all non‑UK students with visa and immigration guidance, specialist advice for refugees and displaced scholars, and a dedicated International Student Advisor who coordinates the annual Welcome Week orientation programme that includes campus tours, information sessions on academic life, health and wellbeing, and introductions to student societies; the university’s Accommodation Office assists international students in securing on‑campus residence across halls such as Passfield, Rosebery, Butler’s Wharf, High Holborn, Carr‑Saunders and Bankside, and also provides private‑housing advice and a 24‑hour wellbeing line for hall residents, while LSE Careers delivers tailored employability support for international students—including one‑to‑one appointments, CV and cover‑letter feedback, workshops on UK labour‑market regulations, country‑specific career guides, access to an international jobs board and employer events both in the UK and abroad—without placing students in jobs but helping them develop the skills and knowledge needed for internships, graduate roles or further study. Đời sống & môi trường: LSE’s campus life is built around a vibrant network of over a hundred LSESU clubs and societies—including a Vietnamese Student Society that hosts cultural festivals, language‑exchange evenings and networking events for Vietnamese and other international students—so joining a society is one of the quickest ways to make friends, feel a sense of community and stay safe through peer support; the university also provides dedicated international‑student services, a 24‑hour campus security team with safe‑walk escorts, a free Students’ Union Advice Service for academic, housing and financial issues, a Wellbeing Service with medical, counselling and mental‑health support, and an Equity, Diversity and Inclusion Office that promotes a safe, inclusive environment for all students, meaning that both social life and personal safety are well‑covered for anyone studying at LSE.
