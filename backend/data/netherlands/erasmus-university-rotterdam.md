## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International students who wish to study at Erasmus University Rotterdam must begin their admission process in the Dutch national registration system Studielink, which opens each year on 1 October; the university offers a main September intake for most bachelor programmes (with some master programmes also admitting students in a February intake) and, for the International Business Administration bachelor, the final Studielink deadline is 15 January (with the RSM online application form due by 31 January), while other bachelor programmes such as International Bachelor Communication & Media require submission in the university’s Application Portal by 15 March and International Bachelor Arts & Culture Studies set deadlines of 1 April for non‑EEA applicants and 1 May for EEA applicants; all applicants holding a non‑Dutch diploma must pay a non‑refundable €100 application fee, which is paid in the Application Portal after completing the Studielink steps, after which they upload the required documents, confirm payment and submit the application before the programme‑specific deadline.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Apply for Bachelor International Bachelor Communication and Media \| Erasmus University Rotterdam](https://www.eur.nl/en/bachelor/international-bachelor-communication-and-media/application) (Cao, score 0.79)<br>[Apply for Bachelor International Bachelor Arts and Culture Studies \| Erasmus University Rotterdam](https://www.eur.nl/en/bachelor/international-bachelor-arts-and-culture-studies/application) (Cao, score 0.78)<br>[Apply for Bachelor Bachelor International Business Administration \| Erasmus University Rotterdam](https://www.eur.nl/en/bachelor/bachelor-international-business-administration/application) (Cao, score 0.77)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International bachelor programmes at Erasmus University Rotterdam admit students whose secondary‑school qualification is recognised as equivalent to the Dutch VWO diploma; the diploma must include general‑education subjects (vocational subjects are not accepted) and mathematics studied up to the final year, with a minimum grade of 7.0 (on the Dutch 1‑10 scale) for Mathematics A or 6.0 for Mathematics B, and a minimum English‑language grade of 7.0. If the VWO‑equivalent does not meet the maths requirement, applicants must sit the Online Mathematics Placement Test (OMPT) and achieve at least 70 % for OMPT‑A or 60 % for OMPT‑B. For holders of an American high‑school diploma the university requires a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or higher plus four independent AP courses in general academic subjects (including AP Calculus and only one language AP). Other foreign diplomas are assessed individually, but must also fulfil the same subject‑profile and grade thresholds; language‑test exemptions are granted for candidates from Curaçao, Sint Maarten and Suriname who hold a VWO diploma. Admission is based on the diploma and grades (học bạ, điểm thi); no portfolio or interview is required for the standard bachelor tracks, although specific programmes may request additional evidence that would be stated separately.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Erasmus University Rotterdam requires proof of English proficiency for all non‑native speakers, with the standard tests being IELTS Academic (overall score 6.5) or TOEFL iBT (minimum score 92 for tests taken after 21 January 2026, 237 for the computer‑based test or 580 for the paper‑based test); the university does not accept TOEFL ITP, TOEFL Home Edition, online IELTS, PTE Academic or Duolingo assessments. For bachelor programmes a second‑language GCSE in English is not sufficient, and applicants must submit IELTS or TOEFL unless they have completed more than 2.5 years of full‑time study in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Ireland, Australia, New Zealand, or South Africa, in which case they are exempt. For master programmes native‑speaker status (from the same English‑speaking countries) also grants an exemption, otherwise the same IELTS/TOEFL thresholds apply, and no alternative tests such as PTE or Duolingo are recognised.

**4. Học phí**

- Erasmus University Rotterdam charges a statutory tuition fee of €2 601 per year for Dutch/EEA students and a higher statutory fee of €5 600 for EUC students, but most international (non‑EEA) students pay the institutional fee; for the 2025‑2026 academic year the institutional bachelor fee for non‑EEA students is €11 400 per year and the standard institutional master fee is roughly €19 950 per year (with a one‑time reduced rate of €18 500 for re‑enrolling master students), while the International Institute of Social Studies – a unit of Erasmus University – lists a flat MA tuition fee of €21 000 (the same for EU and non‑EU students) plus immigration fees of €254, mandatory health‑insurance premium of €681 and an estimated living cost of €1 150 per month (€13 800 for a year), resulting in a total cost of about €35 735; an application fee is charged separately, typically around €200, and all amounts are expressed in euros.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- International students at Erasmus University Rotterdam typically need to budget about €1 000 – €1 800 per month for all living expenses, which translates to an annual cost of roughly €12 000 – €21 600. The main components are accommodation, which on average costs €700 – €1 000 per month (about €8 400 – €12 000 per year), health insurance at €56 per month (€672 per year), and daily expenses such as food, drinks, clothing, leisure and public transport at around €450 per month (approximately €5 400 annually). In addition, one‑off costs include a visa and residence‑permit fee of about €243 and a second‑hand bicycle for around €150.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students wishing to enroll at Erasmus University Rotterdam must satisfy secondary‑school subject and grade requirements (for example a minimum of three A‑levels or equivalent with grades around A‑B, plus required GCSE subjects), demonstrate English proficiency (typically IELTS 7.0 or TOEFL 100 with minimum sub‑scores), and in many programmes present at least 32 prior credits; the bachelor programmes are structured on the ECTS system where one credit equals 28 study hours, each academic year totals 60 ECTS (180 ECTS for the full degree) and is divided into five eight‑week blocks in which two to three courses are taken, including core modules, electives (12 ECTS when the minor is 12 ECTS, otherwise 9 ECTS), and a compulsory minor or abroad internship; the curriculum emphasises the application of mathematical and statistical techniques to economic questions, with learning outcomes that include modelling and forecasting time‑series data, applying optimisation and operations‑research methods, and interpreting quantitative analyses for policy and business decision‑making.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Erasmus University Rotterdam is positioned among the top Dutch institutions, ranking #6 in the Netherlands and #140 worldwide in the QS World University Rankings, while the Times Higher Education World University Ranking places it at #6 in the Netherlands and #99 globally (with the education‑focused THE ranking showing a higher #4 in the Netherlands and #72 worldwide); its subject strengths are reflected in international rankings where Business Administration is ranked #4, Public Administration #5, Management #7 and Economics #33 (according to the ARWU discipline list), and in the National Taiwan University ranking it is #26 worldwide for Clinical Medicine and #23 for Social Sciences, and the university holds full institutional accreditation from the Dutch–Flemish Accreditation Organization (NVAO), confirming that all its programmes meet recognised quality standards.

**2. Student visa (MVV/VVR) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/MVV: Non‑EU students who have an unconditional admission to Erasmus University Rotterdam must obtain an MVV (machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf) entry visa and, after arriving in the Netherlands, a VVR (verblijfsvergunning regulier) residence permit; the university acts as an IND‑recognised sponsor and files a TEV application on the student’s behalf, paying the IND fee of about €254 (often collected from the student) and the combined MVV‑VVR service fee of roughly €210. The IND requires proof of sufficient financial means equal to the Dutch study‑norm, which for 2026 is €13 000 per year (≈ €1 083 per month) and can be shown via recent bank statements, a blocked account, scholarship confirmation or a Nuffic‑issued guarantee for Chinese applicants; a valid passport, health insurance and full‑time enrollment are also mandatory. After the IND grants the residence permit, the MVV sticker (valid 90 days) allows entry, and the biometric VVR card is issued for the duration of the study programme, with the whole process typically taking 60–90 days, after which students may work up to 16 hours per week (full‑time in the summer months). Sau tốt nghiệp: The Dutch orientation‑year (zoekjaar) residence permit allows recent international graduates – including those who have completed an accredited bachelor's, master’s or PhD programme at Erasmus University Rotterdam – to stay in the Netherlands for up to twelve months after graduation to look for employment, start a business or do an internship, and during this period they may work without needing a separate work permit; eligibility requires that the graduate apply within three years of the diploma date, must not have previously held a zoekjaar permit (only one is granted per lifetime), and must have sufficient financial means to support themselves, while the permit is a single‑year stay that cannot be extended and must be replaced by another residence permit if the graduate wishes to remain longer.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Erasmus University Rotterdam actively supports international students in securing internships, placements and graduate jobs through its Career Services and the Magnet.me platform, which match students with a wide range of opportunities—including on‑campus roles and positions with Dutch companies, especially SMEs that make up 99 % of the Dutch market—in sectors such as finance, consulting, logistics, health care and NGOs, and the university reports that about 90 % of its graduates obtain employment or continue to further study within six months of graduation, with many entering multinational corporations, SMEs or the public sector, while also providing guidance on work permits, starting a business and networking to boost employability. Lương & thị trường: Graduates of Erasmus University Rotterdam enter a strong Dutch labour market, with most finding employment quickly and securing salaries that are above the national average; entry‑level earnings typically range from €45 000 to €55 000 per year, while five years after graduation a sizable share of international alumni earn more than €65 000 annually, reflecting the higher earnings trajectory of non‑Dutch graduates. The most in‑demand occupations for these graduates are concentrated in finance (about a quarter of hires), consulting, technology and telecommunications, logistics and transport, healthcare, consumer‑packaged goods and manufacturing, with the finance sector alone accounting for roughly 27 % of placements and technology‑related roles growing rapidly. Around 80 % of Erasmus graduates choose to remain in the Netherlands after completing their studies, and among those who stay, roughly 80 % are employed within five years, indicating both a high retention rate and strong demand for their skill sets across the Dutch economy.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Erasmus University Rotterdam’s International Office serves as the central hub for international students, offering comprehensive assistance with admission queries, orientation and arrival information, and immigration matters, while also providing dedicated support for finding housing in Rotterdam; the university’s career services are highly rated and include personalized career‑goal preparation, in‑person workshops, events and new digital modules that help students develop job‑search skills, explore internships and prepare for the Dutch labour market, with recent efforts focused on increasing awareness of these resources among international students. Đời sống & môi trường: Erasmus University Rotterdam (EUR) offers a vibrant campus life with a wide range of student organisations that cater to both Dutch and international students, including International Students Rotterdam (ISR) and Erasmus Student Network Rotterdam (ESN Rotterdam) which organise social, cultural and corporate activities, as well as region‑specific clubs such as the Eastern European Students Association, the Turkish Mozaik association, and historic corps houses like the Rotterdamsch Studenten Corps (RSC) and Rotterdamsch Studenten Gezelschap (RSG); the university enforces a strict code of conduct for introductions and hazing that all associations sign together with EUR, Hogeschool Rotterdam and InHolland to ensure safety, and provides comprehensive orientation programmes, housing assistance, healthcare information, Dutch language courses and a 24‑hour emergency system to support newcomers; Vietnamese students are represented by a Vietnamese Student Association that arranges Tết celebrations, language practice sessions and networking events, and many also participate in broader Asian or international clubs, creating an active Vietnamese community within the larger international student body.
