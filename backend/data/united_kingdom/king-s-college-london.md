## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply (UCAS)**

- King’s College London admits international students for a main September (autumn) intake and a smaller January (spring) intake; applications are made through UCAS, which charges only its standard fee (about £26 for UK/EU applicants and £30 for overseas applicants) and King’s does not add any extra charge. For the September start, the UCAS deadline is 15 January 2026 for most programmes (with an earlier 15 October 2025 deadline for medicine, dentistry and veterinary science), while the January start deadline is 30 June 2026. Applicants must create a UCAS account, fill in personal and academic details, submit a personal statement, provide a referee’s reference, select King’s as a choice, and submit before the relevant deadline; King’s then assesses the application and issues conditional or unconditional offers. International candidates must also arrange a student‑visa deposit, provide proof of English proficiency and a copy of their passport, and may need to pay visa‑related fees later. For postgraduate programmes, the process is a direct online application on King’s website with an application fee of roughly £100‑£150, and deadlines are typically 30 April for September entry and 31 August for January entry, with the same requirement to meet English language standards and visa requirements.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- King’s College London requires international applicants to meet the same academic standards as UK students, with A‑Level entry typically needing grades from AAA to BBB depending on the programme (e.g., AAA for highly competitive courses, ABB or BBC for others) and no General Studies, Critical Thinking, Thinking Skills or Global Perspectives accepted; the equivalent International Baccalaureate requirement is generally 36 points overall (or 18 points from three Higher‑Level subjects) with the total including the Theory of Knowledge and Extended Essay, while for postgraduate study a UK 2:1 degree is usually expected, which translates to a GPA of about 3.3 / 4.0 (or 3.75 / 5.0) and a first‑class degree to a GPA of roughly 3.7 / 4.0 (or 4.3 / 5.0); applicants must submit official transcripts and proof of English proficiency, and although most academic programmes do not require a portfolio or interview, certain subjects such as architecture, art and design or some creative and performance‑based courses do ask for a portfolio and may hold an interview as part of the selection process, so prospective students should check the specific course page for any additional subject prerequisites, portfolio guidelines or interview requirements.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- King’s College London requires international applicants to demonstrate a Band B level of English proficiency, which for most programmes means an IELTS Academic score of 7.0 overall with at least 6.5 in reading and writing and 6.0 in listening and speaking (a lower threshold of 6.5 overall with 6.0 in each skill is accepted for some pre‑sessional pathways). For the TOEFL iBT (including the Home Edition) the typical benchmark is a total of 92 points with a minimum of 20 in each component, while the Pearson Test of English Academic is accepted at 69 overall with a minimum of 62 in each communicative skill (the UKVI version requires the same overall score but does not accept the online version). King’s does not list Duolingo English Test as an accepted qualification. English‑language waivers are granted to students who are already studying at a U.S. university, to those who have completed a degree that was taught in English, and to applicants who can provide an English‑proficiency letter using the university’s template in place of a formal test result.

**4. Học phí**

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- King’s College London is consistently placed among the top universities in the United Kingdom, ranking 26th in the global QS World University Rankings and being listed as the 6th‑best UK institution for graduate employability in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2025; it also ranked 11th worldwide and 4th in the UK for international outlook in the THE Most International Universities table 2022. In QS subject rankings for 2025, King’s achieved a world‑leading 1st place in Nursing and placed 11th for Life Sciences & Medicine, and it is recognised across 42 disciplines in the QS World University Rankings by Subject. The university holds full institutional accreditation from the UK Quality Assurance Agency for Higher Education (QAA) and its professional programmes carry the relevant statutory accreditations, including the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) for nursing, the General Medical Council (GMC) for medicine, the Bar Standards Board for law, the Chartered Insurance Institute for actuarial studies, and other specialist bodies for engineering, architecture, and allied health professions.

**2. Student Visa và Graduate Route Visa**

- Student Visa: For a King’s College London international student applying on the UK Student Route, the Home Office requires proof of maintenance of £1,529 per month for up to nine months (a total of £13,761) together with evidence that the full tuition fee for the first academic year is available; the funds must be held in a regulated personal or parent‑named bank account (or a recognized student loan) for a continuous 28‑day period, with statements or a bank letter covering the whole period and dated no more than 31 days before the visa application, and if parental accounts are used a signed parental consent letter and the student’s birth certificate must also be submitted; business accounts are not accepted, and the applicant must first receive a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from King’s College, then apply via the official GOV.UK student‑visa portal, paying the visa fee and Immigration Health Surcharge. Sau tốt nghiệp (Graduate Route): International graduates who have successfully completed an eligible degree at King’s College London may apply for the UK Graduate Route (formerly the Post‑Study Work visa) while holding a valid Student (formerly Tier 4) visa, and must submit the application within three months of receiving their final results. Under the current rules that apply until 31 December 2026, holders of a bachelor’s or master’s degree are granted an unrestricted right to live, work or look for work in the UK for two years, whereas PhD or other doctoral graduates receive three years of the same rights; the visa cannot be extended or renewed, but holders may later switch to a Skilled Worker or other long‑term route, and they may bring eligible dependants (spouse/partner and children) with them for the same period. (From January 2027 the two‑year period for bachelor’s and master’s graduates will be reduced to 18 months, while the three‑year period for doctoral graduates will remain unchanged.)

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: King’s College London is ranked sixth in the UK for graduate employability (THE 2025) and its Careers & Employability service delivers dedicated support for international students, including career coaching, a wide range of internship and placement programmes, networking events and industry‑specific webinars that link students with employers in sectors such as law, finance, consulting, health‑care, media, technology and the public sector; the university reports that the majority of graduates secure employment or further study within six months of finishing—overall graduate employment rates regularly exceed 90 % across its faculties, with Business School, Law, Life Sciences & Medicine and Arts & Humanities all showing over 70 % of under‑graduates moving into paid work, further study or professional training shortly after graduation. Lương & thị trường: King’s College London graduates typically earn starting salaries that reflect the strong demand for specialised skills in London’s finance, legal, healthcare and technology sectors, with median earnings 15 months after graduation ranging from roughly £27 000 for humanities degrees to more than £43 000 for medicine, and a five‑year median of about £38 000; these figures consistently exceed the UK national average because employers in high‑growth areas such as financial services, digital analytics, artificial‑intelligence development and clinical research actively recruit graduates, while the UK’s graduate‑route visa permits international alumni to take any role for up to two years, although recent immigration policy has raised the salary thresholds for skilled‑worker visas to £41 700 for graduate‑level positions and £33 400 for other skilled roles, meaning that many international graduates aim for the higher‑paid occupations that meet these thresholds to secure longer‑term work permits.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: King’s College London’s International Student Office provides a comprehensive support package for overseas students, beginning with pre‑arrival guidance and welcome‑week orientation events that introduce campus life, academic resources and community groups; the university also guarantees first‑year accommodation and offers a dedicated housing‑support team through King’s Accommodation to help students find suitable rooms, resolve tenancy issues and settle into their new homes, while the Careers & Employability service delivers specialist assistance for international students—including career coaching, CV and interview workshops, internship and placement opportunities, networking events for key markets such as China and India, and expert advice on UK visa and immigration rules—ensuring graduates are equipped to pursue work in the UK or abroad, and all of these services are coordinated through Student Services Online, which houses detailed articles and contact options for any additional queries. Đời sống & môi trường: King’s College London offers a vibrant campus life for international students, beginning with the “International Student Welcome” programme—a mix of online and in‑person events in September that introduce newcomers to living in London, connect them with peers, and showcase the many societies and clubs listed on the KCLSU website, including cultural groups such as the active Vietnamese Society which organises language‑exchange evenings, cultural festivals and networking gatherings for Vietnamese students and anyone interested in Vietnam; all students living in King’s Residences enjoy free access to the university gyms and the BeActive wellbeing programme, while those in private housing can still join campus activities through the Welcome to King’s app and the @lifeatkings Instagram channel; safety is a core priority with a dedicated “Safety in London” guide for international students covering ID‑carrying advice, personal security tips, and an emergency contact procedure that links directly to university safeguarding services, ensuring that students have 24‑hour support whether they are on campus, on placement abroad, or exploring the city.
