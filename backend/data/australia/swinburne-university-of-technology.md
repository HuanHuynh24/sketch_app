**3. Tiếng Anh**

- International students applying to Swinburne University of Technology must meet one of the listed English language benchmarks: an overall IELTS Academic score of 5.5 with no individual band below 5.0 (or the same result on an IELTS One‑Skill Retake taken within 60 days of the original test), a PTE Academic total of 46 with minimum sub‑scores of 4 in reading, 4 in listening, 14 in speaking and 14 in writing, a TOEFL iBT total of 64 with at least 8 in reading, 7 in listening, 16 in speaking and 18 in writing, or an accepted Cambridge C1 Advanced qualification; the Duolingo English Test is not listed as an accepted test. An English language waiver may be granted when a candidate has completed at least two consecutive years of full‑time study in English at a recognized tertiary institution or holds a prior qualification that was taught entirely in English (such as a degree from an English‑speaking country), in which case test evidence is not required.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- International students enrolling at Swinburne University of Technology must satisfy English language prerequisites such as a minimum overall IELTS score of 5.5 (no band below 5.0) for foundation and diploma pathways and 6.0 (no band below 5.5) for undergraduate and postgraduate courses, or equivalent scores on TOEFL iBT, and for those entering via secondary qualifications they need a VCE Units 3 & 4 English study score of at least 25 (or the IB equivalent of 4 in English A1/A2 or 5 in English B at Higher Level); once admitted, study is organized into “units” that function as individual subjects, each normally worth 12.5 credit points (with some larger units at 25 points), and international students are required to enrol full‑time by taking a minimum of three units (37.5 credit points) or four units (50 credit points) each semester; each unit follows a structured curriculum that outlines specific learning outcomes describing the knowledge, skills and professional competencies students will acquire, and these unit‑level outcomes collectively contribute to the broader program outcomes defined for each bachelor, honours or postgraduate degree.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student visa (subclass 500) for international students at Swinburne University of Technology requires proof of financial capacity of at least AUD 29,710 for the main applicant (covering tuition, living expenses and travel), with additional amounts of AUD 10,394 for a partner and AUD 4,449 per child if dependents accompany, plus school fees of AUD 13,502 per child where applicable, and the applicant must hold Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire duration of the stay; the visa grants the right to work up to 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session (unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks, for research‑based master’s or doctoral programs, and for mandatory CRICOS‑listed placements), and it also obliges the student to remain enrolled, maintain satisfactory attendance and academic progress, notify their education provider of their Australian address within seven days of arrival, and ensure the OSHC remains valid throughout the visa period. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the post‑study work visa for international graduates from Swinburne University of Technology, allowing you to remain in Australia after completing a CRICOS‑registered qualification; if you have a bachelor’s, master’s or doctoral degree you apply to the Post‑Study Work (Post‑Higher Education Work) stream, which normally grants a stay of two to three years (five years for Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders) and up to four years for certain qualifications such as a doctoral degree, while the Graduate Work stream, for qualifications related to an occupation on the Skilled Occupation List, can provide up to four years depending on the level of study, and both streams give unrestricted work rights, no employer sponsorship is required, and you may include your partner and dependent children who meet health and character requirements.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Swinburne University of Technology guarantees that all bachelor‑degree students, including international students, undertake a placement, internship or industry‑linked project as part of its Work Integrated Learning (WIL) program, and this hands‑on experience translates into strong graduate outcomes: more than one‑third of students who complete a placement receive a job offer from the host organisation, about 20 % of internship participants are offered ongoing work, and the university reports that over 90 % of its graduates secure employment or further study within four months of graduation, with many moving into sectors such as engineering, information‑technology, design, health and business. Lương & thị trường: Domestic Swinburne University of Technology undergraduate alumni earn a median full‑time salary of about A$78 000, while international graduates earn considerably less, with median full‑time earnings of roughly A$54 300 for undergraduates, A$54 800 for postgraduate coursework and around A$87 000 for postgraduate research degrees, reflecting a pay gap of roughly 20‑30 %; Jobs and Skills Australia’s labour‑market forecasts show the strongest demand over the next several years for occupations such as construction managers, software and applications programmers, engineers, health professionals, teachers and other roles in professional, scientific and technical services, whereas routine administrative and low‑skill manual jobs are projected to decline, so international graduates seeking higher earnings should target these high‑growth sectors.
