## **B. THÔNG TIN TUYỂN SINH VÀ HỌC BỔNG NĂM 2026**

### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- The University of Canterbury admits international students in two main semesters—Semester 1 (starting late February/early March) and Semester 2 (starting late July)—and applicants should apply through the online myUC portal at least six months before the intended start, with the recommended deadlines being 1 December of the preceding year for Semester 1 and 1 May for Semester 2; an application fee of NZ $115 is payable when the online form is submitted, and the enrolment process involves confirming you meet the academic and English‑language entry requirements, completing the myUC application and uploading transcripts, English‑proficiency evidence and other documents, paying the tuition‑fee deposit (usually the first‑semester fees) which is required for the student‑visa application, and then applying for a New Zealand student visa once you have received an offer of admission.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Enrol as an international student \| UC - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/getting-started/admission-and-enrolment/enrol-international-student) (Cao, score 0.79)<br>[Admission to UC with an overseas qualification](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/getting-started/admission-and-enrolment/admission-pathways/overseas-qualification) (Cao, score 0.72)<br>[International \| University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/communities/international) (Cao, score 0.67)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students seeking admission to the University of Canterbury must normally achieve New Zealand University Entrance through NCEA (Level 3 with at least 14 credits in each of three subjects plus the required literacy and numeracy credits) or present an overseas qualification that is judged equivalent, and most programmes expect a minimum NZ GPA of about 2.5 (roughly a B+ grade) with many competitive courses requiring a GPA of 3.0 or higher; English language proficiency is measured by IELTS, with an overall score of 6.5 and no band below 6.5 being the standard minimum (the university will give preference to scores of 7 or above, while lower scores of 5.5 or 6.0 can be accepted if the student completes the English for Academic Purposes EAP 1 or EAP 2 pathway); a small number of programmes—particularly in the creative arts, design, architecture, music, and some education streams—also ask for a portfolio and/or interview as part of the selection process.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [[PDF] Programme specific entry requirements - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/content/dam/uoc-main-site/documents/pdfs/d-other/Programme-specific-entry-requirements.pdf.coredownload.pdf) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[English for Academic Purposes (EAP) - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/other-study-options/transition-programmes/pathways-for-international-students/english-for-academic-purposes) (Cao, score 0.98)<br>[NCEA University Entrance \| UC](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/getting-started/admission-and-enrolment/admission-pathways/ncea-university-entrance) (Cao, score 0.87)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- The University of Canterbury requires all international students to prove they can study in English either by having New Zealand University Entrance, by holding an overseas qualification that was taught substantially in English, or by achieving the minimum score on an approved English‑language test taken within two years of the intended start date; the accepted tests are IELTS, TOEFL iBT, Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic) and the Duolingo English Test, with the standard IELTS requirement being a band 6.0 for most programmes (some pathways accept a band 5.5) and comparable scores set for the other tests, and students who do not meet these thresholds can enrol in the university’s English for Academic Purposes (EAP 1 or EAP 2) programmes, which upon successful completion satisfy the English‑language condition.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [English for Academic Purposes (EAP) - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/other-study-options/transition-programmes/pathways-for-international-students/english-for-academic-purposes) (Cao, score 0.76)<br>[English language proficiency \| UC - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/getting-started/admission-and-enrolment/enrolment-topics/english-language-proficiency) (Cao, score 0.73)<br>[Enrol as an international student \| UC - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/getting-started/admission-and-enrolment/enrol-international-student) (Cao, score 0.72)

**4. Học phí**

- For the 2026 academic year the University of Canterbury charges international students tuition that varies by programme – the Certificate in Foundation Studies costs NZ$27,470 for the 8‑month course, the Diploma in University Studies is quoted at NZ$36,100–$44,476 for a full year, most bachelor degrees fall between NZ$34,000 and NZ$46,310 per year (depending on credit load), master programmes range from NZ$34,000 to NZ$71,400 and doctoral study is billed at NZ$43,850 per 120 points (with total PhD fees of NZ$8,570–$10,321 per 120 points). All tuition fees are GST‑inclusive and are added to the mandatory annual Student Services Levy of NZ$1,236 and compulsory medical and travel insurance of NZ$899, so the typical annual cost equals tuition plus NZ$2,135 in levies and insurance; the online application fee for international candidates is NZ$150, and students should also budget around NZ$1,000 each year for textbooks and other course materials.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Study Fees - University of Canterbury International College](https://www.ucic.ac.nz/study/study-fees/) (Cao, score 0.83)<br>[International tuition fees \| UC - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/study/getting-started/study-and-living-costs/study-costs/international-tuition-fees) (Cao, score 0.82)<br>[[PDF] 2026 - International Prospectus - University of Canterbury](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz/content/dam/uoc-main-site/documents/pdfs/c-brochures/international-students/international-prospectus-uc.pdf) (Cao, score 0.76)

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student living on a University of Canterbury hall of residence the typical weekly cost is about NZ $525, which over a 48‑week academic year works out to roughly NZ $27,300; adding the annual items that are not covered in the weekly total – books and stationery (≈ NZ $750), parking (≈ NZ $600), a one‑off bed‑linen pack (≈ NZ $205), clothing (≈ NZ $750) and other mandatory one‑off fees such as administration, student‑association and contingency deposits (≈ NZ $3,305) – brings the total yearly expenditure to around NZ $32,900. If a student chooses private “flatting” accommodation, the university’s cost guide lists weekly expenses for food (≈ NZ $145), transport (≈ NZ $25), contents and liability insurance (≈ NZ $10) and incidentals (≈ NZ $20), totalling about NZ $200 per week; the missing housing component typically ranges from NZ $200 to NZ $250 per week in Christchurch, so the full weekly outlay is roughly NZ $400–$450, which translates to an annual cost of approximately NZ $20,800–$23,400 plus the same insurance and incidental items.

**2. Student visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: A fee‑paying student visa for an international student wishing to study at the University of Canterbury requires an official Offer of Place from the university and evidence that the tuition fee shown on that offer has been paid or can be paid, along with proof of sufficient funds to cover living expenses for the duration of the stay (typically NZ$15,000 per year for a single student, or the equivalent amount in the applicant’s currency, demonstrated through recent bank statements, a sponsor’s financial undertaking (INZ 1014) or a guarantor’s guarantee); the applicant must also meet standard immigration criteria such as a valid passport, health and character checks, and may need to show a return ticket or a sponsor’s ability to fund travel out of New Zealand. Once the visa is granted, fee‑paying students are permitted to work up to 25 hours per week during scheduled academic periods and full‑time during official holidays, providing a legal avenue to supplement living costs while studying. Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who have completed an approved qualification at the University of Canterbury are eligible for Immigration New Zealand’s Post‑Study Work Visa; to qualify they must have studied full‑time onshore, hold a valid student visa at the time of graduation, and apply within the prescribed period after their study finishes. For a bachelor’s degree, master’s degree, doctorate or any Level 7‑or‑higher qualification the visa may be granted for up to three years, while a graduate diploma (or other non‑degree qualification) is limited to the length of the study period, capped at one year. Only one Post‑Study Work Visa can be issued to a person, and the work must be in an occupation related to the completed study unless the graduate holds a higher‑level qualification that allows unrestricted work.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: The University of Canterbury supports international students with a range of structured internship, co‑op and placement options—including mandatory professional‑experience components in engineering and science programmes, a dedicated NZUni Talent Jobs Board, and regular industry‑focused career events—so that graduates gain real‑world experience before completing their degrees; as a result, its graduate outcomes are strong, with roughly 22 % of the 2019 graduating cohort being international students and about nine‑tenths of all UC graduates securing full‑time employment or further study within six months of graduation, many entering sectors such as engineering, information‑technology, health, education and business both in New Zealand and abroad. Lương & thị trường: New Zealand university graduates—including those from the University of Canterbury—typically earn a full‑time salary in the $50,000–$60,000 NZD range, with honours‑level bachelor graduates around $40,000–$50,000 NZD and PhD holders earning the highest incomes; international degree graduates are most commonly employed in roles such as early‑childhood teacher, university lecturer, secondary‑school teacher, primary‑school teacher, accountant, software developer, programmer, systems analyst, management consultant, civil engineer, registered nurse and nurse manager, and they concentrate in the Professional, Scientific and Technical Services and Health Care and Social Assistance sectors, which together account for about 30 % of their employment, while the broader New Zealand labour market continues to show a low unemployment rate of roughly 4‑5 % and strong demand for green‑list occupations including software engineers, DevOps and full‑stack developers, cybersecurity specialists, registered nurses, civil, structural and electrical engineers, construction project managers, quantity surveyors, physiotherapists and occupational therapists.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: The University of Canterbury provides a dedicated International Student Office that runs a compulsory New Student Induction and UC International Welcome orientation session for all first‑time international students, offering information on campus life, study support, health and safety, and how to connect with student clubs; the office also assists with housing through its International Student Accommodation service, helping students secure on‑campus or approved off‑campus accommodation, providing advice on leases, budgeting and moving in, and offering a 24‑hour emergency line (Ext 92111 from a campus landline or 0800 823 637 direct) for urgent matters; in addition, the university’s Careers and Employability Centre delivers career services to international students, including one‑to‑one career counselling, CV and interview workshops, employer networking events and a job portal that connects students with local and global employers, and all of these services can be accessed by calling the general university line 0800 827 748 (within NZ) or +64 3 369 3999, or the International enquiries line +64 3 288 0702. Đời sống & môi trường: The University of Canterbury offers a vibrant campus life for its more than 20,000 students, including over 150 student‑run clubs and societies that cover everything from academic and cultural interests to sport, food, gaming and community projects, and new groups can be started easily if a niche isn’t already covered; international students benefit from dedicated support services, a 24‑hour emergency line (ext 92111 from campus landlines or 0800 823 637 direct) and on‑site security, and the university’s Students’ Association runs free and ticketed events such as concerts, barbecues and the famous Tea Party throughout the year; among the cultural groups is a Vietnamese Students’ Society that provides a community for Vietnamese students to share language, culture and support, organise festivals and social outings, and connect with other international peers, helping newcomers settle into life in Christchurch while enjoying the safe, green campus nestled between sea, mountains and sky.
