### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Western Sydney University offers two main intakes for international students – an Autumn Session that begins in February/March with an application deadline of 15 November the previous year, and a Spring Session that begins in July/August with a deadline of 15 May – and you apply for free through the university’s International Online Application Portal (unless you are completing an Australian HSC, IB or New Zealand NCEA, in which case you must apply via the Universities Admissions Centre and pay the UAC fee); the application process involves creating an online account, completing the form, uploading proof of academic results and English proficiency, paying the first‑session tuition and required Overseas Student Health Cover, receiving an offer, accepting it, paying all fees, requesting an electronic Confirmation of Enrolment (eCoE) which is emailed within 24 hours, using the eCoE to apply for your student visa, and attending the compulsory International Orientation before classes start.

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Western Sydney University requires international applicants to provide official academic transcripts and meet a minimum GPA of 2.0 (on the Australian 7‑point scale) or the local equivalent (for example 6 out of 10 in Vietnam, 6 out of 10 in Turkey, 2.0 or 3.0 depending on the programme in Thailand), and to have successfully completed a recognised secondary qualification (e.g., 66 % or higher, or the required number of passes in core subjects such as A‑Level, GCE O‑Level, Cambridge International, or the local senior secondary certificate). For undergraduate entry the university assesses ATAR or equivalent scores, while postgraduate entry requires a completed bachelor’s degree that meets the discipline‑specific GPA threshold. English proficiency must be demonstrated with IELTS 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0) or equivalent scores such as TOEFL iBT 575 (minimum 4.5 in the writing section) or PTE Academic 65 (no communicative skill below 58); certain health‑science courses demand a higher IELTS overall 7.0 with 6.5 in each component. Where the course is creative, design, architecture, or some specialised health programmes, a portfolio and/or interview may be required in addition to the academic and English‑language criteria.

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Western Sydney University requires all international applicants whose first language is not English, or who have not studied in an English‑medium secondary or tertiary program within the two years before enrolment, to demonstrate proficiency with an approved test: IELTS Academic 6.5 overall (no band lower than 6.0), TOEFL iBT 82 total (minimum 21 Writing, 18 Speaking, 13 Reading, 13 Listening), or Pearson Test of English Academic 58 overall (no component below 50); Duolingo English Test is not listed as an accepted qualification. Applicants may be exempt if they are native‑English speakers or can provide satisfactory evidence that English was the language of instruction for a qualification obtained in an English‑speaking country (including Australia, New Zealand, UK, USA, Canada, Ireland, Singapore, South Africa, Kenya, Ghana, Nigeria, Zambia, Zimbabwe, Fiji, Papua New Guinea, Philippines, Tanzania, Solomon Islands, etc.) and have at least one assessable qualification from that country, or if they have completed senior secondary study or at least one full‑time year of university study in such a country in English. For the Masters Qualifying Program, an IELTS 6.0 (no band below 5.5) is accepted but requires 10 weeks of Navitas English (EA3).

**4. Học phí**

- Western Sydney University’s tuition for international students is quoted in Australian dollars (AUD) and varies by program and level: for the 2025 academic year undergraduate courses cost between $30,952 and $38,968 per year (e.g., Business‑related degrees at $36,416 – $38,968 and Science/Arts at $37,744 – $38,968), while postgraduate programmes range from $33,120 to $45,296 per year (e.g., Master of Teaching at $33,400, Master of Science at $39,256 and specialised psychology or health masters at $45,296). The university charges an international application fee of $100 (AUD). In addition to tuition, students must pay compulsory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC), typically $600 – $800 per year, and a student services and amenities levy of roughly $300 – $400 per year, so the total annual cost is approximately $31,500 – $40,000 (AUD) for undergraduate studies and $34,000 – $46,500 (AUD) for postgraduate studies.

**Yêu cầu học thuật chi tiết**

- Western Sydney University requires international students to enrol in a full‑time load of four units (40 credit points) per session, with each unit normally worth 10 credit points (some may be 20), and at least three units (≈30 credit points) to meet the visa‑mandated minimum of 80 % of a full‑time study load; entry into undergraduate or postgraduate programmes is contingent on meeting the university’s prerequisite academic standards and English language requirements (e.g., IELTS 6.5 or equivalent) specific to each discipline, while credit points follow the standard Australian system and are convertible to other international frameworks (10 credit points ≈ 3.75 US credits or 7.5 ECTS); the curriculum is structured around units that articulate clear learning outcomes aimed at developing both disciplinary knowledge and professional competencies, ensuring graduates achieve the graduate outcomes set by the university and, where applicable, accreditation bodies such as the Architects Accreditation Council of Australia.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student visa (subclass 500) requires applicants to prove they have enough funds to cover living costs, travel and tuition, with the baseline amount set at AUD 29,710 for a single student (plus AUD 2,000‑3,000 for travel) and additional amounts for dependents – AUD 10,394 for a partner, AUD 4,449 per child and AUD 13,502 per child for school fees – and applicants must provide genuine evidence such as bank statements, loan documents or scholarship letters; a mandatory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) must be maintained for the whole visa period, otherwise the visa condition 8501 is breached; work rights allow up to 48 hours per fortnight while classes are in session (unlimited hours during scheduled vacation periods) and there is no work‑hour cap for students undertaking a master’s by research or a doctoral degree, and these requirements and conditions are outlined on the Department of Home Affairs website, the Study Australia guide and confirmed by Western Sydney University’s international student FAQs. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) – Post‑Study Work stream – allows international students who have completed a CRICOS‑registered degree at an Australian university such as Western Sydney University to remain in Australia after graduation for a period that depends on the level of qualification, typically two to three years for bachelor and master degrees and up to four years for a doctorate, with Hong Kong and British‑National‑Overseas passport holders eligible for up to five years; the visa grants unrestricted work rights and the ability to include eligible family members, but the extra two‑year extension that was introduced on 1 July 2023 for graduates of degrees on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List was discontinued in mid‑2024, so current holders can only rely on the standard stream durations and any regional‑area additional year or two that may apply if their study was undertaken in a designated regional location.
