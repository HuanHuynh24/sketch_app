**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Griffith University accepts international applications year‑round but recommends three main intake deadlines: for the February (Trimester 1) start submit your application by mid‑November, for the July (Trimester 2) start submit by mid‑April, and for the October (Trimester 3) start submit by mid‑July; some programmes have specific closing dates that are listed on their degree pages. The standard international application fee is AUD 100 for undergraduate courses and AUD 150 for postgraduate courses (fees are paid online when you submit the application). To apply you should first choose the programme you want, then gather certified copies of your academic transcripts, English‑language test results and any required portfolios or audition material, verify that you meet the entry requirements, and complete the online application through the Griffith International Application portal (or via a registered recruitment agent). After submission you will receive a confirmation email, an International Admissions Officer will assess your documents and may request further information, and once you are offered a place you must formally accept the offer and pay the required deposit to secure your enrolment; applicants completing an Australian Year 12 can also apply through QTAC when the August opening period begins.

**4. Học phí**

- For international students at Griffith University the tuition is charged weekly; in 2025 the fee is A$465 per week for programmes up to 25 weeks and A$435 per week for longer programmes, with an enrolment (registration) fee of A$225, which for a typical full‑time study load of 48 weeks works out to about A$20,880 in tuition plus the enrolment charge, giving a total annual cost of roughly A$21,105 (AUD). In 2026 the rates rise to A$470 per week (≤25 weeks) and A$440 per week (>25 weeks) with an enrolment fee of A$230, resulting in an estimated annual tuition of A$21,120 and a total of about A$21,350 (AUD). The standard application fee for international applicants is A$85 for undergraduate courses and A$115 for postgraduate courses. Other mandatory costs for international students include Overseas Student Health Cover (approximately A$600–A$800 per year), a student services and amenities levy (around A$150 per year), and the Australian government’s minimum living‑cost requirement of A$29,710 per year. All amounts are indicative and may vary by specific programme and year.

**5. Chi phí sinh hoạt**

- For an international student at Griffith University the typical yearly cost of living breaks down as follows: rent in a shared off‑campus house ranges from about A$250 to A$450 per week, food (self‑catered) costs about A$140 to A$280 per week, transport to campus is A$30 to A$60 per week, a mobile‑phone/internet plan adds A$15 to A$30 per week and entertainment/other personal expenses are A$100 to A$200 per week, giving a total weekly outlay of roughly A$535 to A$1,020. Multiplying by 52 weeks yields an annual accommodation‑and‑living cost of approximately A$27,800 to A$53,000. Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for a full year is normally around A$800 to A$900, bringing the overall annual budget for housing, food, transport, phone, entertainment and insurance to roughly A$28,600 – A$53,900. The Australian government’s minimum living‑expense guideline for international students is A$29,710 per year, so most students should plan for a total yearly cost in the neighbourhood of A$30,000 to A$55,000, depending on lifestyle and accommodation choice.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Griffith University is recognised globally for both its overall standing and its subject‑specific performance, attracting a large cohort of international students (over 20,000) and holding prestigious accreditations such as EQUIS (granted to Griffith Business School in 2024, placing it in the top 1 percent of business schools worldwide) and AACSB. In the 2024 QS World University Rankings the university sits within the top 250 institutions worldwide, while in the 2024 QS Sustainability Ranking it placed 40th globally (2nd in Queensland and 7th in Australia). Subject‑level rankings show strong results: in the 2026 QS Subject Rankings Griffith moved to 199th in Social Sciences and Management, entered the 49th band for Sports‑related subjects, ranked 149th in Environmental Sciences, achieved a 26‑50 band placement in Music (its first appearance), placed 30th in Nursing and 151‑200 in Psychology. The 2026 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings by Subject placed Griffith’s Arts and Humanities in the 251‑300 band, Computer Science in the 176‑200 band, Physical Sciences in the 201‑250 band, Social Sciences in the 126‑150 band, and Business and Economics in the 101‑125 band, reflecting its broad academic strength across teaching, research, industry engagement and international outlook.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa requires that international students demonstrate sufficient financial capacity to cover the full tuition fees for up to 12 months (minus any amount already paid) and meet the Department’s minimum living cost estimate of around AUD 21,000 per year, plus the cost of Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) which must be in place from the day of arrival and maintained for the entire stay; applicants must provide evidence such as bank statements, scholarship letters or a sponsor’s financial documents showing genuine access to the funds, and any family members joining later must also meet these financial requirements. OSHC is compulsory for the visa holder and any dependents and must be continuous with no gaps in coverage. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) is the post‑study work visa for international graduates from Australian institutions such as Griffith University, and it lets you stay, work and study in Australia for a limited period after you finish your course; the length of stay is determined by the level of your qualification – a bachelor degree (including honours) or a master's by coursework generally gives up to two years, while a research master’s or a PhD provides up to three years of full‑time work rights, and Hong Kong or British National Overseas passport holders may be eligible for up to five years – and from 1 July 2023 graduates who have completed an eligible qualification on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply for an additional two‑year extension to the Post‑Study Work stream, on top of any one‑ to two‑year regional extension if you studied, lived and worked in a designated regional area; you must apply for the visa within the six‑month window after your student visa expires (your student visa itself allows a three‑month period after course completion to arrange a further visa), and you may include eligible family members in the application.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Griffith University embeds work‑integrated learning—including internships, industry‑based projects and co‑op placements—into most undergraduate and postgraduate programmes, allowing students (including internationals) to earn credit while gaining professional experience and expanding their networks; these opportunities are supported by dedicated employability services that connect graduates with employers across sectors such as health, engineering, business, information technology, education and sustainability, and the university reports a graduate employment rate of approximately 85 percent within the standard post‑graduation timeframe, reflecting strong outcomes for international students seeking careers in Australia and globally. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Australia typically earn less than their domestic peers, with the 2024 Graduate Outcomes Survey showing a median salary gap of about $12,000 for Business and Management graduates and similar disparities across most study areas, while health‐related fields such as Dentistry, Pharmacy and Medicine maintain the highest full‑time employment rates and therefore strong labour‑market demand; doctoral (HDR) graduates enjoy very high employment outcomes—with over 93 % employed—reflecting the robust demand for advanced research skills, and the latest Occupation Shortage List from Jobs and Skills Australia highlights persistent shortages in high‑growth occupations including software and data engineers, cybersecurity specialists, registered nurses, allied health professionals, construction managers and renewable‑energy technicians, all of which present lucrative entry‑level salary prospects for international graduates who possess the relevant qualifications.
