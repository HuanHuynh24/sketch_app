### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- International applicants to the University of Western Australia must submit their online application through the UWA Apply portal, upload all required documents (with certified English translations if needed), and pay a non‑refundable application processing fee of AUD 150 before the application is processed; for most countries the deadline to apply for Semester 1 (February‑June) intake is 19 January 2026 (with the same date each year) and for Semester 2 (July‑November) intake is 8 June 2026 (similarly 8 June each subsequent year), while the university also notes that for inbound exchange programmes the deadlines are 30 October of the previous year for Semester 1 and 15 April of the same year for Semester 2; after payment and submission, applications are automatically considered for the Global Excellence Scholarship and other international scholarships, and applicants can track their status and receive offers via the portal.

In addition, applicants must satisfy any subject‑specific prerequisites listed for their chosen major (for example, chemistry and biology for health‑science degrees) and demonstrate English‑language competence through an approved test (such as IELTS, TOEFL, or an Australian secondary English subject). **3. Tiếng Anh**

- International applicants to the University of Western Australia must demonstrate English proficiency through one of the approved tests: IELTS Academic – a minimum overall band score of 6.5 with no individual band below 6.0 (higher‑standard courses require 7.0 overall with no band below 6.5); TOEFL iBT – a minimum overall score of 82 with at least 20 in Speaking and Writing and 17 in Listening and Reading (higher‑standard courses require 92 overall with 23 in Speaking and Writing and 20 in Listening and Reading); PTE Academic – generally a minimum overall score of 70 with each band no lower than 64 (some faculties, such as Law, require 70 in each band); and Duolingo English Test – a minimum overall score of 125 with sub‑scores of at least 115 in Speaking, 125 in Writing, 115 in Reading and 110 in Listening (scores are valid for two years). English‑language waivers are granted when an applicant has completed a previous tertiary qualification that was taught in English, has successfully finished UWA’s CELT bridging program, or meets any specific exemption criteria outlined for the chosen course in the university handbook.

**4. Học phí**

- For international on‑shore undergraduate students at the University of Western Australia the annual tuition fee is charged in Australian dollars and varies by programme; in 2026 it ranges from about $34,200 for a Bachelor of Arts up to roughly $52,800 for combined bachelor‑master degrees such as the Bachelor of Molecular Sciences and Master of Bioinformatics, with many courses falling between $40,000 and $52,000 (e.g., Bachelor of Commerce $40,900, Bachelor of Science $41,500, Bachelor of Modern Languages and Business $48,100). In addition to the tuition amount, all international applicants must pay a non‑refundable application processing fee of $150, and other compulsory charges such as the Student Services and Amenities Fee (SSAF) are added to the statement of account, so the total amount payable equals the listed annual tuition plus the $150 application fee and any additional university fees.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- The University of Western Australia is currently ranked 77th in the world in the QS World University Rankings (2026) and 153rd in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings (2026); its subject‑specific QS rankings include Marine/Ocean Engineering (24th), Mining & Mineral Engineering (32nd), Water Resources (within the 76‑100 band), Agricultural Sciences (13th), Biological Sciences (31st), Clinical Medicine (51‑75), Dentistry & Oral Sciences (48th), Ecology (39th), Oceanography (29th) and Business Administration (76‑100); overall, the university boasts ten subjects placed within the world’s top 50 in the QS 2026 rankings and is recognised for its accredited programs, including a CRICOS registration (00126G, PRV12169) that validates its courses for international students and an internationally accredited Business School.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Subclass 500 Student visa requires international students, including those enrolled at the University of Western Australia, to demonstrate genuine financial capacity by providing evidence that they can cover their travel to Australia, up to 12 months of tuition fees (or a pro‑rata amount for shorter courses), 12 months of living expenses for themselves (generally estimated at AUD 21,000–30,000 per year) and any accompanying family members, as well as school fees for dependent children, with the Department of Home Affairs able to request additional proof of access to those funds; applicants must also obtain mandatory Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) costing roughly AUD 500–800 per year for a single person and maintain continuous coverage from arrival until departure, and they are permitted to work up to a maximum of 48 hours per fortnight during scheduled study periods (including exam weeks) with unlimited hours allowed during recognized course breaks, all of which are stipulated on the official immi.homeaffairs.gov.au and studyinaustralia.gov.au guidance for international students. Sau tốt nghiệp: After graduating from the University of Western Australia, you can apply for the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) under the Post‑Higher Education Work stream, which provides unrestricted work rights and allows you to stay in Australia for a period that depends on your highest qualification: typically two years for a bachelor’s degree, two years for a master’s (research) degree and up to four years for a PhD, with an additional two‑year extension available from 1 July 2023 for degrees on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List and a further one‑year regional bonus for students who studied, lived and worked in regional areas such as Perth, meaning most graduates can remain for three years and PhD holders for up to four years; you may also include eligible family members in the application.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: At the University of Western Australia, the Work‑Integrated Learning (WIL) programme connects students—including international students who may work up to 48 hours per two weeks while studying—to more than 500 host organisations across private, public and not‑for‑profit sectors, enabling them to complete internships, co‑ops or placement projects that count toward their degree and build professional skills such as teamwork, problem‑solving and communication; these placements are a proven pathway to graduate employment, with many host employers hiring interns directly after the placement, and graduate outcome data show placement‑intensive programmes achieve employment or job‑placement rates above 95 % for most postgraduate courses—for example, the Master of Commerce records a 98.4 % placement rate and the Master of Professional Accounting 95.7 %—while the Business School’s overall graduate destination survey reports that the majority of its 1,605 2018 graduates are employed in Australia or abroad in fields such as finance, marketing, logistics, human resources and consulting, and across the university the overall graduate employment rate within four months of graduation consistently exceeds 90 %, with alumni frequently citing the WIL experience as a key factor in securing their first professional role. Lương & thị trường: International graduates in Australia earn significantly less than their domestic peers, with the 2021 median full‑time salary for undergraduate international graduates at about A$54,300 (versus A$65,000 for domestic graduates) and for postgraduate coursework graduates at A$54,800 (versus A$89,700 for domestic), while postgraduate research graduates earn around A$86,900 (domestic peers A$95,000). Labour‑market data shows that the occupations with the strongest demand for new graduates—including international students—are in the technology and engineering sectors: software and applications programmers, ICT business and systems analysts, ICT security specialists, civil‑engineering professionals, construction managers, mining engineers, and advertising/marketing professionals, all of which command some of the highest entry‑level salaries (e.g., software programmers average A$134,000, construction managers around A$150,000). These trends are reflected in official projections from Jobs and Skills Australia, which highlight continued growth in professional, scientific and technical services and construction through 2023, making these fields the most promising pathways for international graduates seeking both employment and competitive earnings in the Australian labour market.
