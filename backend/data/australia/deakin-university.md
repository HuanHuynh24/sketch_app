### **I. Thông tin chung**

**1. Kỳ nhập học, deadline và quy trình apply**

- Deakin University accepts international students for four main intakes each year—Intake 1 begins 25 February, Intake 2 on 1 May, Intake 3 on 1 July and Intake 4 on 27 October 2026—and the typical application deadlines are 31 December for the February intake and 31 May for the July intake (with earlier cut‑offs for high‑demand courses); applicants must complete the online International Student Application, create a Deakin account, select their preferred programme, upload required academic and English‑language evidence, and pay the non‑refundable application fee (AU$85 for most undergraduate courses and AU$90 for most postgraduate courses), after which they receive an Offer Letter, confirm acceptance, and proceed with the Student Visa (subclass 500) process, including health insurance and tuition payment.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Key dates - Deakin University](https://www.deakin.edu.au/study/key-dates) (Cao, score 1.00)<br>[International students](https://www.deakin.edu.au/international-students) (Cao, score 0.99)<br>[How to apply](https://www.deakin.edu.au/international-students/how-to-apply) (Cao, score 0.99)<br>[International students](https://www.deakin.edu.au/students/enrolment-and-fees/international-students) (Cao, score 0.78)

**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- International students applying to Deakin University must submit official academic transcripts showing a minimum Grade Point Average of 5.0 on the Australian 7‑point scale (or the equivalent of a 2:1 undergraduate degree) for undergraduate entry and the same GPA for postgraduate programmes, along with proof of English proficiency such as IELTS 6.5 overall (with no band lower than 6.0), TOEFL iBT 79, or PTE Academic 58; specific courses may also require particular prerequisite subjects— for example, engineering programmes expect prior study in mathematics and physics, health‑related degrees need biology or chemistry, and design, architecture, fine‑arts and related fields mandate a portfolio of recent work and, where indicated, an interview to assess creative capability, while other professional programmes (e.g., teaching, nursing, law) may also request an interview or audition as part of the selection process; if the required GPA or subject background is not met, applicants can be considered for a foundation or bridging pathway before commencing the degree.
- Nguồn nổi bật: [Entry requirements - Deakin University](https://www.deakin.edu.au/international-students/entry-requirements) (Cao, score 0.71)<br>[International students](https://www.deakin.edu.au/international-students) (Cao, score 0.66)<br>[How to apply](https://www.deakin.edu.au/international-students/how-to-apply) (Cao, score 0.60)<br>[International students](https://www.deakin.edu.au/students/enrolment-and-fees/international-students) (Cao, score 0.46)

**3. Tiếng Anh**

- Deakin University requires international applicants to demonstrate English proficiency equivalent to at least CEFR B2, which is met by a minimum overall IELTS score of 6.0 for undergraduate courses and 6.5 for postgraduate coursework programmes (CEFR C1); comparable scores are TOEFL iBT 79, PTE Academic 58, or Duolingo English Test 115. Applicants who have completed a recent tertiary qualification that was taught entirely in English (or who have studied for at least two years in an English‑medium institution) may be eligible for an English‑language waiver, as can holders of passports from recognised English‑speaking countries, though each waiver request must be assessed and approved by the university’s admissions team before enrolment.

**4. Học phí**

- Deakin University charges international students tuition in Australian dollars (AUD) and the typical annual tuition fee ranges from ≈ AUD 30,000 to AUD 38,000 per year for most undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs, with exact amounts depending on the discipline and level of study; the total program cost therefore varies between ≈ AUD 60,000 and AUD 120,000 for two‑year master’s degrees or three‑year bachelor’s degrees. An application (admission) fee of AUD 100 is required for all international applicants, and additional mandatory charges include a Student Services and Amenities Fee of about AUD 300 per year, Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) which typically costs ≈ AUD 600 annually, and miscellaneous costs such as enrolment processing, textbook, and accommodation fees that can add another AUD 1,000‑2,000 per year.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

- Deakin University is positioned at #207 in the 2026 QS World University Rankings and is recognised in the top 1 percent of institutions worldwide in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, holding a 5‑star QS rating; its QS subject rankings place it within the global top 50 for several disciplines—including Education (around 30th), Nursing (approximately 31st), Sports‑related subjects (mid‑40s) and Business & Management (near 45th)—while the university is fully accredited by Australia’s Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) and its professional programmes carry additional accreditations such as Engineers Australia for engineering degrees, the Australian Nursing and Midwifery Accreditation Council for nursing, CPA Australia for accounting, and the Australian Computer Society for information‑technology courses, supporting a diverse international student community drawn from over 130 countries.

**2. Visa và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa: The Australian Student visa (subclass 500) requires each applicant to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity, typically a minimum of AUD 29,710 for the main student to cover 12 months of living expenses, tuition fees, and a return travel allowance, with additional amounts of AUD 10,394 for an accompanying partner and AUD 4,449 for each child (plus school fees of about AUD 13,500 per dependent child if school‑aged); evidence such as bank statements, loan documents or scholarship letters must show that these funds are readily available at the time of application, and the applicant must also maintain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the entire duration of the stay. While on a subclass 500 visa, international students are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight during scheduled study periods and may work unlimited hours during recognized school breaks, although students enrolled in a master’s by research or doctoral program generally have no work‑hour restriction; compliance with these conditions, along with enrolment at a registered provider such as Deakin University, provision of a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE), and adherence to the health‑insurance and address‑notification requirements, is required for visa validity. Sau tốt nghiệp: The Temporary Graduate (subclass 485) visa for international students who have graduated from an Australian institution, such as Deakin University, provides unrestricted work rights and allows the holder to stay in Australia for a period that depends on the qualification and any applicable extensions – typically 2 years for a bachelor’s or master’s degree and 3 years for a doctoral qualification (with Hong‑Kong and British National Overseas passport holders eligible for up to 5 years); from 1 July 2023 graduates whose degree is on the Department of Education’s Eligible Qualifications List can apply for a further two‑year extension at no cost, and those who have studied, lived and worked in a regional area may receive an additional one‑to‑two‑year regional extension, meaning the total possible stay can reach five years, and the visa also permits the graduate’s partner and dependent children to remain, study and work in Australia.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Deakin University actively supports international students in gaining real‑world experience through its Work Integrated Learning (WIL) program, which offers industry‑linked internships, placements and project‑based learning that can be taken as part of a degree or arranged independently via the DeakinTALENT career hub, and this strong emphasis on practical experience translates into very high graduate outcomes – the university consistently ranks as the top Victorian institution for graduate employment, with recent graduate employability figures showing around a 96 % rate of graduates being employed or in further study within four months of completing their studies, and alumni are employed across a broad spectrum of sectors such as health, engineering, information technology, business, finance, creative industries and government, reflecting the university’s extensive industry partnerships and global opportunities that enhance both employability and career progression for international students. Lương & thị trường: In 2024 the median full‑time earnings for Australian graduates were $75,000 for undergraduate degree holders, $100,000 for postgraduate coursework students and $104,400 for postgraduate research graduates, with higher qualifications generally yielding higher pay; graduates in high‑demand fields such as mining engineering, medical studies and general medicine command the strongest starting salaries and typically see about a 35 % increase in earnings within five years of graduation, while 72 % of education graduates move into teaching roles and 60 % of engineering graduates secure professional engineering positions, and labour‑market data for international graduates have been published annually since 2021, showing comparable employment outcomes; Jobs and Skills Australia’s occupation shortage analysis highlights these same sectors—particularly mining engineering and health‑related occupations—as being in acute demand across the nation, reflecting strong labour‑market demand for graduates entering these occupations.
