**2. Điều kiện đầu vào**

- Osaka University admits international under‑graduates who have completed an education equivalent to Japanese upper‑secondary school (12 years) and are at least 18 years old, and who submit a certified copy of their secondary‑school graduation certificate together with an official transcript (học bạ) showing the grades for all subjects; if the last school attended was a university, the applicant must also provide the secondary‑school certificate and transcript. Admission is based on the Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students (EJU) – a copy of the score report (or the equivalent national university‑entrance exam such as the Gaokao, A‑levels, IB, or German Abitur) must be submitted, and the subject scores must meet the minimum thresholds set by the chosen faculty (the required subjects vary by school and are listed in the university’s admissions table). **3. Tiếng Anh / Tiếng Nhật**

- Osaka University requires international graduate applicants to demonstrate English proficiency by achieving at least a TOEFL iBT score of 60, an IELTS Academic overall band of 5.0 with no component below 5.0, or a TOEIC score of 700 or higher; these are the only English tests accepted (TOEFL ITP, TOEIC IP, TOEIC Bridge and IELTS Indicator are excluded), and while the university does not list PTE or Duolingo as accepted exams, applicants who are native English speakers or who hold a degree from an institution where English is the medium of instruction are generally exempt from submitting an English‑language test score. In addition, non‑native Japanese speakers must provide proof of Japanese language ability, typically a JLPT certificate at least N3 level (N2 for certain departments such as Biological Sciences), unless they are Japanese nationals or permanent residents.

## **C. XẾP HẠNG, STUDENT VISA, VIỆC LÀM VÀ ĐỜI SỐNG**

**1. Xếp hạng và kiểm định**

- Osaka University, one of Japan’s leading research institutions, is positioned around the high‑60s globally in the QS World University Rankings (2024) and in the low‑70s in the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings (2024), ranking third among Japanese universities in both lists; its subject‑specific performance is strong, with QS placing its Chemistry program near the top‑30 worldwide, Engineering around the top‑60, and Life Sciences and Medicine within the top‑70, while THE ranks its Natural Sciences and Social Sciences programs similarly high; the university enrolls roughly 5‑7 % international students, offering a growing number of English‑taught master’s and PhD programmes across faculties such as Science, Engineering, Medicine, Economics, and International Public Policy, and all its degree programmes are officially accredited by Japan’s Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT), with engineering curricula additionally holding JABEE (Japan Accreditation Board for Engineering Education) accreditation.

**2. Student visa (COE) và chính sách sau tốt nghiệp**

- Visa/COE: To study at Osaka University as an international student you must first obtain a Certificate of Eligibility (COE), which the university applies for on your behalf by submitting proof of your ability to cover all living and tuition expenses—such as a recent savings balance certificate, income statements for the past year, or documentation of scholarship or sponsor funds—and any required identification of the financial sponsor; once the COE is issued you submit it together with your passport, completed visa‑application form and photos to the Japanese embassy or consulate in your home country to obtain a student visa, and after entering Japan you receive a resident card; the student visa permits part‑time work of up to 28 hours per week (or up to 40 hours during university holidays) provided you obtain a “Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted by the Status of Residence” from the local immigration office and have approval from Osaka University, and all official guidance and forms are available on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs’ Study in Japan website (studyinjapan.go.jp). Sau tốt nghiệp: International graduates who receive a degree (bachelor’s, master’s or PhD) from Osaka University are eligible for the Designated Activities visa for job‑hunting (often called the “continuing job‑hunting” status) if they are 18 years or older, have graduated within the past five years while holding a valid student status, can demonstrate sufficient personal funds (approximately ¥200,000) to support themselves, and intend to seek employment that is related to their field of study; the visa is typically issued for an initial period of six months and may be renewed once, allowing a maximum stay of up to one year for job‑search activities, while a newer “Future Creation Individual” (J‑Find) category can be granted for up to two years with possible further six‑month extensions.

**3. Cơ hội việc làm, lương và thị trường**

- Cơ hội việc làm: Osaka University provides a structured internship system for both domestic and international students, allowing credit‑bearing internships that are coordinated through the KOAN registration platform and the CLE participation‑form system, while paid internships require a “Permission to Engage in Activity other than that Permitted under the Status of Residence Previously Granted” to comply with Japanese immigration rules; the university also integrates overseas internship programs university‑wide, encouraging students to build international work experience that feeds directly into their job‑hunting (shūkatsu) activities, and this support contributes to a very high graduate employment outcome, with roughly 96‑97 % of Osaka University graduates securing regular employment within six months of graduation across a broad range of fields such as engineering, information science, medicine, natural sciences, economics and law. Lương & thị trường: In Japan the first‑month salary for new university graduates averages about ¥210,000, rising to roughly ¥230,000 for those with a graduate degree, with technical fields earning a premium of roughly ¥23,000–¥24,000 more than humanities; the overall employment rate for recent graduates has stayed above 90 % for the past decade, reaching 97.8 % in 2019‑20, and while the domestic labour market remains robust, companies in Osaka and the wider Kinki region are increasingly seeking international graduates for high‑skill occupations such as engineering, information‑technology, data science, artificial‑intelligence and other technical roles, offering them salaries that can be substantially higher than the domestic norm—evidenced by Huawei’s 2020 offer of ¥400,000 per month for engineering graduates, about double the typical starting pay—reflecting a growing demand for the global networks and specialized training that these graduates bring, even as the number of international graduates working in Osaka has declined only modestly compared with Tokyo since the pandemic.

**4. Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế và đời sống**

- Hỗ trợ sinh viên quốc tế: Osaka University’s International Student Office, housed in the Center for International Education and Exchange (CIEE), offers a full suite of services for international students, including a mandatory post‑arrival orientation that introduces campus life, academic procedures and support resources; dedicated housing assistance that helps students secure off‑campus accommodation, provides information on the JEES comprehensive lodging insurance and offers a contact point for visa‑related queries; a Career Assistance program that delivers job‑search workshops, employer networking events and one‑on‑one counseling tailored to international students; and an academic tutoring and advisory service that connects students with faculty mentors and peer tutors to support coursework across all faculties. The office can be reached at 06‑6877‑5111 or global‑ou@ml.office.osaka‑u.ac.jp, and additional support—including visa, accommodation and emergency counseling—is available through the broader CIEE support desk (phone 06‑6850‑5280, email ri‑syomu@office.osaka‑u.ac.jp). Đời sống & môi trường: Osaka University provides a vibrant campus life for international students, with hundreds of circles (clubs) ranging from sports, music, cultural exchange and language practice that welcome non‑Japanese members, and a dedicated Vietnamese Student Association that regularly holds gatherings, cultural festivals and information sessions for Vietnamese scholars and other interested students; safety is supported by a comprehensive guide that advises on issues such as alcohol, drugs, suspicious solicitations and personal data protection, recommends registering with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs “Tabi‑reji” travel‑registration system and, if needed, joining the Overseas Student Safety Management Assistance (OSSMA), and offers multiple consultation points—including the Center for International Education and Exchange (intl‑inbound@office.osaka‑u.ac.jp) and the International Student Affairs Division (outbound@ciee.osaka‑u.ac.jp)—which provide 24‑hour advice on emergencies, campus‑specific hazards and everyday concerns, while the university’s policy aims for a smoke‑free campus and strict enforcement of age‑related drinking laws to ensure a secure environment for all students.
